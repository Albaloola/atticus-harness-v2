# Endowment Policy Statement (Scotland/UK)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Produces a governance instrument for Scottish charity endowment management compliant with the Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005, Part 6, sections 42 to 49, and the Charities SORP (FRS 102).

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **Statutory factors** - 2005 Act, s.44 requires trustees to consider: suitability of investments, need for diversification, and any other relevant matters. Spending decisions must have regard to: the purposes of the charity, the need to maintain the value of the endowment, and the desirability of investing in assets with a particular social, environmental or ethical impact (s.44(3)).
- **Jurisdiction** - Scotland has a unified statute (2005 Act) applying to all Scottish charities, unlike the US state-by-state UPMIFA variation. The Trusts and Succession (Scotland) Act 2024 introduced recent reforms; seek advice on transitional provisions.
- **Designated funds** - clearly distinguish board-designated funds (revocable, not true endowment) from donor-restricted permanent endowment; require separate accounting under Charities SORP (FRS 102).
- **HMRC / trading rules** - charitable trading income is exempt from Corporation Tax if trading falls within primary purpose or is carried out by a trading subsidiary. Unrelated trading is subject to Corporation Tax. No direct equivalent to US private foundation rules under IRC §§ 4943 to 4944; HMRC focuses on trading vs. investment distinction.
- **Delegation** - document any discretionary authority to external investment managers in writing; Board of Trustees retains ultimate accountability under the 2005 Act.
- **No placeholders in final** - replace all bracketed fields with charity-specific data before Board presentation.
- **Legal review required** - solicitor must review for alignment with the 2005 Act, the charity's governing document (trust deed, SCIO constitution, or memorandum and articles), gift agreements, and OSCR regulatory guidance.
- **FCA/PRA regulation** - regulated investment activities require FCA-authorised firms; ensure investment managers hold appropriate FCA permissions.
- **OSCR annual return** - disclose endowment activity and investment policy notes as required in the Scottish Charity Annual Return.

**Adapted for:** Scotland and UK law
**Changes made:**
- 501(c)(3) non-profit → Registered Scottish Charity (OSCR-registered)
- UPMIFA → Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005, ss.42 to 49; Trusts and Succession (Scotland) Act 2024

- State of incorporation → Scottish registered address / principal office, Corporate seal → Charity seal (where applicable)

**Key Scottish/UK considerations:**
- The Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005, Part 6 (ss.42 to 49) provides the statutory framework for charity investment powers, not UPMIFA, Section 44(3) specifically requires trustees to consider the suitability of investments, need for diversification, and desirability of ethical/social/environmental investment where consistent with charitable purposes, Trustees' duties under ss.66 to 69 of the 2005 Act govern fiduciary obligations; different from US trust law, OSCR regulates Scottish charities; HMRC handles tax status, two separate bodies, Charities SORP (FRS 102) is the mandatory accounting framework for UK charities, no equivalent to US GAAP for nonprofits, SC006XXX format OSCR charity numbers, always reference in policy, SCIOs (Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisations) are a common legal form that may have specific constitutional endowment provisions, The Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005 replaced the Trustee Investments Act 1961 for Scottish charities, Trading subsidiaries are the common UK structure for non-primary-purpose trading, no UBIT regime, Annual returns to OSCR require disclosure of endowment funds and investment policies, The Trusts and Succession (Scotland) Act 2024 introduced reforms affecting trustee investment duties, check commencement and transitional provisions, UK Stewardship Code (FRC) and UK Corporate Governance Code may be relevant for larger charities, GDPR/UK data protection applies to donor records and gift documentation
