# Contaminated Land Agreement (Scotland/UK Adaptation)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

## Prerequisites

1. **Desktop study / Phase I to II site investigation reports** - contaminant data, sampling results, remedial options, cost estimates (produced per BS 10175 / CLR11)
2. **Party identification** - legal names, addresses, organisational details for all signatories (owner, purchaser, lenders, SEPA, local authority)
3. **Property records** - legal description (Title Sheet / Folio), site maps, historical use records (mining records, historical OS maps, trade directories), prior SEPA/local authority correspondence
4. **Regulatory programme** - applicable Part 2A regime (contaminated land status), SEPA / local authority remediation notice position, planning condition framework
5. **Cleanup standards** - relevant environmental standards, SEPA policy targets, intended future use (residential / commercial / industrial / public open space)
6. **Financial terms** - funding sources (Scottish Government grants, Vacant and Derelict Land Fund, private capital), cost-sharing arrangements, financial assurance requirements

## Agreement Structure

### Preamble and Recitals

Property location and legal description (Title Sheet / Folio number, Ordnance Survey), contamination history, Part 2A applicability (s.78A to YC of Environmental Protection Act 1990), contaminated land due diligence completion per BS 10175 and CLR11, SEPA/local authority correspondence references.

### Definitions

Source from Part 2A statutory language, Water Environment (Controlled Activities) (Scotland) Regulations, SEPA policy. Define: Contaminated Land, Appropriate Person, Remediation, Remediation Notice, Planning Condition, Relevant Environmental Standards.

### Environmental Liability

Appropriate person liability (Part 2A, s.78F), liability exclusion / exclusion from remediation (Class A / Class B apportionment), mutual indemnification, third-party claim procedures.

### Remediation Scope

Investigation, cleanup, and monitoring activities; relevant environmental standards-based performance targets; contingency protocol for unexpected conditions.

### Planning Conditions and Remediation Notices

| Control Type | Mechanism | Recording |
|---|---|---|
| Land use restriction | Planning condition / Section 75 agreement (Town and Country Planning (Scotland) Act 1997) | Land Register / Register of Sasines |
| Groundwater protection | Planning condition / SEPA CAR licence | SEPA register |
| Cap/barrier maintenance | Planning condition + maintenance covenant | Record + dedicated maintenance fund |
| Vapour intrusion | Remediation scheme / planning condition | Record + inspection schedule |
| Chain-of-title notice | Standard conveyancing searches | Register of Scotland |

Include long-term stewardship funding for each control.

### Regulatory Compliance

SEPA / local authority reporting obligations, Scottish Government grant conditions (if applicable), agency access and inspection rights.

### Financial Terms

- Cost allocation by party with milestone-based payment schedule, Overrun/contingency procedures (typically 10 to 20% reserve)
- Cost recovery rights against other appropriate persons (if identifiable)
- Financial assurance: escrow, letter of credit, or performance bond, Scottish Government grant compliance, Vacant and Derelict Land Fund, other applicable schemes [VERIFY current grant programmes]
- Environmental insurance requirements (coverage amount, policy terms, premium allocation)

### Representations and Warranties

**Owner**: knowledge of conditions, compliance during ownership, no pending enforcement action from SEPA/local authority.

**Purchaser**: appropriate person status for excluded liability, no prior involvement with contaminating activities, financial capacity.

### Covenants

Maintain appropriate person protections, comply with planning conditions, site access for monitoring, insurance maintenance, new contamination notification protocol.

### Dispute Resolution

Negotiation → mediation (environmental law specialist, Scottish qualified) → arbitration or litigation in the Scottish courts. Include interim interdict relief carve-out.

### Execution and Recordation

Signature blocks with authority (Company: two directors / director + secretary; LLP: two members; individuals: witness), conditions precedent to effectiveness, Land Register registration, amendment and assignment procedures.

## Appropriate Person Protections, Continuing Obligations

Part 2A (s.78F) and linked obligations require the following, draft as affirmative covenants with cure periods:

- [ ] Exercise appropriate care with respect to contaminant linkages at the site
- [ ] Comply with all planning conditions, remediation notices, and SEPA directions
- [ ] Not impede effectiveness or integrity of any remediation measures
- [ ] Provide full cooperation, assistance, and access for remediation actions
- [ ] Comply with information requests from SEPA / local authority under s.78X
- [ ] Provide legally required notices upon discovery of new contamination

> **Critical**: Loss of appropriate person protections may shift remediation liability. Consider step-in rights for SEPA / local authority counterparty as a cure mechanism.

## Guidelines

- **Due diligence standard**: Desktop study (Phase I) per BS 10175:2011+A2:2021; ground investigation (Phase II) per BS 5930 / BS EN 1997-2; CLR11 model procedures for contaminated land management [VERIFY current British Standard revisions]
- **Appropriate person screen**: Identify Class A (causer/permitting) vs Class B (owner/occupier where Class A cannot be found) - structure for maximum exclusion from liability
- **Part 2A pre-emption**: Contractual indemnification cannot override statutory Part 2A liability, structure indemnities to complement statutory defences; avoid creating additional liability through remediation activities
- **SEPA/local authority variation**: Remediation standards, planning conditions, and oversight requirements vary by local authority, confirm applicable approach before drafting
- **Unknown contamination**: Include discovery protocol with notification timelines, investigation cost allocation, and remediation responsibility triggers
- **Registration timing**: Register remediation notices / planning obligations promptly on conclusion; confirm Land Register (or Register of Sasines) registration requirements, notarisation, and indexing
- **Assignment**: Preserve liability protections on assignment, require regulatory notice; avoid creating new appropriate person liability for assignee

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill adapts a US CERCLA/EPA brownfields agreement framework for the Scottish and UK contaminated land regime.

### Key Adaptations

| US Concept | Scotland/UK Equivalent |
|---|---|
| CERCLA (Comprehensive Environmental Response, Compensation, and Liability Act) | Part 2A of the Environmental Protection Act 1990 (ss.78A to 78YC) - contaminated land regime |
| EPA (US Environmental Protection Agency) | SEPA (Scottish Environment Protection Agency) - principal environmental regulator in Scotland |
| Brownfields | Contaminated land / Previously Developed Land (PDL) |
| ASTM E1527-21 (Phase I ESA) | BS 10175:2011+A2:2021 (Investigation of potentially contaminated sites, code of practice) / CLR11 model procedures |
| BFPP (Bona Fide Prospective Purchaser) defence | Appropriate person liability exclusion / Class A vs Class B apportionment under Part 2A |
| All Appropriate Inquiries (40 CFR Part 312) | Contaminated land due diligence, desktop study, site visit, ground investigation per BS 10175 / CLR11 |
| ARARs (Applicable or Relevant and Appropriate Requirements) | Relevant Environmental Standards, SEPA policy targets (WAT-PS-06-02, etc.) / planning policy |
| Institutional controls (deed restrictions, engineering controls) | Planning conditions / Section 75 agreements / Remediation notices / Voluntary agreements |
| EPA Brownfields grants (Assessment, Cleanup, Revolving Loan, Job Training) | Scottish Government, Vacant and Derelict Land Fund (VDLF) / Scottish Land Fund / SEPA direct action |
| EPA § 104(k) grant compliance | Scottish Government grant scheme compliance, check VDLF terms [VERIFY current grant programmes and their conditions] |
| Davis-Bacon Act (prevailing wage) | Not applicable in UK, Construction (Design and Management) Regulations 2015 apply for health and safety |
| NEPA/NHPA (National Environmental Policy Act / National Historic Preservation Act) | Not directly applicable, planning permission process includes Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) if required under Town and Country Planning (Environmental Impact Assessment) (Scotland) Regulations 2017 |
| Uniform Guidance (2 CFR Part 200) | Not applicable, Scottish Government grant monitoring and audit procedures apply |
| PRP (Potentially Responsible Party) | Appropriate person under Part 2A (Class A: causer/permitting; Class B: owner/occupier) |
| PRP cost recovery under CERCLA § 107 | Common law / contractual cost recovery between appropriate persons; Part 2A exclusion apportionment mechanisms |
| NPDES (stormwater/discharge permits) | CAR licence (Water Environment (Controlled Activities) (Scotland) Regulations) - SEPA permits for water discharges |
| RCRA (Resource Conservation and Recovery Act) | Environmental Permitting (Scotland) Regulations / Waste management licensing |
| CERCLA § 101(40)(H) - affiliate screen | Part 2A exclusion, check the "knowingly permitted" / "caused or knowingly permitted" test; no equivalent per se to the CERCLA affiliate test |

### Specific Scottish Regulatory Notes

[SCOTS: Part 2A of the Environmental Protection Act 1990 was inserted by the Environment Act 1995 and applies in Scotland with modifications made by the Contaminated Land (Scotland) Regulations 2000 (SSI 2000/178, as amended). The Scottish regime differs from the English/Welsh regime in key respects, notably in the definition of "contaminated land" and the use of "relevant environmental standards."]

[SCOTS: SEPA has published detailed policy on relevant environmental standards (WAT-PS-06-02 - Framework for Risk Assessment and Contaminated Land). Planning Advice Note (PAN) 33 provides guidance on development of contaminated land.]

[SCOTS: The Vacant and Derelict Land Fund (VDLF) is administered by Scottish Government and Scottish Enterprise / Highlands and Islands Enterprise. Check current funding rounds and eligibility criteria.]

[VERIFY: BS 10175 is periodically updated, confirm current edition. CLR11 (Model Procedures for the Management of Land Contamination) was withdrawn by DEFRA/Environment Agency in 2019 but is still widely used as a framework. Check SEPA's current guidance position.]

[VERIFY: CAR (Controlled Activities Regulations) licensing, confirm current version of Water Environment (Controlled Activities) (Scotland) Regulations and SEPA's 3-tier licensing framework.]

[VERIFY: Check whether the property has been designated as "contaminated land" under Part 2A by the local authority, this changes liability and remediation requirements significantly.]

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
