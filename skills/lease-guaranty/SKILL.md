Drafts a guarantee of payment and performance for commercial lease obligations under Scots law. Ensures enforceability by covering guarantor obligations, waivers, duration, remedies, and execution formalities.

[SCOTS: Note: In Scotland, "guaranty" is spelled "guarantee" (same spelling as the verb). Scottish law does not recognise the English concept of an "authorised guarantee agreement" (AGA). Scots law also treats cautionary obligations (guarantees) differently from English law, the Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995 governs formal validity. ## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

1. **Underlying lease** - date, subjects, rent, term, renewal options, special provisions (service charge, insurance)
2. **Parties** - exact legal names, entity types, registered addresses (Scotland/UK), for landlord, tenant, and guarantor
3. **Scope** - full term vs. initial term only, liability cap (if any), joint and several (if multiple guarantors)
4. **Jurisdiction** - Scottish court jurisdiction; Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995 formalities; Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973 time bars

## Core Workflow

### 1. Draft Guarantee Obligations

Explicitly guarantee each:

- [ ] Base rent, additional rent, turnover rent
- [ ] Tax, insurance, service charge, utilities
- [ ] All other monetary obligations
- [ ] Maintenance, repair, and reinstatement duties
- [ ] Compliance with statutes and insurance procurement
- [ ] Indemnification obligations
- [ ] Enforcement costs (solicitor's fees, court costs, outlays)

### 2. Establish Payment-Not-Collection Language

**Critical**: State explicitly that this is a guarantee of **payment**, not collection. Landlord need not proceed against tenant first, exhaust security, or pursue other remedies before enforcing against guarantor. Guarantor's liability is primary, direct, and immediate.

If multiple guarantors, include joint and several liability wording.

### 3. Enumerate Waivers

Draft each waiver separately:

1. All cautionary defences under common law and statute
2. Right to require landlord to proceed against tenant first
3. Right to require landlord to mitigate loss
4. Presentation, demand, protest, notice of dishonour
5. Notice of acceptance and notice of default
6. Subrogation, reimbursement, indemnification, contribution (postponed until obligations indefeasibly satisfied)
7. Right to participate in landlord's security
8. Rights of relief

[SCOTS: Note: Scottish law takes a strict approach to cautionary obligations. The guarantor may be released if the underlying contract is materially varied without the guarantor's consent. Always obtain the guarantor's express consent to any lease variations. This differs from US law where "absolute" language may be sufficient.]

### 4. Set Duration

| Scenario | Approach |
|---|---|
| Full term + renewals | Continues through extensions, renewals, holdovers without further consent |
| Initial term only | Terminates at expiry; guarantor liable for accrued and surviving obligations |
| Burn-off / declining | Reduction schedule tied to tenant performance milestones |

Post-termination survival covers: accrued obligations, indemnification, environmental remediation, reinstatement, and breach damages.

### 5. Execute

- [ ] Signature blocks with printed name, title, date
- [ ] Entity authority representation (if entity guarantor)
- [ ] Attestation / witnessing per Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995 - a guarantee must be in a signed document, and subscription must be witnessed
- [ ] Arbitration / ADR clause (if desired)
- [ ] Counterparts and e-signature provisions (English and Scottish law recognises e-signatures)

## Pitfalls

- **Name mismatches** - all party names must exactly match the underlying lease; inconsistencies create enforcement risk
- **Variation of lease** - under Scots law, the guarantor may be released if the lease is materially varied without the guarantor's consent [VERIFY current law on cautionary obligations]
- **Prescription** - claims under a guarantee may prescribe (time-bar) after 5 years under the Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973
- **Requirements of Writing** - the guarantee must be a written document subscribed by the guarantor; electronic signatures are generally valid under Scots law but verify for heritable security documents
- **No AGAs** - Scottish law does not recognise authorised guarantee agreements (AGAs) as used in English law; use a fresh guarantee from the assignee
- **Insolvency** - tenant's insolvency does not automatically discharge the guarantor; but the guarantor may be affected by the Bankruptcy (Scotland) Act 2016
- Monetary amounts should appear numerically and in words, Defined terms must be consistent; cross-references must be verified, Governing law should specify Scotland; submit to the jurisdiction of the Scottish courts

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from a US lease guaranty to the Scottish legal framework.

### Key Differences

| US Concept | Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Guaranty | Guarantee / Cautionary Obligation |
| UCC Article 9 (security) | Standard Security (heritable) / Pledge (moveable) |
| Cognovit / confession of judgment | Not recognised in Scots law |
| Anti-deficiency statutes (CA CCP §580d) | No direct equivalent; standard securities have different enforcement rules |
| One-action rule (CA CCP §726) | Not applicable in Scotland |
| Community property (spousal consent) | Matrimonial Homes (Family Protection) (Scotland) Act 1981 may apply |
| Jury trial waiver | No jury in Scottish civil commercial cases |
| Notarisation | Attestation/witnessing under Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995 |
| State law | Scots law |
| US Dollar amounts | GBP (£) |

- Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973
- Bankruptcy (Scotland) Act 2016
- Matrimonial Homes (Family Protection) (Scotland) Act 1981

### Forms
There is no prescribed Scottish statutory form for commercial lease guarantees. A solicitor should draft a bespoke guarantee. The Scottish Law Commission's Report on Cautionary Obligations and Representations provides guidance on the law in this area.
