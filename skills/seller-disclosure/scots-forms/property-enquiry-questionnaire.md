# Property Enquiry Questionnaire (PEQ) - Scotland Reference

## Overview

The Property Enquiry Questionnaire (PEQ) is a standard document submitted by the **buyer's solicitor** to the **seller's solicitor** during the conveyancing process. Unlike the Property Questionnaire (which is part of the Home Report and completed by the seller for all prospective buyers), the PEQ is exchanged **after a price has been agreed** through missives.

The PEQ is not a statutory document but is used as a matter of conveyancing practice across Scotland. It is typically issued by the buyer's solicitor once a formal offer has been made and accepted (concluded missives), as part of pre-settlement due diligence.

## Purpose

The PEQ seeks specific factual information from the seller that is not covered by the Home Report or publicly available searches/registers. The seller is expected to answer "to the best of the seller's knowledge and belief."

## Typical Categories

### 1. Property Details, Full address and postal code (postcode)
- Unique Property Reference Number (UPRN)
- Council Tax band and annual charge, Date of construction (if known)

### 2. Title, Boundaries & Servitudes, Boundaries (ownership, maintenance responsibilities)
- Any boundary disputes past or pending, Servitudes affecting the property (access, drainage, rights of way)
- Public or private rights affecting the property, Shared access, common areas, maintenance obligations

### 3. Planning, Building Warrant & Consents, Any alterations, extensions, conversions carried out, Planning permission obtained (with dates and reference numbers)
- Building Warrant obtained and completion certificate issued, Work done without consent, details of any retrospective applications or indemnity insurance, Any enforcement action taken or threatened by the planning authority

### 4. Standard Securities & Encumbrances, Existing standard securities (mortgages)
- Any notices, orders, or other encumbrances affecting the property, Any "home rights" or occupancy rights of third parties, Titles Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003 - real burdens, conditions

### 5. Services & Utilities, Water supply (mains/private)
- Drainage (mains/septic tank/private)
- Gas, electricity, oil supply details, Broadband and telecommunications availability, Any service contracts or warranties in place

### 6. Factoring & Common Repairs, Factor details (name, contact)
- Factoring fees and arrears, Common repair fund and sinking fund, Upcoming major works or recent communal repairs, Title Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003 - tenement management scheme application

### 7. Hazardous Materials & Environmental, Asbestos (location, test/removal records)
- Radon (UK Health Security Agency affected-area status)
- Flooding history, flood risk (SEPA flood maps)
- Japanese knotweed or other invasive species, Contaminated land, Unexploded ordnance (if relevant)
- Coal mining or other historical extraction (if relevant)

### 8. Neighbourhood & Access, Planned developments nearby, Public rights of way, Noise, nuisance, antisocial behaviour complaints, Section 75 / planning agreements affecting the property

### 9. Council / Local Authority Matters, Road adoption status (private vs adopted road)
- Drainage adoption, Local development plan designations, Council Tax reduction or relief

### 10. Guarantees, Warranties & Documents, NHBC or similar new-build warranty, Roofing, damp-proofing, timber treatment guarantees, Fenestration (windows) guarantees / FENSA CERTASS, Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR)
- Gas Safety Certificate, Planning and building warrant documentation, Title deeds (disposition, searches)

## Timing in Conveyancing Process

1. **Home Report** made available before marketing
2. **Offer** submitted by buyer
3. **Acceptance** received; missives concluded (binding contract)
4. **PEQ** issued by buyer's solicitor
5. **Searches** ordered (Land Register, local authority, mining, environmental)
6. **Settlement / Completion** - funds transferred; disposition registered
7. **Post-completion** - registration update, LBTT return, deed registration

## Key Statutes

- **Title Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003** - real burdens, servitudes, tenement management
- **Housing (Scotland) Act 2006** - Home Reports, repairing standard
- **Town and Country Planning (Scotland) Act 1997** - planning permissions and enforcement
- **Building (Scotland) Act 2003** - building warrants and completion certificates
- **Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995** - formal validity of deeds
- **Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973** - title and servitude prescriptive periods

## Notes for Practitioners

- Answers in a PEQ are given on the basis of the seller's **actual knowledge** - there is no duty to investigate
- **Misrepresentation** is possible if the seller gives an answer known to be false or misleading; the buyer may then rescind or claim damages, The PEQ is typically signed by the seller and returned to the seller's solicitor, who forwards it to the buyer's solicitor, Standard PEQ forms are published by: Law Society of Scotland, Scottish Law Agents Society, and local conveyancing committees, The PEQ does **not** replace the Home Report, searches, or the buyer's own survey and enquiries
