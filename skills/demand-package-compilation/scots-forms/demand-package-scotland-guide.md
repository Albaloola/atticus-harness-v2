# Scotland Demand Package Guide

## Court Tracks, Thresholds

| Track | Value Limit | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Simple Procedure** | Up to £5,000 | Sheriff Court; designed for self-represented parties; online portal; sheriff has active case management; fixed costs regime |
| **Summary Cause** | £3,000 to £5,000 | Being phased into Simple Procedure; less formal than Ordinary Cause |
| **Ordinary Cause** | Over £5,000 | Sheriff Court; full written pleadings; more formal procedure; legal representation expected |
| **Court of Session (Outer House)** | Over £100,000 (typically) | Highest civil court in Scotland; complex/high-value claims; jury trial available for personal injury and defamation |

## Prescription and Limitation Periods

**Governing statute:** Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973

| Claim Type | Prescription Period | Long Stop |
|---|---|---|
| Delict (general) - e.g. negligence, nuisance | 5 years from date damage discovered or ought to have been discovered | 20 years from breach/conduct |
| Personal injury (delict) | 3 years from date of injury or date of knowledge (whichever is later) | 20 years from breach/conduct |
| Contract (written) | 5 years from breach | 20 years |
| Contract (oral) | 5 years from breach | 20 years |
| Reparation (property damage) | 5 years from discoverable loss | 20 years |

**Key point:** The "date of knowledge" for personal injury includes knowledge that the injury was significant, attributable to the alleged delict, and the identity of the defender. The triennium runs from the latest of these.

## Solatium, Scotland's Approach to Non-Economic Damages

**No punitive damages in Scots law.** Instead:

- **Solatium** - compensation for pain, suffering, and loss of amenity (the Scottish equivalent of "pain and suffering" + "loss of enjoyment of life")
- **Loss of consortium** - a separate claim available to the spouse/civil partner of an injured person (a derivative claim)
- **Aggravated damages** - limited availability where defender's conduct aggravated the injury (e.g. defamation, trespass); compensatory not punitive

### Valuing Solatium, Judicial College Guidelines

The **Judicial College Guidelines for the Assessment of Damages in Personal Injury** (UK-wide, used in Scotland) provide bracket ranges for solatium by injury severity:

| Injury Severity | Solatium Range (GBP) |
|---|---|
| Severe brain damage | £280,000 to £420,000 |
| Moderate brain damage | £19,000 to £230,000 |
| Quadriplegia | £310,000 to £400,000 |
| Amputation of arm (above elbow) | £115,000 to £155,000 |
| Severe psychiatric damage | £60,000 to £140,000 |
| Moderate psychiatric damage | £7,000 to £53,000 |
| Minor psychiatric damage (good recovery within 2 years) | £2,000 to £7,000 |
| Severe PTSD | £60,000 to £100,000 |
| Back injury (severe) | £45,000 to £75,000 |
| Back injury (moderate) | £15,000 to £45,000 |
| Back injury (minor) | £5,000 to £15,000 |

These are guidelines only; actual awards depend on individual circumstances. The **Scottish Courts** are not bound by English Court of Appeal guidance but do follow the same Judicial College Guidelines in practice.

## Damages in Delict (Scotland)

| Category | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Economic (patrimonial) loss** | Past and future wage loss, medical expenses, care costs, property damage, fully recoverable |
| **Solatium (non-patrimonial)** | Pain, suffering, loss of amenity, valued per Judicial College Guidelines |
| **Loss of consortium** | Available to spouse/civil partner (separate claim) |
| **Aggravated damages** | Limited; only where defender's conduct worsened the harm (not punitive) |
| **Punitive/exemplary damages** | **Not available** in Scots law. Do not include in any demand. |
| **Pre-judgment interest** | Awarded at judicial discretion; typically from date of citation to date of decree |

## Costs Rules (Judicial Expenses)

Scotland follows the **loser pays** principle (opposite of the US):

- **Expenses follow success** - the unsuccessful party usually pays the successful party's judicial expenses
- **Summary decree** - partial awards available at early stages
- **Fixed costs** - in Simple Procedure, expenses are fixed by the sheriff
- **Speculative fee arrangements** - available (no win no fee) but regulated differently from US contingency fees

## Key Differences for Demand Package Drafting

1. **Frame liability as "delict", not "tort"** - e.g. "delictual liability in negligence"
2. **Parties are "pursuer" and "defender"** - not plaintiff/defendant
3. **Don't include punitive damages** - they do not exist in Scots law
4. **Use Judicial College Guidelines** for solatium valuation
5. **Court selection:** Sheriff Court Ordinary Cause (up to £100,000) vs Court of Session (over £100,000)
6. **No jury trial** in Sheriff Court civil cases; jury only available in Court of Session for PI, defamation, and certain actions
7. **Prescription check** - verify the 5-year delict limit and 3-year PI limit before sending demand
8. **Use GBP** for all monetary figures

## Recommended Demand Letter Structure (Scotland)

1. **Header:** Pursuer's full name and address; Defender's full name and address
2. **Introduction:** "We act for [Pursuer] in respect of a claim for damages arising from [incident] on [date]"
3. **Statement of Facts:** Chronological narrative with exhibit references (Production 1, Production 2...)
4. **Delictual Liability:** Duty of care owed → duty breached → causation → loss. Cite Donoghue v Stevenson [1932] AC 562 where relevant.
5. **Damages Summary:** Itemised table in GBP with solatium (not pain and suffering)
6. **Prescription:** State the relevant limitation period and confirm within time
7. **Offer & Deadline:** Specific figure; 21 to 28 days typical; note that failure to respond will result in court proceedings

## Data Protection, UK GDPR / Data Protection Act 2018

Replace HIPAA with:

- **UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR)** - retained EU law post-Brexit
- **Data Protection Act 2018** - domestic implementing legislation
- **ICO (Information Commissioner's Office)** - UK regulator for data protection, Medical records require the patient's explicit consent under UK GDPR Article 9
- No equivalent of HIPAA authorisation form; use a UK GDPR-compliant consent form instead
- **Caldicott Guardian** - NHS-appointed officer overseeing patient data (relevant if NHS records involved)
