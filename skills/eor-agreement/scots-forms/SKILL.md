# Exporter of Record (EOR) Agreement [SCOTS]

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts an enforceable EOR Agreement appointing a qualified third party as exporter/consignor under UK law, allocating export compliance obligations, and managing liability under UK export control legislation.

**IMPORTANT NOTE, UK v US Framework:** This skill is adapted from a fundamentally US-oriented template. UK export control law differs substantially in structure, licensing mechanisms, and enforcement. This adaptation maps the equivalent UK provisions but a UK EOR agreement must be largely rewritten around the UK's distinct regime. The checklists below reflect UK requirements but you should verify each provision against the specific UK export control Order, retained regulation, and sanctions legislation applicable at the time of drafting.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party details** - legal names, entity types, jurisdictions, UK Company Registration Numbers, EORI numbers
2. **Goods/technology** - technical specs, UK Military List classifications, dual-use list classifications (UK Strategic Export Control Lists), UK Trade Control (trafficking and brokering) considerations
3. **Destinations and end-users** - countries, consignees, end-use / end-user certificates, consignment notes
4. **Scope** - compliance-only vs. combined logistics; exclusive vs. non-exclusive
5. **Existing authorizations** - ECJU licences (OGELs, SIELs, OLTs), Open General Trade Control Licences, OFSI authorisations
6. **Business terms** - fees, term length, insurance minimums, governing law/venue

## Output Structure

### 1. Parties & Recitals, Full party identification with UK Company Registration Numbers and EORI numbers, Recitals: principal's export need → regulatory complexity under Export Control Order 2008 (SI 2008/3231) and Retained EU Dual-Use Regulation → EOR expertise, Acknowledgment of penalty exposure under the Export Control Order 2008, SAMLA 2018, HMRC customs regulations

### 2. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **EOR / Exporter** | Party on UK export documentation legally responsible for the transaction as defined in UK export control law and customs regulations |
| **Export Controls** | Export Control Order 2008 (SI 2008/3231); Retained EU Dual-Use Regulation; Trade Controls (trafficking and brokering); UK sanctions under Sanctions and Anti-Money Laundering Act 2018 |
| **SPIRE / CDS Filing** | Export licence applications submitted via the UK SPIRE system; Customs declarations via the Customs Declaration Service (CDS) per HMRC requirements |
| **Controlled Items** | Goods, software, technology subject to UK export control lists (UK Military List, Dual-Use List, trade control goods) |
| **Denied Parties Screening** | Pre-shipment check against HM Treasury Sanctions List (UK Consolidated List), asset freeze targets, persons designated under UK sanctions regulations |
| **Export Documentation** | Commercial invoice, packing list, BOL/AWB, licence/OGEL reference, customs declaration (CDS), certificate of origin, consignment notes |

### 3. Appointment & Scope, Exclusive/non-exclusive; product categories, destinations, carve-outs, Authority limits: regulatory acts only vs. authority to bind commercially, Escalation for novel classifications, sensitive end-users, sanctions hits

### 4. EOR Obligations

- [ ] Classify items under UK Strategic Export Control Lists (Military List, Dual-Use List, trade control goods); document rationale
- [ ] Determine licensing requirements; apply for SIELs, use applicable OGELs/OGTCLs, or obtain ECJU licences as needed
- [ ] Submit customs export declarations via CDS (including Entry in Declarant's Records where applicable)
- [ ] Place destination / control statements on invoices and BOLs where required by licence conditions
- [ ] Screen all participants against UK sanctions lists (HM Treasury Consolidated List and applicable designations) **before each shipment**; document results
- [ ] Prepare complete export documentation per transaction
- [ ] Retain records **5 years** from export, licence expiration, or enforcement action (standard UK record-keeping requirement; verify against specific licence/regime)
- [ ] Notify Principal within **[X] business days** of compliance issues, licence denials, government inquiries
- [ ] Maintain professional indemnity / E&O insurance (£[X]M min.), HMRC customs bond if applicable, public liability (£[X]M min.)

### 5. Principal Obligations

- [ ] Provide accurate technical specs and end-use/end-user info before each shipment
- [ ] Disclose prior commodity jurisdiction determinations, classification requests, and any ECJU/OFSI correspondence
- [ ] Notify EOR of product changes, new destinations/end-users, ownership changes
- [ ] Warrant IP rights and manufacturing authorisations; no export-restricting liens
- [ ] Bear all costs: EOR fees, filing fees, duties, taxes, licence fees
- [ ] Cooperate with government audits and enforcement actions by ECJU, HMRC, OFSI
- [ ] Comply with Bribery Act 2010 (wider scope than FCPA, includes commercial bribery, facilitation payments, strict liability for failure to prevent) and UK anti-boycott provisions (where applicable)

### 6. Representations & Warranties, Both: not on any UK sanctions/restricted party list; no disqualifying investigations; authority to execute
- **EOR**: holds required licences/registrations; maintains compliance systems and trained personnel
- **Principal**: information accurate and complete; no undisclosed item restrictions, Ongoing covenant: immediate notice of restricted-party status change or material compliance issue

### 7. Indemnification

| Party | Covers |
|---|---|
| **Principal indemnifies** | Inaccurate/incomplete info; undisclosed classifications; principal-caused violations; unauthorised changes; breach of warranties |
| **EOR indemnifies** | EOR negligence or wilful misconduct; filing/documentation errors; unauthorised acts beyond scope |

- Procedure: written notice → indemnifying party controls defence → indemnified party cooperates, Unlimited liability carve-outs: gross negligence, wilful misconduct, fraud, intentional export violations
- [VERIFY] Indemnity enforceability under Scottish law (if governed by Scots law): the Law Reform (Contributory Negligence) Act 1945 may apply; ensure wording does not conflict with the Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977

### 8. Limitation of Liability, Cap: lesser of £[X] or [X] months fees paid, No consequential/punitive damages (except third-party awards or wilful misconduct)
- [VERIFY] Ensure limitation does not fall foul of Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977 (UCTA) where acting on the EOR's written standard terms

### 9. Term & Termination

| Event | Notice |
|---|---|
| Convenience | [30 to 90] days written |
| Material breach | [15 to 30] days + cure period |
| Immediate (no cure) | Bankruptcy; restricted-party listing (UK Consolidated List / asset freeze designation); licence revocation; imminent violation risk |

Post-termination: complete in-process shipments; transfer/assign licences per ECJU rules (UK export licences are generally non-transferable, flag in transition planning); return documents within [X] days; refund unearned fees. Surviving: indemnification, confidentiality, records, payment.

### 10. Governing Law & Disputes
- [England and Wales / Scotland / Northern Ireland] law; UK export control law supersedes on regulatory matters, Tiered: executive negotiation [30d] → mediation [60d] → arbitration/litigation, Injunctive relief carve-out for confidentiality breaches and imminent violations
- [VERIFY] If Scottish law: sheriff court or Court of Session depending on value; consider arbitration under Scottish Arbitration Centre rules

### 11. General Provisions, Integration; written amendments; counterparts; e-signatures (confirm e-signature validity under Scots law, generally valid under Electronic Communications Act 2000 / eIDAS Regulation as retained)
- Schedules: product/UK military-and-dual-use list, fee schedule, approved destinations, compliance procedures

## Guidelines

- **UK Military List trigger**: Defence articles on the UK Military List require specific ECJU licences (SIELs or OGELs); the Export Control Order 2008 Parts 2 to 3 covers military goods and dual-use items; consider standalone security annex
- **Customs declaration threshold**: UK customs export declarations required for goods exceeding applicable HMRC value thresholds (generally items over £2,000 in value or requiring licence) - HMRC Notice 275 [VERIFY current threshold and regime under CDS]
- **OFSI / UK sanctions**: Screen any party connected to a designated person or sectoral sanction under SAMLA 2018; the UK Consolidated List is the primary reference; check OFSI General Licences and reporting obligations
- **Anti-boycott / GCC**: The UK has some anti-boycott provisions but they are less comprehensive than the US Arab League boycott regime; consider specific contractual covenants if goods are destined for or transiting countries with boycott laws
- **Insurance**: Confirm PI, bond, and public liability minimums against industry standards and UK export risk profile
- **Governing law**: Indemnification and liability caps may face enforceability limits specifically under the Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977 (UCTA) and the Law Reform (Contributory Negligence) Act 1945 in Scotland
- **Licence transfers**: UK export licences are generally non-transferable to a successor EOR without ECJU approval; the transfer of an OGEL benefit to a new entity requires careful structuring; flag explicitly in transition planning

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
