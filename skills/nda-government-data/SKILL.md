# Non-Disclosure Agreement, UK Government Data

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

[SCOTS: Note] This skill adapts a US federal-government NDA template (NISPOM/EO 13526/FOIA/FISMA) for the UK under the Official Secrets Act 1989, the UK Government Security Classification Policy, and NCSC guidance. The classification scheme, statutory framework, and enforcement regime are entirely different. Scotland does not have separate state secrets legislation, the Official Secrets Act 1989 applies throughout the UK.

Drafts a UK-regulation-compliant NDA governing disclosure of sensitive UK government data to contractors or third parties.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party identification** - UK government entity (department/agency name, statutory authority), receiving party (legal name, UK security clearance status)
2. **Data classification** - TOP SECRET / SECRET / OFFICIAL (including OFFICIAL-SENSITIVE) per UK Government Security Classification Policy (GSCP)
3. **Underlying agreement** - contract, grant, or delivery agreement reference number
4. **Security framework** - applicable NCSC guidance, Security Policy Framework, Defence and Security Public Contracts Regulations (DSPCR)
5. **Authorised personnel list** - individuals with need-to-know and appropriate UK security clearance (SC / DV / CTC / BPSS)
6. **User-uploaded documents** - search for department names, project IDs, prior security incidents, existing protocols, notice clauses

If any prerequisite is missing, pause and ask, do not assume classification level or clearance status.

## Output Structure

### 1. Parties & Recitals

- Full legal name and department designation of disclosing UK government entity (e.g., "The Secretary of State for [Department]")
- Receiving party with legal capacity; note if registered in Scotland/England, Purpose of disclosure, programme/project reference, underlying contract/agreement number, Legal authority for sharing; recital establishing regulatory framework, Statement that disclosure is on a **UK Government Security Classification basis**

### 2. Definition of Confidential Information

Tailor to applicable data category under UK Government Security Classification Policy:

| Classification | Handling Standard | Applicable Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| TOP SECRET | Cabinet Office: Government Security Classification Policy; NCSC Protective Security Guidance |
| SECRET | Security Policy Framework; NCSC guidance; department-specific protective marking |
| OFFICIAL (OFFICIAL-SENSITIVE) | Government Functional Standard GovS 007; NCSC guidance |
| Personal data (Art 4 UK GDPR) | DPA 2018 / UK GDPR obligations |
| Commercially sensitive | Trade Secrets Regulations 2018; FOIA s 43 exemption |

Include provisions for:
- [ ] Oral disclosures, confirmed in writing within 10 business days
- [ ] Unmarked information, treated as confidential if reasonable person would recognise sensitivity given context
- [ ] Derivative works and compilations

[SCOTS: Note] Unlike the US, UK classifications are not statutory in the same sense as EO 13526. They are governed by the Government Security Classification Policy (Cabinet Office, updated frequently) and the Official Secrets Act 1989. Always mark `[VERIFY]` for current classification guidance version.

### 3. Receiving Party Obligations

- Limit access to authorised personnel with need-to-know and appropriate UK security clearance level (SC / DV / STRAP / BPSS as applicable)
- Implement safeguards (administrative, technical, physical) per UK NCSC guidance and department-specific security requirements, No unauthorised copying, reproduction, or removal from approved secure locations, Use restricted to authorised UK government purpose only, no commercial advantage, Subcontractor/third-party access requires prior written approval from the relevant government department, flow-down of all obligations, and equivalent UK security clearance

**For defence contractors:** Comply with the Security Aspects Letter (SAL) issued under the DSPCR 2011; maintain approved security facilities (FSC / List X as appropriate).

### 4. Exclusions & Mandatory Disclosures

**Standard exclusions:** publicly available (not through breach), already known, independently developed, received from third party without breach.

**UK government-specific mandatory disclosure protocol:**
1. Notify government department **within [48/72] hours** of any demand for disclosure (FOIA request, court order, parliamentary question, warrant)
2. Government may seek to assert FOIA exemptions (s 23, security bodies, s 24, national security, s 26, defence, s 27, international relations, s 43, commercial interests)
3. Compliance with lawful demands ≠ breach of this NDA
4. Receiving party cooperates with government's efforts to limit scope and assert exemptions

[SCOTS: Note] FOIA applies to government-held information; the receiving party may need to refer the FOIA requester back to the originating department if government data is sought.

### 5. Term & Survival

| Data Category | Confidentiality Duration |
|---|---|
| TOP SECRET / SECRET | Until declassified by proper authority (Cabinet Office / originating department) |
| OFFICIAL (OFFICIAL-SENSITIVE) | Per departmental retention schedule; typically 5 to 10 years from disclosure [VERIFY] |
| Personal data | Duration of processing (per DPA 2018 data minimisation / storage limitation principles) |
| Other commercially sensitive | 5 years from disclosure (default; adjust per programme) |

Return/destruction obligations and use restrictions survive termination. Interaction with underlying contract term must be addressed.

### 6. Return & Destruction

- Upon termination or government request: return **all** materials including copies, notes, derivative works, Classified (TOP SECRET / SECRET): destroy per NCSC guidance for secure destruction; or return to government facility via approved channels (official courier/secure transmission)
- OFFICIAL: destroy per NCSC Secure Sanitisation guidance [VERIFY]
- **Written certification** of compliance required within [30] days, Government retains audit rights over destruction compliance, For electronic data: ensure deletion from all systems including back-ups and cloud

### 7. Remedies & Enforcement

- Irreparable harm recognised; interim interdict (Scotland) / injunction (rest of UK) available without notice in appropriate cases, Damages: actual and consequential; liquidated damages if agreed and not a penalty, Classified breaches: criminal liability under Official Secrets Act 1989; Computer Misuse Act 1990
- Prevailing party entitled to legal costs (in Scotland: expenses to be taxed on the applicable scale)
- Reporting to relevant authorities: NCSC, police, security service as appropriate
- **In Scotland:** remedies include interdict, specific implement, and damages. Court of Session for higher-value claims; Sheriff Court for lower-value

### 8. Governing Law & Jurisdiction

- **Governing law:** Scots law (if Scottish government entity or Scotland-based contractor), or English law (as agreed)
- **Jurisdiction:** Court of Session (Scotland) / Sheriff Court or High Court (England) as agreed, Crown immunity not waived unless expressly agreed, preserve Crown's statutory immunities, Contract Disputes Act 1978 (MOD contracts) - separate disputes mechanism [VERIFY]

### 9. General Provisions

- Severability, integration, amendment (written, signed by authorised representatives)
- Waiver (non-enforcement ≠ waiver of future enforcement)
- Assignment prohibited without prior written consent from the government department, Notices: formal written notice to designated addresses, Representations: receiving party warrants authority, understanding, and security capability, Force majeure: government operations essential to national security are not excused

### 10. Signature Blocks

- Government entity: authorised signatory with delegated authority (may need Treasury or departmental delegations), name, title, date, department reference, Receiving party: authorised representative, name, title, date, company number (if limited company)
- Include UK DUNS number or Companies House registration number if applicable
- **Scotland-specific:** if executed as a deed (for gratuitous obligations), comply with Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995 - witnessing required

### Exhibits (as needed)

- **Exhibit A** - Authorised Personnel List (name, UK clearance level, need-to-know justification, security briefing date)
- **Exhibit B** - Data Classification Guide (per GSCP)
- **Exhibit C** - Security Requirements Matrix (referencing NCSC guidance)

## Guidelines

- Confirm signatory's delegated contracting authority for the government department, Never assume classification level, require explicit identification from the user per UK GSCP, FOIA exemption 24 (national security) and 26 (defence) require specific, factual justification, do not assert without basis, If receiving party is a foreign entity or connected to foreign state, flag National Security and Investment Act 2021 implications; pause for guidance, Distinguish MOD/DSPCR security NDAs from non-defence government NDAs, regulatory overlay differs significantly, Mark any statutory citation not independently verified with `[VERIFY]`
- For Scottish government contracts, note that the Scottish Government has its own security and information assurance team
- **Scotland-specific remedies**: interim interdict rather than injunction; adjudication only if contract permits

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
