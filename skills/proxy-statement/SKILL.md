# Notice of AGM and Proxy Form, UK/Scotland Adaptation

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a compliant notice of annual general meeting and proxy materials satisfying the Companies Act 2006 disclosure requirements, FCA Disclosure Guidance and Transparency Rules, and the UK Corporate Governance Code, enabling informed shareholder voting.

4. Directors' service contracts and independence determinations.

8. Shareholder requisitions received; Companies Act 2006 s.338 / s.340 procedures.
9. Annual report and accounts and financial year-end date.
10. Company secretarial contact and registered office address.

## Section Checklists

### 1. Notice of Annual General Meeting

- Date, time, location/virtual platform.
- Record date under CA 2006 - not more than 48 hours before meeting.
- Resolutions listed, specifying ordinary or special resolution type.
- Notice period compliance: 21 clear days for AGM of listed company (s.307A CA 2006); 14 days for other meetings (s.307).
- Place and time of meeting stated. Virtual/hybrid meeting provisions authorised by articles.
- Scotland: if registered in Scotland, per s.1139 CA 2006.

### 2. Introduction and Explanatory Notes

- Board recommends voting as indicated; proxy votes are counted.
- Approximate date of circulation (must be sent at least 21 days before meeting for listed co AGM).
- Voting rights by class; no cumulative voting under UK law.
- Appointment of proxy (s.324 CA 2006): two-way proxy form; proxy may vote on a show of hands or poll.
- Section 319A CA 2006: quoted company must publish poll results on a website.
- Electronic availability; paper copy on request; website publication.

### 3. Resolutions to Be Voted On

- Each resolution with board recommendation.
- Ordinary resolution (simple majority) vs special resolution (75%).
- Effect of abstentions (not counted) and withheld votes.
- Director elections: annual re-election recommended by UK Corporate Governance Code for FTSE 350.
- Auditor reappointment: audit committee recommendation; fees disclosure.
- Directors' Remuneration Report: advisory vote (s.439 CA 2006).
- Directors' Remuneration Policy: binding vote every three years (s.439A CA 2006).
- Authority to allot shares (s.551 CA 2006) - typically renewed annually.
- Disapplication of pre-emption rights (s.570/571 CA 2006) - required for listed companies.
- Share buy-back authority (s.693 CA 2006) if on agenda.
- Political donations authority (s.366 CA 2006) if applicable.
- Shareholder resolutions presented under s.338/340 CA 2006.

### 4. Board and Corporate Governance

- Director biographies: current and recent (5 years) directorships; other listed company appointments.
- Board composition: skills, diversity, tenure.
- Board size, meeting attendance.
- Committee membership: Audit, Remuneration, Nomination; chairs; meeting attendance.
- Independence determinations per UK Corporate Governance Code Provision 10 (board identification of independent directors).
- Board leadership: chair, senior independent director (SID), CEO separation.
- Compliance statement: "comply or explain" (UK Corporate Governance Code).

### 5. Directors' Remuneration Report

- Annual statement by chair of Remuneration Committee.
- Single total figure table (current and prior year).
- Scheme interests: share awards, option grants, vesting.
- Pension contributions.
- Payments to past directors.
- Percentage change in CEO remuneration vs employees.
- Relative importance of spend on pay (dividends, buybacks, total pay).
- Implementation report for binding policy vote.

### 6. Related-Party Transactions

- All RPTs requiring disclosure under UK Listing Rules (LR 11) / DTR.
- Smaller RPTs per Companies Act 2006 s.412 (directors' loans/guarantees/quasi-loans).
- For AIM companies: AIM Rule 13 requirements.
- Approval policy and procedure for RPTs.
- Identification and monitoring process.

### 7. Shareholder Requisitioned Resolutions

- CA 2006 s.338 threshold: 5% of voting rights or 100 members with minimum £100 paid-up.
- CA 2006 s.340 threshold for other meetings: same.
- Requisition deadline; publication obligations.
- Statement by members (s.314 CA 2006) - 1,000 words or less; company must circulate at requestor's expense unless eligible for free circulation.
- Need to state which members requisitioned; board's response included.

### 8. Voting Procedures

- Proxy form: appoint chair of meeting or named person; voting directions.
- Proxymity.io or other electronic proxy appointment platforms, confirmation requirements.
- Two-way proxy form (vote for, vote against, discretionary).
- Electronic voting and poll demand.
- Voting in person: admission procedures, shareholding evidence.
- Revocation of proxy; later appointment supersedes earlier.
- Discretionary authority for other business (typically limited).
- Poll results: section 319A CA 2006 quoted company obligation to publish within 15 days.
- Inspector of elections, corporate representative appointment (s.323 CA 2006).

### 9. Additional Information

- Annual report and accounts availability; website link.
- Electronic communications consent (s.333 CA 2006).
- Right to ask questions (s.319A CA 2006).
- CREST members: use of CREST proxy voting service.
- Data protection statement (UK GDPR / Data Protection Act 2018).
- Registered office, company number, contact details.

## Vote-Treatment Matrix

| Resolution Type | Required Majority | Abstentions | Effect of Withheld |
|----------------|-------------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Ordinary resolution | >50% of votes cast | Not counted | Not legally binding |
| Special resolution | ≥75% of votes cast | Not counted | Not legally binding |
| Directors' Remuneration Report | Advisory >50% | Not counted | Not legally binding |
| Directors' Remuneration Policy | Binding ≥50% of votes cast | Not counted | New policy cannot be implemented without approval |

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from US originals for use under UK company law and regulation.

**Key adaptations:**
- **Legal framework**: Companies Act 2006 entirely replaces US securities laws (Securities Exchange Act 1934, SEC Regulation 14A, Rule 14a-3). No SEC EDGAR filing; primary regulator is FCA for listed companies.
- **Regulator**: FCA (Financial Conduct Authority) replaces SEC. FCA Disclosure Guidance and Transparency Rules (DTR) and UK Listing Rules (UKLR) replace SEC proxy rules.
- **Document name**: "Notice of Annual General Meeting" and "Proxy Form" replace "DEF 14A Proxy Statement."
- **Governance code**: UK Corporate Governance Code (FRC) replaces US corporate governance guidelines.
- **Shareholder rights**: Companies Act 2006 statutory rights (s.307-340) replace US state corporate law regimes. No DGCL equivalent; Scotland follows CA 2006 plus Scottish company law provisions.
- **Resolutions**: Ordinary (simple majority) and special (75%) - no plurality/majority-of-votes-cast distinction for director elections as in the US.
- **Director elections**: Annual re-election recommended by UK Corporate Governance Code for FTSE 350 (not staggered US board terms).
- **Remuneration**: Binding vote on remuneration policy (every three years) and advisory vote on implementation report (s.439/439A CA 2006) - distinct from US say-on-pay.
- **Shareholder requisitions**: CA 2006 s.338/340 thresholds (5% or 100 members) replace SEC Rule 14a-8.
- **Broker voting**: Not applicable under UK law; discretionary voting is limited.
- **Proxy access**: UK law does not have a specific proxy access rule comparable to SEC Rule 14a-11; shareholders use CA 2006 s.338 to put forward resolutions.
- **No EDGAR**: Annual report and accounts filed at Companies House; Regulatory News Service (RNS) for listed company announcements.
- **Currency**: All amounts in GBP.
- **Related-party transactions**: UK Listing Rules LR 11 and DTR replace SEC Item 404.
- **Data protection**: UK GDPR / Data Protection Act 2018 replaces any US privacy law references.
- **Political donations**: CA 2006 s.366 requires shareholder authority, separate resolution needed.
- **Scotland-specific**: Scottish-registered companies under CA 2006 s.1139; court of registration is Court of Session. FCA regulation is UK-wide.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
