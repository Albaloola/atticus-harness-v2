## Prerequisites

1. **Facility records** - legal name, address, SEPA permit number, waste permit / environmental permit number, CAR licence references, SIC 2007 codes, Companies House registration number
2. **Chemical/pollutant inventory** - purchase records, production logs, CAS numbers for all listed pollutants manufactured, processed, or otherwise used
3. **Release/emission data** - continuous emission monitoring data, stack tests, discharge monitoring reports, waste transfer notes, emission factor calculations
4. **Prior SPRI submissions** - for consistency and trend analysis
5. **Pollution prevention records** - process modifications, material substitutions, recycling data

## Report Structure

### Part 1: Facility Identification

| Field | Requirements |
|---|---|
| Facility name | Exact legal name as registered with SEPA / Companies House |
| Address | Physical operational address (no PO boxes) |
| Parent company / Group | Legal name + Companies House registration number |
| SIC 2007 codes | Primary code by revenue/production volume |
| SEPA permit / Licence numbers | PPC permit, waste permit, CAR licence, radioactive substances licence |
| Company registration | Registered number (Companies House Scotland / UK) |
| Operator | Names, titles, contact for responsible management |

Flag ownership, operational control, or classification changes since prior year with narrative explanation.

### Part 2: Pollutant Identification & Threshold Determination

For each pollutant, document the SPRI listing name, CAS number, and activity determination:

| Activity | Definition | Threshold (SPRI / UK PI) |
|---|---|---|
| Manufacture / Produce | Produce as principal product or byproduct | [VERIFY current SPRI thresholds, typically per pollutant category] |
| Process | Use in industrial process | [VERIFY current SPRI thresholds] |
| Otherwise use | Any use not manufacturing/processing | [VERIFY current SPRI thresholds] |

**Estimation methods** (in order of preference): actual measurements (CEMS / DMR), mass balance, engineering estimates, published emission factors (UK Environment Agency / SEPA guidance).

**Exemptions to evaluate:**

| Exemption | Criteria |
|---|---|
| De minimis | Threshold-based, check SPRI guidance for specific exemptions |
| Articles / products | Formed to specific shape; no release under normal conditions |
| Laboratory use | Routine analytical/QC operations only, volume-based thresholds |

For pollutant categories: aggregate each member compound with CAS number, quantities by activity, and category total.

### Part 3: Environmental Releases

Quantify all releases by medium. Specify confidence / estimation methodology.

**Air emissions:**
- Stack/point source vs. fugitive/non-point, separate routine from abnormal releases, Data hierarchy: CEMS > stack tests > mass balance > UK emission factors (Environment Agency / SEPA guidance) > engineering judgement

**Water releases:**
- Direct discharge, include CAR licence number, reconcile with monitoring reports, Sewer (Scottish Water / trade effluent) - report separately with consent conditions

**Land disposal:**
- Distinguish on-site landfill, land treatment, other, Report under waste management permitting regulations

**Underground injection:**
- Classify by activity type, report if applicable under environmental permit

Provide narrative for significant year-to-year changes in any medium.

### Part 3a: Waste Transfers (for off-site disposal)

For waste transferred off-site: European Waste Catalogue (EWC) code, waste management method (D or R code), receiving facility details, quantity in tonnes.

### Part 4: Off-Site Transfers

For each receiving facility: legal name, address, SEPA / waste permit number, waste management method (disposal/treatment/recycling/energy recovery), pollutant quantity, waste form (EWC code).

Address recycling methods, energy recovery, treatment categories with efficiency, and disposal reconciled with waste transfer notes.

**Material balance check:** All pollutant quantities must reconcile across products, releases, transfers, and on-site management.

### Part 5: Pollution Prevention

Per relevant regulatory guidance:

1. **Process modifications** - quantify waste reduction per production unit (before/after)
2. **Material substitutions** - less-hazardous replacements with quantified reduction
3. **Operational improvements** - inventory management, training, maintenance impacts
4. **Recycling maximisation** - on-site recovery, off-site arrangements
5. **Future plans** - projects in development, feasibility studies, identified barriers

### Part 6: Certification

**Certifying official:** Senior management with facility operational responsibility, or senior company environmental official.

**Required certification language** (do not modify, adhere to SEPA's prescribed wording):

> "I hereby certify that I have reviewed the attached information and that, to the best of my knowledge and belief, the submitted information is true, accurate, and complete. I am aware that there are significant penalties for submitting false or misleading information."

**Penalty references:**
- Environmental Protection Act 1990 - environmental offences (s.33 waste offences; Part 2A contaminated land)
- Pollution Prevention and Control (Scotland) Regulations, permit conditions enforcement, Fraud / false statement, common law / statutory offences in Scotland

**Pre-certification checklist:**
- [ ] Technical review of calculations and data sources
- [ ] Compliance review against SEPA regulatory requirements
- [ ] Management review of business implications
- [ ] Legal counsel / environmental solicitor review

**Recordkeeping:** Maintain all supporting documentation for minimum 4 years from submission (consistent with SEPA regulatory requirements).

## Submission

- **Deadline:** Typically by end of February following the reporting year (1 Jan, 31 Dec)
- **Method:** SEPA online reporting portal (ePortal)
- **Units:** Kilograms per year (or metric tonnes, confirm SEPA preference for each pollutant)
- **Attachments:** Facility location map with release points, process flow diagrams, methodology documentation

---

## Pitfalls

- Reconcile all data across SEPA databases (SEPA public register, PPC permits, CAR licences, waste permits) - inconsistencies trigger inquiry, Threshold calculations, release totals, transfer quantities, and material balance must all reconcile internally, Document estimation methodology for every release pathway, must withstand regulatory audit, Never paraphrase or modify the statutory certification language, Flag borderline threshold pollutants with conservative analysis, SPRI data is publicly disclosed, draft with awareness of community right-to-know and third party challenges, Final certification requires facility management and environmental solicitor review

### Specific Scottish Regulatory Notes

[SCOTS: The UK Pollutant Release and Transfer Register (UK PRTR) implements the EU E-PRTR Regulation (Regulation (EC) 166/2006) as retained in UK law. SEPA operates the Scottish component. The regime is largely harmonised across the UK, with separate competent authorities: SEPA (Scotland), Natural Resources Wales (Wales), NIEA (Northern Ireland), and Environment Agency (England).]

[SCOTS: Reporting is required for facilities meeting specified activity thresholds under the E-PRTR Regulation. Facilities must report releases of pollutants listed in Annex II where thresholds are exceeded. This is distinct from the US TRI approach of reporting chemicals based on manufacturing/processing/use quantities.]

[SCOTS: The applicable regulations in Scotland are: Pollution Prevention and Control (Scotland) Regulations 2012 (SSI 2012/360, as amended), making PPC-permitted facilities subject to reporting obligations under the UK PRTR.]

[VERIFY: Check current SEPA deadline for SPRI submissions. Reporting years follow the calendar year (1 Jan, 31 Dec). The deadline is typically end of February but confirm for your facility's specific sector.]

[VERIFY: Confirm the current list of SPRI / UK PRTR pollutants and their thresholds. The published UK PRTR dataset includes over 90 pollutants across air, water, and land.]

[VERIFY: Check whether the facility meets the activity-based thresholds for mandatory reporting. If below thresholds, SPRI reporting may be voluntary or may not apply.]

[VERIFY: SEPA has published guidance on SPRI reporting, check current version on SEPA's website (SEPA online / UK PRTR pages).]

[VERIFY: The SEPA ePortal is the current submission channel. Confirm registration and access arrangements for your facility before the reporting deadline.]
