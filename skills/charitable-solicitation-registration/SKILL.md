# Charitable Solicitation Registration [SCOTS]

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Produces a complete registration and compliance package enabling a Scottish charity to legally solicit charitable contributions.

## Quick Start

Gather from the client before drafting:

1. **Organisation basics** - legal name (per OSCR entry), Scottish Charity Number (SC0xxxxx), registered office, entity type (SCI/trust/association)
2. **OSCR determination** - registered charity status, date of entry in the Scottish Charity Register
3. **Formation docs** - constitution, memorandum and articles (SCI), trust deed, or governing document
4. **Financials** - most recent annual accounts submitted to OSCR (all schedules); budget projections if newly formed
5. **Fundraising plan** - methods, geographic scope, projected revenue, professional fundraiser contracts
6. **Governance roster** - trustees with contact info, terms of office, signatory authority
7. **Adverse history** - OSCR inquiries, investigations, critical audit reports, trustee disqualification orders, criminal convictions (5 to 10 year lookback)
8. **Target local authority areas** - where any public collections or street fundraising will occur

## Core Workflow

### 1. Map Regulatory Requirements

For each fundraising method, determine:

| Factor | Key questions |
|--------|--------------|
| OSCR registration | Already registered? Any OSCR compliance conditions? |
| Public collection licence | Street collections, door-to-door, or any public place? |
| Professional fundraiser | Contract requires OSCR-prescribed wording and cancellation rights |
| Commercial participator | Commercial fundraising agreements require prescribed OSCR statement |
| Fundraising preference service | Process UK FPS suppression (note: not Scotland-specific but UK-wide MPS/FPS) |
| Annual return | OSCR annual return with fundraising income breakdown |

### 2. Assess Exemptions

Check for statutory exemptions under the Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005:

- [ ] Religious charities, soliciting only from own congregation/membership
- [ ] Exempt charities under Schedule 2 (universities, some museums etc.)
- [ ] Very small charities with turnover below OSCR filing thresholds
- [ ] Grant-making trusts with no independent public solicitation

Even exempt charities in some areas may still need a **public collection licence** from the relevant local authority. The Fundraising Regulator provides the Code of Fundraising Practice applicable across the UK. Cite the specific statutory provision; burden of proof rests with the charity.

### 3. Assemble Registration / Notification Package

**Core documents:**
- [ ] OSCR annual return / change of particulars notification (if required)
- [ ] Public charitable collection application (local authority to specific)
- [ ] Charity trustee declaration forms
- [ ] Signed declaration by trustees (min. 2/3 of trustees)

**Standard attachments:**
- [ ] OSCR charity registration certificate
- [ ] Governing document (constitution, SCI, trust deed)
- [ ] Latest annual accounts submitted to OSCR
- [ ] Trustee information (names, dates of appointment, relevant experience)
- [ ] Professional fundraiser agreement (if applicable, with prescribed OSCR wording)
- [ ] Commercial participator agreement (if applicable)

**For each professional fundraiser**, document: legal name/registered address, OSCR registration status (if registered as a charity themselves), compensation structure (flat / % gross / hybrid), engagement scope/duration, organisation oversight mechanisms, compliance with s.82 of the 2005 Act (prescribed statement and cancellation rights).

### 4. Prepare Financial Disclosure

Populate from OSCR annual accounts: total income, charitable expenditure, fundraising costs, governance costs, net assets, fundraising cost ratio, projected current-year fundraising revenue.

All figures must **exactly match** the accounts filed with OSCR. Regulators cross-reference; discrepancies trigger enforcement scrutiny regardless of intent.

### 5. Document Solicitation Activities

For each method (public collection, direct mail, online, telephone, events, crowdfunding, commercial participation): geographic scope, projected gross revenue, fund allocation (charitable/administration/fundraising %), and required point-of-solicitation disclosures per the Fundraising Regulator Code of Practice.

### 6. Disclose Adverse History

For the charity and all trustees within the lookback period:

- [ ] Criminal convictions (esp. offences involving dishonesty)
- [ ] OSCR inquiries, investigations, or improvement/remedial action notices
- [ ] Trustee disqualification orders under s.69 of the 2005 Act
- [ ] Charity registration denials, suspensions, removals from register
- [ ] Bankruptcy / sequestration of trustees

For each: describe the matter, current status/resolution, and remedial measures.

### 7. File and Calendar

- [ ] Verify current OSCR filing fees (if any) and local authority licence fees
- [ ] Confirm payment methods and filing route (OSCR Online / post)
- [ ] Confirm pre-solicitation requirements vs. periodic return deadlines
- [ ] Note certificate/permit issuance timeline and posting/public display obligations
- [ ] Add required Fundraising Regulator disclosure wording to solicitation materials
- [ ] Calendar all OSCR annual return and accounts filing deadlines

## Critical Pitfalls

- **Public collection licensing is local** - apply to the relevant local authority; each has its own form and fee
- **Professional fundraiser rules are mandatory** - OSCR-prescribed wording and 7-day cancellation rights; failure is a criminal offence
- **Commercial participator agreements** - prescribed statement must appear in all promotional material; "when you buy X, Y% goes to Z charity"
- **Fundraising Regulator self-regulation** - even if not registered with the Fundraising Regulator, the Code of Fundraising Practice applies as best practice and may be cited in OSCR investigations
- **Annual accounts consistency is non-negotiable** - reconcile all fundraising cost disclosures before submission
- **Trustee responsibilities** - trustees are personally liable for compliance with fundraising regulation; OSCR can issue improvement notices
- **Post-filing duties** - update OSCR with material changes (trustee turnover, constitutional amendments, new fundraising methods)
- **Enforcement priority** - OSCR and local authority licensing officers; Fundraising Regulator may also investigate Code breaches

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill replaces the US multi-state charitable solicitation registration framework with the Scottish regulatory regime under the **Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005** and the **Fundraising Regulator Code of Fundraising Practice**.

Key substitutions:
- IRS determination letter → OSCR charity registration certificate
- 41 US states + D.C. → Single OSCR registration + local authority public collection licensing requirements, US Form 990 → OSCR annual accounts and return, US state Attorneys General → OSCR (Scottish Charity Regulator)
- URS (Unified Registration Statement) → No UK equivalent; OSCR Online is the single portal, US state exemptions → Charities Act 2005 exemptions, USD → GBP

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
