4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

## UK Inventions and Employee Rights

The Patents Act 1977 creates a statutory framework for employee inventions that differs significantly from U.S. state invention laws. Key points:

**s.39(1)** - an invention made by an employee belongs to the employer if:
  - (a) it was made in the course of the employee's normal duties (or duties specifically assigned), and the circumstances were such that an invention might reasonably be expected to result; OR
  - (b) it was made in the course of the employee's duties and the employee had a special obligation to further the employer's interests (e.g. senior management).

**s.39(2)** - any other invention belongs to the employee.

**s.40 to 43** - employee may claim compensation if the invention (belonging to the employer) is of outstanding benefit to the employer. [VERIFY current case law on "outstanding benefit".]

The agreement should not attempt to override s.39 - any clause purporting to assign inventions that properly belong to the employee under s.39(2) must be clear and separately negotiated.

### Trade Secrets Protection (UK)

The Trade Secrets (Enforcement, etc.) Regulations 2018 (SI 2018/597) implemented the EU Trade Secrets Directive (retained post-Brexit). Key provisions:

- Definition: information that is secret, has commercial value because secret, and has been subject to reasonable steps to keep it secret.
- Unlawful acquisition, use, or disclosure gives rise to civil remedies.
- Reg. 5: Whistleblower protection, disclosure for public interest is not unlawful.
- Express contractual confidentiality obligations are recommended to supplement the statutory protection.

### Copyright Assignment (UK)

Copyright in works created by an employee in the course of employment automatically vests in the employer under s.11(2) Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988. For consultants/contractors, express assignment is required.

## UK Carve-Outs (Statutory)

Unlike U.S. states with specific invention assignment statutes (e.g., California Labor Code 2870), UK law relies on the Patents Act 1977 framework. However:

| UK Consideration | Effect |
|---|---|
| Patents Act 1977 s.39 | Invention ownership determined by statutory test |
| Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977 | Certain limitations on excluding liability for breach |
| Employment Rights Act 1996 | Protects employee against detriment for certain disclosures |
| Equality Act 2010 | Protects against discriminatory terms |
| GDPR / UK Data Protection Act 2018 | Controls on processing personal data |

**No U.S.-style state invention carve-outs exist in UK/Scotland.** The Patents Act 1977 provides the statutory framework for invention ownership, and the agreement should not seek to override s.39.

## Key Templates

Adapt to fit facts.

**Present Assignment:**
> The Employee hereby assigns and agrees to assign to the Company all right, title, and interest in and to all Inventions that are conceived, developed, reduced to practice, or authored by the Employee, alone or jointly with others, during the term of employment and that (a) relate to the Company's business or anticipated research or development, (b) result from any work performed for the Company, or (c) are developed using Company equipment, supplies, facilities, confidential information, or time. This assignment is subject to the provisions of the Patents Act 1977.

**Trade Secrets / Confidential Information Notice:**
> The Employee is notified that nothing in this Agreement prevents the disclosure of confidential information (a) in confidence to a government official or to a legal adviser solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law, or (b) in a complaint or other document filed in a legal proceeding, if such filing is made under seal. This is without prejudice to whistleblower protections under the Trade Secrets (Enforcement, etc.) Regulations 2018 and the Public Interest Disclosure Act 1998.

**Prior Inventions Schedule:**
> The Employee has listed below all inventions, original works of authorship, developments, improvements, and trade secrets that were made by the Employee prior to this employment and that the Employee wishes to exclude from the scope of this Agreement. If no items are listed, the Employee represents there are no such prior inventions.

## Pitfalls

- **Do not override Patents Act 1977 s.39** - the statutory test for invention ownership cannot be contractually overridden
- **No non-competes** - do not include unless separately authorised and compliant with UK restraint of trade doctrine
- **Employment law compliance** - post-termination cooperation must not create uncompensated work (National Minimum Wage Act 1998)
- **Multi-jurisdiction roles** - if employee works across E&W and Scotland, specify governing law
- **Confidentiality survival** - obligations survive as long as information remains confidential (but note public interest defences)
- **Personal devices** - if allowed, require approved storage, access controls, and deletion on termination
- **Signature block** - include acknowledgment of voluntary execution and opportunity to consult legal adviser
- **Overbreadth** - use clear defined terms; avoid restraints that could be deemed unreasonable (restraint of trade doctrine)
- **Data protection** - ensure any processing of personal data complies with UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018
