# Motion for Class Certification (Scotland/UK Adaptation)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Produces a plaintiff-side FRCP 23 (or state equivalent) class certification motion grounded in case-file evidence and structured for rigorous judicial scrutiny.

**[SCOTS: Note]** - Scotland does not have a direct US-style class action mechanism. The closest equivalents are:
- **Multi-party actions** under Chapter 26 of the Rules of the Court of Session (RCS) - group pleadings with lead cases
- **Group proceedings** under RCS Chapter 26A (personal injury)
- **Representative pleadings** (RCS 22.4.2) - one pursuer representing others with same interest
- **Sisting (staying) multiple individual actions** for coordinated case management
- **Proposed class action legislation** - the Scottish Parliament may introduce statutory class action mechanisms; practitioners should verify current status [VERIFY]
- UK-wide **opt-out collective proceedings** exist in the Competition Appeal Tribunal (CAT) only, Sheriff Court **Simple Procedure** and **Ordinary Cause** have no formal class mechanism, use sist or multi-party motions instead

## Prerequisites

1. Pleadings, initial writ / summons, defences (answers), amended pleadings
2. Commission and diligence materials / disclosure, interrogatories (RCS 36.13), document recovery, defender's business records showing scope of conduct
3. Expert reports, damages models, group-wide proof methodology, statistical analyses
4. Precognitions / witness statements, named pursuer(s), corporate witnesses, key witnesses
5. Named pursuer affidavit / declaration, representative-role understanding, absence of conflicts
6. Solicitor and counsel qualifications, CV, prior group-action experience, resources

## Quick Start

1. Review case materials and identify the uniform policy/practice at issue
2. Draft a precise, administrable class definition tied to the liability theory
3. Build multi-party / grouping arguments from disclosed evidence
4. Select and brief the applicable RCS chapter or statutory basis
5. Propose notice/administration plan and case management approach
6. Preempt anticipated defence arguments with record citations

## Core Workflow

### 1. Introduction (1 to 2 pages)

State defender's specific misconduct, proposed group definition (temporal/geographic scope), approximate group size, common injury, and why individual proof is impracticable.

### 2. Group Definition

The definition must:
- Track the liability theory (not merits outcomes)
- Be administratively manageable via objective criteria, Specify dates corresponding to period of misconduct, Match geographic scope to defender's conduct and applicable law, Require no individualised inquiry for membership

### 3. Multi-Party / Group Action Prerequisites

**[SCOTS: Note]** - The applicable criteria depend on the mechanism chosen. The following adapts US Rule 23(a) to Scottish equivalents:

**Numerosity** - Cite defender's records, disclosed data, or expert estimates. Address geographic dispersion and economic infeasibility of individual proofs. For negative-value claims, show individual recovery < judicial expenses.

**Commonality / Common Question** - Identify common questions generating common answers. Focus on uniform policies, standardised contracts, common course of conduct. Scottish courts consider "common interest" and "same interest" under RCS 22.4.2. Distinguish substantial common questions from peripheral individual ones.

**Typicality / Lead Case Suitability** - Show named pursuer / lead case suffered the same injury type through the same mechanism. Cite specific evidence. Address arguments that the lead case is atypical; distinguish damages quantum from liability theory.

**Adequacy / Suitability** - Named pursuer: case knowledge, commitment, no conflicts. Solicitor/counsel: prior group actions, results, judicial recognition, resources.

### 4. Statutory Basis Analysis

Select the applicable basis:

| Basis | Standard / Rule | Key Proof |
|---|---|---|
| RCS Chapter 26 - Multi-party Action | Court direction for group pleadings | Common evidence per claim element; common damages methodology; case management efficiency |
| RCS Chapter 26A, PI Group Proceedings | Common interest; lead case | Similar injuries from same incident; common causation |
| RCS 22.4.2 - Representative Party | Same interest; proper representation | Identical interest; no conflict among represented parties |
| Sisting individual actions | Court case management power | Efficiency; risk of inconsistent determinations |

**For predominance analysis**, element-by-element:
1. Identify common proof method for each claim element
2. Show common damages methodology, distinguish amount variation (acceptable) from methodology variation (problematic)
3. Integrate expert findings on group-wide proof feasibility
4. Address reliance/causation through presumptions or burden-shifting frameworks

**For superiority**: economics of individual claims vs. judicial expenses, inconsistent-determination risk, concentration benefits, existing individual litigation.

### 5. Notice and Administration Plan

- Identification method from defender's records, Plain-language notice (lawsuit nature, group definition, opt-out rights if applicable, binding effect)
- Distribution methods (direct mail, email, publication, digital) with justification, Cost estimates and allocation (judicial expenses responsibility)
- Claims administration process

### 6. Case Management Proposal

Address as applicable: liability/damages split, lead case selection, statistical sampling, court-appointed reporting, sub-group structure.

### 7. Anticipatory Defence

| Defence Argument | Response Strategy |
|---|---|
| Individual issues predominate | Common proof resolves core liability; individual damages manageable post-certification |
| Group unmanageable | Propose specific tools: lead case selection, split of proof, court reporter |
| Named pursuer atypical/inadequate | Distinguish damages variation from liability-theory divergence |
| Group definition overbroad | Definition tracks liability theory with objective, administrable criteria |
| Ascertainability problems | Objective identification from defender's own records |
| Sub-group conflicts | Aligned interests on core liability; propose sub-groups only if necessary |

### 8. Conclusion

Restate class definition, specify requested relief (certify / sist group, appoint representative pursuer, appoint counsel), reference notice plan and case management procedures, request hearing if warranted.

## Pitfalls and Checks

- **Pinpoint references required** - every factual assertion must cite document name, page, paragraph, exhibit number. No document-level references.
- **Jurisdiction-specific standards** - Sheriff Court, Court of Session, and UK Supreme Court differ on grouping and representative actions. Identify and apply controlling Scottish precedent.
- **Certification / sisting ≠ summary decree** - present enough merits evidence for group treatment without fully litigating the merits.
- **Expert evidence** - ensure expert methodology survives scrutiny under the applicable test (rule against hearsay in expert reports, Civil Evidence (Scotland) Act 1988).
- **Adverse authority** - distinguish unfavourable cases as factually distinguishable, superseded, or inapposite. Never ignore them.
- **Tone** - professional, confident, evidence-driven. No hyperbole.
- **Length** - 25 to 50 pages depending on complexity and local rules.

### Scottish Court Hierarchy for Group Actions
- **Sheriff Court** (Simple Procedure ≤£5k; Ordinary Cause >£5k) - limited group mechanism; mainly sist of individual actions
- **Sheriff Appeal Court** - appeal from Sheriff decisions
- **Court of Session (Outer House)** - primary forum for multi-party actions (RCS Chapter 26)
- **Court of Session (Inner House)** - reclaiming motion (appeal)
- **UK Supreme Court** - final appeal (Scottish civil appeals)

### Procedural Triggers, Application for group proceedings directions under RCS 26.2
- Lead case selection motion (RCS 26.5)
- Sist (stay) of individual actions pending group determination, Representative pursuer appointment under RCS 22.4.2
- Time-bar and prescription issues, prescriptive periods apply individually; group proceedings do not automatically freeze prescription (Plevin v Paragon Personal Finance [2017] UKSC 23)

### GBP Considerations, US damages figures should be adjusted to ~80% (GBP equivalent)
- Sheriff Court thresholds apply: Simple Procedure ≤£5,000; Ordinary Cause >£5,000
- Court of Session jurisdiction: >£5,000; exclusive >£100,000
- Judicial expenses (costs) follow the "loser pays" principle, Contingency fees prohibited; DBAs / CFAs available for litigation funding

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
