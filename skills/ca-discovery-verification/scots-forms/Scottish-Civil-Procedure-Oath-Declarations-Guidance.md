# Scottish Civil Procedure, Oaths, Affidavits, Declarations & Documentary Evidence

## No Direct "Discovery Verification" Equivalent in Scotland

**Scotland does not use US-style interrogatories, document production requests (RPD), or verification pages.** The procedure for formal statements and documentary evidence follows a different path.

## Scottish Mechanisms for Formal Statements

### Affidavits, Written statement of evidence sworn on oath before a notary public or other authorised person, Used in: interim interdict applications, summary decree, certain motions, and statutory applications, Form: must bear the name of the witness, be signed, and include the jurat (place, date, and signature of notary/magistrate)
- No equivalent of CCP §2015.5 perjury declaration; affidavits are sworn on oath

### Oaths & Oath-Taking
- **Notary public**: authorised to administer oaths for affidavits in Scotland
- **Depositions in Scotland**: evidence on oath before commissioner, used rarely (examination on commission)
- **Criminal proceedings**: Declaration under solemn declaration (equivalent to oath)

### Declarations (Unsworn)
- Certain statutory declarations (e.g., under statutory instruments) may be used
- **No equivalent of CCP §2015.5 "under penalty of perjury"** - Scottish law generally requires sworn statements for formal evidence, Perjury (Scotland) Act 1942: perjury is giving false evidence on oath; does not apply to unsworn written declarations

### Commission for Examination, Court-appointed commissioner to take evidence on oath from a witness, Used for: witnesses unable to attend court (illness, abroad), pre-trial preservation of evidence, Most similar to a deposition but rare and requires court order

## Key Differences from US

| US Concept | Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| CCP §2030.250 (interrogatory verification) | No interrogatories in Scottish procedure |
| CCP §2031.250 (RPD verification) | No request for production equivalent (commission and diligence for documents) |
| CCP §2015.5 perjury declaration | Not applicable, Scottish formal evidence requires oath |
| State of California perjury (Penal Code §118) | Perjury (Scotland) Act 1942 |
| Party vs attorney signature for responses | Affidavit must be sworn by person with knowledge |
| Information-and-belief basis | Affidavit may state source of knowledge (e.g., "from the records kept by [deponent]") |
| Verification of objections | N/A, objections not made in same manner in Scotland |

## Scottish Civil Procedure: Documentary Evidence

- **Reclaiming motion** (appeal) documents include sworn affidavit evidence only where court orders
- **Administration of Justice (Scotland) Act 1972 s.1** - court may order recovery of documents pre- or post-litigation
- **Specification of documents**: detailed list identifying documents sought; not a request for production akin to US
- **Business records**: admissible as evidence under Civil Evidence (Scotland) Act 1988 s.5 (hearsay provisions allow documentary evidence to prove facts)
- **Affidavit evidence**: by court order or specific rule provision; not available as of right

## Scottish Court Systems & Their Rules

- **Sheriff Court**: Ordinary Cause Rules (OCR); Simple Procedure Rules
- **Court of Session**: Rules of the Court of Session 1994 (RCS)
- **Evidence generally**: Civil Evidence (Scotland) Act 1988 - abolished most hearsay rules for civil proceedings
- **Witness statements**: increasingly encouraged in commercial actions and certain complex cases, but not discovery/verification documents

## Practical Notes

- The skill as drafted for US practice has **limited direct applicability** in Scottish civil procedure, no equivalent discovery verification mechanism exists, For Scottish litigation, consider: formal affidavits (where court requires), commission and diligence for document recovery, and specification of documents, For contractual verification/certification: Scots law often uses statutory declarations or formal affidavits, Perjury exposure under Scottish law requires sworn oath; false unsworn statements not per se perjury
