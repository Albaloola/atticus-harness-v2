# Multi-Claimant Wage Claim Pleading (Scotland) [SCOTS]

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a claimant-side Employment Tribunal claim (ET1) or Sheriff Court ordinary cause action for multiple employees seeking recovery of unlawful deductions from wages, unpaid holiday pay, or national minimum wage arrears under the Employment Rights Act 1996 ("ERA 1996") and the Working Time Regulations 1998 ("WTR 1998").

## Prerequisites

1. Named claimant declarations, job duties, pay practices, knowledge of similarly situated colleagues
2. Proposed claimant group definition, job titles, time period, geographic scope (workplaces in Scotland)
3. Evidence of common policy/practice, handbooks, pay records, organisation charts, centralised payroll arrangements
4. ACAS Early Conciliation certificate(s) - required before Employment Tribunal presentation; consider single EC for lead claim
5. Relevant schedule of unlawful deductions, dates, amounts, pattern
6. Local rules reviewed, Employment Tribunal Rules of Procedure 2013 (Schedule 1); Sheriff Court Ordinary Cause Rules if proceeding in civil court

## Quick Start

Frame every section around the **need for a single established common practice** affecting all claimants. [SCOTS: Unlike US FLSA collective actions, there is no US-style "conditional certification" in UK employment law. Multi-claimant claims proceed by: (a) lead claim with multiple claimants listed on a single ET1; (b) group proceedings on a representative basis; or (c) multiple individual claims case-managed together by the Employment Tribunal. The court may order a lead case or a test case to manage overlapping claims.]

## Core Workflow

### 1. Heading and Introduction

- Full Employment Tribunal / court details and case number, Identify lead claimant(s) by name and job title, Define claimant group: role(s), employer(s), date range, Frame violations as systemic (company-wide policies, uniform pay practices)
- [SCOTS: The ET1 does not have a US-style "certification" stage. The tribunal manages multiple claims through case management orders under rr. 29 to 31 of the Employment Tribunal Rules of Procedure 2013.]

### 2. Factual Background

Present facts establishing systematic, uniform violations:

- **Business operations**: respondent's structure, workplaces in Scotland, relevant departments
- **Common policies**: handbook provisions, pay structures, working time recording, classification decisions
- **Specific violations**: unlawful deduction from wages (ERA 1996 s. 13); failure to pay National Minimum Wage (National Minimum Wage Act 1998); failure to pay holiday pay (WTR 1998 regs. 13 to 16); unlawful deduction in respect of working time
- **Uniformity indicators**: standardised job descriptions, centralised pay decisions, uniform pay scales
- **Claimant knowledge**: personal experience AND observation of similarly situated colleagues

Emphasise pattern over isolated incidents. Cite declarations, statements, pay records, and available documents.

### 3. Legal Framework

[SCOTS: No US-style FLSA two-stage framework. Present the correct legal basis for multi-claimant claims:]

- **Unlawful deduction from wages** - ERA 1996 ss. 13 to 27B. Each deduction (or series of deductions) gives rise to a separate complaint. Time limit: 3 months from the date of the last in a series of deductions (ERA 1996 s. 23(2) to (4A); Deduction from Wages (Limitation) Regulations 2014 - 2-year backstop).
- **National Minimum Wage** - National Minimum Wage Act 1998 ss. 17 to 19. Claims for underpayment of NMW.
- **Holiday pay** - WTR 1998 regs. 13 to 16A and reg. 30. The reference period for calculating holiday pay (52 weeks under WTR reg. 13A as amended from 6 April 2020).
- **Working time** - WTR 1998 regs. 4 (maximum weekly working time), 6 (night work), and reg. 30 (complaints to tribunal).

Stress that the Employment Tribunal can hear multiple complaints together through case management, and may order a lead case.

### 4. Argument, Similarly Affected Claimants

Organise around the common practice or policy:

- Same or similar job titles and descriptions, Same or similar pay policies and practices (uniform pay scales or arrangements)
- Similar job duties and working conditions, Deductions/violations stem from common policy, plan, or practice

Apply specific evidence to each factor. Preempt common defences:

- **Different workplaces/locations** - immaterial where centralised payroll policies control compensation
- **Variation in duties** - minor differences do not defeat similarity at case management stage
- **Individual circumstances differ** - common nucleus of claim required, not identical situations

Argue that managing claims together promotes efficient resolution and consistency over numerous individual claims.

### 5. Relief Sought

1. Declaration of unlawful deduction from wages / breach of WTR 1998
2. Order for repayment of unlawfully deducted sums (ERA 1996 s. 24)
3. Order for payment of NMW arrears
4. Order for payment of accrued but untaken holiday pay
5. Interest on arrears (Employment Tribunals (Interest) Order 1990)
6. Claimants to be identified by name on the claim form; additional claimants may be added
7. Case management order for lead case / representative proceedings
8. [SCOTS: No US-style opt-in notice mechanism. The Employment Tribunal manages all named claimants]

### 6. Conclusion

Concise restatement: common pay practice or policy has caused a series of unlawful deductions affecting all claimants; the claims are efficiently managed together.

### 7. Signature Block

- Claimant's solicitor or representative name, firm, address, reference, Statement of truth (ET1 form includes mandatory declaration)
- Date

- [SCOTS: In the Sheriff Court: pursuer's solicitor name, firm, address; signed initial writ with pleas-in-law.]

## Pitfalls and Checks

- **No US-style conditional certification** - UK/Scottish law does not have a two-stage FLSA certification model. Multi-claimant claims are managed through Employment Tribunal case management or representative proceedings. [SCOTS: Do not use FLSA terminology.]
- **Time limits are strict** - 3 months from the last in a series of deductions (ERA 1996 s. 23). The 2-year backstop applies per the Deduction from Wages (Limitation) Regulations 2014. NMW claims: within 6 years (civil proceedings).
- **ACAS Early Conciliation** - Compulsory before presenting an ET1. Obtain an EC certificate for the lead claimant(s), then extend to others.
- **Compensation cap** - No cap on unlawful deduction claims (unlike unfair dismissal). WTR 1998 claims: up to 2 years' back pay of holiday pay.
- **Wrongful jurisdiction** - Employment Tribunal is the proper forum for most deduction and working time claims. Sheriff Court has concurrent jurisdiction for breach of contract / delict claims but ET is usually preferred.
- **Employment status** - Verify each claimant's employment status (worker vs. employee) - WTR 1998 covers "workers" (broader category); NMW also covers workers; ERA 1996 s. 13 covers "workers" (s. 27(1)).
- **Institute trade union representation** - Scottish trade unions frequently support multiple claims; consider union coordination.
- **Legal aid** - Limited availability for Employment Tribunal in Scotland, check the Scottish Legal Aid Board (SLAB) guidelines.
- **Expenses** - Employment Tribunal in Scotland operates on a "each party bears own costs" basis (limited costs orders available for unreasonable conduct). In Sheriff Court, judicial expenses follow success.
- **Wrongful dismissal not included** - This is a wage claim skill only. Do not include unfair dismissal or discrimination claims.

This skill has been adapted from a US FLSA Motion for Conditional Certification (29 U.S.C. § 216(b) collective action) to a UK/Scottish multi-claimant wage claim pleading.

**Key adaptations:**
- Replaced FLSA § 216(b) collective action with ERA 1996 ss. 13 to 27B (unlawful deduction from wages) and WTR 1998.
- Replaced US two-stage (conditional certification / decertification) with UK Employment Tribunal case management.
- Replaced "opt-in" collective action with ET1 multi-claimant listing and case management orders (ET Rules r. 29 to 31).
- Replaced federal court jurisdiction with Employment Tribunal (Scotland) jurisdiction.
- Replaced US circuit standards (Hoffmann-La Roche, Swales) with UK case law on series of deductions (e.g. Bear Scotland; Scottish cases on holiday pay).
- Replaced US class action notice plan with ACAS Early Conciliation requirement.
- Replaced equitable tolling with strict 3-month time limit and 2-year backstop.
- Replaced FLSA compensatory damages with statutory repayment orders and interest.
- Replaced "similarly situated" standard with "common practice/policy" showing.
- Replaced US-style arguments about judicial efficiency with Employment Tribunal objectives (overriding objective r. 2 ET Rules).
- Replaced Bluebook citation with UK neutral citation and law report references.
- Replaced US fee-shifting (FLSA liquidated damages + attorney fees) with limited Employment Tribunal costs / expenses rules.
- Added Scottish Legal Aid Board reference.
- Preserved the drafting methodology and analytical framework.
