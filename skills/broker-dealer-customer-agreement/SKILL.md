# Client Agreement (FCA Authorised Firm)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts the master contractual agreement between an FCA-authorised investment firm and its clients, covering all regulatory requirements under FSMA 2000, FCA Handbook (COBS, CASS, SYSC, MIFIDPRU), and UK AML framework.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

- **Firm registration** - FCA authorisation number, PRA authorisation (if dual-regulated), Part 4A permissions, SMCR regime
- **Business model** - execution-only vs. advisory/discretionary, order routing, client categorisation (eligible counterparty, professional, retail per COBS 3)
- **Product catalog** - all offered investment types (equities, bonds, derivatives, structured products, ETFs, CFDs, spread bets, cryptoassets)
- **Fee schedule** - commissions, account fees, execution charges, custody costs, FX conversion
- **Existing docs** - current terms of business, client agreements, execution policy (RTS 28), conflicts policy, best execution arrangements
- **Regulatory history** - FCA visits, threat notices, Skilled Person reviews, enforcement actions

## Quick Start

1. Confirm firm's business model and permissions (execution-only, advisory, discretionary, or dual-regulated)
2. Categorise each client per COBS 3 (retail, professional, eligible counterparty) - rights vary by category
3. Identify all offered products and applicable conduct rules
4. Draft agreement sections per the structure below
5. Tailor provisions to firm's actual services, omit inapplicable sections
6. Verify all statutory/rule citations against current FCA Handbook and FSMA
7. Ensure risk warnings use prominent formatting (bold/caps, separate acknowledgement)
8. Cross-check consistency with client categorisation, best execution policy, conflicts policy, and cost disclosures

## Agreement Sections

### 1. Party Identification & Client Categorisation

- Firm identification: legal name, registered office, FCA registration number (FRN), company number, Client identification per MLR 2017: name, address, DOB, nationality, proof of identity/address, source of funds/wealth, Entity clients: beneficial owners, directors, PSC register (PSC register per Companies Act 2006)
- Client categorisation per COBS 3: eligible counterparty, professional client, retail client (determines level of regulatory protection)
- Firm reserved rights: reject applications, restrict activities, close accounts, vary categorisation with client consent

**[SCOTS: Note]** For firms operating in Scotland, refer to Scottish Partnerships (Register of People with Significant Control) Regulations 2017 for PSC registers on Scottish partnerships and LLPs.

### 2. Service Relationship & Obligations

- Capacity disclosure: execution-only, advisory (restricted/independent), discretionary portfolio management, or combined model, COBS 2 - client's best interests rule (overarching duty)
- COBS 6 - information about the firm and services (status disclosure)
- COBS 9 - suitability (advisory/discretionary) and appropriateness (non-advised sales) assessments, Product-specific rules: COBS 18 (non-MiFID), COBS 21 (structured products), COBS 22 (options, futures, CFDs), MIFIDPRU (prudential requirements)
- UK MAR, market abuse controls and surveillance, Consumer Duty (PRIN 2A) - fair value, price and value assessments, consumer support

### 3. Client Instruction & Order Handling

- Order channels with authentication requirements, Best execution per COBS 11 (MIFID and UK framework)
- Execution policy disclosure per COBS 11 Annex 1 (RTS 28 top-five execution venues)
- Order type risks: market (no price guarantee), limit (partial/no fill), stop (becomes market), GTC (expiry rules)
- Aggregation and allocation of client orders per COBS 11.3
- Discretionary authority limits if applicable

### 4. Cost & Compensation Disclosure

- All charges, commissions, mark-ups/downs: exact amounts or tariff reference, Cost of financial services and products, Consumer Duty annual cost disclosures, COBS 6.1A and COBS 13 - cost and charges breakdown pre- and post-sale, Bundled/unbundled research costs (MIFID II research unbundling)
- Currency conversion, custody, and settlement costs, Cross-reference with KID/KIID (PRIIPs Regulation / UCITS Directive) for packaged products

### 5. Client Money & Assets (CASS)

- **Regulatory framework:** CASS Rules (CASS 1 to 10)
- Client money segregation: Section 137B FSMA, CASS 7 - statutory trust over client money, daily reconciliation, Client assets: CASS 7A (title transfer collateral arrangements)
- CASS auditor requirement for firms holding client money/assets, FSCS protection: £85,000 per person per firm; explain coverage limits, Financial Ombudsman Service access for retail clients
- **Bold risk warnings:** CASS shortfall risk in insolvency of firm; FSCS limits may not cover total holdings

### 6. Risk Disclosures & Acknowledgements

- Product-specific risks: market, liquidity, equity, derivatives (unlimited liability on short options/CFDs), fixed income, structured products, cryptoassets, CISOCS, Contract for Differences (CFDs): CFD leverage risk warnings (COBS 22), retail leverage limits, negative balance protection per FCA PS19/23
- Required acknowledgements (signature/initial): understood terms, received KID/KIID/supplementary documents, total loss possible, sole responsibility for decisions in execution-only

### 7. Corporate Actions & Account Administration

- Dividends, capital events, proxy voting, stock lending, Confirmations: required per COBS 16 (contract notes)
- Periodic statements: quarterly minimum per COBS 16
- Error reporting: clients must review statements and report discrepancies within stated timeframe, Reporting under UK MAR, suspicious transaction and order reports (STORs)

### 8. Data Protection & Privacy

- Draft per UK GDPR, Data Protection Act 2018, and FCA FCG guidance, Cover: lawful basis for processing, data categories, sharing with third parties (regulators, professional advisers, service providers)
- Privacy notice cross-reference, Data retention schedules, Breach notification procedures (ICO mandatory reporting)

### 9. Amendment, Assignment & Termination

- Amendments: 30 days' notice; continued use = acceptance; regulatory changes may take immediate effect, Assignment: client consent required unless to an authorised person; firm may assign in business transfers (TUPE considerations)
- Termination: client-initiated (settle positions, transfer assets, ACATS equivalent, CREST/ISA transfer) and firm-initiated (regulatory breach, AML concerns, death)
- Records retained per FCA SYSC 3C / DISP rules (minimum 5 years)

### 10. Dispute Resolution

- Firm's internal complaints process (DISP 1): three business days to acknowledge, eight weeks to final response, Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS): eligible complainants (DISP 2), referral right, binding on firm, not on client, FSCS as final protection layer, Court jurisdiction: courts of England and Wales or Scotland (specify)
- Arbitration is NOT standard, FOS provides the primary consumer redress mechanism

**[SCOTS: Note]** For Scottish clients, specify jurisdiction of the Scottish courts. Complaints can be escalated to the Financial Ombudsman Service which covers the whole UK. Arbitration clauses are less common in UK retail client agreements than US equivalents; the FOS is the primary route.

### 11. Execution & Acknowledgements

- Client representations: legal capacity, accurate information, understands risk, Entity-specific: authorised signatory details, constitutional compliance, supporting documents, Signature requirements by client type, Electronic signature compliance per eIDAS / Electronic Communications Act, Entire agreement clause with incorporated documents, Non-waiver provision

## Pitfalls

- **Omitting client categorisation** - retail clients have the highest level of protection; failing to categorise properly is a regulatory breach
- **[SCOTS: Note] Inconsistent court jurisdiction** - if the firm operates in Scotland, ensure the jurisdiction clause specifies Scottish courts; English-only clauses may be challenged as unfair under CRA 2015
- **Weak risk warnings** - CFD and derivative loss warnings must be prominent (COBS 22); retail CFD leverage restrictions must be stated
- **Inconsistent documents** - agreement must align with client categorisation, best execution policy, conflicts policy, and Consumer Duty outcomes
- **Unverified citations** - always verify FCA Handbook provisions against the current version
- **CASS breach is serious** - client money and asset rules carry severe consequences including FCA enforcement, Section 166 Skilled Person review, and possible suspension
- **Consumer Duty impact** - fair value assessments, price and value reviews, and consumer support outcomes must be embedded
- **Omit unused provisions** - do not include services the firm does not hold permission for
- **Retail comprehensibility** - agreement must be clear, fair and not misleading (COBS 4), consistent with Consumer Duty expectations

**Changes made:**
- Replaced SEC/FINRA with FCA/PRA regulatory framework, Replaced US securities laws (Reg BI, Securities Exchange Act) with FSMA 2000, FCA Handbook (COBS, CASS, SYSC, MIFIDPRU)

- Replaced FINRA arbitration with UK FOS complaints route and court jurisdiction, Replaced E-SIGN Act with eIDAS / Electronic Communications Act, Replaced Federal Arbitration Act with DISP rules for FOS, Replaced USD references with GBP, Added Consumer Duty (PRIN 2A) - a uniquely UK concept, Added FSCS protection (£85,000 limit) - no US equivalent, Added [SCOTS: Note] flags for Scottish-specific issues (PSC register, court jurisdiction, unfair contract terms under CRA 2015)

**Key Scottish/UK considerations:**
- FCA is the lead regulator for investment firms; PRA co-regulates for dual-regulated firms (e.g., banks)
- FSMA 2000 s.19 prohibits unauthorised regulated activities, Client categorisation is a three-tier system (retail, professional, eligible counterparty) - more structured than US approach, No mandatory arbitration, FOS is the primary redress mechanism for retail clients, CASS rules provide stronger asset protection than US segregation rules, Consumer Duty (2023) imposes a new overarching duty of care, Scottish courts apply their own rules of jurisdiction; English-only jurisdiction clauses may be unfair under Consumer Rights Act 2015

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
