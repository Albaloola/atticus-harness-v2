# IP Litigation Witness Preparation Supplement (Scotland/UK Adaptation)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Adds IP-specific examination frameworks to @witness-preparation. Covers four case types, patent, trade mark, copyright, trade secret, with witness-specific question lines and damages theory, adapted for Scottish civil procedure.

[SCOTS: Note] - Scotland/UK does not have US-style depositions. Scottish civil procedure uses:
- **Precognitions** (written witness statements, not sworn, not disclosure)
- **Commission and Diligence** (recovery of documents/hostile-witness examination, Scots equivalent of discovery)
- **Proof** (civil trial, oral evidence in court)
- **Affidavits** (sworn written evidence for certain motions)
- **Specification of documents** (order for recovery of specified documents)
- The deposition framework below is adapted for precognition/pre-proof preparation strategy, not live deposition examination.

## Prerequisites

- Relevant patents, registrations, or trade secret identification statements, Prosecution history (patent) and claim construction positions, Prior art references at issue, Accused product/process technical documentation, Licensing history and comparable licences, Expert reports (if instructed)
- Confidentiality agreements / NDAs (trade secret)

## Case Type Reference

| Case Type | Key Issues | Primary Witnesses |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Patent** | Claim construction, infringement (literal/equivalents under *Improver*/*Actavis*), validity (novelty, inventive step, sufficiency), reasonable royalty, lost profits, employee compensation | Inventor, R&D/engineering, licensing/business, technical expert, damages expert |
| **Trade Mark** | Distinctiveness, priority of use, likelihood of confusion (s.10 TMA 1994) or passing off, honest concurrent use, damages | Mark owner, marketing, consumer survey expert, damages expert |
| **Copyright** | Ownership, originality, access/copying, substantial similarity, fair dealing (not fair use) | Author/creator, access witnesses, similarity expert, damages expert |
| **Trade Secret** | Existence, reasonable secrecy measures, misappropriation (breach of confidence), damages | Developer, accused misappropriator, security witnesses, damages expert |

## Patent Witness Preparation Frameworks

### Inventor

- **Conception/reduction to practice**: When conceived, what problem solved, corroborating records (contemporary lab notebooks or technical notes), who was told, prototyping/testing timeline
- **Prior art knowledge**: Awareness at time of invention, searches conducted, how invention differs from specific references - [SCOTS: Note] no inequitable conduct doctrine under UK law, but bears on inventorship and employee compensation
- **Claims/prosecution**: Understanding of claim 1, meaning of disputed terms, involvement in prosecution, reasons for amendments
- **Inventor compensation**: s.40 Patents Act 1977 - for employee inventions of outstanding benefit to employer

### Accused Infringer Technical Witness

- **Product/process**: How accused product works step-by-step, development timeline and team
- **Design choices**: Alternatives considered, reasons for chosen approach, awareness of patent, design-around efforts
- **Claim element mapping**: Whether product has each claim element, how it performs each function
- **Non-infringement**: Which limitation is missing, how product differs from claims
- **Common general knowledge**: What was CGK at the priority date

### Licensing / Damages Witness

- **Licensing history**: Prior licences (parties, terms, royalty rates), negotiation process, comparable licences
- **Wrotham Park / hypothetical negotiation**: *General Tire v Firestone* factors for reasonable royalty; most significant factors, proposed royalty rate and base, non-infringing alternatives and market availability
- **Lost profits but-for infringement**: Manufacturing capacity, market share but-for infringement, patented feature as demand driver, apportionment methodology
- **[SCOTS: Note]** - No enhanced damages or treble damages in UK patent law; no presumption of validity (*Kirin-Amgen*)

### Patent Expert Witness Preparation

Apply @expert-witness preparatory framework, then add:

**Technical expert**: Claim term construction with prosecution history support; element-by-element infringement walkthrough; physical examination and source code review (forensic imaging may be ordered); prior art disclosure analysis; PHOSITA motivation to combine references; common general knowledge assessment

**Damages expert**: Reasonable royalty rate and methodology (*General Tire* factors applied); which factors applied and how; comparable licence analysis; royalty base (appropriate royalty-bearing product); "but for" world for lost profits; apportionment to patented feature

## Trade Mark Witness Preparation Frameworks

### Mark Owner

- Creation timeline, selection rationale, first use in commerce with documentation (UK-wide or Scotland-specific)
- Inherent distinctiveness vs. acquired secondary meaning (or reputation for s.10(3)); supporting advertising, Awareness of defendant's mark, confusion incidents, harm (lost sales, goodwill, costs)
- [SCOTS: Note] - Scottish mark owners: establish use in Scotland as part of UK-wide rights

### Accused Infringer

- Adoption timeline, decision-maker, whether trade mark search was conducted (UKIPO register search)
- Awareness of pursuer's (plaintiff's) mark, legal advice received, Intent to trade on goodwill or cause confusion, relevant for passing off, Knowledge of actual confusion incidents (misdirected orders, customer inquiries)
- Own name defence (s.11(2) TMA 1994) or honest concurrent use

## Trade Secret (Breach of Confidence) Witness Preparation Frameworks

### Owner / Developer

- Specific description of trade secret/confidential information and what makes it confidential, Security measures: physical (locks, access control), electronic (passwords, encryption, IT policies), contractual (NDAs, employment agreements, restrictive covenants, need-to-know)
- Value: development investment, independent replication cost, Misappropriation theory: how defendant acquired/used it, timeline

### Accused Misappropriator

- Relationship with pursuer (plaintiff), confidentiality agreements signed, Access to claimed information, how obtained, Independent development evidence, Knowledge that information was confidential, Disclosure to others, relationship between own product and alleged secret/confidential information

## [SCOTS: Note] Key Procedural Differences

| US Concept | Scotland/UK Equivalent |
|------------|----------------------|
| Deposition (oral exam pre-trial) | Precognition (unsworn witness statement); Commission and Diligence for hostile witness |
| Discovery (document production) | Specification of documents / Commission and Diligence (court order to recover documents) |
| Summary judgment | Summary decree (Scots civil procedure) |
| Trial | Proof (Court of Session) / Evidential Hearing (Sheriff Court) |
| Injunction (TRO / preliminary) | Interim interdict; Perpetual interdict |
| Appeal | Reclaiming motion (Inner House, Court of Session) |
| Jury trial (civil) | Rare in Scottish civil IP cases; judge alone |
| Expert discovery | Expert reports exchanged; single joint expert possible |
| Costs/attorneys' fees | Judicial expenses, loser pays taxed costs |

## Pitfalls

- **Claim construction first** (patent): Establish witness's understanding of disputed terms before infringement/validity questions, inconsistencies become impeachment material
- **No US-style discovery**: Scotland uses specification of documents procedure, document recovery is court-supervised, not self-executing
- **Prosecution history**: Relevant to claim interpretation under *Kirin-Amgen* principles, not estoppel per se
- **Trade secret/breach of confidence specificity**: Ensure confidential information is identified with reasonable particularity in pleadings
- **Willfulness**: Relevant to account of profits/discretionary remedies but no enhanced damages
- **Apportionment**: Required when patented feature is not the entire basis for demand, press experts on methodology
- **Single joint expert**: Scottish courts may order a single expert, be prepared for this even in complex patent cases
- **No presumption of validity**: UK patents are presumed valid once granted but can be challenged by defensive attack in infringement proceedings

## Key Authorities

- Patents Act 1977 (as amended)
- Trade Marks Act 1994
- Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988
- *Actavis v Eli Lilly* [2017] UKSC 48 - reformulated equivalents test
- *Kirin-Amgen v Hoechst* [2004] UKHL 46 - claim construction
- *Improver v Remington* [1990] FSR 181 - pre-Actavis equivalents test
- *General Tire v Firestone* [1975] 1 WLR 819 - reasonable royalty factors
- *Reckitt & Colman v Borden* [1990] 1 WLR 491 - passing off trinity
- *Coco v A.N. Clark* [1969] RPC 41 - breach of confidence requirements

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Adapted for:** Scotland and UK law
**Changes made:**
- Replaced US deposition discovery with Scottish precognition (witness statement) + Commission and Diligence framework, Replaced US statutes: Patent Act (35 USC) → Patents Act 1977; Lanham Act (15 USC) → Trade Marks Act 1994; Copyright Act (17 USC) → CDPA 1988; DTSA → breach of confidence (common law + Human Rights Act)
- Replaced US case law: *Georgia-Pacific* → *General Tire v Firestone*; *Markman* → *Kirin-Amgen*; *Panduit* → but-for factual causation, Replaced DOE (doctrine of equivalents) with UK *Actavis* reformulated approach (not doctrine per se)
- Replaced "summarly judgment" with "summary decree"
- Replaced "injunction/TRO" with "interdict" (interim/perpetual, Scots term)
- Replaced "trial" with "proof" (civil) in Scottish context, Replaced "appeal" with "reclaiming motion"
- Replaced "fair use" with "fair dealing" (CDPA 1988 specific categories)
- Removed inequitable conduct concept (no UK equivalent)
- Removed enhanced/punitive/treble damages (not available in UK patent law)
- Added employee inventor compensation (s.40 Patents Act 1977)
- Added single joint expert possibility (Scottish courts)
- Updated court references: Court of Session (Outer House / Inner House) and Sheriff Court, Added COPFS for criminal IP matters where relevant, Updated pre-action protocol references, Replaced "plaintiff/defendant" with "pursuer/defender" (Scots court terminology)

**Key Scottish/UK considerations:**
- No US-style depositions exist in Scotland, precognition and Commission and Diligence are the key tools, Patent validity is not presumed (defensive attack in infringement proceedings)
- No equitable defences per se, but abuse of rights (Scots / civil law concept) may apply, Employee inventor compensation under s.40 Patents Act 1977 applies to UK patents
- *Actavis* reformulated test for equivalents is UK-specific, Fair dealing categories are narrower than US fair use, No enhanced damages for wilful infringement in UK patent law, Judicial expenses (costs) follow event; taxed by Auditor of the Court of Session, COPFS prosecutes criminal IP offences (counterfeiting, copyright piracy) in Scotland, Scottish IP cases are heard in the Court of Session (Outer House, exclusive for patents and UK/EU registered IP)

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
