[SCOTS: Note] In Scottish civil procedure, the initiating document is an **Initial Writ** (Sheriff Court Ordinary Cause) or **Summons** (Court of Session), not a "complaint." The pursuer replaces "plaintiff" and the defender replaces "defendant." Scottish property contracts are formed through **missives** (exchange of letters), not a single purchase agreement. See the Scotland/UK Adaptation section below for full terminology mapping.

Drafts an Initial Writ asserting breach of a heritable property purchase agreement with appropriate Scottish pleading structure, breach elements, damages analysis, and remedy-specific allegations.

## Prerequisites

1. **Concluded missives** - the exchange of formal letters forming the binding contract, plus any qualifications, missives letters, and ancillary agreements.
2. **Property identification** - postal address, title number (Land Register or Sasines), description.
3. **Party information** - full legal names, entity types, registered addresses (Companies House if applicable).
4. **Performance timeline** - deposit, date of entry, conditions precedent, surveys, mortgage offer, settlement milestones.
5. **Breach evidence** - letter of default, time-limit notices, refusal or failure to perform, key communications.
6. **Damages support** - contract price, market value (loss), carrying costs, replacement transaction costs, mitigation efforts.
7. **Jurisdiction and venue** - Sheriff Court territorial jurisdiction (property location or defender's residence) or Court of Session.
8. **Requested remedies** - damages, specific implement (specific performance), interdict, judicial expenses, interest.

If any prerequisite is missing, pause and ask, do not assume or fill gaps.

## Output Structure

### Step 1 - Instance (Court and Parties)

Draft the instance with:
- Sheriff Court name (e.g., "SHERIFFDOM OF GLASGOW AND STRATHKELVIN AT GLASGOW")
- Or Court of Session ("OUTER HOUSE, COURT OF SESSION")
- Pursuer(s): full name, address; solicitor name and firm, Defender(s): full name, address, Case number placeholder

### Step 2 - Condescendence (Factual Allegations)

Draft numbered articles of condescendence covering:

| Article | Content |
|---|---|
| 1 | Parties and their capacities |
| 2 | The property, address and title number |
| 3 | The missives, negotiation, conclusion, material terms (date of entry, price, conditions) |
| 4 | Pursuer's performance, deposit paid, conditions satisfied, readiness and willingness to settle |
| 5 | Defender's breach, specific obligation breached, date of breach, refusal or failure to perform |
| 6 | Notice and default, time-limit notices served, failure to remedy |
| 7 | Loss, injury, and damage, financial consequences of the breach |

### Step 3 - Pleas-in-Law (Legal Basis)

Plead each element with supporting allegations:

| Plea | Allegations Required |
|---|---|
| 1. The defender being in breach of the concluded missives, is liable to the pursuer in damages | Breach of contract under Scots law |
| 2. The pursuer being willing and able to implement the missives, is entitled to decree of specific implement | Real property is unique; legal remedy inadequate |
| 3. The defender having failed to settle on the date of entry, is liable for the pursuer's consequential losses | Foreseeable losses flowing from breach |
| 4. Judicial expenses should follow success | "Loser pays" principle |

### Step 4 - Crave (Prayer for Relief)

Number each crave:

```
CRAVE
The pursuer craves the court to:

1. Decree for payment of £[amount] as [first conclusion, e.g., damages for breach of contract].

2. Or alternatively, decree for specific implement of the missives dated [date] between the pursuer and the defender, ordaining the defender to implement the missives, and in particular to [specific obligations].

3. Decree for payment of interest on said sums at the judicial rate from [date of citation or breach] until payment.

4. Decree for expenses.

5. Further orders as the court sees fit.
```

`[VERIFY: Local court rules for crave format.]`

### Step 5 - Damages

| Category | Measure | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Loss of bargain | Contract price vs. market value at date of breach | Appraisal, listing data |
| Consequential | Foreseeable losses (financing costs, relocation, additional LBTT, temporary accommodation) | Invoices, statements |
| Incidental | Transactional costs, legal fees, survey costs | Receipts |
| Reliance | Out-of-pocket expenditures in reliance on contract | Financial records |
| Mitigation | Actions taken to reduce loss; offset amounts | Timeline, records |

### Step 6 - Signing and Service

- **Signing** - by solicitor or party; in Ordinary Cause, Initial Writ must be signed by the pursuer's solicitor (or party if acting in person)
- **Service** - by sheriff officer (Scottish equivalent of process server)
- **No civil cover sheet** equivalent in Scotland

## Guidelines

- Identify property with both postal address and Land Register title number, Quote or attach only the missive letters/terms necessary to establish the concluded contract and the breach, Scottish pleading is concise, avoid over-pleading (unlike US fact pleading)
- Do not claim punitive or exemplary damages, not available in Scots law, Do not include settlement communications (without prejudice rule)
- Include interest craves at the judicial rate, Output is draft work product for solicitor review, not a filing-ready document, Mark any unconfirmed statutory reference with `[VERIFY]`

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from a US civil complaint for breach of real estate purchase agreement to a Scottish Ordinary Cause Initial Writ.

### Terminology Changes

| US Term | Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Complaint | Initial Writ (Sheriff Court) / Summons (Court of Session) |
| Plaintiff | Pursuer |
| Defendant | Defender |
| Answer / Defence | Defences / Notice of Intention to Defend |
| Caption | Instance |
| Factual allegations | Condescendence |
| Causes of action | Pleas-in-Law |
| Prayer for relief | Crave |
| Purchase agreement / P&S | Missives (exchange of letters) |
| Specific performance | Specific implement |
| Preliminary injunction/ TRO | Interim interdict |
| Lis pendens | Notice of Pending Action |
| Subject matter jurisdiction | Competency / Venue (Sheriff Court territorial jurisdiction) |
| Compensatory damages | Damages (delictual + contractual) |
| Punitive / exemplary damages | Not available in Scots law |
| Discovery request | Specification of documents / Commission and Diligence |
| Civil cover sheet | No equivalent |
| Judge | Sheriff (Sheriff Court) / Lord Ordinary (Court of Session) |

### Key Differences

- **Scottish pleading is concise** - less detailed than US notice pleading; numbered articles of condescendence
- **Specific implement** is more readily available in Scotland than specific performance in US, property is considered unique
- **Missives** form the contract, not a single standard-form purchase agreement
- **No punitive damages** in Scots contract law
- **"Loser pays" expenses** - the unsuccessful party typically pays judicial expenses
- **No jury trial** for contract actions in Scotland, civil jury only in Court of Session for specific actions
- **Prescription**: Breach of contract: 5 years (s.6 Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973)
- **Sheriff officer** serves court documents (not US marshal/process server)
- **No diversity jurisdiction** - Scottish courts determine jurisdiction by territorial location or value
