# [SCOTS] Consulting Services Agreement (Scotland/UK Adaptation)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a consulting services agreement under Scots law, adapted for the UK regulatory environment, IR35 compliance, and Scottish legal practice.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

1. **Parties** - legal names, entity types (Ltd, PLC, LLP, partnership, sole trader), registered addresses, Companies House numbers (if applicable), signatories
2. **Scope** - deliverables, milestones, deadlines, exclusions, termination provisions
3. **Compensation** - fee type (hourly / fixed / retainer / hybrid), rates, invoicing cadence, expense policy
4. **Term** - start date, duration, renewal preferences
5. **Governing law** - Scots law (Scottish courts); specify Edinburgh or Glasgow sheriff court or Court of Session jurisdiction
6. **IR35 status** - Confirm whether the engagement is inside or outside IR35, and whether the client is public or private sector (off-payroll rules differ)

## Required Sections

Draft each section with the following key content:

**Parties** - Full legal names, entity type (Ltd/PLC/LLP/sole trader), company registration number (if applicable), registered office, notice addresses.

**Scope of Services** - Measurable deliverables, milestones, deadlines, performance standards, explicit exclusions. Reference attached SOW/exhibits.

**Compensation & Payment** - Fee structure, rate schedule, invoicing frequency, payment terms, expense reimbursement with documentation requirements, late-payment interest (Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998), suspension-of-services trigger.

**Term & Termination** - Effective date, fixed vs. rolling, notice period (30 days usual), termination for cause with cure period, IP and work product disposition on early termination.

> [SCOTS: Note] Under Scots law, the common law implies a right to terminate for material breach. The agreement should make express provision to avoid uncertainty. Consider including an express right to terminate for convenience as well, since Scots law does not imply this.

**Independent Contractor / IR35 Status** - Consultant controls manner and means of performance; responsible for own tax, NIC, VAT, professional licences, and insurance. Right of substitution clause is strongly recommended to evidence outside-IR35 status. Subcontracting subject to client approval. Include express IR35 status confirmation: inside or outside.

> [SCOTS: IR35 Note] IR35 (off-payroll working rules) is the key tax-status concept for the UK. Unlike US IC classification (IRS common-law test / DOL economic reality test / state ABC tests), IR35 focuses on three factors: (1) control, (2) substitution, and (3) mutuality of obligation. If the consultant provides services through a personal service company (PSC), the off-payroll rules may apply, especially in the public sector and medium/large private sector clients. Key differences from US:
> - IR35 applies to tax and NIC, not employment rights generally
> - HMRC CEST tool provides a statutory safe harbour
> - No state-by-state variation (UK-wide legislation)
> - IR35 status may differ from employment status for employment rights purposes

**Confidentiality** - Define confidential information (strategies, financials, customer data, trade secrets, proprietary methodologies). Standard exceptions: public domain, independently developed, already known, compelled disclosure (under UK law). Specify post-termination survival period (2-5 years typical; perpetual for trade secrets).

**IP & Work Product** - Client owns deliverables by express assignment in writing. Consultant retains pre-existing IP and general methodologies and know-how. Client receives licence to background IP needed to use the deliverables. Irrevocable, perpetual, royalty-free licence. Section 11: Portfolio use subject to client consent (unless anonymised).

> [SCOTS: Note] Unlike the US work-made-for-hire doctrine (17 U.S.C. § 101), UK law has no automatic work-made-for-hire concept for consultants. IP assignment must be **express and in writing** under the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988, s. 90(3). The agreement must contain a clear assignment clause, reliance on a "work for hire" or "shall vest" clause alone may be insufficient in Scotland/UK.

**Representations & Warranties** - Mutual: authority to contract, no conflicting obligations. Consultant: professional performance, no third-party IP infringement, regulatory compliance. No implied warranties beyond those expressly stated.

**Indemnification** - Reciprocal for breach, negligence, wilful misconduct, IP infringement. Include procedure: prompt notice, cooperation, control of defence/settlement. Under Scots law, be careful with the scope of indemnities, Scots law generally favours narrower interpretation of indemnity clauses than English law.

**Limitation of Liability** - Cap aggregate liability (fees paid in prior 6-12 months or negotiated amount). Exclude consequential/indirect losses. Carve out: confidentiality breach, IP infringement, indemnification obligations, fraud, death/personal injury caused by negligence.

> [SCOTS: Note] Under Scots law, the reasonableness test under the Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977 (UCTA) applies to limitation and exclusion clauses in business contracts. A liability cap that is too low or a blanket exclusion of all indirect losses may be challenged as unreasonable. Document the commercial rationale for the cap.

**Insurance** - Consultant maintains: Professional Indemnity (PII) - mandatory for many professions; Public Liability; Employers' Liability (if consultant has employees); Cyber Liability (if handling personal or sensitive data). Specify minimum cover levels (£1m-£5m typical). Client as interested party / notification rights.

**Governing Law & Disputes** - 
- Governing law: Scots law (specify) - not English law unless both parties agree, Courts: Scottish courts (sheriff court for claims up to £100k; Court of Session for higher value, or by agreement)
- Optional: mediation before litigation (Scottish mediation services available)
- No equivalent of "prevailing party attorneys' fees" - under Scots law, the general rule is that expenses follow success, meaning the losing party pays the winner's costs (but not in full; taxed by the auditor of court)
- Consider an adjudication / expert determination clause for disputes on technical matters

> [SCOTS: Note] Unlike US practice, there is no general principle of "prevailing party attorneys' fees" in Scotland. The court awards expenses (costs) on a party-party basis, which typically covers only a portion of actual legal fees. If full-indemnity costs are wanted, the agreement must be explicit. Also note that Scotland does not have jury trials for civil commercial disputes, cases are heard by a judge (sheriff or Lord Ordinary) alone.

**General Provisions** - Severability (preserve the remainder), entire agreement (put entire agreement clauses, with care re misrepresentation), written amendments, no assignment without consent (good leasone personal contracts), notice procedures, no waiver, force majeure, counterparts.

**Signature Blocks** - Authorised signatory name, title, date, company seal (optional); confirm signatory authority under the Companies Act 2006 (director authority / board resolution).

## [SCOTS] Pitfalls (UK/Scotland-Specific)

- **IR35 misclassification** - Highest exposure for public sector and medium/large private sector clients (off-payroll rules apply since April 2021 for medium/large private sector). Key indicators: control, substitution, mutuality of obligation. Do not rely solely on contractual labels. [VERIFY via HMRC CEST tool or professional advice]
- **No automatic work-made-for-hire** - UK IP assignment must be **express and in writing** (CDPA 1988 s. 90(3)). Do not rely on US-style "work made for hire" language.
- **Late Payment Act** - The Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 applies to B2B contracts. Statutory interest is 8% above Bank of England base rate. Parties cannot contract out if the contract is "unfair" under the Act. Consider whether to apply the Act or negotiate alternative rates.
- **VAT** - If the consultant is VAT-registered, the agreement must state whether fees are exclusive of VAT. Reverse-charge rules may apply for cross-border UK services.
- **Restrictive covenants** - Non-compete and non-solicitation clauses are enforceable under Scots law but subject to the common law restraint of trade doctrine. Must protect a legitimate business interest and be no wider than necessary. Non-competes tend to be more difficult to enforce than non-solicitation. There is no US-style state-level prohibition (cf. California).
- **UCTA reasonableness** - Exclusion/limitation clauses in standard terms are subject to the reasonableness test under Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977. Cap must be proportionate to the contract value and the consultant's insurance cover.
- **Employment rights** - IR35 status for tax purposes does not necessarily determine employment status for employment rights (unfair dismissal, holiday pay, etc.). The agreement should be clear that the consultant is not an employee or worker for employment law purposes.
- **Holiday and sick pay** - Unlike US independent contractor agreements, UK law gives certain categories of workers (not employees) rights to holiday pay. Ensure the agreement explicitly states there is no entitlement.
- **Data protection** - If the consultant processes personal data, the agreement must comply with the UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018. Include data processing schedule, DPA provisions, and specify data controller/processor roles.
- **Professional Negligence** - A professionally unqualified consultant cannot be held to the standard of a qualified professional. Ensure the scope accurately reflects the consultant's qualifications and the services to be provided.
- **Scottish courts jurisdiction** - Specify the court (Edinburgh Sheriff Court, Glasgow Sheriff Court, or Court of Session). For cross-border UK parties, consider practical enforcement and specify which sheriffdom.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
