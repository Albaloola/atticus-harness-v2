## Scotland-Specific

- **Rules of the Court of Session (RCS) Chapter 42A** - Expert Witnesses
- **Ordinary Cause Rules (OCR) Chapter 9A** - Expert Witnesses (Sheriff Court)
- **Simple Procedure Rules** - Expert witnesses in claims up to £5,000
- **Commission procedure**: Commission to take evidence is court-ordered, not automatic (RCS Ch. 42)
- **Kennedy v Cordia (Services) LLP [2016] UKSC 6** - Leading Scottish authority on expert evidence admissibility
- **Civil Evidence (Scotland) Act 1988** - Hearsay and expert evidence provisions
- **Expert Witness Institute (EWI)** - Code of Practice and guidance for experts in Scotland/UK
- **Academy of Experts** - Professional body for expert witnesses

## England & Wales-Specific

- CPR Part 35 - Experts and Assessors, Practice Direction 35 - Expert Evidence
- *The Ikarian Reefer* [1993] - Leading English authority on expert duties, Civil Evidence Act 1972 - Expert opinion evidence, President's Direction on Expert Evidence (Business and Property Courts)

## Key Differences from US Practice

- No pre-trial depositions as of right in Scotland; examination occurs at proof or via commission, Expert's overriding duty is to the court, not the instructing party, Single joint expert (SJE) permitted where proportionate (CPR 35.7)
- Expert reports must contain a declaration of compliance with court rules, Instructions to expert are privileged from disclosure (but court may order inspection if challenged)
