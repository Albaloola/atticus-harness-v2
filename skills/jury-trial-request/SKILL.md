[SCOTS: Note] This skill adapts the US jury trial request procedure for Scots law. There is no direct equivalent to FRCP Rule 38 (filing a jury demand). In Scotland, civil jury trial is available by right in the Court of Session for certain actions (personal injury, defamation, etc.) and in the All-Scotland Sheriff Personal Injury Court. Criminal jury trials are the default for solemn proceedings (serious cases). The skill preserves the core drafting methodology (locating procedural rules, understanding default modes of trial, filing the appropriate motion) while replacing US concepts with Scottish equivalents.

## Quick Start

1. **Identify the court** - Court of Session (Outer House), High Court of Justiciary, Sheriff Court (solemn), All-Scotland Sheriff Personal Injury Court
2. **Identify case type** - civil or criminal; if civil, whether the action is one where jury trial is available
3. **Determine default mode** - some actions are automatically "jury actions" (personal injury in Court of Session); some default to judge alone (judicial review, family law)
4. **If an election is needed** - lodge the appropriate motion or minute
5. **For criminal matters** - note that solemn procedure (before jury) is default for serious charges unless accused opts for summary procedure or both sides consent

## Civil Jury Trial, Availability

| Court | Available For | Default |
|---|---|---|
| **Court of Session (Outer House)** | Personal injury actions, defamation, certain other delict claims | Personal injury cases are "jury actions" by default, either party may lodge a motion to have the case heard by a judge alone (RCS r. 37.1) |
| **All-Scotland Sheriff Personal Injury Court** | Personal injury actions where damages claimed | Jury trial available by default (s. 79 Courts Reform (Scotland) Act 2014) |
| **Sheriff Court (Ordinary Cause)** | Generally not, jury trial not available in Sheriff Court Ordinary Cause procedure except ASPIC | N/A for most cases |
| **Sheriff Court (Simple Procedure)** | No jury trial | Adjudicator only |

### When Jury Trial is Not Available (Court of Session)

- Family actions (divorce, children, financial provision on divorce)
- Commercial actions (Commercial Court procedure)
- Judicial review, Motions and procedural hearings, Certain equitable-type remedies (interdict, specific implement)

## Criminal Jury Trial, Availability

| Court | Procedure | Jury Size | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **High Court of Justiciary** | Solemn (mandatory for certain serious crimes) | 15 | Majority (8-7 minimum for conviction) |
| **Sheriff Court (Solemn)** | Solemn for offences where statutory maximum > 4 years (or common law) | 15 | Majority |
| **Sheriff Court (Summary)** | No jury | None | Sheriff alone |
| **Justice of the Peace Court** | No jury | None | JP alone |

### Right to Jury Trial (Criminal)

- Solemn procedure is mandatory for the most serious offences (murder, rape, etc.)
- For other cases triable either way: the accused may elect jury trial (solemn procedure)
- There is no US-style "Sixth Amendment right" - the right to jury trial in criminal matters is governed by statute (Criminal Procedure (Scotland) Act 1995) and the European Convention on Human Rights (Art. 6)

## Procedural Steps

### Civil, Electing Judge Alone (opting out of jury)

In the Court of Session, personal injury actions are jury actions by default. To opt for judge alone:

1. Lodge a **Motion for Judge Alone Trial** under RCS r. 37.1(4)
2. Grounds: technical/complex evidence, length of trial, medical evidence volume, unusual complexity
3. The Lord Ordinary will decide based on the balance of convenience and interests of justice
4. Filing deadline: at the Options Hearing or within such time as the Lord Ordinary directs

### Civil, Seeking Jury Trial (non-default cases)

For actions where jury trial is not automatic (e.g., non-personal injury delict), the party may lodge a **Motion to Apply for Jury Trial** under RCS rr. 37.1 to 37.3. The Lord Ordinary has discretion.

### Criminal, Seeking Solemn Procedure

If the accused wishes jury trial for a case where summary procedure might otherwise apply (subject to the prosecution's election):
- Lodge a **minute stating a desire for jury trial**
- The court will determine competency, Court can decline if the offence is not serious enough to warrant solemn procedure

## Jury Composition and Procedure

| Element | Civil (Court of Session) | Criminal (Solemn) |
|---|---|---|
| **Number of jurors** | 12 | 15 |
| **Verdict** | Majority (9-12 typically sufficient unless special verdict) | Majority (minimum 8 for conviction; 7 for acquittal) |
| **Not Proven** | N/A | Available as third verdict (not guilty / not proven / guilty) |
| **Judge role** | Presides, directs on law, no commenting on facts | Same (but direction on corroboration required) |
| **Clerk of Court** | Administers oath, records verdict | Same |
| **Jury selected from** | Electoral register (random selection) | Electoral register (random selection) |

## Filing Checklist (Civil, Motion for Judge Alone or Jury Trial)

- [ ] Confirm the case is in a court where jury trial is available (Court of Session / ASPIC)
- [ ] Check whether jury trial is the default or requires a motion
- [ ] If lodging a motion: comply with RCS Form 7.5 (or relevant form)
- [ ] Serve motion on all opposing parties
- [ ] Attend the Options Hearing (or any relevant procedural hearing)
- [ ] Consider grounds: complexity, estimated duration, medical/expert evidence volume
- [ ] Note that the party opposing jury trial bears the burden of showing it is unsuitable

## Key Differences from US System

| US | Scotland |
|---|---|
| Seventh Amendment jury right in civil cases | No constitutional right; statutory availability only (RCS Chapter 37, Courts Reform Act 2014) |
| FRCP Rule 38 - 14-day deadline to demand jury | No fixed deadline; usually determined at Options Hearing |
| 6 or 12 jurors (civil federal) | 12 (civil, Court of Session / ASPIC) |
| Unanimous verdict required in federal criminal | Majority verdict: 8 of 15 minimum for conviction |
| Jury demand as a filed pleading (standalone or in complaint) | No separate "jury demand" document, determined by case type or motion |
| Waiver by not filing within deadline | Case proceeds as jury action unless motion for judge alone is granted |
| Voir dire (jury selection by counsel) | Judge-led jury selection; limited counsel involvement |
| "Guilty / Not Guilty" only | "Guilty / Not Guilty / Not Proven" (criminal) |

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Statutory framework:**
- Courts Reform (Scotland) Act 2014 ss. 79 to 80 (All-Scotland Sheriff Personal Injury Court)
- Criminal Procedure (Scotland) Act 1995 ss. 90 to 96 (solemn proceedings)
- Rules of the Court of Session 1994 (RCS) Chapter 37 - Jury Trials, Ordinary Cause Rules 1993 (r. 36A for ASPIC cases)
- Act of Sederunt (Sheriff Court Personal Injury Claims) 2012
- Human Rights Act 1998 Sch. 1 Art. 6 (right to fair trial, extends to jury trial guarantees)

**Key features of Scottish jury system:**
- Civil jury trial is limited in scope (only Court of Session and ASPIC; not Sheriff Court ordinary)
- No three-verdict system in civil cases (only "for the pursuer" / "for the defender")
- Corroboration (criminal) - Scottish criminal law requires corroboration (two independent sources of evidence), which influences jury directions, Majority verdicts, unlike US unanimity requirement
- "Not Proven" verdict, unique to Scotland (same effect as acquittal)
- No civil jury in Sheriff Court (except ASPIC)
- 15-member juries in criminal cases (larger than US/England & Wales)
- The judge in Scotland sums up the evidence and directs on law but cannot comment on the facts

**Practitioner notes:**
- Jury trial in civil matters is generally considered faster and less expensive than in the US, The All-Scotland Sheriff Personal Injury Court (based in Edinburgh with local hearings) was created to provide jury trial in personal injury cases below Court of Session thresholds, For cross-border UK cases, jury trial availability differs significantly between Scotland and England & Wales (England has wider civil jury availability but it remains rare)
- Never assume US jury trial procedure applies, Scottish practice is sui generis
