### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

## Quick Start

Gather before drafting:

1. **Property** - postal address, title number(s), description, year built
2. **Sellers** - all registered owners per title sheet
3. **Records** - Home Report, Single Survey, Property Questionnaire, Energy Report, local authority searches, reports
4. **Known issues** - defects, prior damage, environmental conditions, disputes, burdens/servitudes
5. **Timing** - marketing date, conclusion of missives, entry date

## Document Structure

### Header

- Title: `Property Information and Disclosure Statement`
- Seller(s) full legal names, Property: address, postcode, title number(s), description, year built, Governing framework: Home Report / Scottish conveyancing standard conditions, Knowledge qualifier: "to the best of Seller's knowledge and belief after reasonable enquiry"
- Notice: disclosure is not a substitute for buyer's survey and searches

### Core Disclosure Sections

Complete all; use `N/A` where not applicable.

| Section | Key Fields |
|---|---|
| Property Overview | Occupancy, year built, additions/alterations, boundary issues |
| Structural | Foundation type, cracks/settlement/damp, previous repairs (dates/contractor/warranty) |
| Roof/External | Age/material, leaks/repairs, windows/doors condition |
| Plumbing/Heating | Pipe material, leaks, boiler age/service history, central heating type |
| Electrical | Condition, last inspection date (EICR), consumer unit type |
| Water/Drainage | Mains public water/drainage or private supply/septic tank; last inspection date |
| Damp/Timber | Damp-proof course, timber treatment, guarantees |
| Interior | Water damage, mould, condensation, floor/wall issues |
| Environmental | Contaminated land, radon, flooding history, USTs |
| Planning/Building Warrants | Planning permissions obtained, building warrants, completion certificates, unauthorised works |
| Title/Burdens | Servitudes, real burdens, wayleaves, access rights, development obligations |
| Council/Government | Council Tax band, non-domestic rates, Building Standards, enforcement notices, compliance orders |
| Boundaries/Fencing | Boundary responsibility, disputes, access rights |
| Other Material Facts | Anything a reasonable buyer would consider material (neighbour disputes, Japanese knotweed, previous deaths if asked) |

### Home Report Consistency

Cross-reference with the Home Report (mandatory for all marketed residential properties since December 2008):

- **Single Survey** - ensure disclosed defects match survey findings; note any material differences
- **Property Questionnaire** - complete all sections: Council Tax, services, parking, factoring, alterations, guarantees, insurance claims, occupier details
- **Energy Performance Certificate** - provide energy rating; note if below minimum standard

### Lead-Based Paint Disclosure

[SCOTS: Note] Lead-based paint disclosure is not a standard Scottish form. However, pre-1970 properties may contain lead paint, disclose known presence and any inspection reports. Not regulated by EPA/LHHA (US-specific). Disclose as a material fact.

### Exhibits

Index every attachment by title and date. Include: Home Report, Single Survey, Property Questionnaire, Energy Report, local authority searches, title sheet and burdens, guarantees/warranties, previous surveys.

### Certification & Acknowledgment

- Seller certification (accuracy to best of knowledge after reasonable enquiry)
- Ongoing duty to update through conclusion of missives, No warranty disclaimer (property sold as inspected)
- Signature lines for all sellers with dates, Buyer acknowledgment with dates

### Delivery Timing

- Home Report must be provided to prospective buyers before/marketing, Property Questionnaire completed before market, Supplementary disclosure on receipt of formal offer/missives, Ongoing disclosure obligation until conclusion of missives

## Pitfalls & Checks

- Use factual, objective language only, no marketing, Cite sources by document name and date, Never leave blanks; use `N/A` and initial where required, Disclose unauthorised works explicitly with known risks, If the property is ex-local authority, note restrictive title conditions, Japanese knotweed disclosure required, if present, flag management plan, Flood risk: refer to SEPA flood maps; disclose known flooding incidents, Checking fixtures/fittings list (Schedule of Inclusions/Exclusions) against disclosed items, Mark uncertain items `[VERIFY]` and request confirmation, All owners must sign; missing signatures can affect contract formation

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

The following adaptations apply when this skill is used for Scottish transactions:

**Fundamental difference:** The US seller disclosure statement is not a standard Scottish form. Scottish practice relies on:

1. **Home Report** (mandatory for residential properties marketed since December 2008): Single Survey + Property Questionnaire + Energy Report
2. **Standard missive conditions** (e.g., Clause 11 - defects; Clause 15 - warranties)
3. **Replies to standard enquiries** - buyer's solicitor submits property enquiries
4. **Pre-conclusion disclosure** - seller must answer specific questions material to the purchase

**No standard form TDS/SPDS:** Unlike many US states, Scotland has no single statutory disclosure form. The Property Questionnaire is the closest equivalent but covers different matters.

**Purchaser's obligations:** Due diligence is primarily through the buyer's solicitor: (a) title examination via Registers of Scotland; (b) local authority searches (planning, building warrants, roads); (c) Home Report; (d) survey if the buyer commissions an additional survey.

**No Title Insurance:** Scotland does not have a title insurance market. Title is guaranteed by the Land Register (Registers of Scotland) through the State guarantee.

**No EPA/Lead Paint regulations:** US-specific EPA lead paint rules do not apply. Disclose lead paint as a material fact; no federally prescribed form.

**Planning:** Town and Country Planning (Scotland) Act 1997 (as amended). Building Standards (Scotland) Regulations. Completion certificates are required for work subject to building warrant.

**Council Tax:** Council Tax band from local authority (Scottish Assessor). Scottish Council Tax rates differ from England and Wales.

**Energy:** EPC required for all marketed properties. In Scotland, EPC is part of the mandatory Home Report.

**Repair and condition:** The common law principle of caveat emptor applies in Scotland, modified by: (a) Home Report disclosure; (b) missive warranties; (c) Consumer Rights Act 2015 (if seller is a trader).

**Mortgage:** Scottish standard security (not US mortgage/deed of trust).
