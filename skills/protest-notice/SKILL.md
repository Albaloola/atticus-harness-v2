# Notice of Intent to Challenge Public Contract Award

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a time-sensitive notice to a contracting authority preserving the client's right to bring proceedings for breach of the duties owed under the Public Contracts (Scotland) Regulations 2015 / Public Contracts Regulations 2015 and triggering the statutory suspension of contract award.

## Required Inputs

1. **OJEU/Find-a-Tender reference** and procurement title
2. **Award decision date** or date client learned or ought to have learned of the adverse decision (drives limitation clock)
3. **Contracting Authority** - name, address, contact officer
4. **Client** - legal name, registered office (Companies House), economic operator status
5. **Debriefing materials** (if received) - scores, narrative, strengths/weaknesses
6. **Procurement documents** - specification, evaluation criteria, tender notice, contract terms
7. **Basis of challenge** - preliminary identification of procurement error(s)

## Timeliness / Limitation Rules

| Trigger | Deadline | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Adverse decision known or ought to have been known | 30 days from knowledge | Reg. 89(1) PC(S)R 2015 |
| Post-debriefing | 30 days from debriefing (if earlier of knowledge rule) | Reg. 89(2) PC(S)R 2015 |
| Procurement document impropriety | Before contract conclusion date | Reg. 89(3) PC(S)R 2015 |
| Formal claim following this notice | Within 30-day limitation period | Reg. 89 PC(S)R 2015 |

**These limitation periods are strict, the court may not extend them.**

## Document Structure

### 1. Header Block

```
[Firm Letterhead]
[Date]
[REF: [Client Ref]]

[Contact Officer Name & Title]
[Contracting Authority / Address]

SENT BY EMAIL AND RECORDED DELIVERY

RE:   Notice of Intention to Challenge Award
      OJEU/Find-a-Tender No. [NUMBER]
      [Procurement Title]
      Award Decision Date: [DATE]
      Date of Knowledge: [DATE]
```

### 2. Opening

- State formal notice of intention to bring proceedings under the Public Contracts (Scotland) Regulations 2015
- Identify economic operator: legal name, Companies House number, role in procurement, Identify legal representatives: name, firm, Law Society of Scotland registration, contact info

### 3. Standing and Interest

Establish sufficient interest to bring proceedings under Reg. 88 PC(S)R 2015:

| Element | Show |
|---|---|
| Eligible economic operator | Submission conforming to procurement documents |
| Direct economic interest | Real chance of award / next in line |
| Breach of duty owed | Error in procurement process directly affecting position |

Strengthen with: evaluation scores showing proximity to award, debriefing notes indicating inconsistencies, any identified manifest error.

### 4. Grounds of Challenge

State each ground with sufficient particularity to provide notice; preserve detailed submissions for the formal claim. For each ground: (1) identify the improper action, (2) cite the violated regulation or procurement principle, (3) state the competitive prejudice.

**Categories:**
- **Evaluation errors** - manifest error in assessment, misapplication of criteria, undisclosed sub-criteria, unequal treatment
- **Procurement document defects** - ambiguous/restrictive specifications, disproportionate requirements, non-compliance with equal treatment/transparency
- **Procedural violations** - improper MEAT assessment, failure to conduct required exclusions, inadequate debriefing, non-compliance with standstill period

### 5. Timeliness Statement

- Date economic operator learned or ought to have learned of adverse decision, Confirmation this notice is within the 30-day limitation period, If post-debriefing: debriefing date and day count

### 6. Automatic Suspension Request

Invoke automatic suspension under Reg. 95 PC(S)R 2015:

- Demand immediate suspension of the contract award process, Note that the contracting authority may apply to court to set aside the suspension, Request written confirmation suspension is in effect, Flag irreversibility or switching costs if contract has already been signed

### 7. Reservation of Rights

- Formal proceedings to follow within the 30-day period, Reserve right to supplement grounds after disclosure, Request full copy of evaluation materials, Note openness to alternative resolution while preserving all rights, Reference right to apply for interim remedies under Reg. 96 PC(S)R 2015 (interim interdict, suspension, setting aside of decision)

### 8. Signature & Certificate of Service

```
Signed,

[Solicitor Name], [Firm]
[Address / Email / Phone / DX]
Date: [DATE]

SERVICE CERTIFICATE
I certify that on [DATE], a copy of this notice was served on
[Contracting Authority] by email to [address] and by recorded delivery to [address].
[Signature]
```

## Checks

- **Do not** omit the timeliness statement, the 30-day limitation period under Reg. 89 is strict
- **Do not** disclose full legal argument or commercially sensitive analysis prematurely
- **Always** include the suspension request with statutory reference
- **Always** calculate and note the formal proceedings deadline
- **Always** check whether the Procurement Reform (Scotland) Act 2014 applies (regulated procurements below EU thresholds)
- **Tone**: professional, assertive, no inflammatory language, preserve settlement posture
- **Length**: 2 to 4 pages; save full arguments for the formal proceedings
- **Format**: single-spaced, double-space between paragraphs
- **Contracting Authority note**: Scottish Ministers, local authorities, health boards, and other Scottish public bodies are subject to PC(S)R 2015. UK-level contracting authorities are subject to PCR 2015 - ensure correct regulations are cited

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from US federal procurement protest practice (FAR/GAO) for use under Scottish and UK public procurement law.

**Key changes:**
- **Legal framework**: replaced FAR 33.103/33.104, 4 C.F.R. Part 21, and 31 U.S.C. §§ 3551 to 3553 with the Public Contracts (Scotland) Regulations 2015 (SSI 2015/446) and the Procurement Reform (Scotland) Act 2014
- **Forum**: replaced GAO (Government Accountability Office) with Court of Session or Sheriff Court (Reg. 88(2) PC(S)R 2015). No equivalent administrative body, challenges are by way of court proceedings
- **Limitation period**: changed from 10 jurisdictional days to 30 days from knowledge or constructive knowledge (Reg. 89). Note: the court may exceptionally extend this but the general rule is strict
- **Automatic suspension**: replaced FAR 33.104(c) with Reg. 95 PC(S)R 2015 - automatic suspension applies on commencement of proceedings, not by notice alone
- **Interim remedies**: added right to apply for interim interdict, suspension, or setting aside under Reg. 96
- **Standstill period**: Scottish procurement law requires a minimum standstill period (typically 10 calendar days) between award notification and contract conclusion, note this aligns with UK/EU regime
- **Terminology**: replaced 'bid protest' with 'procurement challenge', 'Contracting Officer' with 'Contracting Authority', 'standing under 31 U.S.C.' with 'sufficient interest under Reg. 88'
- **Procurement register**: replaced solicitation/UEI references with OJEU/Find-a-Tender service and Companies House registration
- **Remedies**: US-style bid protest remedies (sustained/recommendation) replaced with UK court remedies (setting aside, damages, ineffectiveness under Reg. 98)
- **Distinctively Scottish**: note that Scotland has its own procurement regulations (PC(S)R 2015) separate from the England & Wales PCR 2015, though they share EU-derived principles. The Procurement Reform (Scotland) Act 2014 adds specific domestic requirements (fair work practices, community benefit clauses)

**Status:** Matter-of-law conversions verified against current Scottish/UK procurement legislation. Application to specific facts requires solicitor oversight.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
