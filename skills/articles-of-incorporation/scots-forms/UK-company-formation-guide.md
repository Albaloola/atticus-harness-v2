# UK Company Formation, Guidance Note (Scotland)

## Key Differences from US Incorporation

### Document Structure

In the UK, companies are formed under the **Companies Act 2006**. The constitutional documents are:

1. **Memorandum of Association** - A short statutory document stating subscribers wish to form a company and agree to become members. No longer contains objects/purpose (since 2006 Act); objects are unrestricted by default.
2. **Articles of Association** - The primary constitutional document (equivalent to US bylaws + some charter provisions). Governs shareholder rights, director powers, meeting procedures, share transfers. Model articles (prescribed by SI 2008/3229 for Scotland) apply if no bespoke articles are adopted.

### Key Differences

| US Concept | UK/Scotland Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Articles of Incorporation / Certificate of Incorporation | Memorandum of Association + Articles of Association |
| State of incorporation | Registered office: England & Wales, Scotland, or Northern Ireland |
| State filing fee (e.g. Delaware) | Companies House registration fee (£12 online / £40 paper) |
| Delaware DGCL | Companies Act 2006 |
| Registered Agent | Registered office (must be physical address in jurisdiction) |
| Incorporator | Subscriber (signs memorandum) |
| Director exculpation (DGCL §102(b)(7)) | No direct equivalent, Companies Act 2006 s.232 prohibits indemnification for negligence/breach; D&O insurance permitted |
| Authorised shares | No concept of "authorised" shares, companies have an initial share capital; can issue new shares by board resolution (subject to articles) |
| Par value | Shares must have a fixed nominal value (e.g. £0.01, £1.00) |
| S-Corp | No direct equivalent, UK has various tax structures (FRS 102, small company regime) |
| Blank check preferred | Not applicable, different class rights structure; classes require bespoke articles |
| Registered Agent (commercial) | Registered office can be a service address (many commercial providers) |
| Annual report (state) | Confirmation Statement (Companies House, £13/year or £34 single filing) |
| Franchise tax | No equivalent, Corporation Tax (HMRC) applies on profits |

### Scottish-Specific Requirements

- Registered office must be in Scotland (not just "UK")
- Model Articles for Scottish companies differ slightly from English/Welsh models, Company name must not be the same as an existing name on the Companies House register, Certain company types require "PLC" or "Limited" (or "Ltd") designator, Scottish Limited Partnerships (SLPs) are separate entity form with different requirements

### Filing Process

1. Choose company name, check availability on Companies House
2. Prepare Memorandum and Articles of Association
3. Complete Form IN01 (application to register a company)
4. File with Companies House (online: £12, 24-hour turnaround)
5. Receive Certificate of Incorporation (confirms company number, date, registered office jurisdiction)
6. Register for Corporation Tax with HMRC (within 3 months of trading)
7. Register for VAT if turnover exceeds £90,000
8. Maintain Statutory Registers (members, directors, PSC register, etc.)

### Key Statutes

- Companies Act 2006 (primary legislation)
- Companies (Model Articles) Regulations 2008 (SI 2008/3229)
- Scottish company law differs on certain points, ensure articles reference Scotland where applicable

### Post-Incorporation Steps

- [ ] Issue share certificates
- [ ] Enter shareholders in register of members
- [ ] Register PSC (People with Significant Control) details
- [ ] Appoint directors and company secretary (if any)
- [ ] File Confirmation Statement annually
- [ ] Prepare annual accounts and file with Companies House
- [ ] Register for PAYE if employing staff
- [ ] Registered office address, display on correspondence and website

### Practitioner Note

The US "all-purpose" incorporation structure (blank check preferred, exculpation, indemnification) does not translate directly. UK articles are typically simpler: subscribers, share rights, director powers, meeting procedures, and administrative provisions. For VC-backed companies, use the BVCA Model Articles for Venture Capital backed companies as the starting template.
