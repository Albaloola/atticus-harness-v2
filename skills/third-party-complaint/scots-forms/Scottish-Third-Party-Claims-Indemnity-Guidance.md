# Third Party Claims & Indemnity, Scottish Civil Procedure

## No Direct "Impleader" Equivalent in Scots Law

**Scotland does not have a direct equivalent to FRCP 14 impleader.** However, Scottish civil procedure provides mechanisms for bringing in additional parties and asserting claims for contribution, indemnity, or warranty arising from the same subject matter.

## Scottish Mechanisms

### Counterclaims & Additional Party Orders
- **Counterclaim**: defender may counterclaim against pursuer; must be related to same subject matter
- **Third Party Notice / Additional Defender**: brought into action by defender alleging right of relief, contribution, or indemnity against a person not already party
- **Authority**: Sheriff Court Ordinary Cause Rules (OCR) Chapter 24 / Court of Session Rules (RCS) Chapter 26
- **Time limit**: must be brought before close of pleadings; court permission may be required thereafter

### Contribution and Indemnity
- **Law Reform (Miscellaneous Provisions) (Scotland) Act 1940 s.3** - right of contribution among joint wrongdoers
- **Contractual indemnity**: governed by terms of the contract; Scottish courts enforce clear indemnity clauses subject to standard contractual interpretation rules
- **Common law relief and indemnity**: available where one party's liability results from another's act or omission
- **Warranty claims**: breach of warranty in sale of goods (Sale of Goods Act 1979) or other contracts

### Third Party Procedure (Ordinary Cause Rules)
1. Defender lodges Notice of Third Party
2. Third party becomes "Third Party Defender"
3. Defender pleads case for relief against third party
4. Third Party Defender may state defences
5. Court may determine issues between original parties and third party conjunctly

## Key Differences from US

| US Concept | Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| FRCP 14 impleader | Third party notice procedure (OCR Ch.24 / RCS Ch.26) |
| Indemnification (contractual) | Same concept; governed by contract; require clear express words |
| Contribution (comparative fault) | Law Reform (Miscellaneous Provisions) (Scotland) Act 1940 s.3 - contribution pro rata to fault |
| Subrogation | Recognised; insurer subrogation common; subrogated claim brought in name of insured |
| Class action / multi-party claim | Group proceedings under Civil Litigation (Expenses and Group Proceedings) (Scotland) Act 2018 (opt-in limited) |
| Rule 11 certification | No direct equivalent; court may sanction for abusive pleadings |
| Supplemental jurisdiction (§1367) | No equivalent; mixed claims may require separate proceedings |

## Scottish Court Systems for Third Party Claims

| Value | Track |
|---|---|
| ≤ £5,000 | Simple Procedure (limited third party procedure) |
| > £5,000 | Ordinary Cause (Sheriff Court) |
| High value/complex | Court of Session (Outer House) |

## Time Limits

- Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973
- Contribution claims: 2 years from date of decree/judgment against the person seeking contribution (s.3(4) of the 1940 Act)
- Contractual indemnity: governed by contract terms or 5-year prescriptive period (Sch.1 to the 1973 Act)
- Third party notice: before close of pleadings; after that requires court permission

## Practical Notes

- Scottish procedure places emphasis on documentary production rather than extensive third party joinder, Commercial actions (Court of Session / Sheriff Court) have more flexible case management for multi-party disputes, Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010 applies if contract contains arbitration clause; third party claims may need separate arbitration, No equivalent of "as of right" 14-day period for impleader; timing governed by court stage, Third party must be served with all pleadings and the third party notice
