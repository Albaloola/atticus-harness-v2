# Example: complaint escalation rewrite

## Before

Your response demonstrates a concerning lack of transparency and accountability. It fails to engage with the nuanced and multifaceted issues raised in my original complaint and relies on generic statements which do not inspire confidence. I therefore require this matter to be escalated immediately.

## After

I ask for this complaint to be considered at the investigation stage.

I do not accept the stage 1 response dated 14 June 2024 because it does not answer the main point in my complaint. I asked why the assessment report dated 8 May 2024 was not considered before the decision was made. The response says the decision was made in line with policy, but it does not identify the policy or explain how the report was taken into account.

This matters because the report directly addresses my ability to use the stairs in my home. If the report was not considered, I do not understand how the decision-maker assessed my circumstances.

I ask for:

1. a written explanation of whether the report dated 8 May 2024 was considered;
2. if it was considered, an explanation of why it did not change the decision;
3. if it was not considered, a review of the decision by someone who was not involved in the original decision;
4. a copy of the policy relied on in the stage 1 response.

I enclose the report dated 8 May 2024 and the stage 1 response dated 14 June 2024.

## Notes

- The rewrite keeps the complaint firm but removes broad accusations.
- It asks for a specific escalation and specific remedies.
- It uses the complaint-handling logic of issue, response gap, impact, and outcome sought.
