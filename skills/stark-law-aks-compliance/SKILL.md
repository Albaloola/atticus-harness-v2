# NHS Fraud & Anti-Bribery Compliance Plan

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Produces a compliance plan covering NHS fraud prevention, anti-bribery requirements under the Bribery Act 2010, Fraud Act 2006, and NHS Standard Contract terms, tailored to the organisation's arrangements and risk profile.

## [SCOTS] Important: No US Stark/AKS Equivalent

This skill originates from US Stark Law (physician self-referral) and the Anti-Kickback Statute. **There is no direct equivalent in UK or Scots law.** However, the compliance methodology, arrangement inventory, risk scoring, training, monitoring, audit, governance, transfers directly to NHS/UK healthcare fraud and bribery compliance.

What UK law provides instead:
- **Bribery Act 2010** - ss.1-2 (bribery offences), s.7 (failure to prevent bribery)
- **Fraud Act 2006** - ss.1-4 (fraud by false representation, failing to disclose, abuse of position)
- **NHS Counter Fraud Authority (NHSCFA)** - investigatory body for NHS fraud
- **NHS Standard Contract** - terms governing NHS provider relationships
- **GMC Good Medical Practice** - guidance on conflicts of interest
- **NHS consultant contract** - private practice restrictions, waiting list controls
- **Fit and Proper Persons Test (FPPT)** - NHS governance requirement

## Prerequisites

1. **Organisational documents** - NHS trust / health board structure, service lines, CQC registration
2. **Healthcare arrangement inventory** - consultant contracts, private practice arrangements, service level agreements, research collaborations, procurement contracts
3. **Referral/activity data** - consultant-level activity, NHS-funded care, private patient data
4. **Compliance history** - NHSCFA correspondence, previous audits, self-disclosures, CQC findings
5. **Compensation data** - consultant pay, NHS clinical excellence awards, private practice income, market rate benchmarks

## Core Workflow

### I. Scope & Authority

Define: covered entities (NHS trust/health board, subsidiary entities), covered personnel (employees, consultants, contractors, board members), covered relationships (all financial arrangements with referral sources or decision-makers), and authority basis (board resolution with date).

### II. Regulatory Framework

**NHS Counter Fraud Authority (NHSCFA)**
- Principal body investigating NHS fraud in England (devolved: NHS Scotland Counter Fraud Services)
- Focus: prescription fraud, dental fraud, procurement fraud, payroll fraud, contractor fraud, Powers: investigation, referral to CPS/COPFS for prosecution

**Bribery Act 2010**
- s.1: Bribing another person (intentionally)
- s.2: Being bribed (requesting/accepting)
- s.6: Bribery of foreign public official, s.7: Strict liability, failure of commercial organisation to prevent bribery, Defences: adequate procedures (s.7(2)), no facilitation payments exception, Penalties: individual, up to 10 years + fine; corporate, unlimited fine

**Fraud Act 2006**
- s.2: Fraud by false representation, s.3: Fraud by failing to disclose information, s.4: Fraud by abuse of position, Penalties: up to 10 years imprisonment + fine

**NHS Standard Contract (England/NHS Scotland equivalent)**
- Terms on: conflicts of interest, anti-fraud, data protection, service quality, subcontracting, governance, Sanctions: contract termination, recovery of overpayments, performance notices

**GMC Good Medical Practice (2024)**
- Duties on conflicts of interest: "You must declare any potential or actual conflicts of interest"
- Private practice: restrictions on NHS waiting list patients, facilities/equipment charging, Financial interests: must be declared and managed

**Consultant Contract (England 2003 / Scotland equivalents)**
- Private practice: 11% ceiling for full-time NHS consultants (in England)
- Waiting list controls: no private treatment for NHS waiting list patients without trust consent, Facilities: charges for NHS premises/equipment use

**Fit and Proper Persons Test (FPPT)**
- Regulation 5, Health and Social Care Act 2008 (Regulated Activities) Regulations 2014
- Directors and board members must be fit and proper, Criminal records, misconduct, fitness to practise checks

### III. Risk Assessment

**Arrangement inventory** - catalog: employed consultants, clinical directorships, private practice arrangements, research collaborations with NHS patient overlap, procurement decisions, service level agreements with referral implications, sponsorship relationships, hospitality/events.

**Risk scoring matrix:**

| Factor | High (3) | Medium (2) | Low (1) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Private practice income | >£50k/yr private | £10k to £50k | <£10k or none |
| Procurement influence | Direct decision-maker | Advisory role | No involvement |
| Bribery Act exposure | Foreign official contact | UK public contracts | Low-risk sector |
| Conflict of interest | Unmanaged/written only | Declared but not reviewed | Declared and managed |
| GMC compliance history | Active investigation | Past concerns | Clean record |

**Activity pattern analysis:** compare per-consultant private practice income to NHS commitments; flag post-contract private income spikes >20%; identify referral patterns between NHS consultants and private facilities; validate NHS waiting list compliance.

### IV. Operational Policies

**Arrangement lifecycle:**

| Phase | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Pre-execution | Submit to compliance ≥30 days before effective date |
| Content | Parties, services, time commitment, specific compensation, term dates, termination |
| Market rate threshold | Independent valuation if >£50k/yr OR high procurement exposure |
| Execution | Signed before services begin or compensation paid |
| Annual review | Verify compliance, market rate currency, services rendered |
| Renewal | Re-assess ≥60 days before expiration |

**Prohibited practices:** private treatment of NHS waiting list patients; gifts/hospitality over £25 (refer to internal policy); incentives linked to referral volume; preferential procurement based on personal relationships; unmanaged conflicts of interest; failure to declare financial interests to GMC/NHS board.

### V. Training Program

| Audience | Timing | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| All staff | Hire + annual | Bribery Act, Fraud Act, reporting obligations |
| Consultants/Clinicians | Onboarding + annual | Conflicts of interest, private practice rules, GMC duties |
| Procurement/Finance | Quarterly | Anti-bribery due diligence, contract compliance |
| Board/Senior Management | Annual | Bribery Act s.7 liability, FPPT, governance duties |
| Compliance staff | Ongoing | NHSCFA, COPFS enforcement, regulatory updates |

Require scenario-based assessments, passing score threshold, documented attendance.

### VI. Monitoring & Audit

**Continuous monitoring:** automated tracking of consultant activity, private practice income declarations, procurement decisions, contract expirations, conflict of interest register. Alert on pattern deviations, approaching expirations.

**Annual internal audit** (risk-based sampling, weight toward high-risk):
- Conflict of interest register completeness, Private practice compliance (NHS waiting list, 11% ceiling)
- Bribery Act adequate procedures review, Procurement due diligence documentation, Services actually rendered (time records, deliverables)
- Activity patterns within normal parameters

**External review:** every 3 years minimum, or after mergers, enforcement actions, major regulatory changes.

### VII. Reporting & Non-Retaliation

**Channels:** third-party whistleblowing hotline (24/7), compliance email, physical mail, intranet portal, in-person to compliance/HR.

**Non-retaliation policy** - covers termination, demotion, pay reduction, schedule changes, hostile environment. Protected disclosure provisions (Public Interest Disclosure Act 1998).

**Triage:**

| Severity | Criteria | Response |
|---|---|---|
| Critical | Ongoing violation, significant financial/safety risk | Immediate investigation, suspend arrangement |
| High | Systemic compliance failure | Expedited investigation (30 days) |
| Standard | Isolated potential violation | Standard investigation (60 days) |

### VIII. Investigation & Remediation

1. Immediate assessment, interim protective measures (suspend arrangement, restrict access)
2. Engage external legal counsel for potential criminal violations or significant exposure
3. Document scope, evidence, interviews, timeline, findings, regulatory citations
4. Present findings to Compliance Officer / Committee / Board per severity

**Corrective actions:** terminate/restructure non-compliant arrangements, recover overpayments, discipline responsible individuals, enhance controls, self-report to NHSCFA/NHS Scotland Counter Fraud Services when required, verify implementation.

### IX. Documentation & Retention

**Per-arrangement file:** executed agreement + amendments, market rate analysis with methodology, board/committee approval minutes, conflict of interest declaration, compliance review certification, evidence of services rendered, compensation records, correspondence, annual review documentation.

**Retention:** minimum 10 years from last service/payment; longer if litigation/investigation pending. Secure storage with role-based access.

### X. Governance

| Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| Compliance Officer | Day-to-day oversight, direct board access, authority to challenge business decisions |
| Compliance Committee | Quarterly review of findings, corrective actions, regulatory developments |
| Board of Directors | Ultimate oversight, annual effectiveness review, resource allocation |

**Annual programme review:** policy currency, monitoring effectiveness, training outcomes, reporting utilisation, benchmark against NHSCFA guidance and NHS Standards.

**Regulatory change protocol:** monitor NHSCFA/CQC/GMC final guidance, enforcement actions → draft amendment → legal review → Committee → Board → communication/training.

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **Bribery Act s.7 is strict liability** - only defence is "adequate procedures"
- **No facilitation payments exception** - unlike US FCPA
- **NHS waiting list rules** - consultants must not treat NHS waiting list patients privately without Trust consent
- **Full-time consultant private practice cap** - 11% of NHS salary in England; check Scottish equivalents
- **GMC fitness to practise** - conflicts of interest can lead to tribunal
- **FPPT** - board members must pass fit and proper test
- **NHSCFA self-disclosure** - proactive self-reporting can mitigate penalties
- **Devolved differences** - NHS Scotland Counter Fraud Services is separate from NHSCFA (England/Wales)
- Use directive language ("must," "shall") - not aspirational ("should generally")
- Verify all regulatory references are current before finalising [VERIFY]
- Tailor risk categories and examples to the organisation's actual service lines and jurisdiction (England/Scotland/Wales/NI)

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
