# Seat of Arbitration Agreement, Employment (Scotland)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a mutual arbitration agreement for employment relationships in Scotland, balancing employer protections with procedural fairness to meet the requirements of the Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010.

## Prerequisites

Collect before drafting:

1. **Jurisdiction(s)** - employee work location(s) in Scotland (drives Sheriff Court vs. Court of Session considerations)
2. **Employee type** - prospective vs. current (consideration analysis); executive vs. general
3. **Existing documents** - offer letter, handbook, prior arbitration agreements, share incentive plans
4. **Administrator** - Scottish Arbitration Centre or ad hoc under the Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010
5. **Company entity** - full legal name (registered in Scotland or UK)

### 2. Covered Claims

Draft broad coverage with specific enumeration and "illustrative, not exhaustive" language:

- **Statutory**: Equality Act 2010, Employment Rights Act 1996, Working Time Regulations 1998, National Minimum Wage Act 1998
- **Wage & hours**: overtime, rest breaks, unlawful deduction from wages (ERA 1996 Part II), holiday pay
- **Delict** (Scots tort): wrongful dismissal, breach of contract, misrepresentation
- **Retaliation / whistleblowing**: Employment Rights Act 1996 (protected disclosures), Equality Act 2010 victimisation
- **Post-employment**: breach of confidentiality, restrictive covenants
- **Temporal scope**: before, during, and after employment

### 3. Excluded Claims & Carve-Outs

| Exclusion | Reason |
|---|---|
| Employment Tribunal claims (unfair dismissal, redundancy, discrimination) | Statutory tribunal jurisdiction, cannot be excluded by private agreement (ERA 1996 s. 203 / Equality Act 2010 s. 144) |
| Statutory maternity/paternity/adoption pay | Statutory benefit schemes |
| Restrictive covenant interdicts | Employer needs immediate court remedy |
| ACAS-conciliated matters | Protected statutory process |
| Trade union disputes | Trade Union and Labour Relations (Consolidation) Act 1992 |
| Health & Safety Executive complaints | Regulatory enforcement, cannot be arbitrated |
| Auto-exemption savings clause | Any claim a court determines non-arbitrable under section 8, Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010 |

### 4. Arbitration Procedures

```
Rules:            [Scottish Arbitration Centre Rules / Ad hoc under Arb (Scot) Act 2010]
Arbitral tribunal:  Single neutral; practising solicitor/advocate with 10+ yr employment experience or retired sheriff
Seat:             Scotland (Edinburgh or Glasgow)
Language:         English
Fallback:         Nominated body unavailable → parties to agree → Sheriff Court appointment under s. 16, Arb (Scot) Act 2010
```

**Recovery of evidence and disclosure**:
- Commission and diligence (Scots equivalent of discovery): court orders for inspection, recovery, and preservation of documents, Parties may contractually agree disclosure parameters (standard disclosure, key document categories)
- Arbitrator may resolve disputes on document access

**Arbitrator authority**:
- All court-available remedies (damages, specific implement, declarator, interdict)
- Written award with reasons (s. 53, Arb (Scot) Act 2010)
- Must apply Scots substantive law; may rule on preliminary matters, May not consolidate proceedings without written consent, Punitive damages are not available under Scots law

### 5. Cost Allocation, Judicial Expenses

| Cost | Allocation |
|---|---|
| Employee filing fee | Minimal (nominal); employer may advance |
| Arbitrator fees | Parties share equally unless otherwise agreed |
| Legal expenses | General rule: "loser pays" (judicial expenses follow success) - ordinary rule under Scots costs practice |
| Employer if unsuccessful | Likely liable for employee's reasonable costs |
| Transcript | Ordering party; tribunal if required |
| Savings clause | If term unenforceable → reform to minimum; presumptive allocation per Arb (Scot) Act 2010 schedule 1, rule 76 |

### 6. Waivers

**Jury trial waiver** (Scots civil procedure rarely uses juries in commercial/employment matters, primarily a confirmatory provision):
- State agreement to private arbitration rather than court proceedings, Mutual; separate signature/initial line

**Individual claims only**:
- No representative proceedings (Scots law does not permit class/collective actions in employment arbitration)
- Group proceedings (Civil Litigation (Expenses and Group Proceedings) (Scotland) Act 2018) are opt-in court-based only, not available in arbitration

## Enforceability Checks

**Requirements under Scots law**:

1. Valid agreement under section 4, Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010 - in writing and clear in its terms
2. Claims to be arbitrated must be arbitrable (section 8) - employment tribunal claims generally not arbitrable
3. Fair procedures, natural justice / common law requirements
4. No undue influence or inequality of bargaining power (common law)
5. Written reasoned award (s. 53, schedule 1, rule 46)

**Key statutes and cases**:
- Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010 - the primary legislative framework, Employment Rights Act 1996, s. 203 - restriction on contracting out of statutory employment rights, Equality Act 2010, s. 144 - restriction on contracting out of discrimination rights, Trade Union and Labour Relations (Consolidation) Act 1992
- *Southwell v Jackson* (arbitrator immunity at common law) / *Earl of Halsbury v Sheridan* - Scots arbitration authorities, Contingency fee arrangements (speculative fee arrangements) are restricted in Scotland, do not reference US-style contingency in costs

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from a US framework. Key differences in Scottish employment arbitration:

- **Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010** replaces the FAA as the governing legislation. The Act has a mandatory schedule (schedule 1) of procedural rules that apply unless parties expressly agree alternative terms.
- **Employment Tribunal jurisdiction** cannot be excluded by private agreement, section 203 ERA 1996 and section 144 Equality Act 2010 render void any attempt to contract out of statutory tribunal claims. Arbitration is only available for contractual and common law claims.
- **No class/collective/PAGA mechanism** - class actions do not exist in Scots employment law. Group proceedings (2018 Act) are court-only and opt-in.
- **Punitive damages not available** under Scots law. Compensation is based on actual loss.
- **Judicial expenses** (costs) generally follow success ("loser pays"), the reverse of the US default. Contingency fees are prohibited; speculative (no-win no-fee) funding is regulated.
- **No unconscionability doctrine** - Scots law addresses procedural unfairness through the common law of undue influence and the requirement for fair notice under the Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010.
- **£ not $** - all amounts should be in pounds sterling.
- **Solicitor/Advocate** not attorney. **Sheriff Court** not US District Court. **Court of Session** not US Court of Appeals.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
