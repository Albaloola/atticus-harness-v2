# Compulsory Purchase / Human Rights Compensation Pleading (Scotland) [SCOTS]

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Draft a pleading seeking compensation for heritable property taken or damaged by public authority action without formal compulsory purchase proceedings, under the Land Compensation (Scotland) Acts 1963/1973, Human Rights Act 1998 (A1P1 ECHR), and the law of reparation (delict).

## Prerequisites

Collect before drafting:

1. **Property documents** - disposition, title sheet (Land Register), search sheet (Sasines), legal description, date of acquisition and price
2. **Public authority action records** - notices, planning decisions, compulsory purchase orders, regulatory decisions, correspondence
3. **Valuation evidence** - before/after valuations (Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors, RICS), comparable sales in Scotland, income loss documentation
4. **Administrative exhaustion** - representations made, hearings, planning appeals (DPEA), negotiation history
5. **Physical evidence** - photos, surveys, engineering reports of damage or occupation
6. **Party identification** - full legal names of all proprietor pursuers and defender public authority entities

## Quick Start

1. Identify taking type, compulsory purchase, injurious affection, loss of amenity, or regulatory deprivation under A1P1
2. Confirm exhaustion, final authority decision obtained and pre-action protocol followed
3. Choose forum (Court of Session vs. Sheriff Court) and verify jurisdiction
4. Draft pleading sections in order below
5. Validate against pitfalls checklist

## Pleading Structure

### 1. Caption & Introductory Paragraph

- Title: "Initial Writ / Summons for Compensation for Compulsory Purchase / Injurious Affection"
- State statutory basis (Land Compensation (Scotland) Act 1963, Land Compensation (Scotland) Act 1973, A1P1 Human Rights Act 1998)
- Identify taking type and timeframe

### 2. Jurisdiction and Venue

| Forum | Basis | Requirements |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| **Court of Session (Outer House)** | Complex/high-value claims; A1P1 human rights claims | Value over £100,000 or complex regulatory issues |
| **Sheriff Court** | Standard compulsory purchase compensation | Venue where property is situated; ordinary cause procedure |

Allege: property situs gives jurisdiction; Lands Tribunal for Scotland may have concurrent jurisdiction.

[SCOTS: No direct equivalent to the US ripeness doctrine. Scottish administrative exhaustion requires: (1) final authority decision; (2) any statutory appeal procedure exhausted; (3) pre-action protocol correspondence sent to the public authority under the Scottish pre-action protocol for judicial review / compulsory purchase.]

### 3. Parties

- **Pursuer:** Name (as on title sheet), address, ownership interest type, date of acquisition, proportionate interest if multiple proprietors
- **Defender(s):** Official public authority name (e.g. "The Scottish Ministers", "[Council Name] Council"), registered address, service address; entity in corporate capacity and/or office-holders in official capacity

### 4. Factual Narrative (Chronological, Numbered Paragraphs)

1. **Property** - postal address, title number (Land Register), description, planning classification, permitted uses
2. **Pre-taking condition** - structures, improvements, actual use, development plans
3. **Investment-backed expectations** - purchase price, improvements, reasonable use expectations (relevant to A1P1 proportionality)
4. **Public authority action** - dates, officials/departments, stated justification, legal authority cited (compulsory purchase order, planning refusal, regulatory notice)
5. **Physical impact** - area affected, nature of interference (flooding, occupation, encroachment, planning refusal, regulatory restriction, road closure, nuisance)
6. **Economic impact** - market value diminution with figures, income loss (croft, let property, business), duration, permanence
7. **Exhaustion** - representations made; DPEA appeal; pre-action protocol compliance; authority refusal to compensate

### 5. Grounds of Claim / Causes of Action

Select applicable ground(s):

| Type | Basis | Authority |
|------|-------|-----------|
| **Compulsory Purchase** | No compensation paid or inadequate compensation for compulsory acquisition | Land Compensation (Scotland) Act 1963; Land Compensation (Scotland) Act 1973 |
| **Injurious Affection (land taken)** | Diminution in value of land retained after compulsory acquisition | Land Compensation (Scotland) Act 1973, s. 6 |
| **Injurious Affection (no land taken)** | Damage to land by public works; loss of amenity | Land Compensation (Scotland) Act 1973, ss. 1 to 2 |
| **A1P1 ECHR (Human Rights Act 1998)** | Deprivation of possessions without compensation; disproportionate interference disproportionate to public interest | Human Rights Act 1998, Sch. 1, Art. 1 Protocol 1; [SCOTS: No direct UK equivalent of Penn Central; use proportionality analysis under A1P1] |
| **Reparation (Delict)** | Nuisance; negligence; interference with property rights | Common law; Donoghue v Stevenson 1932 SC (HL) 31 |
| **Unlawful Acts (Judicial Review)** | Ultra vires act; irrational decision; procedural impropriety | Court of Session Act 1988; Chapter 58 Rules of the Court of Session 1994 |

For each: allege the statutory or common law provision relied on, identify the public purpose served, emphasise that compensation arises from the taking or damage.

### 6. Damages / Compensation

| Component | Measure |
|-----------|---------|
| **Total acquisition** | Open market value as at date of valuation (Land Compensation (Scotland) Act 1963, s. 12) |
| **Partial acquisition** | Value of land taken + injurious affection to retained land |
| **Injurious affection (no land taken)** | Diminution in value caused by public works (1973 Act, ss. 1 to 2) |
| **Loss of profits / disturbance** | Reasonable costs of removal, relocation, loss of goodwill |
| **Home loss / farm loss payment** | Statutory additional payments under Part III 1973 Act |
| **Pre-judgment interest** | From date of taking/entry to date of decree |
| **Expenses (judicial expenses)** | Expenses follow success, Scottish "loser pays" rule; [SCOTS: No US-style attorney fee-shifting under 42 U.S.C. § 1988] |

Include preliminary valuation estimate; preserve right to prove exact amount at proof.

### 7. Plea-in-Law and Prayer for Relief

**Pleas-in-Law** (Scottish pleading style, numbered propositions of law):
1. The pursuer having been deprived of heritable property without compensation by the defender, is entitled to compensation.
2. The defender's actings being ultra vires / disproportionate, the pursuer is entitled to reparation.
3. Failing agreement, the amount of compensation falls to be determined by the court.

**Prayer for Relief:**
1. Decree for payment of compensation (not less than £[amount])
2. Interest from date of taking / injurious affection
3. Post-decree interest
4. Expenses
5. Declarator that the defender's action constituted a taking / injurious affection
6. Interdict / specific implement if applicable
7. Such other relief as the court deems just

### 8. Verification & Signature

- Signed by pursuer's solicitor (Scottish solicitors do not use Rule 11 certification; professional obligations under the Law Reform (Miscellaneous Provisions) (Scotland) Act 1990 and Law Society of Scotland Practice Rules apply)
- The initial writ / summons must be signed and state an address for service, The pursuer's oath or affirmation may be required for certain applications

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **No direct US-style inverse condemnation** - Scots law has no direct equivalent. Use Land Compensation Acts for physical taking and A1P1 ECHR for regulatory deprivation. [SCOTS: US cases like Penn Central, Lucas, Loretto are not precedential in Scotland. Use proportionality analysis under A1P1 and Scottish Lands Tribunal case law.]
- **Exhaustion is essential** - Pre-action protocol compliance is expected; failure may result in expenses sanction.
- **Time bar (prescription)** - Claims under the Land Compensation Acts: 6 years from date of damage. A1P1 claims: 1 year from date of act (Human Rights Act 1998, s. 7(5)). Delict: 5 years from date of damage; 3-year triennium (Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973).
- **Lands Tribunal for Scotland** - First-instance tribunal for compulsory purchase compensation disputes. Court of Session has supervisory jurisdiction.
- **Valuation standards** - RICS Red Book applies; chartered surveyor evidence is expected.
- **Paragraph numbering** - Consecutive throughout entire pleading.
- **Format** - Comply with Rules of the Court of Session 1994 or Sheriff Court Ordinary Cause Rules.
- [SCOTS: No Fifth/Fourteenth Amendment Takings Clause, UK has no written constitutional guarantee against uncompensated takings. Use the Human Rights Act 1998 (A1P1) as the closest equivalent. Statutory compensation schemes under the Land Compensation Acts and the Acquisition of Land Act 1981 are the primary UK remedies.]

## Scotland/UK Adaptation [SCOTS]

This skill has been adapted from a US inverse condemnation complaint (Fifth/Fourteenth Amendment takings clause) to a Scottish/UK compulsory purchase compensation and human rights pleading.

**Key adaptations:**
- Replaced Fifth/Fourteenth Amendment Takings Clause with Land Compensation (Scotland) Act 1963, Land Compensation (Scotland) Act 1973, and Art. 1 Protocol 1 ECHR (via Human Rights Act 1998).
- Replaced "Plaintiff/Defendant" with "Pursuer/Defender".
- Replaced "complaint" with "initial writ" (Court of Session) or "summons" (Sheriff Court).
- Replaced US ripeness doctrine with Scottish pre-action protocol / administrative exhaustion requirements.
- Replaced Penn Central/Lucas/Loretto tests with proportionality analysis under A1P1 ECHR and Scottish Lands Tribunal case law.
- Replaced APN/title report with Land Register title number / Sasines search sheet.

- Replaced US-style prayer for relief with Scottish pleas-in-law and prayer format.

- Added reference to Lands Tribunal for Scotland.
- Replaced RICS Red Book valuation standards (UK) with mention.
- Removed references to punitive damages (no punitive damages in Scots law).
