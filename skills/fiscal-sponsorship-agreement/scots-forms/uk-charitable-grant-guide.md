# UK Charitable Grant Agreement Guide

## Important Structural Note

There is **no direct equivalent** of US "Fiscal Sponsorship" under UK or Scots law. The US model (501(c)(3) sponsor receiving tax-deductible contributions on behalf of a non-exempt project) does not exist in UK charity law.

**Instead, the closest UK structures are:**

1. **Charitable Grant Agreement** - a registered charity makes a restricted grant to a project group (unincorporated or otherwise) with conditions ensuring the grant is used only for charitable purposes
2. **CIO with subsidiary** - a Charitable Incorporated Organisation (CIO) creates a restricted fund for a specific project
3. **Umbrella charitable structure** - a large charity "hosts" a project within its own charitable objects

This guide covers option 1 - a **Grant Agreement between a UK Registered Charity and a Project Group** - as the most practical adaptation.

## Key UK Charity Structures

| Structure | Key Features | Regulation |
|---|---|---|
| **Charitable Incorporated Organisation (CIO)** | Separate legal personality; limited liability for trustees; most common modern form | Charity Commission (E&W) / OSCR (Scotland) |
| **Charitable Company (Ltd by guarantee)** | Company limited by guarantee with charitable objects; dual regulation | Companies House + Charity Commission/OSCR |
| **Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisation (SCIO)** | Scotland-specific equivalent to CIO; simpler reporting than charitable company | OSCR only |
| **Unincorporated Association** | No separate legal personality; trustees personally liable; older structure | Charity Commission/OSCR |

## How This Differs from US Fiscal Sponsorship

| US Concept | UK Equivalent | Key Difference |
|---|---|---|
| 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status | Registered charity (Charities Act 2011 / Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005) | UK charity registration is more structured; public benefit test required |
| IRS | HMRC / Charity Commission / OSCR | HMRC handles tax relief; Charity Commission/OSCR handle charity regulation |
| IRC § 170 (donor deductibility) | Gift Aid (HMRC) | Gift Aid allows charities to reclaim basic rate tax on donations; donors get relief only if they pay sufficient tax |
| EIN | Charity number (from Charity Commission or OSCR) | 6 to 7 digit number; public register available online |
| Variance power | Charitable purpose restriction | UK charities CANNOT redirect funds to non-charitable purposes, variance power is not available; funds must always further charitable objects |
| Donor acknowledgment letter | Gift Aid declaration | Must meet HMRC requirements; different format from IRS rules |
| IRS 990 filing | Annual return to Charity Commission/OSCR | Lighter filing requirements for smaller charities |
| Unrelated business income (UBI) tax | Trading by charities / HMRC rules | Charities can trade if within primary purpose; separate trading subsidiary needed for non-primary trading |
| State law (CA/NY/etc.) | Devolved differences (Scotland vs England & Wales) | Scotland has own Charity Regulator (OSCR) and different charity legislation |

## Scotland-Specific: OSCR

**OSCR, Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator**

- Regulates all charities in Scotland (including SCIOs)
- Maintains the Scottish Charity Register, Scottish charities must have "charitable purposes" defined by the Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005
- Scottish charitable purposes include (but are not limited to): prevention of poverty, advancement of education, advancement of religion, advancement of health, saving of lives, advancement of citizenship/community development, advancement of the arts/heritage/culture/sport, promotion of equality and diversity, environmental protection/improvement, relief of those in need, animal welfare, any other purpose beneficial to the community
- **Public benefit test** - Scottish charities must demonstrate public benefit; must meet the "community" test (not unduly restrictive)
- **Charity accounts:** Scottish charities must follow the Charities SORP (Statement of Recommended Practice), which differs slightly from E&W

## Gift Aid

**Gift Aid** is the key UK tax relief mechanism:

- Charities reclaim 25p for every £1 donated (basic rate tax)
- Donor must have paid sufficient UK income/capital gains tax
- **Gift Aid declaration** must: identify the charity, state donor's full name and address, confirm donor pays sufficient UK tax, specify the donation(s) covered, include a revocation mechanism
- **No minimum donation threshold** (unlike the US $250 rule)
- Gift Aid does not apply to purchases of goods/services, membership subscriptions for personal benefit, or donations from companies

## Charitable Purpose Restrictions (Replaces Variance Power)

**UK charities CANNOT:**
- Act outside their charitable objects (ultra vires)
- Apply funds for non-charitable purposes, Distribute profits to trustees or members, Engage in political campaigning beyond ancillary charitable advocacy

**Grant agreements must:**
- Require that all project funds are applied only for charitable purposes consistent with the charity's objects, Include clawback provisions if funds are used for non-charitable purposes, Prohibit the project from undertaking political campaign activities, Restrict the project from engaging in private benefit transactions

## Reporting Requirements

| Threshold (Scotland) | Reporting Requirement |
|---|---|
| Income ≤ £50,000 | Annual accounts and return to OSCR (simplified) |
| Income £50,001 to £250,000 | Full accounts + annual return + trustees' report |
| Income > £250,000 | Full accounts + annual return + trustees' report + professional examination of accounts |
| Income > £1M | Full audit |

## Recommended Grant Agreement Structure (Scotland/UK)

1. **Parties:** Charity (full name, charity number, registered address) × Project Group (full name, contact)
2. **Recitals:** Charity's charitable objects; project description; determination that grant furthers charitable objects
3. **Grant Amount & Purpose:** Specific sum restricted to defined charitable purposes
4. **Project Activities:** Schedule of permitted activities; express prohibition of non-charitable activities
5. **Financial Provisions:** Reporting; segregation of funds; clawback for misapplication; surplus disposition
6. **Gift Aid:** If applicable, Gift Aid declaration wording
7. **Term & Termination:** Fixed term; earlier termination on breach or purpose failure
8. **Liability:** Charity not liable for project's contracts, debts, or obligations
9. **Intellectual Property:** Ownership, licence terms, acknowledgment requirements
10. **Governing Law:** Scots law (if Scottish charity) / English law (if E&W); exclusive Scottish jurisdiction
11. **Execution:** Signed by two trustees/office bearers (or one if a CIO with seal)

## Checklist

- [ ] Charity registered with OSCR (Scotland) or Charity Commission (E&W)
- [ ] Charity number stated in agreement
- [ ] Charitable objects confirmed, grant falls within them
- [ ] Gift Aid declaration included (if applicable)
- [ ] Clawback/covenant for misapplied funds included (replaces variance power)
- [ ] Scotland/UK GDPR compliance for any personal data
- [ ] No political campaign activity permitted
- [ ] HMRC charitable donation rules satisfied
- [ ] SORP compliance note for accounts preparation
- [ ] Contracts (Scotland) Act 1997 / formalities of execution checked
