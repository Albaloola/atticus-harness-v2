# Clinical Trial Agreement, UK (MHRA/HRA)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Draft a protocol-specific CTA governing study conduct, compliance, data rights, payments, and risk allocation for UK clinical trials of investigational medicinal products (CTIMPs).

## Quick Start

Gather before drafting:

1. Final or near-final protocol (number, version, phase, endpoints, schedule of events)
2. Investigator Brochure with current safety profile
3. Draft budget with FMV rationale and payment schedule per UK mCTA / AcoRD cost attribution
4. Site policies on contracting, IP, publication, and indemnification
5. Regulatory status: MHRA CTA authorisation, REC favourable opinion (HRA-approved REC), DSMB plan if any
6. Devolved nation considerations: site location affects HRA approval pathway (Scotland has separate systems for NHS Research Scotland permissions)

## Agreement Sections

| Section | Required Content |
|---|---|
| Parties and Recitals | All parties; protocol reference; regulatory framework reference (UK SI 2004/1031 as amended); GCP |
| Definitions | Study, Protocol, IMP, AE/SAE/SUSAR, GCP (ICH E6(R2)), Study Data, Confidential Information |
| Study Conduct | Protocol incorporation; amendment process; investigator independence; facilities/staffing; MHRA + REC compliance |
| Sponsor Obligations | IMP supply; training; monitoring; regulatory submissions (MHRA); safety updates (SUSAR reporting); DSMB |
| Site/Investigator Obligations | REC approval; informed consent per UK regulations and GCP; records; protocol adherence; safety reporting to sponsor and MHRA |
| Data and Records | Source data access; CRF completion; retention period (25 years post-CTIMP or per UK regulations) |
| Budget and Payment | AcoRD cost attribution: research costs vs excess treatment costs vs support costs; FMV structure; invoicing; screen failures; early withdrawals; withholding rights |
| Confidentiality and Privacy | UK GDPR compliance; s.251 approval if applicable; de-identification |
| IP and Publications | Data/invention ownership; publication review windows (30 to 60 days); UK mCTA IP provisions |
| Indemnification and Insurance | Sponsor insurance (clinical trial insurance); NHS indemnity (CNORIS in Scotland); mutual indemnity; limits; additional insureds |
| Term and Termination | For cause/convenience; subject safety; wind-down; survival; REC notification |
| Dispute Resolution | Negotiation → mediation → court; jurisdiction of Scottish courts (if Scottish site) or EW courts |
| Admin | Notices, assignment, amendment, severability, independent contractor |

**[SCOTS: Note]** The UK has a standard Model Clinical Trial Agreement (mCTA) for commercial sponsors. As of 2026, new versions of the mCTA are pending following changes to the UK Clinical Trials Regulations that took effect April 28, 2026. Check the HRA website for the current mCTA version. The mCTA should be used as the starting point for UK CTA drafting.

## Compliance Checklist

- [ ] MHRA CTA authorisation for CTIMP; or exemption pathway confirmed
- [ ] REC favourable opinion (REC recognised by UKECA)
- [ ] Informed consent per UK regulations (not US 21 CFR Part 50)
- [ ] GCP reference to ICH E6(R2)
- [ ] UK GDPR compliance (DPA 2018) for data processing
- [ ] HRA approval (where applicable, including Scotland-specific pathway)
- [ ] SAE/SUSAR reporting timelines defined: fatal/life-threatening SUSAR to MHRA within 7 days; others within 15 days
- [ ] Record retention ≥ 25 years after trial end (CTIMP), or as per UK regulations
- [ ] AcoRD-compliant cost attribution
- [ ] Clinical trial insurance certificate (sponsor)
- [ ] NHS indemnity coverage confirmed (CNORIS for Scotland)
- [ ] AER (Annual Safety Report) submission to MHRA and REC
- [ ] Debarment/exclusion status check (no US equivalent, use UK debarment register)

## Risk Tailoring

- [ ] High-risk IMP/device → higher insurance limits + enhanced safety monitoring
- [ ] Vulnerable populations → explicit consent safeguards (Mental Capacity Act / Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000)
- [ ] Invasive procedures → explicit injury and compensation language per UK CT regulations
- [ ] Scottish sites → CNORIS coverage, Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000 compliance

## Exhibits

- **A:** Protocol
- **B:** Budget and payment schedule (AcoRD-compliant)
- **C:** Schedule of events/procedures
- **D:** IB (Investigator Brochure)
- **E:** Insurance certificate

## Pitfalls

- Use exact protocol identifiers and dates, do not paraphrase protocol constraints.
- **Use the UK mCTA as the starting point** - deviating from the mCTA without good reason may delay site contracting (NHS sites required to use mCTA).
- Define AE/SAE/SUSAR per current UK regulations and ICH GCP. Mark `[VERIFY]` if deviating.
- Never promise sponsor control over investigator clinical judgment.
- Keep publication review windows time-bounded (30 to 60 days).
- **[SCOTS: Note] Compensation must be FMV** - not tied to outcomes, referrals, or volume. AcoRD rules determine cost attribution.
- **Scottish mental capacity laws differ** - Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000 applies in Scotland (not the Mental Capacity Act 2005 which applies in England and Wales). Ensure consent provisions reflect the correct legislation.
- Preserve subject safety obligations post-termination (follow-up and reporting).
- **[SCOTS: Note] CNORIS vs NHS RLP** - in Scotland, clinical negligence indemnity is provided by CNORIS (operated by the Scottish Government). In England and Wales, it is the NHS Litigation Authority (NHS RLP). Ensure the CTA correctly references the applicable scheme.
- UK-specific: additional requirements if the trial involves ATMPs (Advanced Therapy Medicinal Products) or gene therapy (GTAC oversight).

**Adapted for:** Scotland and UK law

**Key Scottish/UK considerations:**
- MHRA is the UK medicines regulator (one body for all four UK nations)
- HRA provides the approvals framework; in Scotland, NHS Research Scotland co-ordinates permissions, The UK mCTA is the standard template for commercial sponsor CTAs with NHS sites, UK clinical trials regulations were reformed effective April 28, 2026 - verify current SI references, Scottish mental capacity law (Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000) differs from England/Wales (MCA 2005)
- Scottish NHS clinical negligence indemnity is through CNORIS, not the NHS Litigation Authority, AcoRD cost attribution is specific to the UK research funding landscape, GCP is mandated under UK regulations (SI 2004/1031 Reg. 28)
- IMP safety reporting follows EU-derived but UK-specific rules (SUSAR timelines etc.)

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
