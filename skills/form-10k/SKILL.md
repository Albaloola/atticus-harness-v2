# UK Annual Report & Strategic Report

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

[SCOTS: Note, This skill is adapted from US SEC Form 10-K drafting guidance for the UK/Scottish legal context. Core drafting methodology is preserved. References to US law have been replaced with the equivalent UK legislative framework under the Companies Act 2006, FCA Disclosure Guidance and Transparency Rules (DTR), and the UK Corporate Governance Code.]

Draft a complete annual report pursuant to the Companies Act 2006, compliant with:

- **Strategic report** - Companies Act 2006 s. 414A to 414D (fair review of business, principal risks, business model, strategy, KPIs, and, for quoted companies, s. 414C(8) to (11) on environmental, employee, human rights, social and community matters)
- **Directors' report** - CA 2006 s. 415 to 419 and Schedule 7 (business review, dividend/interest information, political donations, greenhouse gas emissions, future developments)
- **Directors' remuneration report** - CA 2006 s. 420 to 422A (quoted companies: single total figure table, annual report on remuneration, implementation report, policy report, shareholder vote)
- **Corporate governance statement** - UK Corporate Governance Code (comply-or-explain); FCA DTR 7.2 (premium listed); LR 9.8.6R (for UK listed companies) / UKLR 6.6 (new UK Listing Rules)
- **Financial statements** - FRS 101/102 (UK GAAP) or UK-adopted IFRS, per CA 2006 s. 393 to 474
- **Annual financial report (AFR)** - FCA DTR 4.1 (XHTML format, iXBRL tags for IFRS group financials, filed via NSM)

## Gather Before Drafting

- **Prior filings** - most recent annual report, half-yearly reports (DTR 4.2), RNS announcements, FCA/Companies House correspondence
- **Audited financials** - balance sheet, profit and loss account, cash flow statement, statement of changes in equity, notes (all per CA 2006 applicable accounting framework), auditor's report (s. 495 to 497 CA 2006)
- **Company records** - articles of association, board minutes (including board committee minutes), material contracts, register of directors' interests
- **Compensation data** - service agreements, LTIP/PSP awards, bonus plans, pension arrangements, directors' emoluments
- **Risk/compliance** - board risk register, internal audit reports, audit committee reports, FCA or PRA correspondence, litigation files
- **Shareholding/ownership** - register of members, significant shareholding notifications (DTR 5), directors' shareholdings, substantial shareholding disclosures (CA 2006 s. 793)

## Cover / Front Page

Include: registered company name, registered number, registered office address, accounting reference date (or period end), place of incorporation (Scotland or England & Wales), company type (public/private, premium/standard listed, AIM, etc.), auditor name and address. For listed companies: LEI (Legal Entity Identifier), ISIN, FCA website for NSM filing reference.

[SCOTS: Note, Scottish-incorporated companies must state "registered in Scotland" on filings. The annual report addresses shareholders directly (s. 423 CA 2006 - duty to circulate). No equivalent to the SEC's "filer status" classification except for company size categories (large, medium, small, micro per CA 2006 s. 382 to 384B and s. 465 to 467).]

## Part I, Strategic Report (CA 2006 s. 414A to 414D)

**Section A, Business Review & Fair Review.** Draft in order: company purpose → business model and strategy (s. 414C(8)) → principal risks and uncertainties (s. 414C(2)(b)) → market overview and competitive position → material developments in the financial year → key performance indicators (s. 414C(4)) → likely future developments → environmental matters (including climate-related financial disclosures per FCA DTR 4.1/ESEF or Streamlined Energy and Carbon Reporting (SECR) per CA 2006 s. 414C(7) to (9) / The Companies (Miscellaneous Reporting) Regulations 2018).

[SCOTS: Note, The strategic report's purpose per s. 414C(1) is "to inform members of the company and help them assess how the directors have performed their duty under section 172 (duty to promote the success of the company)." This corresponds to the MD&A in a Form 10-K, but is explicitly shareholder-focused and framed around s. 172 factors (long-term consequences, employee interests, business relationships, community/environment impact, reputation, fair dealing).]

**Section B, s. 172 Statement.** For quoted companies (and recommended for large private companies): statement describing how directors have had regard to s. 172(1)(a) to (f) factors (The Companies (Miscellaneous Reporting) Regulations 2018). Reference key decisions, stakeholder engagement, board activities.

**Section C, Climate & ESG Reporting.** If in scope:
- Streamlined Energy and Carbon Reporting (SECR) - energy use, emissions (Scope 1, 2, selected Scope 3), methodology, intensity ratio, TCFD/ISSB-aligned disclosures per FCA DTR requirements (premium and standard listed)
- Task Force on Climate-Related Financial Disclosures statement (if mandatory)
- Non-financial and sustainability information statement (s. 414C(7) to (11) for large companies)

**Section D, Principal Risks.** Each principal risk (not a register of every risk): nature → impact → likelihood → mitigation → change during year → trend. Flag new, escalated, reduced, or removed risks vs. prior year. Align with Viability Statement (see Governance section below).

**Section E, Viability Statement.** (Premium listed / UK Corporate Governance Code): statement describing the board's assessment of the company's prospects over a defined period (typically 3 to 5 years), covering principal risks, stress testing, and the basis of assessment. If none, explain.

## Part II, Directors' Report (CA 2006 s. 415 to 419)

**Business review (for companies exempt from strategic report).** Otherwise, include: likely future developments, research and development activities (s. 414(3) to (4)), greenhouse gas emissions (SECR), employee involvement, engagement with suppliers/customers/others (s. 172 statement cross-reference), political donations (s. 414C(5) and s. 415, separate disclosure per Part 1 of Schedule 7), dividend recommendation and payment details, directors' interests in shares, important post-balance-sheet events, existence of branches outside the UK, treatment of third-country branches (s. 419A), financial instruments, risk management objectives and policies, hedging, and price/credit/liquidity/cash flow risk (Schedule 7 para 6).

[SCOTS: Note, Directors' report corresponds loosely to supplemental information in Form 10-K (Items 1B, 9B, and select items in Parts III and IV) but is a distinct statutory document in the UK framework. In Scottish law, the directors' report is governed by the Companies Act 2006 as amended (applies equally to Scotland-registered companies).]

## Part III, Directors' Remuneration Report (CA 2006 s. 420 to 422A)

Applicable to **quoted companies** (as defined in s. 385) and certain AIM companies undertaking to comply.

**Annual Statement by Chair of Remuneration Committee.** Context, key decisions, outcomes, shareholder engagement, voting results at last AGM.

**Annual Report on Remuneration.** Tables:
- Single total figure of remuneration table (each director, current year and prior year)
- Scheme interests awarded (PSP/LTIP, face value, percentage of salary, performance period, metrics)
- Pensions table (company contributions, CETV transfers, defined benefit accrual)
- Payments to past directors / for loss of office, Statement of directors' shareholding and share ownership guidelines, Percentage change in CEO pay vs. employee remuneration (s. 421(2)(c) as amended by The Large and Medium-sized Companies and Groups (Accounts and Reports) Regulations 2008)
- Relative importance of spend on pay (s. 421(2)(d))
- Remuneration outcomes vs. company performance over 5+ years (total shareholder return graph)

**Policy Report.** Forward-looking: policy table for each element (purpose, operation, maximum opportunity, framework), notes on approach to exit payments, shareholding requirements, recoupment/clawback policy.

[SCOTS: Note, Equivalent to "executive compensation" (Item 11 in Form 10-K). The UK framework requires a binding shareholder vote on the remuneration policy at least every three years and an advisory vote on the annual implementation report each year. CD&A → remuneration committee report. The UK regime includes mandatory pay ratio disclosure (CEO pay vs. UK employees at quartiles) for large listed companies, no direct US equivalent.]

## Part IV, Corporate Governance Statement / Report

**Corporate Governance Statement (DTR 7.2 / LR 9.8.6R).** For premium listed: statement of compliance with UK Corporate Governance Code (comply-or-explain). For standard listed: statement of compliance with DTR 7.2 or similar.

**Board Composition and Leadership.** Directors (executive, non-executive, independent): name, role, tenure, relevant experience, key external directorships, committee membership and meeting attendance.

**Board Committees.** Report from each:
- **Audit Committee** - composition, activities (significant issues, external audit (including tender), internal audit, whistleblowing), fair, balanced and understandable assessment
- **Remuneration Committee** - activities (see Part III)
- **Nomination Committee** - board composition, succession planning, diversity and inclusion, board evaluation (external at least every three years)
- **Risk / Audit & Risk Committee** - if separate, risk management framework, risk appetite, emerging risks

**Internal Control and Risk Management.** Board's review of effectiveness of risk management and internal control systems (per UK Corporate Governance Code Provision 29, effective 2026 - declaration of effectiveness of material controls). Describe framework, scope of review, key findings, gaps identified, remediation progress.

**Directors' Responsibilities Statement.** Per CA 2006 s. 393 / DTR 4.1.5R (fair, balanced and understandable; going concern basis; prepared in accordance with applicable accounting framework; true and fair view; responsibility for maintaining adequate accounting records).

**Viability & Going Concern.** Directors' statement that the business is a going concern (s. 393 and UK Corporate Governance Code). Viability statement (3 to 5 years). Cross-reference to principal risks.

## Part V, Financial Statements (CA 2006 s. 393 to 474)

**Primary Financial Statements.** (Per applicable accounting framework, FRS 101/102 UK GAAP or UK-adopted IFRS):
- Profit and loss account (or income statement)
- Statement of comprehensive income (if separate)
- Balance sheet (statement of financial position)
- Cash flow statement (statement of cash flows)
- Statement of changes in equity, Notes to the financial statements

**Auditor's Report.** Per CA 2006 s. 495 to 497 (true and fair view / proper records / adequate explanations / consistency with strategic and directors' report). Include auditor name, date, signature.

**Audit Committee Report (if part of AFR).** Scope, significant issues, auditor independence, non-audit services, effectiveness of audit, tender process.

## Part VI, Additional Statutory & Regulatory Information

**Related Party Transactions.** Per CA 2006 s. 412 / FRS 102 Section 33 or IAS 24. Directors' transactions, material related party transactions, shareholder approval where required (DTR 7.3, LR 11 / UKLR 7).

**Principal Subsidiary Undertakings.** / Group undertakings / Significant holdings. List company name, country of incorporation, proportion held, principal activity.

**Share Capital Information.** Issued share capital, movements during the year, rights attaching to shares, own shares held (treasury shares), shareholder authorities (purchase of own shares, allotment of shares).

**EGM / AGM Information.** Notice of AGM arrangements (if sent with annual report), date, time, venue (or hybrid/virtual). Special business resolutions proposed. Proxy voting procedures.

## Part VII, Exhibits, Filing & Signatures

**Filing with Companies House.** Annual accounts and reports must be filed within:
- **Private companies:** 9 months after accounting reference date (s. 442 CA 2006)
- **Public companies:** 6 months after accounting reference date (s. 442 CA 2006)
- **Filing format:** iXBRL for accounts via approved software (mandatory from 1 April 2027). Directors' report, strategic report and auditor's report can be filed as PDF or as part of the iXBRL package.

**FCA NSM Filing (listed companies).** Annual financial report (AFR) must be filed via Electronic Submission System (ESS) to the National Storage Mechanism:
- **Deadline:** Within 4 months of financial year end (DTR 4.1.3R)
- **Format:** XHTML with iXBRL tagged IFRS group financials (DTR 4.1.10R to 4.1.18R)
- **RNS:** Annual results announcement required via a Primary Information Provider (PIP) under UKLR rules (timing: usually before AFR filing)

**Exhibits Index.** As applicable:
- Articles of association (main constitutional document)
- Material contracts referenced in the annual report, Service agreements with directors, Copy of auditor's report (separate from signed accounts)
- Shareholder circulars / resolutions in force, iXBRL tag list / taxonomy reference (if separate)

**Signatures.** Per CA 2006:
- Balance sheet must be signed on behalf of the board by a director (s. 414)
- Directors' report signed by a director or secretary (s. 419)
- Strategic report signed by a director or secretary (s. 414D)
- Auditor's report signed and dated (s. 503 to 504)
- Certified copy filed at Companies House per s. 441 to 447

**Formatting Notes:**
- Plain English per FRC Guidance on the Strategic Report (avoid boilerplate, inflated language, or vague qualitative statements)
- Every assertion backed by specific facts, figures, or data, Quantify trends with percentages and monetary amounts (GBP)
- Internal consistency across all sections (financials, cross-references, exhibit citations)
- Financial statements: FRS 101/102 UK GAAP or UK-adopted IFRS; narrative: Companies Act 2006 + FRC guidance, Flag incomplete data with `[DATA NEEDED]`, uncertain citations with `[VERIFY]`
- Listed company reports: ensure FCA DTR compliance, UK Corporate Governance Code comply-or-explain, and UKLR content requirements

### Scottish Specifics

- Scottish-incorporated companies (registered with Companies House Scotland in Edinburgh) follow the same Companies Act 2006 framework as the rest of the UK, but companies must state "registered in Scotland" on their registered office and filings.
- Scottish company number prefix: SC (or SO for Scottish Unlimited).
- Scottish charitable incorporated organisations (SCIOs) have their own separate reporting framework under the Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005.
- The Companies House jurisdiction for Scotland differs from England & Wales; filings must be directed to the correct registrar (Edinburgh).
- Scottish company law historically diverged in relation to debenture enforcement and certain corporate capacity matters, but CA 2006 largely unified company law across the UK.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
