# Written Questions / Commission Procedure (Scotland)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts written questions for commission procedure that pin down the opposing party's factual contentions, identify witnesses and evidence, and build the foundation for proof or jury trial. **Note: interrogatories as understood in US civil procedure do not exist as a standalone written discovery device in Scotland.** Written questions in Scotland typically proceed under commission and diligence (recovery of evidence) or as part of a commission to take evidence.

## Required Inputs

1. **Caption details** - court (Court of Session or Sheriff Court), case number, party names
2. **Operative pleadings** - initial writ/summons (claims) and defences (answers/defences stated)
3. **Jurisdiction** - Court of Session Rules (RCS Ch. 35) or Sheriff Court Ordinary Cause Rules (OCR Ch. 28/29); confirm applicable rules
4. **Procedural context** - specification of documents, commission for recovery of evidence, or commission to examine witnesses
5. **Set designation** - first specification, second, etc.
6. **Solicitor info** - name, firm, address, phone, email, practice number

## Document Structure

### 1. Caption

Standard Scottish litigation caption. Title: "Specification of Documents / Commission for Recovery of Evidence" or "Minutes for Commission."

### 2. Introduction

- Presented under the relevant rule: RCS Chapter 35 (Court of Session) or OCR Chapter 28/29 (Sheriff Court)
- State that the specification proceeds under the Administration of Justice (Scotland) Act 1972
- Require answers on oath/affirmation within the period ordered by the court, Outline: call for documents, interrogatories (written questions), and other recovery

### 3. Definitions

Define at minimum:

| Term | Scope |
|------|-------|
| IDENTIFY (person) | Name, address, phone, employer, relationship to parties |
| IDENTIFY (document) | Author, date, type, custodian, location |
| IDENTIFY (communication) | Date, participants, medium, substance |
| PERSON | Natural persons and all entities |
| DOCUMENT | All tangible and electronic formats |
| COMMUNICATION | Every form of information exchange |
| INCIDENT | Specific event(s) giving rise to the action |
| YOU/YOUR | Defender including agents, employees, representatives |

Draft broadly to prevent evasion; keep defensible.

### 4. Instructions

- Duty of disclosure extends throughout proceedings (Scots law, not FRCP 26(e); open commission and diligence procedure)
- Answer each call/interrogatory separately and fully, Privilege claims require specification (legal professional privilege; without prejudice communications; no privilege logs as in US)
- Duty to conduct a reasonable search, Business records should be produced in the order in which they are ordinarily kept, Copies of documents may be produced unless originals are required

### 5. Calls and Interrogatories

Tailor to the specific facts, claims, and defences. Organise by category:

**A. Witness Identification (Recovery of Evidence)**
- All persons with knowledge of relevant facts (identity, substance of knowledge)
- Proposed witnesses for proof or jury trial and subjects of evidence, Expert witnesses (identity, qualifications, report if prepared)

**B. Defender's Version of Events**
- Factual account of the incident/events, Acts or omissions attributed to pursuer (contributory negligence basis)
- Any counterclaims or third-party notices

**C. Evidence Identification (Specification of Documents)**
- Documents, photographs, recordings, correspondence related to the incident, Statements obtained from any person (written, audio, video)
- Surveillance evidence regarding pursuer or incident, Medical records, accident reports, investigation documents

**D. Personal Injury to Specific**
- Defender's knowledge of pursuer's injuries, Insurance cover (insurer, policy number, limits) - recall Insurance Act 2015
- Prior similar incidents or claims involving defender, Mitigation-of-damages contentions with factual basis

**E. Contention Interrogatories**
For each specific defence: state every supporting fact, identify every supporting document, identify every knowledgeable person.

### 6. Signature Block

- Solicitor signature, date, name, firm, address, phone, email, practice number, Signed for the pursuer (or defender as applicable)

### 7. Form of Commission (if applicable)

- Appointment of commissioner (usually a solicitor or advocate)
- Date, time, and place for commission hearing, Witness(es) to be examined, Whether interrogatories are to be adjusted and approved by the court

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **Not a separate discovery device.** Interrogatories/final written questions in Scotland are part of a commission procedure, not a standalone set like FRCP 33. Use specification of documents (RCS Ch. 35 / OCR Ch. 28) as the primary recovery device
- **Court approval required.** Unlike US interrogatories served without court order, Scottish interrogatories generally require court approval via commission motion
- **Tailor every set** - never submit generic interrogatories without case-specific customisation; Scottish courts require specification of each document or class
- **Proportionality** - each interrogatory and call must be relevant and material to the case; overly broad or "fishing" spec may be refused
- **Avoid speculation** - do not ask the defender to speculate or form an opinion on matters within the pursuer's own knowledge
- **Strategic sequencing** - front-load recovery of critical documents; interrogatories for examination follow after documents recovered
- **Check which court** - Court of Session (RCS Ch. 34/35) has different rules from Sheriff Court Ordinary Cause Rules (OCR Ch. 28/29) and Simple Procedure
- **Personal injury** - 3-year prescriptive period (Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973 s.17); note triennium relevance to timing
- **Legal professional privilege** - narrower than US work product doctrine; litigation privilege in Scotland is delimited by case law

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from the US original for Scottish civil procedure.

| US Concept | Scotland/UK Equivalent |
|---|---|
| FRCP 33 (Interrogatories) | RCS Ch. 35 / OCR Ch. 28-29 (Commission and Diligence; Specification of Documents) |
| Plaintiff | Pursuer |
| Defendant | Defender |
| Complaint (civil) | Initial Writ (Sheriff Court) / Summons (Court of Session) |
| Interrogatories (standalone) | Written questions as part of commission procedure (not standard independent device) |
| FRCP 26(e) (supplementation) | Duty of disclosure under Scots procedural rules (no direct equivalent; genuine/open procedure) |
| FRCP 33(d) (business records option) | Production of business records in usual order; no specific rule equivalent |
| Discovery period | Commission and diligence on specification of documents; no automatic discovery |
| FRCP 26(b)(1) (proportionality) | Common law and procedural rules: relevant, specified, not a fishing diligence |
| Privilege log | No parallel requirement; privilege claimed on specified categories |
| Requests for production | Specification of Documents / Commission for Recovery |
| Depositions (oral) | Commission to take evidence (court-appointed commissioner examines witnesses) |
| FRCP 30 (oral depositions) | Commission procedure, written interrogatories + oral examination before commissioner |
| State court rules | Sheriff Court Ordinary Cause Rules / Simple Procedure Rules |
| Subpart counting (cap of 25) | No numerical cap; court discretion controls scope of commission |
| US state pattern interrogatories (e.g. CA DISC-001) | No Scottish equivalent; each commission tailored to case |
| Applicable statute | Administration of Justice (Scotland) Act 1972 (recovery of evidence) |

**Key differences from US civil procedure:**
- No automatic discovery/disclosure in Scotland; each recovery requires court application, Commission and diligence is the primary mechanism, not interrogatories as a separate device, Pursuer (claimant) must plead a relevant case (prima facie) before recovery is permitted, Fishing diligence (speculative recovery) is not permitted, Written interrogatories are typically for examination of witnesses at a commission hearing, not served independently, The court controls the scope of recovery via interlocutor, No US-style "contention interrogatories" in Scottish practice, Legal professional privilege under Scots law: narrower than US work product doctrine, Pre-action disclosure available under Administration of Justice (Scotland) Act 1972 s.1

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
