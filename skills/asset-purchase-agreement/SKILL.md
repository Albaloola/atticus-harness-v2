# Asset Purchase Agreement, Scotland/UK

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Draft a transaction-ready APA that precisely allocates assets, liabilities, and post-closing risk between buyer and seller under Scots law.

[SCOTS: Note] This skill adapts the US asset purchase agreement model for the Scottish/UK legal context. Key structural differences: (1) No direct UK equivalent to IRS Form 8594; tax apportionment is handled through contractual allocation and SDLT/LBTT returns; (2) TUPE (Transfer of Undertakings (Protection of Employment) Regulations 2006) automatically transfers employees in asset sales, a major difference from US at-will employment; (3) UK non-compete provisions are significantly more restricted than US state-law approaches; (4) Scottish heritable property transfer requires a formal disposition recorded in the Land Register.

## Prerequisites

1. **Parties and structure** - legal names, entity types, registered numbers (Companies House), jurisdictions (Scotland, England & Wales, or other), signing authority, deal structure
2. **Asset and liability inventory** - preliminary lists for Purchased Assets, Excluded Assets, Assumed Liabilities, Excluded Liabilities
3. **Economic terms** - purchase price, payment form, adjustments, earnouts, escrow/holdback, allocation
4. **Key deal risks** - regulatory approvals, consents, IP chain of title, material contracts, TUPE implications for employees, environmental exposure
5. **Timeline** - signing/closing schedule, deliverables, conditions

If any prerequisite is missing, pause and ask, do not assume or fill gaps.

## Output Structure

### Step 1: Document Skeleton

Draft in this order:

1. Parties; Effective Date; Recitals
2. Definitions
3. Purchase and Sale of Assets
4. Excluded Assets
5. Assumed Liabilities
6. Excluded Liabilities
7. Purchase Price; Payment; Adjustments; Allocation
8. Closing; Closing Deliveries
9. Representations and Warranties of Seller
10. Representations and Warranties of Buyer
11. Covenants (Pre-Closing; Post-Closing)
12. Non-Competition / Non-Solicitation (if applicable)
13. Conditions to Closing
14. Indemnification
15. Miscellaneous; Signatures; Exhibits and Schedules

### Step 2: Exhibits and Schedules

| Label | Purpose | Must Include |
|---|---|---|
| Exhibit A | Purchased Assets | Detailed categories and itemised lists |
| Exhibit B | Excluded Assets | Cash, retained contracts, corporate records, insurance policies |
| Exhibit C | Assumed Liabilities | Specific obligations, amounts, contract IDs |
| Exhibit D | Purchase Price Allocation | Tax apportionment schedule for SDLT/LBTT purposes (no direct UK equivalent to IRS Form 8594) |
| Disclosure Schedules | R&W exceptions | Numbered to match each R&W subsection |

### Step 3: Asset Scope and Exclusions

- Define "Purchased Assets" by category; cross-reference Exhibit A
- "As is, where is" only if consistent with negotiated reps/warranties, Include books/records, data, IP, goodwill, domain names, social accounts if intended, Catch-all exclusion: anything not in Exhibit A is excluded, List retained corporate records, tax returns, insurance policies, non-assigned contracts

### Step 4: Liability Allocation

- "Assumed Liabilities" strictly limited to Exhibit C items
- "Excluded Liabilities" covers pre-closing operations, taxes, employee obligations (subject to TUPE, see note below), litigation, environmental

[SCOTS: Note] Under TUPE (Transfer of Undertakings (Protection of Employment) Regulations 2006), all employees assigned to the business being transferred automatically become employees of the buyer on the same terms and conditions. This is a statutory transfer that cannot be excluded by contract. The APA must address employee liability information (ELI) and any proposed dismissal/change to terms.

### Step 5: Price, Adjustments, and Tax Allocation

- Payment timing, wire instructions, escrow/holdback terms, Working capital or inventory true-up: baseline, measurement method, dispute process, timing, Tax allocation: unlike US law, there is no HMRC equivalent to IRS Form 8594. Apportion the consideration between different asset classes for SDLT/LBTT and capital allowances purposes. Include a covenant for consistent treatment, Consider LBTT (Land and Buildings Transaction Tax) on Scottish heritable property or SDLT (Stamp Duty Land Tax) for E&W property transfers

### Step 6: Reps and Warranties

**Seller R&W**: Organisation/authority (Companies Act 2006 compliance); title to assets; accounts/financials; compliance; material contracts; litigation; taxes; IP; employees/pensions (TUPE, auto-enrolment); environmental. Apply materiality and knowledge qualifiers where negotiated.

**Buyer R&W**: Organisation/authority; enforceability; funds/financing; no conflicting approvals; regulatory consents.

Number each R&W subsection to match its corresponding disclosure schedule.

### Step 7: Covenants

- **Pre-closing**: ordinary-course operation; no asset transfers; no new debt; no material contract changes
- **Post-closing**: preserve goodwill; restrict Seller use of transferred name/marks
- **Non-compete / non-solicit** (if applicable): define restricted business, geography, duration, consideration; include reformation/severability clause

[SCOTS: Note] UK post-termination non-compete restrictions in an asset sale context (vendor non-compete) are enforceable if reasonably necessary to protect legitimate business interests (goodwill, trade connections, confidential information). However, UK competition law (Chapter I of the Competition Act 1998 prohibitions) may apply if the restriction is too broad. Duration and geography must be proportionate. Unlike US state law, there is no general enforceability standard divergence, Scots law and English law are similar on this issue.

### Step 8: Closing Conditions and Deliveries

| Party | Conditions | Deliveries |
|---|---|---|
| Seller | Accuracy of reps, covenant performance, no MAE, consents received | Bill of sale, assignments (formal assignations in Scots law), lien releases, officer's certificate, good standing (Companies House certificate) |
| Buyer | Accuracy of reps, covenant performance | Payment, assumption agreement, officer's certificate |

**Scottish heritable property**: if the transaction includes land/buildings in Scotland, closing requires a formal Disposition recorded in the Land Register of Scotland. Engage a Scottish conveyancing solicitor.

### Step 9: Indemnification

- Seller indemnifies for breaches, excluded liabilities, pre-closing operations, Buyer indemnifies for breaches and assumed liabilities (note: TUPE liabilities may affect scope)
- Include notice procedures, defence control, cooperation obligations
- **Limits**: basket (deductible vs. tipping), cap, survival periods, fraud carve-out, insurance offset

### Step 10: Assignment and Assumption

- Reference separate assignment agreements (formal assignations) for contracts and IP, Identify third-party consent requirements; state non-assignment where prohibited by law or contract, Carve out non-assignable items at closing, Note: Scots law distinguishes "assignation" (of rights) from "novation" (of obligations) - ensure correct mechanism for each category

## Guidelines

- Keep asset and liability schedules itemised and cross-referenced; never use "all assets" without categories, Confirm third-party consent requirements before drafting assignment language, Match disclosure schedule numbering to each R&W subsection exactly, Use defined terms consistently; avoid duplicate definitions across exhibits, Vendor non-compete enforceability is assessed under UK competition law reasonably, tailor duration and geography to protect goodwill only [VERIFY with competition counsel for transactions above relevant thresholds]
- Tax allocation must align with economics and both parties' SDLT/LBTT and corporation tax reporting, If regulated assets or licences are involved, add specific regulatory approval conditions (FCA, PRA, Ofcom, etc.)
- If data or IP is included, confirm chain of title and include transfer instruments (registered IP requires separate UK IPO assignment)
- **TUPE obligations**: the buyer must provide employee liability information (ELI) 28 days in advance. The seller must disclose all "assigned" employees. Failure to comply attracts a financial penalty (minimum £500 per employee as of 2025/26)
- LBTT in Scotland replaces SDLT for Scottish land transactions, apply correct tax and return procedure, Output is draft work product requiring Scottish solicitor review, not legal advice

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Statutory framework:**
- Companies Act 2006 - corporate capacity, directors' duties, accounts, charges register, Transfer of Undertakings (Protection of Employment) Regulations 2006 (TUPE) - employee transfer, Sale of Goods Act 1979 - title and transfer of goods, Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995 - formalities for contracts and deeds, Land Registration etc. (Scotland) Act 2012 - registration of heritable property, Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999 - third party rights, Competition Act 1998 Ch. I / Art. 101 TFEU (retained) - non-compete restrictions, Income Tax (Earnings and Pensions) Act 2003 - employment tax on transfers, The Taxes (Stamp Duty Land Tax and Stamp Duty Reserve Tax) (EU Exit) Regulations 2019
- Land and Buildings Transaction Tax (Scotland) Act 2013 - LBTT for Scottish land, Regulatory Reform (Execution of Deeds and Documents) (Scotland) Regulations 2023
- Employment Rights Act 1996 - continuity of employment, dismissal and redundancy, Pensions Act 2008 - auto-enrolment obligations on transfer

**Key differences from US APA:**
- No IRS Form 8594 equivalent; tax allocation is contractual + SDLT/LBTT return, TUPE automatically transfers employment, unlike US at-will where buyer can choose not to hire, Non-compete restrictions are governed by UK competition law, not state-to-state divergence, Scottish heritable property transfer requires a Scottish solicitor (formal disposition, Land Register)
- "Assignation" (Scots law term) rather than "assignment"
- Different approach to purchase price allocation for tax purposes (no separate class reporting to HMRC equivalent to IRS Form 8594)
- UK does not use escrow as standard; holdbacks and warranties & indemnity insurance more common, LBTT applies to Scottish land, SDLT to E&W land (different rates, rules, and returns)
- UK has no Delaware-style corporate law dominance, all UK companies governed by Companies Act 2006
- Charges (security interests) registered at Companies House (not UCC filings)

**Regulatory bodies:**
- Companies House, registration of charges, annual returns, HMRC, stamp taxes, corporation tax, VAT, employment taxes, Revenue Scotland, LBTT (Scottish land transactions)
- UK IPO, registered IP assignments, FCA / PRA, regulated business transfers (e.g., financial services)
- CMA, competition clearance for transactions above thresholds, The Investment Association, public company M&A guidelines

**Forms and resources (download to scots-forms/):**
- Companies House Charge Registration form (MR01)
- Land Register of Scotland, Disposition template, UK IPO Patent/Copyright/TM Assignment forms, HMRC LBTT return forms (Tax for Scotland portal)
- LBTT return (Revenue Scotland)
- HMRC Form 64-8 (agent authorisation)

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
