# UK Commission Agreement, Guidance Note (Scotland)

## Adapting US Commission Plans for Scotland/UK

### Key Legal Framework

| US Concept | UK/Scotland Equivalent |
|---|---|
| State commission statutes (CA, IL, NY, TX) | No direct equivalent, contract law governs; Employment Rights Act 1996 applies to employees |
| Independent contractor (ABC test) | Genuinely self-employed / worker / employee tripartite test |
| Form 1099-NEC | HMRC reporting: Real Time Information (RTI) for employees; Self Assessment for self-employed |
| Non-compete (state-regulated) | Restraint of trade doctrine, must protect legitimate business interest; duration typically 3 to 12 months |
| ESIGN / UETA | Electronic Communications Act 2000 / eIDAS Regulation |
| Governing law (state) | Governing law: Scotland (or England & Wales if cross-border) |
| FTC rule on non-competes | No direct equivalent, common law restraint of trade; Competition Act 1998 |
| AAA Arbitration | Scottish Arbitration Centre / ad hoc arbitration under Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010 |

### Scottish Employment Status

The UK has three tiers of engagement:
1. **Employee** - entitled to full employment rights (minimum wage, holiday, pension auto-enrolment)
2. **Worker** - intermediate category (some rights but less than employee)
3. **Genuinely self-employed (independent contractor)** - no employment rights

Commission-only arrangements are common for self-employed sales agents but carry **misclassification risk** if the degree of control suggests employment.

### Key Drafting Points for Scotland

- **Commission as wages**: For employees, commission is "wages" under Employment Rights Act 1996 - must be paid correctly; unauthorised deductions claims possible
- **Written statement of particulars**: Employees must receive written terms within 2 months (Employment Rights Act 1996 s.1)
- **Working Time Regulations 1998**: Apply minimum rest breaks, holidays (28 days pro-rated)
- **Agency Workers Regulations 2010**: Apply if using agency sales staff
- **Restrictive covenants**: Enforceable only if protecting **legitimate business interest** - wider than US standard; garden leave clauses often used instead
- **Governing law**: If the salesperson works in Scotland, Scottish law should govern and Scottish courts have jurisdiction
- **HMRC IR35**: For personal service companies, off-payroll working rules may apply

### Tax Treatment

| Payment Type | Reporting |
|---|---|
| Commission to employee | PAYE + National Insurance through RTI |
| Commission to self-employed | Self Assessment tax return (Sole Trader) |
| Commission via limited company | IR35 rules may apply; dividends or salary |
| Non-UK resident agent | UK withholding tax considerations |

### Typical UK Commission Structure Clauses

- "Net Sales" definition similar to US, returns, refunds, discounts, Payment timing: typically 30 days after month-end, Dispute resolution: 14 to 28 day dispute window, No "clawback" statutory requirement (contractual)
- Survival of commission post-termination, must be express

### Statutes Applicable in Scotland

- Employment Rights Act 1996
- Working Time Regulations 1998
- Agency Workers Regulations 2010
- Equality Act 2010
- The Conduct of Employment Agencies and Employment Businesses Regulations 2003
- Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010
