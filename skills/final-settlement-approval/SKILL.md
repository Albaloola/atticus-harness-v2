# Note/Minute for Final Approval of Multi-Party Action (Group Proceedings) Settlement

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Produces a court-ready note demonstrating the settlement is fair, reasonable, and adequate under the applicable multi-factor test, with full procedural compliance documentation.

## Workflow

### 1. Instance & Introduction

- Full instance matching court records (court, case number, all parties)
- Title: "Note for the Pursuers in support of Approval of Settlement" or "Minute for the Pursuers craving approval of Settlement"
- Opening paragraph referencing preliminary approval interlocutor (date, court ref #), key settlement benefits, and the crave

### 2. Procedural & Litigation History

Chronological narrative from court process covering:

- Raising of proceedings date, condescendence/pleas-in-law, amendments, class/group allegations, Group certification date, group definition, modifications, Commission and diligence scope (recovery of documents/depositions, specification of documents, expert work)
- Procedural motions (summary decree, preliminary pleas)
- Settlement negotiations (mediation/AEJ sessions, mediator, timeline)
- Preliminary approval (date, conditions, intimation plan)

Purpose: demonstrate informed decision-making and litigation risk awareness.

### 3. Settlement Terms

Extract from the settlement agreement:

- **Monetary relief** - total fund, per-claimant estimates, payment structure
- **Non-monetary relief** - interdict terms, practice changes, compliance monitoring
- **Claims process** - eligibility, deadlines, distribution plan
- **Release scope** - claims discharged, carve-outs, future claims
- **Reversion/cy-près** - unclaimed fund disposition

### 4. Intimation Compliance

Document due process satisfaction under Chapter 26 / Sheriff Court equivalent:

| Method | Metric |
|---|---|
| Direct intimation (recorded delivery/email) | Sent count, delivery rate |
| Newspaper advertisement | Outlets, dates, circulation |
| Digital/social media | Impressions, click-through |
| Settlement website | Unique visitors, page views |
| Free-phone hotline | Call volume |

Report opt-out count/percentage, objection count/substance, claims filed vs. eligible.

### 5. Fairness Analysis

Apply each factor of the jurisdiction's controlling test with process citations:

- Strength of pursuers' case on merits, Complexity, expense, and duration of further litigation, Amount of commission and diligence completed, Stage of proceedings, Risk of establishing liability in delict/breach of duty, Risk of establishing damages, Risk of maintaining group proceedings through proof, Ability of defender to withstand greater decree, Reasonableness relative to best possible recovery, Reaction of the group (opt-out rate, objections, claims rate)

Cite binding Inner House/Outer House or Sheriff Appeal Court precedent for each factor. Add persuasive authority where helpful.

### 6. Judicial Expenses (Fees & Costs)

**Percentage method:** requested percentage and monetary amount; cite comparable Scottish settlements; cross-check with time ledger.

**Time ledger (feeing) method:** total hours × rates = base fee; justify any uplift; cite governing guidance (see Practice Notes on taxation of expenses).

**Outlays:** itemise major categories (experts, commission and diligence, mediation, travel); demonstrate necessity.

[SCOTS: Note] Contingency fees are prohibited in Scotland. Damages-Based Agreements (DBAs) are not available. Any fee arrangement must comply with the Solicitors (Scotland) Act 1980 and Axiom rules. "Loser pays" (judicial expenses) is the default rule.

### 7. Crave (Prayer for Relief)

1. Approve the settlement as fair and reasonable
2. Approve the plan of division (allocation)
3. Sanction the taxation of judicial expenses and outlays including any service payments
4. Grant decree of absolvitor dismissing the action (or as craved)
5. Reserve jurisdiction for enforcement of settlement terms

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **Wrong fairness test** - Outer House, Inner House, and Sheriff Court may vary. Confirm the controlling standard before drafting. [VERIFY]
- **Uncited assertions** - every factual claim needs a process cite (court ref #, production, affidavit para); every legal argument needs binding authority
- **Dismissed objections** - address each objection individually and substantively, never dismissively
- **CAFA notice** - [SCOTS: Note] No UK equivalent to CAFA (Class Action Fairness Act). Cross-border issues may arise where parties are domiciled in England/Wales/NI.
- **Service payments** - not common in Scotland. Cite any authority on disposit of residue. [VERIFY availability]
- **Sheriff Court** - when in Simple Procedure or Ordinary Cause, apply the applicable rules (Chapter 10 of Ordinary Cause Rules or Chapter 3 of Simple Procedure Rules) instead of Chapter 26.
- **Tone** - persuasive but respectful of the court's supervisory role in group proceedings

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

### Jurisdictional Framework

This skill is adapted from US federal class action practice under FRCP 23(e) to Scottish multi-party action (group proceedings) procedure.

- **Governing rules:** Rules of the Court of Session 1994, Chapter 26 (Group Proceedings); Sheriff Court Ordinary Cause Rules for multi-party cases; Simple Procedure Rules for lower-value claims (≤£5,000).
- **No direct FRCP equivalent:** Scottish group proceedings operate under specific Chapter 26 procedures, not a general class action rule. Certification involves a "group certificate" and an opted-in group, rather than US-style opt-out classes.
- **Court hierarchy for appeals:** Sheriff Court → Sheriff Appeal Court → Court of Session (Inner House) → UK Supreme Court (on reserved matters only).

### Key Differences for Drafting

- **No punitive damages** in Scottish delict law. Aggravated damages only in limited circumstances. Damages are compensatory only in group proceedings.
- **"Loser pays"** - judicial expenses follow success, with taxation by the Auditor of the Court.
- **Prescription periods:** 5 years for delict (Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973, s.6); 3 years for personal injury; 20-year long-stop (s.7).
- **Contingency fees prohibited.** DBAs and US-style contingency fee arrangements are not permitted in Scotland. Fee arrangements must comply with the Solicitors (Scotland) Act 1980.
- **Funding:** Third-party litigation funding (TPLF) is increasingly available but unregulated in Scotland. No US-style common fund doctrine.
- **Group opt-in vs opt-out:** Scottish group proceedings are primarily opt-in under Chapter 26. This fundamentally changes claims rate analysis and group size estimation.
- **Intimation:** Scottish procedural rules govern intimation of proceedings and settlement; newspaper intimation and recorded delivery are standard.
- **Devolved vs reserved matters:** Scottish Parliament has competence over civil procedure and most delict law. UK Parliament reserves cross-border matters and financial services regulation. [VERIFY applicable law for multi-jurisdictional cohorts]

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
