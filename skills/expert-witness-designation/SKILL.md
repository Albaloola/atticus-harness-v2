# Expert Witness Designation (Scotland)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a procedurally compliant expert intimation and report that positions the expert's qualifications and opinions clearly while satisfying all Scottish civil procedure requirements.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

- **Case info** - instance and crave (pursuer + defender), case number, court (Court of Session Outer House / Sheriff Court), sheriffdom or division, proof deadline
- **Operative pleadings** - condescendence / defences / answers to map disputed issues the expert addresses
- **Expert details** - full name, address, phone, email, title, current CV, letter of instruction, preliminary report/findings
- **Prior experience** - cases where acted as expert (Law Society of Scotland Code of Practice recommends disclosure of relevant prior appointment)
- **Prior reports** - from other matters for consistency
- **Compensation** - hourly rates by activity type, total paid to date

## Quick Start

1. Confirm governing rules: Court of Session Rules Ch. 27 or Sheriff Court Ordinary Cause Rules Ch. 27A (personal injury) / OCR generally
2. Confirm whether court permission is required to lead expert evidence (required in Court of Session; Sheriff Court may schedule proof without separate permission stage)
3. Identify each discrete opinion the expert will offer
4. Map every qualification to a specific case issue, no generic CV dumps
5. Draft opinion summaries with reliability assessment built in (*Kennedy v. Cordia (Services) LLP* [2016] UKSC 6 framework)
6. Attach or reference expert report per the applicable rule

## Document Structure

### Front Matter

- Instance matching court records (PURSUER: [name]; DEFENDER: [name])
- Title: "INTIMATION OF EXPERT EVIDENCE - [EXPERT NAME]" or "EXPERT REPORT OF [EXPERT NAME]"
- Governing rule citation: "Chapter [27/27A] of the Rules of the Court of Session / Ordinary Cause Rules"
- Procedural context: intimation in process, served with [number] days notice, lodging for proof

### Expert Identification

- Full name, address, phone, email, Precise speciality tied to case issues
  - Good: "Consultant forensic pathologist specialising in biomechanics of traumatic injury"
  - Bad: "Medical expert"
- Current professional role, employing institution or private practice

### Qualifications

Present under the *Kennedy v. Cordia* framework (admissibility of expert opinion):

- **Education** - degrees, institutions, honours, relevant fellowships/certifications
- **Experience** - positions relevant to disputed issues; quantify where possible ("has performed over 500 of the procedure at issue")
- **Credentials** - professional body memberships (e.g. Royal College of Physicians of Edinburgh), General Medical Council / relevant regulatory registration, practising status
- **Publications** - peer-reviewed articles on case-relevant topics, textbooks, treatises, editorial positions
- **Prior experience as expert** - relevant cases, courts, issues; note which party instructed; address any judicial criticism proactively

### Opinion Summary

For each opinion:

1. **Conclusion** - definitive statement addressing a claim element or disputed fact
2. **Methodology** - analytical framework, testing protocols, standards applied, peer-reviewed basis
3. **Data reviewed** - specific documents, productions (list by production number), pleadings, records
4. **Reasoning** - logical chain from data + methodology to conclusion
5. **Reliability** - *Kennedy v. Cordia* factors: (i) evidence must be necessary to assist the court; (ii) expert must be qualified; (iii) evidence must be reliable, based on logical reasoning from established facts, not speculative; (iv) expert must be impartial and independent
6. **Limitations** - assumptions, rejected alternatives, qualifications

Use technically accurate language accessible to the court.

### Expert Report Requirements

Confirm the report includes (*Kennedy v. Cordia* [2016] UKSC 6; Law Society of Scotland Expert Witness Code of Practice):

- [ ] Expert's qualifications and relevant experience
- [ ] List of instructions received and materials provided
- [ ] Summary of factual matters relied on
- [ ] Opinion expressed with reasoning
- [ ] Sources, literature, or research relied on
- [ ] Any matters that may affect the reliability of the opinion
- [ ] Statement of impartiality, acknowledging duty to court
- [ ] Signed and dated

**Note**: No formal statement of truth is required for Scottish civil proceedings.

### Compensation Disclosure

- Rates by activity (consultation, document review, report writing, attendance at proof, precognition)
- Total paid to date and estimated total through proof, Non-standard arrangements (flat fees, retainers, expenses)
- Note: disclosure of funding arrangements follows Kennedy v. Cordia, full disclosure of the basis of the expert's remuneration is expected

### Closing

- Duty to update acknowledged (if new information materially affects opinion)
- Expert's signed declaration of impartiality, duty to the court overrides duty to instructing party, Solicitor's signature block with firm name and practising certificate number, Execution of service note

## Pitfalls

- **Generic qualifications** - every credential must connect to a specific case issue
- **Untraceable opinions** - each opinion must cite specific case productions and records, not generalities
- ***Kennedy v. Cordia* vulnerability** - build reliability, necessity, impartiality, and qualification arguments into the report itself
- **Bias exposure** - address repeat retention, publication positions, and any personal/professional connections proactively
- **Jurisdiction mismatch** - confirm Court of Session or Sheriff Court rules; Ordinary Cause Rules differ from Simple Procedure rules
- **Single joint expert** - Scottish courts encourage parties to agree a single joint expert (Ch. 27 Rule 27A.1); confirm whether SJE or party-appointed
- **Simple Procedure** - different rules apply to claims under £5,000; expert evidence is restricted; check the claim track
- **Civil recovery / personal injury** - specific rules under Ch. 27A for personal injury cases; clinical guidelines and rehabilitation code considerations

## Scotland / UK Adaptation

This section documents how the US-origin expert witness designation skill has been adapted for Scotland/UK.

### Key Scots Law Considerations
1. ***Kennedy v. Cordia (Services) LLP*** [2016] UKSC 6 - the leading Scottish case on expert evidence. Sets out a four-part admissibility test: (1) the evidence must be necessary to assist the court (not merely provide a view); (2) the expert must be qualified in the relevant field; (3) the evidence must be reliable (logical reasoning from established facts, not speculation or assumption); (4) the expert must be impartial, their duty to the court overrides any duty to the instructing party
2. **No Daubert hearings** - Scotland does not have pre-trial admissibility hearings for expert evidence. Reliability is tested at proof (trial), though the court may exclude unreliable evidence
3. **Single Joint Expert** - Chapter 27A rules encourage parties to agree a single joint expert in personal injury cases. If SJE is appointed, the report is commissioned jointly and each party may put additional written questions
4. **Impartiality** - The expert's paramount duty is to the court, not the party instructing them. This must be stated in the report
5. **No requirement for 4/10-year list of prior testimony** - Scottish rules do not prescribe a specific look-back period for prior testimony. Best practice is to disclose relevant recent experience and any judicial criticism
6. **Productions** - Scottish procedure uses "productions" (documents lodged in process) rather than "exhibits". Expert reports are lodged as productions and intimated to the other side
7. **Proof (not trial)** - Civil cases in Scotland are decided at "proof" (pronounced to rhyme with "roof"), not trial. Expert evidence is led at proof
8. **Precognition** - Unlike US depositions, expert evidence is not taken on deposition as a matter of course. The expert prepares a written report which stands as their evidence-in-chief, with cross-examination at proof
9. **Privilege** - Communications with expert witnesses are confidential/factual information only; the expert's report and opinion are disclosable. Legal advice privilege does not generally protect expert reports
10. **Simple Procedure** - For claims under £5,000 in the Sheriff Court, expert evidence is significantly restricted. Expert reports require the court's permission and are typically only allowed where proportionate

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
