Drafts a comprehensive life insurance trust that holds life insurance proceeds outside the settlor's estate for Inheritance Tax (IHT) purposes under UK law, using Scottish trust law.

## Prerequisites

Collect before drafting:

1. **Settlor** - full legal name, domicile (Scottish/UK for IHT purposes)
2. **Policy info** - insurer, policy number(s), sum assured, life assured, current ownership, term vs. whole of life
3. **Trustees** - initial trustee (NOT settlor if they wish to avoid Gifts with Reservation of Benefit), successor(s), corporate trustee if any
4. **Beneficiaries** - primary and contingent with names, relationships; minor beneficiary trust provisions
5. **Funding plan** - trust-applied new policy vs. existing policy transfer
6. **Jurisdiction** - Scotland governs trust (Trusts (Scotland) Acts); execution formalities under Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995
7. **Existing estate plan** - coordination with will, other trusts, IHT nil rate band usage

## Drafting Workflow

### Article I, Declaration of Trust

- State irrevocability explicitly, no amendment, modification, or revocation by settlor, Purpose: hold life insurance outside settlor's estate for IHT purposes, Settlor surrenders all beneficial interest in the policy, Reference Inheritance Tax Act 1984 ss.3A (gifts) and s.43 (settled property)

### Article II, Parties

- Settlor and trustees: full legal names, addresses, fiduciary acceptance, Succession: resignation procedure, removal, appointment of new trustees per Trusts (Scotland) Act 1921
- **Settlor must NOT retain benefit** - any benefit retained triggers Gifts with Reservation of Benefit (GWR) rules (FA 1986 ss.102-102A)

### Article III, Trust Property & Policy Transfer

- Schedule A: each policy with insurer, number, sum assured, life assured, Assignation (Scottish term for assignment) of all ownership rights to trustees, Procedure for future policy additions
- **Seven-year rule**: gifts into trust may be Potentially Exempt Transfers (PETs) - if settlor dies within 7 years, IHT may apply. Existing policy transfers trigger this.
- New policies: trust applies and owns the policy from inception (clearer IHT treatment)

### Article IV, Distribution & Dispositive Provisions

**Discretionary trust model:**

| Parameter | Standard |
|---|---|
| Trust type | Discretionary / Flexible trust |
| Default distribution | Trustee discretion over income and capital |
| Beneficiary class | Settlor's spouse, children, grandchildren, remoter issue |
| Default beneficiaries | On failure of discretion: equally among children |

### Article V, Trustee Powers & Duties

Grant powers to:
- Pay premiums when due, Exercise policy rights (loans, conversions, surrenders, beneficiary changes per trust terms)
- Invest non-insurance assets under Trustee Investments Act / prudent investor standards, Hire professionals; pay from trust, Settle claims with insurers

Fiduciary standards: Trusts (Scotland) Acts. Specify compensation, indemnification for good faith, annual accounting to beneficiaries.

### Article VI, Distributions on Death of Life Assured

**Upon death of life assured:**
- Outright distribution vs. continuing sub-trusts per beneficiary class, Per stirpes for predeceased beneficiaries, Survivorship requirement: 30 days, Simultaneous death provision (common law / standard clause)

**Minors/incapacitated:**
- Sub-trust until specified age (e.g., 18, 21, or 25)
- Discretionary power to apply income and capital for minor's benefit

Payouts are tax-free for income tax purposes. No IHT if properly structured (policy in trust from inception, no reservation of benefit).

### Article VII, Spendthrift & Creditor Protection

- No assignation or encumbrance of interests before distribution, Assets not subject to beneficiary creditors, bankruptcy, or divorce, Scottish trust law protects assets held on discretionary trust from beneficiary creditors

### Article VIII, General Provisions

- Governing law: Scotland (Trusts (Scotland) Acts)
- Severability, Notice provisions, trust situs
- **No rule against perpetuities** - Scottish trusts may last indefinitely (perpetuities period abolished in Scotland)

### Article IX, Execution

- Settlor signature, Trustee signature (acceptance of appointment)
- Witnesses - 1 witness sufficient in Scotland (writing required per Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995)
- Notarisation, required for certain property rights; recommended as best practice

## Critical Checks

- **Never allow settlor to retain benefit** - triggers Gifts with Reservation of Benefit rules, bringing proceeds back into the estate for IHT
- **No Crummey powers** - unused in UK practice; no requirement for contribution notices
- **No IRC § 2042** - IHT treatment depends on trust status, not retention of "incidents of ownership"
- **Seven-year rule** - flag potential chargeable transfers if existing policy is transferred into trust
- **Verify Scottish trust law** - Trusts (Scotland) Act 1921 powers differ from English Trustee Act 2000
- **Coordinate with existing estate plan** - trust beneficiary designations must not conflict with will
- **Nil rate band** - if settlor has other trusts, coordinate NRB utilisation
- **Discretionary trust IHT** - periodic 10-year charges may apply if the trust holds significant value
- **Review periodically** - recommend annual review of policy performance and trust provisions

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from a US ILIT (Irrevocable Life Insurance Trust) for Scottish/UK practice.

### Terminology Changes

| US Term | Scottish/UK Equivalent |
|---|---|
| IRC § 2042 (estate tax) | Inheritance Tax Act 1984 - trust keeps proceeds outside estate |
| IRC § 2503(b) gift tax exclusion | Annual Exemption (£3,000) / Normal Expenditure / Small Gifts |
| Crummey withdrawal rights | Not used in UK practice, trusts use discretionary powers |
| Grantor (ILIT) | Settlor |
| Incidents of ownership | Gifts with Reservation of Benefit (FA 1986 ss.102-102A) |
| GST tax (generation-skipping) | No direct equivalent, discretionary class can include grandchildren |
| Community property | No equivalent in Scotland |
| Rule against perpetuities | Abolished in Scotland, trusts can be perpetual |

### Key Differences

- **No Crummey powers** - Scottish trust law uses discretionary trusts; no requirement for withdrawal notices
- **No gift tax return** - UK uses self-assessment for IHT/gifts; no annual filing threshold (keep records)
- **IHT nil rate band** - currently £325,000 (frozen); residence nil rate band may also apply
- **Seven-year rule** - gifts to trust are Potentially Exempt Transfers (PETs) for IHT
- **No IRC § 101(a)** - life insurance payouts are tax-free for income tax in the UK
- **Discretionary trust charges** - periodic 10-year IHT charges may apply (usually nil or minimal for life insurance trusts)
- **Sole trustee permitted** - Scottish trust law allows a sole trustee (unlike England)

### Recommended UK/Scottish Sources

- Inheritance Tax Act 1984
- Finance Act 1986 (Gifts with Reservation of Benefit)
- Trusts (Scotland) Act 1921 and 1961
- Trusts and Succession (Scotland) Act 2024
- Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995
- HMRC Trusts and Estates Manual, STEP Scotland guidance on life insurance trusts
