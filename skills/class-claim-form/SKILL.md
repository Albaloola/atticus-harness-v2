# Group Member Claim Form, Scottish Group Proceedings

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Produces a court-compliant claim form for Scottish **group proceedings** (opt-in model) enabling group members to submit claims with verifiable eligibility and documentation. This is NOT a US-style opt-out class action.

[SCOTS: Note] This skill adapts the US class action claim form model for Scottish group proceedings under the Civil Litigation (Expenses and Group Proceedings) (Scotland) Act 2018. Critical difference: Scottish group proceedings are **opt-in only** - group members must actively register to be part of the group. There is no US-style opt-out class with absent class members bound by settlement. The Act of Sederunt (Group Proceedings Rules) governs the procedure. The Court of Session has exclusive jurisdiction for group proceedings (no Sheriff Court equivalent).

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

1. Court order granting permission for group proceedings under the 2018 Act
2. Group proceedings order (defining the group, common issues, and opt-in period)
3. Settlement agreement (if settlement has been approved) or distribution scheme
4. Case caption, court (Court of Session), Lord Ordinary, case number
5. Group definition and class period dates (per the court order)
6. Claim categories, eligibility criteria, payment formula/tiers
7. Opt-in deadline (date, time, set by the court order, not the parties)
8. Group proceedings administrator contact (court-appointed or parties' nominee)
9. Documentation requirements and no-doc thresholds
10. Data protection compliance (UK GDPR / DPA 2018) and accessibility requirements

## Workflow

1. **Header and deadline** - "Group Member Claim Form," exact Court of Session case caption, case number, administrator contact, opt-in deadline as set by the court order.
2. **Eligibility summary** - Plain-language group definition referencing the court order and class period.
3. **Claimant identification** - Full legal name (during class period and current), address, email, phone, National Insurance number (or alternative identifier per court order).
4. **Group criteria fields** - Relevant dates, transaction/account IDs, factual nexus to the common issues, tailored to the group definition.
5. **Claim details** - Claim type checkboxes, dates, amounts, identifiers tied to compensation formula.
6. **Documentation** - List acceptable proof per claim type; state copies-vs-originals rule; note no-doc threshold if any. Under Scottish group proceedings, documentary evidence is expected.
7. **Certification and release** - Declaration of truth (perjury/contempt of court basis), confirmation of group membership, opt-in consent to be bound by the outcome. Separate release acknowledgment if settlement. No specific equivalent to Cal. Civ. Code § 1542 - use Scottish general release wording for unknown claims if authorised by the court order.
8. **Signature** - Signature, printed name, date, representative relationship if applicable (e.g., executor, guardian). E-signature per Electronic Communications Act 2000.
9. **Submission instructions** - Registered post, email (per court order), or portal; confirmation process; contact info.
10. **Post-submission** - Review timeline, approval/denial notice; note there is no automatic right of appeal against administrator decisions unless provided in the group proceedings order.

## Required Notices

Every form must include:

- Opting in means the group member is bound by the final decree (whether favourable or not)
- Attorneys' fees and expenses may be deducted from the settlement fund (subject to court approval)
- False statements may lead to contempt of court proceedings or criminal penalties, Privacy and data-use disclosure under UK GDPR / DPA 2018
- No guarantee of payment (settlement or decree subject to court approval)
- Group proceedings operate on an opt-in basis, no claim without active registration, Accessibility and alternative format availability

## Documentation by Claim Type

| Type | Primary Proof | Alternative |
|---|---|---|
| Purchase-based | Receipts, invoices, bank/card statements | Sworn affidavit (if permitted by the order) |
| Account-based | Account statements, confirmations | Administrator verification (if arranged) |
| Employment-based | Pay slips, employment contract, HMRC records | Employer verification |
| Property-based (heritable) | Land Register title sheet, missives | Solicitor's certificate |
| Personal injury | Medical records (with consent), GP reports | Hospital discharge summary / clinical notes |

## Pitfalls and Checks

- **Opt-in only**: Unlike US FRCP 23(b)(3) opt-out class actions, Scottish group proceedings are opt-in. Every group member must actively register. Non-registration = no claim, no binding outcome.
- **Mirror the court order**: The group definition, common issues, and opt-in mechanics are set by the court order granting group proceedings, never deviate from the court's wording.
- **Use plain language**; define any legal terms.
- **Align all language with the court order and group notice**.
- **Never promise payment or specific amounts** unless the decree or settlement order mandates it.
- **Require authority documents** for estates, children, adults with incapacity (AWIA certificate if applicable), or representatives.
- **Avoid collecting full NI numbers** unless the court order or settlement mechanics require them for tax purposes; consider applying for a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA).
- **Ensure ECA 2000 compliance** for electronic submissions (Electronic Communications Act 2000 s. 7).
- **Costs**: Scottish group proceedings operate on a modified "loser pays" rule (parties bear own expenses unless the court orders otherwise, s. 32 of the 2018 Act). The claim form should not create expectations about cost recovery.

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Statutory framework:**
- Civil Litigation (Expenses and Group Proceedings) (Scotland) Act 2018 ss. 28 to 38
- Act of Sederunt (Group Proceedings Rules) (enacted under the 2018 Act)
- Rules of the Court of Session 1994 (as modified by Act of Sederunt)
- Data Protection Act 2018 / UK GDPR, data handling, Electronic Communications Act 2000 - e-signatures, Contempt of Court Act 1981 - false statements

**Key differences from US class action claim forms:**

| US Class Action (FRCP 23) | Scottish Group Proceedings (2018 Act) |
|---|---|
| Opt-out model (b)(3); absent class members bound if they don't opt out | Opt-in only, group members must actively register |
| FRCP 23 applies | Act of Sederunt (Group Proceedings Rules) + 2018 Act |
| Settlement administrator often private company | Claim form may be administered by parties' solicitors, a court-appointed person, or the Accountant of Court |
| Punitive/exemplary damages possible in some states | No punitive damages; limited aggravated damages |
| Contingency fees standard | No contingency fees; success fee agreements permitted (s. 1 to 4 of the 2018 Act) |
| FRCP 23(c)(2)(B) - best practicable notice | Scottish notice procedure set by the court in the group proceedings order |
| CAFA jurisdiction for federal class actions | Court of Session, exclusive jurisdiction for group proceedings |
| SSN collected for tax reporting | NI number (but privacy-protected) |
| Claims processed by private claims administrator | Can be court-supervised via the Accountant of Court |
| State-specific release waiver laws (e.g., Cal. Civ. Code § 1542) | No direct equivalent, Scottish release wording based on common law |

**Limitations of Scottish group proceedings:**
- Only available in the Court of Session (not Sheriff Court)
- Opt-in model significantly constrains group size compared to US opt-out classes, No punitive damages available, Modified "loser pays" costs rule creates different settlement dynamics, Group proceedings are a recent development (2018 Act; first orders made in the early 2020s) - case law is still developing, No counterpart to multidistrict litigation (MDL) in Scotland, Damages-based agreements (DBAs) and speculative fee arrangements are available but heavily regulated

**Practitioner notes:**
- Scottish group proceedings are not yet common, only a handful of cases have received permission, The 2018 Act is specific to Scotland, group proceedings in England & Wales follow the Competition Act 1998 (opt-out collective proceedings in the Competition Appeal Tribunal) or CPR Part 19 (opt-in group litigation orders)
- Cross-border UK group actions may need coordination between the Scottish Court of Session and English High Court, Engage a solicitor with rights of audience in the Court of Session for group proceedings work, The Accountant of Court may supervise the distribution of settlement funds

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
