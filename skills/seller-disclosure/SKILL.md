# Home Report & Property Disclosure, Scotland/UK

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Draft and review Scottish property disclosure documentation that complies with the Housing (Scotland) Act 2006 Home Report requirements, the Property Questionnaire, and the pre-settlement Property Enquiry Questionnaire (PEQ) process, while protecting sellers from post-settlement misrepresentation claims.

> **Important note on caveat emptor:** Scotland retains the principle of *caveat emptor* (buyer beware) in heritable property transactions. There is **no separate US-style Seller Disclosure Statement**. Instead, disclosure is achieved through the mandatory Home Report (Single Survey + Energy Performance Certificate + Property Questionnaire) and the contractual pre-settlement Property Enquiry Questionnaire (PEQ).

## Prerequisites

1. **Property identification** - full address, postcode, UPRN (Unique Property Reference Number), Land Register title number (or Sasine Register search reference)
2. **Seller information** - full legal names of all owners as registered in the Land Register / Sasines
3. **Jurisdiction** - Scotland (primary); separate rules for England & Wales (not covered here)
4. **Available documents** - Home Report (Single Survey, EPC, Property Questionnaire), survey reports, repair invoices, Factor statements, title deeds, building warrant/completion certificates, planning permissions, guarantees
5. **Property age** - if pre-2000, consider asbestos (Control of Asbestos Regulations 2012); radon testing recommended for affected areas (UK Health Security Agency maps)
6. **Lead-based paint** - Not a standalone requirement in Scots law; covered by the repairing standard obligations under the Housing (Scotland) Act 2006

### 3) Structural & Systems, Property Questionnaire Completion

In Scotland, the Property Questionnaire (part of the Home Report) covers the following systems. For each, disclose: **condition to best of seller's knowledge, known defects, age if known, repair history (contractor + date + warranty), and any specialist installers who carried out work**.

| System | Key Disclosure Points |
|---|---|
| Foundation / structure | Movement, settlement, cracks, past repairs + guarantees, underpinning |
| Roof | Age, material, leak history, warranty status, specialist roofer identification |
| Plumbing | Pipe materials (flag lead piping in older properties), leaks, heating system type, boiler age and service history |
| Electrical | Last Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR) date, any known deficiencies |
| Heating / Central heating | Boiler age, fuel type, service history, any grants/Green Homes Scheme involvement |
| Water heating | System type, age, if part of central heating or separate |
| Windows / Glazing | Type (double/triple glazing), installer, FENSA/CERTASS certificate, any guarantees |

Reference the Home Report Single Survey (section references) and any specialist inspection reports by name and date.

### 4) Environmental & Hazardous Disclosures

**Lead-based paint:**
- Not a standalone federal requirement (as in US law). However, if pre-1970s property and lead paint known, it should be disclosed in the Property Questionnaire (section on specialist works/altered property).
- The repairing standard under Housing (Scotland) Act 2006 requires the property to be free from "serious disrepair" - lead paint hazards may engage this.

**Additional environmental items:**

| Hazard | Disclosure Requirements |
|---|---|
| Asbestos | Known location, testing history, abatement/removal records; Control of Asbestos Regulations 2012 (UK-wide) |
| Radon | UK Health Security Agency radon-affected area status; test results or "no testing conducted" statement |
| Flooding | Event history (date, source, damage, insurance claims); SEPA flood risk map status (FRA, Flood Risk Assessment portal) |
| Underground oil / fuel tanks | Status (in use/decommissioned/removed); soil contamination; remediation records |
| Damp / Condensation / Mould | Locations, water damage history, any timber/damp specialist treatment guarantees |
| Contaminated land | SEPA records, former industrial use, planning condition remediation |
| Ground stability | Coal mining (Coal Authority search), historical mining, landfill sites, natural subsidence |
| Japanese knotweed / invasive species | Known presence, treatment history, management plan |
| Unexploded ordnance (UXO) | Known risk, survey history (relevant in specific areas: former industrial/military sites) |

### 5) Legal Encumbrances & Title

- **Servitudes** - purpose, location, practical impact on property use
- **Real burdens** - Title Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003; positive/negative obligations, consents required
- **Boundary disputes** - nature, parties, resolution status
- **Factor / Common areas** - Factor name and contact, annual factoring charges, arrears, pending major works, sinking fund, upcoming special assessments, any pending litigation
- **Tenement Management Scheme** - Title Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003 Sch. 1; default rules for common repairs in tenements/flatted properties
- **Planning permission** - classification (full/outline/prior notification), any non-conforming uses, retrospective applications
- **Building warrants** - issued warrants, completion certificate status; flag unpermitted work explicitly
- **Standard securities** - existing lenders (mortgage holders), redemption status
- **Planning enforcement / breach of condition notices** - current status
- **Use restrictions** - rental restrictions, holiday let restrictions, occupancy limits from title conditions or local planning

### 6) Neighbourhood & Area Conditions

Disclose material facts affecting value or desirability:
- Noise sources (airports, e.g., Glasgow, Edinburgh, Inverness, Aberdeen; railways, motorways, commercial/industrial, wind turbines)
- Proximity to waste facilities, agricultural operations, sewage works, Planned public works (Scottish Government / Local Authority development plans, Section 75 agreements)
- Rights of way, core paths, access disputes, Deaths on property / crimes, no statutory disclosure requirement (unlike some US states), but could be material if specifically asked in PEQ; answer truthfully if known, Any fact a reasonable buyer would consider material, Scots law on misrepresentation in heritable property applies (not *uberrimae fidei*, but responses must not be misleading)

### 7) Certification & Execution

**Seller certification:**
- Truth and correctness attestation (to best of seller's knowledge and belief)
- Duty to update if material facts arise before settlement (missives concluded)
- Home Report / Property Questionnaire is provided for information only; does not substitute for independent buyer surveys and searches

**Signature block:**
- Signature line for every owner (printed name, signature, date)
- Where Property Questionnaire is signed (as part of Home Report)
- PEQ signed and returned through seller's solicitor to buyer's solicitor

**Delivery:**
- Home Report must be available **before** property is marketed (s.98 Housing (Scotland) Act 2006)
- Property Questionnaire is completed at point of marketing, PEQ is issued by buyer's solicitor **after conclusion of missives** - no statutory deadline; covered by missive terms, Note: late delivery does not give same rescission rights as US state statutes; buyer remedies are under contract and misrepresentation rules

## Guidelines

- **Identify the governing framework first** - Housing (Scotland) Act 2006 sets the Home Report requirements; caveat emptor operates unless the seller makes misrepresentations
- **"Unknown" is valid** - clearly distinguish "not present," "unknown," and "not inspected"; never fabricate conditions
- **The Property Questionnaire is completed by the seller based on actual knowledge** - there is no duty to investigate, but answers must not be misleading
- **Over-disclose in the PEQ** - undisclosed material defects could ground a claim for misrepresentation
- **Ongoing duty** - remind sellers that if material facts change between Home Report preparation and settlement, the buyer should be informed
- **Never advise concealment** - misrepresentation in heritable property transactions can lead to rescission and damages; ethics rules apply
- **Verify all statutory references against current law** - Housing (Scotland) Act 2006, Town and Country Planning (Scotland) Act 1997, Title Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003, Building (Scotland) Act 2003 [VERIFY]
- **The Home Report remains valid for re-marketing** - new report required if gap exceeds 30 days

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Adapted for:** Scotland and UK law

**Changes made:**
- Replaced "Seller Disclosure Statement" with Scotland's mandatory Home Report (Housing (Scotland) Act 2006, Part 3)
- Replaced all US property terminology: Real Property → Heritable property; Personal Property → Moveable property; Easement → Servitude; Zoning → Planning permission; Title Insurance → not directly applicable (Land Register / Registers of Scotland searches used instead); Closing → Settlement/Completion; HOA → Factor/Common Repair Management, Replaced US court/legal terminology: Injunction → Interdict; Tort → Delict; Discovery → Commission and Diligence; Class Action → Group Proceedings (Civil Litigation (Expenses and Group Proceedings) (Scotland) Act 2018); Summary Judgment → Summary Decree; Punitive Damages → no equivalent, Replaced US agencies: EPA → SEPA (Scottish Environment Protection Agency); HUD → no direct equivalent (Scottish Government Housing Directorate); FTC → CMA / Trading Standards Scotland; FEMA → SEPA flood maps; IRS → HMRC, Replaced US statutes: state disclosure statutes → Housing (Scotland) Act 2006; Town and Country Planning (Scotland) Act 1997; Title Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003; Building (Scotland) Act 2003; Control of Asbestos Regulations 2012 (UK-wide)
- Replaced federal lead-based paint requirements (42 U.S.C. § 4852d) → covered by Housing (Scotland) Act 2006 repairing standard, no separate disclosure form required, Replaced APN → UPRN / postcode, Replaced state-specific variations → note on devolved competence (Scottish Parliament for housing/planning; UK Parliament for reserved matters)
- Replaced dollar amounts (£) - fines referenced to Scottish Government standard scale (Level 3 = currently £1,000 for Home Report non-compliance)
- Replaced USA-centric inspection-right/10-day lead-paint right → buyer's right to commission independent surveys and searches (standard practice; no statutory inspection period)
- Replaced county recorder → Registers of Scotland (Land Register of Scotland / Sasine Register)
- Replaced mortgage/deed of trust → Standard Security / Heritable Security, Removed "pre-1978 lead-based paint trigger" as standalone item; retained under environmental disclosures as practical guidance

**Key Scottish/UK considerations:**
- Scotland has **no separate Seller Disclosure Statement** - the legal framework is fundamentally different from the US. Disclosure occurs through: (a) the mandatory Home Report (Single Survey + EPC + Property Questionnaire) required before marketing under the Housing (Scotland) Act 2006; and (b) the Property Enquiry Questionnaire (PEQ) exchanged after conclusion of missives (contract formed)
- **Caveat emptor** (buyer beware) is the governing principle. The seller is not obliged to volunteer information beyond the Property Questionnaire and Home Report unless specifically asked. However, actively misleading answers in the PEQ or Property Questionnaire may constitute **misrepresentation**
- **No lead-based paint disclosure form** exists. Lead paint hazards are addressed through general property condition disclosure and the repairing standard
- **No punitive damages** in Scots law (limited aggravated damages available)
- **No class actions** - group proceedings introduced by the Civil Litigation (Expenses and Group Proceedings) (Scotland) Act 2018 (opt-in, limited scope)
- **UK Supreme Court** remains the final civil appeal court from the Inner House of the Court of Session
- **Council Tax band** replaces US property tax assessment
- **Land Register of Scotland** (1981+) or **Sasine Register** (pre-1981) - both maintained by Registers of Scotland
- **Law Society of Scotland** publishes standard PEQ forms used in practice
- **Property factors** are regulated by the Property Factors (Scotland) Act 2011 and must be registered with the Scottish Government
- **Energy Performance Certificate (EPC)** is a mandatory component of the Home Report, valid for 10 years, lodged on the Scottish EPC Register
- **Settlement/Completion** involves the buyer's solicitor lodging the disposition (deed of transfer) and discharging standard securities. Land and Buildings Transaction Tax (LBTT) is payable to Revenue Scotland
