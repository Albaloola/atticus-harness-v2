# Application for Variation of Family Law Order (Scotland)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

Drafts a post-decree application to modify an existing family law court order based on material changes in circumstances under Scots law.

## Prerequisites

Collect before drafting:

1. **Original order** - full text (interlocutor or decree), date of grant, issuing court, provisions at issue
2. **Changed-circumstances evidence** - financial, medical, employment, school, or incident records
3. **Baseline declarations** - prior financial disclosures and conditions at time of original order
4. **Jurisdiction** - Sheriff Court (most family law) or Court of Session; applicable statutory provisions, local court rules
5. **Modification history** - any prior variation attempts and outcomes; prior minutes of variation

## Quick Start

1. Identify variation type and governing legal standard (statutory provisions under Family Law (Scotland) Act 1985 or Children (Scotland) Act 1995)
2. Verify statutory authority, burden of proof, and procedural prerequisites
3. Draft application with structured changed-circumstances argument
4. Prepare supporting affidavit and inventory of productions
5. Run review checklist before finalising

## Workflow

### Step 1: Jurisdiction & Standard Research

Identify the governing standard by variation type:

| Type | Standard | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Child periodical allowance / aliment | Material change in circumstances | Family Law (Scotland) Act 1985, s 5; Child Support Act 1991 (for CMS) |
| Spousal periodical allowance | Change of circumstances (s 12(2)(b)); cohabitation; remarriage | Family Law (Scotland) Act 1985, s 12 to 13 |
| Residence / contact (parental responsibilities) | Best interests of the child + material change | Children (Scotland) Act 1995, s 11; s 11(7)(a to d) checklist |
| Financial provision on divorce | Variation restricted (s 12(1)); capital orders final; pension sharing can be varied in limited circumstances | Family Law (Scotland) Act 1985, s 12(1) to (2) |

Verify:
- [ ] Statutory authority for this variation type (Family Law (Scotland) Act 1985, Children (Scotland) Act 1995, Child Support Act 1991)
- [ ] Burden of proof (applicant bears burden for material change)
- [ ] Threshold showing required before evidentiary hearing (affidavit evidence)
- [ ] Options for agreement, minutes of agreement registered in the Books of Council and Session
- [ ] Mediation or FLS (Relationships Scotland) alternative dispute resolution

### Step 2: Instance & Formatting

- [ ] Full court name - "[Sheriffdom] Sheriff Court" / "Court of Session"
- [ ] Exact case number
- [ ] Party designations (Pursuer/Defender or Applicant/Respondent)
- [ ] Document title per local rules ("Minute for Variation," "Application to Vary," "Initial Writ (Variation)")
- [ ] Act of Sederunt formatting rules (font, margins, spacing, page numbers)
- [ ] Required companion documents (proposed order, financial statement, affidavit, inventory of productions)

### Step 3: Draft the Application

Structure the application as follows:

**1. INTRODUCTION** - Applicant identity, original order (interlocutor title, date, court), provisions to vary (cite paragraph/section numbers of decree), summary of variation sought, one-sentence changed-circumstances statement.

**2. PROCEDURAL HISTORY** - Timeline from original decree through present application. Note prior variations or attempts and distinguish current facts. Address compliance history with the order.

**3. STATEMENT OF FACTS** - Chronological narrative:
- Baseline conditions at time of original order, Specific changes with dates, quantified data, concrete details, Each fact tied to admissible evidence (cite production number)
- Address foreseeability, why changes were unanticipated, Preempt counterarguments (temporary vs. enduring; foreseeable vs. new)

**4. LEGAL ARGUMENT** - Cite governing statutory provision with quoted language. State elements the court must consider. Apply each element to specific facts. Cite controlling appellate authority (Scottish civil cases, Court of Session / Sheriff Appeal Court). Distinguish unfavourable authority if applicable. Mark unverified citations `[VERIFY]`.

**5. RELIEF SOUGHT (CRAVE)** - Specific variations in adoptable order language:
- Dollar amounts (GBP), effective dates, payment arrangements, Detailed contact/residence schedules (days, times, holidays, school vacations)
- Step-down or sunset provisions if applicable, Alternative relief if primary request denied
- "Such other and further relief as the Court thinks fit"

**6. CONCLUSION** - Restate each variation concisely. Prayer (crave) for relief.

### Step 4: Supporting Documents

**Applicant Affidavit:**
- First person, paragraph-per-fact structure, Establish personal knowledge for each assertion, Sworn or affirmed before a notary public / justice of the peace, Authenticate all referenced productions, Quantified data and specific incidents, no conclusory statements

**Production Organisation:** Sequential numbering (1, 2, 3...). Each production referenced by authenticating paragraph in the affidavit.

| No. | Description | Authenticating ¶ |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Original decree / interlocutor | Aff. ¶ __ |
| 2 | Financial records / payslips / tax returns | Aff. ¶ __ |
| 3 | Medical / school / incident records | Aff. ¶ __ |

## Pitfalls

- **Accuracy over advocacy** - every assertion must be verifiable from the record; credibility is dispositive in discretionary rulings
- **Quantify everything** - dollar amounts in GBP, dates, percentages, schedule times; never use vague language
- **Distinguish prior attempts** - if earlier variations were denied, explicitly differentiate current facts
- **No settlement references** - do not reference without-prejudice settlement discussions or mediation offers (without prejudice privilege)
- **Child-focused framing** - for residence/contact variation, anchor every argument to the child's welfare and best interests (Children (Scotland) Act 1995, s 11(7)), not parental preference
- **Local rule compliance** - rules vary between Sheriff Courts and Court of Session; always verify required forms, fees, and hearing procedures
- **Mark unverified citations** - use [VERIFY] for any citation not confirmed against source material
- **Capital orders vs. periodical allowances** - capital orders (financial provision on divorce) are generally final and cannot be varied; only periodical allowances can be varied under s 12(1) to (2) Family Law (Scotland) Act 1985
- **Child Support Agency / CMS** - the Child Maintenance Service (CMS) handles child maintenance for non-resident parents; variation of CMS assessments goes through CMS, not the court (unless opting out by agreement)

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been converted from a US family law order modification skill to Scottish family law. Key differences:

- **No FRCP 60(b):** US post-judgment modification under FRCP 60(b) has no direct Scottish equivalent. Variation applications are made by Minute in existing proceedings or by Initial Writ if the original decree was in the Court of Session.
- **Family Law (Scotland) Act 1985:** This is the primary statute for financial provision on divorce and variation of periodical allowances. Capital awards are generally final.
- **Children (Scotland) Act 1995:** Governs parental responsibilities and rights (residence, contact, specific issue). The court's paramount consideration is the welfare of the child (s 11(7)). No "best interests" test per se, welfare is the paramount consideration.
- **No "custody/visitation":** Scots law uses "residence" (where the child lives) and "contact" (time with the non-resident parent). No "visitation" concept; parental responsibilities are shared in principle.
- **No "spousal support":** Scots law uses "periodical allowance" (s 12 Family Law (Scotland) Act 1985). Awards are typically limited-term, not indefinite. Remarriage ends the obligation; cohabitation may justify variation.
- **No "child support guidelines":** Child maintenance is primarily through the Child Maintenance Service (formula-based via CMS) or by court-ordered aliment (Family Law (Scotland) Act 1985, s 1 to 5).
- **Sheriff Court jurisdiction:** Most family law variation applications are in the Sheriff Court (not a state family court). Court of Session is for complex or high-value cases.
- **No state-specific variation:** Scotland has a single family law regime; no variation between separate state statutes.
- **Mediation emphasis:** The F9 Form requires certification that parties have considered mediation (Relationships Scotland / family mediation); not mandatory but strongly encouraged.
- **No "FRCP" references:** Scottish civil procedure is governed by the Act of Sederunt (Sheriff Court Ordinary Cause Rules 1993, Chap. 33 for family actions; Court of Session Rules Chap. 49).
- **No punitive or contempt-based enforcement:** Enforcement of family orders in Scotland is typically through diligence (arrestment, earnings arrestment) or application to the court for specific implement.
- **Proof vs. trial:** Family law variation is heard by way of Proof (evidential hearing), not jury trial.
- **Citation style:** US Bluebook replaced by OSCOLA / Edinburgh Legal Style; US case law replaced by Scottish Court of Session and Sheriff Appeal Court decisions.
- **US dollar → GBP:** All amounts expressed in pounds sterling (£).

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
