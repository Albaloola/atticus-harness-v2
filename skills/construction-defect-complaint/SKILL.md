# Construction Defect Initial Writ / Summons (Scotland)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a litigation-ready construction defect initial writ (Sheriff Court) or summons (Court of Session) that avers defect facts, satisfies pre-action protocol prerequisites, and states a relevant and specific case.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

1. **Property**: address, title number, ownership chain, acquisition date, description
2. **Project docs**: building contract, plans/specifications, variations, completion certificates, warranties
3. **Defect evidence**: photos, inspections (diligence), expert reports, repair estimates
4. **Communications**: pre-action correspondence, inspection access requests, responses
5. **Pre-action requirements**: any relevant pre-action protocol (Construction Pre-Action Protocol if applicable); notice requirements
6. **Prescription/limitation**: prescriptive period (5 years for delict; 20-year long-stop; personal injury 3 years), date of discovery
7. **Defenders**: entity names, roles, registered office, company number, contract chain to pursuer

## Initial Writ / Summons Structure

Draft sections in this order:

1. **Instance** - court (Sheriff Court / Court of Session), parties, case number
2. **Parties** - pursuer's title and interest; each defender's role and contract chain
3. **Jurisdiction** - basis of jurisdiction (domicile, place of performance, delict)
4. **Background** - project scope, dates, contract price, limited warranties (condescendence)
5. **Defect Averments** - system-by-system using template below (plead with specification)
6. **Pre-Action Compliance** - correspondence dates, protocol steps, access
7. **Damages** - repair costs, loss, consequential loss (attach schedules of loss)
8. **Pleas-in-Law** - separate pleas with legal basis
9. **Crave** - remedy sought (payment, specific implement, declarator)
10. **Signature** - solicitor's name and address (initial writ); counsel's signature (summons)

## Defect Averment Template

Repeat for each defect system or location. In Scottish pleading, **specific averments** are required, no general or blanket allegations:

```
Defect: [Component/system]
Defender(s): [Entity + trade role]
Condescendence: [The pursuer avers that the [component] at [location] is defective in that [specific factual description of defect]
Date of discovery: [Month/Year]
Manifestation: [Water ingress, cracking, failure pattern]
Cause: [Defective installation / materials / design]
Damage caused: [Consequential damage description]
Supporting evidence: [Expert report reference, photo schedule, quotation]
```

## Causes of Action & Pleas-in-Law Checklist

Include only claims supported by facts:

- **Breach of Contract** - contract + term + breach + loss + causation (condescendence + plea)
- **Breach of Warranty** - express warranty terms and limited warranty provisions + breach
- **Negligence (Delict)** - duty of care + breach of standard of care (reasonable skill and care) + causation + loss (Donoghue v Stevenson [1932] AC 562 basis)
- **Breach of statutory duty** - Building (Scotland) Act 2003 / Building Standards (Scotland) Regulations / other relevant statutory provisions
- **Misrepresentation** - pre-contract statement + inaccuracy + inducement (state with specification; note the Contract (Scotland) Act 1997 provisions for unilateral error)
- **Adjudication** - in the alternative, may seek adjudication under the Housing Grants, Construction and Regeneration Act 1996

## Damages Checklist

- Repair and remediation costs (itemised schedule)
- Consequential losses (loss of use, alternative accommodation, storage)
- Diminution in value (if not fully repairable)
- Interest on sums due (judicial interest, Court of Session / Sheriff Court interest)
- Expenses (judicial expenses, loser pays, subject to judicial discretion)
- Note: No punitive damages in Scots law

## Pitfalls

- **Pre-action protocol is not jurisdictional but expected**: Scottish courts expect pre-action correspondence and compliance with the Pre-Action Protocol for Construction and Engineering Disputes (where applicable). Failure may affect expenses.
- **No group pleading** - Scottish pleading requires specific averments against each defender. General or blanket averments risk being struck out as irrelevant.
- **Prescription**: Delictual claims prescribe after 5 years from the date the obligation became enforceable (Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973, s 6). Personal injury: 3 years. 20-year long-stop applies. The pursuer must plead the date of discovery (knowledge of the defect).
- **Economic loss in delict generally not recoverable**: Pure economic loss for defective buildings is not recoverable in delict against contractors under Scottish case law (unless there is a special relationship akin to contract, consider *Junior Books* exception, authority limited).
- **Building Standards**: Building (Scotland) Act 2003 and Building Standards (Scotland) Regulations apply, not the International Building Code (IBC).
- **Expert evidence**: Expert reports are expected. The pursuer must disclose reports timeously. Consider the rules on expert witnesses in Scottish civil procedure.
- **Fraud must be pled with specification**: Heightened pleading standard for fraud/misrepresentation.
- **Jury trial**: Construction defect cases are not jury trials; they are proof before a judge or Lord Ordinary.

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

- **Court system**: Construction defect cases are raised in the Sheriff Court (Ordinary Cause for claims over £5,000) or the Court of Session (Outer House) for high-value/complex cases.
- **Terminology**: "Pursuer" (plaintiff), "Defender" (defendant), "Initial Writ" (Sheriff Court), "Summons" (Court of Session), "Condescendence" (statement of facts), "Pleas-in-Law" (legal grounds), "Crave" (remedy sought), "Proof" (trial).
- **No pre-suit notice statutes (US Right to Repair)**: Scotland has no equivalent to US Right to Repair/NOR statutes. However, the Pre-Action Protocol for Construction and Engineering Disputes (Scottish version) encourages pre-action correspondence and early expert disclosure.
- **Building regulations**: Building (Scotland) Act 2003 and Building Standards (Scotland) Regulations (mandatory standards) replace US IBC and state building codes.
- **Prescription**: 5-year prescriptive period (delict/negligence) under the Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973. 20-year long-stop.
- **Collateral warranties**: Common in Scottish commercial construction, providing a contractual route for subsequent owners and funders to sue contractors/consultants.
- **Adjudication**: The Housing Grants, Construction and Regeneration Act 1996 applies in Scotland, providing a statutory right to adjudication for construction disputes. This is a quicker alternative to court proceedings.
- **Expenses**: The unsuccessful party generally pays the successful party's judicial expenses (loser pays, opposite of US).

[SCOTS: Note] The core methodology, defect identification, element-based pleading, causation, damages, transfers well. The significant differences are: (a) Scottish pleading (condescendence, pleas-in-law, crave) rather than US complaint structure (caption, parties, jurisdiction, counts, prayer); (b) 5-year delictual prescription period vs US statute of limitations; (c) no US-style Right to Repair statutes; (d) Building (Scotland) Act 2003 rather than IBC; (e) adjudication alternative available; (f) no punitive damages; (g) loser-pays expenses. The skill preserves the defect-specific methodology while replacing all US procedural content.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
