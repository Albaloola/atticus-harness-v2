# Scotland/UK Forms Reference, Preservation Letters

## Key UK Legislation

- Civil Evidence (Scotland) Act 1988
- Administration of Justice (Scotland) Act 1972 (s.1 - commission and diligence for recovery of documents)
- Data Protection Act 2018 / UK GDPR, Human Rights Act 1998 (Art. 6 ECHR, fair trial implications)
- Legal Profession and Legal Aid (Scotland) Act 2007

## Key Court Rules

- **Court of Session Rules 1994**: Chapter 35 (Commission and Diligence for Recovery of Documents); Chapter 43 (Commercial Actions, Practice Note on electronic disclosure)
- **Sheriff Court Ordinary Cause Rules 1993**: Chapter 28 (Recovery of Documents)
- **Practice Note No. 1 of 2020** - Electronic Disclosure in Commercial Actions (Court of Session)

## Key Scottish Authorities on Spoliation/Adverse Inferences

- *McGlinn v. Clark* 1993 - duty to preserve evidence recognised
- *Sinclair v. Bamber* 1954 - adverse inference from non-production
- *Walsh v. Heritable & General* 1982 - duty to preserve relevant documents
- *Dingley v. Chief Constable, Strathclyde Police* 2000 - inference drawing from missing evidence

## Comparison: US Spoliation → Scottish Equivalent

| US Concept | Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Independent tort of spoliation | Not recognised |
| Adverse inference jury instruction | Adverse inference in proof (judge-directed or submissions) |
| Monetary sanctions | Expenses sanction (party/party costs against offending party) |
| Default judgment | Decree in default |
| Discovery sanctions | Failure to comply with commission and diligence order |
| Punitive damages | Not available |
| FRE 502 (privilege waiver) | Legal professional privilege (Scots rules) |
| FRCP 26(g) certification | No direct equivalent |
| EDRM model | Practice Note 1/2020 guidance |

- **Faculty of Advocates** - regulatory body for Scottish advocates
