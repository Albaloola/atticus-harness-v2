# Scottish Appellate Procedure, Reclaiming Motions & Appeals Guidance

## No "En Banc" Procedure in Scottish Courts

### Key Difference from US Federal System

The US federal **en banc** rehearing (FRAP 35) - where the full circuit court reviews a panel decision - **does not exist** in Scotland. The Scottish procedure closest in spirit is:

### Reclaiming Motion (Inner House, Court of Session)

A **reclaiming motion** is the equivalent of an appeal from the Outer House (first instance) to the Inner House (appellate division) of the Court of Session.

**Key Features:**
- Governed by **Rules of the Court of Session (RCS), Chapter 38**
- Heard by a bench of **three Inner House judges** (not the full court)
- No mechanism for the full Inner House to rehear a 3-judge decision, One reclaiming motion only (no second-tier rehearing)
- The "whole court" (5 judges) may sit on exceptionally important points, but this is **discretionary, rare**, and not analogous to en banc

### Comparison Table

| US (FRAP 35) | Scotland |
|---|---|
| En banc rehearing | No equivalent, reclaiming motion to Inner House (3 judges) |
| Petition for Rehearing | Reclaiming motion (note: this is the first appeal, not rehearing) |
| Panel opinion → en banc application | Outer House decision → reclaiming motion |
| Circuit split | No "circuit" system, single Court of Session |
| 14-day deadline (FRAP 35(c)) | 21 days for lodging reclaiming motion (RCS 38.4) |
| 3,900 word limit | No word limit, but Note of Argument typically 5 to 20 pages |
| Certificates, Compliance, Service | Same, must certify intimation/service |
| Exceptional importance | "Whole court" (5+ judges) may be convened for important cases |
| Rehearing by full active judges | 3 judges usual; 5+ for exceptional constitutional points |

### Scottish Appellate Routes

**In civil matters:**
1. **Sheriff Court** (first instance) → **Sheriff Appeal Court** (civil appeals from Sheriff Court) 
2. **Outer House, Court of Session** (first instance) → **Inner House** (reclaiming motion)
3. **Sheriff Appeal Court or Inner House** → **UK Supreme Court** (permission required)

**In criminal matters:**
1. **Sheriff Court / High Court** → **High Court of Justiciary (Appeal Court)**
2. No appeal to UK Supreme Court except on devolution/compatibility issues

### Drafting a Reclaiming Motion / Note of Argument

**Document:**
- **Reclaiming Motion** (lodged with the court) - brief notice of intention to reclaim
- **Note of Argument** - the substantive legal submission

**Structure of Note of Argument:**
1. **Introduction** - nature of the action, the interlocutor (order) reclaimed against
2. **Background** - concise procedural history and facts
3. **Grounds of Appeal** - numbered grounds with legal submissions:
   - Error of law / misdirection
   - Procedural irregularity
   - Unreasonable exercise of discretion
   - New evidence (limited circumstances)
4. **Submissions** - statutory provisions, case law (Scottish, UK Supreme Court, ECHR)
5. **Disposal Sought** - allow reclaiming motion, recall the interlocutor, grant the remedy sought
6. **Certificates** - service, compliance with rules, deadline met

**Deadline:** 21 days from the date of the interlocutor being reclaimed against (RCS 38.4)
**Extension:** Possible by motion before expiry (not after)

### "Whole Court" Hearing (5+ Judges)

The Lord President may direct that a reclaiming motion be heard by a larger court (RCS 38.15):
- Typically used for cases of exceptional public importance, Or where the court is asked to depart from a prior Inner House decision, Still not "en banc" in the US sense, no plenary rehearing

### Key Differences from FRAP 35 Practice

| Element | FRAP 35 (US) | Scottish Reclaiming Motion |
|---|---|---|
| Who requests | Losing party petitions | Losing party lodges reclaiming motion |
| Grant standard | Conflict or exceptional importance | Leave not required for first reclaiming motion |
| Must show panel error | Not required | Yes, this IS the appeal on merits |
| Deadline | 14 days (jurisdictional, non-extendable) | 21 days (may be extended by motion before expiry) |
| Success rate | <1% | Variable, this is the standard appeal route |
| Effect if granted | Panel decision vacated; full court rehears | Court allows appeal, recalls interlocutor, substitutes its own decision |

### Practical Note

For Scottish legal work, the closest skills to "en banc petition" are:
1. **Reclaiming motion practice** - for appealing an Outer House decision
2. **Appeal to Sheriff Appeal Court** - for appealing a Sheriff Court decision
3. **Appeal to UK Supreme Court** - from Inner House or Sheriff Appeal Court (permission required)
4. **Review of an Inner House decision** - limited; only by application to the court or to UKSC
