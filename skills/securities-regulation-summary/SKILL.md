# Securities / Financial Services Regulation Summary, UK/Scottish Edition

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Produces thematic compliance summaries of UK financial services regulation and securities law developments for senior management, boards, or clients. Covers the FCA Handbook, PRA Rulebook, UK Parliament legislation, HM Treasury policy, and retained EU law (as amended).

## Quick Start

Gather before drafting:

1. **Topic scope** - specific area(s) or "general update" for broad survey
2. **Time window** - period to cover (default: trailing 12 months)
3. **Audience** - issuer, investment firm, bank, asset manager, broker, payment services firm, or general counsel
4. **Source materials** (optional) - FCA Policy Statements (PS), Consultation Papers (CP), Handbook notices, enforcement orders, PRA supervisory statements, internal compliance memos

## Workflow

### 1. Executive Overview (2 to 3 paragraphs)

Cover: most significant developments and practical impact, urgent compliance matters requiring immediate action, overall regulatory environment orientation. Reference the FCA's business plan priorities, Parliament's Treasury Committee reports, and Scottish Financial Enterprise (SFE) consultations where relevant.

### 2. Thematic Sections

Organise by topic, not chronology. Use applicable headings:

| Section | Covers |
|---|---|
| Disclosure & Transparency | DTR (Disclosure Guidance & Transparency Rules), ongoing and periodic obligations, inside information, UK MAR, National Storage Mechanism filings, Companies House requirements |
| Market Structure & Trading | UK MAR, market abuse offences, short selling (SSR), algorithmic trading, systematic internalisers, trading venue rules, best execution (RTS 28) |
| Enforcement & Supervision | FCA enforcement priorities, penalty trends, Decision Procedure and Penalties Manual (DEPP), SMCR accountability, criminal prosecution powers, whistleblowing |
| Authorisation & Supervision | FCA authorisation / Part 4A permission, PRA dual-regulated firms, approved persons (SMF), variation of permission, change in control, regulatory references |
| Consumer Protection | Consumer Duty (PRIN 2A), fair value, vulnerable customers, product governance (PROD), financial promotions regime, DISP complaints handling |
| Financial Crime & AML | FCG (Financial Crime Guide), MLR 2017, sanctions, counter-terrorist financing, bribery, fraud, market abuse links |
| Investment Funds & Asset Management | COLL (Collective Investment Schemes), FUND, AIFMD retained, UCITS retained, SDR (Sustainability Disclosure Requirements), investment labels, depositary/custody |
| Listing & Public Offers | UKLR (UK Listing Rules), PRM (Prospectus Rules: Admission to Trading), public offers regime, cash shells, sponsors, eligibility, listing categories |
| Prudential & Capital | PRA Rulebook, CRR/CRD retained, IFPR (Investment Firms Prudential Regime), MIFIDPRU, ICARA, Pillar 3 disclosures, Solvency II retained |
| Emerging Areas | Cryptoassets (financial promotion perimeter, FCA crypto regime, MiCA equivalence), AI governance, operational resilience (CMA, DORA retained), digital securities, sustainable finance |

For each development include:

- **What changed** - rule, guidance, or action with FCA Policy Statement (PS), Consultation Paper (CP), or Handbook instrument number
- **Effective date / timeline**
- **Who is affected** - firm type (FCA-only, PRA/FCA dual-regulated, appointed representative), market participant category
- **Compliance implications** - what firms must do differently
- **Recommended actions** - concrete next steps, including any required FCA notification or application

### 3. Regulatory Intersections

Flag overlapping obligations:

- Consumer Duty cross-cutting rules ↔ product governance ↔ fair value ↔ vulnerable customer treatment ↔ financial promotions, Operational resilience ↔ outsourcing ↔ third-party risk ↔ critical third parties (CTP) regime ↔ data protection, SMCR conduct rules ↔ certification ↔ regulatory references ↔ enforcement individual accountability, Cryptoasset financial promotion rules ↔ MLR registration ↔ perimeter guidance ↔ Consumer Duty, ESG claims ↔ anti-greenwashing rule ↔ SDR labelling ↔ TCFD-aligned disclosures ↔ UK Corporate Governance Code

### 4. Forward Look

- Pending rulemaking (cite FCA Consultation Paper numbers and HM Treasury call for evidence dates)
- Open comment periods and deadlines, Upcoming FCA policy statements and Handbook instruments, Parliament bills and statutory instruments in progress, Scottish-specific consultations by Scottish Government or SFE, Emerging issues warranting monitoring (e.g. Edinburgh Reforms, wholesale markets review, payments review)

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **Citations**: FCA Policy Statement PSYY/XX; Consultation Paper CPYY/XX; Handbook instrument or sourcebook reference; UK statutory instrument (SI) number; UK Parliament bills; Scottish Parliament bills where applicable. Mark any unverified citation with `[VERIFY]`
- **Interplay**: Always note where a development triggers obligations under multiple frameworks (e.g. Consumer Duty + PROD + DISP)
- **Uncertainty**: Flag compliance risk where FCA/PRA staff positions are evolving (Dear CEO letters, portfolio letters, final notices, market watch publications)
- **Tone**: Authoritative, practical, no unexplained jargon, suitable for non-specialist senior readers including board members
- **Length**: Target 3 to 6 pages, sufficient for action, concise for efficient review
- **Jurisdiction**: UK financial services law (FSMA 2000, PRA/FCA rulebooks, retained EU law as amended, UK MAR, UK Prospectus Regulation, UKLR, PRM, DTR). Note Scotland-specific legal context (Scots law of contract, delict, property rights under Scottish trusts and securities over moveable property) only where directly relevant
- **No editorialising** on policy merits, focus on compliance obligations and practical risk
- **Scottish context**: Where regulation interacts with Scots law (e.g. heritable security, floating charges, diligence, bankruptcy/sequestration, partnership law, trusts), note this explicitly. The Scottish financial services sector employs over 150,000 and is concentrated in Edinburgh and Glasgow; SFE is the representative body

### Additional UK-Specific Sections

- **FCA Consumer Duty** - A core UK regulatory innovation. Cross-cutting rules under PRIN 2A: four outcomes (products and services, price and value, consumer understanding, consumer support). No US equivalent. Applicable to all firms with retail customers.
- **SMCR (Senior Managers and Certification Regime)** - Individual accountability framework: Senior Management Functions (SMFs), certification, conduct rules. Criminal liability for senior managers in certain circumstances. No equivalent in US securities law.
- **IFPR / MIFIDPRU** - UK Investment Firms Prudential Regime: ICARA, Fixed Overheads Requirement, K-factors, Pillar 3 disclosures. Distinct from US broker-dealer net capital rules.
- **Retained EU law** - UK onshored EU regulations (UK MAR, UK Prospectus Regulation, UK EMIR, UK SFTR) with ongoing amendments by UK Parliament and HM Treasury. Track the Retained EU Law (Revocation and Reform) Act effect.
- **Edinburgh Reforms** - HM Treasury reforms to the UK financial services regulatory framework (2022+), including Smarter Regulatory Framework, wholesale markets review, prospectus regime reform, and the new public offers and admissions to trading regime (effective January 2026).

### Scottish Financial Services Context

- Scotland's financial services sector is regulated by the FCA and PRA at UK level, there is no separate Scottish financial regulator, Key Scottish-specific considerations:
  - Scots law of contract, delict (negligence), property rights, and trusts
  - Heritable security (land/mortgage) and floating charges under Scots law
  - Scots law of sequestration (personal bankruptcy) and liquidation
  - Scottish partnership law (including limited partnerships used in investment fund structures)
  - Scottish charitable trusts and their regulation by OSCR (Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator)
  - Scottish Financial Enterprise (SFE) represents the sector and responds to FCA/HMT consultations
  - Major Scottish financial institutions include Scottish Widows, abrdn, Baillie Gifford, Phoenix Group (formerly Standard Life Aberdeen), and TSB, Scottish elements of UK legislation: FSMA 2000 extends to Scotland; HM Treasury consults across Great Britain; Scottish courts jurisdiction in financial services disputes follows ordinary domicile rules, The Council of Scottish Financial Firms and the Scottish Government publish periodic financial services strategies

### [SCOTS: Note] - US-Specific Sections Remaining

Where original US material references concepts with no direct UK/Scottish equivalent, the following [SCOTS: Note] markers apply:

- **Reg S-K / Form 10-K / Form 8-K** → replaced by DTR (ongoing/periodic obligations). UK does not have a direct 10-K/8-K equivalent. Annual financial report (DTR 4.1), half-yearly report (DTR 4.2), inside information (UK MAR Article 17).
- **Schedule 13D/13G beneficial ownership** → UK has no direct equivalent. DTR 5 (notifiable interests) operates differently, threshold at 3%+, no equivalent of short-swing profit rules under §16(b).
- **Rule 10b-5 / antifraud** → UK has the general prohibition under s.19 FSMA 2000, market abuse under UK MAR, and misleading statements/impressions under s.89 to 91 FSMA 2000 and s.2 to 5 Fraud Act 2006. No direct class action equivalent in Scotland/UK.
- **EDGAR filing system** → replaced by National Storage Mechanism (NSM) for regulated information and Companies House (for filings/prospectuses). UK NSM portal is run by the FCA; separate from Companies House.
- **SEC No-Action Letters** → FCA has no direct no-action letter process. FCA primary market technical notes (PN/TN series), portfolio letters, and Market Watch publications serve a broadly similar interpretive guidance function.
- **Self-Regulatory Organisations (SROs)** → UK does not have SROs equivalent to FINRA/MSRB. FCA is the statutory conduct regulator. Designated Professional Bodies (DPBs) have limited exempt regulated activities scope under FSMA 2000.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
