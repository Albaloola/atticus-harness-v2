# UK Restrictive Covenant Guide (Scotland/UK)

## Restraint of Trade Doctrine

**Governing principle:** Post-termination restrictions are **void as a restraint of trade unless** they protect a legitimate business interest and are reasonable in geographic scope, duration, and activities.

**Key UK cases:**

| Case | Principle |
|---|---|
| *Nordenfelt v Maxim Nordenfelt Guns and Ammunition Co Ltd* [1894] AC 535 | Restraint of trade is prima facie void; must be shown reasonable between parties and consistent with public interest |
| *Herbert Morris Ltd v Saxelby* [1916] 1 AC 688 | Employer must show a restraint is reasonably necessary to protect a legitimate proprietary interest (trade secrets, customer connections, staff stability) |
| *Office Angels Ltd v Rainer-Thomas* [1991] IRLR 214 | Non-solicitation and non-dealing clauses are more readily enforced than blanket non-competes |
| *TSC Europe (UK) Ltd v Manor* (1992) | Garden leave is a distinct mechanism; employer can require employee to stay away during notice period |
| *Tillman v Egon Zehnder Ltd* [2019] UKSC 32 | Supreme Court confirmed blue pencil test applies; non-compete must be unambiguous; severance of offending words is permissible |

## UK vs US: Key Differences

| Aspect | UK | US |
|---|---|---|
| Legal basis | Common law restraint of trade (unified UK-wide) | Varies state-by-state; some states ban non-competes |
| Reformation | Blue pencil only (strike offending words; cannot rewrite) | Blue pencil, reformation, or red pencil depending on state |
| Consideration | Continued employment during notice period or new consideration on variation | Varies by state; some require independent consideration |
| Who is bound | Only where legitimate business interest (trade secrets, customer relationships, staff stability) | Broader scope; some states enforce with minimal justification |
| Maximum duration | 3 to 12 months typical; rarely enforce more than 12 months | 6 to 24 months common; some states allow longer |
| Remedies | Injunction (interdict in Scotland), damages, account of profits | Injunction, damages, liquidated damages, varies by state |
| FTC | No equivalent US FTC rule | Recent FTC proposed rule (challenged); no UK equivalent |
| Jury trial | Employment Tribunal (no jury); not in civil courts for these cases | Available in some US states for enforcement |
| Costs | Loser pays (judicial expenses) | American rule (each side pays own costs) |

## Garden Leave

**Garden leave** is a common UK alternative to non-compete clauses:

- Employee remains employed (draws salary) but stays away from work during notice period, Preserves employee's duty of fidelity and good faith, Does NOT rely on restraint of trade doctrine (enforceable as a matter of contract)
- Typical duration: 3 to 6 months
- **Advantage over non-compete:** No risk of being void for restraint of trade; employee receives full pay
- **Drawback:** Employer must pay salary and benefits during garden leave

**Drafting approaches:**
- Include a contractual right to place employee on garden leave, Reserve the right to pay in lieu of notice (PILON) to terminate earlier, Garden leave and non-compete should be expressed as alternatives, not cumulative

## UK Non-Compete Reform, Proposed 3-Month Cap

**Current state (as of 2026):**

- The UK Government consulted on introducing a statutory 3-month cap on non-compete clauses, No legislation has been enacted as of 2026
- The proposal would limit non-compete clauses to 3 months maximum, Non-solicitation and confidentiality clauses would be unaffected, The cap would apply to England, Wales, and Scotland (employment is GB-wide)
- **Current position:** No cap in force; continue to draft per common law principles but flag potential reform

**If the cap is enacted:**
- Non-compete clauses > 3 months would be void, Non-solicitation and confidentiality unaffected, Garden leave unaffected (not a post-termination restriction)

## Scottish Employment Tribunals

- **Employment Tribunals Scotland** - hears unfair dismissal, breach of contract, discrimination, and other employment claims
- **Jurisdiction:** GB-wide employment legislation applies (Employment Rights Act 1996, Equality Act 2010)
- **Restrictive covenant enforcement** - typically in **Sheriff Court** or **Court of Session**, NOT Employment Tribunal (as these are breach of contract claims, not statutory claims)
- **Interim interdict** (injunction) - available in Sheriff Court for urgent enforcement
- **Procedure:** Summary application for interim interdict can be heard within days

## Unfair Contract Terms, UCTA 1977

The **Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977** applies to employment contracts:

- Section 1 - applies to business liability, Section 3 - requires reasonableness for clauses excluding/restricting liability for breach
- **Relevance:** Non-compete clauses are not themselves UCTA-regulated, but any clause that purports to exclude employer liability for inducing breach of the non-compete, or that imposes unreasonable consequences for breach, may be challengeable

**Consumer Rights Act 2015** - generally not applicable to employment contracts (employees are not consumers)

## Drafting for UK/Scotland

### Legitimate Business Interests

Before drafting, identify **which** of these protectable interests apply:

- **Trade secrets** - must be genuinely confidential, not merely skill and experience
- **Customer connections** - regular contact with customers who identify with the employee, not the employer
- **Staff stability** - risk of team poaching; must be key personnel, not junior staff
- **Confidential information** - non-public commercial, financial, or technical information

### Blue Pencil Doctrine (UK)

The UK applies **blue pencil severance** (confirmed in *Tillman v Egon Zehnder*):

- Courts can **strike** offending words/phrases that are unreasonable, Courts **cannot** rewrite or modify the clause to make it reasonable, If severance changes the character of the agreement, the entire clause may be void, Draft with separate, severable sub-clauses for each restriction

### Typical UK Enforceable Restrictions

| Restriction | Typical UK Duration | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Non-compete | 3 to 6 months (rarely > 12 months) | Most strictly scrutinised; narrow scope essential |
| Customer non-solicitation | 6 to 12 months | More readily enforced; limit to customers with material contact |
| Non-dealing (accepting business) | 6 to 12 months | Wider than non-solicitation; harder to justify |
| Employee non-solicitation | 6 to 12 months | Protect staff stability; senior roles only |
| Confidentiality | Perpetual (for trade secrets) | Not a time-limited restriction; best practice to define categories |

### Recommended Structure (UK)

1. **Parties:** Employer (UK-registered company) × Employee (UK employee)
2. **Definitions:** Legitimate business interests defined specifically; competitor list; connected customers
3. **Garden leave:** Right to place employee on garden leave during notice period
4. **Non-compete:** Narrowly defined by role, geography (employer's actual market), and duration (6 months maximum recommended)
5. **Customer non-solicitation:** Only customers with material employee contact in last 12 months
6. **Employee non-solicitation:** Senior employees and key personnel only
7. **Confidentiality:** Perpetual obligation with return/destruction of materials
8. **Blue pencil severance:** Expressly severable sub-clauses
9. **Remedies:** Interdict (interim and permanent), damages, account of profits
10. **Governing law:** Scots law / English law; jurisdiction clause matching place of work
11. **Threshold review:** Confirm employee role genuinely justifies each restriction; document reasons

### Checklist

- [ ] Legitimate business interest identified and documented
- [ ] Restriction no wider than necessary to protect that interest
- [ ] Duration within UK common law norms (3 to 6 months for non-compete)
- [ ] Geographic scope tied to actual market presence
- [ ] Activities restricted are reasonable and specific
- [ ] Blue pencil severance clause included with separate sub-clauses
- [ ] No attempt to rewrite overbroad restrictions (UK courts won't do it)
- [ ] Garden leave clause included as alternative mechanism
- [ ] Consideration adequate (new hire: employment is sufficient; existing employee: new consideration or agreed variation)
- [ ] Employment Rights Act 1996 - written statement of particulars in place
- [ ] No FRCP references (remove all US Federal Rules of Civil Procedure)
- [ ] UK GDPR compliance noted for any data processing
- [ ] Reform risk flagged (potential 3-month statutory cap)
