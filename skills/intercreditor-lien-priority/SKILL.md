## Scotland/UK Adaptation

The US concept of "lien priority" is governed by different principles in Scotland. Key adaptations:

**Key differences:**
- **Standard securities**: The Scottish equivalent of a mortgage/lien over heritable property (land and buildings) is a **Standard Security** under the Conveyancing and Feudal Reform (Scotland) Act 1970. There is no UCC Article 9 - real property security is governed by the 1970 Act, and moveable property (goods, intangibles) is governed by common law and statute.
- **Floating charges**: Scotland recognises floating charges under the Companies Act 2006 (Part 25, Chapter 2, applying the Insolvency Act 1986 provisions). A floating charge is a Scottish creation adapted from English law, it is a charge over a company's whole property and undertaking which crystallises on winding up or appointment of an administrator.
- **Ranking**: Priority among standard securities is determined by date of registration in the Register of Sasines or the Land Register of Scotland. For floating charges, ranking depends on the date of creation and the terms of the instrument. Intercreditor agreements can contractually vary the statutory ranking.
- **No UCC Article 9**: There is no single unitary secured transactions code. Security over moveables is created by: standard security (for certain moveables), assignation in security (for receivables), pledge (for corporeal moveables), and floating charge (for companies).
- **Insolvency Act 1986**: Winding up and administration in Scotland follow the Insolvency Act 1986 with Scottish-specific modifications (ss. 51-79, 103-112, 169-185). Subordination in insolvency is governed by the common law (National Westminster Bank v Spectrum Plus Ltd [2005]) and the Insolvency Rules (Scotland).
- **Currency**: All monetary obligations and security values are in GBP (£).
- **Governing law**: Scots law. If the transaction crosses the border, consider English law governing law clauses with Scots law for property/security matters.
- **Register of Inhibitions**: For personal rights, rather than Register of Inhibitions for interdicts affecting land.

**The US "first lien / second lien" distinction** maps to "first-ranking standard security / second-ranking standard security" or "first-ranking floating charge / second-ranking floating charge", or a mixture of both over different asset classes.

- [ ] Subordination continuing until Discharge of First-Ranking Obligations
- [ ] Exclusive enforcement control by first-ranking security holder during standstill
- [ ] Turnover obligation for junior receipts (any payment received by second-ranking in breach of ranking)

- [ ] Restrictions on second-ranking security document amendments that worsen senior position
- [ ] Notice mechanics and amendment consent requirements
- [ ] Rank priority to remain effective on administration / winding up
- [ ] Provisions for crystallisation of floating charge and interaction with fixed security

## Template Snippets

Standstill clause:

```text
"Standstill Period" means the period beginning on the date the First-Ranking Security Agent delivers written notice of a First-Ranking Event of Default and ending [__] days thereafter.

During the Standstill Period, the Second-Ranking Secured Parties shall not take any Enforcement Action with respect to the Shared Collateral or contest any Enforcement Action taken by the First-Ranking Secured Parties.
```

Turnover clause:

```text
Any payment or distribution received by any Second-Ranking Secured Party in violation of this Agreement shall be held in trust and immediately turned over to the First-Ranking Security Agent for application in accordance with the Payment Waterfall.
```

## Pitfalls and Checks

- **Ranking vs subordination**: Ensure the agreement clearly distinguishes contractual ranking from debt subordination. A ranking agreement governs priority of enforcement; a subordination agreement governs priority of payment in insolvency.
- **Discharge definition**: Must include cash collateralisation or backstop of contingent hedging/bank guarantee exposure.
- **Permitted junior payments**: If allowed, limit to scheduled interest and fees when no first-ranking default exists.
- **Insolvency provisions**: Confirm recognition of contractual subordination in Scottish winding up and administration. The Insolvency Act 1986, Sch. B1 and the Insolvency (Scotland) Rules 2018 govern the statutory scheme.
- **Registration**: Standard securities must be registered in the Land Register (or Register of Sasines). Floating charges must be registered at Companies House (Register of Charges) within 21 days of creation per the Companies Act 2006, s.859A. Failure to register renders the charge void against a liquidator, administrator, or other creditor.
- **Heritable vs moveable property**: Standard securities cover heritable property (land/buildings). Floating charges cover the entire property and undertaking. Assignations in security cover receivables. Different perfection and priority rules apply to each.
- **Crystallisation**: Floating charges crystallise on winding up, appointment of an administrator, or on enforcement of a standard security. Verify when and how the second-ranking floating charge crystallises relative to the first-ranking.
- **Administrator's powers**: Under the Insolvency Act 1986, Sch. B1, an administrator has a statutory duty to pay prescribed part to unsecured creditors (s.176A). This overrides contractual ranking for the prescribed part, flag this to parties.
- **Post-standstill rights**: Define narrowly whether junior creditors may lodge claims or seek security after standstill expiry.
- **Consistency**: Keep defined terms aligned across agreement; cross-reference finance documents precisely. Match notices, consent, and assignation provisions to deal structure.
- **[SCOTS: No direct equivalent to UCC 9-339]**: Scots law does not have a specific statutory provision allowing subordination, it is governed by common law contractual principles. The key authority is National Westminster Bank v Spectrum Plus Ltd [2005] UKHL 41 on the characterisation of fixed vs floating charges.
