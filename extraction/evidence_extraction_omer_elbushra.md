## 4. LEGAL CLAIMS IDENTIFIED

### 4.1 Equality Act 2010 Claims

**A. Direct Discrimination (s.13) — Race, Religion, Disability**
- Shepherd's remarks on 12 March: "you're not the only homeless student, you're not special"; Gateway program reference
- Evidence IDs: OME-SRC-0027, OME-SRC-0046
- Confidence: **Medium-High** — explicit remarks are direct evidence; comparator inference needed for full proof

**B. Discrimination Arising from Disability (s.15)**
- Tone-policing emails (manifestation of ADHD, Autism, Alexithymia)
- FtP referral based on disability-related behaviours (direct communication style, persistence)
- Punishing disability-related communication as misconduct
- Evidence IDs: OME-SRC-0027, OME-SRC-0028, OME-SRC-0046
- Confidence: **High** — extensive documentary evidence of disability knowledge and nexus between disability and targeted behaviours

**C. Failure to Make Reasonable Adjustments (s.20-21)**
- No adjustments for neurodivergent communication style
- No recording permitted in meetings despite ASD processing needs
- No support person in critical meetings
- Rigid hardship fund process without ADHD accommodations
- Evidence IDs: OME-SRC-0027, OME-SRC-0028, OME-SRC-0046
- Confidence: **High** — anticipatory duty; specific PCPs identified; adjustments not made

**D. Harassment (s.26)**
- Shepherd's 12 March meeting: degrading, hostile, intimidating
- Welfare team interrogation (17 Feb)
- SEB gaslighting (Sneddon)
- SRC ban and ostracism
- Evidence IDs: OME-SRC-0027, OME-SRC-0028, OME-SRC-0046
- Confidence: **High** — conduct objectively harassing; clearly unwanted; related to protected characteristics

**E. Victimisation (s.27)**
- CRITICAL: FtP referral initiated 48 days AFTER complaint against Shepherd was filed
- SRC ban immediately after feedback/complaint
- SAR obstruction after protected acts
- Campus card confiscation after legal notices
- Shepherd's lie about complaint knowledge demonstrates consciousness of guilt
- Evidence IDs: OME-SRC-0027, OME-SRC-0028, OME-SRC-0046
- Confidence: **Very High** — temporal proximity + no alternative explanation = overwhelming inference; St Helens principles apply

**F. Public Sector Equality Duty (s.149)**
- No disability/race equality impact assessments
- Systemic failure to eliminate discrimination
- Evidence IDs: OME-SRC-0027
- Confidence: **High** — no evidence of PSED compliance

### 4.3 Common Law Claims

**A. Negligence / Psychiatric Injury**
- Duty of care owed: University knew of suicide risk, mental health crisis, homelessness
- Breach: 12 March meeting conducted without safeguards, despite known vulnerabilities
- Causation: 10-day timeline from meeting to psychiatric emergency (999 call, A&E)
- Harm: Medically documented psychiatric injury, crisis team involvement, ongoing treatment
- Evidence IDs: OME-SRC-0027, OME-SRC-0028, OME-SRC-0046
- Confidence: **Medium-High** — foreseeability strong; need expert psychiatric report to confirm diagnosis and causation

**B. Breach of Contract**
- Educational services contract breached: constructive exclusion from education
- Implied terms of mutual trust and confidence broken
- University policies (CHP, FtP Regs, Code of Conduct) not followed — contractual terms
- Breach of Consumer Rights Act 2015 s.49 (reasonable care and skill)
- Evidence IDs: OME-SRC-0027, OME-SRC-0028, OME-SRC-0046, OME-SRC-0047
- Confidence: **Medium-High** — express and implied terms; multiple procedural breaches documented

**C. Misfeasance in Public Office** (targeted against Shepherd and McMillan)
- Public officers (university officials exercising statutory powers)
- Unlawful acts: victimisation through FtP, abuse of process, breach of natural justice
- Malice: Shepherd knew complaint existed yet denied knowledge; timing proves conscious retaliation
- Harm: psychiatric injury, career threat
- Evidence IDs: OME-SRC-0027, OME-SRC-0046
- Confidence: **Medium** — strong inference; courts set high bar for malice; needs further evidence of knowing illegality

### 4.4 Breach of Natural Justice

- **Nemo iudex in causa sua**: Shepherd involved in FtP after being subject of complaint
- **Audi alteram partem**: No evidence disclosed before investigation meeting
- FtP proceedings void ab initio due to conflict of interest
- SRC ban: no hearing, no appeal
- Evidence IDs: OME-SRC-0027, OME-SRC-0028, OME-SRC-0047
- Confidence: **Very High** — clear procedural breaches; Porter v Magill bias test met

### 4.5 Human Rights Act 1998

- **Art. 8**: Breach of medical confidentiality, privacy violations
- **Art. 14**: Prohibition of discrimination
