# Notice of Group Proceedings

**[FORM, DRAFT, VERIFY AGAINST COURT DIRECTION]**

**IN THE COURT OF SESSION**

**GROUP PROCEEDINGS, NOTICE TO POTENTIAL GROUP MEMBERS**

[Case number]

**Important, This notice may affect your legal rights**

**1. What are these proceedings about?**

[Brief, neutral description of the action, e.g. "A group of individuals has raised proceedings in the Court of Session claiming [summary of claims] against [defender name]."]

**2. Who is in the group?**

The court has granted permission for group proceedings in respect of persons who fall within the following definition:

[Verbatim group definition from court order]

**3. What does this mean for you?**

If you fall within the group definition, you may be eligible to join these proceedings. Group proceedings in Scotland are voluntary, you are **not** part of the group unless you take steps to opt in.

**4. How to opt in**

To join the group proceedings, you must complete and return the opt-in form by **[date]**.

[Method: submit online at [URL], call [number], or return by post to [address].]

**5. What happens if I opt in?**

If you opt in, you will be bound by any judgment or settlement in the group proceedings. You may also be liable for a share of expenses if the proceedings are unsuccessful (subject to any protective expenses order or cap on liability).

**6. What happens if I do nothing?**

If you do not opt in, you will not be affected by these proceedings. You remain free to raise your own proceedings individually.

**7. Legal representation**

The group is represented by [solicitor/firm]. [If a representative party has been appointed: The representative party is [name].]

**8. Further information**

See [group proceedings website], call [number], or contact [solicitor details].

---

*Note: Scottish group proceedings are opt-in only. This is a fundamental difference from US opt-out class actions. The notice must clearly communicate the opt-in requirement and the potential expenses liability.*
