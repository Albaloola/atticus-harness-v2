# UK GDPR Personal Data Breach Notification, Guidance Note

## Key Legislation

- **UK GDPR** (retained EU GDPR as amended by Data Protection, Privacy and Electronic Communications (Amendments etc) (EU Exit) Regulations 2019)
- **Data Protection Act 2018** - supplements and tailors UK GDPR
- **Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003 (PECR)** - breach notification for telecoms/ISPs

## Breach Notification Requirements (UK GDPR)

### To ICO (Art. 33)
- **When**: Personal data breach likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons
- **Timing**: Without undue delay and, where feasible, within **72 hours** of becoming aware
- **Content**: Nature of breach, categories and approximate number of data subjects/records, DPO contact, likely consequences, measures taken/proposed
- **If late**: Must provide reasons for delay

### To Affected Individuals (Art. 34)
- **When**: Breach likely to result in **high risk** to rights and freedoms
- **Content**: Nature of breach, DPO contact, likely consequences, measures taken/proposed
- **Language**: Clear and plain language
- **Exemptions**: Encryption/rendering unintelligible; subsequent measures eliminating high risk; disproportionate effort (substitute public communication)

## Key Differences from US Approach

| US | UK |
|----|----|
| Multi-state deadlines (30 to 90 days) | Single federal (UK) deadline: 72 hours to ICO |
| State-specific content requirements | One standard for all UK (including Scotland) |
| SSN/credit monitoring focus | Focus on risk to rights and freedoms |
| HIPAA 60 days | UK GDPR 72 hours (no healthcare-specific exception) |
| Substitute notice thresholds (500K+/$250K+) | Only for disproportionate effort (Art. 34(4)) |
| Credit freeze/bureau notification | Recommendation to notify ICO early if financial data |

## Scotland-Specific

- ICO covers the whole UK including Scotland, Scottish data subjects have same rights under UK GDPR/DPA 2018
- Sheriff Court / Court of Session jurisdiction for compensation claims, Prescription period: 6 years for delict (data breach claims)
- No separate Scottish data protection authority

## Notification Quick Reference

| Scenario | ICO Notify? | Individual Notify? |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Encrypted data stolen, key compromised | Yes (72h) | Yes (high risk) |
| Encrypted data stolen, key secure | Possibly (assess risk) | No (unlikely risk) |
| Lost unencrypted laptop (PII) | Yes (72h) | Yes (high risk) |
| Internal misdirected email (no PII) | No | No |
| Ransomware affecting PII | Yes (72h) | Yes if high risk |
| Exfiltration of customer database | Yes (72h) | Yes (high risk) |
