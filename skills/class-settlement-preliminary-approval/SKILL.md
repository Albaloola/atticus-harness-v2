## Scotland/UK Adaptation

The US class action (opt-out, Rule 23) does not exist in Scots law. The equivalent mechanism is **Group Proceedings under the Civil Litigation (Expenses and Group Proceedings) (Scotland) Act 2018**, which operates on an **opt-in** basis only.

**Key differences:**
- **Opt-in, not opt-out**: Every group member must positively consent to participate. There is no automatic inclusion.
- **No US-style class certification**: The court grants a "Group Proceedings Order" (GPO) - the Scottish equivalent of certification, but only on an opt-in basis.
- **Expenses (costs)**: The general rule is loser-pays (the "English rule"). Group proceedings have specific costs protections under the 2018 Act. The court may cap the lead party's liability for expenses.
- **Settlement approval**: The court must approve any settlement that binds group members, but the opt-in structure means members individually agree to be bound rather than being automatically included.
- **No cy pres**: Unclaimed funds typically revert to the defender rather than being distributed to a charitable purpose, unless the settlement agreement specifically provides otherwise.
- **GDPR/privacy**: Notice to group members must comply with UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.
- **Funding**: Third-party litigation funding (TPLF) and after-the-event (ATE) insurance are available but subject to different regulation than US third-party funding.
- **Statute**: Civil Litigation (Expenses and Group Proceedings) (Scotland) Act 2018, as supplemented by the Group Proceedings (Scotland) Rules 2024 (SSI 2024/XXX, verify current SI number).
- **Court of Session**: Group proceedings are heard in the Outer House of the Court of Session. Sheriff Court group proceedings are not currently provided for.
- **Currency**: All monetary relief should be pleaded and settled in GBP (£).

## Prerequisites

1. **Executed settlement agreement** - fully signed, with all exhibits
2. **Case docket** - court process, interlocutors, procedural steps
3. **Group definition** - proposed group with precise opt-in boundaries
4. **Notice plan materials** - proposed forms of notice, claims forms, group administrator identity
5. **Fee arrangement** - solicitor's fee structure (speculative fee / contingency fee arrangements regulated in Scotland, note that damages-based agreements are restricted)
6. **Supporting affidavits** - from lead party, group solicitor, or administrator

## Output Structure

### 1. Heading & Introduction

- Format heading per Court of Session rules (Chapter of Rules, Form number)
- State motion's purpose in 2 to 3 sentences: nature of claims, lead party, key relief, List four requests: (a) preliminary approval, (b) Group Proceedings Order (GPO), (c) notice plan approval, (d) final hearing scheduling

### 2. Background

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| Factual allegations | Core group claims and defender(s)' conduct |
| Legal theories | Causes of action asserted |
| Procedural history | Key dates: summons, defences, procedure roll, proof, group proceedings application |
| Negotiation history | Settlement negotiations, arm's-length indicators, counsel experience |

Cite specific discovery volume, expert involvement, and negotiation details to demonstrate informed settlement.

### 3. Settlement Terms

| Component | Detail |
|---|---|
| Total monetary relief | Gross settlement fund amount in GBP (£) |
| Allocation methodology | Tiers, formulas, per-member estimates |
| Non-monetary relief | Interdict / specific implement provisions, practice changes |
| Fee/cost allocation | Solicitor's fees cap, administrative costs |
| Lead party awards | Amounts for lead party or representative |
| Release scope | Claims released, persons bound |
| Unclaimed fund provision | Typically reverts to defender unless otherwise agreed |

### 4. Legal Standard, Preliminary Approval

Apply the "appropriate for approval" standard: settlement must appear fair, reasonable, and adequate, and the court must be satisfied that the proposed GPO is appropriate.

**Section 7 of the 2018 Act factors:**

- [ ] Adequate representation by lead party and group solicitor
- [ ] Arm's-length negotiation
- [ ] Adequate relief considering litigation costs, risks, and delay
- [ ] Equitable treatment among group members
- [ ] Reasonable solicitor's fees

Cite relevant Scottish authority. Tie each factor to case-specific facts.

### 5. Group Certification (Group Proceedings Order)

| Requirement | Showing |
|---|---|
| **Opt-in membership** | Each group member has positively consented in writing |
| **Common issues** | Substantial common issue of fact or law |
| **Suitability** | GPO is the most efficient and just means of resolving the claims |
| **Lead party** | Suitable person to act as lead party (no conflicts of interest) |
| **Group solicitor** | Qualified and experienced to conduct the proceedings |

### 6. Fairness Analysis

1. **Strength of pursuer's case** - merits assessment, key legal risks
2. **Litigation risk** - risk of proof failures, procedure roll dismissal, appeal timeline
3. **Recovery comparison** - settlement value vs. realistic maximum recovery (not theoretical ceiling)
4. **Stage of proceedings** - sufficiency of disclosure (discovery equivalent) and investigation
5. **Anticipated objections** - preemptively address fund adequacy, allocation fairness, fee concerns

### 7. Notice & Administration

**Notice methodology:**
- Direct notice (recorded delivery post/email) to identifiable group members who have opted in, Publication/digital notice for publicising the opt-in window, Must satisfy the 2018 Act and GDPR requirements

**Required notice content:** settlement terms in plain language, right to object (procedure/deadline), right to withdraw from group (procedure/deadline), claims submission process/deadline, final hearing date/time/location.

**Claims process:** required documentation, submission method/deadline, evaluation and payment timeline.

Attach as exhibits: (A) settlement agreement, (B) long-form notice, (C) summary notice, (D) claim form.

### 8. Proposed Schedule

| Event | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Preliminary approval hearing | [Date] |
| Notice dissemination | [X] days after GPO granted |
| Opt-in deadline | [X] days after notice |
| Objection deadline | [X] days after notice |
| Claims submission | [X] days after notice |
| Final approval motion | [X] days before final hearing |
| Fee petition | [X] days before final hearing |
| Final approval hearing | [X] days after GPO |

Conform to Court of Session Rules and the judge's typical timelines.

### 9. Conclusion & Attachments

Restate each request: (1) preliminary approval/GPO, (2) provisional group certification, (3) notice plan approval, (4) administrator appointment, (5) opt-in/objection procedures, (6) final hearing date.

Include signature blocks, amended summons / pleadings where required, and affidavit language per Court of Session rules.

## Guidelines

- **Jurisdiction**: Confirm that the matter falls within the Court of Session's competence for group proceedings. Sheriff Court group proceedings are not yet available.
- **Opt-in requirement**: Unlike US Rule 23, there is no opt-out mechanism. Every group member must have positively consented.
- **Expenses**: The loser-pays rule applies, though the court has discretion to cap a lead party's expenses exposure. Flag this prominently, it affects settlement dynamics.
- **Funding arrangements**: Damages-based agreements (DBAs) are subject to the Damages-Based Agreements (Scotland) Regulations 2011. Speculative fee arrangements are more common.
- **CBILS / state aid**: If the defender is a public body or has received state support, consider whether settlement requires notification under subsidy control rules.
- **Plain language**: Notice forms must be readable by lay group members.
- **Record citations**: Support every factual assertion with court process or exhibit references.
- **[SCOTS: No direct equivalent to CAFA notice]**: There is no US-style Class Action Fairness Act notification requirement to a government body in Scotland. However, where the defender is a Scottish public authority, consider whether the settlement requires specific authorisation.
- **[SCOTS: No direct equivalent to Amchem/Ortiz]**: Scottish group proceedings are governed by the 2018 Act and the Group Proceedings Rules, not by US constitutional due process precedents. Nonetheless, the court retains inherent jurisdiction to ensure fairness.
