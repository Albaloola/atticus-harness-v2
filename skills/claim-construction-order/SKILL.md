# Patent Claim (Specification) Construction Order, UK/Scotland

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a court order/opinion (or formal legal submission) construing disputed patent claims under the framework established by *Kirin-Amgen Inc v Hoechst Marion Roussel Ltd* [2004] UKHL 46, *Actavis UK Ltd v Eli Lilly & Co* [2017] UKSC 48, and Article 69 EPC / the Protocol on Interpretation.

## Prerequisites

- Patent(s) at issue, GB/EP(UK) patent in full, with claims and specification, Prosecution history, file wrapper (IPO / EPO register where relevant)
- Parties' proposed constructions, joint claim construction schedule, skeleton arguments, Hearing record, transcripts, expert reports, technical primers, written submissions, Case caption, court (Patents Court, IPEC, or Court of Session), case number, full party names

## Quick Start

1. Gather intrinsic record (patent specification, prosecution history / file wrapper)
2. Map each disputed term to parties' proposed constructions
3. Apply UK construction framework: claim language → specification → prosecution history (where relevant) → purposive construction per *Kirin-Amgen* → Protocol Questions per *Actavis*
4. Draft per-term analysis with pinpoint citations
5. Produce summary table and formal order/opinion paragraph

## Order Structure

### Caption & Title
Standard court caption. Title: `CLAIM CONSTRUCTION ORDER` (Patents Court) or `OPINION OF THE COURT on Specification Construction` (Court of Session). Follow practice directions (Patents Court Guide / Court of Session Ch. 55).

### Introduction, Patent number(s) and title(s)
- Procedural posture (post-trial, post-hearing, or agreed construction)
- Number of disputed terms, Cite *Actavis v Eli Lilly* [2017] UKSC 48; *Kirin-Amgen* [2004] UKHL 46; Patents Act 1977, s. 125; Article 69 EPC and Protocol

### Background, Plain-language technical description of the invention (neutral summary)
- Alleged infringement summary (if known, neutral)
- Procedural history (claim form; particulars of infringement; defence; case management conference; technical primer exchange; expert evidence; hearing)

### Legal Standard

| Principle | Rule |
|---|---|
| Governing standard | Article 69 EPC and Protocol on Interpretation - "fair protection for the patentee" while providing "reasonable certainty for third parties" |
| Purposive construction | *Kirin-Amgen Inc v Hoechst Marion Roussel Ltd* [2004] UKHL 46 - ascertain what the person skilled in the art would have understood the patentee to have used the language of the claim to mean |
| Primary source | Claim language read in light of the specification and common general knowledge (CGK) |
| Specification | "Context in which the claim must be read" - *Electric & Musical Industries Ltd v Lissen Ltd* (1939) |
| Prosecution history | Limited role in UK, see *Virgin Atlantic v Premium Aircraft* [2009] EWCA Civ 1062; contrasting with US prosecution history estoppel |
| Equivalents (scope of protection) | *Actavis UK Ltd v Eli Lilly & Co* [2017] UKSC 48, Lord Neuberger, two-stage approach: (1) purposive construction (normal interpretation); (2) Protocol Questions for equivalents |
| Protocol Questions (Actavis) | (i) Does the variant achieve substantially the same result in substantially the same way? (ii) Would it have been obvious to the POSITA that it does? (iii) Would the POSITA understand that the patentee intended strict compliance with the literal meaning? |
| Appellate review | Appeal on construction is a question of law - *Reid v Ramlort* [2020] UKSC 27; but can involve mixed fact and law |

### Per-Term Construction

For each disputed term:

**Term:** "[exact claim language]" (Claim [X] of [Patent No.])
**Claimant's Construction:** ...
**Defendant's Construction:** ...

**Construction Analysis:**
- Claim language, usage in asserted and dependent claims, Specification, paragraph [X], lines [X] to [X]: [quote/paraphrase]
- Common general knowledge - [relevant CGK at priority date]
- Prosecution history (if material) - [IPO file reference / EPO opposition history if relevant]
- Extrinsic evidence (expert evidence) - [identify areas of agreement/disagreement]

**Normal Interpretation:** [Court's finding on literal/purposive construction under Kirin-Amgen]

**Actavis Protocol Questions** (if variant/non-literal infringement alleged):
1. Substantially same result in substantially same way? [Yes/No/Unnecessary]
2. Obvious to POSITA? [Yes/No/Unnecessary]
3. Intended strict compliance? [Yes/No/Unnecessary]

**Court's Construction/Opinion:** [definitive language]
**Reasoning:** 1 to 3 sentences supporting the construction.

**Special cases:**
- **Means-plus-function**: Patents Act 1977 s. 125(3) and EPC Art. 69 - note that UK law does not treat means-plus-function claims identically to US § 112(f); structure must be discernible from the specification.
- **Insufficiency / clarity**: Flag if construction raises Art. 84 EPC issues (support/clarity) or s. 14(3) Patents Act 1977 (support for invention). Note without resolving insufficiency unless the issue is before the court.
- **Numerical ranges / parameters**: Treat with precision but purposively - *Anacon Corp Ltd v Environmental Research Technology Ltd* [1994] FSR 659.

### Conclusion, Summary table: all terms and final constructions, Note consequentials: any amendment to pleadings, further disclosure, or revised product descriptions, Confirm construction binds all subsequent proceedings

### Formal Order / Opinion Block

**Patents Court / IPEC (England):**

> IT IS ORDERED that the disputed terms of [Patent No(s).] are construed as set out in the Annex/Schedule to this Order. This construction shall govern all subsequent proceedings.
>
> [Parties are to serve amended pleadings, if so advised, within 28 days.]
>
> Dated this ___ day of __________, 20___.
>
> [Judge Name]

**Court of Session (Scotland):**

> The Lord Ordinary having considered the cause, Finds that the disputed terms of [Patent No.] fall to be construed as set out in the Appendix to this Opinion; and continues the cause for further procedure.
>
> [Date]
>
> [Judge Name / Lord Ordinary]

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **Tone** - Authoritative, neutral, analytical. No advocacy or party-favouring language.
- **Pinpoint citations** - Spec: paragraph numbers (EP/UK patents use paragraph numbering). Patents Court: neutral citation and paragraph numbers. Law reports: lawtel/Bailii references with pinpoints.
- **Internal consistency** - Verify constructions are consistent across related terms; no construction should render claims insufficent or read out disclosed embodiments.
- **Do not** import limitations from preferred embodiments, treat claims as "blueprint" rather than purposes, or adopt overbroad constructions lacking support in the specification.
- **No US-style Markman**: UK does not have a separate pre-trial "Markman hearing" as standard. Construction is determined as part of the main trial or at a separate hearing where ordered (Patents Court Guide).
- **Prosecution history estoppel**: UK law applies prosecution history only in exceptional circumstances, do not import US doctrine directly. *Virgin v Premium Aircraft* [2009] EWCA Civ 1062.
- **Common general knowledge**: Expert evidence on CGK is routine in UK patent trials, not secondary as in US.
- **[VERIFY]** - All case citations must be verified against current authorities, especially *Actavis* Protocol Questions application in subsequent cases.
- **Jurisdiction**: GB patents litigated in the Patents Court (England & Wales), IPEC (claims < £500K), or the Court of Session (Scotland). Scottish procedure follows Chapter 55 of the Rules of the Court of Session 1994.

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

- **Governing Framework**: Patents Act 1977 (c. 37) - applies throughout UK including Scotland. EPC Article 69 and the Protocol on Interpretation govern claim construction.
- **Key Authorities**: *Catnic Components Ltd v Hill & Smith Ltd* [1982] RPC 183 (HL) - original purposive construction; *Improver Corp v Remington Consumer Products Ltd* [1990] FSR 181 - Improver questions (precursor); *Kirin-Amgen Inc v Hoechst Marion Roussel* [2004] UKHL 46 - current purposive construction framework; *Actavis UK Ltd v Eli Lilly & Co* [2017] UKSC 48 - Protocol Questions for equivalents and scope of protection.
- **No Markman**: UK does not hold separate pre-trial Markman hearings. Claim construction is determined at trial or in a construction hearing ordered by the court.
- **Prosecution History**: UK courts give prosecution history much less weight than US, the doctrine of prosecution history estoppel as known in US law does not apply in the UK. *Virgin Atlantic v Premium Aircraft* [2009] EWCA Civ 1062.
- **Scotland**: Patent litigation in Scotland proceeds in the Court of Session Outer House under RCS Chapter 55. Scottish patent judgments (opinions of the Lord Ordinary) use different formatting and procedural nomenclature from English Patents Court orders (interlocutor vs. order, reclaiming motion vs. appeal).
- **Terminology**: US term → UK equivalent: Markman hearing → Construction hearing / trial; District Court → Patents Court / IPEC / Court of Session (Outer House); Phillips v AWH → Kirin-Amgen / Actavis framework; FRCP → CPR (Civil Procedure Rules) / RCS (Rules of Court of Session); Federal Circuit → Court of Appeal (England) / Inner House (Scotland); Supreme Court (US) → UK Supreme Court / Supreme Court (on appeal from Court of Session); 35 U.S.C. § 112 → Patents Act 1977 s. 14(3) / s. 125; intrinsic record → specification + CGK; extrinsic evidence → expert evidence / technical primers.
- **Currency**: All damages awards are in GBP (£) - no USD conversions.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
