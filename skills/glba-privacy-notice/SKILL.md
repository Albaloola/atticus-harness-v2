# Privacy Notice, UK GDPR / DPA 2018 (Financial Services)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Produces a UK GDPR- and DPA 2018-compliant privacy notice for financial services firms, covering the mandatory fair processing information under Articles 13 to 14 UK GDPR, categories of personal data processed, lawful bases, sharing arrangements, individual rights, and data security safeguards.

**[SCOTS: Note]** This skill replaces the US GLBA approach entirely. The UK has no direct equivalent to GLBA; financial services privacy is governed by the UK GDPR framework, DPA 2018, and FCA regulatory expectations (FCG guidance, Consumer Duty fair value outcomes). No safe-harbour model form exists, each notice must reflect the specific controller's processing.

## Gather Before Drafting

- Institution legal name, trading names, company number, ICO registration number, FCA FRN, Affiliate structure and group data-sharing arrangements, Categories of personal data collected: identity, financial, contact, transaction, special category, criminal offence, Lawful bases for each processing purpose (Article 6 UK GDPR)
- Special category data condition (Article 9) and criminal records condition (DPA 2018 Schedule 1)
- All third-party data recipients: processors, group companies, regulators, fraud prevention agencies, credit reference agencies, International transfers (outside UK): adequacy decisions, SCCs, or derogations, Individual rights provision: SARs, erasure, restriction, portability, objection, Retention schedules by data category, Data security measures: technical and organisational controls, ICO registration number and data protection officer contact

## Privacy Notice Structure

### 1. Controller Identity

Required opening information:

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Who we are | Institution legal name, company number, ICO registration number |
| Contact | Registered address, DPO contact details, email, phone |
| Regulator | FCA firm reference number (FRN) |
| Data Protection Officer | Name and contact (if appointed per Article 37 UK GDPR, mandatory for FCA firms? [VERIFY for specific size/type]) |

**[SCOTS: Note]** For Scottish-regulated entities (credit unions, Scottish building societies), include relevant oversight bodies (FCA, PRA, Prudential Regulation Authority).

### 2. Personal Data Collected

Group by category:

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Identity Data | Full name, title, date of birth, nationality, NI number, UTR |
| Contact Data | Residential address, email, phone, correspondence address |
| Financial Data | Account numbers, sort codes, balances, transactions, credit/debit history, income, assets |
| Special Category Data (if processed) | Health data for insurance, biometrics, trade union membership, political opinions [VERIFY applicability] |
| Criminal Offence Data (if processed) | Convictions, cautions for fraud/AML screening |
| Transaction Data | Payment details, standing orders, direct debits, trading history |
| Technical Data | IP address, login data, device info, browsing behaviour, cookies |
| Usage Data | Website/app usage, marketing preferences, customer journey |
| Communications Data | Records of calls, emails, secure messages, complaints |

**[SCOTS: Note]** For firms using the Scottish Credit Register or Scottish Housing Register, include relevant credit reference data sharing.

### 3. Lawful Basis for Processing

For each processing purpose, identify the lawful basis (Article 6 UK GDPR) and, where relevant, special category condition (Article 9):

| Processing Purpose | Lawful Basis (Art. 6) | Special Category Condition (Art. 9) |
|---|---|---|
| Account opening and ongoing administration | Contractual necessity (b) / Legal obligation (c) | - |
| AML/CTF screening and sanctions checks | Legal obligation (c) / Public interest (e) | Substantial public interest (g) |
| Credit reference checks | Legitimate interests (f) / Legal obligation (c) | Schedule 1 DPA 2018 |
| Marketing (analytics/profiling) | Consent (a) or Legitimate interests (f) | - |
| Fraud prevention | Legal obligation (c) / Legitimate interests (f) | Substantial public interest (g) |
| Insurance underwriting/claims | Contractual necessity (b) / Legal obligation (c) | Insurance condition, Schedule 1 DPA 2018 |
| Regulatory reporting (FCA, PRA, HMRC) | Legal obligation (c) | - |

**[SCOTS: Note]** In Scotland, the same UK GDPR framework applies. The Scottish courts (Sheriff Court, Court of Session) handle DPA 2018 claims. The Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) enforces UK-wide.

### 4. Data Sharing Categories

| Sharing Purpose | Recipient Type | Lawful Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Transaction processing | Payment processors, banks | Contractual necessity |
| Credit referencing | Credit reference agencies (e.g., Experian, Equifax, TransUnion) | Legitimate interests / Legal obligation |
| Fraud prevention | CIFAS, N Hunter, fraud databases | Legal obligation / Legitimate interests |
| Professional advisers | Solicitors, accountants, auditors | Legitimate interests |
| Regulators | FCA, PRA, HMRC, ICO | Legal obligation |
| Debt collection | Tracing agents, debt collectors | Legitimate interests |
| Marketing (with consent) | Third-party marketing partners | Consent |
| Group entities | Affiliates within corporate group | Legitimate interests |

### 5. International Transfers

State clearly:
- Whether personal data is transferred outside the UK, To which countries / entities, The safeguard mechanism: UK adequacy regulations, International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA), or Addendum to SCCs, Whether a Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) has been completed

**[SCOTS: Note]** For Scottish-based firms, data may be transferred from Scotland to England (no restriction within UK), but transfers to the EU/EEA are subject to UK adequacy decisions and the IDTA framework. Post-Brexit adequacy decisions for the EU are in place (as of July 2025 [VERIFY current status]).

### 6. Individual Rights

List all applicable rights under Articles 15 to 22 UK GDPR:

| Right | Description | Exceptions |
|---|---|---|
| Right to be informed | What this notice provides | - |
| Right of access (SAR) | Receive copy of personal data | May refuse if manifestly unfounded / excessive |
| Right to rectification | Correct inaccurate data | - |
| Right to erasure (right to be forgotten) | Delete personal data | Applicable exceptions (legal obligation, fraud prevention) |
| Right to restrict processing | Limit how data is used | Conditional |
| Right to data portability | Receive data in machine-readable format | Only applies to automated processing |
| Right to object | Object to direct marketing; object to processing under legitimate interests | Must be complied with for marketing; balanced for other |
| Rights re automated decision-making | Not to be subject to solely automated decisions with legal effect | Applicable exceptions (contractual necessity, explicit consent) |

Provide SAR contact details, response timeframes (one month, extendable by two months for complex requests), and ICO complaint right.

### 7. Data Retention

Summarise retention periods by data category (or refer to separate retention schedule). Include:
- Duration or criteria used, Statutory minimums (e.g., MLR 2017 - 5 years; FCA SYSC - 5 years)
- Deletion/anonymisation after retention period

### 8. Security Safeguards

Describe technical and organisational measures such as:
- Encryption (at rest and in transit)
- Access controls and authentication, System monitoring and intrusion detection, Staff training, Business continuity and disaster recovery, Regular security assessments and penetration testing

## Pitfalls

- **No safe-harbour model form** - unlike US GLBA, there is no pre-approved model form. Each firm's notice must reflect actual processing activities. Generic notices risk ICO enforcement.
- **Fair processing vs. privacy policy** - ensure the notice meets the Article 13/14 "layered" requirements; a single dense document may not satisfy transparency obligations.
- **[SCOTS: Note] No direct equivalent to GLBA opt-out** - the UK GDPR does not have a US-style GLBA opt-out for information sharing. Individual rights (erasure, restriction, objection) fulfil a similar function but operate differently.
- **Separate consent for marketing** - legitimate interests may apply for certain processing but marketing/e-marketing requires consent (PECR 2003 and electronic marketing rules).
- **MLR 2017 retention** - must retain AML records for 5 years after business relationship ends; GDPR erasure rights do not override this legal obligation.
- **Consumer Duty** - fair value assessments and transparency about data processing may fall within the FCA's Consumer Duty expectations.
- **ICO complaints** - any UK GDPR privacy notice must prominently include the right to complain to the ICO (Wycliffe House, Water Lane, Wilmslow, Cheshire, SK9 5AF).
- **Scotland-specific** - while data protection law is UK-wide, Scottish courts have jurisdiction for claims against controllers established in Scotland or where the data subject is in Scotland.
