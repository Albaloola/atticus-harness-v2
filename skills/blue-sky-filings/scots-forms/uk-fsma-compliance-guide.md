# UK FSMA Securities Compliance, Quick Reference

## Core Legislation
- **Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (FSMA)** - s.19 (general prohibition), s.21 (financial promotion), s.85 (prospectus requirement)
- **Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (Financial Promotion) Order 2005 (SI 2005/1529)** - key exemptions
- **Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (Regulated Activities) Order 2001 (SI 2001/544)** - RAO
- **Prospectus Regulation (retained EU 2017/1129)** - UK prospectus rules
- **Companies Act 2006** - allotment, share capital, filings

## Key UK Agencies
- **FCA (Financial Conduct Authority)** - https://www.fca.org.uk
- **Companies House** - https://www.gov.uk/government/organisations/companies-house
- **HMRC** - share schemes, EIS/SEIS/VCT, stamp duty

## Common FSMA Exemption Pathways

| Investor Type | FPO Article | Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| Professional clients (per se) | 50 | FCA-authorised, government, large company (€20m+ turnover) |
| Professional clients (elective) | 50 | FCA-approved; €500k portfolio/€1m turnover/€100k+ staff role |
| Certified HNW individuals | 72 | £100k+ income or £250k+ net assets; certificate valid 12 months |
| Self-certified sophisticated | 68 | Angel network / business angel / HMRC-approved advanced |
| Certified sophisticated | 69/70 | FCA-authorised confirmation of experience |
| Employees / club members | 54 | Employee of issuer or group / member of club/society |
| Overseas persons | 59 FPO / 18(2) RAO | No UK solicitation or direction |

## Companies House Obligations
- **SH01**: within 1 month of allotment
- **Confirmation Statement (CS01)**: annual
- **Register of members**: maintained at registered office or SAIL address
- **Scotland**: registered office in Scotland; filings to Companies House Edinburgh

## Key Differences from US Blue Sky, No state-by-state regime, single UK jurisdiction, No "resale" restrictions per se (but s.21 applies to secondary promotions)
- FSMA s.21 covers communications from within or targeting the UK, Financial promotion must be "fair, clear and not misleading" (FCA rules)
- Exemptions are statutory, no merit review by state regulators
