Drafts a court-ready Initial Writ (Sheriff Court) or Summons (Court of Session) for servitude interference in Scottish heritable property litigation. Structures element-driven allegations, jurisdictional pleading, and comprehensive crave for remedies.

[SCOTS: Note: In Scotland, "easements" are known as "servitudes" (praedial servitudes). The initiating document is an Initial Writ in the Sheriff Court (Ordinary Cause) or a Summons in the Court of Session. The terminology differs materially from US practice, the pursuer (plaintiff) sues the defender (defendant) and seeks interdict (injunction) rather than an injunction.]

## Prerequisites

Collect before drafting:

1. **Servitude deed or title** - registered deed of servitude, title burden, or basis for implied/prescriptive claim (Land Register title number or Sasines search)
2. **Legal descriptions** - dominant and servient tenements, plans, surveys
3. **Interference evidence** - dated photos, correspondence, obstruction descriptions
4. **Party information** - full legal names, addresses, ownership/interest roles
5. **Jurisdiction/venue** - sheriffdom, Sheriff Court district; Court of Session if value exceeds £100,000 or complex
6. **Damages** - alternative access costs, property value impact, repair/removal expenses

## Quick Start

1. Gather prerequisites and identify servitude type (express, implied, necessity, positive prescription)
2. Determine appropriate court (Sheriff Court Ordinary Cause vs. Court of Session)
3. Draft Initial Writ or Summons following the output structure below
4. Attach exhibits: title deeds, Land Register entries, plans, photos, correspondence
5. Run the checklist in Pitfalls before finalising

## Output Structure

### 1. Instance & Caption

- Court name with sheriffdom/district, Full legal names for all parties, Instance statement identifying parties, Document title (e.g., "Initial Writ for Interference with Servitude Rights, Trespass, and Interdict")

### 2. Party Allegations

| Party | Required Allegations |
|---|---|
| Pursuer | Name, address, interest in dominant tenement, title and interest to sue |
| Defender | Name, address, relationship to servient tenement, role in interference |

### 3. Jurisdiction & Venue

- **Sheriff Court**: venue determined by location of the heritable property (ordinary cause)
- **Court of Session**: where value or complexity warrants; servitude disputes over £100,000 may be better suited
- **Venue**: cite the relevant property sited within the sheriffdom

### 4. Condescendence (Factual Narrative, Chronological)

[SCOTS: Note: Scottish Initial Writs use numbered "Condescendences" (statements of fact) followed by "Pleas-in-Law" (legal grounds). This replaces the US-style factual narrative and causes of action.]

**Servitude creation, plead by type:**

| Type | Required Facts |
|---|---|
| Express grant | Deed date, parties, recording info (Land Register / Sasines); quote scope/location wording; transmission in titles |
| Reservation | Conveyance history, reservation wording, recording info |
| Implied / necessity | Common ownership, severance date, landlocked/no alternative access at severance |
| Positive prescription | Possession for 20 years, open, peaceable, without judicial interruption; registered interest (Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973) |

**Physical description:**
- Dimensions, boundaries, location per Title Plan/OS map, Improvements (road, driveway, utilities) and who maintains them, Historical use pattern with duration

**Defender's interference, plead with specification:**
- Each act: date, time, location, description (gate, barrier, parked vehicles, debris, threats)
- Obstruction dimensions relative to servitude boundary, Correspondence and failed negotiations, Defender's stated position (abandonment, scope dispute) - address preemptively

**Loss, injury and damage:**
- Alternative access costs, Obstruction removal expenses, Diminution in dominant tenement value, Loss of use and enjoyment

### 5. Pleas-in-Law (Legal Grounds)

[SCOTS: Note: In Scotland, causes of action are presented as numbered "Pleas-in-Law".]

**Plea-in-Law 1 - Interference with Servitude Rights**
1. Valid servitude exists [incorporate creation facts]
2. Defender has interfered through specific acts
3. Interference is substantial and wrongful
4. No legal justification
5. Continuing injury to pursuer
6. Cite applicable law of servitudes

**Plea-in-Law 2 - Trespass to Heritable Property**
1. Pursuer holds dominium in the servitude area
2. Defender has encroached without title or permission
3. Unlawful interference with possessory rights
4. Continuing vs. single act as facts support

**Plea-in-Law 3 - Declarator**
1. Actual, justiciable controversy exists
2. Questions for determination: location, scope, maintenance obligations, validity
3. Harm from uncertainty; defender has taken adverse position
4. Cite authority for declaratory remedy

**Plea-in-Law 4 - Interim and Perpetual Interdict**
1. No adequate remedy in damages (access loss not fully compensable; interference ongoing)
2. Irreparable harm, describe specifically
3. Balance of convenience favours pursuer
4. Seek interdict (interim, interim interdict, and permanent interdict)

**Additional pleas to consider:**
- **Nuisance** - interference beyond servitude area
- **Breach of real burden** - if the servitude was created by deed with express obligations

### 6. Crave for Remedies (Prayer for Relief)

1. **Declarator** - existence, validity, location, scope; defender's obligation to cease interference
2. **Interdict** (interim + permanent) - cease interference; remove obstructions; court retains power
3. **Damages** - alternative access costs, removal expenses, property value diminution
4. **Consequential damages** - foreseeable harm
5. **Expenses** (solicitor's fees and outlays) - judicial discretion
6. **Interest** at the judicial rate (per statute)
7. **Further orders as the court deems appropriate**

### 7. Signing & Service

- Signature block for solicitor/pursuer (if acting in person)
- Instance of service instructions for sheriff officer

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **Pleading specification**: each interference act needs date, description, and location, conclusory allegations are insufficient
- **Exhibits**: reference each exhibit (title deed, plan, photos, correspondence) in the condescendence
- **Paragraph structure**: one allegation per numbered paragraph; consecutive numbering throughout
- **Anticipate defences**: preemptively rebut abandonment, scope excess, and location disputes in factual narrative
- **Prescription**: positive prescription for servitudes requires 20 years under the Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973; verify the statutory period
- **Court selection**: servitude disputes in the Sheriff Court follow Ordinary Cause Rules (OCR); Court of Session for complex or high-value cases
- **Citation format**: verify all statutory citations; use [VERIFY] for uncertain references
- **Internal consistency**: every plea-in-law must be supported by factual allegations; every crave item must correspond to an asserted plea

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from a US easement dispute complaint to the Scottish legal framework.

### Key Differences

| US Concept | Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Easement | Servitude (praedial servitude) |
| Plaintiff / Defendant | Pursuer / Defender |
| Complaint | Initial Writ (Sheriff Court) / Summons (Court of Session) |
| Injunction / TRO | Interdict / Interim Interdict |
| Tort (interference) | Delict |
| Real property | Heritable property |
| Personal property | Moveable property |
| State court | Sheriff Court |
| Diversity jurisdiction | Not applicable (Scotland has no equivalent) |
| Bluebook citation | Scots Law citation (Scots Law Times, Session Cases) |
| Prescriptive easement (adverse, open & notorious) | Positive prescription (20 years) - Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973 |
| Discovery | Commission and Diligence |
| Trial | Proof / Proof before Answer |
| Record title / title insurance | Land Register / Sasines Register |

### Applicable Legislation, Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973 (servitudes)
- Title Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003
- Land Registration (Scotland) Act 2012
- Courts Reform (Scotland) Act 2014
- Ordinary Cause Rules (Sheriff Courts (Scotland) Act 1907)
- Law Reform (Husband and Wife) (Scotland) Act 1984

### Forms
Scottish servitude disputes use:
- Initial Writ (Form OCA1 under Ordinary Cause Rules)
- Form for Interdict applications (Chapter 30 of OCR)
- Forms available from www.scotcourts.gov.uk
