Drafts an application to suspend enforcement of a decree, interlocutor, or judgment pending appellate review in the Scottish civil courts, applying the appropriate test.

**Important:** [SCOTS: Note] The US "motion for stay pending appeal" (FRCP 62 / FRAP 8) has no direct equivalent in Scots civil procedure. The appropriate routes are: (a) Reclaiming motion (Court of Session) - the appeal itself operates as a stay in certain circumstances but usually requires a separate application for interim suspension of the decree; (b) Bill of suspension (Sheriff Court / High Court) - used to suspend execution of a decree pending appeal; (c) Application for interim suspension under rules of court. The terminology and procedure differ significantly from US federal practice.

Gather before drafting:

- **Decree/Interlocutor** - date of issue/pronouncement, relief ordered, monetary amounts

- **Applicable rules** - Rules of the Court of Session (RCS), Sheriff Court Rules, Act of Sederunt provisions

## Test for Interim Suspension

In Scotland, the test for suspending a decree pending appeal considers:

**Factor 1 - Substantial Question / Primâ Facie Case**
- Identify 2 to 3 strongest legal errors in the appealed interlocutor, Demonstrate that the appeal (reclaiming motion) is not frivolous and raises a substantial question, Cite controlling authority from the Inner House or UK Supreme Court, If the interlocutor raises unsettled points of law or first impression, argue this supports suspension

**Factor 2 - Balance of Convenience / Prejudice**
- Quantify: financial impact, business disruption, asset dissipation risk, Explain why monetary compensation cannot adequately remedy (unique property, business dissolution, insolvency)
- Document imminence: scheduled arrestment, inhibition, property sale, Support with affidavits and financial evidence

**Factor 3 - Effect of Refusal on the Appellant**
- Does refusal render the appeal academic or frustrate its purpose?
- Would enforcement prevent return to status quo if appeal succeeds?
- Consider third-party impacts (employees, customers, secured creditors)

**Factor 4 - Position of the Respondent**
- Does caution/security adequately protect the respondent's interests?
- If the decree is monetary, caution (bond) fully protects the respondent, If the decree is interdict or specific implement, propose modified compliance, Acknowledge legitimate concerns directly, builds credibility, Distinguish genuine harm from delay in receiving what may be overturned

**Factor 5 - Public Interest**
- Address only when genuinely implicated (government action, constitutional rights, public safety)
- Private disputes: state neutrality honestly; do not manufacture arguments

## Caution and Security

If the decree is monetary, the appellant will typically be required to find caution (surety/bond) as a condition of the suspension.

- **Amount:** Decree sum + interest + estimated expenses
- **Reduced caution:** Present financial hardship evidence
- **Alternatives:** Letter of credit, asset pledge, consignation (payment into court), interim payment (to a stakeholder)
- **Waiver:** Cite specific authority if party is publicly funded, insolvent, or suspension is otherwise justified without caution

## Prayers / Craves

Crave the Court to:
1. Suspend execution and diligence of the decree/interlocutor attacked pending final determination of the reclaiming motion / appeal
2. Fix the amount of caution at £[amount] or as the court deems appropriate
3. Approve alternative security if applicable
4. Otherwise ordain as the court sees fit

## Pitfalls and Checks

- **Sliding scale:** Strength in one factor may offset weakness in another, lead with the strongest grounds
- **Jurisdiction check:** Confirm whether application goes to the Outer House (first instance), Inner House (reclaiming motion), Sheriff Appeal Court, or nobile officium
- **Deadlines:** Note time bar for lodging reclaiming motion (usually 14 or 21 days from date of interlocutor, RCS 38.4). Suspension application must be prompt
- **Court rules:** Verify formatting, page limits, service requirements, and answers/opposition timeline
- **Evidence:** Every factual assertion needs affidavit, documents, or record references
- **Diligence context:** If diligence (arrestment/inhibition) has been executed, the suspension application must address both the decree and the diligence
- **Specialist standards:** Interdict suspension, administrative appeals, and judicial review may apply modified tests
- **Urgency:** If enforcement is imminent, invoke urgency and seek interim orders before the Lord Ordinary or Sheriff
- **Tone:** Measured and respectful; present as preserving the court's appellate process, not as criticism of the first-instance decision

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

The following adaptations apply when this skill is used for Scottish proceedings:

**Fundamental differences:**

**No FRCP 62/FRAP 8:** Scots civil procedure has different mechanisms:
- Court of Session: A reclaiming motion against an interlocutor of the Lord Ordinary. The appeal does NOT automatically suspend the judgment. An application for interim suspension or interim sist of execution is required.
- Sheriff Court: Note of appeal to Sheriff Appeal Court. The appeal does not automatically suspend.
- Bill of suspension: Equitable remedy to suspend execution of a decree, available in both Court of Session and Sheriff Court (though less used since procedural reforms).

**Reclaiming motion (Court of Session):** The primary form of full appeal. Lodged within 14 or 21 days. The Inner House may grant interim suspension of the appealed interlocutor.

**Caution (surety/bond):** Typically required as condition of suspension. Caution is a guarantee or deposit to secure the respondent's position.

**Consignation:** Payment of funds into court as alternative to caution.

**Expenses:** The "loser pays" principle, judicial expenses follow success. The court may require expenses as a condition of suspension.

**Diligence:** 
- Arrestment, attaches debts owed to the debtor, Inhibition, prevents debtor from dealing with heritable property, Diligence on the dependence, pre-judgment security, Charge for payment, formal demand before enforcement

**Nobile officium:** The Court of Session's equitable power to intervene where no specific remedy exists, potentially relevant for unique suspension situations.

**Category of judgment:**
- Monetary decree: Suspension usually requires caution, Decree of interdict: Specific rules for interim suspension of interdict, Decree ad factum praestandum (do something): Court may suspend on conditions, Declarator: Often suspended without caution as no enforceable obligation

**Urgent procedure:** If enforcement is imminent, present an interim application (ex parte where necessary with full disclosure) before the Lord Ordinary or the Vacation Judge if out of term.

**Citations:** Use OSCOLA format, not Bluebook. Cases include House of Lords/UK Supreme Court, Court of Session, and Sheriff Court decisions.
