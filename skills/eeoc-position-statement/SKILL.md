# Employment Tribunal Response / Grounds of Resistance (Scotland/UK Adaptation)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts the employer's formal response to an employment tribunal claim (ET1), shaping tribunal proceedings while building a defensible record for a final hearing.

> [SCOTS: This skill adapts a US EEOC charge framework for the Scottish employment tribunal system. ## Prerequisites

1. **ET1 claim form** - claim number, protected characteristics under Equality Act 2010, allegations, dates of alleged conduct
2. **Claimant personnel file** - start date, position history, appraisals, disciplinary records, attendance
3. **Workplace policies** - equality/dignity at work, disciplinary and grievance procedures, staff handbook
4. **Comparator evidence** - records for similarly situated employees without the relevant protected characteristic
5. **Corroborating records** - emails, meeting notes, witness statements
6. **Business justification documents** - redundancy criteria, promotion requirements, selection processes
7. **ACAS Early Conciliation certificate** - certificate number, dates (mandatory pre-claim step)

## Quick Start

1. Extract claim number, protected characteristics, specific allegations, and date of claim
2. Map each allegation to the applicable Equality Act 2010 section and prohibited conduct (direct discrimination, indirect discrimination, harassment, victimisation)
3. Assemble chronological employment timeline with documentary support
4. Identify comparators and substantive defences
5. Draft per the output structure below
6. Confirm response deadline (typically 28 days from receipt of ET1; can be extended by application)

## Output Structure

### 1. Header Block

```
RESPONDENT'S GROUNDS OF RESISTANCE / ET3
Employment Tribunal Case No. [number]

Respondent:     [Full legal name]
Address:        [Complete business address]
Claimant:       [Name as listed on ET1]
Date:           [Submission date]
```

### 2. Introduction (1 to 2 paragraphs)

- Deny allegations or specify contested vs. admitted aspects, Roadmap response structure, Frame: legitimate business reasons, consistent policy application, Tone: professional, measured, non-dismissive

### 3. Factual Background

Chronological timeline with specific dates, names, and titles:

| Date | Event | Documentation |
|---|---|---|
| [Start date] | Appointment, position, department, line manager | Contract, offer letter |
| [Date] | Performance milestones/issues | Appraisals, PIPs |
| [Date] | Relevant incidents | Emails, witness statements |
| [Date] | Employment decision at issue | Decision memo, policy basis |

For each event include: date, individuals involved, actions taken, documentary corroboration, and any discrepancies with the claimant's account.

### 4. Allegation-by-Allegation Response

For **each** allegation:

1. **Factual response** - what actually occurred
2. **Documentary contradiction** - evidence disproving inaccurate assertions
3. **Legitimate non-discriminatory reason** - material, non-discriminatory justification
4. **Absence of discriminatory motive** - no nexus to protected characteristic

#### Claim-Specific Frameworks under Equality Act 2010

**Direct Discrimination (s.13):**
- Comparator analysis: actual (same material circumstances, no protected characteristic) or hypothetical comparator, Burden of proof under s.136: if prima facie case established, burden shifts to respondent to prove no discrimination, If no actual comparator: articulate hypothetical comparator and why treatment was not less favourable

**Indirect Discrimination (s.19):**
- Identify provision, criterion or practice (PCP)
- Demonstrate PCP is a proportionate means of achieving a legitimate aim (objective justification)

**Harassment (s.26):**
- Anti-harassment policy details: communication, training, complaint procedures, Claimant complaint history: when reported, investigation, findings, corrective action, If unreported: document available reporting mechanisms

**Victimisation (s.27):**
- Timeline showing: (a) no protected act, (b) employer unaware of protected act, or (c) treatment independent of protected act, Evidence of: decision-maker unawareness, pre-existing plans, temporal gaps, intervening reasons

**Unfair Dismissal (Employment Rights Act 1996):**
- Identify potentially fair reason (capability, conduct, redundancy, statutory obligation, SOSR)
- Demonstrate reasonable procedure (Band of Reasonable Responses test)

### 5. Legal Analysis & Defences

Identify applicable provisions and assert defences:

| Defence | Application |
|---|---|
| No prima facie case | Missing element(s) - claimant cannot establish facts from which tribunal could conclude a contravention |
| Legitimate non-discriminatory / material reason | Business necessity, consistent policy application |
| Conduct / capability / redundancy / SOSR | Potentially fair reasons for dismissal |
| No severe/pervasive conduct | Harassment below threshold |
| Reasonable steps defence (employer liability) | Employer took all reasonable steps to prevent unlawful conduct (s.109(4)) |
| Justification (indirect discrimination) | PCP is proportionate means of achieving a legitimate aim |
| Occupational requirement | If applicable (Schedule 9 para. 1) |
| Time limit | Claim out of time (s.123, Equality Act 2010 - 3 months minus 1 day) - if not reasonably practicable, consider whether time limit extension applies |
| Contributory conduct | Reduce compensation for contributory fault |
| Failure to use grievance procedure | Impact on remedy / costs |
| ACAS EC not valid | No valid early conciliation certificate |

Cite Equality Act 2010 provisions, Employment Tribunal Rules, and relevant Court of Session / EAT / Supreme Court authority. [VERIFY] all citations.

### 6. Conclusion

- State that the respondent denies the claim(s) and seeks dismissal, Specify any admissions (if any)
- State co-operation commitment, Designate point of contact with full contact information

### 7. Exhibits

```
Exhibit 1:  [Description, e.g., Equality and Dignity at Work Policy, rev. date]
Exhibit 2:  [Description, e.g., Claimant Performance Appraisals 2023 to 2025]
Exhibit 3:  [Description, e.g., Disciplinary Policy and Procedure]
```

- Number sequentially matching narrative reference order, Redact irrelevant third-party personal data (subject to GDPR / Data Protection Act 2018)
- Include statement of truth / statement from authorised company representative

## Burden of Proof under Equality Act 2010 s.136

The burden of proof in discrimination claims under the Equality Act 2010 is a two-stage process:

1. **Claimant must prove facts** from which a tribunal could conclude, in the absence of an adequate explanation, that the respondent committed an unlawful act of discrimination.
2. **If claimant meets this threshold**, the burden shifts to the respondent to prove that the treatment was **in no sense whatsoever** because of the protected characteristic.

This is distinct from the US McDonnell Douglas / Burdine framework. In practice:

- Tribunals do not apply rigid categories, they look at all the evidence holistically, An ET3 / Grounds of Resistance should anticipate the shifting burden and provide a clear, non-discriminatory explanation for any difference in treatment

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **Discoverability**: The ET3 / Grounds of Resistance is disclosed to the claimant, exclude privileged communications and litigation strategy
- **Evidence standard**: Every factual assertion must be supported by documentary evidence or verifiable witness statement; do not make unsupported claims
- **Evidentiary gaps**: Acknowledge honestly rather than overreach, credibility is paramount
- **Tone**: No personal attacks on the claimant, no dismissive language, no speculation about motives
- **Comparators**: Use specific names, dates, and outcomes, not generalisations
- **Confidential material**: Follow tribunal orders for designated confidential information
- **Deadline**: Confirm response is within the 28-day ET3 deadline from receipt of ET1; extensions by application (ET3 cannot be submitted late without a tribunal order)
- **Length**: Proportional to the case, typically 15 to 40 paragraphs plus exhibits for standard cases; longer for complex multi-day hearings
- **ACAS Early Conciliation**: Confirm the ET1 was preceded by a valid ACAS EC certificate; failure is jurisdictional
- **Protected characteristics**: All relevant protected characteristics under s.4 of the Equality Act 2010 must be addressed, including mixed claims

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill adapts a US EEOC position statement framework for the Scottish employment tribunal system.

### Key Adaptations

| US Concept | Scotland/UK Equivalent |
|---|---|
| EEOC (Equal Employment Opportunity Commission) | ACAS (Advisory, Conciliation and Arbitration Service) / Employment Tribunal Scotland |
| EEOC Charge | ET1 (claim form) - lodged with Employment Tribunal; ACAS Early Conciliation precedes this |
| Position Statement | ET3 (response form) / Grounds of Resistance |
| Title VII of the Civil Rights Act 1964 | Equality Act 2010, Part 5 (ss.39 to 60) - employment provisions |
| ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act) | Equality Act 2010 - disability provisions (s.6, Schedule 1; duty to make reasonable adjustments under ss.20 to 21) |
| ADEA (Age Discrimination in Employment Act) | Equality Act 2010 - age provisions (s.5) - note: unlike US, UK age discrimination has no upper age limit for protection |
| McDonnell Douglas / Burdine burden-shifting framework | Equality Act 2010 s.136 - statutory burden of proof: shifts to respondent if claimant establishes prima facie case |
| No-cause determination (EEOC dismisses charge) | Strike out / Deposit order / Dismissal under ET Rules 2013/2024 (r.37 strike out; r.39 deposit; r.41 strike out for no reasonable prospects) |
| EEOC mediation | ACAS conciliation (mandatory early conciliation before ET claim) / Judicial mediation (Employment Tribunal) |
| Statute of limitations (300 days / 180 days for filing charge) | 3 months minus 1 day from the act complained of (Equality Act 2010 s.123) - extended to "such other period as the tribunal thinks just and equitable" if not reasonably practicable |
| EEOC charge number | ET case number (assigned by Employment Tribunal office) |
| After-acquired evidence (McKennon v Nashville Banner) | Not directly applicable, contributory conduct provisions apply under s.122(2) / s.123(6) Equality Act 2010 and Employment Rights Act 1996 s.122(2) |
| FRCP (Federal Rules of Civil Procedure) for litigation | Employment Tribunal Rules 2013 (SI 2013/1237) as amended by 2024 Rules, applies in both England & Wales and Scotland |
| Federal circuit courts of appeal | Employment Appeal Tribunal (EAT) / Inner House of the Court of Session / Supreme Court |
| Comparator "similarly situated" under Fed. employment law | Comparator under Equality Act 2010 s.23 - "same material circumstances" - no material difference in circumstances |

### Scottish-Specific Notes

[SCOTS: Employment Tribunal Scotland (ETS) operates from regional offices in Glasgow, Edinburgh, Dundee, and Aberdeen. It applies the same Employment Tribunal Rules (2013/2024) as England & Wales. Scottish employment law is identical to English employment law in these areas because employment tribunal jurisdiction is reserved to Westminster under the Scotland Act 1998.]

[SCOTS: Appeals from Employment Tribunal Scotland go to the Employment Appeal Tribunal (EAT) in Scotland (sitting in Edinburgh). Further appeals go to the Inner House of the Court of Session, and then to the Supreme Court.]

[SCOTS: Pre-claim ACAS Early Conciliation is mandatory (Employment Tribunals Act 1996 s.18A). The claimant must obtain an Early Conciliation Certificate before lodging an ET1. Failure is jurisdictional, the claim may be rejected.]

[SCOTS: Unlike the EEOC framework, there is no administrative investigation stage. The ET1 is lodged directly with the Employment Tribunal, which notifies the respondent, who then submits the ET3.]

[VERIFY: Check the current version of the Employment Tribunal Rules, the 2024 amendments introduced changes to case management and costs provisions.]

[VERIFY: ACAS Early Conciliation deadlines, the certificate is valid for 1 month from issue, and the claimant has a further 1 month from the certificate date to lodge the ET1.]

[VERIFY: For disability discrimination claims, check whether the claimant meets the Equality Act 2010 disability definition (s.6 + Schedule 1), and whether the duty to make reasonable adjustments was engaged.]

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
