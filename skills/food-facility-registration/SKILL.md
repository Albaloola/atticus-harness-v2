Drafts a food business registration (or approved premises application) under UK/Scottish food law for facilities manufacturing, processing, packing, handling, storing, holding, or distributing food for human consumption.

> **Scotland/UK Adaptation:** This skill has been converted from FDA FSMA facility registration (21 CFR Part 1 Subpart H) to UK food business registration under the Food Safety Act 1990, Regulation (EC) 178/2002 (as retained), the Food Hygiene (Scotland) Regulations 2006, and the Official Feed and Food Controls (Scotland) Regulations. Registration is with the local authority (environmental health) rather than a central federal agency. Scotland's primary food regulator is Food Standards Scotland (FSS); England/Wales/NI are regulated by the Food Standards Agency (FSA).

| US (FDA) | UK/Scotland |
|----------|-------------|
| FDA central registration | Local authority registration (28 days before trading) |
| Biennial renewal (Oct-Dec even years) | Single registration; must notify changes |

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

1. **Facility identity** - legal name, physical address (no P.O. Box), trade/DBA names
2. **Companies House registration** - if incorporated, provide company registration number
3. **Food activity categories** - primary activities at the facility (manufacturing, packing, storage, distribution, retail)
4. **Operational type** - manufacturing/processing vs. storage/distribution vs. retail/catering
5. **Responsible Person** - name, position, contact phone, email
6. **Existing registration number** - for updates only
7. **Products of animal origin status** - determines whether approval (not just registration) is needed

## Quick Start

1. Confirm facility is **not exempt** under Schedule 6 of the Food Hygiene (Scotland) Regulations 2006 (primary producers supplying small quantities; domestic premises not including childminders)
2. Determine whether the facility requires **registration** (most food businesses) or **approval** (POAO handling for supply to other businesses)
3. Determine submission type: New / Update / Material Change
4. Collect all prerequisites above
5. Draft sections in order below
6. Include statutory declaration verbatim

## Pitfalls and Checks

- **Registration timeline** - Must register at least 28 days before opening (Scotland and rest of GB)
- **Multiple premises** - Each premise must be registered separately with its local authority
- **Material changes** - Must notify local authority of any material change to operations, food types, or ownership
- **Approval vs. Registration** - Facilities handling POAO for B2B supply require approval. Check whether local authority or FSS grants the approval
- **No fee** - Registration is free; approval may involve inspection fees
- **Enforcement risk** - Trading without registration is an offence under the Food Safety Act 1990 §31; penalty: fine and/or imprisonment
- **Scotland-specific** - Food Standards Scotland (FSS) is the national food safety body; local authority environmental health services enforce in their areas
- **Childminders** - If providing food as part of childminding in Scotland, must complete the FSS childminder food business registration form
- **Online/distance selling** - Distance selling (including online) requires registration; the physical location of the business is the registered address
- **Feed businesses** - Businesses redistributing surplus foodstuffs as animal feed must register with FSS, Mark uncertain citations with [VERIFY]
