# UK/NHS Fraud & Anti-Bribery Compliance Guide

## [SCOTS] Scotland/UK Adaptation, Stark Law & AKS Compliance

### Important: No Direct UK Equivalent

Stark Law (physician self-referral) and the Anti-Kickback Statute are uniquely US healthcare fraud laws, created in the context of Medicare/Medicaid fee-for-service billing and private healthcare markets. **The UK and Scottish legal systems have no direct equivalent.**

However, the UK provides alternative frameworks addressing the same underlying risks (conflicts of interest, inducements, fraud against the public health service). This guide maps the US Stark/AKS concepts to the UK/NHS framework.

### UK/NHS Regulatory Framework

#### NHS Counter Fraud Authority (NHSCFA)
- **Jurisdiction:** England (NHSCFA); Scotland (NHS Scotland Counter Fraud Services); Wales (NWSSP Counter Fraud); NI (NI Counter Fraud Unit)
- **NHSCFA categories of fraud:** Prescription fraud, dental fraud, procurement fraud, payroll fraud, patient services fraud, contractor fraud, grant fraud, research fraud
- **Powers:** Investigation, referral for prosecution (COPFS in Scotland; CPS in England/Wales)
- **Guidance:** NHSCFA Standards for NHS Counter Fraud Services; NHS fraud prevention framework

#### Bribery Act 2010
- Extends to Scotland (s.19, Bribery Act 2010)
- **s.1 - Bribing another person:** Giving, offering, or promising a financial or other advantage with the intention of inducing improper performance
- **s.2 - Being bribed:** Requesting, agreeing to receive, or accepting a financial or other advantage
- **s.7 - Failure to prevent bribery:** Corporate offence, only defence is "adequate procedures"
- **No facilitation payments exception** (unlike US FCPA)
- **Penalties:** Individual, up to 10 years imprisonment + unlimited fine; Corporate, unlimited fine
- **Prosecution:** COPFS (Scotland) / SFO (Serious Fraud Office) / CPS (England/Wales)

#### Fraud Act 2006
- s.2: Fraud by false representation, s.3: Fraud by failing to disclose information, s.4: Fraud by abuse of position, Penalties: Up to 10 years imprisonment + fine

#### NHS Standard Contract (England) / NHS Scotland Equivalent, Mandatory terms on: conflicts of interest, anti-fraud, data protection, quality, governance, NHSCFA model clauses for procurement contracts, Sanctions: termination, recovery, performance notices

#### GMC Good Medical Practice (2024) - Conflicts of Interest, Para 66: "You must be honest and open when you have, or could be seen to have, a financial or other interest in an organisation providing healthcare or in a pharmaceutical or biomedical company"
- Para 67: "You must declare any potential or actual conflicts of interest"
- Para 68: "You must not ask for or accept gifts, hospitality or other inducements that may affect, or be seen to affect, the way you practise"
- Private practice: restrictions on NHS waiting list patients; charges for NHS facilities

#### NHS Consultant Contract, Private Practice Rules
- **England (2003 contract):** Full-time NHS consultants limited to 11% of NHS salary from private practice
- **Scotland:** Different contractual arrangements, check Health Board-specific terms
- **Waiting list controls:** Consultants must not treat NHS waiting list patients privately without trust/board consent
- **Facilities charges:** Consultants using NHS premises for private practice must pay charges

#### Fit and Proper Persons Test (FPPT)
- **Regulatory basis:** Regulation 5, Health and Social Care Act 2008 (Regulated Activities) Regulations 2014
- **Applies to:** Directors, executive board members of NHS trusts/health boards
- **Requirements:** No criminal record, no fitness to practise findings, no misconduct findings
- **Failure:** Must not be appointed or must be removed from position

### Scotland-Specific Notes

- **No separate Stark/AKS regime in Scotland** - UK-wide legislation applies
- **NHS Scotland Counter Fraud Services** - equivalent to NHSCFA but operates under NHS National Services Scotland
- **COPFS** prosecutes Bribery Act and Fraud Act offences in Scotland
- **Scottish courts** apply Scottish Sentencing Guidelines
- **Scottish Health Boards** operate under NHS Scotland terms (distinct from NHS England)
- **Scottish Public Sector Procurement** regulated by Procurement Reform (Scotland) Act 2014
- **NHS consultant private practice rules** differ, check individual Health Board contractual terms
- **Scottish public sector conflicts of interest** regulated under the Ethical Standards in Public Life etc. (Scotland) Act 2000

### Recommended Compliance Approach (UK/NHS)

1. **Conduct a full consultant/decision-maker arrangement inventory** covering all financial relationships
2. **Establish a conflicts of interest register** - GMC/NHS mandatory
3. **Implement Bribery Act adequate procedures** following MoJ Six Principles
4. **Ensure NHSCFA/NHS Scotland Counter Fraud Services reporting channels** are documented and accessible
5. **Train all staff** on Bribery Act, Fraud Act, NHS fraud risks
6. **Establish procurement due diligence** for NHS contract suppliers
7. **Run annual internal audit** of conflicts, private practice compliance, procurement integrity
8. **Self-report** to NHSCFA/NHS Scotland Counter Fraud Services if fraud identified
