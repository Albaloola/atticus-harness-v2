# Early Conciliation Certificate, Employment Tribunal

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts the ACAS EC certificate (or early conciliation exemption) that closes the mandatory ACAS early conciliation process and enables an employment tribunal claim under the Equality Act 2010, ERA 1996, or other UK employment legislation. This is the UK/Scotland equivalent of the US EEOC Right to Sue notice.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

- **Claim details** - prospective claimant name/address, respondent employer name/address
- **ACAS early conciliation reference number** - provide if EC process has been conducted
- **Closure basis** - no conciliation possible, respondent unwilling, settlement not achievable, or exemption applies
- **Statutory bases** - Equality Act 2010, Employment Rights Act 1996, Pensions Acts, agency worker regulations, etc.
- **Issuing body** - ACAS (via telephone, online, or paper EC form); name of conciliation officer

## Quick Start

1. Collect prerequisites above
2. Determine whether early conciliation (EC) is required or an exemption applies
3. Draft EC certificate or confirm ACAS certificate date (the equivalent of a right-to-sue notice)
4. Include 3-month employment tribunal time limit paragraph, this is stricter than the US 90-day period
5. List all applicable statutory claims
6. Review against Pitfalls checklist

## Document Layout

Draft sections in this order:

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| Heading | ACAS, Early Conciliation Certificate or EC completion letter with reference number |
| Date | Date of issue; employment tribunal time limit adjustments (see Pitfalls) |
| Reference block | EC reference, prospective claimant and respondent details |
| Salutation | "Dear [Claimant]" |
| Title | **EARLY CONCILIATION CERTIFICATE, EMPLOYMENT TRIBUNAL** - capitalised, bold |
| EC Process Summary | EC number, respondent employer, dates of conciliation window |
| Closure rationale | "Early conciliation has concluded" or exemption paragraph |
| Merits disclaimer | ACAS does not determine merit; certificates do not reflect on claim strength |
| Time limit | Bold/underlined paragraph explaining tribunal time limit adjustments (see below) |
| Applicable statutes | All statutes authorising the tribunal claim (see Statute Reference) |
| Right to representation | Advice re solicitor, Law Society of Scotland, Citizens Advice, trade union |
| State/UK bodies | Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC) referral for wider issues |
| Signature block | Conciliation officer name, "On behalf of ACAS" |
| Enclosures | Copy of EC notification if applicable; time limit information sheet |

## Closure Reasons

Use the applicable language:

| Reason | Language |
|---|---|
| Conciliation concluded | "The Advisory, Conciliation and Arbitration Service (ACAS) has fulfilled its duty under s.18A Employment Tribunals Act 1996 to endeavour to promote a settlement. Early conciliation has concluded without a settlement being reached." |
| Respondent unwilling | "The prospective respondent has indicated it does not wish to participate in early conciliation." |
| No conciliation possible | "ACAS has determined that early conciliation is not possible in the circumstances." |
| Exemption applies | "This claim is exempt from early conciliation requirements. [Specify exemption category per ET Regs 2024]." |
| Pre-claim settlement | "This certificate is issued following the conclusion of early conciliation in which no settlement was reached." |

## Employment Tribunal Time Limit

Include verbatim or substantially similar:

```
IMPORTANT: A claim to an employment tribunal MUST be presented within the
applicable statutory time limit. Generally, this is:
- THREE MONTHS LESS ONE DAY from the date of the act complained of (for
  most claims including unfair dismissal, discrimination, detriment)
- Extended where early conciliation applies: if ACAS receives the EC form
  within the limitation period, the time limit is paused during EC and
  for a period after the EC certificate is issued.
Check the Employment Tribunal Rules of Procedure 2024 and the applicable
statute for your specific time limit. The time limit is strictly enforced.
Failure to present a claim within the applicable period may result in the
tribunal refusing to accept the claim regardless of its merits.
```

**Time limit extension formula:** When early conciliation runs, the time limit is extended:
- The day ACAS receives the EC form starts a "stop-the-clock" on the limitation period, The clock resumes when the EC certificate is issued (date of certificate)
- The claimant then has at least one calendar month from the certificate date to present the claim IF the original deadline would have been earlier

## Pitfalls

- **[SCOTS: Note] Time limits are THREE MONTHS LESS ONE DAY** - not 90 days. This is the critical difference from the US system. The strict ET time limit is the most common reason for claim rejection
- **Early conciliation is mandatory** for nearly all tribunal claims; a claim cannot be presented without an ACAS EC certificate number (s.18A Employment Tribunals Act 1996)
- **ACAS does not determine merit** - never imply the EC certificate reflects on claim strength
- **Neutral tone** - do not prejudge merits, discourage claim, or imply ACAS opinion
- **All statutes** - list every statute from the claim to preserve full scope
- **[SCOTS: Note] Scottish Employment Tribunals** - while the substantive law is UK-wide, the Employment Tribunal (Scotland) operates under its own procedural rules (ET Rules 2024). The Glasgow and Edinburgh hearing centres are the main venues. Specifying Scotland matters for venue, and for appeals which go to the Employment Appeal Tribunal (EAT) sitting in Scotland
- **No cause equivalent** - in the UK there is no "no cause" determination by ACAS. ACAS conciliation is process-based and neutral
- **Reasonable adjustments** - for disabled claimants, the tribunal must make reasonable adjustments (Equality Act 2010, s.15 and Schedule 1)
- **Union or advice representative** - note any representative's details and whether they hold a current practising certificate (if solicitor)

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Adapted for:** Scotland and UK law
**Changes made:**
- Replaced EEOC (US) with ACAS (UK) early conciliation framework, Replaced Title VII, ADA, ADEA, EPA, GINA with Equality Act 2010, Employment Rights Act 1996, and UK statutory instruments, Replaced 90-day filing window with 3 months less one day (UK employment tribunal time limits)
- Replaced EEOC charge number with ACAS EC reference number, Replaced "Notice of Right to Sue" with "Early Conciliation Certificate" / EC completion letter, Replaced US District Court / state court with Employment Tribunal (Scotland)
- Replaced "no cause" / "insufficient evidence" closure reasons with ACAS-specific language (conciliation concluded, no conciliation possible, respondent unwilling)
- Replaced state deferral / FEPA with UK-wide framework, Replaced certified mail with standard ACAS notification, Removed punitive damages references (no punitive damages in UK employment law)
- Added time-limit extension formula for EC (unique UK rule)
- Added [SCOTS: Note] flags for Scottish-specific issues

**Key Scottish/UK considerations:**
- ACAS early conciliation is mandatory pre-requisite for nearly all tribunal claims, Employment Tribunals in Scotland follow ET Rules 2024
- Time limit is 3 months less one day, significantly shorter than US 90-day window and calculated differently, No punitive damages, compensation in employment tribunals is typically capped (e.g., unfair dismissal basic award + compensatory award capped at £105,707 as of 2025-26; discrimination awards are uncapped)
- ACAS does not make merit determinations, it is a neutral conciliation body, Fees were abolished for employment tribunal claims in 2017 (following the Supreme Court decision in UNISON)
- Appeals from the ET in Scotland go to the Employment Appeal Tribunal (EAT) sitting in Scotland, then to the Court of Session (Inner House), then to the UK Supreme Court

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
