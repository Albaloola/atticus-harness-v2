# Healthcare Billing Compliance Policy (Scotland/UK Adaptation)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

## [SCOTS: Critical Note] - US Medicare "Incident-To" Concept Does Not Apply in UK

The US-original of this skill addresses "incident-to billing" - a US Medicare rule allowing non-physician practitioner (NPP) services to be billed under a supervising physician's National Provider Identifier (NPI) at the higher physician rate. This concept has **no equivalent** in the UK NHS or Scottish private healthcare.

**Why the concept doesn't transfer:**
- UK NHS operates on block contracts, capitation, and Payment by Results, not fee-for-service per encounter, Scottish NHS clinicians (GPs, nurses, AHPs) all have independent scope of practice, no "billing under supervision" concept, No individual practitioner billing to NHS; reimbursement is to the practice/trust, Private healthcare in Scotland does not use Medicare-style billing rules

**What remains useful:** The general methodology, eligibility criteria checklists, supervision documentation, internal audit programs, training frameworks, and compliance governance, transfers as a **billing compliance methodology** for private healthcare or NHS contract compliance purposes.

The adapted skill below retains the **compliance framework structure** but replaces incident-to specifics with UK healthcare fraud prevention and contract compliance content.

Drafts a healthcare billing compliance policy governing:
- **NHS contract compliance** (GMS/PMS, LES/DES under the GP Contract)
- **Private medical billing compliance**
- **Prescription and referral compliance**
- **Fraud prevention** aligned with the NHS Counter Fraud Authority (NHSCFA) Framework

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

1. **Practice/organisation profile** - GP practice, NHS Trust, private hospital, independent clinic
2. **Location** - nation (Scotland-specific rules differ from England)
3. **Contract type** - GMS/PMS/APMS (NHS), or private insurer agreements
4. **Staff roles** - GPs, nurses, AHPs, paramedics, and scope of practice
5. **Existing policies** - current billing, fraud prevention, data protection policies
6. **Regulatory profile** - NHS Counter Fraud Authority, Healthcare Improvement Scotland, care inspectorate

## Quick Start

Identify the billing/compliance context (NHS contract, private billing, or cross-sector). Build the compliance checklist from the relevant domains below.

## Policy Drafting Workflow

### 1. Policy Header

Include: effective date, annual review cycle, scope (all clinical and administrative staff), governing authority (NHS Counter Fraud Authority framework, Health and Social Care Standards, relevant Acts). State that billing compliance is a governance duty with potential fraud or contractual consequences for misuse.

### 2. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Billing Compliance | Ensuring all NHS and private billing is accurate, appropriate, and lawful |
| NHS Contract | GMS (General Medical Services), PMS (Personal Medical Services), or APMS (Alternative Provider) |
| NHSCFA | NHS Counter Fraud Authority, the UK body for NHS fraud investigation |
| GP | General Practitioner, independent contractor or salaried |
| AHP | Allied Health Professional |

### 3. Compliance Framework

**NHS Contract Compliance Checklist:**
- [ ] Contract type confirmed and current
- [ ] DES/LES requirements met and documented
- [ ] Quality and Outcomes Framework (QOF) claims accurate
- [ ] List size reconciliation accurate
- [ ] Premises and staffing within contract terms
- [ ] Enhanced services delivered and documented

**Private Billing Compliance Checklist:**
- [ ] All charges accurately reflect services provided
- [ ] No upcoding or unbundling of services
- [ ] Private patient consents documented
- [ ] PMI insurer requirements satisfied
- [ ] No duplicate NHS/private billing for same service

**Fraud Prevention Checklist:**
- [ ] All staff trained on NHS fraud awareness
- [ ] Prescription pad security procedures in place
- [ ] Controlled drug monitoring compliant
- [ ] Referral letters accurate and not fabricated
- [ ] Patient registration checks (identity, address)
- [ ] No "phantom patient" claims

### 4. Documentation & Records

| Record Type | Retention (Scotland) |
|---|---|
| Medical records | Until patient reaches 40th birthday (children), or 6 years from last entry |
| NHS contract documents | Duration of contract + 6 years |
| Billing records (private) | 6 years (Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973) |
| Training records | Employment duration + 6 years |
| Audit work papers | 6 years |

All records retrievable within a reasonable time of an audit or regulatory request.

### 5. Compliance Oversight

**Governing authorities (Scotland/UK):**
- NHS Counter Fraud Authority (NHSCFA)
- Healthcare Improvement Scotland, Scottish Public Services Ombudsman (SPSO)
- Care Inspectorate (social care)
- Information Commissioner's Office (data)
- British Medical Association (contractual guidance)

**Audit program (adapted):**
- Quarterly review: minimum 10 NHS contract claims/samples per quarter, Check: services delivered as claimed, supporting documentation, patient consent, Review private billing: no duplicate charges, correct coding, patient understood

**Error correction:**
- Isolated → education, repayment, remediation, Systemic → corrective action plan, consider NHSCFA self-referral (consult solicitor)

### 6. Training & Governance

All clinical and administrative staff: trained on NHS fraud awareness before commencing duties, annual refresher. Cover: fraud risks, reporting channels, documentation requirements, non-compliance consequences.

**Governance roles:** Practice Manager / Business Manager (policy owner, audit), Clinical Lead (clinical governance), Caldicott Guardian (data protection), Compliance Officer (where appointed).

Review policy annually; update within 30 days of regulatory change.

### 7. Appendices Checklist

Include in final document:
- [ ] NHS Contract Compliance Self-Assessment Checklist
- [ ] Private Billing Audit Template
- [ ] Prescription Security Procedures
- [ ] Fraud Incident Reporting Form
- [ ] Training Record Log

## Pitfalls

- **NHS vs private boundaries**: Never bill NHS for a private service or vice versa; maintain clear segregation
- **GP contract variations**: Scotland has separate GMS contract terms from England, always reference Scottish regulations
- **Record retention**: Scotland's prescription (limitation) period is 5 years for delict claims, shorter than 6 years typical
- **NHSCFA referrals**: Must be considered where fraud suspected; delaying referral can worsen organisational liability
- **No US equivalent of FCA**: UK fraud investigated under Fraud Act 2006 and NHS-specific regulations
- **Mark citations with `[VERIFY]` if regulatory currency is uncertain**

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from the US Medicare Incident-To Billing Policy. Key changes:

- **US concept removed**: "incident-to billing" (NPP under physician NPI) has no UK equivalent
- **Replaced with**: UK NHS contract compliance + private healthcare billing compliance + NHSCFA fraud prevention
- **Legal framework**: False Claims Act (31 U.S.C. §§3729 to 3733) → Fraud Act 2006 + NHSCFA framework
- **Oversight body**: OIG → NHSCFA (NHS Counter Fraud Authority)
- **Audit triggers**: RAC/OIG → Local Counter Fraud Specialists / NHSCFA
- **Supervision rules**: US direct supervision (physician in suite) → UK independent scope of practice
- **Documentation retention**: 10 years → 6 years typical (Scotland: 5 years for some)
- **Payment model**: Fee-for-service → Block contracts / capitation / Payment by Results
- **Scotland-specific**: Healthcare Improvement Scotland; Scottish GMS Contract; NHS National Services Scotland; Scottish Public Services Ombudsman; Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973
- **No individual billing**: NHS pays practice/trust, not individual practitioners
- **Fraud awareness**: NHSCFA fraud prevention framework replaces FCA/OIG compliance concerns
- **Private healthcare**: FCA-regulated PMI; Healthcare Improvement Scotland for independent sector

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
