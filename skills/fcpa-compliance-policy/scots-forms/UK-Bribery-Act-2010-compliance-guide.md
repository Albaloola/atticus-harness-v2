# UK Bribery Act 2010 - Compliance Guidance Note

## Overview

The Bribery Act 2010 is the UK's primary anti-bribery legislation. It is broader than the US FCPA in several respects:
- No facilitation payments exception, Covers private-sector bribery (not just foreign officials)
- Section 7 creates strict liability for failure to prevent bribery, Only defence: "adequate procedures" (s.7(2))

## Key Offences (Bribery Act 2010)

| Section | Offence |
|---------|---------|
| s.1 | Bribing another person |
| s.2 | Being bribed |
| s.6 | Bribery of a foreign public official |
| s.7 | Failure of commercial organisation to prevent bribery |
| s.11 | Penalties: individual, up to 10 years imprisonment + fine; corporate, unlimited fine |

## Ministry of Justice Six Principles (Adequate Procedures)

1. **Proportionate procedures** - bribery prevention procedures proportionate to the risks faced
2. **Top-level commitment** - senior management participation in anti-bribery culture
3. **Risk assessment** - periodic, documented assessment of internal/external bribery risks
4. **Due diligence** - proportionate due diligence on persons who perform services for the organisation
5. **Communication (including training)** - policies communicated internally and externally; training proportionate to risk
6. **Monitoring and review** - ongoing monitoring and periodic review of procedures

## Key Differences from FCPA

| FCPA (US) | Bribery Act 2010 (UK) |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Facilitation payments exception exists (narrow) | No facilitation payment exception |
| Covers foreign officials only | Covers public and private bribery |
| Territorial nexus: US jurisdiction | Territorial nexus: UK close connection |
| Penalties: corporate $2M/violation | Penalties: unlimited corporate fine |
| Books/records provisions separate | No separate accounting provisions |
| DOJ/SEC enforce | SFO, CPS (or COPFS in Scotland) enforce |
| No corporate "failure to prevent" offence | s.7 failure to prevent offence |

## Scotland-Specific

- COPFS (Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service) prosecutes Bribery Act offences in Scotland, Scottish courts apply Scottish Sentencing Guidelines, The Bribery Act extends to Scotland (s.19, Bribery Act 2010)
