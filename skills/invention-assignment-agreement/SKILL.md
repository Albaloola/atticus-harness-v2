## Key UK/Scottish Legal Framework

### Employee Inventions, Patents Act 1977
- **Section 39**: An invention made by an employee belongs to the employer if:
  (a) it was made in the course of the employee's normal duties (or specifically assigned duties), and the circumstances are such that an invention might reasonably be expected to result; or
  (b) it was made in the course of the employee's duties and at the time of making the invention the employee had a special obligation to further the employer's interests (e.g. senior management, R&D director).
- **Section 40**: Employee compensation for inventions of "outstanding benefit" to the employer, unique to UK law; no direct US equivalent
- **Section 41**: Assessment of compensation

### Employee Copyright, CDPA 1988
- **Section 11(2)**: Employer is first owner of copyright in literary, dramatic, musical or artistic works made by an employee in the course of employment (unless otherwise agreed)
- Does NOT apply to works by independent contractors, explicit assignment required

### Data Protection, UK GDPR / Data Protection Act 2018
- ICO registration requirement, Employee data handling provisions

## Core Workflow

### 1. Parties & Recitals

- Full entity name, company registration number, registered address, Employee name, address, position, Recitals: access to confidential information, IP creation during employment/engagement, legitimate business interest

### 2. Confidential Information

**Definition categories:**

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Technical | Source code, algorithms, formulae, processes, designs, R&D data |
| Business | Strategic plans, pricing, financials, customer/supplier lists |
| Personnel | Compensation, performance reviews, organisational structure |
| Third-party | Information received under NDA from customers, partners, suppliers |

**Standard exceptions (all four required):**
1. Publicly available through no breach by employee
2. Rightfully in employee's possession pre-disclosure (written records required)
3. Received from third party without confidentiality obligation
4. Independently developed without use of company CI (written records required)

**Obligations:** strict confidence; company-purposes only; reasonable care standard; return all materials + written certification on termination (including personal devices/cloud); no copying/removal beyond job requirements.

**Duration:** trade secrets, indefinite (never cap with fixed term); other CI - [3 to 5 years] post-termination.

**Whistleblowing provision [SCOTS: replaces DTSA immunity notice]:**
> Nothing in this agreement prevents the employee from disclosing information
> which is a protected disclosure under section 43A of the Employment Rights
> Act 1996 (public interest disclosure), reporting a matter to a relevant
> regulator, or exercising any statutory right.

**Note:** [SCOTS: No direct equivalent to US DTSA immunity notice (18 U.S.C. § 1833(b))]. UK whistleblowing protections under the Employment Rights Act 1996 provide a different framework, the above clause preserves those rights but is not mandated by statute in the same way as the DTSA notice.

### 3. Invention Assignment

**Scope:** inventions, discoveries, improvements, works of authorship, software, algorithms, processes, know-how, data, whether or not patentable, made alone or jointly during engagement.

**Under UK law, the default position is:**
- **Employees**: inventions within the course of duties belong to employer automatically (Patents Act 1977, s. 39)
- **Contractors**: inventions belong to the contractor unless explicitly assigned in writing, always include explicit assignment for contractors

**Assignment triggers (for contractors and non-UK-protected inventions):**
1. Relates to company's current or anticipated business/R&D
2. Uses company equipment, facilities, time, materials, or confidential information
3. Results from assigned tasks

**Assignment language:** use present-tense "hereby assigns" for immediate vesting. Include fallback "agrees to assign."

**Employee Compensation (Patents Act 1977, ss. 40 to 41):**
Flag that employees may be entitled to compensation for inventions of "outstanding benefit" to the employer, this is a statutory entitlement that cannot be contracted out of. [SCOTS: No equivalent to US state invention assignment carve-outs (CA Labor Code 2870 etc.) - UK law applies uniform national rules]

**Prior inventions (Schedule):** employee lists excluded pre-employment IP or represents none exists. Use checkbox: ☐ No prior inventions / ☐ See attached list.

**Disclosure:** employee promptly discloses all inventions in writing; company confirms ownership in writing.

**Cooperation / POA:** employee cooperates post-termination at company expense; irrevocable POA, surviving death/incapacity, limited to IP prosecution for assigned inventions.

**Copyright / Employee works (CDPA s. 11(2)):** works within scope of employment belong to employer. Backup assignment included for non-qualifying works. Contractors: no automatic vesting, explicit assignment essential.

**Moral rights (CDPA 1988 ss. 77 to 89):** employee waives to extent permitted by law. Note: Moral rights cannot be assigned but can be waived in writing. Limited waiver permissible.

### 4. Restrictive Covenants

**Non-solicitation of employees** (generally enforceable in Scotland):
- Prohibit direct/indirect solicitation or recruitment, Limit to employees with whom departing employee worked or about whom they obtained confidential information, Duration: 6 to 12 months post-termination (Scots courts consider 12 months reasonable maximum for most roles)

**Non-solicitation of customers** (generally enforceable in Scotland):
- Competitive-purpose solicitation only; "customers" = material contact during final [12 to 24] months, Duration: 6 to 12 months post-termination

**Non-compete, enforceability under Scots law:**

| Factor | Scottish Position |
|---|---|
| General enforceability | Enforceable if reasonable and protects legitimate business interest |
| Geographic scope | Must be limited to actual trading area, must not be worldwide unless justified |
| Duration | 3 to 6 months typical; 12 months for senior roles; longer rarely enforced |
| Consideration | Need not be separate if part of initial employment contract; post-hire changes require fresh consideration |
| Blue pencil | Scottish courts may sever unenforceable parts but will not rewrite |
| Public policy | Restraint of trade is void unless exception applies, employer must show legitimate interest |

If including: 6 months maximum recommended; geographic scope = actual operating territory; activity scope = actual products/services. Include severability clause.

**Note:** Unlike US states (California, Minnesota, etc.), Scotland/UK has no specific statutory prohibitions on non-competes, enforceability is governed by common law restraint of trade doctrine.

## Guidelines

- **Present-tense assignment**: "hereby assigns" vests on creation; advisable even though Patents Act s. 39 already vests employee inventions in employer for s. 39 inventions
- **Contractor IP**: No automatic vesting under UK law, explicit assignment is essential
- **Employee compensation**: Flag s. 40 "outstanding benefit" provisions, cannot be contracted out of
- **Moral rights waiver**: Must be in writing and specific; blanket waivers for future works of unknown character may be unenforceable
- **Non-compete in Scotland**: Restraint of trade is void unless exception applies, employer must clearly show legitimate business interest
- **Data protection**: Include ICO registration details and employee privacy notice reference
- **No DTSA requirement**: UK law does not require a specific immunity notice, whistleblowing protections are under ERA 1996
- **Verify all [VERIFY] citations** against current legislation on legislation.gov.uk before use

## Troubleshooting

- **No contractor assignment**: Contractor retains all IP, add explicit assignment clause
- **Non-compete too broad**: Risk of unenforceability, narrow scope, restrict duration to ≤6 months, limit geography
- **No prior invention disclosure**: Ambiguity over pre-existing IP ownership, require Schedule 1 regardless
- **Moral rights not addressed**: Employee may assert paternity/integrity rights on termination, require waiver
- **No data protection clause**: UK GDPR Art. 13 requires specific employee privacy information, include as Schedule

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Legal framework:**
- Patents Act 1977, ss. 39 to 43 - employee inventions, ownership, and compensation, Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988, s. 11(2) - employee copyright vesting, Employment Rights Act 1996, s. 43A to 43L, public interest disclosures (whistleblowing)
- UK GDPR / Data Protection Act 2018 - employee data processing, Scots common law, restraint of trade, confidential information, contract, Law Applicable to Contracts (Scotland) Act 2020 - governing law rules

**Key differences from US model:**
- **[SCOTS: No DTSA immunity notice requirement]** - UK has no statutory equivalent to the Defend Trade Secrets Act immunity notice. Instead, whistleblowing protections under ERA 1996 apply
- **[SCOTS: No state invention assignment carve-outs (CA Labor Code 2870 etc.)]** - UK has uniform national rules under Patents Act 1977 s. 39
- **[SCOTS: Employee compensation for outstanding benefit]** - No US equivalent to Patents Act ss. 40 to 41. Employee may be entitled to additional compensation if invention is of "outstanding benefit" to the employer, No §2870 exhibit requirement, UK does not require statutory attachment, Data protection: UK GDPR requires privacy notice; ICO registration; different enforcement framework, Employment tribunal jurisdiction for certain claims (unfair dismissal, whistleblowing) - not civil courts, Non-compete enforceability under common law restraint of trade doctrine, no state-specific statutory prohibitions (except awaiting potential UK-wide reform)
- Legal costs: "loser pays" (judicial expenses follow success) - Scottish rule differs from US, No requirement for notarisation of agreements under Scots law, Service of notice requirements under UK law differ (recorded delivery sufficient)
