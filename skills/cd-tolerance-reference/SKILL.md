Determines whether mortgage offer fees comply with FCA disclosure rules and any binding fee quotation given at illustration stage.

## Prerequisites

- Most recent ESIS / KFI with stated fees and charges, Final Mortgage Offer / Loan Offer with binding fee schedule, Any revised illustrations or fee re-quotes due to changed circumstances, Documentary evidence of any material changes in borrower circumstance or property condition

## UK Disclosure & Fee Binding Regime

Authority: FCA Handbook MCOB 5, MCOB 6, and the Mortgage Credit Directive Order 2015. Comparison is between the **ESIS (European Standardised Information Sheet)** (or KFI if pre-MCD) and the final **Mortgage Offer / Loan Offer**.

### Key Differences from US TRID

[SCOTS: Note] - UK does **not** have a zero/10%/unlimited tolerance framework comparable to TRID. The UK approach is:

- The ESIS / KFI provides a **binding estimate** of fees at the point of offer, Any material variation from the ESIS may constitute a **misrepresentation** or **breach of FCA rules**
- Remedy is through: complaint → Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS) → FCA enforcement, or civil action for misrepresentation

### UK Fee Categories

| Category | Rule | Remedy If Changed |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| Binding fees (ESIS) | Fees stated in ESIS must be honoured or explained | Pass on to FOS; potential s.75 CCA claim [SCOTS: Note: not a tolerance system, FOS awards redress for fair value] |
| Non-binding indicative items | Administration/processing fees subject to change only with reasonable notice | Borrower entitled to cancel if materially different |
| Third-party charges | Valuation, legal, survey, may vary if borrower uses different provider | Must be estimated in good faith per MCOB 5 |
| Early repayment charges | Stated in ESIS; must match Key Facts / offer | Fixed percentage/schedule; challenge through MCOB or FOS |
| Valuation fees | Refundable if transaction does not proceed (lender policy dependent) | Policy-dependent, not statutory |

## UK ESIS / MCOB Disclosure Categories

Authority: FCA MCOB 5 (Illustrations), MCOB 6 (Disclosure at Offer Stage).

| Category | MCOB Rule | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Interest rate type | MCOB 5.4.3R | Fixed, variable, tracker, discount, capped |
| APR (Annual Percentage Rate of Charge) | MCOB 5.4.4R | Total cost expressed as APR |
| Total amount payable | MCOB 5.4.5R | Includes all fees, interest, charges |
| Early repayment charge (ERC) | MCOB 5.4.6R | Stated in illustration; schedule of amounts |
| Arrangement fees | MCOB 5.4.7R | Admin, booking, completion fees |
| Valuation fee | MCOB 5.4.8R | Stated whether refundable |
| Legal fees | MCOB 5.4.9R | Lender's solicitor / panel solicitor costs |
| Insurance requirement (if any) | MCOB 5.4.10R | Buildings insurance, life cover |
| Foreign currency risk (if applicable) | MCOB 5.4.11R | Foreign currency mortgage warnings |

## Changed Circumstances

A material change in borrower circumstance **may** permit a revised ESIS (MCOB 5.6). Per MCOB 5.6.1R:

- Deliver revised illustration **before** mortgage offer is made binding, Borrower must have at least **7 days** to consider (earliest completion rule)
- If borrower disagrees, they may withdraw without penalty

| Type | Examples |
|------|---------|
| Property change | Different property, lower valuation, survey reveals defects |
| Borrower change | Income change, credit score change, added/removed borrower |
| Product change | Switch from fixed to variable, different LTV band |
| LTV change | Valuation differs from expected, fee recalculated |
| Regulatory change | New FCA requirement affecting product terms |

**Invalid reasons:** lender system error, administrative oversight, failure to check correct product tier, pricing mistake that lender should have caught.

## UK Redress / Cure Mechanisms

### Pre-Completion

- Withdraw without penalty if materially different terms (MCOB 5.6.6G)
- Right to cancel (14-day cooling-off period for non-advised sales) [SCOTS: Note: some mortgages have 7-14 day reflection period]
- Complaint to lender's internal complaints team

### Post-Completion

| Issue | Mechanism | Time Limit |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| Fee not as illustrated | Complaint to lender → FOS | 6 months from lender final response; 6 years from event |
| Breach of MCOB rules | FCA enforcement | No fixed limit (FCA exercises discretion) |
| Misrepresentation (pre-contract) | Civil action (delict / breach of contract) | 5 years (Scots law, delict) / 6 years (contract) |
| s.75 CCA claim (if applicable) | Joint liability of lender and credit broker | 6 years from breach |
| Unfair relationship (CCA s.140A) | Court may reopen credit agreement | After payment default (broad discretion) |

### FOS Remedies

- Compensation for financial loss (difference in fees paid)
- Distress and inconvenience award (max £1,600 for 2024/25)
- Direction to change terms (rare)
- Refund of excessive fees with interest

## Pitfalls

| Mistake | Correction |
|---------|-----------|
| Treating UK ESIS as TRID tolerance system | UK has no zero/10%/unlimited tolerance; comparison is to MCOB binding estimate rules |
| Ignoring 7-day reflection period | ESIS must be delivered at least 7 days before completion |
| Assuming "binding" means no fee variation | Fee variations are allowed but must be justified and communicated with right to cancel |
| Confusing APR with total interest | APR includes fees; it is a total-cost measure, not a tolerance baseline |
| Missing FOS deadlines | 6 months from lender's final response is strict |

## Key Rules

- **General credits** (borrower cashback / incentive) - affect net but not gross; UK disclosure focuses on APR and TA (total amount payable)
- **Specific lender credits** (e.g., free valuation) - reduce the fee they relate to
- **Construction loans** - separate ESIS per phase (if phased draw-downs)
- **Second charges** - regulated under CCA / CONC rules, not MCOB
- **Shared ownership / Help to Buy** - additional disclosure requirements
