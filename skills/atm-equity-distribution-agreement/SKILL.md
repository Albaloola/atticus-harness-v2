### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Generates an equity distribution agreement adapted for the UK and Scottish legal context. US ATM (at-the-market) offering programmes under Rule 415(a)(4) do not exist in UK securities law. The closest equivalent structures are:

- **Placing programme** (broker-facilitated placings under UKLR / AIM Rules)
- **Open offer** or **rights issue** (pre-emptive equity raise)
- **Broker engagement letter** (non-discretionary, best-efforts distribution)

This skill adapts the US ATM concept to a UK **placing / broker engagement** framework under Scots law, referencing the FCA Handbook, UK Listing Rules (UKLR), AIM Rules for Companies, and MAR.

## Prerequisites

Collect before drafting:

1. **Issuer** - legal name, registered office, registration number (Companies House), jurisdiction of incorporation (Scottish or other UK)
2. **Prospectus / Admission document** - FCA-approved prospectus (PRM regime) or AIM admission document; file number; date of publication; maximum aggregate offering amount
3. **Securities** - class, nominal value, ISIN, proposed programme maximum (must not exceed available headroom)
4. **Broker/Agent** - legal name(s), FCA-registered (Part 4A permission), commission rate (UK market: 1 to 3%)
5. **Exchange / MTF** - London Stock Exchange Main Market (UKLR) or AIM (AIM Rules); shareholder approval status for non-pre-emptive issues
6. **Existing arrangements** - prior placing agreements, lock-ups, co-broker arrangements, relationship agreements

## [SCOTS] Placing Mechanics

**Placing Notice** - Issuer delivers written notice specifying maximum shares, minimum price, and placing period. Broker places on best endeavours basis and provides daily confirmations (shares placed, gross/net proceeds, placing price).

**Volume Limits** - Daily cap to be agreed (typically referenced to average daily trading volume or bespoke). Programme cap must not exceed available authority.

**Settlement** - T+2 via CREST through issuer's registrars and settlement agents.

**Mandatory Suspension Triggers:**
- Issuer possesses undisclosed inside information (MAR Art 17)
- Quarterly reporting closed period (MAR Art 19 / DTR)
- No FCA-approved prospectus in force / FCA review ongoing, Trading halt on LSE or AIM, Regulatory investigation or sanction

> [SCOTS: Note] The US "ATM" concept, continuous at-the-market offerings through a sales agent under an effective shelf registration, does not exist as such in the UK. UK placings under the UKLR or AIM Rules are typically structured as one-off or programme placings, where a broker places shares with institutional investors within a short time window (often hours). For a continuous placing facility, a broker engagement letter with best endeavours placing provisions is the closest UK equivalent. Consider also open offer or rights issue structures for pre-emptive fundraising.

## [SCOTS] Representations & Warranties

**Corporate** - Duly incorporated and validly existing under the Companies Act 2006; full corporate authority; agreement enforceable (subject to insolvency/equity carve-outs); no constitutional conflicts; no outstanding consents.

**Registration & Disclosure** - Admission document or prospectus approved by FCA; no FCA stop notice; compliant with FSMA 2000 and the PRM sourcebook; no material misstatement/omission; ongoing disclosure obligations under DTR current.

**Capitalisation** - Accurate share counts per Companies House; shares when issued will be validly issued, fully paid, non-assessable under the Companies Act 2006; no undisclosed pre-emption rights; options/warrants/convertibles disclosed (Companies Act Part 17).

**Financial** - Financials present fairly per FRS 102 or UK-adopted IFRS; no undisclosed material adverse change since most recent audited accounts.

**Compliance & Litigation** - Material regulatory permissions held under FSMA 2000; no material proceedings that would make the issue unlawful.

## [SCOTS] Issuer Covenants

- Maintain prospectus/admission document validity; provide broker advance copies of amendments, Furnish all regulatory filings (FCA, LSE, Companies House) and material communications, Immediately notify broker of any event causing prospectus misstatement/omission, Suspend placings during possession of inside information (MAR) and closed periods, Provide reasonable due diligence access, Deliver as required: directors' certificate (CEO + CFO), legal opinions, working capital confirmation

## [SCOTS] Indemnification & Contribution

- **Issuer → Broker**: Losses from misstatement/omission in admission document, prospectus, or supplements (except broker-furnished information)
- **Broker → Issuer**: Losses from broker-furnished written information only
- **Defence**: Prompt notice; indemnitor may assume defence; no settlement without consent unless unconditional release with no admission of liability
- **Contribution**: When indemnification unavailable; allocated by relative benefit and fault; broker cap = total commissions received

## [SCOTS] Conditions Precedent

**Execution** - Signed agreement; board resolutions; legal opinions (Scots law and English law as applicable); corporate authorisations; admission document confirmed effective by FCA (where required).

**Per-Placing** - Reps true in all material respects; covenants performed; no FCA stop order; no material adverse change; shares not suspended/delisted; updated legal opinions and confirmations as required.

## [SCOTS] Termination

| Type | Terms |
|---|---|
| Mutual notice | 5 to 10 business days (allows pending transaction wind-down) |
| Broker immediate | Trading suspension; FCA stop order; issuer material adverse change; material breach; market disruption |

## [SCOTS] Pitfalls & Verification

- **No direct ATM equivalent**: UK does not have Rule 415 shelf offerings or continuous ATM programmes. Ensure the document is framed as a placing programme or broker facility, not an ATM.
- **Pre-emption rights**: Companies Act 2006 s.561 gives existing shareholders statutory pre-emption rights on equity issues for cash. Check whether shareholder authority was obtained (disapplication by special resolution) or if the placing is within the 5% / 10% annual allowance under the Pre-Emption Group's Statement of Principles.
- **MAR inside information**: The issuer must have robust procedures to determine when inside information exists and must not place during such periods. Art 17 disclosure requirements apply.
- **Shareholder authority**: Listed companies need shareholder authority to allot shares (Companies Act s.551) and to disapply pre-emption rights (s.570/571). Verify authority available before each placing.
- **FCA approval**: If shares are being admitted to a regulated market, a prospectus approved by the FCA under the PRM sourcebook may be required. For AIM, an admission document is usual but not FCA-approved (AIM is an MTF).
- **UKLR Eligibility**: Main Market listing requires compliance with UKLR. Check eligibility letter and sponsor requirements. AIM has its own rules administered by the LSE.
- **MAR market soundings**: If sounding investors before a placing, comply with MAR Art 11 market soundings regime.
- **Financial promotions**: Any communication about the placing must comply with FSMA s.21 financial promotion restriction. Broker must be authorised or the communication approved.
- **UKLA / Listing Rules**: The FCA's UK Listing Rules (UKLR) replaced the previous LR regime from January 2026. Check current UKLR requirements for equity issuances on the Main Market.
- **Pricing**: UK placings are typically priced at a discount to the mid-market price (commonly 3-10%), often based on a bookbuild. The placing agreement should specify the pricing mechanism clearly.
- **Scots law governing**: Specify Scots law as the governing law and Scottish courts as jurisdiction. If English law is preferred by the broker, note this negotiation point. Under Scots law, jury waiver is not relevant, civil jury trials are not available.
- **IR35 / Employment status**: Ensure the broker is engaged as principal or agent, not as employee. HMRC employment status tests apply.
- **Survival**: Indemnification must expressly survive termination.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
