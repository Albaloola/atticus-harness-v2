# Investment Advisory Agreement, Scotland/UK Adaptation

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Produces a compliant investment advisory agreement covering fiduciary duties, authority scope, fee structures, client categorisation, disclosure requirements, and regulatory compliance under FSMA 2000 and FCA rules.

## Prerequisites

1. **Firm details** - FCA authorised legal entity name, FRN, principal office, permissions (Part 4A permission per FSMA 2000)
2. **Client details** - full legal name(s), account type (individual, joint, trust, corporate, pension), investor profile (retail, professional, or eligible counterparty), investment objectives, risk tolerance, time horizon
3. **Services & Markets Act 2000 permissions** - scope of regulated activities the firm is permitted to undertake
4. **Fee schedule** - AUM percentages, tier breakpoints, billing frequency, minimum fees (including VAT considerations)
5. **Custodian / depositary** - identification or selection process; CASS rules if firm holds client money
6. **Service scope** - discretionary or advisory; execution-only where applicable; ancillary services
7. **Execution policy / RTS 28 report** - best execution arrangements and annual publication
8. **Conflicts of interest policy** - written policy disclosure

Search uploaded documents for existing FCA permissions, prior client agreements, fee schedules, and client data before drafting. If any prerequisite is missing, pause and ask, do not assume or fill gaps.

## Core Workflow

### 1. Cover Page

Agreement title, party legal names, effective date, FCA status statement, and table of contents if >5 pages.

### 2. Services

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Authority type | Discretionary or advisory with practical implications |
| Portfolio management | Methodology, asset allocation, rebalancing frequency, review cadence |
| Ancillary services | Financial planning, retirement planning, inheritance tax planning, only if in scope |
| Out-of-scope | Tax compliance, legal drafting, conveyancing excluded; referrals to qualified professionals |
| Execution arrangements | Scope of execution-only or advised execution; best execution commitment |

### 3. Fees & Billing

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Advisory fee | Annual % of AUM; tiered schedule with breakpoints and worked monetary examples |
| Calculation basis | Specify: daily / weekly / monthly average; asset valuation methodology |
| Billing frequency | Quarterly in arrears / in advance; monthly for smaller portfolios |
| Payment method | Direct debit (written mandate required) or invoice |
| Pro-ration | Mid-period opens/closes, significant deposits/withdrawals |
| Third-party costs | Platform/custodial fees, fund OCFs, transaction costs, distinguish from advisory fee |
| VAT | Indicate whether fees are exclusive of VAT; state current VAT rate |
| Minimums | Minimum portfolio size and/or minimum annual fee if applicable |

### 4. Custody & Client Money

- Identify the custodian / platform provider, Firm's compliance with CASS rules (Client Assets sourcebook) if holding client money or assets, Client receives periodic valuations; access to platform reports, Disclosure of any soft commission arrangements or third-party remuneration (COBS 11.6)
- Disclosure of any retained interest from cash holdings

### 5. Risk Disclosures

Include with client acknowledgement, selecting those applicable to the strategy:

- [ ] Market risk
- [ ] Interest rate risk
- [ ] Credit / default risk
- [ ] Liquidity risk
- [ ] Concentration risk
- [ ] International / currency risk
- [ ] Alternative asset risk
- [ ] ESG / sustainability risk (if applicable)
- [ ] No guarantee of performance; capital at risk; past performance ≠ future results
- [ ] Inflation risk

### 6. Regulatory Disclosures

| Requirement | Provision |
|---|---|
| Client categorisation | Confirm retail / professional / eligible counterparty status |
| Appropriateness assessment | Acknowledgement that client has received and understood suitability/appropriateness information |
| FCA handbook compliance | Agreement governed by FCA Conduct of Business Sourcebook (COBS) |
| Client money (CASS) | If applicable, detail CASS compliance |
| Data protection | FCA regulation; UK GDPR compliance |
| E-signatures | Valid under Electronic Communications Act 2000; client consent to electronic business |
| Complaint handling | Reference to firm's complaint procedure; FOS rights |
| Financial Ombudsman Service | Client's right to refer complaints to FOS |
| FSCS | Statement on Financial Services Compensation Scheme eligibility |

### 7. Investment Restrictions

- Negative screening / ethical exclusions (if agreed)
- Sector, geography, or asset class limits, Constraints on derivatives, leverage, structured products, Sustainability preferences (if SFDR / UK SDR applies)

### 8. Term, Termination & Amendment

| Provision | Language |
|---|---|
| Term | Effective on execution; continues until terminated |
| Termination | Written notice; typical 30 to 90 days |
| Fees on termination | Pro-rate fees up to termination date; refund any prepaid unearned fees |
| Amendment | Material changes require prior written notice; regulatory changes may require consent |
| Assignment | Client consent required; firm may not assign without consent |

### 9. General Provisions

- Governing law (Scots law or law of England and Wales)
- Severability, Entire agreement / integration clause, Confidentiality and personal data processing (UK GDPR)
- Limitation of liability to extent permitted by FCA rules, Dispute resolution / FOS referral right

### 10. Signature Block

| Signatory Type | Requirements |
|---|---|
| Individual | Full legal name as on account registration + date |
| Joint account | All holders sign; each confirms receipt of disclosures |
| Entity | Authorised representative + title + representation of binding authority |

Include dated acknowledgement that client received, read, and understands the agreement and all regulatory disclosures.

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **Fiduciary standard** - while not statutory in UK terminology, FCA COBS rules impose equivalent duties: act honestly, fairly, professionally, and in the client's best interests (COBS 2.1.1R).
- **No guarantee language** - exclude anything that could be construed as guaranteeing returns, promising outcomes, or limiting regulatory protections
- **Commission / inducement rules** - if you use restricted advice model and receive commission, disclose per COBS 6.1A and COBS 11.6
- **Client categorisation verification** - confirm categorisation is consistent with FCA rules, especially for elective professional clients
- **Length target** - 8 to 20 pages; use numbered sections and TOC for longer agreements
- **Annual review** - flag for periodic review and updated disclosure where material terms change
- **Brexit retained provisions** - ensure references to EU directives (MiFID II, PRIIPs) are accurate as retained and onshored by the UK

## Scotland/UK Adaptation, Key Differences

### Regulatory framework
- **No SEC / IAA 1940** - UK regulation is under the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (FSMA), the FCA Handbook, and PRA rules (if applicable).
- **Client categorisation** - UK rules require categorisation as Retail, Professional, or Eligible Counterparty with corresponding levels of protection.
- **No Form ADV** - Instead: FCA Disclosure Document, Key Features Document, and Statements of Suitability per COBS.

### Terminology replacements

| US Term | Scotland/UK Equivalent |
|---------|----------------------|
| SEC-registered adviser | FCA-authorised person |
| Investment Advisers Act of 1940 | Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 |
| Form ADV Part 2A (brochure) | FCA Disclosure Document / Key Features Document |
| SEC Rule 206(4)-2 (custody) | CASS Rules (Client Assets Sourcebook) |
| SEC Rule 206(4)-3 (solicitors) | FCA COBS Rules on inducements |
| SEC Rule 204-3 (ADV delivery) | COBS 6.2 (disclosure before providing services) |
| CRD number | FCA Firm Reference Number (FRN) |
| ESIGN Act / UETA | Electronic Communications Act 2000 |
| Regulation S-P (privacy) | UK GDPR / Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations |
| Qualified custodian | FCA-authorised custodian / platform / depositary |
| SEC | FCA (Financial Conduct Authority) |
| USD | GBP (£) |
| Soft dollars | Third-party remuneration / inducements (COBS 11.6) |
| Free-look (5 business days) | No equivalent statutory right of withdrawal for advisory agreements |

### Flagged: Concepts with no direct UK/Scots equivalent

- **5 business day free-look right under SEC Rule 204-3** - no equivalent statutory right in UK advisory context.
- **Form ADV / CRD number** - replaced by FCA Disclosure Document and FRN.
- **Full IAA 1940 § 205(a)(2) assignment restrictions** - UK rules address assignment but through different mechanisms (FCA change of control notification).
- **US-style 12-month ADV update cycle** - FCA requires summary of changes within 60 days; full disclosure document update at least annually (or earlier on material change).
- **Legal review required** before execution, this is a financial services regulatory document under FCA rules.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
