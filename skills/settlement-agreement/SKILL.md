# Settlement Agreement, Scotland/UK [SCOTS]

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

1. **Parties** - legal names, capacities, registered addresses, authority to bind (witnessed for execution under the Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995)
2. **Dispute** - case caption, court (Sheriff Court / Court of Session), claim type, key dates
3. **Terms** - amounts, payment schedule, non-monetary obligations
4. **Confidentiality** - scope, permitted disclosures, remedies (interdict for breach)
5. **Litigation** - court reference number, disposal preference (decree of dismissal, with/without expenses; or joint minute granting decree in terms of settlement)
6. **Governing contract** - forum-selection, choice-of-law (apply Scots law), fee-shifting (expenses/judicial expenses) clauses

## Document Structure

| Section | Key Contents |
|---|---|
| Title & Preamble | "Settlement Agreement and Mutual Discharge"; party names, defined terms; reference to "Minute of Agreement" (bilateral contract under Scots law) |
| Recitals | Neutral dispute background (no admissions); mutual desire to resolve; consideration acknowledged (or expressed as "onerous cause" under Scots law) |
| Definitions | Capitalise consistently; define on first use or in standalone section |
| Payment Terms | Amount, schedule, method, bank details, late-payment acceleration (contractual interest per Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 if applicable) |
| Mutual Releases (Discharges) | Scope (known/unknown claims), carve-outs, effective date; use discharge in favour of each party; note Scottish rules on discharge of unknown claims |
| Confidentiality | Protected info, permitted disclosures (legal/tax/regulatory/subpoena), breach remedies (interdict, damages) |
| Non-Admission | Settlement does not = admission of liability |
| Representations & Warranties | Authority, no assignment of claims, accuracy |
| Disposal Mechanics | Party lodging joint minute, timeline, expenses position (each party bears own expenses; or party A pays party B's expenses), prejudice status (decree of dismissal simpliciter; or decree in terms of settlement) |
| Tax Provisions | Tax responsibility, HMRC reporting, s.44 Income Tax (Earnings and Pensions) Act 2003 (termination payments), indemnification |
| General Provisions | Governing law (Scots law), jurisdiction (Sheriff Court or Court of Session), dispute resolution, severability, integration (entire agreement), counterparts, expenses |
| Signature Blocks | Name, title, date, witness (one witness required for valid execution under s.2 Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995); also see signature by commercial parties |
| Schedules | Payment schedule, joint minute of disposal, property descriptions |

## Release (Discharge) Drafting Checklist, Scots Law

- [ ] Define released claims specifically (causes of action, time period, subject matter)
- [ ] State whether the release covers known and unknown claims
- [ ] If unknown claims included, add a discharge provision consistent with Scots law, there is no exact equivalent to Cal. Civ. Code § 1542:

```
Each Party acknowledges that the discharges contained in this Agreement
are intended to include all claims and rights of action, known and unknown,
whether arising under statute, delict, contract, or otherwise. Each Party
waives any right of relief arising from any claim or circumstance existing
at the date of this Agreement which is not within its current knowledge
or contemplation.
```

[SCOTS: Note] Unlike US Civil Code § 1542, there is no statutory provision in Scotland requiring explicit waiver of unknown claims. However, the case of *Bristol & West v Bartlett* (1997) and subsequent authority indicates that a general discharge will be interpreted by reference to the mutual intention of the parties at the time of contracting. For maximum certainty, expressly state that the discharge extends to unknown and unanticipated claims. Use of a "full and final settlement" clause with express reference to known and unknown claims is standard practitioner practice in Scotland.

- [ ] Identify carve-outs (agreement rights, indemnification, future conduct, post-dated diligence)
- [ ] Confirm release is mutual unless agreed otherwise
- [ ] Ensure discharges are framed as **discharges** (Scots law uses "discharge" not "release"; but "release" is also commonly used and understood)

## Equality Act 2010 Compliance (Age / Discrimination Claims)

When releasing age discrimination or other equality claims (Equality Act 2010, ss. 39, 47, 83, 120, 123):

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| Knowing & voluntary | Plain-language explanation of rights waived |
| Consideration | Must exceed existing entitlements |
| Written advisement | Advise to consult solicitor (but not a statutory requirement for EqA 2010) |
| Consideration period | No statutory minimum under EqA 2010; best practice: 14 days |
| Revocation period | No statutory revocation period for EqA 2010; best practice: 7 days after execution |
| Specific reference | Should reference the Equality Act 2010 claims by name |
| ACAS | The settlement agreement must include a statement that the statutory conditions regulating settlement agreements under s.147 EqA 2010 are satisfied |

[SCOTS: Note] The UK regime differs from the US (ADEA). In the UK, settlement agreements (formerly "Compromise Agreements") must satisfy the conditions in s.147 Equality Act 2010: (a) in writing, (b) relates to the particular complaint, (c) the employee has received legal advice from an independent adviser, (d) the adviser has a relevant insurance policy, and (e) the agreement identifies the adviser. Unlike the US ADEA scheme, there is no codified 21/45-day consideration or 7-day revocation period for EqA 2010 claims, though best practice may adopt similar timeframes. For discrimination/equal pay claims, the ET1 claim under the Equality Act 2010 may also require COT3 (ACAS-conciliated settlement) as an alternative route.

## Special Parties

- **Minors / Incapable adults (s.1 Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000)** - condition effectiveness on court approval; guardian or curator ad litem signs; note the role of the Office of the Public Guardian (Scotland)
- **Structured settlements** - identify funding mechanism, periodic payment order (Damages Act 1996, s.2); IRC equivalent: s.104 Income Tax (Trading and Other Income) Act 2005 may apply; also consider Periodical Payment Orders (PPOs) in Scottish personal injury cases

## Critical Checks

- **Neutral recitals** - use "disputed claims," "alleged," "without conceding"; no admissions; Scottish courts respect "without prejudice" labels
- **Discharge precision** - enumerate claims or use "any and all" with express carve-outs; vague discharges invite re-litigation; Scottish authorities on *res judicata* and *exception of settlement* apply
- **Disposal timing** - tie to payment completion, not execution date; joint minute to be lodged after payment
- **Confidentiality carve-outs** - always permit legally required disclosures; include notice-and-object mechanism for subpoena/FCA/HMRC inquiries
- **Integration clause** - "entire agreement… supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements, whether written or oral"; note the parol evidence rule is less strict in Scots law, integration clause helps, but extrinsic evidence may still be admitted (*Dunfermline District Council v Blyth & Blyth*)
- **E-signature** - include counterparts clause; confirm validity under the Electronic Communications Act 2000 and s.7 Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995 (electronic documents are valid as writings under Scots law where they satisfy the "tested" form)
- **Jurisdiction rules** - verify enforceability; Sheriff Court decrees can be granted in terms of a joint minute; decree in absence is also available; note Sheriff Court vs. Court of Session expenses differences
- **Tax allocation** - allocate explicitly across claim types (solatium vs. wage loss vs. termination payment); treatment differs under ITEPA 2003, ITTOIA 2005, and HMRC guidance (termination payments over £30,000 are taxable above the threshold; personal injury damages arising from PI are generally exempt under s.51 ITEPA 2003 [VERIFY])
- **Judicial expenses** - clarify who bears the costs of the settlement (each party bears own; or defender pays pursuer's expenses); note the "loser pays" rule applies absent agreement; if joint minute, the sheriff may pronounce on expenses; early tender before proof reduces the defender's expenses exposure

### Key Scottish/UK Legal Framework

- **Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995** - formal validity: a settlement agreement is a valid "writing" if subscribed (signed) by the granter; a witness is required for documents relating to land (standard security, disposition) but not strictly for contracts per se; however, best practice for commercial settlement agreements is to have each signature witnessed.
- **Damages (Scotland) Act 2011** - statutory structure for personal injury damages; periodical payments, solatium, services, etc.
- **Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973** - settlement agreement is an obligation that prescribes after 5 years (s.6, Sch.1) unless it is constituted in probative form (s.7) or by decree, in which case it prescribes after 20 years.
- **Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977** - may limit enforceability of some settlement terms (especially exclusion/limitation of liability clauses).
- **Human Rights Act 1998** - s.6: any settlement involving a public authority as a party must comply with ECHR rights.
- **Civil Litigation (Expenses and Group Proceedings) (Scotland) Act 2018** - expenses reform and group proceedings; affects how settlement terms might work for multi-claimant cases.
- **Employment Tribunals (Scotland)** - different rules apply to ET settlement agreements (COT3 / ACAS-conciliated settlements); see separate skill coverage for employment disputes.
- **Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010** - if settlement includes arbitration clause (Scottish Arbitration Centre) for dispute resolution.

### Post-Draft Alignment (Mandatory)

After delivering, ask:
1. Is the settlement to be embodied in a joint minute (to be granted decree in terms) or a standalone extra-judicial contract?
2. Should expenses be addressed in the settlement or left to the court (joint minute)?
3. Does the settlement involve the vesting or transfer of heritable property (requiring formal execution with witness)?
4. Is HMRC tax clearance needed for the allocation of payment across categories?
5. Are there employment/pension aspects that require separate advice?
