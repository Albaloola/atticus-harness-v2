# Document Retention Policy (Charity / Nonprofit)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a board-adoptable document retention policy with legally grounded retention schedules, destruction protocols, and litigation hold procedures for Scottish charities and UK charitable organisations.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

- **Organisation details** - name, registered charity number (OSCR for Scotland, Charity Commission for England/Wales), company number (if incorporated), VAT number, HMRC charity reference
- **Record inventory** - types and volume (paper and electronic)
- **Industry context** - charitable purpose area (healthcare, education, etc.) for sector-specific regulations
- **UK public grant status** - whether org receives UK government funds (triggers grant-specific retention periods)
- **Existing policies** - any current retention or records management documents

## Quick Start

1. Collect prerequisites above
2. Draft header block: policy title, effective date, version, board resolution reference, chair signature line
3. Define scope: all personnel, all formats, adopted by board resolution
4. Build retention schedule using the table below, adjusted for Scottish/UK requirements
5. Add storage, destruction, litigation hold, and roles sections
6. Close with review cadence and adoption block

## Policy Sections

### Scope

Applies to all employees, officers, trustees, volunteers, and contractors. Governs all records in any format (paper, electronic, cloud).

### Retention Schedule

Minimum periods, adjust upward for sector-specific requirements.

| Category | Examples | Minimum Retention | Authority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tax Returns & Charity Accounts | Annual accounts, trustees' annual report, HMRC charity returns, gift aid records | 6 years after financial year end | HMRC guidance; Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005; Companies Act 2006 s.394 |
| Supporting Finance Docs | Receipts, invoices, bank statements, payroll records | 6 years after year end | HMRC; Limitation Act 1980 (not applicable in Scotland, see Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973 - 5 year prescriptive period) |
| Governance | Royal Charter / constitution, memorandum & articles of association, minutes, conflict of interest register | Permanent | Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005; Companies Act 2006 |
| Audit / Independent Examination | External audit reports, independent examiner's report, management letters | Permanent | Best practice; OSCR / Charity Commission guidance; funder requirements |
| Bank & Financial | Statements, reconciliations, credit control | 6 years | HMRC; Companies Act 2006 |
| Grant Records | Applications, grant agreements, monitoring reports, budgets | 6 years after grant closeout | Funder terms; OSCR guidance; grant agreement conditions |
| PAYE & National Insurance | RTI submissions, P60s, P45s, P11D, PAYE settlement agreements | 3 years after tax year | HMRC PAYE regulations |
| Personnel Files | Applications, contracts of employment, performance reviews, termination letters | 6 years after employment ends | Employment Rights Act 1996; s.1 statement retention; Equality Act 2010 claims (6 months to ET) |
| Right to Work Checks | Passport/visa copies, BRP, share code checks | 2 years after employment ends | Immigration, Asylum and Nationality Act 2006; Home Office guidance |
| Payroll Records | Timesheets, pay slips, overtime records | 6 years | HMRC; Working Time Regulations 1998; National Minimum Wage Act 1998 |
| Pension / Auto-Enrolment | Trust deeds, scheme booklets, employer records, AE opt-out forms | 6 years after cessation | Pensions Act 2008; Pensions Regulator |
| Contracts & Leases | Supplier/service agreements, MOUs, property leases, missives | 10 years after expiry (6 years minimum - 5 year prescriptive period in Scotland) | Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973 (5 years) + buffer |
| Insurance Policies | Policies, certificates, claims, PI/liability records | 15 years after expiry | Latent damage; employer's liability claims (ELTO, must retain EL insurance certificates permanently or 40 years) |
| Program / Client Records | Service delivery, case files, beneficiary records | 6 years or per funder/licensing | UK GDPR storage limitation; sector regulation |
| Correspondence | Substantive letters, decision-making emails | 3 to 6 years per content category | Operational (unless tied to above category) |
| Electronic Backups | System backups, databases | Per source record category | Mirror source retention |

**Always verify**: OSCR / Charity Commission sector-specific guidance; data protection registration requirements; and applicable Scottish prescriptive periods.

### Storage

**Physical:** Secure climate-controlled storage, restricted access, labelling tied to retention categories, annual inventory review.

**Electronic:** Encrypted storage with access controls and audit logging; automated backups (on-site + off-site/cloud); email retention aligned to schedule; version control for governance documents. UK GDPR storage limitation principle (Art. 5(1)(e)) requires documented justification for retention periods.

### Destruction Protocol

1. Records Manager confirms retention period expired
2. Cross-check against active litigation holds
3. Department head approves destruction list
4. Execute: paper via cross-cut shredding; electronic via secure deletion/overwriting (NCSC guidance; CESG CPNI standards)
5. Log: document type, date range, destruction date, method, authorised by, executed by
6. Retain destruction logs permanently (for audit trail)

### Litigation Hold

**Triggers:** Anticipated litigation, pre-action correspondence, FCA/OSCR/HMRC investigation, Serious Fraud Office inquiry, data subject complaint (ICO).

1. CEO or COO / board / external solicitor issues written hold notice identifying matter, affected categories, and preservation obligations
2. Distribute to all relevant staff and departments
3. **Suspend all destruction** of identified categories immediately
4. Confirm receipt and compliance in writing from each custodian
5. Maintain hold until written release by counsel

Non-compliance risk: civil contempt of court, adverse inference in litigation, regulatory sanctions (ICO, OSCR, HMRC, FCA). Criminal penalties for destroying documents relevant to investigation: Fraud Act 2006 s.9 (fraud by false representation includes document suppression); Criminal Justice and Licensing (Scotland) Act 2010.

### Roles

| Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| Board of Trustees | Adopt policy; review biennially; approve amendments |
| CEO / Chief Officer | Overall compliance; authorise holds; approve destruction |
| Records Manager / Finance Director | Maintain schedule; coordinate annual review; manage logs |
| Department Heads | Identify records; ensure proper storage; flag expirations |
| All Staff & Volunteers | Comply; report questions; preserve records under hold |

### Review & Adoption

- Management review: **annually**; board review: **every two years** or upon material legal changes, Amendments require board (trustee) approval with version tracking, Staff training at adoption and upon substantive amendment, Include adoption block: board resolution date, chair/minutes reference, effective date, version number

## Pitfalls

- **Scottish prescriptive periods** - Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973 applies 5-year prescriptive period for delict and 20-year long-stop. Contrast with 6-year limitation in England/Wales. Retention schedules should use the longer where UK-wide records are kept
- **Charity regulation** - Charities registered in Scotland regulated by OSCR; cross-border charities may have dual registration. Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005 governs Scottish charity accounting and records
- **UK public grantees** - Government grants (e.g., National Lottery, Scottish Government) may specify retention periods in grant agreements beyond standard schedules
- **Scottish charity tax** - Gift Aid, charitable tax exemptions from HMRC, supporting records must be kept 6 years
- **Employer's Liability Insurance** - UK law (Employers' Liability (Compulsory Insurance) Act 1969) requires retention of EL certificates for 40 years, or permanent. Maintain on register available for HSE inspection
- **Sector overlays** - UK GDPR / DPA 2018, Care Inspectorate (Scotland), Education Scotland regulation may exceed general minimums; flag for separate analysis
- **No compliance gap advice** - flag existing issues for counsel review, do not opine
- **Plain language** - policy must be implementable by non-lawyers
- **Mark uncertainties** with [VERIFY], especially sector-specific regulations

### Key Scots / UK Law Considerations
1. **Prescription and Limitation** - Scotland has distinct prescriptive periods: 5 years for delict (personal injury: 3 years), 20-year long-stop. Contract claims: 5-year prescriptive period under Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973. This affects retention schedule length
2. **UK GDPR Storage Limitation** - Art. 5(1)(e) UK GDPR: "kept in a form which permits identification of data subjects for no longer than is necessary." ICO requires documented retention periods and rationale. This is the primary legal driver for data retention periods
3. **Charities SORP** - Statement of Recommended Practice (Charities SORP) governs charity accounting. FRS 102 SORP applies in UK and is mandatory for larger charities
4. **Scottish charity regulation** - OSCR (Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator) registers and regulates Scottish charities. Different requirements from Charity Commission for England/Wales
5. **HMRC record keeping** - Charity tax records, Gift Aid records, PAYE/NI records must be kept 6 years. HMRC may request records beyond this if returns are late
6. **Company records** - Incorporated charities must comply with Companies Act 2006 record retention (accounts, registers, minutes, generally permanent for governance records)
7. **Employers' Liability insurance** - Must be retained permanently under the Employers' Liability (Compulsory Insurance) Act 1969. ELTO database maintained

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
