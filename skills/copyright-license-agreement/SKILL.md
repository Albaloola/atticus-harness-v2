# Copyright Licence Agreement (Media)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a litigation-ready copyright licence for media assets covering grant scope, compensation, representations, and termination under UK copyright law (Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988).

## Quick Start

Gather before drafting:

1. **Media description** - title, format, copyright details (registration optional; no UK registration system for automatic copyright)
2. **Party details** - full legal names, entity type, registration number, addresses
3. **Licence scope** - exclusive or non-exclusive; rights granted; territory; term
4. **Compensation** - flat fee, royalties, advance + royalty, or combination
5. **Field-of-use restrictions** - commercial, editorial, educational, broadcast, digital

## Core Workflow

### 1. Recitals & Definitions

Define these terms:

| Term | Content |
|---|---|
| Media | Title, format, description |
| Licensed Rights | Specific rights granted (see §2) |
| Territory | Worldwide or enumerated regions |
| Term | Start date, expiration, renewal mechanics |
| Authorised Uses | Permitted channels and formats |
| Derivative Works | Scope and approval process if permitted |
| Gross/Net Revenue | Royalty base definition if applicable |

### 2. Grant of Licence

Specify in the core grant clause:
- Exclusive or non-exclusive, Sublicensing (default: no, requires written consent)
- Assignment (default: no without consent; Licensor may assign to successors)

Address each right separately under the CDPA 1988:

| Right | Granted? | Formats/Channels | Territory |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reproduction | Y/N | print, digital, broadcast | [Territory] |
| Distribution (issuing copies to public) | Y/N | retail, streaming, download | [Territory] |
| Rental/Lending | Y/N | where applicable | [Territory] |
| Public Performance/Showing/Playing | Y/N | broadcast, live, streaming | [Territory] |
| Communication to the Public (making available) | Y/N | online, on-demand | [Territory] |
| Adaptation (derivative works) | Y/N | type + approval required | [Territory] |

Include a reservation-of-rights carve-out and disclose any third-party encumbrances.

### 3. Financial Terms

**Option A, Flat Fee:** Amount, due date, VAT treatment.

**Option B, Royalty:** Rate (% of Net/Gross Revenue), accounting period, payment deadline, minimum guarantee, recoupable advance.

Both structures require:
- Payment currency and method, VAT/GST treatment (UK VAT at applicable rate)
- Late payment interest (e.g., 8% over Bank of England base rate under Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998)
- Withholding tax (if applicable, note double tax treaties)
- Licensee recordkeeping (minimum 6 years, UK limitation period)
- Licensor audit right with notice period, frequency cap, and underpayment threshold (e.g., Licensee pays audit costs if underpayment > 5%)
- Accounting statement format and frequency

### 4. Representations & Warranties

**Licensor warrants:** sole copyright ownership or full authority to grant; no infringement of third-party IP, privacy, or moral rights; no conflicting licences or encumbrances; corporate authority to execute.

**Licensee warrants:** authority to execute and perform; use only as authorised; no ownership challenges.

### 5. Usage Restrictions & Quality Control

- **Attribution:** `© [Year] [Licensor Name]. All rights reserved.`
- **Modifications:** prohibited or permitted only with prior written approval
- **Moral rights:** The Licensor asserts their moral rights under the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 (right to attribution and right to object to derogatory treatment). Licensee shall not infringe these rights. Unlike US law, moral rights are inalienable under CDPA 1988 - complete waiver may not be possible
- **Prohibited uses:** illegal, defamatory, or objectionable content; competing products if exclusive
- **Quality approval:** Licensor review right with business-day turnaround (deemed approved if no response)
- **Technical standards:** resolution/format specs for reproductions

### 6. Term & Termination

State commencement date, expiration, and renewal mechanics (auto-renew or affirmative extension).

Termination triggers:

| Trigger | Notice | Cure Period |
|---|---|---|
| Material breach | Written | 30 days |
| Insolvency/bankruptcy | Automatic | N/A |
| Convenience (Licensee) | 60 days written | N/A |
| Convenience (Licensor) | 90 days written | N/A |

Post-termination: cease all new use; destroy/return copies with written certification (30 days); sell-off period for inventory (90 days or none); pre-termination royalties remain payable; prepaid fees non-refundable or pro-rated.

Survival: indemnification, payment, confidentiality, dispute resolution, and representations survive.

### 7. Indemnification

Mutual indemnification for rep/warranty breaches. Licensor additionally indemnifies for third-party IP infringement from the Media. Licensee additionally indemnifies for unauthorised use.

Procedure: prompt written notice, indemnifying party controls defence with cooperation, no settlement without consent (not unreasonably withheld).

Liability cap: aggregate fees paid in prior 12 months; consider carving out IP indemnity from cap.

### 8. Governing Law & Disputes

Specify governing law (Scots law or English law) and jurisdiction (Court of Session, Edinburgh; or High Court, London).

Optional dispute ladder: senior executive negotiation (30 days) → mediation (Scottish mediation provider, e.g., Core Solutions / CEDR) → binding arbitration (LCIA or Scottish Arbitration Centre) or litigation. Address legal costs: Scots law generally follows "loser pays" (judicial expenses).

### 9. General Provisions

Entire agreement/integration, written amendments signed by both parties, severability, non-waiver, notice mechanics (recorded delivery or email with read receipt), force majeure (include or exclude), electronic signature validity under Electronic Communications Act 2000 and eIDAS Regulation (UK).

### 10. Signature Blocks & Exhibits

Include signature blocks for both parties (name, title, date).

Attach as needed:
- **Exhibit A** - Media description (title, format, sample)
- **Exhibit B** - Approved uses / usage examples
- **Exhibit C** - Payment schedule or royalty formula
- **Exhibit D** - Territory definitions

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **No registration system:** UK copyright is automatic on creation (no equivalent of US Copyright Office registration). Statutory damages and fee-shifting under US 17 U.S.C. § 412 do not apply. However, bring an action for copyright infringement in Scotland/UK without registration barriers
- **Work-for-hire:** The UK concept of "works made in course of employment" (CDPA 1988 §11(2)) is more limited than US work-for-hire doctrine. Freelancers/contractors own copyright unless expressly assigned
- **Moral rights:** UK moral rights (CDPA 1988 §§77 to 85) are stronger than US VARA, right to attribution, right to object to derogatory treatment, false attribution, and privacy of certain photographs/films. Cannot be fully waived in the same manner
- **Exclusivity recording:** Exclusive licences should be in writing and signed by the licensor (CDPA 1988 §90(3)). No official recording/registration system equivalent to US Copyright Office
- **Music licensing:** Sync and master rights are separate. The UK recognises both mechanical and performing rights through collecting societies (PRS for Music, PPL, MCPS)
- **AI-generated media:** UK law states that for computer-generated works the author is the person making necessary arrangements (CDPA 1988 §9(3)). Position may change with UK government consultation
- **Fair dealing (not fair use):** UK permits limited fair dealing for research/private study, criticism/review, quotation, and news reporting. Much narrower than US fair use
- **International scope:** Post-Brexit, UK licences may need separate EU licences. EEA exhaustion no longer applies automatically

## Troubleshooting

- **Unclear rights scope:** If the client cannot articulate which rights are needed, walk through the CDPA 1988 exclusive rights row by row against their intended use cases
- **Collecting societies:** Advise client on PRS/PPL/MCPS licensing if the use will be public performance, broadcast, or streaming
- **Royalty vs. flat fee ambiguity:** Default to flat fee for one-time uses; royalty for ongoing exploitation with revenue streams
- **Conflicting licences:** Request Licensor's disclosure of all existing licence grants to confirm no overlap with the proposed grant

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from US copyright law (Copyright Act 1976 / 17 U.S.C.) to UK copyright law (Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988) and Scots/UK contract law.

### Key Adaptations

- **Primary statute**: CDPA 1988 replaces Title 17 of the US Code; UK IPO replaces US Copyright Office
- **Automatic protection**: No US-style registration system; copyright arises automatically on creation
- **Statutory damages**: No US-style statutory damages or fee-shifting under §412; UK remedies are actual damages, account of profits, or additional damages for flagrant infringement (CDPA 1988 §97(2))
- **Moral rights**: UK moral rights are broader than US VARA (which only covers visual art): includes right to attribution, integrity against derogatory treatment, false attribution, and privacy in certain works. Inalienable and cannot be fully waived
- **Fair dealing**: Replaces US fair use; limited to specific statutory purposes and narrower in scope
- **Work for hire**: UK provision limited to employees (CDPA 1988 §11(2)); no US-style commissioned work-for-hire
- **Exclusive licence recording**: Must be in writing signed by or on behalf of the copyright owner (CDPA 1988 §90(3)); no official recording system
- **Performing rights**: Administered by collecting societies (PRS for Music for composition, PPL for sound recording, MCPS for mechanical); not ASCAP/BMI/SESAC
- **Government works**: Crown copyright (55 years for published works, 125 years for unpublished) replaces US government works (public domain)
- **Arbitration**: LCIA or Scottish Arbitration Centre replaces JAMS/AAA; Scots law has distinctive arbitration framework under Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010
- **Legal costs**: Scots law follows "loser pays" principle (judicial expenses), not the US American Rule
- **Electronic signatures**: Valid under Electronic Communications Act 2000 and eIDAS (UK), replacing US ESIGN Act
- **Currency**: All amounts in GBP (£), not USD ($)
- **VAT**: UK VAT (currently 20%) applies to license fees; no US sales tax regime
- **Governing law**: Scots law (Court of Session) or English law (High Court); no US federal/state court split

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
