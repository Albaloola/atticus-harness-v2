# Explanatory Statement for Corporate Restructuring / CVA / Scheme of Arrangement (Scotland/UK Adaptation)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts an explanatory statement providing sufficient information to enable a hypothetical reasonable creditor or member to make an informed judgment about a proposed Company Voluntary Arrangement (CVA), Scheme of Arrangement under ss. 895 to 901 Companies Act 2006, or Administration proposal under the Insolvency Act 1986 and the Insolvency (Scotland) Rules 2018.

## Prerequisites

1. **Case details** - company name, registered number (Companies House), court (if Administration/Scheme), date of proposal
2. **Restructuring proposal** - final or near-final draft of the proposed CVA, Scheme, or Administration proposals
3. **Financial records** - P&L, balance sheets, cash flow (min. 3 years, or since incorporation)
4. **Statement of Affairs** - filed or prepared schedule of assets and liabilities
5. **Asset valuations** - valuations for significant company property (internal and independent where available)
6. **Exit funding** - term sheets, LOIs, or rescue finance terms
7. **Creditor schedule** - list of creditors with amounts, security, and connected party status
8. **Executory contracts and IP** - key contracts, leases, IP licenses with abandonment/retention decisions

## Quick Start

1. Gather all prerequisites; confirm restructuring proposal is near-final
2. Draft sections 1 to 8 below, cross-checking every figure against the proposal
3. Run the guidelines checklist before finalising
4. Submit to creditors/members for voting in accordance with the Insolvency Act 1986 (for CVAs) or court convening hearing (for Schemes) - do not circulate until approved nominee/chair or court order obtained

## Output Structure

### 1. Introduction

- Company name, registered number, proposed restructuring vehicle (CVA / Scheme / Administration)
- State the applicable statutory provisions (ss. 1 to 7 IA 1986 for CVAs; ss. 895 to 901 CA 2006 for Schemes; Sch. B1 IA 1986 for Administration)
- Document roadmap and cross-reference to the binding proposal

### 2. Company Background

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Corporate history | Incorporation date, registered office (England/Wales/Scotland), ownership, key subsidiaries |
| Operations | Revenue streams, customers (concentration risk), geography |
| Assets | Categories with current valuations (book and market) |
| Liabilities | Secured, preferential, unsecured, with amounts and ranking |
| Events of distress | Precipitating factors (operational, market, structural, COVID, supply chain) |
| Directors' statement | Summary of directors' assessment and s. 172 CA 2006 duty to creditors |

### 3. Class/Creditor Treatment Summary

| Class | Description | Impaired? | Treatment | Est. Recovery % |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Preferential | HMRC (from Dec 2020), employees | No (deemed accept) | Paid in full | 100% |
| Secured | [Bank/Creditor name] | [Yes/No] | [Cramdown / variation / payoff] | [X%] |
| Unsecured trade | General unsecured | Yes | [Dividend / % payout / equity] | [X%] |
| Connected parties | [Directors/Group cos.] | [Yes/No] | [Subordinated / deferred] | [X%] |
| Members | Shareholders | [Yes/No] | [Retained / cancelled / new issuance] | [X%] |

Flag which classes are deemed to accept (unimpaired/receive full payment) or deemed to reject (receive nothing) and do not vote.

### 4. Financial Projections and Feasibility

- **Historical**: 3-year (or life of company) P&L, balance sheet, cash flow
- **Projections**: 3 to 5 years, revenues, expenses, EBITDA, debt service, working capital
- **Assumptions**: state explicitly (turnaround assumptions, cost savings, headcount, CapEx)
- **Comparison**: side-by-side restructuring recovery vs. liquidation outcome per class (required for CVAs and Schemes)
- **Feasibility**: address whether the proposal is likely to succeed (s. 4 IA 1986 for CVAs; ss. 899 CA 2006 for Schemes, fair value/shareholder protection)

### 5. Risk Factors

- **Implementation**: Feasibility challenges, creditor/member objection risk, funding contingencies
- **Operational**: Customer concentration, regulatory exposure, key-person dependency, supply chain
- **Legal**: Pending litigation, antecedent transaction challenges (preferences, transactions at undervalue under ss. 238 to 246 IA 1986), retention of title claims
- **Funding**: Rescue finance conditions, asset realisation delays, IP/contract vulnerabilities
- **Scottish-specific**: floating charge issues under the Companies Act 2006 (ss. 462 to 464), inhibition/arrestment complications

### 6. Implementation and Funding

- **Sources**: Cash on hand, trading receipts, asset sales, new equity/debt, exit facility
- **Rescue finance details**: Lender, amount, conditions precedent, priority/ranking
- **Distribution timeline**: Effective date trigger, initial distribution, periodic dividend schedule
- **Key contracts**: Table of retained, assigned, and terminated, with rejection consequences
- **Restructuring steps**: Entity changes, asset transfers, governance changes, board changes

### 7. Voting and Approval

| CVA | Scheme of Arrangement | Administration |
|---|---|---|
| 75% by value of creditors; 50% by value of unconnected creditors | 75% by value of class; 50% by number of class members | Creditors' meeting for proposals (or deemed consent) |
| Nominee chair reports to court | Court convening hearing followed by sanction hearing | Administrator reports to creditors |
| 28-day challenge period (s. 6 IA 1986) | 28-day appeal period | Challenge period per para. 74 to 76 Sch. B1 |

- **Voting deadline**: [DATE]
- **Voting method**: Virtual meeting / written resolution / deemed consent
- **Objection deadline**: [DATE, typically 21 days from notice]
- **Court hearing** (Schemes/Admin): Date, time, court
- **Effect**: Binding on all creditors/members who were entitled to vote

### 8. Conclusion

- Recommendation to vote in favour, Why the proposal is superior to liquidation/administration, IP/Advisor contact information, Standard disclaimer on forward-looking statements and reliance on assumptions

## Guidelines

- **Proposal consistency**: Every treatment, amount, and timing must match the formal proposal exactly, discrepancies are grounds for challenge
- **Plain language**: Class treatments must be readable by unsecured trade creditors; define all terms on first use
- **Statutory references**: All statutory provisions must be precisely cited (IA 1986, CA 2006, Insolvency (Scotland/England) Rules)
- **Source attribution**: Pinpoint-cite factual assertions to filed documents (SoA, financial statements, valuations)
- **Liquidation comparison**: Must be prepared or reviewed by a qualified insolvency practitioner, flag for client's IP
- **Scotland-specific rules**: Unless the company is registered in Scotland, note that Insolvency (England and Wales) Rules 2016 apply. For Scottish companies: Insolvency (Scotland) Rules 2018. Different rules apply to voting, forms, and court procedures
- **Projections disclaimer**: Include forward-looking statement disclaimer per R3 / IPA guidelines
- **Vote solicitation**: For CVAs, no vote solicitation until nominee's report is issued. For Schemes, no vote solicitation until after the convening order is granted
- **Pre-pack administration**: If applicable, flag compliance with SIP 16 / Statement of Insolvency Practice (Scotland)

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from US Chapter 11 disclosure statement practice for use under UK/Scottish insolvency restructuring law.

**Key changes:**
- **No direct Chapter 11 equivalent**: The US Chapter 11 disclosure statement/plan of reorganisation model has no direct UK equivalent. Replaced with three UK restructuring mechanisms: Company Voluntary Arrangement (CVA, ss. 1 to 7 IA 1986), Scheme of Arrangement (ss. 895 to 901 CA 2006), and Administration (Sch. B1 IA 1986)
- **Legal framework**: replaced 11 U.S.C. §§ 1125, 1129 and FRBP 3016 with the Insolvency Act 1986, Companies Act 2006 Parts 26/26A, and Insolvency (Scotland/England) Rules
- **Document type**: replaced 'Disclosure Statement' with 'Explanatory Statement' (CVA) or 'Explanatory Statement' (Scheme) or 'Statement of Proposals' (Administration). Different documents for each vehicle
- **Regulator**: replaced USTP (US Trustee) with the Insolvency Service (UK) / Accountant in Bankruptcy (Scotland). No equivalent of the US Trustee in Scotland, IPs regulate themselves via Recognised Professional Bodies (RPBs)
- **Voting thresholds**: replaced US Bankruptcy Code class voting (impaired classes, 2/3 amount, 1/2 number for plans) with: CVA requires 75% by value of creditors voting + 50% by value of unconnected creditors. Schemes require 75% by value + 50% by number of class members
- **Cramdown**: no direct US-style cramdown. Dissenting classes in Schemes are bound if the statutory majorities are met. For CVAs, the 75%/50% test applies with no cross-class cramdown
- **Exclusivity period**: no equivalent to the US debtor-in-possession exclusivity period. Any party may propose a CVA; Schemes are proposed by the company; Administration proposals by the administrator
- **Best-interests test**: replaced US liquidation analysis requirement with a comparison showing that each creditor receives no less than they would in liquidation (inherent in UK CVA/IA 1986)
- **Feasibility**: no § 1129(a)(11) feasibility requirement per se, CVAs require the proposal to be 'capable of implementation'; Schemes require the court to be satisfied that the compromise is fair
- **Debtor-in-possession**: no DIP concept under UK law. In Administration, the administrator takes control; CVAs are supervised by a nominee/supervisor (IP)
- **Currency**: converted from USD ($) to GBP (£)
- **Scotland-specific**: noted the distinction between Insolvency (Scotland) Rules 2018 and Insolvency (England and Wales) Rules 2016 for Scottish-registered companies. Accountant in Bankruptcy (Scotland) as regulatory body
- **Terminology**: replaced 'debtor' with 'company/member', 'Chapter 7' with 'liquidation', 'proof of claim' with 'statement of claim/admit', 'claim objection' with 'admission/rejection'

**Status:** Matter-of-law conversions verified against Insolvency Act 1986, Companies Act 2006, and applicable Insolvency Rules. Specific procedures (pre-pack, SIP compliance) require IP oversight.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
