# UK Guidance: Elder Law in Scotland

## Key Legislation and Framework

### Adult Support and Protection

- **Adult Support and Protection (Scotland) Act 2007** - the primary Scottish legislation protecting adults at risk of harm
- **Adult Protection Committees (APCs)** - multi-agency bodies in each local authority area
- **Three duties**: investigation (s.4), removal (s.14), banning orders (s.19-22) - similar to US APS but statutory framework differs
- **"Adults at risk"** defined as adults aged 16+ unable to safeguard themselves due to disability, illness, or mental disorder, and at risk of harm

### Healthcare Rights & Advance Directives

- **Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000** - governs mental capacity, powers of attorney, and guardianship
- **Power of Attorney (Continuing and Welfare)** - Scottish POA replaces US durable POA; Continuing covers financial, Welfare covers health/personal decisions
- **Advance Directives** - recognised in Scotland but less codified than US living wills; common law recognition
- **NHS Scotland** - free at point of use; no Medicare/Medicaid equivalent in US sense
- **NHS Continuing Healthcare** - fully funded care for those with primary health needs (not social care)
- **Self-directed Support (Scotland) Act 2013** - governs social care; service users can direct their own care budget

### Benefits & Income (UK Equivalent of US Entitlements)

| US Benefit | UK Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Medicare (Part A/B) | NHS (universal, free at point of use) |
| Medicaid (long-term care) | Means-tested social care via local authority / NHS CHC |
| Social Security | State Pension / Pension Credit |
| SSI | Pension Credit / Universal Credit (for working-age) |
| SSDI | Personal Independence Payment (PIP) / Attendance Allowance (65+) |
| Veterans' benefits | Armed Forces Pension / War Disablement Pension |

### Guardianship (Scotland)

- **Guardianship Order** (not conservatorship) - under Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000
- **Welfare Guardian** - health/personal decisions
- **Financial Guardian** - property/financial affairs
- **Intervention Order** - for specific one-off decisions (less restrictive option)
- **Office of the Public Guardian (Scotland)** - oversees financial guardians and attorneys
- **Mental Welfare Commission** - oversees welfare guardians and monitors care

### Financial Protection

- **Office of the Public Guardian (Scotland)** - registers POAs and guardianship orders
- **Financial abuse** - reported to local authority Adult Protection Committee and, if criminal, to COPFS
- **Court of Protection** is England & Wales only; Scotland has Sheriff Court (with OPG) for capacity matters

## Key Differences from US

| US Concept | Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| State-specific Medicaid | Means-tested social care (varies by local authority) |
| APS (state-level) | Adult Protection Committees (local authority) |
| Durable POA | Continuing and Welfare Power of Attorney |
| Conservatorship | Guardianship Order (financial/welfare) |
| HIPAA | Data Protection Act 2018 / UK GDPR + common law confidentiality |
| Medicare | NHS Scotland (universal) |
| Look-back period | No direct equivalent, Deprivation of Assets rules under Social Work (Scotland) Act |
| Protected categories | Adults at Risk (s.3 ASP Act 2007) |
| Nursing home residents' rights | Care Inspectorate / Scottish Public Services Ombudsman |

## Practical Note

Elder law in Scotland operates within a completely different health and social care framework from the US. The core analysis skills (identifying issues, prioritising actions, reviewing documents) transfer, but substantive law on benefits, capacity, abuse, and long-term care is entirely different. The Scottish system has no Medicare/Medicaid, no HIPAA, no SSI, all replaced by UK-wide or Scotland-specific equivalents listed above.

Sources: Adult Support and Protection (Scotland) Act 2007; Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000; Self-directed Support (Scotland) Act 2013; NHS Continuing Healthcare guidance; Office of the Public Guardian Scotland.
