# UK Securities Compliance (FSMA) Framework

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

## Why This Skill Exists

In the US, Blue Sky compliance is a multi-state maze. In the UK, securities regulation is a **single jurisdiction** under the FCA and FSMA 2000. [SCOTS: Note] The primary risk is not missed state filings but breaches of FSMA s.19 (general prohibition on regulated activities), s.21 (financial promotion restriction), and s.85 (prospectus requirement) - each carrying criminal penalties. This skill produces an integrated compliance memo and execution plan for UK securities offerings, covering FSMA exemptions, FCA notification, and ongoing obligations.

## Checkpoint A: Pre-Draft Intake (Mandatory)

Ask every time unless the user says "use defaults" or "just draft." Gather:

1. **Offering summary** - security type, aggregate amount, price/valuation, planned closing dates, single-close vs. rolling/evergreen
2. **UK legal basis** - FSMA exemption pathway (see Step 1): Article 50 (professional clients), Article 69/70 (high net worth/certified sophisticated), Article 54 (club/debenture), s.86(1) prospectus exemption, or full FCA prospectus
3. **Offering documents** - PPM, investor deck, term sheet, subscription agreement, investor questionnaire, suitability/certification forms
4. **Investor geography** - UK residents vs. non-UK (offshore); note UK covers Scotland, England, Wales, NI
5. **Intermediaries** - identity of any placing agents, authorised representatives, or corporate finance advisers; FCA-authorised status and permissions
6. **First issue date** - confirmed or projected
7. **Prior offerings** - existing securities, ongoing programmes, prior FCA notifications

**If the user doesn't respond**, apply and clearly label these defaults: private placement to professional clients (Article 50); no general solicitation; single UK jurisdiction; no FCA prospectus approval required.

Optional: priority investor categories, issuer type (UK plc/Ltd/LLP), non-UK investors (overseas persons exemption), expected secondary transfers or SPV structures.

## Step 1: Classify FSMA Exemption Pathway

Identify which FSMA gateway the offering uses:

| Exemption | FSMA/Statutory Instrument | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Article 50** (Professional clients) | Financial Promotion Order 2005 (SI 2005/1529) | No financial promotion restriction |
| **Article 54** (Members' clubs/employees/credit unions) | FPO 2005 | No financial promotion restriction |
| **Articles 69-70** (High net worth / certified sophisticated) | FPO 2005 | Conditional exemption |
| **Article 68** (Self-certified sophisticated) | FPO 2005 | Conditional exemption |
| **Article 72** (Certified high net worth individuals) | FPO 2005 | Conditional exemption |
| **s.86(1) prospectus exemption** | FSMA 2000 | No approved prospectus needed (professional/de minimis/retail) |
| **Prospectus Regulation** | UK PR (retained EU 2017/1129) | Full FCA-approved prospectus required |
| **Overseas persons** | RAO Art. 18(2)/FPO Art. 59 | No UK permission needed if no UK solicitation |

Document factual basis for exemption selection, investor category confirmation, certification process, and why all conditions are satisfied. [SCOTS: Note] Unlike US Blue Sky analysis, there is no state-by-state dimension, the UK is a single regulatory jurisdiction.

---

## Step 2: Map Investor Categories and Financial Promotion Compliance

For each investor category, document:

- **Professional clients** (per FCA COBS 3.5): per se (regulated entities, governments) or elective (meeting size/experience criteria)
- **Certified high net worth individuals**: £100k+ annual income / £250k+ net assets (Art. 72 FPO); self-certification or verification?
- **Certified / self-certified sophisticated investors**: angels, business experience, or network membership
- **Article 54**: employees of the issuer or group, or members of a club/society

**Evidence to preserve:** Investor certificates (signed and dated), income/asset verification, suitability records, financial promotion fair value and clear analysis under FCA rules.

**Negative space:** Document investor categories excluded and why (e.g., retail clients limited to FCA-authorised firms).

---

## Step 3: Build Compliance Pathway

For the single UK jurisdiction:

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| FSMA gateway | Exemption relied upon |
| FCA notification required? | Not normally for exempt offerings; check for specific instruments |
| Financial promotion review | "Fair, clear and not misleading" (FCA PRIN 2A/MCOB/COBS) |
| Forms required | None for most exempt offerings; prospectus via FCA systems |
| FCA fees | None for exempt; prospectus approval fee ~£60k-£100k+ |
| Filing deadline | Before communication (financial promotion) / before offer (prospectus) |
| Anti-money laundering | Register with HMRC / FCA if relevant |

**Intermediary check:** Confirm any placing agents or finders are FCA-authorised with appropriate permissions (Article 25A RAO exemption for introducing). An unauthorised intermediary can invalidate the financial promotion exemption and create s.19 / s.21 criminal exposure.

**UK-specific flags:**
- **Financial promotion restriction (FSMA s.21)**: A criminal offence to communicate an invitation or inducement to engage in investment activity unless made by an authorised person, approved by an authorised person, or covered by an exemption
- **Prospectus Requirement (FSMA s.85)**: An approved prospectus is required for transferable securities offered to the public or admitted to trading on a UK regulated market, unless an exemption applies
- **MAR (Market Abuse Regulation)**: Monitoring for inside information disclosure obligations; managing closed periods for share schemes
- **Consumer Duty (PRIN 2A)**: For retail offerings, the FCA Consumer Duty requires firms to deliver good outcomes; impacts communications and product design

## Step 4: Execute Documentation and Notification Workflow

**Document preparation timing:** All financial promotions (PPM, investment summary, teaser) must comply with FSMA s.21 before distribution.

**"First issue/sale" definition:** When the investor is bound (acceptance of offer) - not when marketing materials are distributed.

**Documentation checklist:**
- [ ] Confirm issuing entity and share class/security type
- [ ] Verify constitutional documents (articles of association, shareholder agreements) permit the issue
- [ ] Draft board resolution approving the issue and allotment
- [ ] File SH01 (return of allotment) at Companies House within 1 month
- [ ] File confirmation statement confirming share capital
- [ ] Complete Companies House register of members update
- [ ] For plc: additional requirements under Companies Act 2006 s.551 (authority to allot) and s.561 (pre-emption rights)
- [ ] Complete AML/KYC checks on all investors

**Multi-tranche offerings:** File separate SH01 returns for each allotment.

**Fee budgeting:** Companies House fees for SH01 are nominal. FCA fees for authorised fund or prospectus depend on structure.

---

## Step 5: Manage Ongoing Compliance

**Post-issuance obligations:**
- **Companies House**: Maintain register of members; file annual confirmation statement (including statement of capital); file SH01 within 1 month of each allotment
- **FCA**: For listed/regulated entities, continuing obligations (DTR, MAR, LR); for fund-raising from professional/high net worth, typically no ongoing FCA compliance beyond general law
- **HMRC**: Issue share certificates; maintain statutory books; report EMI/share scheme filings (if applicable)
- **Venture Capital Schemes**: If using EIS/SEIT/VCT relief, comply with HMRC requirements, submit compliance statements within 2 years
- **Scotland-specific**: Scottish incorporated companies registered at Companies House Edinburgh; Scottish registers; Scottish rate of income tax for investor reporting

**Amendment triggers:**
- Change in security type or rights attaching to shares, Issue to new investor category not covered by original exemption analysis, Material change in use of proceeds, Expansion into retail investors (requires full FCA authorisation or prospectus)

**Escalation protocol:** "If any investor is added in a category or jurisdiction not covered by the existing FSMA exemption analysis, counsel must be notified before circulating final subscription documents."

## Guidelines

- **Anti-hallucination**: Every regulatory requirement (deadline, fee, form, portal) must cite a verifiable primary source or carry `[VERIFY]`
- **Criminal exposure framing**: Primary risk is FSMA s.19 (general prohibition) and s.21 (financial promotion restriction) - both criminal offences; frame all compliance gaps in terms of this exposure
- **Offer = communication**: FSMA s.21 tracks the communication of financial promotions, not the "sale" - the compliance trigger is the first marketing communication to a person in the UK
- **No state variation**: Unlike US Blue Sky, the UK is a single regulatory jurisdiction, but Scottish company law differs slightly (e.g., registration in Edinburgh, Scottish rate of income tax)
- **Local counsel**: Not normally needed for straightforward exempt offerings; recommend for complex structures, authorised funds, or retail offers
- **Confidentiality**: Treat all drafts as privileged under common law legal professional privilege (Scotland: see Bare v Lightwater)
- **Solicitor review required**: All output is draft guidance requiring solicitor review before use or filing. FSMA rules and FCA guidance change frequently; verify all requirements via primary sources before reliance

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Adapted for:** Scotland and UK law
**Changes made:**
- Complete replacement: US Blue Sky multi-state framework → UK single FSMA/FCA regulatory regime, SEC → FCA (Financial Conduct Authority)
- NSMIA / preemption analysis → FSMA s.19 (general prohibition), s.21 (financial promotion restriction), s.85 (prospectus requirement)
- Rule 506(b)/(c) / Reg D → FSMA exemption pathways: Articles 50, 54, 68, 69, 70, 72 of the Financial Promotion Order 2005
- State-by-state filing plan → single UK jurisdiction (FCA + Companies House)
- Form D/EFD → Board resolution, SH01 (return of allotment), Companies House filings, State fee schedules → nominal Companies House fees; FCA prospectus fees where applicable, State renewal obligations → annual confirmation statement + SH01 on each allotment, Rule 506(d) bad actor screening → UK disqualified directors / FSMA prohibited persons checks, Offer vs. sale → FSMA s.21 communication of financial promotion vs. actual investment, Merit review states → no UK equivalent (FCA does not "approve" exempt offerings)
- New York/California state-specific → no UK equivalent (single regime)
- Added Consumer Duty (PRIN 2A) for retail considerations, Added MAR (Market Abuse Regulation) for listed entities, Added Companies Act 2006 allotment/pre-emption requirements

**Key Scottish/UK considerations:**
- No multi-state Blue Sky regime, single UK jurisdiction (Scotland, England, Wales, NI)
- FSMA s.21 financial promotion restriction is criminal, compliance starts with first marketing communication, FCA-authorised status of intermediaries critical, unauthorised activity can invalidate exemptions, Companies House filings (SH01, confirmation statement) are statutory, failure is a criminal offence, Scottish incorporated companies file at Companies House Edinburgh, EIS/SEIS/VCT relief requires HMRC compliance (separate from FSMA)
- Prospectus approval is expensive and slow, most private placements avoid it

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
