# Apportionment Schedule (Scotland, Property Completion)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a mathematically precise proration/apportionment schedule allocating property expenses, income, and obligations between buyer and seller at settlement (completion).

## Prerequisites

Collect before drafting:

1. **Missives / contract of sale** - completion date, apportionment terms, effective date
2. **Council tax records** - current band, billing period, direct debit status
3. **Factor / property management statements** - common charges, maintenance funds, special assessments
4. **Lease agreements** (if tenant-occupied) - rent rolls, security deposits, prepaid rent
5. **Utility bills** - water, sewer, waste collection, property-linked services
6. **Title deeds / Land Register entries** - property description, title number, burdens
7. **Buildings insurance** (if transferring) - premiums and coverage periods
8. **LBTT return** - confirmation of effective date for tax purposes

## Quick Start

1. Gather all prerequisite documents
2. Set completion date and day-count convention (365-day default)
3. Determine completion-date allocation (buyer or seller bears completion date, check missives)
4. Identify all apportionment items from the checklist
5. Calculate daily rates and allocations for each item
6. Draft post-completion adjustment provision for estimated amounts
7. Verify net apportionment ties to the settlement statement

## Document Header

Include: document title ("Apportionment Schedule"), preparation date, completion date, property (address + title number), file number, seller(s) and buyer(s) full legal names, and day-count convention used.

## Apportionment Items Checklist

Identify all applicable items:

- Council tax (actual or estimated)
- Factor / property management charges, Rental income and security deposit transfers (if tenanted)
- Water/sewer, waste collection, other property-linked utilities, Prepaid insurance premiums (if policy transfers)
- Service charges / common maintenance fund, Ground burdens / feu duties (if still applicable, rare post-2004)

## Calculation Table

For each apportionment item, present:

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Item | Description of charge |
| Total Amount | Full periodic amount |
| Period Covered | Start to end dates |
| Daily Rate | Total ÷ days in period |
| Seller Days / Amount | Days responsible × daily rate |
| Buyer Days / Amount | Days responsible × daily rate |
| Credit To | Buyer or Seller |

### Day-Count Convention

- **Default:** 365-day year (actual days per period)
- Document the convention used in the schedule header

### Completion-Date Allocation

1. Check missives for explicit allocation
2. If silent, apply prevailing local convention (typically buyer pays from completion date)
3. Document the convention in the schedule header

## Council Tax Rules

- Apportion council tax based on the daily rate of the current annual charge, Confirm whether the property is in council tax band A to H (or business rates for commercial)
- State whether based on **actual** or **estimated** (prior year) bill, Disclose pending rebanding or council tax reduction and explain impact, Allow for single-adult discount if seller claims it, Always include post-completion true-up provision if based on estimate

## Rental Income Rules

- Apportion collected rent as of completion date, Advance rent beyond completion → credit to buyer, Security deposits: **transfer** to buyer (not apportioned); seller receives corresponding debit, Note partial-month rent calculations, Consider the Tenant's deposit scheme requirements (must be registered and transferred)

## Summary

Net apportionment must appear as:

- Total credits to seller, Total credits to buyer, Net apportionment adjustment (credit to buyer or seller)

This net figure must tie exactly to the settlement statement.

## Post-Completion Adjustment Provision

Include clauses for:

1. **Council tax** - true-up if actual bill differs from estimate by more than £[threshold]; buyer obtains final bill; adjustment within [X] days
2. **Utilities** - final meter readings post-completion; seller liable through completion date; 60-day adjustment deadline
3. **Retrospective charges** - seller liable for charges attributable to seller's ownership period (e.g., retroactive factor assessments)
4. **Refunds** - buyer remits to seller any refund/credit for seller's ownership period
5. **Settlement method** - direct payment between parties unless otherwise agreed

## Regulatory Compliance

- **LBTT**: Land and Buildings Transaction Tax (Scottish equivalent of SDLT) return must be lodged within 30 days of the effective date. The apportionment schedule supports the completion figures but is not an LBTT filing.
- **Council tax**: The seller must provide a closing account and the buyer must register with the local authority. The schedule documents the apportionment.
- **Title registration**: The disposition is recorded with the Land Register of Scotland. The apportionment schedule does not affect registration but documents financial adjustments.
- **Money Laundering Regulations 2017**: Applicable to solicitors handling property transactions, consider AML compliance.

## Execution Block

Signature lines for all buyers and sellers: printed name, signature, date, and acknowledgment that parties reviewed calculations and agree to allocations. Label as exhibit to settlement statement. Under Scots law, signatures generally need witnessing (Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995).

## Pitfalls and Checks

- Show all math, every daily rate derivation must be visible, Match name/entity references to title documents exactly (Land Register / Sasines)
- Number all pages; include property address and completion date in footer, Flag estimated amounts and tie to post-completion adjustment provision, Never assume council tax amounts, use prior-year estimate if bills unavailable and label accordingly, Add witness lines if required by solicitor or local custom
- **Final check:** net apportionment must reconcile to settlement statement line item

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

- **No HOA or property taxes (US style)**: Scotland uses Council Tax (residential) or Non-Domestic Rates (commercial). No US-style property taxes or HOA fees. Equivalent: factor charges for tenements/maintained properties.
- **LBTT replaces TILA-RESPA/CDs**: Scotland uses Land and Buildings Transaction Tax (LBTT) through Revenue Scotland. There is no Closing Disclosure equivalent. The apportionment schedule is prepared by the solicitor and forms part of the completion statement.
- **Currency**: All amounts in GBP (£). No USD references.
- **Date convention**: Scottish practice typically uses 365-day year and actual days per period. No 360-day convention.
- **Missives replace Purchase Agreement**: The contract for sale in Scotland is constituted by missives (exchange of letters). Check missives for specific apportionment terms.
- **Legal fees**: Solicitor's fees are separate; no US-style "closing costs" concept. Settlement agents do not exist, the solicitor handles the financial settlement.
- **Land Register**: Scotland has its own Land Register (not UK-wide). Sasines Register for older titles. Title numbers are Scottish-specific.

[SCOTS: Note] The core apportionment methodology, daily rates, credits/debits, true-up provisions, is functionally identical. The differences are the specific charges being apportioned (council tax replaces property tax; factor charges replace HOA fees; no US-style closing disclosure), the regulatory framework (LBTT replaces TILA-RESPA), and the conveyancing procedure (missives, no settlement agent, solicitor-handled settlement). The skill is structurally sound for Scottish practice with these terminology and regulatory substitutions.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
