# Art Law Summaries [SCOTS]

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Produces structured art law research with UK/Scottish case citations, holdings, reasoning, and stakeholder-specific guidance across major art market legal categories, applying Scots law and relevant UK legislation.

### Key Differences from US Art Law
- **[SCOTS: No VARA]** - UK has moral rights under CDPA 1988 ss. 77 to 89 (paternity, integrity, false attribution), which differ substantively from US VARA
- **[SCOTS: No US Copyright Office registration]** - copyright is automatic under CDPA 1988
- **[SCOTS: No punitive damages]** - Scots law does not recognise punitive damages; limited aggravated damages
- **[SCOTS: No class actions]** - Group proceedings available from 2018 under Civil Litigation (Expenses and Group Proceedings) (Scotland) Act 2018 (opt-in, limited scope)
- **[SCOTS: Artist's Resale Right (Droit de Suite)]** - applies in UK; no equivalent in US, Cultural property claims: Holocaust (Return of Cultural Objects) Act 2009 empowers UK museums to deaccession; Spoliation Advisory Panel provides alternative dispute resolution, Legal costs follow success in Scotland (judicial expenses) - opposite of US rule

## Stakeholder Guidance

**Artists** - Copyright is automatic under CDPA 1988; moral rights (paternity, s. 77, integrity, s. 80) must be asserted to benefit; ARR royalty applies on qualifying resales (via DACS/ACS). Destruction of work, no VARA equivalent in UK; check contract terms. Gallery contract terms: consignment, exclusivity, commission, ARR collection.

**Galleries & Dealers** - Provenance due diligence (Art Loss Register, INTERPOL, National Archives SPol database); consignment essentials (title retention, insurance, authority-to-sell); buyer disclosure warranties (Sale of Goods Act 1979 ss. 12 to 15); AML obligations under Money Laundering Regulations 2019.

**Collectors** - Acquisition docs (bill of sale, provenance, export/import certificates); forgery recourse (Sale of Goods Act 1979 s. 14 - satisfactory quality); estate planning (Inheritance Tax, IHT relief for heritage assets; Capital Gains Tax on art sales).

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **All citations**: Use neutral citation format (e.g. [2023] CSIH 12); OSCOLA / Scots Law Citations style; mark unverified ones `[VERIFY]`
- **[SCOTS: No VARA]** - UK moral rights differ: paternity (asserted, not automatic), integrity (derogatory treatment), false attribution, privacy of certain photographs/films; waiver is possible, assignment is not
- **ARR**: Only applies where seller is an art market professional; royalty capped; applies to deceased artists' estates (life + 70 years)
- **Holocaust (Return of Cultural Objects) Act 2009**: Limited to national institutions; does not create private right of action, Spoliation Advisory Panel is the primary forum
- **Treasure (Scotland)**: Treasure Trove unit (Queen's and Lord Treasurer's Remembrancer) - broader than English Treasure Act; all archaeological objects of cultural significance require reporting
- **Export controls**: Export of Cultural Objects regulations; deferral period for objections; Reviewing Committee on the Export of Works of Art (RCEWA)
- **NFT/AI authority**: Rapidly evolving, always flag publication date of cited sources

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Legal framework:**
- Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 (CDPA 1988) - primary copyright statute; registration not required; duration life + 70 years, Artist's Resale Right Regulations 2006 - droit de suite; no US equivalent, Holocaust (Return of Cultural Objects) Act 2009 - museum deaccession power, Dealing in Cultural Objects (Offences) Act 2003 - criminal offence for dealing in tainted cultural objects, Money Laundering Regulations 2019 - art market participants subject to AML obligations (effective 2021)
- Scots common law of theft, good faith, and personal property, UK GDPR / DPA 2018 - data protection for provenance/collector records

**Key differences from US art law model:**
- **[SCOTS: No VARA]** - UK moral rights under CDPA 1988 are broader (four rights) but substantively different: must be asserted (paternity), assignment not permitted, waiver in writing required
- **[SCOTS: No US Copyright Office registration]** - no registration prerequisite for infringement suit; no statutory damages regime
- **[SCOTS: No punitive damages]** - Scots law does not award punitive damages; limited aggravated/exemplary damages available
- **[SCOTS: No class actions]** - Group proceedings under 2018 Act (opt-in); Spoliation Advisory Panel for Holocaust claims (non-binding recommendations)
- **[SCOTS: Artist's Resale Right]** - Royalty on resale (droit de suite); no equivalent in US law, Treasure/portable antiquities: Separate regime in Scotland (Treasure Trove / QLTR) vs. England/Wales (Coroner's Treasure)
- Legal costs: Judicial expenses follow success; legal aid may be available via Scottish Legal Aid Board for certain proceedings, No equivalent to US Native American Graves Protection and Repatriation Act (NAGPRA) - Scotland/UK has different frameworks for human remains and cultural objects, Brexit impact: UK no longer subject to EU cultural goods export regime; export controls under UK law; copyright duration no longer harmonised with EU (transitional provisions may apply - [VERIFY])
- Collecting societies: DACS, ACS (visual arts); ALCS (literary); PRS for Music, PPL (music); CLA (educational/business copying) - different from US counterparts (ASCAP/BMI/Copyright Clearance Center)

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
