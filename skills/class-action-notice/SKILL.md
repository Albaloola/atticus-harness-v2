# Group Proceedings Notice [SCOTS]

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a notice compliant with the Civil Litigation (Expenses and Group Proceedings) (Scotland) Act 2018 and Rules of the Court of Session Chapter 26A, informing eligible group members of their rights, options, and deadlines in opt-in group proceedings in the Court of Session.

[SCOTS: Key distinction, Scottish group proceedings are currently opt-in only (Civil Litigation (Expenses and Group Proceedings) (Scotland) Act 2018, s.20(7)(a)). No opt-out class action procedure exists yet. This is fundamentally different from US Rule 23 opt-out class actions.]

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

1. **Initial writ** - factual allegations, legal claims, named parties, group definition
2. **Court order granting permission** - exact group definition, court-specified notice requirements, Chapter 26A compliance
3. **Settlement agreement** (if applicable) - terms, amounts, allocation, release scope
4. **Court orders on notice** - format, length, reading level, mandatory language, distribution method
5. **Key deadlines** - opt-in deadline, case management hearing date/time/location
6. **Contacts** - group solicitor, defence solicitor, court details

## Quick Start

1. Collect all prerequisites and court-ordered specifications
2. Draft notice sections in order (heading → action → group definition → options → procedures → hearing → resources → disclaimers)
3. Verify [SCOTS: the Civil Litigation (Expenses and Group Proceedings) (Scotland) Act 2018 Part 4 mandatory elements are covered]
4. Apply due process test: would a non-lawyer understand their rights and options?
5. Cross-reference every factual statement against source documents

## Notice Sections

### 1. Heading & Introduction

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Court ID | Court of Session, Edinburgh - [SCOTS: Group proceedings must be in the Court of Session; Sheriff Court has no group proceedings jurisdiction] |
| Case caption | All named representatives/defenders, exactly as in the initial writ |
| Court reference number | As assigned |
| Alert banner | **Bold** attention statement (e.g., "LEGAL NOTICE: Court of Session group proceedings may affect your rights.") |
| Subject matter | Concrete facts, specific conduct, dates, amounts |
| Why received | Court-authorized notice; not a solicitation or lawsuit against recipient |
| Neutrality disclaimer | Court has made no determination on the merits |

### 2. Description of the Action

Draft in this order:

1. **Factual background** - chronological, specific dates/conduct from initial writ; concrete details only
2. **Legal claims translated** - convert statutory citations to plain English
3. **Defender's position** - from defences presented, with equal clarity
4. **Procedural history** - key milestones in lay terms

### 3. Group Definition & Membership

- Quote exact group definition from court's permission order
- [SCOTS: "opt-in proceedings" - group proceedings brought with the express consent of each group member (s.20(7)(a))]
- Break each element into separately explained criteria, Address: who qualifies, qualifying conduct, geographic scope, temporal boundaries (inclusive dates)
- Label and explain sub-groups separately if they exist, List all exclusions (directors/officers, judicial officers, prior releasors, etc.)
- Provide practical membership guidance, Include contact for membership questions
- [SCOTS: Group members must provide express written consent to opt in, unlike US Rule 23 automatic inclusion]

### 4. Settlement Terms (If Settlement Notice)

| Component | Include |
|---|---|
| Total value | Exact amount in £ sterling |
| Allocation | Fund → group payments, expenses, legal fees |
| Distribution method | Pro rata, tiered, or flat; concrete £ examples per tier |
| Legal fees | Exact amount/percentage; note requires separate court approval |
| Service awards | Amount per representative pursuer; note requires court approval |
| Declarator / injunctive relief | Specific conduct changes, not generalities |
| Release scope | Exactly which claims released vs. preserved |
| Risk assessment | Balanced: case strengths vs. litigation risks; why settlement is reasonable |

[SCOTS: Unlike US practice, there is no punitive damages element; no attorneys' fees as percentage of fund, Scottish fees follow judicial expenses rules.]

### 5. Legal Rights & Options

Present each as a discrete block:

**Option A, Opt In to Proceedings (Participate)**
- [SCOTS: Express written consent required, must complete and return opt-in form]
- What to do, where to get form, required info/docs, Deadline with time and timezone, Consequence: bound by court's decision; entitled to share in any award/settlement

**Option B, Do Nothing (Do Not Opt In)**
- [SCOTS: Unlike US Rule 23, non-responding individuals are NOT automatically included]
- **PROMINENT WARNING**: If you do not opt in by the deadline, you will NOT be a member of the group proceedings. You will NOT share in any award or settlement. However, prescriptive periods (limitation) continue to run, you may lose your right to bring your own claim.
- [SCOTS: The prescriptive period continues running for potential claimants who do not opt in, unlike US class actions, the filing does not toll the prescriptive period for non-opt-ins unless specifically ordered]

**Option C, Object to Settlement** (if applicable)
- Written objection lodged with court + copies to group solicitor and defence solicitor, Contents: case name/number, name/address/phone, specific reasons, supporting docs, intention to appear at hearing, signature, Deadline: `[DATE]`
- Consequence: court considers objection; objector remains bound if settlement approved

**Option D, Enter Appearance Through Own Solicitor**
- Group members may instruct their own solicitor to appear in the proceedings, Must still complete opt-in form if they wish to be included, Does not affect entitlement to share in group award

### 6. Opt-In Process

1. **Obtain form** - website URL, phone with hours/timezone, postal address
2. **Complete form** - required fields: full name, address, details of claim, express consent statement
3. **Submit** - online, post, or email; deadline with specific date/time
4. **Retain copy** for records

### 7. Exclusion / Opt Out

[SCOTS: In opt-in system, the concept of "opting out" does not apply, individuals who do not complete and return the opt-in form are simply not included. No formal exclusion procedure is needed.]

### 8. Case Management Hearing

- Date, time, courtroom, Court of Session address (Parliament House, Edinburgh)
- Purpose: court manages progress of group proceedings; rules on procedural matters, Attendance not required for group members, How to confirm hearing updates (website, post, court)
- **Binding effect**: court's final judgment is binding on all group members who have opted in

### 9. Prescription / Time Bar

[SCOTS: Unlike US class actions, the filing of group proceedings does NOT automatically suspend the prescriptive period (limitation) for potential group members who have not yet opted in. Critical warning:
- The general prescriptive period for delict claims is 5 years (3 years for personal injury)
- The long-stop prescriptive period is 20 years, Potential claimants should urgently seek independent legal advice regarding time bar]

### 10. Resources & Contacts

- Group solicitor: name, firm, address, phone, email, website, Defence solicitor: name, firm, address, Group proceedings website: writ, court orders, claim form, FAQs, deadline calendar, Court records: Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service website
- **Scam warning**: Only communicate through named solicitors; never pay to join

### 11. Disclaimers

- Not legal advice, Group solicitor represents the group, not individual members, Suggest consulting personal solicitor for individual advice, Law Society of Scotland solicitor referral service

## Drafting Rules

- **Reading level**: plain English; adjust for group composition
- **Tone**: Scrupulously neutral, do not encourage or discourage any option
- **Style**: Average 15 to 20 words per sentence; active voice; one concept per paragraph
- **Jargon**: Avoid; if unavoidable, add parenthetical plain-language definition immediately
- **Placeholders**: Bracket format - `[DATE]`, `[ADDRESS]`, `[URL]`, `[PHONE NUMBER]`
- **Court orders override** any default format guidance in this skill

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
