# Examination Outline: HR Manager (Employment Tribunal Proceedings)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Produces a topic-bullet outline (not verbatim script) targeting institutional knowledge, policy compliance gaps, decision-making pretext, and comparator evidence for employment tribunal proceedings in Scotland.

**Important:** [SCOTS: Note] There is no pre-trial deposition (examination on commission) in Scottish employment tribunal proceedings as of right. This outline is designed for preparing a witness for cross-examination at tribunal hearing, or for preparing to cross-examine the employer's HR representative. For exceptional cases where commission and diligence is granted, adapt accordingly.

## Prerequisites

If any item is missing, pause and ask before generating.

1. **Claim and response** - ET1 (claim form), ET3 (response), amendments with causes of action identified (unfair dismissal, discrimination under Equality Act 2010, whistleblowing detriment, breach of contract)
2. **Chronology** - start date through termination/settlement; key dates for protected activity, performance docs, grievances, disciplinary action
3. **Deponent/Witness ID** - full name, title, tenure; organisation chart, prior statements
4. **Personnel file** - application, contract of employment, job descriptions, appraisals, disciplinary records, dismissal letter
5. **Disclosure production** - emails, investigation files, policies/handbooks, training records
6. **Comparator data** - similarly situated employees identified from disclosure

## Output Structure

Generate sections in order. Use topic headings with bullet-point question areas. Reference documents by index.

### 1. Preliminary Matters

| Item | Content |
|------|---------|
| Admonitions | Oath/affirmation, audible answers, clarify-don't-guess, no interruptions |
| Preliminary issues | Witness statement verification, corrections, time parameters |
| Confidentiality | Tribunal order scope for personnel/medical data |

### 2. Witness Background & Authority

- [ ] Career progression, dates, titles, reporting chain per role
- [ ] Education, HR certifications (CIPD), specialised training
- [ ] Training on: Equality Act 2010, ACAS Code of Practice, unfair dismissal, grievance and disciplinary procedures, whistleblowing
- [ ] Scope: advisory vs. decision-making; role in hiring, discipline, investigations, dismissals
- [ ] Involvement with claimant, when, capacity, at whose direction
- [ ] Reporting relationship to legal counsel; when counsel consulted

### 3. Policies & Compliance Infrastructure

For **each** relevant policy (equal opportunities/EDI, dignity at work/anti-harassment, grievance, disciplinary, capability, attendance, family leave, flexible working):

- [ ] Adoption date, drafter/approver, legal review status
- [ ] Communication method (handbook, intranet, induction, training)
- [ ] Employee acknowledgment process; last revision date
- [ ] Witness's own-words explanation of the policy

**Practice vs. paper:**
- [ ] Deviations from written policy
- [ ] Who authorises exceptions; documentation method
- [ ] Enforcement consistency across employees

**Training:**
- [ ] Frequency, format, mandatory attendance tracking
- [ ] Whether claimant's managers completed required training before relevant decisions

### 4. Claimant's Employment History

**Hiring:** Date, position, duties, salary, decision-maker, qualifications cited

**Performance (each appraisal period):**
- [ ] Appraiser, rating system, scores, strengths, deficiencies
- [ ] Objective/measurable vs. subjective standards
- [ ] Rating changes after protected acts or raising grievances
- [ ] Standard consistency across similarly situated employees

**Discipline (each event):**
- [ ] Date, conduct/performance issue, initiator, investigation conducted
- [ ] Claimant's opportunity to respond
- [ ] Policy cited; ACAS Code of Practice followed?
- [ ] Comparator discipline for same conduct

**Grievances & investigations:**
- [ ] **Raised by** claimant: date, method, recipient, written record, investigation, findings, remedial action, notification
- [ ] **Against** claimant: same details, probe whether investigated more aggressively or credited more readily

### 5. Dismissal Decision

Primary pretext-testing section.

- [ ] Date decision made vs. date communicated
- [ ] Final decision-maker identity
- [ ] Reasons cited **contemporaneously** in dismissal documentation
- [ ] Reasons communicated to claimant at dismissal
- [ ] Whether defence position matches contemporaneous reasons
- [ ] Alternatives considered (training, transfer, final written warning) - if not, why not
- [ ] Every person involved: line manager, department head, HR, senior management
- [ ] Each decision-maker's knowledge of: performance, grievances, protected characteristics, protected activity
- [ ] Legal counsel: when consulted, advice given, whether followed
- [ ] Temporal proximity between protected activity, grievance, or whistleblowing and dismissal
- [ ] Why issues not addressed earlier; why dismissal-worthy now
- [ ] ACAS early conciliation: was it engaged; any open offers

### 6. Documentary Examination

Foundation for each key document:

| Element | Question |
|---|---|
| Recognition | Recognise this document? |
| Creation/receipt | Created, received, or reviewed it? |
| Business records | Created in ordinary course, at or near the time? |
| Accuracy | Accurately reflects information described? |
| Reliance | Relied on in employment decisions? |

**Sequencing:** Lock testimony **before** presenting impeaching documents. Use documents to refresh recollection. Identify referenced-but-undisclosed documents.

**Priority categories:**
- [ ] Personnel file completeness
- [ ] Investigation files (intake, notes, conclusions, remedial actions)
- [ ] HR/management emails re: claimant
- [ ] Performance appraisals and disciplinary records
- [ ] Dismissal approval chain documentation

### 7. Comparator / Disparate Treatment

- [ ] All employees in same/similar position during relevant period
- [ ] Protected characteristics for each (age, race, sex, disability, pregnancy/maternity, religion, sexual orientation, gender reassignment, marriage/civil partnership)
- [ ] Comparable performance/conduct issues
- [ ] Discipline received vs. claimant's
- [ ] Witness's criteria for "similarly situated"
- [ ] Policy waivers/exceptions granted to others, who, justification, documentation

### 8. Reservation & Follow-Up

- [ ] Topics reserved pending further disclosure or other witness statements
- [ ] Areas to revisit if later testimony contradicts
- [ ] Cross-reference against ET1/ET3: every material issue addressed
- [ ] Cross-reference against disclosure: all witness-knowledge topics covered

## Guidelines

- **Topic bullets, not scripts** - maintain flexibility for follow-up
- **Sequence strategically** - build the record before impeaching; establish what the witness should have known before testing what they claim
- **Anticipate objections** - prepare alternative phrasings and additional foundation
- **Flag knowledge gaps** - if witness disclaims knowledge, probe whether their role required it

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

The following adaptations apply when this skill is used for Scottish/UK proceedings:

**Fundamental difference:** This is a witness examination outline for cross-examination at employment tribunal, not a US deposition. In the UK employment tribunal system, there are no pre-trial depositions as of right. Witnesses provide written witness statements that stand as evidence-in-chief, then are cross-examined at hearing.

**Governing legislation:** Equality Act 2010 replaces Title VII, ADEA, ADA, FMLA. Employment Rights Act 1996 (unfair dismissal, wrongful dismissal). ACAS Code of Practice on Disciplinary and Grievance Procedures.

**Protected characteristics** under Equality Act 2010: age, disability, gender reassignment, marriage/civil partnership, pregnancy/maternity, race, religion/belief, sex, sexual orientation.

**Unfair dismissal:** Must demonstrate (a) potentially fair reason (capability, conduct, redundancy, statutory illegality, some other substantial reason), (b) fair procedure, (c) reasonableness (band of reasonable responses test). No at-will employment.

**Compensation:** Compensatory award capped at the greater of one year's salary or £93,878 (updated annually) [VERIFY current cap]. Basic award based on age, length of service, and weekly pay (capped at £643/week) [VERIFY current limits].

**Preliminary hearings:** Case management discussions for listing, strike-out, deposit orders, and preliminary issues (e.g. disability status, time limits).

**ACAS early conciliation:** Mandatory before presenting a claim. Tribunal has no jurisdiction if EC certificate not obtained.

**No punitive damages:** Compensation in employment tribunal is compensatory only (loss of earnings, injury to feelings). Injury to feelings awards follow Vento bands.

**Disclosure/discovery:** Ordered by tribunal; no broad pre-trial discovery as in US. Witness statements are exchanged sequentially.

**Procedure:** Hearings in Scotland are held in Employment Tribunals Scotland (separate administration from England & Wales). Appeals go to the Employment Appeal Tribunal (EAT), then Court of Session (Inner House), then UK Supreme Court.

**Legal representation:** Solicitors and advocates. No contingency fees; DBAs/CFAs available. Judicial expenses rarely awarded in tribunal.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
