# GSA Schedule Contract (Scotland/UK Adaptation)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Draft an execution-ready GSA Schedule contract with verified FAR/GSAR clause set, pricing framework, and compliance obligations.

**[SCOTS: Note]** - GSA Schedule contracts are a US-only federal procurement mechanism. This skill has been adapted for Scotland/UK equivalents:

- **Crown Commercial Service (CCS) frameworks** - UK-wide central government procurement (e.g., G-Cloud, DOS, RM-series)
- **Scottish Procurement** - devolved Scottish public procurement via Scottish Procurement & Property Directorate (SPPD)
- **Procurement Act 2023** - UK Procurement Act 2023 (in force 2024) replacing PCR 2015. **Scotland has devolved procurement** - separate regulations apply.
- **Scottish Public Contracts Regulations (SSI 2016/no.)** - currently still in force post-Brexit, with Scottish-specific Procurement Reform (Scotland) Act 2014
- **Dynamic Purchasing Systems (DPS)** - Scottish equivalent to MAS
- **Framework agreements** - the primary UK mechanism (vs. GSA Schedule/MAS in the US)

**For purely GSA Schedule work (US federal market), use the US sections below. For Scottish/UK public procurement, use the Scottish-equivalent sections.**

## Required Tables

### US (GSA Schedule)

**Cover Sheet** - contract number, contractor name/UEI/CAGE, schedule designation, awarded SINs, base/option periods, CO and contractor POC. Source each field (GSA, SAM, Solicitation, Award, Contractor).

**SIN Scope** - per SIN: current title (verbatim from solicitation), contractor offerings, scope limits, certifications.

**Legacy SIN Crosswalk** (if applicable) - legacy SIN -> current MAS SIN with in/out-of-scope confirmation.

**Authorized Price List** (appendix) - per item: SIN, item/service, part/service ID, unit, GSA price, commercial price, discount %, MOQ, lead time.

**TAA Compliance** - per item: country of origin, TAA designated (Y/N), substantial transformation basis (CBP ruling/analysis).

**Section 508** (if IT scope) - per product: VPAT/ACR version, conformance level, exceptions.

**Clause Matrix** - all clauses incorporated by reference: clause number, title, date, applicability. Must include at minimum: FAR 52.212-4, FAR 52.212-5, GSAR 552.238-74, GSAR 552.238-81, GSAR 552.238-82.

### Scotland/UK (CCS / Scottish Framework)

**Cover Sheet** - framework reference (RM-number / G-Cloud lot), contractor name/company number, lot(s) awarded, call-off period, contracting authority and contractor POC.

**Lot Scope** - per lot: current lot title (from CCS/SPPD), contractor offerings, scope limits, qualifications.

**Rate Card / Commercial Schedule** (appendix) - per item: lot, item/service description, unit price, volume discounts if applicable, MOQ, lead time.

**Trade / Origin Compliance** - per item: country of origin; WTO GPA compliance statement or equivalent.

**Accessibility** (if IT scope) - per product/service: WCAG conformance level (A/AA/AAA), accessibility statement, exceptions.

**Cybersecurity** (if applicable) - Cyber Essentials self-assessment / CE Plus certification / DSPT if NHS-related; incident reporting process.

**Clause Matrix** - key clauses from framework agreement: standard terms, call-off terms, key performance indicators, service levels, TUPE implications if applicable.

**Subcontractor Register** - per subcontractor: flow-down of framework terms (Y/N), type (supply/service/IT), key personnel if relevant.

## Pricing and Fee Blocks

### US (GSA)

Insert from current GSAR clause text, do not paraphrase:

**Pricing relationship**: Basis of Award customer/category, commercial discount to Basis of Award, negotiated GSA discount, pricing relationship rule.

**PRC** (GSAR 552.238-81): clause number/title/date, notification window in days, effective date rule.

**EPA** (if applicable): clause number/title/date, index name, cap (max %/period), required documentation, CO modification approval.

**IFF** (GSAR 552.238-74): rate (verify current), reporting portal name, cadence with due date rule, pass-through prohibition, IFF never separately charged to ordering agencies.

### Scotland/UK

**Pricing schedule**: Rate card or fixed-price schedule as tendered; discount structure (if applicable); volume pricing.

**Most Favoured Customer / Best Price** (if required by framework): obligation to notify if lower prices offered elsewhere.

**Price variation**: CPI/RPI indexation mechanism (if applicable); capped percentage; notice period; documentation requirements.

**Management charge / CCS levy**: CCS charges a management fee (typically % of contract value, verify current rate). **[VERIFY]** current CCS management charge rate and whether it applies to Scottish-specific frameworks.

**[SCOTS]**: For **Scottish Procurement** frameworks, check SPPD guidance on management charges and reporting obligations.

## Administrative Provisions (Scotland/UK)

- Modifications only by formal contract variation signed by both parties, Audit rights per standard CCS / Scottish framework terms, Record retention per framework agreement duration and UK statutory requirements (6 years for contracts; longer for deeds)
- Disputes: contract-specific dispute resolution clause; arbitration or court jurisdiction clause, Termination: standard framework termination rights; break clauses; termination for convenience

## Compliance Checks

### US (GSA)
- Verify all FAR/GSAR clause numbers, titles, and dates against official sources; mark `[VERIFY]` when uncertain.
- Never assume IFF rate, period lengths, or PRC notification windows, insert from current solicitation/GSAR.
- Use incorporation by reference unless solicitation requires full text.
- Use UEI only, DUNS is obsolete.
- TAA, Section 508, cybersecurity, and PRC must use current clause text verbatim; do not paraphrase.
- If solicitation or mass modification conflicts with legacy terms, flag the conflict and adopt the current requirement with citation.
- IFF must not appear as a separate line item to ordering agencies.

### Scotland/UK
- **Procurement Act 2023**: Applies to England, Wales, and Northern Ireland. **Scotland is devolved** - Scottish procurement regulations continue separately. **[VERIFY]** status of post-2024 Scottish procurement regulations.
- **Modern Slavery Act 2015**: Transparency in supply chains statement required; mandatory exclusion ground.
- **GDPR / Data Protection Act 2018**: Data processing clauses, DPIAs where relevant.
- **FOISA / FOI**: Scottish public contracts subject to Freedom of Information (Scotland) Act 2002.
- **Contract Award Notice (CAN)**: must be published on Public Contracts Scotland within 48 hours (Scottish framework).
- **Transparency**: any subcontract opportunities over 250k must be advertised on Public Contracts Scotland (if applicable).
- **Verification**: all CCS framework reference numbers, lot descriptions, and clause dates must be verified against original framework documentation. Mark `[VERIFY]` when uncertain.
- **Management charge**: CCS management fee and reporting portal must be confirmed from current guidance.
- **Cyber Essentials**: required for central government contracts involving handling of personal data or ICT. Verify if CE Plus or full certification needed.
- **Welsh / Scottish specific**: separate framework arrangements exist, confirm jurisdiction at outset.
- **Rate cards**: standard rate card formats vary by framework (G-Cloud, DOS, RMXXXX each have distinct formats).

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
