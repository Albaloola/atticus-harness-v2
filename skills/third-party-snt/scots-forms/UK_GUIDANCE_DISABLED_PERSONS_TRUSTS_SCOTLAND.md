# UK Guidance: Disabled Person's Trusts in Scotland

## Key Differences from US Special Needs Trusts

### Legal Framework

The US third-party SNT is designed to preserve **SSI and Medicaid** eligibility for disabled beneficiaries. In the UK/Scotland, the equivalent goal is to preserve eligibility for **means-tested benefits** (PIP, Universal Credit, Pension Credit, means-tested social care).

### UK Trust Types for Disabled Beneficiaries

1. **Disabled Person's Trust (DPT)** - IHT-qualifying trust under s.89 Inheritance Tax Act 1984 / s.74 Finance Act 2006
2. **Vulnerable Person's Trust (VPT)** - Income tax treatment under Finance Act 2005
3. **Third-party discretionary trust** - set up by parent/grandparent for a disabled child (no special UK statutory form, standard discretionary trust with specific provisions)

### Key Differences from US Model

| US Concept | UK/Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| SSI (Supplemental Security Income) | Universal Credit / Pension Credit / Income Support / means-tested benefits |
| Medicaid (long-term care) | NHS Continuing Healthcare / Local authority means-tested social care |
| 42 U.S.C. § 1396p(d)(4)(A) | No equivalent, no statutory SNT category in UK law |
| ABLE accounts | No direct UK equivalent, Junior ISA / Trust |
| In-Kind Support and Maintenance (ISM) rules | DWP benefit rules, trust distributions may affect means-tested benefits |
| Payback / Medicaid reimbursement | No statutory payback requirement, remainder can pass to named beneficiaries |
| Crummey powers (gift tax) | Not used in UK, use normal expenditure exemption / annual exemption |
| Sole/absolute discretion standard | Same concept, discretionary trust with trustee discretion |
| Spendthrift clause | Used in Scottish trusts, assets not reachable by creditors |

### Scottish Trust Law

- **Trusts (Scotland) Act 1921 and 1961** - core trust legislation
- **Trusts and Succession (Scotland) Act 2024** - modernised trust administration
- **Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995** - formal execution
- **No rule against perpetuities in Scotland** - trusts can last indefinitely

### Means-Tested Benefits & Trusts

In Scotland/UK:
- Assets held in a discretionary trust **are not the beneficiary's capital** for means-testing, Trust can make distributions for the beneficiary's benefit **without** the assets counting as capital (distributions may count as income)
- Key risk: **deprivation of capital rules** - if a beneficiary transfers their own assets into trust, this may be treated as deliberate deprivation for benefit purposes
- **Third-party trusts** (funded by parents/grandparents) do NOT trigger deprivation rules

### HMRC Trust Tax Treatment

- **Income tax**: Trustees pay tax at standard rates (20% dividend, 20% savings, 45% other income, for discretionary trusts)
- **Capital gains tax**: 20% for most trusts
- **IHT**: Disabled Person's Trusts have favourable IHT treatment (not subject to 10-yearly charges)

### Scottish Guardianship

- **Financial Guardian** - under Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000 - manages property and financial affairs if the disabled person lacks capacity
- **Continuing Power of Attorney** - can be granted if the beneficiary has capacity
- **Welfare Guardian** - manages health and personal welfare decisions

### Practical Note

The US third-party SNT skill is structurally very useful because the core concepts (discretionary trust, supplemental-only distributions, spendthrift clause, no payback) transfer well to UK practice. The main adaptations needed are:
1. Replace SSI/Medicaid references with UK means-tested benefits (PIP, Universal Credit, NHS continuing healthcare)
2. Remove all 42 U.S.C. § 1396p references (no equivalent)
3. Remove ABLE account references (no direct UK equivalent)
4. Remove Crummey powers (not used in UK)
5. Map trust types to UK DPT/VPT framework
6. Use Scottish trust law (Trusts (Scotland) Acts) not state trust law
