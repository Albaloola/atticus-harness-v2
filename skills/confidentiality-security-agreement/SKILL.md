# Employee Confidentiality and Security Agreement (Scotland/UK)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

[SCOTS: Note] This skill is adapted from a US framework using DTSA/UTSA/state-specific rules. The UK/Scottish legal framework is different: trade secrets are protected under the Trade Secrets (Enforcement, etc.) Regulations 2018 and common law breach of confidence; data protection is governed by the Data Protection Act 2018 / UK GDPR; employment protections are under the Employment Rights Act 1996; and restrictive covenants are assessed under the Scots common law restraint of trade doctrine.

Drafts an execution-ready agreement protecting company proprietary information, trade secrets, and digital assets while establishing employee security obligations and post-employment restrictions.

## Step 1: Draft Confidential Information Provisions

### Definition, Layered Category Approach

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Technical/Proprietary | Trade secrets, source code, algorithms, R&D, manufacturing processes |
| Business Strategy | Business plans, pricing, margins, financial projections, M&A targets |
| Customer/Relationship | Customer lists, supplier networks, contract terms, referral sources |
| Financial/Operational | Financial statements, budgets, compensation structures, performance metrics |
| Intellectual Property | Inventions, patents, copyrights, trademarks, proprietary methodologies |

- Cover all formats: written, oral, electronic, visual, Include derivative works (analyses, compilations, summaries)
- Protection applies regardless of whether marked "confidential"
- Note the *Faccenda Chicken* distinction: post-employment, the implied duty of confidentiality only survives to protect genuine trade secrets, not "mere confidential information" ([SCOTS: Note] *Faccenda Chicken v Fowler* [1987] 1 Ch 117; the Scots/UK approach distinguishes three levels: trivial information, confidential information (post-employment duty expires), and trade secrets (post-employment duty continues indefinitely))

### Standard Exceptions

Employee bears burden of proof (balance of probabilities):

1. Already public at disclosure (not through employee's breach)
2. Lawfully in employee's possession pre-disclosure (documented)
3. Received from third party without restriction
4. Independently developed without reference to Confidential Information (contemporaneous documentation required)

### Obligations

- Non-disclosure without prior written authorisation from authorised officer, Duration: indefinite for trade secrets; [3 to 5] years for other Confidential Information (but note *Faccenda Chicken* - express contractual clauses can extend post-employment protection for confidential information, but reasonableness is key)
- Use limited to assigned duties within employment scope, Standard of care: reasonable care, Need-to-know restriction; internal sharing only to authorised personnel under equivalent obligations, Secure storage: encryption (electronic), locked storage (physical), secure disposal, Immediate incident notification to security officer / legal

### Compelled Disclosure Carve-Out

Immediate notice to legal on receipt of court order, warrant, or subpoena → cooperate with protective order efforts → disclose only what is legally required.

### Protected Activity Savings Clause (REQUIRED)

- **Protected disclosures under Employment Rights Act 1996, Part IVA**: nothing in this agreement prevents the employee from making a protected disclosure (whistleblowing) under sections 43A to 43L of the Employment Rights Act 1996
- **Public interest disclosures**: disclosure to a legal adviser, relevant prescribed person, or employer in accordance with the Act
- **Criminal offence reporting**: nothing prevents reporting a criminal offence to any law enforcement body or regulatory authority
- **Data protection rights**: the employee's rights under the Data Protection Act 2018 / UK GDPR are not affected, including the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office
- **Equality Act 2010**: rights under the Equality Act 2010 are preserved (including raising grievances about discrimination)

## Step 2: Draft Security Responsibilities

### Password and Access Control

- Personal credentials; never shared, Minimum: 12+ characters, mixed case/numbers/symbols, unique per system (NCSC guidance recommends 3 random words as alternative)
- No plaintext storage; company-approved password managers only, MFA required on all available systems (NCSC guidance)
- Lock workstations when unattended; log out of sessions, Report compromised credentials immediately, All access terminates upon separation

### Acceptable Use

| Permitted | Prohibited |
|---|---|
| Primary business use of company systems | Unauthorised software/extension installation |
| Limited personal use (non-interfering) | Circumventing security controls or monitoring |
| Professional communications via company tools | Unauthorised devices on company networks |
| | Illegal, explicit, or infringing content |
| | Competitive activities on company systems |
| | Company data on unapproved personal cloud |

- BYOD (if applicable): company MDM required, remote wipe consent, security software mandatory (must comply with ICO guidance on employee monitoring and the Investigatory Powers Act 2016)
- Remote access: approved VPN only; adequate privacy at remote locations
- **No expectation of privacy** on company systems, monitoring may occur without notice (subject to the Data Protection Act 2018 and ICO Employment Practices Code; employee must be informed of monitoring policy)

### Incident Reporting Protocol

Reportable: data breaches, unauthorised access, malware, phishing, lost/stolen devices, inadvertent disclosure, suspicious behaviour, physical security breaches.

1. Report to IT security + direct supervisor within [2 to 4] hours of discovery
2. Preserve all evidence, no deletion, alteration, or destruction
3. Document: what happened, when discovered, systems/data affected, actions taken
4. Maintain incident confidentiality; share only with authorised personnel
5. Follow incident response team instructions
6. **GDPR reporting**: data breaches involving personal data must be reported to the ICO within 72 hours (Article 33 UK GDPR)

**Non-retaliation:** Good faith reporting carries no negative consequences, even if incident resulted from employee's error. Protected disclosures under ERA 1996 must not be penalised.

## Step 3: Draft Termination and Post-Employment Provisions

### Return of Property (immediately upon termination or earlier upon request)

- [ ] All company-issued equipment (laptops, phones, tablets, tokens, keys, cards)
- [ ] All physical documents containing Confidential Information
- [ ] Delete company data from personal devices, cloud accounts, personal email
- [ ] Written certification of compliance (specify devices/systems wiped)
- [ ] Certification required before release of final compensation / settlement

Company rights: inspect workspace/devices, remotely wipe MDM-enrolled devices, pursue legal remedies.

### Survival of Obligations

| Obligation | Duration |
|---|---|
| Trade secret confidentiality | Indefinite (while information qualifies as trade secret - *Faccenda Chicken* category 3) |
| Other Confidential Information (express) | [3 to 5] years (if expressly agreed; reasonableness required) |
| Employee non-solicitation | [6 to 12] months (shorter than US - 6 months typical in Scotland/UK) |
| Customer non-solicitation | [6 to 12] months, material-contact customers only |

- Non-solicitation = active solicitation only; does not bar accepting competitor employment or responding to unsolicited enquiries, Employee must notify prospective employers of continuing obligations, Cooperation: respond to legal process, assist with litigation/investigations, provide truthful testimony (reasonable compensation for time if substantial)

## Step 4: Draft Legal Framework

### Acknowledgments (employee confirms)

- Read and understood; opportunity to consult counsel (solicitor)
- Voluntary execution without duress, Restrictions reasonable in scope, duration, and geography, Confidential Information is valuable; unauthorised disclosure = irreparable harm, Adequate consideration received, For post-hire execution: specify additional consideration (promotion, raise, bonus, or continued employment per Scots/UK contract variation law) `[VERIFY]`
- Scots/UK disclaimer: The employee has signed this agreement after having the opportunity to take independent legal advice

### Protected Rights Acknowledgment (REQUIRED)

- Whistleblowing / protected disclosures under Employment Rights Act 1996, Part IVA, Public interest disclosure rights preserved, Right to report criminal offences to law enforcement, Rights under the Equality Act 2010 preserved, Data protection rights under UK GDPR and DPA 2018 preserved

### Enforcement Provisions

- Governing law: Scotland (the law of Scotland)
- Exclusive venue: Scottish courts (Sheriff Court or Court of Session depending on value); note that for breach of confidence claims the Court of Session has jurisdiction, Equitable relief available (interdict / specific implement) without proof of special damage, Interdict: Scottish equivalent of injunction; an interim interdict can be obtained for urgent cases, Damages: common law damages for breach of contract / breach of confidence, Prevailing party: reasonable legal expenses (solicitor and client costs) - standard in Scots litigation; the court has discretion on expenses, Severability: reasonable restrictions may be struck down while severing the remainder (Scots law applies blue-pencil approach - *First PM Ltd v MacFarlane* 2015; the court will not rewrite a clause but may delete offending parts leaving a valid remainder)
- Integration clause; supersedes prior understandings on subject matter, Amendment: written, signed by both parties; no oral modifications, Assignment: company may assign (merger/acquisition/sale); employee may not, Supplements (does not replace) other confidentiality/IP agreements, most protective provision controls, Third party rights: the Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999 is excluded (Scots law also recognises *jus quaesitum tertio*)

### Signature Block

Employee signature, printed name, date; authorised company representative signature, title, date.

## Guidelines

- **Scots/UK approach to post-employment confidentiality**: The implied duty of confidentiality after employment ends only protects genuine trade secrets (*Faccenda Chicken v Fowler* [1987]). Express contractual clauses can extend protection but must be reasonable.
- **Restrictive covenant reasonableness** is assessed under Scots common law (restraint of trade doctrine). 3 to 6 months non-compete / 6 to 12 months non-solicitation is typical. The UK Government is consulting on a statutory 3-month cap on non-competes.
- **Consideration requirement**: Scots law requires fresh consideration for variations to an existing contract made after employment has started. A simple "continued employment" may not be sufficient. Consider a bonus, promotion, or payment for signing.
- **Blue-pencil doctrine** (Scotland): Courts may strike unenforceable restrictions but will not rewrite them (*First PM Ltd v MacFarlane* 2015; *Caterpillar Logistics Services (UK) Ltd v Cleary* 2017).
- **Data protection**: Employee security agreements must comply with the Data Protection Act 2018 / UK GDPR, including transparency requirements for monitoring, data minimisation, and the ICO Employment Practices Code.
- **Protected disclosures**: Confidentiality provisions must not restrict or penalise protected disclosures under the Employment Rights Act 1996. Overbroad clauses that attempt to do so are likely void (*Parkins v Sodexho* [2002] IRLR 109).
- **Role-based customisation**: Adjust categories, security requirements, and restriction durations to employee access level and seniority.
- Do NOT include non-compete provisions unless specifically requested and confirmed enforceable under Scots common law.
- Do not fabricate statutory citations, case law, or enforceability standards.
- **All outputs require solicitor review** in the governing jurisdiction.

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill was originally based on US employee confidentiality and security agreement practice (DTSA, UTSA, state-specific rules). It has been substantially rewritten for the Scottish/UK legal environment.

**Key changes:**
- Replaced DTSA (18 U.S.C. § 1833(b)) with Trade Secrets (Enforcement, etc.) Regulations 2018 / common law breach of confidence, Replaced UTSA (Uniform Trade Secrets Act) with Scots common law and *Faccenda Chicken* three-category framework, Replaced US state restrictive covenant analysis with Scots common law restraint of trade doctrine, Replaced US data privacy references (CCPA, HIPAA) with UK GDPR / Data Protection Act 2018 / ICO, Replaced NLRA Section 7 with Employment Rights Act 1996 Part IVA (protected disclosures)
- Replaced US equitable remedies (TRO, injunction) with Scottish equivalents (interim interdict, interdict, specific implement)
- Replaced California blue pencil/reformation with Scots severability / blue-pencil approach, Replaced US dollar amounts with GBP, Replaced US monitoring rules (Stored Communications Act, state privacy laws) with ICO Employment Practices Code / Investigatory Powers Act 2016
- Added *Faccenda Chicken v Fowler* framework (critical distinction in UK law between trade secrets, confidential information, and trivial information)
- Added GDPR Article 33 data breach notification requirements (72-hour ICO reporting)
- Added reference to NCSC password guidance (3 random words)
- Date format changed to DD/MM/YYYY, Language changed to British English spelling (authorised, minimise, enforceability)

**Relevant forms:**
- See `/scots-forms/` directory for relevant UK/Scottish employment and data protection forms, ICO guidance on employee monitoring (available from ico.org.uk)
