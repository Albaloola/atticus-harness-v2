# UK/Scottish IP Assignment and Commissioned Works Agreement

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Drafts a UK/Scottish IP Assignment Agreement vesting all intellectual property rights in the commissioning party (Hiring Party) through a written assignment of rights, with creator warranties and indemnification. [SCOTS: Note] In UK/Scots law, there is no statutory "work made for hire" concept for independent contractors, copyright vests automatically in the creator under CDPA s.11(1). A written assignment under CDPA s.90(3) is the operative transfer mechanism.

## Quick Start

Gather before drafting:

1. **Parties** - legal names, entity types, registered addresses (Scotland if applicable)
2. **Work scope** - deliverables, specifications, format, acceptance criteria
3. **Compensation** - flat fee / hourly / milestone; payment schedule and triggers
4. **IP category** - does the work qualify as an employee work (CDPA s.11(2)) or an independent contractor engagement requiring assignment?
5. **Governing law** - Scots law (default for Scottish parties) or England and Wales

## Core Workflow

### 1. Recitals & Definitions

Define "Work," "Hiring Party," "Creator," "Deliverables," "Confidential Information," "Intellectual Property Rights." Identify whether the Creator is an employee (CDPA s.11(2) - employer owns automatically) or an independent contractor (assignment required).

### 2. IP Assignment (Primary Mechanism)

**UK/CDPA framework:**

Unlike US law, there is **no statutory work-for-hire doctrine** for independent contractors in the UK. Copyright vests in the creator (CDPA s.11(1)). For employees, copyright vests in the employer (CDPA s.11(2)), provided the work is created in the course of employment.

Include: *"The Creator hereby assigns to the Hiring Party with full title guarantee all Intellectual Property Rights in the Work throughout the world for the full term of such rights (including any extensions and renewals). This assignment is made under section 90(3) of the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988."*

**Key UK/Scottish points:**
- Written assignment must be in writing and signed by or on behalf of the assignor (CDPA s.90(3))
- "With full title guarantee" is the standard UK covenant form (Law of Property (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 1994)
- Future works: an assignment of future copyright is effective when the work comes into existence (CDPA s.91)
- For Scottish contracts, use "with full title guarantee" which applies equally in Scots law

### 3. Moral Rights Waiver

**UK moral rights (CDPA ss.77-89):** Broader than US VARA:

| Moral Right | CDPA Section | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| Right to be identified (paternity) | s.77 | Applies to literary, dramatic, musical, artistic works, films |
| Right to object to derogatory treatment (integrity) | s.80 | Treatment that distorts or mutilates the work |
| Right not to be falsely attributed | s.84 | Broadly applicable |
| Right to privacy of certain photographs/films | s.85 | Private/domestic context |

**Include:** An express waiver of the Creator's moral rights under CDPA ss.77, 80, 84, and 85. Waiver must be in writing and signed (CDPA s.87). For Scottish law: confirm waiver is binding and not contrary to public policy.

### 4. Compensation & Payment

Specify: amount (flat / hourly / milestones), payment triggers (execution, delivery, acceptance), expense reimbursement terms, and conditions precedent (acceptable delivery, signed assignment docs). State whether VAT is chargeable.

### 5. Creator Warranties

Creator warrants:

- Work is original; no infringement of third-party IP, Full authority to enter agreement and grant rights, No defamatory, libelous, or unlawful material, No conflicting prior assignments, licenses, or encumbrances, Work does not contain any computer virus or malicious code

### 6. Indemnification

Creator defends (at Creator's own cost), indemnifies, and holds harmless Hiring Party from all claims, damages, losses, and expenses (including legal costs on a solicitor-and-own-client basis) arising from warranty breach or third-party IP claims. [SCOTS: Note] In Scots law, indemnity clauses are enforceable but must be clearly worded; indemnity for the indemnifying party's own negligence requires express wording.

### 7. Delivery & Acceptance

- Format and delivery method, Revision rounds and acceptance procedure, Hiring Party's right to modify Work without Creator approval, Return/destruction of confidential materials on completion

### 8. Attribution (Optional)

Specify credit form and placement, or confirm no attribution required. Hiring Party may remove attribution upon substantial modification. Note: even with a moral rights waiver, the Creator may retain residual rights to be identified in certain circumstances.

### 9. Boilerplate

| Provision | Key Points |
|---|---|
| Independent contractor | No employment relationship; Creator handles own tax, NI, VAT |
| Confidentiality | Creator's obligations re Hiring Party proprietary info |
| Governing law & jurisdiction | Scots law, Scottish courts (Sheriff Court or Court of Session depending on value) |
| Assignment | Hiring Party may assign; Creator may not without consent |
| Termination | Surviving rights and obligations |
| Entire agreement | Supersedes prior understandings |
| Severability | Invalid provisions severed without voiding remainder, valid in Scots law |

### 10. Signature Block

Printed name, title (if entity), date, signature for each party. For Scottish companies: signatures under s.44 Companies Act 2006 (two directors or director + secretary) or s.78 Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995.

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **No statutory WFH for ICs** - copyright does not vest in the commissioning party automatically for independent contractors; a written assignment under CDPA s.90(3) is essential
- **Employee vs. IC** - for employees, copyright vests automatically in employer under CDPA s.11(2) if work is created in course of employment; but still document scope and confirm no pre-existing IP
- **Moral rights waiver** - must be in writing and signed by the creator (CDPA s.87); waivers cannot extend to future works not contemplated at the time of the waiver
- **UK employment status** - HMRC status tests (IR35 for intermediaries) can reclassify ICs as employees for tax purposes; the contractual label is not decisive
- **Scottish-specific** - Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995 governs formal validity; electronic signatures valid under s.6 of the Act
- **Cite statutes** - always reference CDPA ss.11, 90(3), and applicable moral rights sections explicitly

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Adapted for:** Scotland and UK law
**Changes made:**
- Replaced 17 U.S.C. §§ 101 and 201(b) ("work made for hire") with CDPA s.11 (ownership of copyright) and s.90(3) (assignment)
- Removed the nine statutory WFH categories (US-specific; no equivalent in UK)
- Fallback assignment promoted to primary mechanism, written assignment under CDPA s.90(3) is the operative transfer for ICs, Added UK moral rights framework (CDPA ss.77-89) replacing VARA reference
- "Attorneys' fees" → "legal costs on a solicitor-and-own-client basis"
- State IC rules → UK HMRC employment status / IR35 framework, Governing law: Hiring Party's state → Scots law (default for Scottish parties)
- Indemnification: Scots law requirement for express wording for own negligence, Signature: US forms → UK Companies Act 2006 s.44 / Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995
- Added VAT reference to compensation section
- "With full title guarantee" covenant (UK property law form)
- Future copyright assignment recognised under CDPA s.91

**Key Scottish/UK considerations:**
- No work-for-hire doctrine for independent contractors, written assignment is mandatory, Moral rights cannot be waived for future works not contemplated at time of waiver, Employment status is determined by HMRC tests, not contractual label, UK copyright is automatic (no registration requirement)
- Electronic signatures valid in Scotland under Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995 s.6
- Assignment of future copyright effective when work created (CDPA s.91)
