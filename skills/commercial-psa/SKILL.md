# Purchase and Sale Agreement, Commercial Heritable Property (Scotland)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. ## Output Structure

### 1. Header & Parties

- Title: "CONTRACT FOR SALE OF HERITABLE PROPERTY" or "DISPOSITION AGREEMENT"
- Effective date: date of conclusion of missives (Scots law) or specified effective date, Full party ID: legal name, company registration number, registered office address, Identify parent/guarantor relationships for SPEs or multi-tiered entities

### 2. Property Description & Price

**Property description:** postal address, title number(s), county, plot area/floor area, improvements summary, servitude rights, moveable property schedule (conveyed without warranty as to condition).

**Price structure:**

| Component | Details |
|---|---|
| Total price | Numerical + written form |
| Allocation | Land vs. buildings (for LBTT purposes) |
| Deposit | 5 to 10% typical; cleared funds to stakeholder (not agent) |
| Additional deposits | Amount, trigger, refundability |
| Cash at entry | Price minus deposits minus financing |
| Financing | Loan amount, type (commercial mortgage) |

All closing payments by CHAPS or bank transfer.

### 3. Title Review & Searches

**Title:** Seller delivers copies of title sheet and burdens section from Registers of Scotland. Buyer instructs solicitor to examine title. Buyer review period: 10 to 15 working days. Distinguish mandatory cure defects (standard securities, monetary charges) from voluntary (servitudes, real burdens). [SCOTS: Note] There is no "title commitment" or "title insurance" in Scotland, title is examined from the Register.

**Searches required:**
- Searches in the Register of Inhibitions, Council Tax certificate, Property Enquiry Certificate, Environmental search, Contaminated land search, Planning and building warrant search, Roads and sewers search
- [VERIFY] Additional searches specific to the local authority area

### 4. Due Diligence

**Period:** 30 days (single-tenant) to 60 to 90 days (complex/multi-tenant). Time is of the essence.

**Access:** 24 to 48 hours' notice, business hours. Coordinate tenant access through Seller/managing agent.

**Scope:** Physical (building systems, structure, utilities) | Environmental (Phase I per relevant standards; Phase II if RECs) | Financial, income properties (leases, rent rolls, operating statements, capital expenditure) | Regulatory (planning permissions, building warrants, certificates of lawfulness, fire safety).

**Buyer obligations:** Public liability insurance ≥ £5M; no interference with operations; repair damage; bear all costs.

**Seller cooperation:** Provide all property documents; may require confidentiality; may redact tenant confidential business info (not lease terms).

**Termination:** Buyer may terminate for any/no reason by 5:00 PM on last DD day. On termination: deposit refunded in 3 to 5 business days; Buyer delivers third-party reports (retains work product). If NOT terminated: deposit becomes non-refundable (except Seller default or express contingency failure).

### 5. Representations & Warranties

**Seller reps** (true as of effective date and entry): authority and capacity | title absolute owner (or recorded as such) | no pending/threatened litigation or compulsory purchase | environmental compliance (knowledge-qualified) | planning/building warrant compliance (knowledge-qualified) | leases: rent roll accurate, no defaults, deposits held per law | financial statements accurate | all contracts/options disclosed.

**Knowledge qualifier:** "To Seller's actual knowledge without independent investigation" for environmental and compliance reps.

**Buyer reps:** Authority/capacity | as-is acknowledgment based on own investigation (does NOT limit remedies for breach of express reps or fraud).

**Survival & caps:** 12 to 18 months | de minimis threshold £5,000 to £15,000 | aggregate cap 5 to 10% of price | fraud: unlimited, no caps.

### 6. Entry and Completion

**Entry date:** Date certain or formula (e.g., 30 days post-DD). Time is of the essence.

**Seller delivers:** Disposition (in registrable form) | Bill of sale (moveables, as-is) | LBTT certificate | certificate of title | assignation of leases (with Buyer assumption) | rent deposit deeds | assignation of contracts/warranties | keys/codes | updated certified rent roll | licences/permits/certificates of lawfulness | settlement statement.

**Buyer delivers:** Price (CHAPS, net of deposit ± prorations) | authority docs | assumption agreements | lender-required docs | LBTT return (completion agent).

### 7. Apportionments & Adjustments

Prorate as of 11:59 PM day before entry:

- **Council tax** - most recent bill; true-up within 30 days of actual bill
- **Rent** (income properties) - collected (not accrued); post-entry collections: current first, then arrears
- **Tenancy deposits** - transfer or credit; Buyer assumes return obligations
- **Service charge and insurance** - prepaid/accrued
- **Utilities** - final reading and transfer

Post-entry adjustment period: 90 to 120 days.
[SCOTS: Note] LBTT (Land and Buildings Transaction Tax) applies in Scotland, not UK SDLT. LBTT return must be filed with Revenue Scotland within 30 days of completion.

### 8. Default & Remedies

**Seller default, Buyer elects:** (a) Terminate: full deposit refund + reasonable OOP expenses (cap £15,000 to £30,000); OR (b) Specific implement (Scots law equivalent of specific performance; may record notice of litigiosity or register an inhibition).

**Buyer default, Seller's sole remedy:** Forfeit deposit as liquidate damages. Include enforceability language (reasonable pre-estimate, not a penalty). Stakeholder: notice, cure period, interpleader if disputed.

**General:** Written notice + 5 to 10 business day cure. No cure period for failure to complete, repudiation, or fraud. Judicial expenses (prevailing party fees) optional.

### 9. General Provisions

- **Notices:** Personal delivery, DX, email (with confirmation), recorded delivery. Deemed delivered: same day / next business day / 3 business days (recorded)
- **Assignation:** Affiliate assignation without consent if assignor remains liable; unaffiliated requires consent (not unreasonably withheld)
- **Governing law:** Scots law
- **Disputes:** Mandatory mediation first (30 to 60 days); exclusive jurisdiction in Scottish courts (Court of Session or Sheriff Court depending on value)
- **Jury waiver:** Not required, no civil jury trials in Scotland except limited Court of Session actions
- **Standard:** Entire agreement, written amendments only, severability, counterparts, e-signatures

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

The following adaptations apply when this skill is used for Scottish transactions:

**Fundamentally different system:** Scottish conveyancing is based on the Register of Scotland (Land Register), not title insurance or ALTA surveys. Key documents: Disposition (not deed), Standard Security (not mortgage/deed of trust), Missives (the contract formed by exchange of letters, though a single formal contract is also used for complex commercial).

**Deposit held in stakeholder** (not escrow agent). Scots law distinguishes "stakeholder" (cannot release without both parties' consent) from "agent for seller."

**Land registration:** Completed at Registers of Scotland. Registration creates the real right. Prior to registration, the Buyer has a personal right.

**Tax:** LBTT (devolved to Scotland) replaces SDLT. File with Revenue Scotland within 30 days. Non-resident surcharge applies to residential transactions.

**Searches:** Register of Inhibitions, Council Tax certificate, Property Enquiry Certificate, not ALTA/NSPS survey.

**Zoning:** Planning permission under Town and Country Planning (Scotland) Act 1997 (as amended) - not US zoning.

**Reps and warranties:** Scots law treats misrepresentation under common law delict. Contractual warranties are similar to US practice but with different limitation periods (prescription: 5 years for delict).

**Servitudes and real burdens:** Replace easements and covenants. Feudal abolition (Abolition of Feudal Tenure etc. (Scotland) Act 2000) means all land is held in allodial/ownership tenure.

**Title examination:** Solicitor reviews registered title; no title insurance market. Searches including Planning, Building Standards, Roads, and Sewers.

**Specific implement:** Scots law offers specific implement (equivalent to specific performance) available as of right for heritable property, subject to court discretion.
