# FINDINGS AND RISKS: Omer Elbushra v University of Glasgow et al.

## FINDINGS

### Finding 1: Victimisation (EqA 2010 s.27) — Primary Claim
| Field | Value |
|---|---|

## RISKS

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **R1: Causation challenge** to psychiatric injury claim — University may argue pre-existing vulnerabilities caused crisis, not 12 March meeting | **HIGH** | Obtain independent psychiatric report distinguishing between pre-existing condition and actionable harm; establish precise counterfactual |
| **R2: Qualified privilege** may protect Blatchford's defamatory statement (internal FtP investigation context) | **MEDIUM** | Frame as malice/abuse of process rather than standalone defamation; rely on s.27 EqA victimisation for damages |
| **R3: SRC is not a public body for PSED or HRA purposes** — may limit public law remedies | **LOW** | Lead EqA claims against SRC instead; contract and tort sufficient |
| **R4: University may argue FtP process was independent** despite Shepherd's involvement — "referring officer" not "panel member" | **MEDIUM** | Use Porter v Magill test; prove real possibility of bias through Mr. Matthew Walters' 29 May email (pre-determination) |
| **R5: Damages may be capped** in Sheriff Court (Ordinary Cause) or reduced if contributory negligence found | **MEDIUM** | Plead alternative remedy; track pre-litigation correspondence carefully |
| **R6: Chronology weakness** — mental health crisis (Mar 15-17) occurred before formal complaint (Apr 16), so University may argue FtP was not victimisation | **MEDIUM** | FtP referral was June 3 (after complaint); crisis does not break victimisation chain; s.27 applies to detriment after protected act |
| **R7: ARB rules** allow summary dismissal if claim appears weak at preliminary hearing | **LOW** | Claim is strongly pleaded; risk managed through detailed Initial Writ |
| **R8: Cost/expenses risk** if multiple defenders succeed in defending separate elements | **LOW-MEDIUM** | Issue joinder; lead systemic defence; cap open offer at early stage |

## GAPS

| Gap | Impact | Action Needed |
|---|---|---|
| **G1: No independent psychiatric report** — needed for both psychiatric injury and causation | Blocks highest damages head | Commission medico-legal expert report |

| **G3: No evidence yet on whether SRC constitution creates enforceable contract** | Affects breach of contract claim against SRC | Review SRC constitution document; seek via SAR if not held |
| **G4: Full FtP Panel written decision not yet in evidence** (only interim outcome) | Limits ability to challenge procedural flaws | Await or obtain final FtP Panel written reasons |

| **G6: No evidence of Walters' 29 May email** (key to proving pre-determination) | Weakest pre-determination proof | Request via SAR to University; seek any cc/drafts |
