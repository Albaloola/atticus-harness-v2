Drafts a request to a lender for the amount required to fully redeem a loan as of a specific date, for use in conveyancing closings (settlements), refinancing, and debt satisfaction in Scotland.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting, pause and request any missing items:

1. **Loan documents** - loan agreement, standard security, personal bond, promissory note, recent statements
2. **Loan identifiers** - account number, original principal, origination date, current lender/servicer
3. **Property address** - full address including unit, city, postcode (secured loans, subjects heritable)
4. **Borrower identity** - full legal name as on loan documents
5. **Requested redemption date** - exact date funds will be tendered
6. **Transaction context** - settlement date (date of entry), rate lock, sale date, or other deadline
7. **Authorization** - if not borrower: Power of Attorney (registered in Books of Council and Session if necessary), agency agreement, or letter of authority; identify role
8. **Prepayment provisions** - early repayment charges (ERC), redemption penalties, or specific repayment procedures

## Process

### 1. Document Review and Gap Analysis

Search matter files for loan agreements, standard securities, personal bonds, statements, and lender correspondence. Extract:

| Data Point | Source |
|---|---|
| Loan account number | Statement or loan agreement |
| Original principal and date | Personal bond or loan offer |
| Current approximate balance | Most recent statement |
| Lender/servicer name and address | Statement or correspondence |
| Early repayment charge (ERC) provisions | Loan agreement / mortgage conditions |
| Required redemption procedures | Loan agreement or lender website / UK Finance standards |
| Notice clause requirements | Loan agreement / standard security conditions |
| Title number (if registered) | Land Register of Scotland title sheet |

If critical fields are missing (account number, redemption date, property address, authorisation), list gaps and explain why each is needed before proceeding.

### 2. Assess Urgency

| Context | Timeframe | Sample Language |
|---|---|---|
| Scheduled settlement (date of entry) | 5-7 business days | "in connection with a scheduled settlement on [DATE]" |
| Refinancing with rate lock | Expedited | "rate lock expires on [DATE]; prompt response essential" |
| Sale transaction | Align with missives | "settlement is contingent upon timely receipt" |
| General redemption | Standard 7-10 days | Standard professional request |

### 3. Draft the Letter

**Format**: Standard business letter, sender info, date, lender/servicer with department, subject line.

**Subject line**: `Redemption Statement Request, Loan Account #[NUMBER]`

**Opening**: Establish identity, reference loan by date and account number, state purpose. If representative:

> I am writing as [solicitor for / conveyancing agent for / authorised representative of] [BORROWER NAME], who has authorised me to request and receive all redemption information regarding the above-referenced loan.

**Loan identification block**:

- Loan account number (as on statements)
- Original loan amount and origination date, Borrower's full legal name (as on documents)
- Property address (subjects heritable, for secured loans)
- Title number (if known)
- Approximate current balance (if known, with caveat)

**Redemption date and per diem**: State exact redemption date; request per day interest rate for date adjustments.

**Itemised breakdown request**:

- [ ] Principal balance as of redemption date
- [ ] Accrued interest through redemption date
- [ ] Early repayment charge (ERC) or redemption penalties
- [ ] Registration / discharge of standard security fees
- [ ] Administrative / processing fees
- [ ] Escrow / ring-fenced account balance (refund or application)
- [ ] Arrears or late payment charges
- [ ] Other charges for full redemption and discharge
- [ ] Validity period of the quoted amount
- [ ] CHAPS / bank transfer instructions

**Delivery**: Specify preferred method (email, DX, post, lender portal, fax) with full details.

**Response deadline**: 5-7 business days. Note:

> Under the Lending Code / UK Finance voluntary arrangements, lenders are requested to provide redemption statements within 5 business days. [VERIFY: lender-specific or contractual timeline]

[VERIFY: standard UK mortgage lender redemption statement timelines, typically 5 business days; no equivalent of RESPA in UK law]

**Closing**: Professional sign-off with full contact information and credentials (firm name, Law Society of Scotland registration number if applicable).

## Compliance Checks

- **Consumer Credit Act 1974** - early settlement rights under s. 94 to 95 for regulated credit agreements; right to rebate of unearned charges [VERIFY: applicability to specific loan type]
- **Council of Mortgage Lenders (CML) / UK Finance** - standardised redemption statement practice; most lenders issue within 5 business days
- **Scots conveyancing** - standard security discharge registered with Registers of Scotland (SASINE); discharge must be recorded to clear title
- **Authorisation** - confirm written authorisation attached or on file if not borrower; Scottish Power of Attorney rules (if applicable)
- **Early repayment charges** - flag provisions from loan documents; may be regulated under Consumer Credit Act 1974 [VERIFY: enforceability in specific context]
- **Deeds / title documents** - confirm lender holds title deeds and request their release upon full redemption

## Guidelines

- Verify all account numbers, dates, amounts, and addresses, errors cause delays, Do not estimate the redemption amount; request the lender's official calculation, Include enough detail that the lender can process without follow-up, Follow redemption procedures specified in the loan agreement exactly, Mark as draft requiring solicitor review before sending, Maintain client confidentiality (Law Society of Scotland Practice Rules)

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from a US framework. Key differences for Scottish practice:

- **No RESPA** - the US Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act has no UK equivalent. Redemption statement timelines are governed by UK Finance / CML voluntary standards, not statute.
- **Standard Security** replaces mortgage/deed of trust. Discharge is registered with Registers of Scotland.
- **Consumer Credit Act 1974** replaces TILA/Reg Z for regulated credit agreements, providing early settlement rights and rebate calculations.
- **Conveyancing practice** differs significantly: settlements are conducted through solicitors (not escrow/title companies); missives form the contract; date of entry is the settlement date.
- **£ not $** - all amounts in pounds sterling.
- **Solicitor/Conveyancer** replaces attorney/settlement agent.
- **Registers of Scotland** (SASINE) replaces county recorder/register of deeds.
- **CHAPS** replaces wire transfer as standard for funds transfer.
