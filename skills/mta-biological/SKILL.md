# Material Transfer Agreement, Biological Materials (Scotland/UK)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts MTAs governing transfer of biological materials between research institutions and/or commercial entities, balancing scientific collaboration with IP protection, biosafety compliance, and regulatory requirements under Scots/UK law. Reference the UK MTARA (Material Transfer Agreement for Research Activities) or MRC/MIC/CRUK model MTAs as baseline; deviate only where parties' needs require it.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

1. **Parties** - legal names, addresses, departments, signatories, institutional type (university/RI/foundation/corporate)
2. **Material** - scientific nomenclature, strain/cell line IDs (ECACC, ATCC, RRID), physical form, quantity, BSL classification, select agent status, GMO details
3. **Upstream restrictions** - prior MTAs or third-party IP encumbrances
4. **Research scope** - project description, grant number, funding source (UKRI/MRC/BBSRC/Wellcome/private), PI names
5. **Regulatory status** - export control classification (UK Dual-Use / military end-use), biosafety committee approvals, AWERB/ethical approvals if applicable
6. **Commercial intent** - whether recipient anticipates commercialisation of derivatives

## Quick Start

For a standard academic-to-academic transfer of non-select-agent, non-GMO materials under UKRI funding:

- Start from UK MTARA or MRC/MIC model MTA, Confirm funder IP terms (Research Councils UK Terms and Conditions)
- Use research-only permitted use with prohibition on commercial use and third-party transfer, Apply three-tier IP allocation (background → Provider retains; materials → Provider; new inventions → inventive party)
- Include 30-day publication review period (no veto right)
- Add standard AS-IS disclaimers in conspicuous format under Sale of Goods Act 1979 / Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977

## Drafting Workflow

### 1. Parties & Authority

- Full legal name, address, department, authorised signatory with binding authority, PI is agent of institution (not a party); institution bears compliance responsibility, Public institutions (universities): confirm statutory contracting authority under Scots public law, Conditions precedent: biological safety committee approval, dual-use review, conflict-of-interest review, International transfers: allocate responsibility for foreign government approvals; UK export licence responsibility

### 2. Material Description

Draft with scientific precision:

- Taxonomy (genus/species/strain), identifiers (ECACC, ATCC, RRID), physical form, storage conditions, Biosafety: Containment Level 1 through 4 (CL1 to CL4) classification and COSHH requirements, Select agents: confirm both parties' HSE registrations if applicable under UK Anti-Terrorism, Crime and Security Act 2001 / Health and Safety at Work Act 1974
- GMO status: nature of modifications, GMO (Contained Use) Regulations 2014 compliance, biological safety committee approvals, Encumbrances: upstream MTA restrictions, patents covering materials, Hazard disclosures: known risks, Safety Data Sheets (SDS), COSHH assessment references

### 3. Transfer Logistics

- Shipping: IATA-compliant packaging, UK Dangerous Goods Regulations (Carriage of Dangerous Goods and Use of Transportable Pressure Equipment Regulations 2009), temperature control (dry ice/LN2/refrigerated)
- Cost allocation: provider-paid, recipient-invoiced, or recipient-arranged, Timeline: conditions before shipment (signed MTA, permits); receipt inspection within [X] days, Export controls: UK Strategic Export Control Lists / Dual-Use Regulation; consider Export Control Joint Unit (ECJU) licence requirement, International: customs invoices (UK Trade Tariff), dangerous goods declarations, import permits, phytosanitary certificates (for certain biological materials)

### 4. Permitted Use & Restrictions

- Define permitted use by specific project/protocol (exhibit) or field of use, Prohibit without consent: commercial use, third-party transfer, human subjects research, germline modification, Derivatives ownership: unmodified progeny → Provider retains; modifications with inventive contribution → Recipient owns with Provider licence-back option, Third-party transfer of derivatives requires Provider consent with equivalent MTA restrictions

### 5. Confidentiality & Publication

- Scope: material properties, performance data, unpublished research, know-how, Standard exceptions: public knowledge, independent development, third-party receipt, compelled disclosure, Duration: [X] years post-termination (typically 3 to 5)
- Publication review: Provider gets 30 to 60 days to review; may request removal of confidential info; max 90-day patent-filing delay; Provider may NOT veto publication

### 6. Intellectual Property

Three-tier allocation:

| IP Category | Owner | Other Party |
|---|---|---|
| Background IP | Original owner | Licence only if expressly granted |
| Materials & unmodified progeny | Provider | Recipient use licence within permitted scope |
| New inventions | Inventive party (sole/joint) | Option to negotiate licence within [X] days |

- **UKRI funding** (Research Councils UK): institution retains title; UKRI gets non-exclusive licence for research purposes; reporting obligations per RCUK Terms and Conditions
- **Reach-through rights**: draft narrowly; UKRI policy does not mandate reach-through on publicly funded materials
- **Patents Act 1977** governs patentability of biological inventions; note nature-derived exclusions in s. 1(3) and Schedule A2

### 7. Warranties & Liability

- Limited warranties: right to transfer, materials match description, no known contamination, Disclaimers (CONSPICUOUS, ALL CAPS or bold, complying with Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977): AS-IS; no warranty of merchantable quality or fitness for particular purpose implied, Liability: direct damages only; exclude consequential/indirect; optional monetary cap, Carve-outs: gross negligence, wilful misconduct, confidentiality breach, IP infringement, Public institutions: confirm Crown immunity / public body liability constraints under Scots law, Note: UCTA 1977 restricts exclusion of liability for negligence and breach of statutory implied terms

### 8. Indemnification & Insurance

- Mutual indemnification: Recipient covers use/handling/disposal claims; Provider covers undisclosed defects and wilful misconduct, Procedure: prompt written notice → indemnitor assumes defence → no adverse settlement without consent, Insurance: public liability, professional indemnity, employer's liability (statutory requirement under UK law); Provider named as co-insured for high-risk materials

### 9. Term & Termination

- Term: fixed (project/grant duration) or indefinite with termination rights, Convenience: 30 to 90 days written notice, Cause: material breach + 30-day cure period, Auto-termination: insolvency/bankruptcy, loss of regulatory approvals, legal prohibition, Post-termination: return or destroy materials + written certificate; archival samples only for regulatory retention, Surviving: confidentiality, IP, indemnification, disclaimers, dispute resolution

### 10. Regulatory Compliance

Include affirmative obligations for applicable frameworks:

- **GMO (Contained Use) Regulations 2014** (SI 2014/2529) - biological safety committee review, HSE notification, containment levels for genetically modified organisms [VERIFY]
- **Human / animal tissue** - Human Tissue (Scotland) Act 2006 for Scotland (separate from HT Act 2004 for England/Wales); AWERB approval if animal-derived material
- **Select Agent / Pathogen Programme** - Anti-Terrorism, Crime and Security Act 2001; Schedule 5 materials require HSE notification [VERIFY]
- **Export controls** - UK Dual-Use Regulation SI 2008/3231 (as amended); Export Control Joint Unit (ECJU); military end-use controls [VERIFY]
- **Animal (Scientific Procedures) Act 1986** - if material derived from regulated procedures; Home Office Project Licence consideration
- **Human tissue** - Human Tissue (Scotland) Act 2006; IRAS / NHS Research Ethics Committee approval if human-derived; also UK GDPR for donor-identifiable material
- **Environmental protection** - Environmental Protection Act 1990; waste disposal, spill response, decontamination protocols
- **Sanctions** - representations that neither party is in a sanctioned country or on OFSI / UN sanctions lists
- **Dual-use research of concern (DURC)** - UK DURC policy; flag qualifying materials; institutional biosecurity review may be required

### 11. Governing Law & Disputes

Tiered resolution (recommended for institutional parties):

1. Senior official escalation (Research Office director / VP Research) - 30 days
2. Mediation - 60-day window, cost-sharing
3. Litigation (preferred for IP/interdict relief; specify Court of Session or Sheriff Court) OR arbitration (Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010 - faster for contract disputes; carve out interdict relief)

Specify that the unsuccessful party bears the successful party's judicial expenses (Scots costs rule).

### 12. Boilerplate & Execution

Amendments (written/signed), assignment (consent required; M&A exception), notices (addresses/methods/timing), severability, integration, waiver (written only), counterparts, signature blocks, exhibits (material description, research protocol, destruction certificate).

**Execution**: Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995 governs formal execution; electronic signatures per Electronic Communications Act 2000 [VERIFY: institutional acceptance of e-signatures].

## Pitfalls

1. **Select agents** - verify current HSE Schedule 5 list (Anti-Terrorism, Crime and Security Act 2001); non-compliance carries criminal penalties under UK law
2. **Export controls** - do not assume biological materials are exempt; UK Dual-Use Regulation controls many biological agents and toxins; ECJU licensing required
3. **UKRI funding** - always confirm funding source; RCUK Terms and Conditions must be incorporated; funder may have reach-through or exploitation provisions
4. **Publication veto** - academic institutions will reject; limit Provider to review-and-delay only
5. **Reach-through royalties** - controversial in academia; UKRI policy discourages broad reach-through on publicly funded materials
6. **Disclaimer format** - UCTA 1977 requires reasonableness test for exclusion clauses, not just conspicuousness; confirm enforceability under Scots law
7. **Scotland-specific regulation** - separate legislation applies to human tissue (Human Tissue (Scotland) Act 2006), GMOs, and animal welfare; verify Scottish vs. UK-wide applicability
8. **Dual-use research of concern (DURC)** - flag qualifying materials; UK DURC policy under ABS (Academics for Biosecurity Security) framework
9. **Statutory citations** - mark all SI/USC references with [VERIFY] for confirmation against current regulations
10. **Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977** - applies to all business-to-business MTA terms; excludes liability limitations that fail the reasonableness test

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from a US framework. Key differences for Scottish/UK biological MTAs:

- **No NIH UBMTA** - the UK uses the MTARA (Material Transfer Agreement for Research Activities) model or funder-specific models (MRC, BBSRC, CRUK, Wellcome). Use these as templates instead.
- **No Bayh-Dole Act** - UKRI funding terms and RCUK Conditions govern IP arising from publicly funded research instead.
- **HSE replaces CDC/USDA** - the Health and Safety Executive (HSE) regulates biological agents and GMO contained use in the UK.
- **Containment Level (CL1 to CL4)** replaces BSL-1 to BSL-4 classification.
- **UK Dual-Use Regulation (SI 2008/3231)** replaces ITAR/EAR, controls are similar but licensing is through the Export Control Joint Unit (ECJU), not DDTC/BIS.
- **OFSI (Office of Financial Sanctions Implementation)** replaces OFAC for sanctions screening.
- **ECACC (European Collection of Authenticated Cell Cultures)** and UK collections often replace ATCC.
- **Sale of Goods Act 1979 / Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977** replace UCC Article 2 for warranty disclaimers and limitation of liability.
- **Human Tissue (Scotland) Act 2006** - separate legislation from England's Human Tissue Act 2004; must use the Scottish Act for Scotland-situated material.
- **Patents Act 1977** governs patentability (not US patent law); note biotech patentability exclusions.
- **COSHH (Control of Substances Hazardous to Health) Regulations** apply to laboratory handling of biological materials.
- **£ not $** - all amounts in pounds sterling.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
