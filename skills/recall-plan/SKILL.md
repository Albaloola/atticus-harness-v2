# Recall Plan and Procedure

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts the company's official recall protocol under UK regulatory framework.

[SCOTS: Note] This skill has been adapted from a US FDA-focused template to the UK regulatory landscape. The general methodology is transferable, but specific regulatory requirements differ by sector and jurisdiction. Always verify current guidance with the relevant UK regulator. For Scotland-specific enforcement, local authority Trading Standards and Environmental Health Officers (EHOs) are the primary enforcers.

## Core Sections

### 1. Purpose and Scope

State commitment to consumer and patient safety, applicability to all regulated products, and governing authority (relevant UK legislation, see below).

**Sector-specific legislation:**
- **Medicines:** The Human Medicines Regulations 2012 (SI 2012/1916); MHRA guidance on defect reporting and recalls
- **Medical Devices:** UK Medical Devices Regulations 2002 (SI 2002/618, as amended); MHRA device safety guidance
- **Food:** Food Safety Act 1990; Food Standards Scotland (FSS) / Food Standards Agency (FSA) guidance on traceability, withdrawals and recalls
- **General Consumer Products:** The General Product Safety Regulations 2005 (SI 2005/1803) and successor UK GPSR; Office for Product Safety and Standards (OPSS) guidance
- **Workplace/Industrial Products:** Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974; HSE enforcement

### 2. Recall Committee

Define these roles with named personnel:

| Role | Typical Position | Key Duties |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| Chair | VP Quality (or equivalent) | Final recall decisions, regulator liaison oversight |
| Regulatory Lead | Head of Regulatory Affairs | Regulator notifications (MHRA/FSA/OPSS/HSE), classification coordination |
| Legal Counsel | GC / External Solicitor | Risk assessment (including苏格兰 delictual liability), notification review, litigation hold |
| Operations Lead | Head of Manufacturing/Supply Chain | Retrieval logistics, inventory quarantine |
| Communications Lead | Head of Corporate Comms | Press releases, social media, consumer contact |
| Quality Lead | QA Director | HHE, effectiveness checks, CAPA |

**Convening:** Within 4 hours (business) / 12 hours (after-hours). Maintain emergency contact tree with 2 backups per role and documented alternates.

**Decisions:** Consensus preferred; Chair has unilateral emergency authority. Document rationale, attendees, and dissenting views.

### 3. Health Hazard Evaluation (HHE)

Assess these factors in a formal report with supporting data:

- Nature of defect (contamination, mislabelling, malfunction)
- Adverse outcome likelihood (scientific/medical evidence)
- Vulnerable populations (paediatric, immunocompromised, elderly)
- Severity × probability of harm, Defect prevalence in distributed product

**UK recall classification:**

#### Medicines (MHRA classification):

| Class | Standard | Examples |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Class 1 | Potential risk of death or serious deterioration in health | Contamination, incorrect active ingredient, sterility failure (parenterals) |
| Class 2 | Temporarily/reversibly hazardous; remote risk of serious harm | Minor contamination, labelling errors, packaging defects |
| Class 3 | No significant hazard | Minor labelling violations, aesthetic defects |

#### Medical Devices (MHRA classification):
Follow the MHRA Device Safety Information (DSI) / Field Safety Corrective Action (FSCA) framework. FSCAs are categorised as:
- **FSCA requiring a DSI** - where there is a significant risk to health
- **FSCA not requiring a DSI** - low-risk corrective actions

#### Food (FSA/FSS classification):
Recalls are categorised as:
- **Class I** - Serious health risk requiring urgent recall
- **Class II** - Health risk not requiring urgent action
- **Product withdrawal** - Product removed from the supply chain before reaching consumers
- **Product recall** - Product removed after reaching consumers

#### General Products (OPSS classification):
Risk assessment under the UK General Product Safety Regulations using the OPSS risk assessment methodology (RAPEX-style risk levels: Serious, Medium, Low).

[SCOTS: Note] The company proposes the classification; the regulator (MHRA/FSA/FSS/OPSS/HSE) makes the final determination. Complete HHE proportionate to urgency.

### 4. Recall Strategy

**Depth of recall:**

| Level | Scope | When |
|-------|-------|------|
| Consumer/User | End users; public warnings | Serious hazard + product reached consumers |
| Retail/User | Retailers/dispensers only | Hazard manageable at retail; product distinguishable |
| Wholesale | Wholesale chain only | Product not yet at retail |

**Public warning required when:** Class 1 (medicines) / Class I (food) + product reached consumers, product not easily distinguished from safe product, or regulator requests it.

**Document:** Depth justification, public warning plan, timeline, regulator consultation record, adjustment provisions.

### 5. Notification Protocols

#### Regulator Notification

**Medicines (MHRA):** Report defect immediately upon discovery. Submit via MHRA Defect Report Form. For Class 1, telephone notification is expected within 24 hours; formal report within 2 working days.

```
- [ ] Product identity (marketing authorisation number, product name, batch/lot numbers)
- [ ] Reason for recall, specific defect
- [ ] HHE and proposed classification
- [ ] Quantity produced and time span
- [ ] Quantity in distribution
- [ ] Distribution pattern (UK nations, export)
- [ ] Proposed strategy (depth, warnings, effectiveness level)
```

**Medical Devices (MHRA):** Report via MHRA Adverse Incident Reporting system. For Field Safety Corrective Actions (FSCAs), submit proposed FSCA to MHRA for review before implementation.

**Food (FSA/FSS):** Notify local authority Environmental Health and FSA/FSS Food Incidents Branch. Complete Food Incident Report form.

**General Products (OPSS):** Notify OPSS via the Product Safety Database (PSD). Submit a Product Safety Report (PSR) / Recall notification form.

**HSE-regulated products:** Notify HSE as required under relevant regulations.

#### Trade Notifications

Legal + Regulatory must approve before distribution.

```
- [ ] Prominent "RECALL NOTICE" / "FIELD SAFETY NOTICE" header
- [ ] Product ID with lot numbers and visual aids
- [ ] Plain-language recall reason
- [ ] Instructions for identifying affected inventory
- [ ] Required actions (quarantine, return, destroy)
- [ ] Lot verification procedure
- [ ] Contact information
- [ ] Request for written confirmation
```

#### Public/Consumer Notifications

Coordinate press releases with the relevant regulator (MHRA/FSA/FSS/OPSS). Use social media for rapid dissemination. Contact consumers directly when registry data available.

**Record all notifications:** date/time, recipients, method, copies, responses.

### 6. Effectiveness Checks

| Level | Typical Class | Requirement |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| A | Class 1/Class I | Contact 100% of direct accounts |
| B | Class 2/Class II | Contact representative sample |
| C | Class 3/Class III | Confirm notification receipt |

**Collect per check:** notification receipt confirmation, product identified, quarantine confirmed, quantity reconciliation, sub-distribution info.

**Report to regulator:** At intervals agreed with the regulator (commonly 2-week, 4-week, and 8-week intervals, or as directed by MHRA/FSA/FSS/OPSS).

**Non-responsive escalation:** Repeated contact → recorded delivery with return receipt → personal visit → notify regulator.

### 7. Product Disposition

| Option | When | Requirements |
|--------|------|-------------|
| Destruction | Contamination, fundamental defects (default) | Witnessed, certificate (date/method/qty/lots), photo/video for high-value |
| Reconditioning | Correctable defect; regulator pre-approval | Detailed proposal, QC verification per unit, batch records |
| Relabelling | Labelling-only; regulator pre-approval | Proposal, per-unit verification, records |
| Return to manufacturer | Evaluation/rework | Full chain of custody |

**Storage:** Segregated, restricted access, conspicuously marked, full inventory controls.

**Accountability:** Periodic reconciliation of distributed vs. returned vs. disposed quantities. Investigate discrepancies. Prevent unauthorised re-entry into distribution.

### 8. Recall Termination

**The regulator decides termination, not the company.** Never unilaterally declare completion or make public statements suggesting recall has concluded without written regulator agreement.

```
- [ ] Final effectiveness check results (% accounted for)
- [ ] Quantity reconciliation with explanations for gaps
- [ ] Complete disposition records
- [ ] All recall notifications/communications
- [ ] Outcome summary with lessons learned
- [ ] Formal termination request to relevant regulator
```

### 9. Post-Recall Review

**Monitoring:** Handle recalled product surfacing after termination; notify regulator if significant quantities found.

**Retention:** Complete recall file minimum 2 years beyond termination (longer per litigation holds). Also retain as per UK regulatory record-keeping requirements.

**Review checklist:**

```
- [ ] Timeliness of initial HHE
- [ ] Adequacy of recall strategy and mid-course adjustments
- [ ] Effectiveness of notifications
- [ ] Efficiency of recovery and disposition
- [ ] Resource and personnel adequacy
- [ ] Plan gaps identified
- [ ] Root cause / systemic quality issues
```

Produce written report with CAPA recommendations tracked through QMS.

## Pitfalls

- [SCOTS: Note] **Never self-terminate:** Only the relevant UK regulator can authorise recall completion
- **Classification is the regulator's call:** Company proposes; regulator assigns final classification
- **Reconditioning/relabelling require pre-approval:** Do not proceed without regulator authorisation (MHRA/FSA/OPSS as applicable)
- **Guidance evolves:** Flag sections with `[VERIFY CURRENT GUIDANCE]` where UK regulatory guidance may have changed
- **Cross-reference requirements:** Medical devices, adverse incident reporting (MHRA Yellow Card scheme); Medicines, defect reporting (MHRA Defect Report); Food, FSA/FSS Food Incidents
- **Export considerations:** Products exported to EU/NI may need separate notification under EU GPSR / Northern Ireland Protocol arrangements
- **Four-nation consideration:** UK regulatory framework differs across England/Wales/Scotland/NI. Food recalls are handled by FSS in Scotland and FSA in England/Wales. Engage Scotland-specific legal counsel for Scottish delictual liability exposure
- **Dual audience:** Maintain legal precision for counsel/solicitors while keeping operational sections accessible to non-legal staff

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from a US FDA-focused recall plan template to the UK and Scottish regulatory environment.

### Key changes

- **Regulatory agencies:** FDA → MHRA (medicines/devices), FSA/FSS (food), OPSS (general products), HSE (workplace)
- **Legislation:** 21 CFR Part 7/FD&C Act → Human Medicines Regulations 2012, UK MDR 2002, Food Safety Act 1990, General Product Safety Regulations 2005 (and successors), Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974
- **Recall classification:** FDA Class I/II/III → MHRA Class 1/2/3 (medicines); FSCA framework (devices); FSA/FSS Class I/II (food); OPSS RAPEX-style (general products)
- **Notification forms:** FDA notification form → MHRA Defect Report Form / FSA Food Incident Report / OPSS Product Safety Report
- **GBP reference:** No USD amounts to convert
- **Terminology:** FDA-approved → regulator-authorised; litigation → delictual liability exposure; counsel → solicitor/advocate
- **Jurisdictional notes added:** Scotland-specific enforcement by local authority Trading Standards and Environmental Health Officers; Food Standards Scotland for food recalls; Scottish delict (Donoghue v Stevenson [1932] AC 562) governs product liability civil claims

| Regulatory Domain | Primary Regulator | Scotland-Specific |
|---|---|---|
| Medicines | MHRA (UK-wide) | N/A |
| Medical Devices | MHRA (UK-wide) | N/A |

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
