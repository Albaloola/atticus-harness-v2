Produce a property condition disclosure for Scottish residential sales or leases addressing known lead-based paint and lead-related hazards. [SCOTS: Note, no statutory lead-paint disclosure exists in UK law; this is a best-practice voluntary disclosure based on Home Report / common law obligations.]

## Prerequisites

1. Property address, legal description, and year built (pre-1965 for lead paint risk)
2. Transaction type (sale or lease) and execution timeline
3. Full legal names and contact info for all parties (seller/landlord, buyer/tenant)
4. All known lead-based paint or lead hazard details, locations, dates, remediation history
5. All available records/reports (inspection, risk assessment, abatement, clearance, environmental health correspondence)
6. Home Report documents (Single Survey, EPC, Property Questionnaire)
7. Solicitor/agent names and firm details (if any)

## Output Structure

### Sections

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| Header | Property address, legal description, transaction type, date, party names/addresses, agent details |
| Seller/Landlord Disclosure | One of: (a) known lead hazards with specifics, (b) no knowledge after reasonable inquiry, or (c) hazards remediated with documentation |
| Hazard Definition | Lead-based paint (pre-1965), lead pipework, lead-contaminated dust or soil |
| Records/Reports | List all available records; if none, state "No records available" |
| Home Report References | Relevant Single Survey findings, Property Questionnaire entries |
| Inspection Opportunity | Buyer encouraged to obtain independent survey; no statutory 10-day period (Scotland has no equivalent inspection right - [SCOTS: Note]) |
| Acknowledgments | Separate initials/signatures for each: disclosure received, records received, Home Report acknowledged (sale only) |
| Certifications & Signatures | Seller/landlord, buyer/tenant, and solicitors/agents, each with printed name, signature, date |

## Guidelines

- Do not omit the lead hazard definition.
- List every available record/report; if none exist, state so explicitly.
- Scotland: Home Report is mandatory for residential sales, refer to Property Questionnaire and Single Survey findings, For leases, note the landlord's Repairing Standard obligations (Housing (Scotland) Act 2006 s.12)
- Use plain English; state only what is actually known, no extra assurances, Check with buyer's solicitor whether additional enquiry is being made about lead paint/lead pipes

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Adapted for:** Scotland and UK law
**Changes made:**
- Replaced 42 U.S.C. § 4852d / 24 CFR Part 35 with Housing (Scotland) Act 2006, EPA 1990 Part IIA, CPUTR 2008, common law, Replaced EPA/HUD with SEPA / Local Authority Environmental Health, Replaced EPA pamphlet "Protect Your Family from Lead in Your Home" with Home Report / Single Survey references (no UK pamphlet equivalent)
- Replaced 10-day buyer inspection period (US federal law) - Scotland has no equivalent statutory right, Removed mandatory inspection waiver line (not applicable in Scotland)
- Replaced seller treble damages liability with delictual liability for negligent misrepresentation, Replaced $16,000 civil penalty with Repairing Standard enforcement (PRS: max £5,000 fine)
- Replaced records retention (3 years US federal) with 5-year recommendation (Scots prescriptive period for delict)
- Added Home Report / Single Survey / EPC references (Scotland-specific mandatory documents)
- Added caveat emptor notice (buyer beware, key Scottish property law principle)
- Added Repairing Standard obligations for private rented sector, Added lead pipes and lead-contaminated soil to hazard definition (broader than US lead-paint-only focus)
- Replaced Agent/Broker (US real estate) with Solicitor/Agent (Scottish conveyancing practice)
- Removed Lease disclosure renewal requirement, replaced with Repairing Standard ongoing duty

**Key Scottish/UK considerations:**
- No direct equivalent of US federal lead-paint disclosure law exists in UK/Scotland, Caveat emptor applies: seller is not required to volunteer latent defects (but must not actively conceal)
- Lead pipes are a Tolerable Standard issue (s.71 Housing (Scotland) Act 2006) - enforceable by local authority, PRS only: Repairing Standard (s.12) imposes absolute duty on landlords for minimum condition, Contaminated land (EPA 1990 Part IIA): lead in soil/dust can trigger SEPA/local authority remediation, Home Report (Scotland) is mandatory for residential sales, includes Single Survey (condition report)
- Property Questionnaire asks about alterations, damp, timber defects but NOT specifically lead paint, General practice: buyers rely on independent survey (Home Report Single Survey level 1-3) and solicitor's enquiries, For serious concern: commission separate lead sampling before purchase (cost typically £200-500 for basic test)
- Prescriptive period (limitation) for delict: 5 years from when damage discovered (or could reasonably have been discovered); 20 year long-stop
