Drafts a franchise area development agreement granting exclusive territorial rights for phased multi-unit development under UK law, balancing brand control with developer protections.

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

The US franchise model (FTC Franchise Rule, state registration laws) does not apply in the UK. Scotland and the rest of the UK have a different regulatory framework:

**Key differences:**
- **No FTC Franchise Rule**: The UK does not have a federal franchise-specific disclosure statute equivalent to the FTC's pre-sale disclosure requirements. There is no mandatory FDD or Item schedule system.
- **UK Franchise Industry**: Self-regulation through the British Franchise Association (BFA). Many ethical franchisors voluntarily comply with the BFA Code of Ethical Conduct (which requires pre-sale disclosure and compliance with the European Code of Ethics for Franchising).
- **Commercial Agents (Council Directive) Regulations 1993 (retained)**: These retained EU regulations apply to commercial agents. If the area developer acts as a commercial agent (negotiating sales on behalf of the franchisor), these may apply. However, a true area developer who buys franchises and operates them is typically not a commercial agent, they are a franchisee. The distinction matters for termination and compensation rights.
- **Scots contract law**: Scottish contract law differs from English contract law in several respects relevant to franchise agreements, particularly on:
  - **Consideration**: Scots law does not require consideration for a contract to be binding (a promise in writing is sufficient).
  - **Ius quaesitum tertio**: Third-party rights can be created under Scots law (Contract (Third Party Rights) (Scotland) Act 2017), which may affect enforcement of development schedules.
  - **Penalty clauses**: Scots and English law both apply the penalty clause doctrine (Cavendish Square Holding v Makdessi [2015]) - liquidated damages must be a genuine pre-estimate of loss.
  - **Specific implement**: Scots law permits specific implement (specific performance) more readily than English law, which may affect enforcement of development obligations.
- **Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977 (UCTA)**: UCTA applies across the UK and can render unreasonable exclusion or limitation clauses unenforceable in franchise agreements.
- **Consumer Rights Act 2015**: If the developer is a sole trader or partnership, some terms may attract consumer protections.
- **Competition Act 1998**: Vertical agreements (including franchise agreements) are subject to UK competition law. The Competition and Markets Authority (CMA) may investigate anti-competitive territorial restrictions that go beyond what is necessary.
- **Trade Marks Act 1994**: UK trade mark registration (UKIPO) protects the franchisor's marks. Community Trade Marks (EUTM) ceased to cover the UK post-Brexit, converted UK marks are required.
- **Data protection**: UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018 apply; an international data transfer mechanism may be needed if the franchisor is based outside the UK.
- **Currency**: All amounts in GBP (£).
- **Governing law**: The agreement should specify Scots law (if the territory is in Scotland or the parties have a Scottish connection) or English law (for cross-border UK arrangements).
- **Jurisdiction**: Scottish Courts (Sheriff Court or Court of Session) for Scottish governing law agreements.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party information** - legal names, entity types, registered addresses (Scotland / England), Companies House registration numbers, parent relationships
2. **Franchise disclosure** - BFA-compliant disclosure document (or equivalent pre-sale information, which may include a franchise agreement summary, financial information, territory analysis, and the franchisor's recent accounts). No statutory FDD.
3. **Territory definition** - county lines (Scottish local authority areas), postcode sectors, or defined boundaries; maps if available
4. **Development schedule** - number of units, phased opening deadlines, total term
5. **Fee structure** - development fee, per-unit franchise fee, royalties (management service fees), marketing fund contribution, credit/offset terms
6. **Unit franchise agreement form** - the FA each opened location will execute
7. **Term sheet or LOI** - pre-negotiated terms, special conditions, side letters

## Core Workflow

### 1. Definitions, Key Drafting Points

- **Territory** - use objective boundaries (postcode sectors, local authority areas, defined map); never vague ("Scottish central belt")
- **Development Schedule** - reference Exhibit A; define "opened" as trading after full system compliance, not soft opening
- **Gross Revenues** - all revenue streams; exclude only VAT collected and remitted; specify delivery charges, catering, gift cards
- **Confidential Information** - operations manual, methods, formulas, customer data, supplier terms, financials, and must comply with UK GDPR data processing provisions

### 2. Territorial Grant

Grant exclusive right to develop [X] units within Territory per Development Schedule.

**Reserved rights (negotiate):**
- Franchisor company-owned units, Alternative distribution channels (e-commerce, wholesale, delivery platforms)
- National/institutional accounts serviced in Territory, Franchisor acquisition of competing systems with existing Territory locations

**Exclusivity conditions:**
- Tied to Development Schedule compliance, Loss of exclusivity for undeveloped sub-areas on milestone default, ROFR for adjacent territories (if negotiated)

Development rights ≠ operating rights, each unit requires separate FA execution.

### 3. Development Schedule (Exhibit A)

| Phase | Cumulative Units | Deadline | Milestone Details |
|-------|-----------------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | [X] | [Date] | Site approved + lease by [Date−6mo]; fit-out by [Date−3mo] |
| 2 | [X] | [Date] | Same staggered milestones |

### 4. Default & Remedy Escalation

| Trigger | Remedy | Cure Period |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| Single milestone missed | Written notice + cure | 60 to 90 days |
| Repeated milestone failures | Loss of exclusivity for undeveloped sub-territory | 30 days notice |
| Persistent non-compliance | Territory reduction | 30 days |
| Material/incurable default | Termination | Immediate |

Force majeure extensions require prompt written notice + documentation; cover acts of God, pandemic, permitting delays, war/terrorism.

### 5. Financial Terms

| Fee Type | Structure | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Development fee | Lump sum or milestone instalments | Non-refundable; specify VAT treatment |
| Per-unit franchise fee | Standard fee minus multi-unit discount | Payable at each FA execution; VAT applicable |
| Management service fee (royalties) | [X]% of Gross Revenues | Weekly/monthly; standard UK VAT rate applicable |
| Marketing fund | [X]% of Gross Revenues | National + local; BFA best practice: ring-fenced and audited |
| Late payment charges | [X]% + interest at 8% over Bank of England base rate (Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998) | Statutory interest applies |
| Audit rights | Franchisor audit on [X] days notice | Developer pays if underreporting >2% |

### 6. Termination

**Non-curable (immediate):** bankruptcy/insolvency, criminal conviction of principals, abandonment, material misrepresentation, repeated defaults after prior cure. In Scotland, note sequestration (personal insolvency) and winding up (company insolvency) under the Bankruptcy (Scotland) Act 2016 and Insolvency Act 1986.

**Curable (30 to 60 day cure):** single missed deadline, operational standards breach, insurance lapse, reporting/payment delinquency.

**Post-termination:** loss of development rights; existing unit FAs continue on own terms; de-identification of partial sites; return confidential materials; non-compete and confidentiality survive.

**Termination and Commercial Agents Regulations:** If the developer could be characterised as a commercial agent, termination may trigger compensation or indemnity under the Commercial Agents Regulations, expressly disclaim agency if the developer is a franchisee only. [SCOTS: Fully performing franchisees are typically not commercial agents, but define the relationship clearly to avoid recharacterisation.]

### 7. Dispute Resolution

1. **Mediation** - good faith, costs shared equally, 30 to 60 day window. CEDR or Scottish mediation body.
2. **Litigation or Arbitration**: For Scots law-governed agreements, the Court of Session or Sheriff Court. Consider arbitration (Scottish Arbitration Centre) as an alternative.
3. **Injunctive carve-outs** - confidentiality breach, non-compete violation, trade mark infringement, IP misappropriation.

## Compliance Checks

- **FTC Franchise Rule**: NOT applicable in the UK. Do not reference.
- **BFA Membership**: If the franchisor is BFA-affiliated, the agreement must comply with the BFA Code of Ethical Conduct (including pre-sale disclosure obligations, cooling-off period of 14 days, and dispute resolution).
- **Disclosure**: UK franchise law does not mandate a specific format, but BFA standards require a disclosure document covering: the franchisor's history, financial information, key terms, territory details, and a list of existing franchisees.
- **Commercial Agents Regulations**: If the developer is a commercial agent (e.g., they introduce franchisees for commission rather than developing their own units), the agreement must comply with the 1993 Regulations on termination notice, compensation/indemnity, and commission periods [VERIFY: agency vs franchisee distinction].
- **Competition Act 1998**: Vertical agreements are generally exempt under the Competition Act 1998 (Vertical Agreements Block Exemption) Order 2022, but hardcore restrictions (resale price maintenance, absolute territorial protection in some cases) are prohibited [VERIFY].
- **UK GDPR**: The agreement must include data processing provisions compliant with UK GDPR Art. 28 (data processor clauses) if the developer processes personal data (customer data, employee data).
- **Consumer Rights Act 2015**: If the developer is a consumer (individual not acting in the course of business), certain terms may be challenged as unfair. Most franchisees are businesses, but sole traders may be borderline.
- **UCTA 1977**: Exclusion clauses (e.g., limitation of liability) must satisfy the reasonableness test under UCTA.
- **Non-compete enforceability**: UK common law on restraint of trade applies (Scots or English). Post-term non-competes must protect a legitimate business interest and be no wider than reasonably necessary per *Tillman v Egan* [2019] UKSC 57 (applicable to both Scots and English law on the issue).
- **VAT**: All fees are subject to UK VAT at the applicable rate. The agreement must specify whether fees are exclusive or inclusive of VAT.
- **UKIPO trade marks**: Confirm UK trade marks are registered for the relevant classes. Post-Brexit, EUTM registrations no longer cover the UK, converted UK marks should be in place.
- **Forum/arbitration limits**: UCTA and the Consumer Rights Act 2015 may limit the enforceability of exclusive jurisdiction clauses against certain parties. The Scottish Arbitration Centre provides Scottish-seated arbitration.
- **Independent contractor**: Reinforce throughout; avoid operational control language creating employment or partnership risk under UK/Scots law.
- Do **not** guarantee developer profitability or include earnings projections without appropriate disclaimers.
- Do **not** draft provisions purporting to exclude statutory rights where prohibited (e.g., Consumer Rights Act 2015).

## Pitfalls and Checks

- **VAT treatment**: All fees are subject to UK VAT. Confirm whether the development fee and management service fees are standard-rated, zero-rated, or exempt.
- **Territorial restrictions under Competition Act**: Hardcore restrictions (resale price maintenance, absolute territorial bans on passive sales) are prohibited even in vertical agreements. Ensure the developer can respond to unsolicited orders from outside their territory.
- **Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998**: Statutory interest at 8% over Bank of England base rate applies to commercial debts, may override contractual late payment provisions.
- **TUPE**: If the developer hires staff from a previous operator, TUPE 2006 may apply.
- **Construction (Design and Management) Regulations 2015**: If the developer leads fit-out works, CDM 2015 duties apply.
- **Planning permission**: Scottish planning law (Town and Country Planning (Scotland) Act 1997) is devolved, different from England.
- **Building (Scotland) Regulations 2004**: Scottish building standards are devolved and differ from English building regulations.
- **Non-compete**: UK courts apply the *Tillman v Egan* severance principle, draft conservatively; the court will not rewrite an overly broad covenant.
- **Data protection**: Ensure a separate data processing agreement or data-sharing schedule if the developer processes personal data on the franchisor's behalf.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
