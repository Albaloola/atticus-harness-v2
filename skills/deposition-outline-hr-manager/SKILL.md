# Deposition Outline, HR Manager (Scotland/UK)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Generates a structured examination/precognition outline for examining the respondent employer's HR manager in contested employment matters before the Employment Tribunal (Scotland) or Sheriff Court.

## ⚠️ Important: Depositions Are Not Standard in Scotland

There is **no deposition** procedure in Scotland comparable to US practice. Use this outline as a framework for:

1. **Precognition** - informal interview with a witness to obtain their account (the Scottish equivalent of a deposition). Precognitions are not sworn evidence and are used for case preparation, not cross-examination at hearing.
2. **Evidence on Commission** - sworn examination before a commissioner (rare in employment cases).
3. **Examination at hearing** - direct or cross-examination during the employment tribunal hearing itself.
4. **Written witness statement** - the primary method of adducing evidence in UK employment tribunals.

Adapt the techniques accordingly. The questioning structure translates well to cross-examination at hearing.

## Quick Start

Gather before drafting:

- **Operative pleadings** - ET1 (claim) and ET3 (response); claims and defences in Sheriff Court
- **Personnel file** - applications, evaluations, disciplinary records, termination docs
- **HR policies** - handbook, Equality Act 2010 policies, anti-harassment policy, disciplinary and grievance procedures (ACAS Code)
- **Communications** - emails, grievance investigation notes involving the HR manager
- **Prior statements** - responses to schedule of loss, ET3, grievance outcome letters
- **Comparator data** - similarly situated employees, treatment, outcomes
- **Investigation files** - grievance reports, investigation summaries

## Outline Sections

### I. Background and Foundation

- Name, title, reporting structure, tenure, HR qualifications, CIPD membership/accreditation, Role in hiring, discipline, termination decisions
- **Lock in**: "You were responsible for [company]'s HR policies at the material time?"

### II. Policies and Procedures

- Authenticate staff handbook version in effect, Equality/diversity, anti-harassment, disciplinary, grievance, and capability policies, Termination approval process
- **Pin down deviations**: "Was this policy followed in the claimant's case?"

### III. Claimant's Employment History

- Hiring decision (who, why, qualifications)
- Duties, performance expectations, appraisals, trajectory, Commendations, pay rises, promotions, prior disciplinary action
- **Establish competence baseline** before adverse events

### IV. Triggering Events

- When and how HR became involved, Protected activity timeline (grievance, whistleblowing complaint, flexible working request, maternity/paternity)
- HR's response and communications with decision-makers
- **Temporal proximity**: timeline between protected activity and unfavourable treatment

### V. Investigation

- Investigator, methodology, timeline, witnesses, documents reviewed, Findings, conclusions, credibility assessments
- **Probe thoroughness**: compare investigation of claimant vs. others

### VI. Termination / Dismissal Decision

- Decider identity, HR as decider or advisor?
- Stated reasons, lock in precisely, Supporting documents; decision date vs. communication date, Alternatives considered (PIP, written warning, redeployment)?
- **Shifting explanations**: compare to ET3 response, grievance decision, prior correspondence

### VII. Comparative Treatment

- Similarly situated employees outside the protected characteristic, Same supervisor, position, time period; comparable conduct, Different outcomes, document systematically:

| Employee | Protected Characteristic | Conduct | Outcome | Decision-Maker |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Claimant | [characteristic] | [conduct] | Dismissed | [name] |
| Comparator A | [characteristic] | [comparable] | [outcome] | [name] |

### VIII. Pattern and Practice

- Prior discrimination/harassment/whistleblowing complaints in the department, HR's handling of prior complaints, Employment Tribunal claims involving same decision-makers, Equality and diversity training records (when, content, attendance)

### IX. Document Authentication

Mark and authenticate: personnel file, grievance investigation files, emails, policy documents, dismissal correspondence.

### X. Impeachment Preparation

- Compare testimony to: ET3 response, schedule of loss response, grievance outcome letters, claimant's version, Note inconsistencies for cross-examination at hearing

## Examination Techniques

- **Open-ended first**: "Tell me everything you know about…" - lock in the narrative
- **Then narrow**: specific questions with document references
- **Commit-credit-confront**: get commitment, show favourable document, confront with inconsistency
- **Exhaust "I don't recall"**: use specific documents to refresh recollection

## Pitfalls and Checks

- Lock in stated dismissal reason early, before showing contradictory evidence, Track which documents were in the file at dismissal vs. added later, Note documents missing from file that should be there (e.g., grievance notes under ACAS Code)
- Time allocation: ~60% dismissal decision and comparators, ~20% policies/investigation, ~20% background/authentication
- **No depositions**: precognitions are not sworn, cannot be used for direct cross, use written witness statements instead
- **Employment Tribunal rules**: ET rule 31/33 (disclosure), rule 23/26 (witness orders in Scotland), rule 52 (depositions unavailable in Scotland ET)
- **ACAS Code relevance**: Code of Practice on Disciplinary and Grievance Procedures, compliance is considered by tribunals; unreasonable failure may increase award by up to 25%
- **CIPD**: professional HR body in UK, analogous to SHRM but with different qualification recognition

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

### Key Differences from US Deposition Practice

| US Concept | Scottish/UK Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Deposition (pre-trial oral examination) | Precognition (informal, not sworn) / Evidence on Commission (rare in employment) |
| FRCP 30 (deposition rules) | No equivalent, ET rules 31-33 (disclosure in Scotland); CPR Part 31 (court of session/Sheriff Court) |
| Court reporter and transcript | No transcript, precognition notes are solicitor's own record |
| Discovery of documents (broad) | Standard disclosure (ET) / Specification of documents (civil courts) - narrower scope |
| FRE (Federal Rules of Evidence) | Scottish common law of evidence / Civil Evidence (Scotland) Act 1988 |
| Plaintiff/Claimant | Claimant (ET) / Pursuer (civil) |
| Defendant | Respondent (ET) / Defender (civil) |
| EEOC Charge | Early conciliation (ACAS) / ET1 claim form |
| Wrongful termination (tort/contract) | Unfair dismissal (Employment Rights Act 1996) / Wrongful dismissal (breach of contract) |
| Jury trial (employment) | No jury in Employment Tribunal; panel of judge + 2 lay members |
| Punitive damages | Not available; compensatory award only + basic award for unfair dismissal |
| ACAS | Advisory, Conciliation and Arbitration Service, GB-wide |
| EEOC (enforcement) | Employment Tribunal Scotland / Equality and Human Rights Commission |
| Discovery depositions of witnesses | Written witness statements exchanged before hearing; no depositions |

### Applicable Law (Scotland/UK Employment)

- **Employment Rights Act 1996** - unfair dismissal, written particulars
- **Equality Act 2010** - discrimination, harassment, victimisation
- **Employment Tribunals Act 1996** - tribunal jurisdiction and powers
- **Employment Tribunal Procedure Rules** - SI 2024/1155 (rule 30/33: disclosure, Scotland)
- **Employment Tribunals (Constitution and Rules of Procedure) (Scotland) Regulations 2024**
- **ACAS Code of Practice** - disciplinary and grievance procedures
- **Human Rights Act 1998** - convention rights in tribunals
- **Trade Union and Labour Relations (Consolidation) Act 1992**

### Practitioner Notes

1. There is no right to take a pre-trial deposition in Scotland's Employment Tribunals.
2. The best substitute is a **precognition** (informal interview) - your notes are not admissible as evidence.
3. Key evidence-in-chief is presented via **written witness statements** exchanged before the hearing.
4. Cross-examination happens at the hearing itself, not at a deposition.
5. Disclosure in ET Scotland is governed by rules 30-33 of the 2024 Procedure Rules, narrower than US discovery.
6. The ACAS Early Conciliation process must be completed before an ET1 can be lodged (mandatory).
7. Compensatory awards are capped (currently the lower of £105,707 or 52 weeks' gross pay for unfair dismissal, no cap for discrimination).
8. No punitive or exemplary damages, compensatory principle only.
9. In Sheriff Court contractual claims (wrongful dismissal), use Ordinary Cause Rules for specification of documents and commission procedures.
10. CIPD qualifications (Assoc CIPD / Chartered MCIPD) are the UK HR professional accreditation, not PHR/SPHR.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
