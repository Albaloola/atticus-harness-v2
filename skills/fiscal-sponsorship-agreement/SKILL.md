3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

Drafts a grant agreement between a registered charity (CIO, SCIO, or charitable company) and a project group, preserving the charity's charitable status while enabling restricted-purpose project funding.

## Important Structural Note

There is **no direct UK equivalent** of US fiscal sponsorship. This skill adapts the closest available structure: a **restricted grant agreement** between a registered charity and a project group. Key differences are noted throughout.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

1. **Charity details** - legal name, charity number (OSCR or Charity Commission), registered address, charitable objects
2. **Charity structure** - CIO, SCIO, charitable company, or unincorporated association (affects liability and execution)
3. **Project details** - name, mission, planned charitable activities, authorised representative(s)
4. **Jurisdiction** - Scotland (OSCR) or England & Wales (Charity Commission). Scotland has separate legislation: Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005
5. **Financial terms** - grant amount, any admin fee (must be reasonable and justifiable), disbursement approval thresholds
6. **Term** - fixed or at-will; notice period (30 to 90 days)
7. **IP allocation** - charity-owned, project-retained, or shared with licence-back
8. **Fund disposition on termination** - must be applied to charitable purposes consistent with charity's objects

## Quick Start

Draft in formal contract prose, numbered for cross-reference. Core sections:

1. Header & parties (names, charity number, effective date)
2. Recitals (charitable objects, project mission nexus, charitable purpose restriction)
3. Charity responsibilities (grant administration, oversight, Gift Aid, reporting)
4. Project responsibilities
5. Financial terms & fund management
6. Term, renewal & termination
7. Legal relationship & liability
8. Intellectual property
9. Compliance & records (OSCR/Charity Commission, HMRC)
10. Miscellaneous (governing law, Scots law for Scottish charities, dispute resolution, severability, notices)
11. Signature block

## Core Provisions

### Charity Responsibilities

- Receive and hold grant funds in a named restricted fund with separate accounts prepared per the Charities SORP (Statement of Recommended Practice)
- Issue Gift Aid declarations and receipts in accordance with HMRC rules, Review and approve all project grant expenditure; retain ultimate discretion to ensure funds are applied only for charitable purposes, File required annual returns with OSCR (Scotland) or Charity Commission (E&W) covering project activity, Carry public liability and trustee indemnity insurance with specified minimums

**Non-waivable retained authority:**
- Final approval over all budgets and expenditure for charitable purpose compliance, Right to suspend/terminate activities jeopardising charitable status
- **Charitable purpose restriction:** Charity must apply funds only to its charitable objects. Cannot exercise variance power for non-charitable purposes (critical difference from US model)

### Project Responsibilities

- Conduct activities consistent with the charity's charitable objects, Submit expense requests with invoices and charitable-purpose explanation, Obtain written approval before contracts, hiring, or commitments above £[threshold]
- Submit quarterly financial reports and annual narrative report, Notify charity immediately of any matter threatening charitable status

**Prohibited activities:**
- Political campaign intervention (absolute prohibition)
- Lobbying beyond ancillary charitable advocacy, Private benefit or inurement (trustees, project personnel)
- Fundraising without charity's prior written approval, Any activity that would jeopardise the charity's charitable status

### Financial Terms

| Item | Provision |
|---|---|
| Grant amount | £[X] restricted to defined charitable purpose |
| Admin fee | [X]% (must be reasonable and justifiable to OSCR/Charity Commission) |
| Fund ownership | Grant funds are the charity's property, held in a restricted fund |
| Disbursement | Written request → charity review → payment within [X] business days |
| Charitable purpose restriction | Funds must always be applied to charitable objects; clawback if misapplied |
| Earned income | Consider whether a separate trading subsidiary is needed for non-primary-purpose trading |

### Termination & Fund Disposition

- Voluntary: [30/90]-day written notice by either party, For cause: immediate on material breach or charitable status threat; [X]-day cure for curable breaches
- **Funds on termination:** retained by charity and applied to a charitable purpose as near as possible to the original project purpose (cannot be applied to non-charitable purposes)
- Project returns charity-owned assets and IP within [X] days

### Legal Relationship & Liability

- Project personnel are not employees or agents of the charity unless separately engaged, Charity not liable for project's contracts, delicts, or obligations unless expressly approved, Mutual indemnification: project indemnifies charity for project activities; charity indemnifies for own negligence/wilful misconduct, Project carries insurance naming charity as additional insured
- **SCIO note:** SCIOs have separate legal personality; trustees have limited liability

### Intellectual Property

- Specify ownership of project-created IP (charity or project)
- Define post-termination licence-back terms if project-retained, Require prior written approval for use of charity's name, logo, or charitable status, Publications must acknowledge the charity's grant in charity-approved form

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **No variance power** - UK charities cannot redirect funds to non-charitable purposes. Include a charitable purpose restriction clause with clawback.
- **Charitable objects alignment** - confirm every project activity falls within the charity's registered objects. If not, the charity acts ultra vires.
- **OSCR vs Charity Commission** - identify the correct regulator; Scotland has separate legislation and SORP requirements.
- **Gift Aid** - only if donations are from individuals who pay UK tax. Gift Aid cannot apply to purchases or membership benefits.
- **Trading** - if the project generates significant earned income, a separate trading subsidiary may be required to avoid UBI-type issues (charities can trade within primary purpose only).
- **SCIO** - Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisation; trustees have limited liability; simpler reporting than charitable company. Preferred structure for Scottish charities.
- **Trustee duties** - charity trustees owe fiduciary duties under the Charities Act 2011 (E&W) or Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005. Failure to oversee grant may breach trustee duties.
- **Insurance** - ensure public liability and trustee indemnity insurance covers project activities.

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been fundamentally restructured from a US Fiscal Sponsorship Agreement to a UK Charitable Grant Agreement. The US fiscal sponsorship model (501(c)(3) sponsor receiving tax-deductible contributions on behalf of a non-exempt project) does not exist in UK charity law.

### Structural Differences

| US Concept | UK/Scotland Equivalent |
|---|---|
| 501(c)(3) tax-exempt organisation | Registered charity (Charities Act 2011 / Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005) |
| IRS | HMRC (tax) + Charity Commission (E&W regulatory) / OSCR (Scottish regulatory) |
| IRC § 170 (donor deductibility) | Gift Aid (HMRC) - charity reclaims basic rate tax on donations; donors get relief only if they pay sufficient UK tax |
| EIN | Charity number (from OSCR or Charity Commission) |
| Variance power | **Not available** - UK charities must apply funds only to their registered charitable objects |
| Donor acknowledgment letter | Gift Aid declaration |
| IRS 990 | Annual return to OSCR / Charity Commission (lighter requirements) |
| State law (CA, NY) | Devolved differences: Scotland (OSCR) vs England & Wales (Charity Commission) |

### Scotland-Specific

- **OSCR** - Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator; separate register, separate legislation
- **SCIO** - Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisation; most common Scottish charity structure
- **Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005** - primary Scottish charity legislation
- **Charities SORP** - Scottish charities must follow SORP (with Scottish variations from E&W)
- **Public benefit test** - Scottish charities must demonstrate public benefit to a community, not a restricted class
- **Scotland-only charitable purposes** - includes: advancement of citizenship/community development (broader than E&W)

### Recommended Governing Law

- **Scots law** for charities registered with OSCR
- **English law** for charities registered with Charity Commission (E&W)
- Disputes: Sheriff Court (Scotland) / High Court (E&W) depending on claim value

### Key Agency Mappings

- **SEC** → **FCA** (not typically relevant for charities)
- **IRS** → **HMRC** / **OSCR** / **Charity Commission**
- **EPA** → **SEPA** (if environmental charitable activities)
- **FTC** → **CMA** / **Fundraising Regulator**

### Currency

- All amounts in **GBP (£)**
- No USD references

### Costs

- Scotland: loser pays (judicial expenses follow success)
- Legal aid available for qualifying charity disputes (limited)

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
