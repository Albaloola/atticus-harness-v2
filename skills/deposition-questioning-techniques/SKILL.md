Reference and question-sequence generator for six core examination techniques, adapted for Scottish legal practice where depositions do not exist. These techniques apply to precognition (private preparation of witness evidence) and examination in chief / cross-examination at trial.

## Important Scottish Context

**Scotland does not have a deposition system.** The closest equivalents are:

1. **Precognition** - Private interview conducted by the solicitor (or by a precognition officer for the Crown) to prepare a witness's evidence. Not under oath. Not recorded verbatim, a precognition note summarises the expected evidence. The witness may later adopt the precognition as their evidence in chief.
2. **Examination on Commission** - Court-appointed commissioner takes sworn evidence from a witness unable to attend trial (e.g. due to illness or being out of jurisdiction). Evidence is transcribed. Governed by Scottish civil procedure rules (OCR Rule 34, for example).
3. **Commission and Diligence** (civil) - Court orders for recovery of documents or hospital records. Not a witness examination mechanism.

These questioning techniques remain fully applicable in precognition (private preparation) and at trial (examination in chief / cross-examination), but the legal framework is different from US depositions. **Do not use the term "deposition" in Scottish legal documents.**

## Technique Selection

| Goal | Technique | Question Type |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Learn new information (precognition) | Funnel Method | Open → Targeted → Closed |
| Lock in / exhaust testimony (precognition or trial) | FWD Close | Closed, exhaustive |
| Maintain control (cross-examination) | Looping | Closed, using witness's words |
| Impeach prior statement (cross-examination) | Three C's | Commit → Credit → Confront |
| Build inescapable admission (cross-examination) | Admission Ladder | Short leading, one fact each |
| Handle evasive witness (cross-examination) | Redirect + Looping | Yes/No redirect, repeat |

## Technique 1: Funnel Method

**Open → Targeted (6Ws+H) → Closed lock-in**

1. **Open** - "Tell me about… / Describe… / Walk me through…" then stop. Use silence. Do not interrupt.
2. **Targeted** - Apply Who / What / When / Where / Why / Which / How to each thread from the narrative.
3. **FWD Close** - Before leaving any topic:
   - **F**: "Any **fact** about [topic] you haven't told me?"
   - **W**: "Anyone with **knowledge** you haven't mentioned?"
   - **D**: "Any **document** you haven't identified?"
   - "Is that everything? Are you sure?"

FWD close prevents witnesses from surfacing new information later and supports fabrication arguments for late-disclosed evidence.

## Technique 2: Boxing-In

Eliminate alternative explanations *before* confronting with damaging evidence.

**Sequence: Commit → Close escapes → Confront**

Example:
- Commit: "The decision was based solely on qualifications, correct?" / "Not on age or any protected characteristic?"
- Close escapes: "That was your understanding at the time?" / "Nobody told you something different?" / "You communicated it was qualifications-based?"
- Confront: "Showing you Exhibit 5, your email: 'We need to bring in some younger energy.' Did I read that correctly?"

## Technique 3: Looping

Embed a word or phrase from the witness's prior answer into the next question.

Example: A: "Various performance concerns." → Q: "These 'performance concerns' - who raised them first?" → A: "Janet." → Q: "When Janet raised these 'performance concerns,' what specifically did she say?"

Uses witness's own words (harder to dispute), forces precision, prevents evasion. With documents, quote the document language and loop it forward.

## Technique 4: Three C's Impeachment

### Commit
Lock witness into current testimony: "You stated X, correct?" / "You're certain?" / "Clear memory?"

### Credit
Build reliability of the prior statement:
- **Prior precognition statement**: prepared for court, near event date, memory fresher, prepared with solicitor
- **Witness-authorised document**: witness provided the information, near event date, intended accuracy

### Confront
**You read the prior statement aloud. Do not let the witness read it.**

"Let me read from the precognition note dated [date], page 2, paragraph 2: 'I saw the defender at the scene at 8pm.' Did I read that correctly?"

**After confrontation**: do not ask witness to explain the inconsistency, ask which version is true, or invite rehabilitation. Move on. Let the inconsistency stand for argument.

Note: In Scottish practice, precognition notes are privileged and not generally used for cross-examination unless the witness has adopted them as evidence. For cross-examination using prior inconsistent statements, use signed witness statements (if available) or prior comments recorded in other documents (emails, letters, police statements).

## Technique 5: Evasive Witnesses

| Problem | Response |
|---------|----------|
| Non-responsive | "My question was [X]. Can you answer that specific question?" |
| Narrative instead of answer | "Let me ask a yes-or-no question: [rephrase]." |
| "I don't recall" | "Do you *deny* that occurred?" → "So it may have happened, you just don't remember?" |
| Won't answer without documents | Establish no independent recollection, then show document to refresh |
| Argumentative / speeches | "Are you finished? Let me ask my question again: [repeat]." |

Do not argue, show frustration, or repeat identical questions. Use the record: "The transcript will reflect your answer."

## Technique 6: Admission Ladder

Stack undeniable facts until the conclusion is inescapable.

**Rules:**
- One fact per question, never compound, Keep questions short, Anchor to documents ("According to this email, [fact], correct?") or prior testimony, Do NOT ask "why" - opens explanation; save for argument

Example (establishing pre-termination knowledge of complaint): "Ms Smith worked in your department?" → "You were her supervisor?" → "She raised a grievance in January 2024?" → "HR notified you?" → "You read that email dated 15 January?" → "Ms Smith was dismissed 1 February?" → "So when you made that decision, you knew about her grievance?"

## Checks

- In Scottish civil procedure, precognition notes are generally privileged (you cannot be compelled to disclose them)
- Examination on commission is governed by the court's discretion and specific procedural rules (e.g. OCR Rule 34 for Sheriff Court ordinary cause)
- Cross-examination is limited to matters arising from evidence in chief and credit, no Scottish equivalent of FRE 607/611/613
- "Asked and answered" is not a formal objection in Scottish courts, but the court may disallow repetition, Preserve the record: identify productions by number, read quoted language into the transcript, Avoid using cross-examination techniques in precognition (which is a cooperative fact-finding interview)
- Related Scottish skills: `precognition-preparation`, `courtroom-examination`, `examination-on-commission`, `cross-examination-scotland`
- **Criminal precognition** for the Crown has additional caution: precognition notes are not disclosed to the defence in Scottish criminal procedure

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill is adapted from US deposition practice to Scottish legal procedure:

| US Concept | Scottish Equivalent |
|------------|-------------------|
| Deposition (oral examination under oath before trial) | No equivalent; **precognition** (private, not under oath, not verbatim) or **examination on commission** (court-appointed) |
| Deposition transcript (verbatim, certified) | Precognition note (summarised, not verbatim) or Commissioner's Report (transcribed) |
| Impeachment using deposition testimony | Use prior inconsistent statements from witness statements, emails, letters, or adopted precognition notes |
| FRE 607, 611, 613 (witness impeachment, form of examination) | No direct equivalent; Scottish evidence rules governed by common law and Criminal Justice (Scotland) Act 2016 / Civil Evidence (Scotland) Act 1988 |
| Deposition objections (form objections) | Limited, examination on commission may have objections recorded for the court |
| Discovery (pre-trial document exchange) | Commission and diligence / specification of documents (different mechanism) |
| 30(b)(6) corporate representative deposition | No equivalent; nominate a witness for precognition or examination on commission |
| Preparing for deposition | Preparing for precognition or for trial |
| USD / dollar references | GBP (£) |

## Concepts with No Direct Scottish Equivalent [FLAGGED]

- **Depositions generally** - Scotland does not have a deposition system. Do not use the term "deposition" in Scottish legal documents.
- **30(b)(6) corporate representative** - no direct equivalent
- **FRE 607, 611, 613** - no equivalent in Scottish evidence law
- **Deposition objections (preservation of objections)** - no equivalent; Scottish courts handle objections at trial
