# UK Guidance: Breach of Purchase Agreement Actions in Scotland

## Key Differences from US Practice

### Scottish Civil Procedure

#### Court Options

| Court | Jurisdiction | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Sheriff Court (Ordinary Cause)** | Claims up to £200,000 (non-PI) | Most property disputes |
| **Court of Session (Outer House)** | High-value / complex property cases | Rare for standard purchase agreement disputes |
| **All-Scotland Sheriff Court** | Personal injury only | Not for property |

#### Initiating Document

- **Initial Writ** - used in Sheriff Court Ordinary Cause (not called a "complaint")
- **Summons** - used in Court of Session (not called a "complaint")
- **No "complaint"** in Scottish civil procedure (complaint is a criminal document in Scotland)

#### Key Terminology

| US Term | Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Complaint | Initial Writ (Sheriff Court) / Summons (Court of Session) |
| Plaintiff | Pursuer |
| Defendant | Defender |
| Answer / Response | Defences / Notice of Intention to Defend |
| Motion | Motion (same word, different procedure) |
| Discovery | Commission and Diligence / Specification of Documents |
| Summary Judgment | Summary Decree |
| Injunction | Interdict |
| Complaint caption | Instance (parties) and Condescendence (facts) |
| Prayer for relief | Crave (what the pursuer asks the court to do) |
| Lis pendens | Notices of Pending Action / Inhibition |
| Jury trial | Rare in civil; only in Court of Session for limited actions |
| Specific performance | Specific implement (Scottish term) |

### Structure of an Ordinary Cause Initial Writ

The Scottish Initial Writ in Ordinary Cause has three parts:

1. **Instance** - court name, parties, their representatives
2. **Condescendence** - numbered paragraphs setting out the facts (equivalent to "factual allegations" in US)
3. **Plea-in-Law** - legal basis for the action
4. **Crave** - what the pursuer asks the court to do (equivalent to "prayer for relief")

### Specific Implement (Specific Performance)

In Scotland, **specific implement** is the primary remedy for breach of a heritable property purchase agreement, real property is considered unique, so specific implement is generally available as of right. This is more generous than US law (where specific performance is discretionary).

### Missives

Scottish property transactions are conducted through **missives** - an exchange of formal letters that forms a binding contract. The missives constitute the contract, not a single purchase agreement document. The "concluded missives" date is the contract date.

### Key Scottish Legislation

- **Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995** - formal validity of contracts for heritable property
- **Title Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003** - burdens, servitudes, and real conditions
- **Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973** - 5-year prescriptive period for breach of contract
- **Contract (Scotland) Act 1997** - contract law
- **Courts Reform (Scotland) Act 2014** - court jurisdiction and procedure

### Interest and Expenses

- **Judicial interest**: usually at the judicial rate set by the Court of Session (currently 8% from citation)
- **Expenses**: "loser pays" - court may award expenses to the successful party
- **No punitive damages** in contract claims in Scots law

### Heritable Property Specifics

| US Concept | Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Purchase agreement | Missives (exchange of letters forming contract) |
| Title | Land Register / Sasines Register |
| APN/Parcel Number | Title Number (Land Register) |
| Escrow | Settlement / Completion (usually through solicitor) |
| Closing date | Date of entry / settlement date |
| Deed of trust | Standard security (mortgage) |
| Title insurance | Searches (property, personal, planning) |
| Lis pendens | Notice of Pending Action (registered against title) |
| HUD-1 statement | Completion statement / LBTT return |

### Practical Note

The US complaint structure (caption, jurisdiction, parties, factual allegations, causes of action, prayer) maps reasonably well to the Scottish Initial Writ structure (instance, condescendence, pleas-in-law, crave), but the pleading rules differ significantly, Scottish pleadings are more concise, structured as numbered articles of condescendence, with separate pleas-in-law stating the legal basis.

Sources: Courts Reform (Scotland) Act 2014; Contract (Scotland) Act 1997; Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995; Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973; Act of Sederunt (Sheriff Court Ordinary Cause Rules).
