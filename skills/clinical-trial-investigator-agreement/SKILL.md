# Clinical Trial Investigator Agreement (UK/Scotland Adaptation)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a sponsor to PI/site agreement that operationalises MHRA, REC, and GCP obligations for a specific clinical trial conducted in the UK (including Scotland).

## Prerequisites

1. Protocol number, title, version date, phase, endpoints, enrolment target.
2. Investigator's Brochure or device manual; investigational medicinal product (IMP) handling requirements.
3. Sponsor identity, CRO involvement, contracting structure (institution, PI, or both).
4. Site legal name, governing law (England & Wales / Scotland / Northern Ireland), institutional policies, and any required template language (HRA mCTA).
5. PI credentials, sub-investigators, study team roles.
6. Budget and payment model (per subject, per visit, milestones); invoicing rules.
7. REC (Research Ethics Committee) details, consent/assent forms, recruitment materials, vulnerable population plan.
8. Data ownership, publication expectations, IP posture, sample handling.
9. Insurance positions and indemnity constraints (NHS indemnity, public entity limits, clinical trial insurance).

## Output Structure / Process

1. **Intake** - targeted questions; confirm medicinal product (SI 2004/1031) vs device (UK MDR 2002) track.
2. **Draft** - follow the section order below, incorporating site-specific policies.
3. **Exhibits** - attach and incorporate by reference.
4. **Verify** - run compliance checks using the tables and checklist below.

### Regulatory Baseline

| Topic | Authority |
|---|---|
| Informed consent | SI 2004/1031 Part 4; Mental Capacity Act 2005 (E&W) / Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000 |
| Ethics review | UK REC (HRA / NHS Scotland NRS) |
| Medicinal Products | SI 2004/1031 (The Medicines for Human Use (Clinical Trials) Regulations 2004 as amended); SI 2025/538 (2025 amendments) |
| Medical Devices | UK Medical Devices Regulations 2002 (SI 2002/618) |
| GCP | ICH GCP E6(R2) / E6(R3) |
| Financial disclosure | MHRA transparency requirements; UK policy on payments to investigators |
| Privacy | UK GDPR / Data Protection Act 2018; common law duty of confidentiality |
| Vulnerable populations | HRA guidance; Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000 for Scottish sites |
| Anti-bribery | Bribery Act 2010 |
| Website | HRA model Clinical Trial Agreement (mCTA) template; MRC/DH framework |

### Agreement Sections

| Section | Must Include |
|---|---|
| Parties and Definitions | Sponsor, institution/Trust, CI (Chief Investigator) / PI; define Study, Protocol, IMP, SAE, Confidential Information |
| Study Description | Protocol incorporated by reference; scope; single vs multi-centre |
| Investigator Responsibilities | Conduct per protocol; no deviations except to eliminate immediate hazard; amendment approval workflow |
| REC and Approvals | No study activities before written REC and HRA approval (England) / NRS permission (Scotland); continuing review; submissions shared with sponsor |
| Informed Consent | Current REC-approved forms; qualified designee (GCP-trained); no coercion; consultee/representative for adults lacking capacity |
| Study Personnel | Delegation of responsibility log; training documentation; PI retains ultimate responsibility |
| Investigational Medicinal Product | Receipt only from sponsor/manufacturer; storage conditions per IB; accountability logs; no off-protocol use; return/destroy per sponsor; Qualified Person (QP) release |
| Safety Reporting | AE/SAE definitions per SI 2004/1031; sponsor reporting to MHRA/REC timelines; SUSAR reporting |
| Data Integrity | ALCOA+; source data verification; correction rules; EDC audit trail; UK GDPR data processing schedule |
| Records and Retention | Essential documents per ICH GCP § 8; retention per SI 2004/1031 Sch. 8, or longer per law/policy; archiving in NHS-approved facility |
| Monitoring and Access | Reasonable advance notice; direct source access; HRA standards; corrective actions |
| Regulatory Inspections | Notify sponsor within 24 hours; provide MHRA inspection correspondence within 5 business days |
| Confidentiality | Scope; permitted disclosures to REC/regulators/GPs; term and return/destruction |
| Data and IP Ownership | Sponsor owns study data and results; invention assignment with university carve-outs; sample ownership with NHS/institutional policies |
| Publications | HRA publication policy; sponsor review window; patent delay; multi-centre coordination; ICMJE authorship standards |
| Compensation and Budget | FMV compliance per Bribery Act 2010 due diligence; invoicing; screen fail and early withdrawal rules; startup/closeout costs |
| Financial Disclosure/COI | Institutional and HRA disclosure requirements; update obligations; conflict management |
| Indemnification | Sponsor clinical trial insurance indemnity; carve-outs for PI/site negligence or noncompliance; NHS standard indemnity for NHS sites |
| Insurance | Sponsor clinical trial liability (minimum £5m per claim typical); site professional liability; certificate requirements |
| Term and Termination | For cause and convenience; subject safety wind-down; payment of earned fees |
| Governing Law/Disputes | Law of England & Wales, Scotland, or Northern Ireland per site; negotiation/mediation steps |
| Compliance | Bribery Act 2010; UK GDPR; Export control; Transparency reporting (ABPI Disclosure UK) |

Adjust to protocol/REC policy.

| Event | Default |
|---|---|
| Sponsor notification of SAE | Within 24 hours of awareness |
| REC submission of urgent safety measures | Within 3 days |
| Publication review by sponsor | 45 to 60 days before submission |
| Patent delay after review | Up to 60 to 90 days |

- [ ] Governing law matches site (Scotland: Scots law; England: English law)
- [ ] All regulatory citations align with medicinal product vs device track
- [ ] Consent and privacy requirements align with UK GDPR
- [ ] Payment terms are Bribery Act compliant

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Resolution |
|---|---|
| Unclear whether CTIMP or device track | Confirm MHRA classification; CTIMPs follow SI 2004/1031; devices follow UK MDR 2002 |
| NHS Trust requires its own template | Merge sponsor terms into HRA mCTA template; flag conflicts for negotiation |
| NHS site cannot indemnify | NHS standard indemnity applies; sponsor clinical trial insurance covers remainder |
| PI is also sponsor-investigator | Apply sponsor obligations from SI 2004/1031 in addition to investigator duties |
| Scottish site with Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act | Apply consultee provisions per Act; separate from English MCA 2005 framework |
| Budget triggers Bribery Act concerns | Document FMV analysis methodology; transparent recording of investigator payments per ABPI Code |

## Guidelines

- Apply the most stringent requirement among UK legislation, EU-derived retained law, and institutional policy.
- No study activity before REC favourable opinion + HRA Approval / NRS permission if required.
- Keep confidentiality and publication clauses consistent with multi-centre coordination and HRA principles.
- Preserve a clear audit trail for data corrections and monitoring access.
- Flag any unclear regulatory citation with `[VERIFY]`.

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill is adapted for UK/Scottish clinical trials from US practice. Key differences:

| US Concept | UK/Scotland Equivalent |
|---|---|
| FDA (21 CFR Parts 50, 56, 312/812) | MHRA (SI 2004/1031; UK MDR 2002) |
| IRB (Institutional Review Board) | REC (Research Ethics Committee) under HRA / NHS Scotland |
| HIPAA | UK GDPR / Data Protection Act 2018 / common law confidentiality |
| False Claims Act / AKS | Bribery Act 2010 / ABPI Code of Practice |
| FCPA | Bribery Act 2010 (broader scope) |
| 21 CFR Part 54 (financial disclosure) | MHRA transparency / ABPI Disclosure UK |
| State law | Law of England & Wales, Scotland, or Northern Ireland |
| OMB Uniform Guidance (public entities) | NHS Standard Contract / HRA mCTA |
| AAA / JAMS arbitration | Negotiation → mediation → litigation (Chancellor's Court / Court of Session) |
| SoC / Schedule of Events per HRA | HRA mCTA / Statement of Activities |
| Sunshine Act (Open Payments) | ABPI Disclosure UK |
| OFAC export controls | UK export control regulations (Export Control Order 2008) |

**For Scottish sites specifically**: Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000 governs consent for adults lacking capacity (not Mental Capacity Act 2005). NHS Research Scotland (NRS) coordinates permissions.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
