# UK Guidance: Franchise Disclosure in the UK

## Key Difference: No FTC Franchise Rule Equivalent

**Unlike the US, the UK has no statutory franchise disclosure or registration regime.**

There is:
- **No legal requirement** for a franchisor to provide a Franchise Disclosure Document (FDD)
- **No registration** requirements with any UK regulator
- **No mandatory waiting period** before signing or paying
- **No federal agency** equivalent to the FTC for franchise regulation

## The British Franchise Association (BFA) Code of Ethics

The BFA (bfa.org.uk) is the self-regulatory body for franchising in the UK. Its Code of Ethics requires BFA-member franchisors to provide "full and accurate" pre-contract disclosure, but this is voluntary and membership-based.

Non-member franchisors have no such obligation under the Code.

## Governing Law

Franchise agreements in Scotland are governed by:
- **General contract law** (Contract (Scotland) Act 1997; common law)
- **Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977** - restricts unreasonable exclusion clauses
- **Consumer Rights Act 2015** - applies where the franchisee is a consumer (rare, usually business-to-business)
- **Competition Act 1998** - resale price maintenance, vertical restraints
- **Business Protection from Misleading Marketing Regulations 2008** - regulates advertising
- **Agency regulations** if the franchise relationship has agency characteristics

## What This Means for the Skill

The core drafting ability (producing an acknowledgment of receipt) has some transferable value, a UK franchisor may want to document delivery of pre-contract information as evidence of good practice. However:

- **No FDD form exists** in UK practice
- **No FTC-mandated content** to acknowledge
- **No 14-day waiting period** is legally required (though BFA Code recommends 14 days for members)
- **No registration states** equivalent

## Alternative UK Documents

- **Franchise Agreement** - the main trading document (not an FDD)
- **Pre-contract disclosure letter** - voluntary best-practice document
- **BFA Compliance Document** - for BFA member franchisors

## Practical Recommendation

For a UK/Scotland adaptation, this skill should either:
1. Be re-cast as a "Pre-Contract Disclosure Acknowledgment" for BFA-compliant franchisors (voluntary best practice), or
2. Flag that the US FTC framework has no UK equivalent and the skill is retained only for its drafting methodology and form-structuring approach

Sources: BFA Code of Ethics; Pinsent Masons, Operating a Franchise in the UK (2025); ICLG Franchise Laws and Regulations, England & Wales (2026); Practical Law UK.
