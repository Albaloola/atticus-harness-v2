# Pension Sharing Order Drafting (Scotland) [SCOTS]

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Produces a court-ready Pension Sharing Order dividing pension rights between transferor and transferee pursuant to a decree of divorce or dissolution of civil partnership, compliant with the Welfare Reform and Pensions Act 1999 ("WRPA 1999") Part IV, the Family Law (Scotland) Act 1985 ("FLSA 1985"), and the Pensions on Divorce etc. (Pension Sharing) (Scotland) Regulations 2000.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

- **Decree of divorce / dissolution** - case number, date of decree, financial provision terms
- **Pension scheme documents** - exact scheme name, scheme number, type (DB/DC/Public Sector/SIPPP), administrator contact details
- **Party information** - full legal names, dates of birth, National Insurance numbers, current addresses (both parties)
- **Key dates** - date of marriage/civil partnership, date of separation (if agreed), agreed valuation date
- **Valuation** - Cash Equivalent Transfer Value (CETV) or scheme-specific valuation; dependant on type of pension arrangement
- **Division terms** - percentage of pension rights (pension sharing) or specified amount (earmarking/attachment), treatment of post-valuation investment growth/losses

## Output Structure

### 1. Court Caption & Recitals

- Match caption to local court rules of the divorce jurisdiction (Sheriff Court or Court of Session)
- Reference the decree of divorce or dissolution of civil partnership by exact date and case number, State that the order is made under s. 8(1)(baa) of the FLSA 1985 and Part IV of the WRPA 1999
- State that the purpose is to divide pension rights under the specified pension arrangement, Include the three mandatory findings under Sch. 1 para. 6 WRPA 1999 - the order complies with the scheme and UK legislation

### 2. Definitions

Define at minimum: Transferor, Transferee, Pension Arrangement (full legal name + scheme number), Scheme Administrator (entity + address), Valuation Date, CETV or Account Balance (per scheme type), Relevant Decree, Pension Credit / Pension Debit.

For DB schemes, also define: Pension Credit Benefit, Benefit Commencement Date, [SCOTS: No US-style marital coverture fraction, Scottish family law uses s. 10(6) FLSA 1985 "fair sharing of matrimonial property" principles including pension rights accrued during the marriage].

### 3. Division Methodology

**Defined Contribution (Money Purchase):**
- Exact percentage of the transferor's rights as at the valuation date, Treatment of investment growth/losses between valuation and implementation, Whether the transferee can transfer the pension credit to another arrangement

**Defined Benefit (Final Salary / Career Average):**
- Percentage of the transferor's pension rights, cannot be specified as a fixed monetary amount, Method: Pension Sharing (percentage of cash equivalent value)
- The pension credit is calculated and implemented by the scheme according to WRPA 1999 provisions, Disposition of early retirement rights, pension increases (including statutory revaluation), dependants' benefits, Form of benefit, must comply with scheme rules; the order cannot require benefit forms the scheme does not provide

**Public Sector Schemes (NHS Scotland / SPPA / Teachers / LGPS):**
- Follow scheme-specific rules; Scottish public sector schemes have their own pension sharing templates, Check the SPPA guidance on pension sharing orders

### 4. Transferee Protections

Include provisions for:
- Right to transfer pension credit to another registered pension scheme, Right to receive scheme information from the administrator, Protection from any post-order diminution of the pension credit by subsequent scheme amendment, The pension credit vests immediately on implementation, separate from the transferor's benefit schedule

### 5. Scheme Administrator Directives [SCOTS: No equivalent to US plan administrator directives, Scottish pension sharing orders are implemented by the scheme actuary]

1. Calculate the pension credit and pension debit in accordance with WRPA 1999 Part IV
2. Adjust records to reflect the pension debit against the transferor's rights
3. Create a pension credit for the transferee within 4 months of receipt of the order (or longer if the scheme rules provide)
4. Provide written notice to both parties of implementation, including the pension credit amount and options available to the transferee
5. [SCOTS: The order must be presented to the scheme administrator within 4 months from the date of decree, the Implementation Period under WRPA 1999]

### 6. Compliance & Effective Date

- Certify compliance with the WRPA 1999 Part IV, the FLSA 1985, and the Pensions on Divorce etc. (Pension Sharing) (Scotland) Regulations 2000
- State that the order is intended to qualify as a pension sharing order under UK legislation, Effective date: the date the order is made by the court (or such later date as specified)
- The order ceases to have effect if not implemented within the Implementation Period

### 7. Signature Block

- Sheriff / Lord Ordinary signature line (printed name, title, date)
- Court seal
- [SCOTS: No equivalent to US "Court certification of ERISA compliance" - Scottish pension sharing orders are certified by the court at the time of the decree]

## Pitfalls

- **Use scheme-specific language** - Public sector schemes (NHS, Teachers, LGPS, Civil Service, Armed Forces) have specific requirements. Scottish public sector schemes may differ from UK-wide schemes.
- **Never require unavailable benefits** - e.g., lump sum from annuity-only DB scheme.
- **UK public sector schemes are not ERISA-governed** - they are governed by the relevant public service pension scheme regulations. Scottish schemes are under the SPPA.
- **National Insurance number handling** - include on a confidential schedule if required by court rules; do not include in the main body of the order if local practice otherwise.
- **Tax treatment** - The transferee's pension credit is treated as the transferee's own pension rights for tax purposes. No immediate tax charge arises on the pension sharing transaction itself.
- **Annual Allowance / Lifetime Allowance** - Implications for both parties. Flag potential tax charges to client. [SCOTS: UK tax rules apply, no Scotland-specific variation.]
- **Flag gaps explicitly** - never assume or omit missing information; incomplete or incorrect orders are routinely rejected.
- **Implementation Period** - The order must be served on the scheme administrator within 4 months of the date of decree. Failure invalidates the order.
