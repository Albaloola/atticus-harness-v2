# Director and Officer Questionnaire

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts structured D&O questionnaires for UK corporate governance compliance, annual report disclosures, conflict assessments, and transaction due diligence.

## Quick Start

Gather before drafting:

1. **Company profile** - public/private, exchange listing (Main Market, AIM, AQSE), regulated industry status (FCA, PRA, SRA)
2. **Prior questionnaires** - existing D&O questionnaires, annual report director biographies, Companies House filings
3. **Governance docs** - articles of association, board charter, code of ethics, related party transaction policy, insider dealing policy
4. **Disclosure baseline** - latest annual report (strategic report / directors' report), director roster, committee appointments
5. **Purpose** - annual AGM, IPO prep, M&A due diligence, or governance record

## Questionnaire Sections

### Cover Page

- Company name, questionnaire purpose, submission deadline, designated contact (Company Secretary / General Counsel)
- Confidentiality notice: information may appear in Companies House filings, annual report disclosures, be shared with auditors/counsel/counterparties, Continuing obligation: report material changes within **5 business days**

### Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Immediate family | Spouse or civil partner, children, stepchildren, parents, household members (excl. tenants/employees) |
| Interest in shares | Direct + indirect holdings per CA 2006 s.793; DTR 5 (voting rights notification); includes holdings via trusts, family, controlled entities |
| Related party transaction | Transaction exceeding threshold where related person has direct/indirect material interest (IAS 24; UK Listing Rules Chapter 11; AIM Rule 13) |
| Material | Information a reasonable shareholder would consider important; err toward disclosure |
| PSC (Person with Significant Control) | Individual holding >25% shares or voting rights, or exercising significant influence (CA 2006 Part 21A; PSC Register) |

### I, Personal and Identifying Information

- Full legal name; former names/aliases (past 10 years)
- Service address (public Companies House record); residential address (confidential, protected from public register)
- Contact info; DOB (month/year on public register; full DOB shared only with specified bodies)
- National Insurance number (limited use, store under data security controls)
- Family relationships with other directors, connected persons (CA 2006 s.252)
- Country of residence; nationality

### II, Professional Background

- Current role: title (executive/non-executive), appointment date, committee memberships, Employment history - **10 years** (recommended for due diligence; CA 2006 requires name and service address for register)
- Education, professional qualifications, professional body memberships (include disciplinary history)
- Qualifications narrative for annual report biographical disclosure per UK Corporate Governance Code Provisions 19 to 27

### III, Board Service and External Positions

All directorships, trusteeships, advisory roles - **past 10 years**:

- Public companies: ticker, exchange, principal business (FCA Main Market / AIM / AQSE)
- Audit committee financial expertise (UK Code Provision 24 - at least one member with recent/relevant financial experience)
- Senior Independent Director, non-executive chair roles, Attendance records; industry association service

**Adverse events during tenure (CA 2006 s.161A, disqualification grounds; Companies Act disqualified directors register):**

- Bankruptcy/receivership/administration/liquidation at organisations where respondent served, FCA, PRA, HMRC, CMA, SFO regulatory enforcement actions, censures, financial penalties, Financial statement restatements, material litigation, regulatory sanctions, Director disqualification orders/undertakings (Company Directors Disqualification Act 1986)
- Decisions not to stand for re-election or pre-term resignations

### IV, Conflicts and Related Party Transactions

*(UK Listing Rules Chapter 11 - threshold rules and FCA notification for related party transactions; IAS 24 disclosures; AIM Rule 13 for AIM companies; CA 2006 s.177 and s.182 require director declaration of interest)*

Disclose for respondent, immediate family, or connected persons (CA 2006 s.252):

- **Business relationships**: goods/services, consulting, leases, loans, charitable contributions, with the company or any subsidiary
- **Competing interests**: roles at competitors, financial interest in customers/suppliers/lenders, corporate opportunities taken without board authorisation (CA 2006 s.175 - duty to avoid conflicts of interest)
- **Indebtedness**: company loans to director/connected persons (CA 2006 s.197 to 214 regulates loans/quasi-loans/credit to directors, substantial property transactions also require member approval); company guarantees
- **Independence-impairing relationships**: auditor connections (past 3 years), cross-directorships, significant personal relationships affecting perceived independence (UK Corporate Governance Code Provision 10 - independence criteria)

### V, Legal Proceedings and Regulatory Matters

*(CA 2006 s.161A / s.1186 - disqualification; Company Directors Disqualification Act 1986 - no fixed look-back, any unspent convictions)*

- **Criminal**: convictions, cautions, pending charges (unspent convictions per Rehabilitation of Offenders Act 1974)
- **Civil litigation**: fraud, misrepresentation, breach of fiduciary duty, misfeasance, wrongful trading (Insolvency Act 1986 s.214)
- **Regulatory**: FCA, PRA, HMRC, CMA, SFO, ICO, Ofcom, enforcement actions, fines, public censures, prohibitions
- **Professional**: professional body disciplinary (SRA, ICAS, Law Society of Scotland, ACCA, ICAEW), FCA prohibition orders
- **Other**: FCA investigation notices, SFO investigation, whistleblowing findings, internal control deficiencies
- **Disqualification**: current or past director disqualification orders under Company Directors Disqualification Act 1986

*Disclose regardless of outcome. Non-disclosure creates greater risk than over-disclosure.*

### VI, Financial Interests and Securities

- **Interest in shares** (CA 2006 s.793 notices): direct and indirect holdings by class, amounts, percentages, voting rights
- **PDMR dealings**: share holdings, options, awards for Persons Discharging Managerial Responsibilities (UK MAR Art. 19 - notification of dealings)
- **Equity compensation**: options, restricted shares, LTIP awards, share incentive plans, grant dates, vesting, expiry
- **Derivatives and hedging**: CFD/derivative positions, hedging arrangements, short sales
- **Perquisites**: cars, medical insurance, club memberships, pension contributions, non-standard indemnities

*Supports: annual report directors' remuneration report; directors' shareholding disclosures; PDMR dealing notifications to FCA; PSC register; group accounts-related party notes.*

### VII, Certification and Signature

Respondent certifies:

- All information true, complete, and accurate, No material omissions, Will notify Company Secretary within **5 business days** of changes, Understands use in annual report, Companies House filings, audit, and due diligence, Agrees to comply with code of ethics, share dealing code (UK MAR), corporate governance code, Acknowledges fiduciary duties under CA 2006 s.170 to 177 (duty of care, skill, diligence; duty to promote success; duty to avoid conflicts)

*Electronic signatures valid per Electronic Communications Act 2000.*

## Pitfalls and Checks

- **Regulated industries**: add enhanced sections for financial services (FCA/PRA Senior Managers & Certification Regime, SMCR), healthcare (CQC, MHRA), legal (SRA, Law Society of Scotland)
- **Public vs. private**: AIM and Main Market rules apply to listed companies; private companies should still collect in standardised format if IPO/M&A anticipated
- **Look-back period**: no statutory fixed look-back for director disqualification; recommend 10 years for consistency. Unspent criminal convictions per Rehabilitation of Offenders Act 1974
- **Independence**: cross-reference UK Corporate Governance Code Provisions 10 to 12 (independence criteria); AIM Rule 26
- **Annual updates**: structure for year-over-year refresh, flag stable bio data vs. fields needing annual review
- **Privacy**: NI number under data security controls; reference UK GDPR in confidentiality notice; limit access to need-to-know; service address (not residential) is the public record
- **Materiality**: when uncertain, err toward disclosure, counsel evaluates significance
- **Non-subscribing companies**: different requirements for guarantee companies, LLPs, charitable incorporated organisations, adapt sections accordingly

## Scotland / UK Adaptation

This section documents how the US-origin D&O questionnaire skill has been adapted for Scotland/UK.

### Statutory Replacements
| US Statute / Regulation | UK / Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| SEC (Securities and Exchange Commission) | FCA (Financial Conduct Authority) / Companies House |
| Reg S-K Items 401, 402, 404, 407 | Companies Act 2006 Parts 10 to 16; UK Corporate Governance Code; FCA DTR 7; IAS 24 |
| Form 10-K / Proxy Statement | Annual Report (Strategic Report / Directors' Report / Directors' Remuneration Report) |
| Rule 13d-3 (beneficial ownership) | CA 2006 s.793 (notice to disclose interests); DTR 5 (voting rights notification); PSC Register |
| Forms 3, 4, 5 (insider reports) | UK MAR Art. 19 (PDMR dealings notification to FCA) |
| SOX Section 402 (loans to officers) | CA 2006 s.197 to 214 (loans, quasi-loans, credit to directors; substantial property transactions) |
| NYSE / Nasdaq listing standards | UK Listing Rules; AIM Rules for Companies; AQSE Rules |
| SEC / DOJ enforcement | FCA / PRA / SFO enforcement |
| FINRA / OCC / FDIC | FCA / PRA (financial services regulation, Senior Managers & Certification Regime) |
| E-SIGN Act / UETA | Electronic Communications Act 2000 |
| CCPA (California privacy) | UK GDPR / Data Protection Act 2018 |
| SSN (Social Security Number) | National Insurance Number |
| USD | GBP (£) |
| $120,000 related party threshold | IAS 24 materiality; UKLR Chapter 11 - varies by company |
| Nolo contendere | No direct UK equivalent, guilty / not guilty pleas; deferred prosecution agreements (DPAs) available |

### Key Scots / UK Law Considerations
1. **Company Directors Disqualification Act 1986** - UK-wide act; grounds include unfitness, wrongful trading, fraud. Disqualification orders 2 to 15 years. Register of disqualified directors maintained by Companies House
2. **Directors' duties** - Codified in CA 2006 s.170 to 177 (not common law fiduciary duties as in US). Includes duty to promote success (s.172), duty of care/skill/diligence (s.174), duty to avoid conflicts (s.175), duty to declare interest (s.177)
3. **Directors' register** - CA 2006 s.162 to 167 requires register of directors and register of directors' residential addresses (protected from public inspection). Service address (not residential) appears on public register
4. **Conflicts of interest** - CA 2006 s.175 (duty to avoid conflicts) and s.177/182 (duty to declare interest in proposed/existing transactions). Board authorisation of conflicts permitted by CA 2006 s.175(4) to (6)
5. **Related party transactions** - UK Listing Rules Chapter 11 for premium listed companies; IAS 24 for accounting disclosure; AIM Rule 13 for AIM companies. Different threshold structure from SEC Item 404
6. **PSC Register** - CA 2006 Part 21A requires companies to maintain register of people with significant control (>25% shares/voting rights, >25% right to appoint directors, significant influence)
7. **Insider dealing / market abuse** - UK MAR (Market Abuse Regulation) retained post-Brexit; criminal sanctions under Part V Criminal Justice Act 1993
8. **Dispute resolution** - Scottish courts (Sheriff Court / Court of Session) for director claims. Arbitration available. No equivalent of US shareholder class actions, UK has group proceedings introduced 2018 (opt-in)
9. **FCA SMCR** - Senior Managers & Certification Regime applies to FCA-authorised firms. Senior managers require FCA approval; certification staff assessed annually

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
