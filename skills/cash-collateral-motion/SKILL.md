# Cash Collateral, Insolvency (Scotland)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts an application for authority to use cash assets in administration or liquidation proceedings, a critical initial application. Covers expenses of administration, budget constraints, ring-fencing arrangements, and interim/final hearing procedures. Note: the US "cash collateral" concept under § 363 of the Bankruptcy Code does not have a direct equivalent in Scots insolvency law; this skill adapts the motion structure to Scottish procedures.

## Required Inputs

1. **Case info** - company/debtor name, court (Court of Session or Sheriff Court), case number, insolvency type (administration or liquidation)
2. **Pre-insolvency debt** - secured creditor(s), loan documents, amounts, security descriptions
3. **Cash asset sources** - accounts receivable, bank accounts, stock-in-trade proceeds, rents
4. **Budget** - 13-week cash flow projection with line-item detail
5. **Protection proposal** - what the administrator/liquidator offers secured creditors regarding their security
6. **Security records** - Register of Charges (Companies House), standard securities, floating charges, assignations, intercreditor agreements
7. **Urgency basis** - why interim relief is needed before final hearing

## Workflow

### 1. Introduction and Relief Requested

- Identify company, case, administration/liquidation, State applicant seeks court authorisation to use cash assets in the administration/liquidation, Preview protection package for secured creditors

### 2. Background

- Business operations and necessity of using cash assets, Pre-insolvency secured debt (nature, amount, security ranking, priority)
- Events leading to administration/liquidation

### 3. Jurisdiction

Cite: Courts Reform (Scotland) Act 2014 · Insolvency Act 1986 (ss 8, 14, Sch. B1) · Bankruptcy (Scotland) Act 2016 · Insolvency (Scotland) Rules 2016 (SSI 2016/210) · RCS Chapter 74 (Court of Session) or Ordinary Cause Rules / Sheriff Court Rules.

### 4. Cash Assets Description

- All cash and cash equivalents traced to secured creditors' security, Bank/deposit accounts, book debts / accounts receivable proceeds, rents and profits (if applicable)

### 5. Necessity

- Company cannot continue trading or winding-up without access to cash assets, Immediate obligations: employee wages, essential suppliers, utilities, premises, Consequences of denial: cessation of trading, job losses, asset deterioration, No alternative financing on better terms available

### 6. Proposed Budget

- 13-week cash flow projection (attach as schedule)
- Categories: employee costs, rent, utilities, insurance, critical suppliers, professional fees, Variance reporting (typically ±10 to 15%) and budget modification procedures

### 7. Protection Package (equivalent to § 363(e) in US practice)

Select from and tailor to facts:

- **Preservation of security ranking** - same ranking on post-insolvency assets of same kind
- **Expenses of administration priority** - payment as an expense of the administration
- **Periodic payments** - interest or capital payments to secured creditors
- **Equity cushion** - if asset values exceed secured debt
- **Reporting** - financial statements, budget variance, books-and-records access
- **Insurance** - maintained on secured assets
- **Additional** - milestones, operating covenants, default events

### 8. Interim and Final Orders

**Interim** (initial hearing):
- Limited period (2 to 3 weeks), budget compliance, immediate protection, schedule final hearing

**Final**:
- Extended authorisation, full protection package, default events/remedies, Ring-fencing for office-holder's professional fees (administrator/liquidator's legal advisers, creditors' committee advisers, AiB fees if applicable)
- Reservation of rights for all parties

### 9. Ring-Fencing Provisions

- Professional fee ring-fence (amount and structure)
- Accountant in Bankruptcy (AiB) fees if court supervision applies, Triggers that reduce or eliminate ring-fencing

## Checks

- File as urgent/initial application requesting interim relief, Comply with Insolvency (Scotland) Rules 2016 - highlight all specified provisions in the application, Address intercreditor agreement issues and multiple-creditor priority, Budget must be realistic, inflated projections destroy credibility, Protection must be substantive, not perfunctory, Include a supporting statement from the administrator or liquidator, Proposed order must be submitted as a separate document

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

### Key Adaptations, Replaced 11 U.S.C. § 363 → Insolvency Act 1986 / Bankruptcy (Scotland) Act 2016
- Replaced Chapter 11 bankruptcy → Administration / Liquidation (Scotland)
- Replaced UCC filings → Register of Charges (Companies House)
- Replaced debtor-in-possession → Administrator / Liquidator, Replaced "adequate protection" → Expenses of administration / Preservation of security ranking, Replaced superpriority claims → Expenses of administration priority, Replaced 28 U.S.C. §§ 157, 1334 → Courts Reform (Scotland) Act 2014 / Insolvency Act 1986
- Replaced Bankruptcy Rule 4001(b) → Insolvency (Scotland) Rules 2016 (SSI 2016/210)
- Replaced "UST" (US Trustee) → Accountant in Bankruptcy (AiB) / Insolvency Service, Replaced "§ 507(b)" → Insolvency Act 1986 Sch. B1 para 99 / Bankruptcy (Scotland) Act 2016
- Replaced "carve-out" → "ring-fence" / "ring-fencing"
- Replaced US spelling and terminology throughout

### [SCOTS] Notes, Cash collateral as a US bankruptcy concept (property subject to a security interest that is cash or cash equivalents) does not have a direct equivalent in Scots insolvency law, In Scottish administration, the administrator manages the company's property; secured creditors' rights are protected under the Insolvency Act 1986 Sch. B1, particularly paras 70 to 72 (floating charges) and para 99 (expenses)
- In Scottish liquidation, the liquidator realises assets; secured creditors' rights are governed by the Bankruptcy (Scotland) Act 2016 or the Insolvency Act 1986 as applicable, Floating charges in Scotland function differently from US blanket liens, consider the holder's position under the Companies Act 1985 (as amended) and Insolvency Act 1986
- Prescribed Part (s 176A Insolvency Act 1986) may apply to floating charge realisations, The Accountant in Bankruptcy (AiB) has supervisory functions for certain insolvency proceedings

### [VERIFY] Items Before Use, Verify the specific insolvency procedure applicable (administration under Sch. B1, creditors' voluntary liquidation, or court-ordered winding up)
- Verify current Insolvency (Scotland) Rules 2016 provisions on applications for directions / court authorisation, Verify the Register of Charges search results are current at Companies House, Check whether the secured creditor holds a floating charge, fixed charge, standard security, or other security type, implications differ, Confirm with the instructing solicitor whether the application should be made to the Court of Session or Sheriff Court based on the company's share capital and location, Verify any intercreditor or subordination agreements that affect priority

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
