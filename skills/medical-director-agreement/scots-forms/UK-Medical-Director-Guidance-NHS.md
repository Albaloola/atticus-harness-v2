# Medical Director Agreements, UK & Scottish Reference

## Key Legislation & Framework

- **Health and Social Care Act 2008** (regulated activities) - CQC registration framework (England)
- **NHS Scotland** operates under separate governance from NHS England
- **Medical Act 1983** - GMC registration and fitness to practise
- **Corporate governance**: Companies Act 2006 (if NHS trust or private entity)
- **Bribery Act 2010** - replaces US Anti-Kickback Statute concerns (no direct AKS equivalent)
- **Criminal Finances Act 2017** - corporate criminal liability for failure to prevent tax evasion
- **NHS Standard Contract** - template for NHS commissioning relationships

## Key Differences from US

| US Concept | UK/Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Anti-Kickback Statute (AKS) 42 U.S.C. §1320a-7b | No direct equivalent. Bribery Act 2010 covers corrupt payments. NHS anti-fraud guidance. |
| Stark Law 42 U.S.C. §1395nn | No direct equivalent. NHS manages self-referral via GMC guidance and NHS contracting rules. |
| FMV requirement | Less codified in UK. Must be commercial reasonableness and arm's-length pricing. |
| OIG exclusions | No direct UK equivalent; GMC fitness to practise determines ability to practice. |
| HIPAA | Data Protection Act 2018 / UK GDPR, equivalent obligations for patient data |
| Corporate practice of medicine | Not a concept in UK; doctors can be employed by hospitals/companies directly |
| DEA registration | GMC registration + prescribing rights (no separate controlled substance registration) |
| EMTALA | No direct equivalent. NHS has Accident & Emergency duties under NHS Act 2006. |
| False Claims Act | Fraud Act 2006, NHS fraud investigation |
| Malpractice/liability | NHS indemnity covers employed clinicians; independent contractors need own cover |

## Scottish Differences

- **NHS Scotland** - governed by Scottish Ministers under the National Health Service (Scotland) Act 1978
- **Healthcare Improvement Scotland** - equivalent of CQC for regulation in Scotland
- **Scottish Public Services Ombudsman** - handles complaints about NHS Scotland
- **GMC** - UK-wide regulatory body for doctors
- **Independent sector**: Private healthcare in Scotland regulated by Healthcare Improvement Scotland
- **Medical Director role**: Each NHS Scotland Health Board has a Medical Director; private hospitals similarly appoint

## FMV & Compensation (UK Context)

- **Private sector only**: NHS Medical Directors are salaried under Consultant contract terms
- **BMA** (British Medical Association) provides benchmarking for medical manager roles
- **NHS Consultant contract**: 10 PA (Programmed Activities) per week typical; additional PAs for management
- **Private healthcare**: Compensation should reflect market rate for time, responsibility, and clinical risk, No AKS/Stark prohibition on compensation varying with referrals, but Bribery Act prohibits corrupt arrangements

## Restrictive Covenants

- **NHS**: non-compete clauses uncommon; non-solicitation of patients/other employees limited
- **Private sector**: enforceable if reasonable and protect legitimate business interest
- **GMC guidance**: Good Medical Practice, doctors must not let commercial arrangements affect clinical judgment
- **Duty of continuity of care**: may limit ability to enforce restrictive covenants against medical professionals
