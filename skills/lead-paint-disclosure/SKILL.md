## Governing Legal Framework (Scotland)

[SCOTS: Note, No direct UK equivalent of the US Residential Lead-Based Paint Hazard Reduction Act 1992]

| Authority | Citation | Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| Housing (Scotland) Act 2006 | ss.12-19 (Repairing Standard) | PRS: landlord must keep property wind/watertight, free from rising/chronic damp |
| Housing (Scotland) Act 2006 | ss.71-73 (Tolerable Standard) | House must be free from lead (pipes) - s.71(2)(c) |
| Environmental Protection Act 1990 | Part IIA (Contaminated Land) | Lead contamination in soil/dust may trigger investigation |
| Consumer Protection from Unfair Trading Regs 2008 | Regs 5-6 | Misleading omission of material fact in sale/letting |
| Consumer Rights Act 2015 | ss.9-10, 49 | Goods/services must be of satisfactory quality; fitness for purpose |
| Common law (delict) | Negligent misrepresentation | Liability if seller knowingly conceals hazard [SCOTS: Note] |
| Common law (caveat emptor) | Latent defects | Buyer beware; seller not obliged to disclose unless actively concealing [SCOTS: Note] |
| Building Standards (Scotland) Regulations | Reg 4.2 | Lead pipe restrictions in new builds/renovations |

**Penalties:** No direct lead-paint disclosure penalty regime as in US. Liability flows through: (a) misrepresentation action (delict), (b) breach of Repairing Standard (PRS only, landlord faces enforcement by local authority, fines up to £5,000), (c) contaminated land remediation (SEPA/LA, cost recovery), (d) complaint to First-tier Tribunal (Housing Chamber) for PRS.

## Document Structure

Draft these sections in order:

### 1. Header & Party Identification

- Title: **"Property Condition Disclosure, Lead Hazard Information"**
- Property address + legal description; disclosure date, Full legal names and contact addresses of all parties, Transaction type checkbox: Sale / Lease

### 2. Seller/Landlord Disclosure Statement

Disclosing party selects **one**:

| Option | Detail Required |
|---|---|
| **A, Known lead hazards** | Locations, condition/extent, date acquired; distinguish lead-based paint from lead pipework from contaminated dust/soil |
| **B, No knowledge after reasonable inquiry** | Affirmative statement confirming no known lead hazards |
| **C, Remediated** | Remediation description, professional credentials, attach documentation |

"Lead hazards" include: (a) peeling/flaking lead-based paint (pre-1965 decorative paints), (b) lead pipework (water supply or internal plumbing), (c) lead-contaminated soil or dust (near busy roads or industrial sites).

### 3. Records & Reports Schedule

List all attached documents by annex number: inspection reports, abatement certificates, clearance reports, environmental health correspondence.

If no records exist, include affirmative "no records available" statement.

### 4. Home Report / Single Survey References

- Reference any relevant findings from the Home Report Single Survey (if a sale)
- Note any conflicting information between seller knowledge and survey findings, If a survey flagged lead-related issues, attach relevant extract

### 5. Buyer/Tenant Acknowledgments

Individual initialed/signed acknowledgments:

- Receipt of seller/landlord disclosure statement, Receipt of Home Report (sale only) or property condition report (lease)
- Receipt of all attached records and reports
- *(Sales only)* Additional due diligence opportunity acknowledged

Each item gets its own initial/signature line.

### 6. Certifications & Signature Blocks

Three categories, each with printed name + signature + date:

| Party | Certifies |
|---|---|
| **Seller/Landlord** | Information true, accurate, complete to best of knowledge and belief |
| **Buyer/Tenant** | Receipt of disclosure confirmation; advised to seek independent survey |
| **Solicitor/Agent** | Advised seller/landlord of disclosure obligations |

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **Caveat emptor** applies, seller is not obliged to volunteer latent defects (but must not lie)
- **Active concealment** (e.g., painting over flaking lead paint to hide it) = potential delictual liability
- **PRS only**: Repairing Standard (s.12 Housing (Scotland) Act 2006) is absolute; landlord must ensure property is free from rising/chronic damp and that installations are safe
- **Tolerable Standard**: lead **pipes** are a statutory hazard (s.71(2)(c)) - if property fails, local authority can serve notice
- **Lead paint vs. lead pipes** are legally distinct, never conflate
- **No EPA pamphlet** equivalent in UK/Scotland, consider directing to HSE lead safety guidance
- **Check EPC** - building fabric noted may reference lead elements

- Contaminated land (EPA 1990 Part IIA): lead in soil is enforceable by SEPA/local authority, Home Report is mandatory for residential sales in Scotland, includes single survey condition report, Check local authority environmental health for enforcement history on the property
