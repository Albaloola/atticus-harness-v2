# Example: simple procedure style rewrite

## Before

This case stands as a clear example of the respondent's blatant disregard for their contractual obligations. Despite repeated requests, the respondent has failed to engage constructively or act in good faith. The claimant has been navigating this challenging situation for months and now respectfully seeks the intervention of the court.

## After

I ask the sheriff to order the respondent to pay £850, with expenses.

The claim arises from a written agreement dated 12 March 2024. Under that agreement, I paid the respondent £850 to supply and fit a replacement door by 30 April 2024.

The respondent did not fit the door. I emailed on 2 May, 10 May and 24 May 2024 asking for either the work to be completed or the money returned. The respondent replied on 27 May saying the money would be refunded within 7 days. I have not received the refund.

I rely on the agreement, proof of payment, my emails dated 2 May, 10 May and 24 May, and the respondent's email dated 27 May 2024.

## Notes

- The rewrite identifies the order sought at the start.
- It uses simple procedure terms: claimant, respondent, sheriff.
- It replaces vague phrases with dates, documents and the amount claimed.
- It removes allegations of bad faith because the facts can be stated without that label.
