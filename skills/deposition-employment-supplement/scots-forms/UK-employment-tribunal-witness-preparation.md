# UK Employment Tribunal, Witness Preparation & Hearing Framework

## Key Differences: US Deposition vs UK Employment Tribunal

| US Concept | UK/Scotland Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Deposition (pre-trial oral examination) | No direct equivalent. ET uses witness statements + cross-examination at hearing |
| EEOC Charge | ET1 Claim Form (submitted to Employment Tribunal) |
| Employer Position Statement | ET3 Response Form |
| Discovery | Disclosure of documents via Order for Specific Disclosure (ET Rules, r.31 to 34) |
| Interrogatories | Written questions via Order for Further Information (ET Rules, r.30) |
| Summary Judgment | Summary Judgment (ET Rules, r.37 to 39 - limited scope) |
| McDonnell Douglas burden-shifting | *Igen v Wong* [2005] IRLR 258 (modified burden of proof) |
| Title VII discrimination | Equality Act 2010 (ss.13 to 27, s.39) |
| ADEA | No direct equivalent (age = protected under EqA 2010) |
| ADA | No direct equivalent (disability = protected under EqA 2010) |
| FLSA | Employment Rights Act 1996 / National Minimum Wage Act 1998 / Working Time Regulations |

## Scotland-Specific

- Employment Tribunals in Scotland: Glasgow, Edinburgh, Aberdeen, Dundee, Scottish ET Rules of Procedure (Schedule 1, Employment Tribunals (Constitution and Rules of Procedure) Regulations 2013)
- ACAS (Advisory, Conciliation and Arbitration Service) - mandatory Early Conciliation before ET1 can be issued, Sheriff Court may hear some employment-related claims (breach of contract, unlawful deductions)
- Prescription (limitation): 3 months less one day for most claims (ET); 5 years for breach of contract (Sheriff Court)

## Key Case Law (UK Employment)

- *Igen Ltd v Wong* [2005] IRLR 258 - burden of proof in discrimination claims
- *Shamoon v Chief Constable of RUC* [2003] IRLR 285 - comparators in discrimination
- *Vento v Chief Constable of West Yorkshire* [2002] IRLR 177 - injury to feelings bands (updated annually)
- *Buckland v Bournemouth University* [2010] IRLR 445 - constructive dismissal
- *Chief Constable of Lincolnshire v Stubbs* [1999] IRLR 81 - knowledge in disability claims

## Witness Statement Preparation

In UK Employment Tribunals, witnesses:
1. Provide a written witness statement exchanged before hearing
2. The statement stands as evidence-in-chief
3. Witness is then cross-examined by the other side
4. Re-examined by the party calling them
5. Tribunal members may ask questions

Preparation should focus on:
- Ensuring statement is the witness's own words, Testing for inconsistencies with documents, Preparing for cross-examination on weak points, Understanding the ET burden of proof framework
