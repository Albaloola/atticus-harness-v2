# [SCOTS] Plan of Reorganisation (Administration / CVA / Scheme of Arrangement)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a statutory-compliant restructuring document with precise claim classifications, treatment provisions, and implementation mechanics under Scottish and UK insolvency law.

## Prerequisites

1. **Company identification** - legal name, registered number, registered office, jurisdiction of incorporation
2. **Case information** - court reference (if applicable), petition date, circumstances of distress
3. **Financial data** - asset valuations, liability amounts, creditor schedules, existing financing
4. **Claims register** - lodged claims with disputed amounts, security positions, preferential status
5. **Executory contracts/leases** - complete list with amounts due, rights in leasehold property
6. **Funding sources** - exit finance term sheets, projected cash flow, asset sale proceeds, capital contributions
7. **Schedules/disclosures** - statement of affairs, schedule of creditors, monthly monitoring reports

## Output Structure

Draft using the following article structure. Each article must satisfy referenced statutory provisions. Number sections: Article I → Section 1.1 → (a) → (i).

### Article I, Preamble

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Company identification | Legal name, registered number, registered office, jurisdiction of incorporation |
| Statutory authority | Insolvency Act 1986 Sch. B1 (administration), Parts I to II (CVA), Companies Act 2006 Part 26 (scheme) |
| Case narrative | Concise summary of financial distress, relevant insolvency events, restructuring strategy |

### Article II, Definitions

Define all terms across four categories:

- **Statutory** (cite Insolvency Act / Companies Act provisions): Administration Expense (Sch. B1 para 99), Admitted Claim, Secured Creditor, Preferential Creditor (IA 1986 s. 386 / Sch. 6), Prescribed Part (IA 1986 s. 176A), Moratorium (Sch. B1 para 42 to 43), Office-Holder (administrator / supervisor / liquidator), Creditor
- **Temporal** (use actual dates): Presentation Date, Administration Date, Effective Date, Scheme Sanction Date, Proposal Date
- **Party**: Company, Office-Holder, Supervisor, Creditor, Member
- **Operational**: Distribution Date, Professional Fees, Convenience Class, Disputed Claim, plus plan-specific terms (Exit Facility, New Shares, Litigation Trust) as applicable

### Article III, Classification

**Preferential claims** (paid in priority per IA 1986 s. 386 / Sch. 6):
- Pension contributions, employee remuneration, certain levies, Secondary preferential creditors (depositors with financial institutions, Banks Act 2009)

**Secured creditors:**

| Class | Description | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Fixed charge holders | Property-specific security; enforce rights outside moratorium with court permission |
| 2 | Floating charge holders | Prescribed part (s. 176A) carve-out for unsecured creditors; separate representation |

**Unsecured creditors:**
- Preferential (see above)
- Ordinary unsecured, Prescribed part beneficiaries (part of floating charge realisations)
- Connected persons (loans from directors/associates, subordinated by statute or agreement)

**Deferred / subordinated:**
- Claims subordinated by agreement or statute, Members / shareholders

### Article IV, Treatment Provisions

**Unsecured treatment options:**
- Payment in full in cash on Effective Date, CVA: reduced dividend over period (e.g. pence in the £)
- Scheme: compromise or arrangement with class voting, Deferred or instalment terms

| Element | Specification |
|---|---|
| Recovery | Pence in the £ or percentage of admitted claim |
| Distribution form | Cash, new securities, or combination |
| Payment timing | Lump sum vs. periodic dividend schedule |
| Funding source | Operations, exit facility, asset sales, contributions |

**Secured creditor options** (one per class):
- Variation of security terms by consent or scheme, Surrender or release of security, Cram-down on cross-class basis (Restructuring Plan, Corporate Insolvency and Governance Act 2020)

**Connected persons / subordinated claims:**
- Treatment must not unfairly prejudice other creditors, Subordination by agreement or IA 1986 s. 74 / s. 249

**Restructuring Plan (CIGA 2020):** Cross-class cram-down available, if a class votes against but the plan satisfies:
- Condition A: none of the dissenting class would be worse off under the plan than under the relevant alternative, Condition B: the plan has been approved by 75% by value of a class that would receive a payment or have a genuine economic interest

### Article V, Implementation Mechanics

**Funding sources** - quantify each:

| Source | Details |
|---|---|
| Cash on hand | Projected Effective Date balance |
| Operating cash flow | Reference financial projections |
| Asset sales | Process and timeline |
| Exit financing | Terms, lender, commitment status |
| Capital contributions | Source and conditions |

**Post-implementation:** Company structure, management, corporate governance changes.

**Securities compliance:** FCA listing rules, Market Abuse Regulation, Companies Act 2006 s. 549 to 551 (authority to allot shares).

### Article VI, Distribution Procedures

- **Office-Holder / Supervisor** - identity and duties (calculations, records, tax forms, distributions)
- **Initial Distribution Date** - later of Effective Date or 10 business days after disputed claim becomes admitted
- **Method** - cheque (default) or bank transfer above threshold
- **Disputed Claims Reserve** - reserve distributable amount pending resolution
- **Unclaimed distributions** - revert to company or prescribe under statute after period
- **Tax compliance** - withholding per HMRC requirements; require UTR / tax reference
- **Priority waterfall** - Expenses of the administration/arrangement → Preferential creditors → Fixed charge holders (from their asset) → Floating charge holders → Prescribed part → Ordinary unsecured → Subordinated → Members

### Article VII, Executory Contracts, Leases & Licences

- **Adopted contracts** - list by counterparty, description, amount due; payment per terms on Effective Date
- **Disclaimed contracts** - list specifically, or unlisted contracts disclaimed on Effective Date; disclaimer per IA 1986 s. 178 / s. 315
- **Rejection claims** - prove as unsecured debt; time limit for lodging
- **Special contracts** - IP licences: licensee rights outlined in contract; real property leases: timing and LIETA (Landlord and Tenant Act implications); CBAs: Transfer of Undertakings (TUPE) / s. 188 TULRCA implications

### Article VIII, Conditions Precedent

**To Sanction/Approval:** Court sanction (scheme) or creditor approval (CVA); at least 75% by value of each class voting (scheme); 75% by value, 50% by number (CVA).

**To Effective Date:** Sanction order final and not stayed; regulatory approvals obtained; exit financing closed; capital contributions received.

**Waiver/deadline:** Company or office-holder may waive non-statutory conditions. Deadline: 60 to 90 days post-sanction (vacate if not met).

### Article IX, Effects of Sanction

- **Binding effect** - binds company, all creditors (CVA), all class members (scheme)
- **Discharge** - all pre-Effective Date debts discharged on Effective Date
- **Moratorium ends** - on administration exit or conclusion of CVA
- **Vesting** - property vests per terms of the arrangement
- **Releases** - consensual company releases; third-party releases limited per case law

### Article X, Retained Jurisdiction

Court retains jurisdiction for: interpretation of the arrangement/scheme; claims adjudication; professional fees; post-sanction modifications (Companies Act 2006 s. 900 for schemes); dispute resolution; avoidance of transactions at undervalue / gratuitous alienations (IA 1986 s. 242); preferences (s. 243); winding-up petitions.

### Article XI, Miscellaneous

- **Severability** - invalid provisions severed; remainder enforceable
- **Amendments** - pre-sanction per scheme/CVA proposal rules; post-sanction with consent and/or court approval
- **Governing law** - Scots law / laws of Scotland; plan controls over proposals or scheme documents
- **Notices** - written to specified addresses; deemed given on delivery or 2 business days after first-class posting
- **Statutory fees** - HMRC quarterly returns, AiB fees (if registered), Companies House filings
- **Voting** - CVA: 75% by value, 50% by number of creditors voting; Scheme: 75% by value of each class

## Guidelines

- Verify all cross-references and statutory citations before finalising; mark uncertain cites with [VERIFY]
- Coordinate with independent report (CVA) or explanatory statement (scheme) - adequate information standard
- **Cram-down readiness (CIGA 2020):** if any dissenting class exists under a Restructuring Plan, verify Conditions A and B
- **Feasibility:** projections must demonstrate reasonable probability of success
- **Good faith:** proposal made for honest and legitimate purposes, Required exhibits: independent business review, financial projections, contract schedules, Include table of contents if document exceeds 10 pages, Adapt to relevant court rules (Rules of the Court of Session, Sheriff Court Rules)

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted for use under Scottish and UK insolvency law. Key differences from the original US Chapter 11 approach:

| US Concept | Scottish/UK Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Chapter 11 / 11 U.S.C. §§ 1121 to 1129 | Administration (IA 1986 Sch. B1), CVA (IA 1986 Parts I to II), Scheme of Arrangement (CA 2006 Part 26), Restructuring Plan (CIGA 2020) |
| bankruptcy court | Court of Session (Outer House) - commercial and insolvency proceedings |
| U.S. Trustee / Office of the UST | Accountant in Bankruptcy (AiB) - for personal insolvency; no equivalent office for corporate insolvency |
| § 362 automatic stay | Moratorium in administration (Sch. B1 para 42 to 43); interim moratorium in CVA (IA 1986 s. 1A, Sch. A1) |
| Debtor in possession | Administrator (office-holder); directors remain under CVA with supervisor oversight |
| DIP financing | Administrator's borrowing powers (Sch. B1 para 66); administration expenses priority |
| Cramdown ($ 1129(b)) | Cross-class cram-down under Restructuring Plan (CIGA 2020, IA 1986 s. 901C to 901G as inserted); no general cram-down in administration/CVA |
| 11 U.S.C. references | Insolvency Act 1986; Companies Act 2006; Corporate Insolvency and Governance Act 2020 |
| Federal Rules of Bankruptcy Procedure | Rules of the Court of Session (Chapter 74, Insolvency and Companies); Sheriff Court Rules (Ordinary Cause Rules) |
| Disclosure statement | Explanatory statement (scheme, CA 2006 s. 897); independent report (CVA, IA 1986 s. 2) |
| Plan supplement | Scheme proposal / CVA proposal; modifications require consent and/or court order |
| USD figures | GBP (£) |

Statutory triggers: inability to pay debts (IA 1986 s. 123), winding-up petition presented, or directors' duty to consider insolvency (s. 214, wrongful trading).

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
