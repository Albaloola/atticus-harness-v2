# Scotland/UK Forms & References for Lead Paint & Housing Condition Disclosure

## Scottish Housing Forms & Legislation

| Form / Document | Purpose |
|-----------------|---------|
| Home Report | Mandatory for residential sales in Scotland, includes Single Survey, EPC, Property Questionnaire |
| Property Questionnaire | Seller's disclosure: council tax, factoring, alterations, services |
| Standard Survey (RIAS) | Building condition report |
| EPC (Energy Performance Certificate) | Energy rating; required for sale/lease |
| Repairing Standard Notice | Landlord's obligation under Housing (Scotland) Act 2006 |
| Tolerable Standard Assessment | Coverts to minimum housing quality (s.72, Housing (Scotland) Act 2006) |
| Repairs Notice (s.30, s.32) | Local authority enforcement for substandard housing |
| Housing HAZ Assessment (Scotland) | Scottish HHSRS-equivalent toolkit |
| Medical Referral Form | Environmental health for health-related housing hazards |

## Key Scottish Legislation

| Act | Relevance |
|-----|-----------|
| Housing (Scotland) Act 2006 | Repairing Standard, Tolerable Standard, HAZ assessment |
| Housing (Scotland) Act 2001 | Scottish Secure Tenancy, Right to Repair |
| Environmental Protection Act 1990 | Part IIA, Contaminated Land (includes lead contamination) |
| Control of Lead at Work Regulations 2002 | Lead safety in workplace (limited housing relevance) |
| Building Standards (Scotland) Regulations | Building warrant requirements re: lead pipes |
| Tenancy Deposit Schemes (Scotland) Regulations 2011 | Deposit protection |

## UK-Wide Lead & Housing Condition Context

- **No UK equivalent** of the US Residential Lead-Based Paint Hazard Reduction Act 1992
- UK approach: environmental health enforcement via HHSRS (England/Wales) / HAZ (Scotland) rather than mandatory transactional disclosure, Lead paint is not specifically singled out in Scottish property sales disclosures, The Home Report Property Questionnaire asks generally about defects/alterations but not specifically about lead paint, For Private Rented Sector: Repairing Standard (s.12-19, Housing (Scotland) Act 2006) requires property be free from rising/chronic damp and have safe electrical/gas installations but does not require lead paint testing, Common law: seller's duty to disclose latent defects known as **caveat emptor** (buyer beware) unless seller actively conceals
- **Consumer Rights Act 2015** may provide remedies for misdescription of condition
