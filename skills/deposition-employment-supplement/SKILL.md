# Employment Litigation, Witness Preparation & Hearing Supplement (Scotland/UK)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Claim-specific examination frameworks for UK Employment Tribunal hearings. Use with @witness-statement-preparation and @et-hearing-strategy.

## Prerequisites

1. Personnel file, performance reviews, disciplinary records
2. ET1 Claim Form and ET3 Response
3. Applicable workplace policies (equality, discipline, grievance, whistleblowing)
4. Comparator employee information and treatment history
5. Correspondence and investigation files
6. ACAS Early Conciliation certificate

## Claim Elements & Defences

| Claim | Elements | Key Defences |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| **Discrimination** (Equality Act 2010 ss.13, 15, 18, 19) | Protected characteristic; unfavourable/adverse treatment; actual or comparator evidence; causal link | Material factor not related to characteristic; occupational requirement; objective justification (indirect) |
| **Harassment** (EqA s.26) | Unwanted conduct related to relevant protected characteristic; purpose or effect of violating dignity or creating intimidating/degrading/humiliating/offensive environment | Conduct not unwanted; not related to characteristic; reasonable person would not consider harassment |
| **Victimisation** (EqA s.27) | Protected act; detriment; causal connection | No knowledge of protected act; independent legitimate reason; timing alone insufficient |
| **Constructive Dismissal** (ERA s.95(1)(c)) | Fundamental breach of contract by employer; employee resigns in response (not affirmed); no delay | No fundamental breach; employee affirmed contract; resignation not caused by breach |
| **Unfair Dismissal** (ERA s.94) | Employee status; dismissed; qualifying period | Potentially fair reason (capability/conduct/redundancy/SOSR/statutory); reasonable procedure (s.98(4)) |
| **Unauthorised Deductions / Wages** (ERA ss.13 to 27) | Deduction from wages; worker status; no authorisation or consent | Authorised by statute/contract; worker consent; overpayment recovery |
| **Working Time** (WTR 1998) | Hours worked; rest breaks; holiday entitlement; employer knowledge | Worker agreement; relevant exemptions |

## [SCOTS: Note] UK Employment Tribunals vs US Court Process

UK Employment Tribunals do not have pre-hearing depositions or interrogatories as known in US practice. Evidence is adduced through:
- Written witness statements (stand as evidence-in-chief)
- Cross-examination at hearing, Orders for Specific Disclosure (ET Rules r.31 to 34)
- Orders for Further Information (ET Rules r.30)

The examination frameworks below should be read as **cross-examination preparation outlines** rather than deposition scripts. Witness preparation focuses on testing statement consistency and preparing for cross-examination.

## Claimant (Pursuer) Examination Topics

### Universal Topics

- **Employment history**: start date, positions, promotions, duties, reviews, discipline, termination/resignation
- **Alleged wrongdoing**: each act, dates, actors, locations, claimant's contemporaneous response
- **Grievances/Complaints**: to whom, exact words, when, formal grievance outcome, ACAS involvement
- **Prior issues**: prior employment, discrimination complaints, personal injury claims, benefits history

### By Claim Type

**Discrimination** - Protected characteristic (how/when employer learned); qualifications and deficiencies; comparators (not materially different circumstances); intent evidence (discriminatory comments, bias incidents); burden of proof shift

**Harassment** - Each incident separately (description, actors, witnesses, frequency); unwelcomeness (told harasser to stop, participated in similar conduct); effect (dignity/ environment test); reporting chain and employer response; employer's preventative steps

**Victimisation** - Protected act (what, to whom, when); detriment (what, when, who); causal connection (timeline, linking statements, comparable disciplinary outcomes)

**Constructive Dismissal** - Breach relied upon (express term or implied); mutual trust and confidence; date of breach; date of resignation; whether employee affirmed; timing

**Unfair Dismissal** - Dismissal details (effective date, stated reason, who communicated); procedure followed; contract terms; appeals outcome; contributory fault

**Wage / Working Time** - Schedule (regular hours, overtime, breaks, holiday taken); compensation (rate, overtime eligibility, actual pay, unlawful deduction); off-the-clock work; employer's records vs actual pattern

## Respondent (Defender) Witness Topics

### Decision-Maker

- **The decision**: role, timing, who else involved, factors considered, stated reason (s.98(4) fairness)
- **Knowledge**: what knew of claimant's performance, grievances, protected characteristic; when learned
- **Process**: governing policies, whether followed, HR/legal consulted, documentation, right to be accompanied
- **Comparators**: analogous situations (not "like for like" but no material difference); differential treatment explanation

### HR Representative / People Manager

- **Policies**: equality policy, dignity at work policy, grievance/disciplinary procedures
- **Compliance**: whether procedures followed; deviations and approvals
- **Grievances**: what reported, investigation steps, witnesses, findings, remedial action
- **Personnel file**: documents present, creators, timing, anything missing (data subject access request)
- **Training**: equality and harassment training records and attendance

### Other Witnesses

- **Conduct witnesses**: what seen/heard, when/where, others present, whether reported
- **Comparator witnesses**: role, circumstances, treatment, similarities/differences to claimant

## Document Topics

| Document | Cover |
|----------|-------|
| Personnel file | Authenticate each document; identify creators/approvers |
| Performance reviews | Who prepared; input sources; approvals; changes; whether claimant saw |
| Disciplinary/ grievance records | Triggering conduct; decision-maker; process; comparator treatment; outcome |

| Investigation file | Investigator; terms of reference; witnesses; findings; recommendations |

## Impeachment Sources

1. **Performance reviews** - contradict claimant's self-assessment vs. claimed treatment
2. **Prior grievances** - contradict "never complained" with documented procedure
3. **Investigation findings** - challenge claimant's characterisation with official conclusions
4. **Comparator treatment** - counter "singled out" with evidence of equal application
5. **Timeline inconsistencies** - ET1 dates vs. hearing statement; grievance date vs. dismissal/resignation timeline
6. **Application/CV** - undisclosed prior employment, qualifications, gaps; tribunal may draw adverse inference

## Pitfalls

- Lock in comparator "materially different circumstances" foundation before exploring differences, Examine each harassment incident separately, prevent lumping into general narrative, Pin down exact timing of decision-maker's knowledge relative to protected act (victimisation)
- Get specific hour estimates with methodology; cover each category separately (wages)
- Equality Act 2010 s.136: once prima facie case established, burden shifts to respondent, Vento bands for injury to feelings are updated annually, verify current amounts [VERIFY]

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from the US Employment Litigation Deposition Supplement. Key changes:

- **No depositions in UK Employment Tribunals**: replaced deposition framework with cross-examination preparation and witness statement testing
- **Statutes**: Title VII/ADA/ADEA → Equality Act 2010; FLSA → ERA/WTR; FMLA → Shared Parental Leave Regulations
- **Burden of proof**: McDonnell Douglas → *Igen v Wong* modified burden (EqA s.136)
- **Defences**: Faragher-Ellerth → employer's statutory defence under EqA s.109(4)
- **Harassment**: severe/pervasive → purpose/effect violating dignity (EqA s.26)
- **Retaliation**: adverse action → detriment due to protected act (victimisation, EqA s.27)
- **Wrongful termination**: at-will / contract → constructive dismissal / unfair dismissal
- **Comparators**: similarly situated → no material difference (*Shamoon*)
- **Tort/delict**: discrimination is statutory in UK, not tort-based
- **Disclosure**: broad discovery → Orders for Specific Disclosure (ET Rules)
- **Scotland-specific**: Scottish ET Rules; Sheriff Court for breach of contract; ACAS Early Conciliation; 3-month limitation period
- **Timing**: US multiyear → UK 3 months less one day for most ET claims
- **No jury trials** in Employment Tribunal; judge alone (or judge + panel members)
- **No punitive damages**: compensation capped; injury to feelings per Vento bands [VERIFY current figures]

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
