# Motion for Expenses (Formerly Attorneys' Fees)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Draft a post-judgment motion for expenses establishing successful-party status, applying judicial discretion on reasonableness, and presenting a documented account of expenses.

## Prerequisites

- Final decree or interlocutor establishing expenses (successful party entitlement)
- Expenses authority, statute (e.g., Legal Aid (Scotland) Act 1986), contract, or court rules, Contemporaneous time records for all fee-earners, Fee-earner qualifications (CVs, specialisations, recognitions)
- Rate evidence, current fee scales (Table of Fees), judicial findings, market data, Court process and procedural history, Act of Sederunt rules on format, page limits, and time limits for lodging accounts

## Quick Start

1. Confirm expenses basis and successful party status from decree
2. Collect time records, outlays, and fee-earner credentials
3. Prepare account of expenses following the Table of Fees
4. Document excluded work (if any) to build credibility
5. Draft motion following the section structure below
6. Prepare supporting affidavit and inventory of productions

## Motion Structure

### I. Caption & Compliance

- Full court name, sheriffdom (if Sheriff Court), case number, party designations, Pursuer/Defender, Title: "Motion for Decree for Expenses"
- Verify local rules on formatting and time limits

### II. Introduction

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Moving party | Name + successful party identification |
| Expenses authority | Statute or contractual clause (verbatim) |
| Decree reference | Date, findings, relief granted |
| Total expenses sought | Amount (in GBP) with breakdown |
| Roadmap | Authority → rates → work done → outlays |

### III. Litigation Narrative

Chronological narrative emphasising expense-reasonableness factors:

- Nature of dispute, claims, defences, stakes, Procedural milestones with dates, motions (with outcomes), commission and diligence, proof, Complexity indicators, novel issues, document volume, expert witnesses, party count, Opposing conduct increasing work, cite specific interlocutors/correspondence, Quality of representation, favourable outcomes, results achieved

### IV. Legal Standard

Present in this order:

1. **Expenses authority** - quote statutory/contractual language verbatim
2. **Successful party test** - jurisdiction-specific; address partial success issues
3. **Judicial discretion on reasonableness** - *MacPhail v. MacPhail* 1988 SLT (Sh Ct) 34; *Reid v. J. & J. Ingram* (1867) 6 M 248
4. **Table of Fees** - applicable Sheriff Court or Court of Session table [VERIFY current version]
5. **Judicial expenses factors** - work done, complexity, importance, urgency, skill required
6. **Expenses enhancement** - aggravated damages (where applicable); no punitive damages in Scots law
7. **Expense allocation** - partial success adjustment
8. **Expenses of expenses** - authority for time spent on the expenses motion itself

### V. Successful Party Analysis

- Quote interlocutor establishing favourable outcome, Apply jurisdiction's test to specific findings, Address degree of success relative to expenses sought, Preempt limited-success arguments

### VI. Account of Expenses

**A. Fee-Earner Rate Justification** - for each fee-earner:

| Fee-Earner | Role | Years Post-Qualifying | Rate | Market Evidence |
|------------|------|-------|------|-----------------|
| [Name] | Partner/Solicitor Advocate | [X] | £[X]/hr | [Table of Fees, awards] |
| [Name] | Solicitor | [X] | £[X]/hr | [Table of Fees, awards] |
| [Name] | Trainee/Paralegal | [X] | £[X]/hr | [Table of Fees, awards] |

Support with: Table of Fees rates, judicial fee awards, fee-earner qualifications.

Note: Contingency fees (speculative fee arrangements / pactum de quota litis) are prohibited in Scotland. No enhancement/multiplier for contingent risk.

**B. Work by Phase**

| Phase | Hours | Justification |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| Case assessment & pleadings | | Complexity, investigation scope |
| Commission and diligence | | Volume, disputes |
| Document review | | Document count, privilege review |
| Witness preparation | | Witness count, preparation |
| Motion practice | | Per-motion breakdown with outcomes |
| Expert work | | Retention, reports |
| Settlement/negotiation | | Sessions, preparation |
| Proof preparation | | Witnesses, productions (exhibits) |
| Proof/hearing | | Days in court |
| Post-decree | | Expenses motion, enforcement |
| **Total** | | |

**C. Billing Judgment** - document excluded time:
- Administrative/clerical tasks at professional rates, Duplicative or learning-curve work, Error correction, Quantify hours excluded per category

**D. Staffing Appropriateness** - routine tasks to trainees/paralegals; justify senior time on routine work.

**E. Aggravated Damages / Enhanced Expenses** (if sought) - basis (exceptional conduct, bad faith), jurisdiction-specific authority.

### VII. Supporting Affidavit

Numbered-paragraph affidavit (signed before notary public or authorised person):

- ¶¶ 1 to 3: Deponent qualifications, role, personal knowledge
- ¶¶ 4 to 6: Time record authentication, contemporaneous, accurate
- ¶¶ 7 to 9: Excluded work, categories, amounts
- ¶¶ 10 to 12: Rate reasonableness, customary rates, Table of Fees consistency
- ¶¶ 13 to 15: Hours reasonableness, necessity, complexity, staffing
- ¶¶ 16+: Additional fee-earners (qualifications, rates)
- Execution block: venue, date, signature, notarisation

Consider supplemental affidavits: peer rate affidavits, client value declaration.

### VIII. Productions (Exhibits)

| No. | Contents |
|----------|---------|
| 1 | Detailed time records (date, fee-earner, hours, description) |
| 2 | Interlocutor / decree establishing expenses entitlement |
| 3 | Expenses authority (statute or contract provision) |
| 4 | Fee-earner CVs/resumes |
| 5 | Market rate evidence (fee awards, surveys) |
| 6 | Comparable expense awards / schedules |
| 7 | Key interlocutors showing complexity or opposing conduct |

Redact work product from time entries; explain redactions in affidavit.

### IX. Crave (Prayer for Relief)

- Solicitors' fees: £[amount] (+ outlays/disbursements = £[total])
- Outlays and disbursements per schedule, Auditor of the Court to tax expenses (if not agreed)
- Pre/post-decree interest (specify rate and calculation date)
- Expenses of expenses (supplemental application reserved)
- Such other relief as the court deems just

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **Cite precisely** - OSCOLA / Edinburgh Legal Style format; verify citations are current and not overruled
- **Quote the record** - every factual assertion must cite a specific document or interlocutor
- **No inflation** - time entries must be truthful; never exaggerate hours
- **Candor to tribunal** - disclose adverse authority; represent holdings accurately
- **Protect privilege** - redact work product; generalise strategy-revealing descriptions
- **Anticipate opposition** - preempt rate, hours, staffing, block-billing, and limited-success objections
- **Deadline compliance** - typically 7 to 14 days from decree under Act of Sederunt; check local rules
- **Jurisdiction-specific** - Sheriff Court and Court of Session have different Tables of Fees; verify current versions
- **Mark uncertain citations** with [VERIFY]
- **No contingency fees** - speculative fee arrangements prohibited; no lodestar or multiplier
- **No punitive damages** - use aggravated damages where appropriate
- **Expenses follow success** - judicial expenses follow success; partial success adjustments may apply

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been converted from a US post-judgment fee-motion skill to Scottish civil procedure. Key differences:

- **Attorney→Expenses motion:** In Scotland, expenses in civil litigation are sought through a motion for decree for expenses, not a separate fee petition. The successful party is generally entitled to expenses.
- **Lodestar method→Judicial discretion:** The US lodestar method (reasonable hours × reasonable rate) has no direct Scottish equivalent. Scottish courts assess expense reasonableness under the Table of Fees and general judicial discretion, with the Auditor of the Court taxing accounts where disputed.
- **No enhancement/multiplier:** Scots law does not permit lodestar enhancements for contingent risk or exceptional results. Aggravated damages may be available in delict cases for bad conduct.
- **Contingency fees prohibited:** Speculative fee arrangements (pactum de quota litis) are prohibited in Scotland. Success fee agreements (CFAs) exist in limited forms.
- **No punitive damages:** Scots (and UK) law does not recognise punitive/exemplary damages as a general remedy.
- **Expenses follow success:** The "loser pays" principle applies; judicial expenses follow the outcome.
- **No FRCP 54(d)(2):** Scottish time limits are governed by Act of Sederunt (Sheriff Court Ordinary Cause Rules r. 28.7(3), Court of Session Rules Ch. 41).
- **No § 1988 fee-shifting:** UK statutory fee-shifting equivalents include Legal Aid (Scotland) Act 1986 and relevant costs provisions in specific statutes.
- **Citation style:** US Bluebook replaced with OSCOLA / Edinburgh Legal Style; US case law replaced with Scottish/UK equivalents.
- **Taxation of accounts:** In Scotland, disputed expense accounts are remitted to the Auditor of the Court for taxation (audit), not assessed by a judge.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
