Produces a legally defensible annual general meeting (AGM) notice that protects shareholder voting rights and supports validity of meeting actions, compliant with the Companies Act 2006 and applicable articles of association.

[SCOTS: Note] UK company law is governed by the Companies Act 2006, which applies throughout Great Britain. Scottish companies have the same legislative framework as English companies, though Scottish courts (Court of Session, Sheriff Court) handle any litigation. The Act uses "annual general meeting" (AGM) rather than "annual shareholders meeting" and is administered by Companies House.

## Gather Before Drafting

- Jurisdiction (registered in Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland); memorandum and articles of association, Legal name, share classes, voting rights per class, Meeting date/time/timezone, format (in-person / virtual / hybrid), venue or platform URL, Record date, confirm under s.360(2) Companies Act 2006 (48 hours before meeting); articles may require a longer period, Full agenda with proposed resolutions and board recommendations. Distinguish ordinary resolutions (simple majority) from special resolutions (75% majority)
- Quorum rules per articles (typically 2 shareholders present in person or by proxy)
- Proxy appointment, ss.324-331 Companies Act 2006; electronic proxy appointments permitted, Traded companies (UK premium/main market / AIM): additional FCA Disclosure Guidance and Transparency Rules (DTRs) apply, Authorised signatory (director or company secretary) and approved distribution method

## Core Workflow

### 1. Validate Compliance Inputs

For each item, apply the stricter of Companies Act vs. articles:

- **Notice period** - private company: 14 clear days (s.307). Public company: 21 clear days (s.307). Check articles for longer period.
- **Notice window** - calculate "clear days" (exclude day of meeting, exclude day of deemed receipt). Section 1147 CA 2006.
- **Record date** - s.360(2): shareholders on register 48 hours before meeting are eligible. Deemed 6pm on day before meeting.
- **Meeting access** - no ambiguity on venue, platform, or registration steps
- **Vote standards** - tie each proposal to its exact threshold (ordinary resolution = simple majority; special resolution = 75%).
- **Proxy rules** - s.324: members may appoint proxy of choice (not limited to other members). Deadline: articles set deadline; default is 48 hours before meeting. Electronic proxy appointments must be accepted (s.333A).

### 2. Draft Required Sections

1. Header: "NOTICE OF ANNUAL GENERAL MEETING"
2. Corporate identity and legal authority (articles + Companies Act 2006)
3. Meeting date, time, timezone, place or platform
4. Record-date statement and voting eligibility (s.360(2) CA 2006)
5. Agenda with short description of each item; distinguish ordinary from special business
6. Resolutions: ordinary vs. special, state which threshold applies
7. Quorum and voting requirements by resolution type
8. Proxy appointment and electronic proxy instructions
9. Signature block: date, director/secretary name, title, registered office address

### 3. Template

```text
NOTICE OF ANNUAL GENERAL MEETING
[Company Legal Name], a company registered in [Scotland/England & Wales] (No. [CRN])

To: Members of the Company whose names appear on the register of members as at [Record Date].

Notice is hereby given that the Annual General Meeting of the Company will be held on [Meeting Date], at [Time] [Timezone], at [Location / Virtual Platform].

Business to be transacted:

ORDINARY BUSINESS:
1. [Resolution, e.g. Receive and adopt annual accounts and reports]
2. [Resolution, e.g. Re-elect director]
3. [Resolution, e.g. Re-appoint auditor]

SPECIAL BUSINESS (if any):
4. [Special resolution, e.g. Amend articles, requires 75% majority]

Record Date: [Record Date] (48 hours before meeting)
Eligibility: Members registered as at 6.00 pm on [Record Date]
Quorum: [Per articles, typically 2 members]
Vote Requirements: Ordinary resolutions, simple majority; Special resolutions - 75% majority

Proxy Instructions:
- Appointment deadline: [Deadline, typically 48 hours before meeting]
- Delivery: [Hard copy / electronic via platform or email]
- Revocation: [Written notice to registered office / platform; must arrive before meeting]

[Registered Office]
[Signed: [Name], Director/Secretary]
[Date]
```

[SCOTS: Note] Template uses "registered office" (not principal place of business) and "Company Registration Number" format. All monetary thresholds should be in GBP (£).

### 4. Quality Gates

- Notice timing verified against s.307 CA 2006 AND articles, use stricter.
- Distribution method and proof-of-service documented.
- Proposal descriptions match actual resolutions, no "any other business" for substantive matters.
- Public-company DTR compliance confirmed before final output [VERIFY].
- For premium listed companies: FCA Listing Rules and DTRs checked.
- Articles of association reviewed for class rights and special quorum provisions.
- Electronic voting / virtual meeting procedures confirmed under articles.

## Pitfalls

- Never include unnoticed substantive business under "any other business."
- Do not conflate AGM notice periods (21 days public / 14 days private) with general meeting timing rules.
- For public companies, FCA Disclosure and Transparency Rules (DTRs) and UK Corporate Governance Code provisions override articles when they conflict.
- "Clear days" calculation: exclude day of meeting and day of deemed receipt (s.1147 CA 2006).
- Proxy appointments: members may appoint ANY person as proxy, restriction to another member is not permitted (s.324 CA 2006).
- Electronic communications: consent required; check articles for deemed consent provisions (s.333 et seq.).
- Include contingency language for venue/platform changes.

### Scottish-Specific Considerations

- Scottish companies are registered with Companies House under the Companies Act 2006 - same registration system as England & Wales, A Scottish company's registered office must be in Scotland, Court actions (if contested) would be in the Court of Session or Sheriff Court, The Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995 does not apply to notices (written form is sufficient)
- No notarisation required for notice, Scots law does not recognise the US concept of "corporate seal" as a requirement (abolished in 2006)
