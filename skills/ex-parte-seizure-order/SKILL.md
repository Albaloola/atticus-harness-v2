## Prerequisites

Collect before drafting:

- **Case caption** - court (Court of Session / Sheriff Court), division, case number, parties with procedural designations (pursuer/defender or claimant/respondent as appropriate)
- **Factual record** - affidavits, investigative reports, or financial records establishing urgency (dissipation risk, destruction, irreparable harm)
- **Property description** - physical descriptions, serial numbers, locations, valuations, or digital/IP asset identifiers
- **Legal basis** - Bankruptcy and Diligence etc. (Scotland) Act 2007 for diligence; common law for interim interdict; or specific statutory authority
- **Executing party** - Sheriff Officer, Messengers-at-Arms, or court-appointed receiver
- **Caution/surety** - security covering assets value plus potential damages for wrongful diligence

## Order Structure

### 1. Caption and Title

Standard case caption followed by "INTERIM DILIGENCE ORDER (WITHOUT NOTICE)" or "INTERIM INTERDICT (WITHOUT NOTICE)." [SCOTS: Use Scots court formatting, Court of Session Outer House or Sheriff Court]

### 2. Recitals

- Date/time of application; note no appearance by defender, Documents reviewed (motion, minute, affidavits, productions)
- Finding that intimation would defeat relief (specify dissipation/concealment risk)
- [SCOTS: Reference to relevant statutory powers, Bankruptcy and Diligence etc. (Scotland) Act 2007; Court of Session Act 1988 s.47 for interdict]

### 3. Findings of Fact, Standard for Without Notice Relief

Each factor requires a specific finding with record citations:

| Factor | Required Finding |
|--------|-----------------|
| Prima facie case | Credible averments and supporting evidence, cite Aff. ¶ ___, Production ___ |
| Irreparable prejudice | Delay causes dissipation/destruction/ongoing harm not compensable in damages |
| Inadequacy of other remedies | Ordinary process and diligence insufficient |
| Balance of convenience | Pursuer's prejudice from refusal exceeds defender's prejudice from grant |

[SCOTS: Scottish courts apply a balance of convenience test for interim interdict, similar to American Cyanamid. For diligence on the dependence, the court must be satisfied there is a genuine risk of asset dissipation or obstruction of enforcement.]

### 4. Diligence Provisions

**Arrestment on the dependence** [SCOTS: Equivalent to US attachment/freezing order]:
- Arrest funds, moveable property, or debts owed to defender in hands of third-party arrestee, Specific description of property and accounts to be arrested, Location(s) with address, account numbers, institution details, Digital/crypto assets: specify method of restraint

**Inhibition on the dependence** [SCOTS: Equivalent to US lis pendens]:
- Prohibit defender from selling, burdening, or otherwise dealing with heritable property, Specific property descriptions with title numbers and Land Register references, Register with Registers of Scotland Keeper of the Registers

**Executing parties** - identify authorised agents (Messengers-at-Arms for Court of Session; Sheriff Officers for Sheriff Court)

**Execution parameters:**
- Permissible hours, Entry conditions (peaceable; forced entry only if specified condition met)
- Inventory requirement: itemised list with photographic documentation, Minimise interference with non-seized operations

**Effective period** - specify start date/time through expiration date/event or further order of court.

### 5. Custody and Storage

- Custodian/facility for arrested items, Preservation conditions (perishable goods, digital forensic images)
- Access restricted to specified parties pending further order

### 6. Caution and Security

[SCOTS: No direct equivalent to US bond. Scottish courts may require caution (security for expenses/compensation) as a condition of granting without notice diligence.]

- Pursuer provides caution of £[AMOUNT] by [DATE/TIME] as condition of order taking effect, Form: consignation with the court, bond of caution by an insurance company, or letter of guarantee from a financial institution, Failure to provide caution: order automatically recalled/sistated

### 7. Post-Execution Intimation and Hearing

- Serve defender within 14 days of execution with copy of order, application, and all supporting papers
- [SCOTS: Defender may apply for recall or restriction of diligence under Bankruptcy and Diligence etc. (Scotland) Act 2007]
- Hearing on the by-order (or continued) no later than 14 days post-execution, Defender may move to recall or vary on showing of material change or wrongful diligence, Counter-caution option: defender provides security to release arrested assets pending final resolution

### 8. Prohibitions and Safeguards

- No unnecessary damage during execution, No diligence outside described categories, No review of seized materials by pursuer/agents until further order unless expressly authorised

### 9. Signature Block

Standard judicial signature block with date, judge/sheriff name, title, and court.

## Pitfalls and Checks

- **Property specificity** - vague descriptions are grounds for recall; err toward maximum detail
- **Diligence on the dependence vs. interim interdict** - distinct legal bases; confirm which applies. Diligence preserves assets; interdict prohibits action.
- **Caution adequacy** - must reflect realistic value of all arrested property plus potential damages from wrongful diligence; underestimating creates liability for expenses
- **14-day hearing** - due process floor under ECHR Article 6; never omit scheduling
- **Digital assets** - include specific forensic preservation protocol; generic language is routinely challenged
- **Sheriff Court vs. Court of Session** - verify applicable rules (Ordinary Cause Rules or Rules of the Court of Session)
- **No intimation to defender** - application must not be disclosed to opposing party before entry
- **Senior review** - required before lodging; due process errors risk sanction of expenses
- **[SCOTS: ECHR compatibility]** - Article 6 and Article 1 Protocol 1 (peaceful enjoyment of possessions) require proportionality and robust safeguards
