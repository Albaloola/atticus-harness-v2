# UK Securities Offering & Financial Promotion, Guidance Note (Scotland)

## Adapting US Merit Review Analysis to UK Law

### Key Differences: No "Blue Sky" Merit Review in the UK

The concept of **state-level merit review** does not exist in the UK. Securities regulation is entirely **centralised** under the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (FSMA).

| US Concept | UK Equivalent |
|---|---|
| SEC (SEC) | FCA (Financial Conduct Authority) |
| State securities regulator (Blue Sky) | No state-level equivalent, single UK regime |
| NASAA / Merit Review | No equivalent, conduct-of-business rules via FCA Handbook |
| Covered securities (fed pre-emption) | Not applicable, all securities regulation is national |
| Form 1-A (Reg A) | Prospectus regime under FSMA / UK Prospectus Regulation |
| Rule 506(b)/(c) | Article 4 exemption of UK Prospectus Regulation |
| SEC comment letters | FCA review of prospectus (regulated by PRR / prospectus regulation rules) |

### UK Securities Offering Regime (FSMA 2000)

**General prohibition** (s.19): No person may carry on a regulated activity unless authorised or exempt.
**Restricted activities** include dealing in investments, arranging deals, managing investments, advising on investments.

The UK Prospectus Regulation (UK PR, onshored EU Prospectus Regulation) requires a prospectus for:
- Shares admitted to trading on a regulated market, Offers to the public exceeding €8 million (approx. £7 million) in 12 months

**Exemptions from prospectus requirement:**
- Offers to qualified investors only, Offers to fewer than 150 persons per EEA state, Offers with minimum consideration of €100,000 per investor, Offers with total consideration under €8 million (12-month rolling)
- Employee share schemes, Takeover / merger exempt offers

### Financial Promotion (FSMA s.21)

The equivalent of US securities marketing regulation:

- No person may communicate a financial promotion unless:
  1. The person is FCA-authorised; or
  2. The promotion is approved by an FCA-authorised person; or
  3. An exemption in the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (Financial Promotion) Order 2005 (SI 2005/1529) applies

**Key exemptions (similar to US private placement exemptions):**
- Investment professionals (Art. 19)
- High net worth companies, unincorporated associations, etc. (Art. 49)
- High net worth individuals (Art. 48) - £100,000+ income or £250,000+ net assets, Sophisticated investors (Art. 50, 50A)
- Self-certified sophisticated investors, Certified high net worth individuals, Restricted investor schemes (Art. 20A)

### Cap Table / Cheap Stock Equivalent

No direct NASAA-style cheap stock analysis. However:
- **FCA connected party rules**: Related-party transactions require disclosure (LR 11 / MAR)
- **Pre-emption rights**: Shareholders have statutory pre-emption rights under Companies Act 2006 s.561 (may be disapplied by special resolution)
- **Director dealings**: Must be disclosed under MAR (Market Abuse Regulation)
- **Substantial shareholding disclosures**: Must be notified if reaching 3%, 5%, 10% thresholds

### Promoter Equity / Voting Rights

- No merit review equivalent of "promoter equity" analysis, UK takes a disclosure-based approach:
  - Prospectus must disclose related-party transactions and directors' interests
  - Corporate governance code (comply or explain) addresses board independence
  - Takeover Code regulates control transactions
  - Dual-class shares are rare and limited to premium/standard listing segments

### Key UK/Scotland Statutes

- Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (FSMA 2000)
- UK Prospectus Regulation (EU) 2017/1129 as onshored, FCA Handbook (Prospectus Regulation Rules, PRR)
- Market Abuse Regulation (UK MAR)
- Companies Act 2006
- Financial Promotion Order 2005 (SI 2005/1529)
- Financial Services Act 2021 (new financial promotion gateway for unauthorised persons)

### Practitioner Note

The closest UK equivalent to US limited offering / direct public offering compliance is the **FCA financial promotion regime** combined with **Prospectus Regulation exemptions**. There is no merit-review style pre-clearance mechanism. Instead, issuers rely on properly structured exemptions from the prospectus requirement and/or financial promotion restriction.

For Scottish-incorporated companies, company law (Companies Act 2006) applies identically to rest of UK, but **Scottish courts** (Court of Session / Sheriff Court) would have jurisdiction for any disputes.
