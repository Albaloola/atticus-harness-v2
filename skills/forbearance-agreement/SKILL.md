## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

1. **Loan documents** - loan agreement, standard security / bond and floating charge, cautioner undertakings, prior amendments
2. **Loan details** - principal balance, interest rate, payment history, maturity date
3. **Default information** - nature of defaults, missed payment dates/amounts, covenant violations
4. **Security subjects / secured property** - type, location (including Land Register title number), current valuation
5. **Parties** - full legal names, entity types, place of incorporation / registered office, cautioners
6. **Forbearance terms** - desired duration, proposed payment structure, lender conditions
7. **Amendment requirements** - original loan doc provisions governing modifications; check Standard Security for calling-up / default notice provisions

## Core Drafting Workflow

### 1. Recitals

Include all of:
- Original loan reference (date, principal, balance, rate, maturity)
- Security / security subjects description establishing lender's secured position (Standard Security title number, Land Register details, floating charge registration number at Companies House)
- Specific default description (dates, amounts, covenant violations) - professional tone, Borrower requested forbearance; lender agrees in sole discretion without obligation
- **Borrower acknowledgment**: debt validity, balance accuracy, no set-offs or counterclaims (subject to Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977 enforceability)
- Recitals are contractual and part of the operative agreement

### 2. Forbearance Period Terms

Define with precision:
- **Dates** - commencement and termination (or duration with date-certain auto-termination)
- **Scope** - which obligations are suspended, reduced, or modified
- **Payment** - exact amount, due dates; if suspended, specify interest-only or reduced P&I
- **Deferred amounts** - re-amortised into principal, balloon payment, maturity extension, or other
- **Interest accrual** - whether interest continues; applicable rate; treatment of accrued unpaid interest (capitalised, deferred, or paid currently)
- **Completion outcome** - return to original schedule, permanent modification, or other
- **Explicit statement** - forbearance is temporary, not permanent modification unless expressly stated

### 3. Conditions Precedent & Ongoing Covenants

**Initial conditions:** execution and delivery, payment of fees/expenses, current financials, insurance evidence on secured property, additional lender-required documentation (e.g., updated Land Register entries, floating charge amendment).

**Ongoing obligations:**
- Timely payment per forbearance schedule - **no grace period, no cure right** (unless expressly agreed)
- Maintain insurance (lender as loss payee / noted on policy)
- Pay non-domestic rates / council tax on heritable property, Comply with laws; preserve condition of security subjects, Periodic financial reporting (specify frequency, format, deadline)
- Maintain legal existence; operate in ordinary course, No asset disposals or additional debt without consent, Good faith workout negotiation (if applicable)

**Breach = immediate default**, terminates forbearance, restores all enforcement rights. Specify whether notice/cure applies or auto-termination for certain breaches.

### 4. Lender Reservation of Rights

Lender's commitment is **narrow**: refrain from specified remedies (calling-up / enforcement under Standard Security, winding-up, administration) only while borrower is in strict compliance.

Preserve explicitly:
- Covers only **pre-existing identified defaults** - new defaults enforceable immediately, One-time accommodation; no future forbearance obligation, No waiver except as expressly stated, Right to accrue interest at contract/default rate, Right to assess late fees/default charges post-forbearance, Right to credit reporting, collateral preservation (valuations, inspections)
- Absolute discretion to refuse future accommodations, Full enforcement upon expiry/termination, Right to register enforcement steps (e.g., calling-up notice, inhibition) if required

### 5. Events of Default & Termination

**Triggers:**
- Missed forbearance payment (no grace period)
- Breach of any forbearance covenant/condition, New default under original loan documents, Material misrepresentation, Sequestration / administration / liquidation (voluntary or involuntary)
- Trust Deed for Creditors or company voluntary arrangement (CVA)
- Attachment / arrestment / inhibition against security subjects, Material adverse change in financial condition, Unauthorised transfer of security subjects or ownership interests

**Consequences:**
- Entire balance immediately due without further notice/demand, All remedies available (enforcement of Standard Security via calling-up / default procedure; winding-up; appointment of administrator / administrative receiver)
- Borrower liable for enforcement costs including judicial expenses, Forbearance payments applied per original loan terms, No credit or benefit for forbearance participation

### 6. Release, Waiver & Reaffirmation

- **Release** - borrower releases lender and affiliates from all claims (known/unknown) arising from loan, security subjects, or lender conduct; knowing and voluntary with independent legal advice acknowledgment
- **Waivers** - defences to enforcement (subject to Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977 and reasonableness); counterclaims/set-offs; **no jury trial waiver needed** (jury trial not available in Scottish civil commercial cases)
- **Reaffirmation** - all obligations and security interests remain in full force; forbearance is not novation or discharge

### 7. General Provisions

| Provision | Key Points |
|---|---|
| Governing law | **Scots law**; exclusive jurisdiction of the **Court of Session, Edinburgh**, or the **Sheriff Court** depending on amount (Court of Session for claims over £100,000); no conflicts-of-law |
| Jurisdiction | Exclusive forum in Scotland; lender may also enforce where security subjects are located |
| Judicial expenses | Preveiling party; reasonable expenses (Scots law term); taxed if not agreed |
| Integration | Agreement + loan documents = entire agreement |
| Amendment | Written and signed only; no oral modifications |
| Conflict | Forbearance agreement controls where conflicting |
| Notices | Addresses, delivery methods, deemed-given timing |
| Counterparts | Electronic / PDF signatures effective (Scots law generally does not require notarisation for commercial loan documents; only registration in Land Register may require notarial execution) |
| Assignment | Binding on successors; borrower may not assign without consent |
| Time of essence | Strict and timely performance is material, expressly recognised in Scots law |

### 8. Execution

- Signature blocks with printed name, title, date; entity language ("Duly Authorised" / "Duly Signed for and on behalf of")
- Check original docs for modification formalities (board resolutions, constitutional consents)
- **Notarisation** - not required for most commercial loan documents under Scots law; required only for deeds registered in the Land Register (advance notices / Standard Securities)
- **Cautioner consent**: if cautioners exist, include consent/reaffirmation with separate signature blocks

## Pitfalls & Checks (Scotland/UK)

- Review original loan amendment and variation provisions first, they dictate required formalities
- **Standard Security enforcement**: the calling-up procedure under C(FRS)A 1970 requires a 2-month calling-up notice (Form A) before enforcement; a notice of default (Form B) gives 21 days for non-monetary defaults. Forbearance temporarily suspends this procedure, Scope forbearance precisely to identified pre-existing defaults only, Never imply permanent modification unless expressly intended, All borrower acknowledgments (debt validity, balance accuracy) must be explicit and unambiguous
- **Draft for litigation** in the Court of Session or Sheriff Court, assume forbearance will fail and a court will review every provision
- **Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977**: broad releases and waivers must satisfy the reasonableness test to be enforceable in non-consumer contracts
- **Consumer credit**: if the borrower is an individual, the Consumer Credit Act 1974 applies (s. 87 requires default notice, s. 129 time orders). This agreement is designed for **commercial lending only**; separate advice needed for regulated consumer credit, Include table of contents if document exceeds 10 pages, Attach exhibits: note copy / loan agreement extract, forbearance payment schedule, current redemption / payoff statement, Target length: 8 to 15 pages depending on complexity

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Adapted for:** Scotland and UK law

**Changes made:**
- Mortgage/Deed of Trust → **Standard Security** (Conveyancing and Feudal Reform (Scotland) Act 1970)
- Real Property → **Heritable property**
- Personal Property → **Moveable property**
- Governing law → **Scots law**
- Court system → **Court of Session** (Edinburgh) / **Sheriff Court** depending on claim value, UCC (Uniform Commercial Code) → **No direct equivalent** - Scots common law on pledge, assignation, and security, TILA (Truth in Lending Act) → **Consumer Credit Act 1974** / **Consumer Rights Act 2015**
- UDAP (Unfair/Deceptive Acts and Practices) → **Consumer Protection from Unfair Trading Regulations 2008** / **Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977**
- IRS → **HMRC**
- SEC → **FCA** (Financial Conduct Authority) / **PRA** (Prudential Regulation Authority)
- "Recorded" → **Registered in the Land Register**
- "Foreclosure" → **Enforcement of standard security** / **repossession proceedings**
- "Bankruptcy" → **Sequestration** (Scottish personal insolvency)
- "Receiver" → **Administrator** or **Administrative Receiver** (Insolvency Act 1986)
- "Guarantor" → **Cautioner** (Scots law term)
- "Security documents" → **Standard Security, Bond and Floating Charge**
- "Deed of Trust" → **Trust Deed for Creditors** (voluntary arrangement) or **Standard Security**
- "Attorneys' fees" → **Expenses of enforcement** / **judicial expenses**
- "Personal jurisdiction" → **Jurisdiction based on domicile** (post-Brexit common law / retained EU rules)
- "Usury laws" → **Consumer Credit Act 1974** / **FCA regulation** - Scotland has no general usury law
- **No jury trial waiver** needed, jury trial not available in Scottish civil commercial cases (different from US)
- **Notarisation** not required for most commercial loan documents; only for Land Register registration, Amounts converted from USD to **GBP (£)**

**Key Scottish/UK considerations:**
- The calling-up procedure under the Conveyancing and Feudal Reform (Scotland) Act 1970 is fundamental, a Standard Security cannot be enforced without the correct notice period (2 months for calling-up; 21 days for default notice)
- Floating charges register at Companies House; Standard Securities register at the Land Register of Scotland, The Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977 imposes a reasonableness test on exclusion/limitation clauses in non-consumer contracts, waivers and releases must be drafted to survive UCTA scrutiny, For Scottish heritable property secured by Standard Security, the borrower retains ownership; the lender does not hold title (unlike US mortgage systems). Enforcement requires court application or Form notice procedure, Guarantors must use the term "Cautioner" and Scots cautionary obligations law applies, Company borrower insolvency is governed by the Insolvency Act 1986; personal insolvency by the Bankruptcy (Scotland) Act 2016
- The FCA regulates UK lending; commercial loans to limited companies are typically unregulated consumer credit, but caution is needed if the borrower is a partnership, sole trader, or individual, Late payment interest may be limited by the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998
- See `scots-forms/README.md` for detailed form and statute references
