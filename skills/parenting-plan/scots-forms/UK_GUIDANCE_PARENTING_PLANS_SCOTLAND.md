# UK Guidance: Parenting Plans in Scotland

## Key Legislation

### Children (Scotland) Act 1995

The primary legislation governing parental responsibilities and rights (PRRs).

**Section 11 orders** - the court can make orders relating to:
- Who a child lives with (residence order)
- Contact between a child and another person (contact order)
- Specific issue orders / interdicts

**Parental Responsibilities and Rights (PRRs)**:
- Responsibility to safeguard and promote the child's health, development, and welfare, Responsibility to provide direction and guidance, If child not living with parent: maintain personal relations and direct contact regularly, Right to have the child living with the parent, Right to maintain personal relations and direct contact, Right to act as the child's legal representative

### Children (Scotland) Act 2020

Recent reforms that emphasise:
- Child's best interests as the paramount consideration, Child's right to express their views (UNCRC compliance)
- Consideration of abuse (domestic abuse) and impact on contact, Child welfare reporters and child welfare hearings

### "No Order" Presumption

The court shall not make a Section 11 order unless it would be better for the child than making no order at all.

### Parental Responsibilities and Rights (PRRs)

| US Equivalent | Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Custody (legal) | Parental Responsibilities and Rights |
| Custody (physical) | Residence |
| Visitation / Parenting time | Contact |
| Parental authority | Parental Responsibilities and Rights |
| Decision-making authority | Section 11 order / PRRs |

### The Scottish Terminology Shift

Recent Scottish Government policy recommends moving away from "residence" and "contact" labels toward more neutral terms like "with whom the child is to live" and "maintaining personal relations" - a shift similar to but different from the US transition from "custody" to "parenting time."

## Scottish Courts for Family Cases

- **Sheriff Court** - main family court; most cases
- **Court of Session (Outer House)** - complex cases, appeals

## Child Support

- **Child Maintenance Service (CMS)** - UK body that calculates and enforces child maintenance
- **Scottish Child Maintenance** - some distinct rules post-devolution
- **Formula**: Based on gross income, number of children, and number of overnight stays
- **Voluntary arrangement** - parents can agree privately (must be registered or independently enforceable)

## Key Differences to Flag

1. **Terminology**: "custody/visitation" is not used in Scottish law. Use "residence" and "contact" or, following the 2020 Act, "with whom the child lives" and "maintaining personal relations"
2. **No FERPA**: Scottish educational records access governed by Data Protection Act 2018 and Education (Scotland) Act
3. **No parenting plan statutes**: Unlike many US states, Scotland has no statutory parenting plan form or required content. Plans are private agreements.
4. **Holidays**: Replace US holidays (MLK, Presidents' Day, July 4th, Thanksgiving) with UK holidays
5. **Child welfare hearing**: Fast-track process in Sheriff Court for urgent residence/contact disputes
6. **Child Welfare Reporter**: Independent reporter who investigates and reports to court, specific to Scotland

Sources: Children (Scotland) Act 1995; Children (Scotland) Act 2020; CM Options (Child Maintenance Service); Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service, family law guidance.
