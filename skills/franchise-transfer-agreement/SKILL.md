Produces a tripartite agreement for the orderly transfer of franchise rights from an existing franchisee (transferor) to a qualified successor (transferee) with franchisor consent, in compliance with the original franchise agreement and applicable UK/Scots law. **This version applies to Scotland and the United Kingdom.**

## Checkpoint A: Pre-Draft Intake (Mandatory) [SCOTS]

Gather before drafting unless the user says "use defaults" or "just draft." Replace US FDD and franchise registration with UK equivalents.

1. **Original franchise agreement** - execution date, parties, all amendments and exhibits; governed by Scots or English law
2. **[SCOTS] BFA Code of Ethics compliance** - British Franchise Association (BFA) membership status; code compliance; no mandatory FDD disclosure regime in UK
3. **Transfer correspondence** - franchisor's preliminary approval or qualification requirements
4. **Financial statements** - transferor (3-5 years, prepared under UK GAAP or FRS 102) and transferee (qualification evidence)
5. **Premises lease** - assignment provisions, landlord consent; note Scots law on commercial leases (implied hypothec for rent arrears)
6. **Asset inventory** - equipment, vehicles, IP, POS/tech systems, signage
7. **Compliance history** - royalty payment records, default/cure history, outstanding disputes
8. **Transaction structure** - asset sale vs. equity sale of franchise-owning entity
9. **Jurisdiction** - franchise location (Scotland/England/Wales/NI); Scots law if located in Scotland
10. **[SCOTS] VAT registration** - VAT status of all parties; VAT implications of asset vs. share sale
11. **[SCOTS] HMRC considerations** - Capital gains tax; business asset disposal relief (formerly entrepreneurs' relief); stamp duty (on share transfers only)

## Step 1: Liability & Asset Allocation

### Assets Transferred

[SCOTS: Note, UK asset sales often use a detailed asset schedule. Scots law distinguishes between heritable (land/buildings) and moveable property. Intellectual property assignment requires writing in Scots law.]

| Category | Inclusions | Common Exclusions |
|---|---|---|
| Tangible | Equipment, fixtures, inventory, vehicles, POS/IT, signage | Transferor's personal property |
| Intangible | Franchise licence, phone numbers, domains, social media, customer lists, goodwill | Corporate records unrelated to franchise ops |
| Heritable | Leasehold premises (if transferring) | Separate property |
| Excluded | List specifically in schedule | Assets subject to separate disposition |

### Liability Split

| Party | Assumes |
|---|---|
| Transferee | All franchise obligations from closing forward (royalties, ad fund, standards, lease) |
| Transferor | Pre-closing vendor invoices, tax liabilities, employee/wage claims, pending litigation |
| Straddling | Pre-closing incidents reported post-closing stay with transferor; partial services prorated |

## Step 2: Purchase Price & Financial Terms

- Total consideration with allocation by category (tangible property, covenant not to compete, goodwill, franchise rights)
- Payment schedule: deposit at execution, balance at closing, seller-financing/earnout if applicable
- **Franchisor transfer fee**: exact amount or calculation per original agreement; allocate between parties; state timing
- **[SCOTS] VAT**: Consideration may be VAT-exclusive or inclusive; VAT treatment of goodwill, transfer of going concern (TOGC) - seek accountant advice; flag HMRC treatment, Working capital: minimum post-closing cash, A/R and A/P treatment, inventory adjustment from pre-closing physical count
- **[SCOTS] Tax**: No UK equivalent to IRS Form 8594; allocation influences capital gains vs. income tax treatment; HMRC may challenge allocation, flag for accountant review. Business asset disposal relief may be available.

## Step 3: Conditions Precedent

| Condition | Key Details |
|---|---|
| Franchisor written approval | Complete application (financials, background, experience, conflict check); franchisor evaluates per franchise agreement standard; BFA Code requires good faith |
| Training completion | Franchisor's programme (1-6 weeks); may include testing or supervised operation |
| New franchise agreement | Transferee signs franchisor's then-current form (may differ: higher royalties, shorter renewal) |
| Lease assignment or new lease | Landlord consent; address whether transferor released from personal guarantee; Scots commercial lease assignation rules apply |
| Default cure | Transferor cures all defaults; location brought to current brand standards at transferor's cost |
| Lender consent | If assets encumbered by security interests (Scots floating charge or standard security) |
| Transferee due diligence | Access to location, financial records, employees, system inspection; property searches (if heritable) |

## Step 4: Representations & Warranties

**Transferor reps:**
- Financial statements accurate, consistent basis (UK GAAP/FRS 102 unless stated otherwise), no undisclosed liabilities, Compliance with all franchise obligations (royalties, ad fund, standards, insurance)
- Assets in good operating condition; clear title free of charges except as scheduled, No material adverse change since most recent financials, No pending/threatened litigation or regulatory proceedings
- [SCOTS] Title to heritable property (if transferring) confirmed by Registers of Scotland search

**Transferee reps:**
- Financial capacity to close and fund operations, Receipt and review of franchise agreement, BFA documentation, and assumed contracts, Opportunity to consult legal and financial advisors, Qualifications and experience; full-time devotion if required, Acknowledgment franchisor makes no financial performance guarantees

## Step 5: Restrictive Covenants

[SCOTS: Note, UK restraint of trade doctrine applies. Non-compete clauses are enforceable only if they protect a legitimate business interest and are reasonable in scope, duration, and geography. Scotland follows the same common law doctrine. There is no equivalent to California's blanket voiding of non-competes, but the UK government has proposed limiting non-compete clauses to 3 months.]

| Covenant | Scope | Duration | Geography |
|---|---|---|---|
| Non-compete | No direct/indirect ownership or operation of competing business | [SCOTS] Typically 6 to 12 months (UK courts rarely uphold 2 to 3 years except in exceptional circumstances) | Territory + reasonable buffer |
| Non-solicitation (employees) | No soliciting or hiring franchise employees | 6 to 12 months | System-wide |
| Non-solicitation (customers) | No soliciting customers or interfering with vendor relationships | 6 to 12 months | Location-specific |
| Confidentiality | Ops manuals, supplier arrangements, trade secrets | Indefinite or until enters public domain | N/A |

- Extend to transferor's affiliates, family, controlled entities, Carve out: passive ownership of publicly traded securities below threshold, Require return/destruction of all confidential materials at closing
- [SCOTS: Note, UK Government consultation (2023) on limiting non-compete clauses to 3 months. Monitor legislative developments.]
- [SCOTS: Note, Scottish courts will not enforce a covenant that goes beyond what is reasonably necessary to protect the franchisor's legitimate business interests. Blue-pencil severance may be applied to unreasonable restrictions, but not rewriting.]

## Step 6: Closing Mechanics

### Deliverables

| Party | Deliverables |
|---|---|
| Transferor | Bills of sale (Scots law: written assignation of intangibles); assignment/assumption agreements (contracts, leases); IP assignment (domains, social media, phones); vehicle registration documents; HMRC clearance certificates (if applicable); discharge of floating charges/standard securities; closing certificate |
| Transferee | Purchase price (bank transfer); executed assumption agreements; executed new franchise agreement; evidence of insurance (franchisor and transferor as additional insureds); personal guarantees if required |
| Franchisor | Written consent to transfer; acknowledgment of transferee as new franchisee; confirmation transferor's obligations satisfied |

### Adjustments & Transition

- **Prorations**: rent, utilities, property taxes (non-domestic rates in Scotland), insurance, prepaid ad contributions
- **Inventory**: physical count pre-closing; price adjusted to cost basis
- **Risk of loss**: transferor bears casualty risk through closing; transferee may terminate if material damage
- **Transition assistance** (30-90 days): transferor provides operational training, customer/supplier introductions, cooperation on licence/permit transfers; specify if compensated separately or included in price

## Step 7: Indemnification

[SCOTS: Note, Scots law does not use the term "indemnification" in the same broad US sense; indemnity clauses are contractual and strictly construed. Scots law of warranty and misrepresentation (including the Law Reform (Miscellaneous Provisions) (Scotland) Act 1985) provides alternative remedies.]

| Indemnitor | Covers |
|---|---|
| Transferor | Pre-closing liabilities; rep/warranty breaches; excluded liabilities; pre-closing third-party claims |
| Transferee | Post-closing liabilities; assumed obligations; rep/warranty breaches; post-closing third-party claims |

| Parameter | Terms |
|---|---|
| Notice | Prompt written notice with description, estimated amount, supporting docs |
| Defence | Indemnifying party controls; consent required for non-monetary settlements [SCOTS: Note, Scots procedure for third-party notices differs from US] |
| Survival | General reps: 1 year; tax and title: 3 years [SCOTS: Note, Prescription and limitation periods under Scots law (Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973) affect survival periods] |
| Basket | 1-3% of purchase price (specify deductible vs. threshold) |
| Cap | 25-50% of purchase price; excludes fraud, wilful misconduct, fundamental reps |

## Step 8: Dispute Resolution & General Provisions

- **[SCOTS] Governing law**: Scots law for franchises operating in Scotland; English law for E&W-based franchises. Note that Scots law (Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977) may restrict reliance on exclusion/limitation clauses
- **[SCOTS] Dispute resolution**: (1) executive negotiation, (2) mediation (30-60 days, shared costs), (3) Commercial Action (Court of Session, Edinburgh) or Sheriff Court depending on value
- **[SCOTS] No prevailing party attorneys' fees** (no equivalent to US fee-shifting); each party bears own costs unless contractually agreed, Integration clause; written amendment only; assignment restricted; notices to all three parties
- [SCOTS] Signature blocks: Scots law does not require notarisation for private company signatures; holograph writings valid (Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995)
- Signature blocks for all three parties with authority documentation

## Guidelines [SCOTS]

- **[SCOTS] No mandatory FDD regime**: The UK has no equivalent to the FTC Franchise Rule. The British Franchise Association (BFA) publishes a voluntary Code of Ethics; some franchisors provide a disclosure document but there is no statutory requirement.
- **[SCOTS] BFA Code of Ethics**: If the franchisor is a BFA member, check compliance with BFA Code requirements for transfer approvals, good faith dealing, and disclosure.
- **[SCOTS] No franchise registration states**: There are no state-level franchise registration requirements in the UK.
- **[SCOTS] Lease assignment (Scots law)**: Commercial leases in Scotland often include an obligation on the landlord not unreasonably to withhold consent to assignation (assignation is the Scots term). Check whether personal guarantees continue or require re-grant.
- **[SCOTS] Restraint of trade**: UK courts apply the common law restraint of trade doctrine strictly; non-compete periods beyond 6 to 12 months are difficult to justify for franchises.
- **[SCOTS] Tax**: No Form 8594 equivalent. HMRC focuses on substance over form. Business asset disposal relief may reduce CGT rate on qualifying disposals. VAT on transfer of a going concern (TOGC) is outside scope if conditions met.
- **Equity vs. asset sale**: Equity transfers may require different consent provisions, confirm structure early.
- **Do not** warrant franchisor will approve the transfer.
