Drafts a registrable environmental real burden or planning obligation restricting land use to protect public health following contamination, remediation, or regulatory settlement in Scotland. US-style environmental easements do not exist in Scots law.

## Prerequisites

1. **Property records** - legal description, Title Sheet / folio number, Land Register or Sasine details, title deeds
2. **Environmental reports** - Phase I/II site assessments, remediation action plans, site investigation reports (in Scottish context)
3. **Regulatory instruments** - Part 2A remediation notices, voluntary remediation agreements with SEPA, planning conditions under s.75 Town and Country Planning (Scotland) Act 1997, SEPA correspondence
4. **Parties** - disponer (property owner with title to grant real burdens) and beneficiary (SEPA, local authority, or Scottish Ministers as holder with statutory authority)
5. **Contamination specifics** - hazardous substances, remedial measures completed, ongoing monitoring, engineered controls

### 2. Recitals

- Legal description with Title Sheet / folio number and Land Register details, Nature and extent of contamination, Remediation summary and ongoing obligations, Triggering regulatory action (Part 2A remediation notice, s.75 planning condition, SEPA voluntary agreement, PPC permit)
- Public health purpose and statutory authority (Part 2A Environmental Protection Act 1990, Title Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003, Town and Country Planning (Scotland) Act 1997)

### 3. Definitions

Define: Property, Restricted Activities, Contamination, Remedial Action, Institutional Controls, Environmental Agency (SEPA / Local Authority / Scottish Ministers), and site-specific terms. Align with Title Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003 definitions and underlying regulatory documents.

### 4. Grant of Covenant and Easement

**Prohibited Activities** (enumerate with specificity):
- Residential use or occupancy, Potable groundwater well installation, Excavation or soil disturbance below [specified depth] without agency approval, Activities disturbing remedial systems, monitoring wells, or engineered caps/barriers, Any use increasing exposure to contaminated media

**Affirmative Obligations:**
- Maintain caps and engineered barriers, Conduct groundwater monitoring per agency schedule, Submit annual compliance reports, Provide agency inspection access, Notify agency before transfer or use change

**Real burden clause** - burdens the property and binds the disponer and successors as owners (s.1, Title Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003). Must relate to the property (s.3) and be for the benefit of the beneficiary or the public interest.

**Servitude of access** - beneficiary access to inspect, monitor, maintain remedial systems, and enforce terms. Note: servitudes are accessory to dominant tenement; an environmental body without neighbouring title may need statutory access powers instead.

### 5. Duration and Termination

- **Perpetual or fixed term** - real burdens may be time-limited. Perpetual unless otherwise specified
- **Modification/Discharge**: s.98 Title Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003 - application to the Lands Tribunal for Scotland for variation or discharge of real burdens
- **Termination path**: owner petition → evidence of remediation to unrestricted-use standards → beneficiary consent or Lands Tribunal order
- **No modification or release without beneficiary written consent or Lands Tribunal determination**
- **Ranking**: real burdens rank by date of registration; existing standard securities must rank prior or be postponed
- **Note**: Unlike US covenants, Scottish real burdens cannot be unilateral terminated by the beneficiary alone, statutory procedure applies

### 6. Enforcement and Remedies

- Beneficiary right of entry for inspection, monitoring, verification (may require statutory access powers where servitude is not feasible)
- Violations enforceable via interdict (Scots equivalent of injunction), specific implement, civil penalties, cost recovery, Beneficiary may carry out corrective works on owner default; costs recoverable as a debt, Preserves independent enforcement under Part 2A Environmental Protection Act 1990 or PPC regulations, Third-party rights may be created under the Contracts (Third Party Rights) (Scotland) Act 1999 where needed

### 7. Miscellaneous

- **Notices** - recorded delivery; addresses for all parties
- **Governing law** - Scots law (where property is in Scotland)
- **Severability** and **amendment** (written consent of all parties + where applicable, Lands Tribunal consent)
- **Registration** - disponer must register in the Land Register of Scotland (or Register of Sasines for older title) and provide confirmation of registration to the beneficiary
- **Keeper of the Registers of Scotland** - application for registration in the Land Register; registration required for real burden to be created and have real effect

### 8. Execution Block

- Signature lines (name, title, date) for all parties, Beneficiary acceptance block
- **Witnessing**: Scottish deeds require one witness for each granter (no notary required for most deeds; notarial execution where grantor is unable to sign)
- **Testing clause**: place, date, witness details per Scottish conveyancing practice, Registration statement for Land Register application

## Pitfalls and Checks

- [VERIFY] US-style environmental covenants do not exist in Scots law, use real burdens under Title Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003 or s.75 planning obligations
- [VERIFY] Real burden must satisfy the conditions in s.3 of the Title Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003: must relate to the burdened property, must be for the benefit of (a) the beneficiary's property or (b) the public interest
- [VERIFY] SEPA's statutory powers under Part 2A EPA 1990 - whether a real burden is required or SEPA can rely on statutory controls alone
- [VERIFY] PPC (Scotland) Regulations 2012 permit conditions may already cover many institutional controls, Obtain ranking agreements from existing heritable creditors before registration, Failure to register in the Land Register defeats real effect, confirm Keeper's registration requirements
- **Never omit**: real burden clause satisfying s.3 TC(S)A 2003, registration in the Land Register, and statutory access powers where servitude is not feasible
- **Modification**: Unlike US UECA, Scottish real burdens are subject to Lands Tribunal for Scotland jurisdiction under s.98 TC(S)A 2003 - draft to anticipate potential modification applications, Flag all site-specific depth thresholds, monitoring frequencies, and restricted activity lists for solicitor and agency review

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

### Key Structural Differences

Scots property law is fundamentally different from US/common law property. Key distinctions:

1. **No environmental covenants/easements**: US-style recordable environmental covenants (UECA) and common law environmental easements do not exist in Scotland
2. **Use real burdens instead**: Created under the Title Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003, real burdens are the closest equivalent, they run with the land and bind successors
3. **Planning obligations (s.75)**: Under the Town and Country Planning (Scotland) Act 1997, planning authorities can enter into agreements imposing land-use restrictions as an alternative to real burdens
4. **Servitudes vs. access easements**: Servitudes under Scots law require a dominant and servient tenement. An agency without neighbouring property may need statutory access powers rather than a servitude

### Statutory Framework (Scotland)

| US Framework | Scotland/UK Equivalent |
|---|---|
| UECA (Uniform Environmental Covenants Act) | Title Conditions (Scotland) Act 2003 - real burdens |
| CERCLA | Part 2A Environmental Protection Act 1990 (contaminated land regime) |
| RCRA | Pollution Prevention and Control (Scotland) Regulations 2012 |
| EPA | SEPA (Scottish Environment Protection Agency) / Local Authority / Scottish Ministers |
| County land records | Land Register of Scotland / Register of Sasines |
| Run-with-the-land | Real burden (s.1, TC(S)A 2003) |
| Grantor | Disponer / Property Owner |
| Grantee | SEPA / Local Authority / Scottish Ministers |
| Injunction | Interdict |
| Modification / Termination | s.98 Lands Tribunal for Scotland (not unilateral agency release) |

### Registration Requirements

- Real burdens are created by registration in the Land Register of Scotland (or Register of Sasines for older titles)
- The Keeper of the Registers of Scotland administers registration, Registration must be in the prescribed format with a plan where necessary, Real burdens rank by date of registration; existing standard securities must be postponed or ranked, Once registered, the burden appears on the Title Sheet for the burdened property

### Practical Considerations for Scottish Practitioners

- SEPA will usually prefer statutory controls (Part 2A remediation notices, PPC permit conditions) over real burdens where available, Real burdens should only be used where the restriction needs to bind successive owners independently of the regulatory regime, s.75 planning obligations are preferred where the trigger is a planning consent for redevelopment, If the beneficiary is a non-landowner (SEPA, council without adjacent land), consider: (a) public interest real burden; (b) s.75 planning obligation; or (c) statutory access powers, Legal advice from a Scottish conveyancing solicitor is essential before drafting environmental real burdens
