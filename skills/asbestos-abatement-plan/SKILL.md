# Asbestos Removal Plan (UK/Scotland)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

[SCOTS: Note] This skill adapts a US OSHA/EPA asbestos abatement plan template for the UK under the Control of Asbestos Regulations 2012 (SI 2012/632). The UK uses a "licensed / notifiable non-licensed / non-licensed" tripartite system instead of OSHA's Class I/II/III. HSE replaces OSHA; SEPA replaces EPA for waste regulation in Scotland. Scotland-specific enforcement is via HSE Scotland, the regulations apply GB-wide.

Produces a CAR 2012-compliant removal plan serving as both an HSE notification basis and operational blueprint for ACM removal. Covers scope, containment, removal methodology, worker protection, air monitoring, waste disposal, and final clearance.

## Prerequisites

Before drafting, collect:

1. **Asbestos survey report** - surveyor name/accreditation (UKAS-accredited), survey date and type (management / refurbishment / demolition), all ACM locations with material type, condition, friability, fibre type, % content, quantities (m²/m/items)
2. **Facility ID** - legal entity, address, UPRN / postal address, owner/authorised representative, tenant info if applicable
3. **Contractor credentials** - HSE asbestos licence number + expiry date, licence type (full / restricted), competent person (named individual, training record), asbestos analyst (UKAS accredited)
4. **Project schedule** - start date, phasing, expected completion
5. **Work type determination** - licensed / notifiable non-licensed (NNLW) / non-licensed work. Key thresholds:
   - **Licensed work** - higher risk: sprayed coatings, loose-fill insulation, asbestos insulating board (AIB), >1 m² or >1 hour NNLW in any 7-day period
   - **NNLW** - certain short-duration work on AIB, encapsulation, asbestos cement removal
   - **Non-licensed** - lower risk: textured coatings, asbestos cement (if not to HSE standards requiring licensed work)

## Output Sections

### 1. Regulatory Authority & Purpose, Cite governing regulation: **The Control of Asbestos Regulations 2012 (SI 2012/632)** - "CAR 2012"
- Reference HSE Approved Code of Practice **L143** (Managing and Working with Asbestos)
- Reference HSE guidance **HSG247** (Asbestos: The Licensed Contractors' Guide) and **HSG248** (Asbestos: The Analysts' Guide)
- Plan is the controlling document for removal operations; deviations require written competent person approval + HSE notification if material, Notifiability determination and notification deadlines, Plan availability for HSE inspectors and employee representatives

### 2. Project Identification

Table with: facility legal name, address / UPRN, owner/authorised representative, contractor name / HSE licence number / expiry, UKAS-accredited analyst, competent person (named individual), project dates. Populate from documents.

### 3. Scope of Work & ACM Characterisation

Reference survey report. Table each ACM location with: material type, friability, condition, asbestos fibre type, % composition, quantity, work type (licensed/NNLW/non-licensed). State aggregate totals and confirm HSE notification requirements.

### 4. Work Area Preparation & Containment

- **Enclosure** - polyethylene sheeting (≥1000-gauge, fire-retardant where required); full floor-to-ceiling containment; all seams, penetrations, and openings sealed
- **Warning signage** - HSE-approved asbestos warning signs (Regulation 14) at all enclosure entry points
- **Negative pressure units (NPUs)** - HEPA-filtered, calculated air changes per hour (≥4 ACH), continuous negative pressure monitoring with alarms, maintain ≥−5 Pa differential pressure, continuous during removal + minimum period post-cleaning as specified
- **Decontamination unit (DCU)** - 3-stage (dirty → shower → clean), or full DCU system per HSG247 for licensed work; running hot/cold water; airlock separation; suitable for safe removal of PPE
- **Waste transit** - seal waste at point of removal; separate airlock for waste bag-out
- **Pre-removal preparation** - remove/cover furniture/fixtures, isolate HVAC, seal electrical outlets and penetrations

### 5. Removal Methodology

- Keep ACM continuously dampened with surfactant-treated water (wetting agent) from removal through containerisation. Use **Class H HEPA-filtered vacuum** for dust extraction, Work section by section; hand tools preferred. If power tools essential: use those with integral local exhaust ventilation (LEV)
- **Waste containerisation**: double-bag in red asbestos waste bags (with asbestos warning labels); or rigid UN-approved packaging for larger pieces; ≤2/3 full; label per CAR 2012 + Hazardous Waste Regulations
- **Progressive cleaning**: HEPA vacuum at end of each shift; post-removal cleaning cycle: HEPA vacuum → wet wiping → visual inspection, repeat until visually clean, Use appropriate **RPE (Respiratory Protective Equipment)** as specified in the site-specific COSHH assessment

### 6. Worker Protection

- **Licensed work RPE**: Full-face, negative-pressure, **EN 12941 (P3)** / **EN 143 (P3)** disposable or reusable; alternatively powered respirator (THP3 / TM3). Half-face is not acceptable for licensed work
- **COSHH assessment**: detailed risk assessment under COSHH 2002 (as amended), including asbestos-specific risk reduction measures
- **Respiratory programme**: employee medical surveillance (Regulation 22 CAR 2012) - initial + periodic (at least every 2 years) + on cessation of exposure; fit testing per HSE guidance (INDG479) and clearance to wear RPE; face seal-check training
- **Decontamination sequence**: (1) dirty room, HEPA-vacuum overalls, remove outer PPE, bag as asbestos waste; (2) shower, full body wash with RPE on; (3) clean room, remove RPE, dress in clean clothes
- **Personal monitoring**: personal air sampling to determine personal exposure; control limit = **0.1 f/ml (fibres per millilitre)** over 4 hours (or equivalent short-term exposure)

### 7. Air Monitoring Protocol

| Sample Type | Timing | Locations | Method | Action / Target |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Background | Pre-removal | Outside enclosure, adjacent areas | Phase contrast (PCM) - MDHS 39/4 | Baseline reference level |
| Personal | During removal | RPE-filtered, workers inside enclosure | PCM, MDHS 39/4 | Below 0.1 f/ml 4-hr TWA |
| Static area / enclosure | Each shift | Inside enclosure + perimeter outside | PCM, MDHS 39/4 | Enclosure containment check: ≤0.01 f/ml outside |
| **Clearance (reoccupation)** | Post-cleaning, after clearance inspection | ≥4 locations per enclosure (more for >100m³) | **Stage 4 clearance** per **HSG248** guidelines; PCM + if required SEM/analytical | ≤0.01 f/ml (HSG248 clearance indicator) |

- Stage 4 clearance: (1) **Preliminary check** - visual inspection of whole enclosure, certification of visual cleanliness; (2) **Recheck after remediation** - repeat inspection if required; (3) **Final visual** - supplementary lighting, all surfaces clean; (4) **Air test** - aggressive sampling (leaf-blower or fan for ≥30 mins) → PCM → ≤0.01 f/ml, All air analysis by **UKAS-accredited laboratory** (ISO 17025)
- Clearance failure → re-clean, re-inspect, repeat clearance test

### 8. Waste Management & Disposal

- All asbestos waste (ACM, PPE, poly, HEPA filters, wipes) = **Hazardous Waste** per the Hazardous Waste Regulations 2005 (SI 2005/895) and the Special Waste Regulations 1996 (Scotland)
- **In Scotland**: Waste (Scotland) Regulations 2012; register with SEPA as hazardous waste producer, Waste must be stored in a secured, locked skip/area with asbestos warning signage, weather-protected, Licensed waste carrier with SEPA registration (Scotland) / EA registration (England/Wales)
- **Waste consignment note** completed for each load, WAC analysis if required, Permitted landfill for asbestos (specifically licenced cell); retain consignment notes as permanent records, minimum 3 years (Hazardous Waste Regulations)
- Tracking log: date, quantity, carrier, destination, consignment note number

### 9. Emergency Procedures

| Scenario | Response |
|---|---|
| Containment breach | Stop work immediately → isolate breach → switch on additional NPU → repair → have analyst inspect/clear adjacent area → resume only if clear |
| Worker injury/illness | First-aid responder wears RPE; injured person decontaminated before medical treatment unless life-threatening; HSE notification (RIDDOR 2013) for specified injuries/dangerous occurrences |
| Fire/evacuation | Evacuate via decontamination unit if safe; if immediate threat, direct exit, person decontaminated + RPE worn until safe area; medical surveillance post-event |
| Unexpected ACM | Stop work immediately → competent person evaluates → sample if in doubt → amend plan → notify HSE if work type changes to licensed |

### 10. Final Clearance & Project Closeout

**Clearance sequence**: visual inspection (competent person + analyst, using supplementary lighting) → any remedial cleaning required → final visual certification → air testing (aggressive method, ≥4 locations) → results ≤0.01 f/ml → issue **Certificate of Reoccupation**

**Final report must include**: executive summary, work narrative with deviations, air monitoring results with lab reports/chain of custody, photo documentation (pre/during/post), waste tracking log and consignment notes, all licences/certifications, competent person + analyst certification statements.

**Signatures**: competent person, contractor authorised representative, facility owner authorised representative, UKAS-accredited analyst (name, title, date).

**Record retention**: HSE requires **minimum 40 years** for personal exposure records (Regulation 22, CAR 2012). Facility owner maintains results as part of the asbestos management plan, permanent building record accessible to future owners/occupiers/contractors.

**Post-project filing**: submit results to the asbestos management plan (Regulation 4 dutyholder); update the site asbestos register accordingly.

## Pitfalls & Checks

- Extract from survey documents first, never fabricate project-specific data, Confirm notification status before drafting, ASB5 (licensed) vs online NNLW form vs non-notifiable
- **Licensed work**: only HSE-licensed contractors may undertake the work. Confirm licence expiry covers planned duration
- **Analyst independence**: the UKAS-accredited analyst / air testing provider must be independent of the removal contractor
- **Scotland-specific**: SEPA registration for waste; Scottish Environment Protection Agency enforces hazardous waste, separate from EA in England, Mark uncertain or potentially amended HSE guidance with `[VERIFY]`
- Describe performance/labour specifications, avoid commercial product names, Plan must withstand HSE enforcement scrutiny as a defensible 40-year record

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
