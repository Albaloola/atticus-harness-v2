# Environmental Indemnity Agreement [SCOTS]

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Protects lenders and secured parties from environmental liabilities on financed or acquired properties under Scots and UK law. Allocates contamination risk, remediation costs, and regulatory exposure to the indemnitor.

> **[SCOTS: Note]** This skill has been adapted from the US CERCLA/RCRA environmental indemnity framework to the UK/Scottish environmental liability regime. See the Scotland/UK Adaptation section at the end for the cross-reference table.

## Prerequisites

1. **Primary transaction documents** - loan agreement / standard security / disposition with defined terms, party names, amounts, dates
2. **Property information** - legal description (title number from Land Register / Sasines), address, current/prior uses
3. **Environmental reports** - Phase I/II site investigations (aligned to BS 10175 / Environment Agency/SEPA guidance), remediation reports, SEPA correspondence
4. **Party details** - full legal names, entity types, registered office addresses, Companies House numbers
5. **Governing jurisdiction** - Scotland (Scots law) or England/Wales

## Document Sections

### 1. Recitals

| Element | Source |
|---|---|
| Transaction type | Loan agreement (standard security) / purchase agreement/ disposition |
| Parties & roles | Indemnitor = borrower/owner/guarantor; Indemnitee = lender/heritable creditor + successors/assigns |

| Purpose statement | Risk allocation for environmental liabilities |

### 2. Definitions

Required defined terms (adapt for UK/Scotland):

- **Environmental Laws** - Environmental Protection Act 1990 (Part I, II, IIA), Environmental Authorisations (Scotland) Regulations 2018, Water Environment (Controlled Activities) (Scotland) Regulations 2011, Water Industry (Scotland) Act 2002, Pollution Prevention and Control (Scotland) Regulations 2012, Waste (Scotland) Regulations 2012, UK REACH, and all regulations, orders, notices thereunder including any amendment or re-enactment
- **Hazardous Substances / Contaminants** - broad definition including any substance that is classified as hazardous under UK REACH, or which constitutes "contaminant" or "pollutant" under Section 78A EPA 1990, including asbestos, PCBs, lead, petroleum hydrocarbons, solvents, PFAS/PFOA
- **Environmental Claim** - any demand, action, investigation, notice, or proceeding (including remediation notice, SEPA enforcement notice, statutory nuisance abatement notice) relating to Environmental Laws or Hazardous Substances
- **Remediation** - investigation, assessment, cleanup, removal, containment, monitoring, any remediation or mitigation as defined in Section 78A EPA 1990
- **Release** - any spill, leak, escape, discharge, deposit, or disposal into the environment

### 3. Indemnification Provisions

Scope checklist:

- Pre-existing contamination (known and unknown)
- Releases during indemnitor's ownership/operations, Environmental law violations (including statutory nuisance)
- Remediation costs (voluntary and compelled by SEPA or Local Authority)
- Regulatory enforcement actions (SEPA civil sanctions, COPFS prosecution costs)
- Third-party toxic tort claims, Damage to property value (diminution in value)
- Defence costs, solicitors' fees, expert consultant fees, Environmental assessment and monitoring costs

Drafting requirements:
- Covers conditions regardless of when discovered, Survives regardless of indemnitee's knowledge or due diligence findings, Absolute and unconditional, not contingent on loan default, Indemnitee has right to undertake remediation and seek reimbursement, Includes consequential damages (lost loan interest, opportunity costs)

### 4. Representations and Warranties

| Category | Representation |
|---|---|
| Compliance | Property complies with all Environmental Laws |
| No enforcement | No pending/threatened SEPA or Local Authority enforcement action or investigation |
| No undisclosed contamination | No contamination except as disclosed in identified reports |
| Full disclosure | All environmental reports and regulatory correspondence provided |
| USTs | No underground storage tanks, or all USTs registered and compliant with Oil Storage Regulations |
| Asbestos | Asbestos survey and management plan status disclosed |
| Permits | All required SEPA permits/registrations obtained and current under EASR |
| Hazardous materials | All storage, use, and disposal in compliance with duty of care |

Qualify representations only where Phase I/II findings require specific disclosures, no blanket knowledge qualifiers.

### 5. Covenants

- Maintain compliance with all Environmental Laws, Immediate written notice of: SEPA/Local Authority communications, inspections/notices/orders, releases or threatened releases, third-party claims, Maintain all required SEPA permits/registrations, No new hazardous substance storage/use without indemnitee consent, Grant access for environmental assessments at indemnitee's discretion, Cooperate with investigation or remediation, No settlement of Environmental Claims without indemnitee's prior written consent

### 6. Remediation and Enforcement

Contamination response sequence:

1. Indemnitor promptly investigates and remediates to applicable standards
2. Indemnitor submits remediation plan for indemnitee approval
3. If indemnitor fails to act within `[__]` days, indemnitee may undertake remediation
4. All costs reimbursable on demand with interest at default rate
5. Indemnitee may require escrow/reserve establishment
6. Remediation must achieve no-suitable-for-use standard or SEPA no-further-action letter

### 7. Survival and Duration

- Survives completion of the underlying transaction, Continues until the **latest** of: full loan repayment, property transfer by indemnitee, completion of all remediation with regulatory sign-off, expiry of all applicable prescriptive periods (see Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973)
- Survives enforcement of standard security, insolvency, sequestration, liquidation, Independent of and not merged into any judgment on loan documents

### 8. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

- Governing law: Scots law (if property is in Scotland)
- Jurisdiction: Scottish courts (Sheriff Court or Court of Session depending on value)
- No jury trial available (Scots civil procedure)
- Prescription: Consider 5-year prescriptive period (negative prescription) for breach of contract claims under Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973
- Prevailing party entitled to judicial expenses (solicitor-and-client scale subject to court discretion)

### 9. General Provisions

- Severability with reformation preference, Amendments require written consent of all parties, Notices: addresses, methods, effective dates, Assignment: indemnitee may freely assign; indemnitor may not assign without consent, No waiver by conduct; waivers must be written, Entire agreement as to environmental indemnity matters (does not supersede loan/security documents)
- Counterpart execution; electronic signatures per Electronic Communications Act 2000 / Scots law requirements

### 10. Signature Blocks

- Entity signatories with name, title, authority recital, Witness lines per Scots law (attestation requirements)
- Guarantor signature blocks if indemnitor is SPE (special purpose entity)
- Note: Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995 governs formal execution

## Pitfalls and Checks

- **Deal document alignment** - every defined term, party name, and property description must match primary transaction documents exactly; note that property descriptions use Land Register title numbers and postal addresses, not legal metes and bounds
- **Part IIA liability** - Section 78F EPA 1990 established the "polluter pays" principle; liability attaches to Class A persons (causer/permitters) and Class B (current owner/occupier). Indemnity should address both routes to liability
- **No knowledge qualifiers on indemnity** - representations may be knowledge-qualified; the indemnification obligation itself must be absolute
- **Phase I/II integration** - UK reports follow BS 10175 and Environment Agency/SEPA guidance, not ASTM E1527. If reports reveal contamination in the context of a SEPA investigation, draft specific disclosure schedules
- **PFAS/emerging contaminants** - Hazardous Substances definition must be broad enough for contaminants being assessed under UK REACH
- **SEPA enforcement** - SEPA can issue enforcement notices, suspension/revocation of permits, and civil sanctions. Criminal prosecution for serious breaches. Indemnity should cover all enforcement costs
- **Prescription** - Distinguish between obligations (prescription period) and rights to enforce contractual indemnity. Seek legal advice on prescription periods
- **Multiple indemnitors** - if guarantors or related entities are co-indemnitors, obligations must be joint and several under Scots law

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

### Overview
This skill has been adapted from a US-style environmental indemnity (based on CERCLA, RCRA, and state laws) for use under Scots and UK law.

### Key Differences from the US Original

| US Concept | UK/Scotland Equivalent |
|------------|----------------------|
| CERCLA (federal) | Part IIA Environmental Protection Act 1990 (contaminated land regime) |
| RCRA (hazardous waste) | Waste (Scotland) Regulations 2012 / Environmental Protection Act 1990 Part II |
| EPA (federal agency) | SEPA (Scottish Environment Protection Agency) |
| NPL (National Priorities List) | No equivalent, Part IIA is locally enforced by Local Authorities / SEPA for special sites |
| PRP (Potentially Responsible Party) | Class A / Class B liability under Section 78F EPA 1990 |
| CERCLA strict, joint & several liability | Part IIA strict liability; liability follows a two-class framework (causer/permitter; owner/occupier) |
| CERCLA cost recovery | S.78N EPA 1990 - recovery of remediation costs by enforcing authority |
| RCRA manifest | SEPA waste consignment notes (special waste) |
| 42 USC § 9608(a) (CERCLA lender liability) | No direct equivalent; lender liability under Part IIA is limited but not statutory, governed by common law / specific contractual protections |
| State environmental agencies | SEPA (Scotland); Local Authority for non-special sites |
| All Appropriate Inquiry (AAI) | Due diligence under BS 10175 / Law Society of Scotland guidance on environmental due diligence |
| SEC disclosure of environmental liabilities | Companies Act 2006 / Strategic Report regulations on environmental matters |
| US dollar amounts | GBP amounts; penalty figures should reference current sentencing guidelines or SEPA enforcement policy |

### Key UK / Scottish Legislation
- **Environmental Protection Act 1990** - Part IIA (contaminated land); Part II (waste)
- **Environmental Authorisations (Scotland) Regulations 2018** - SEPA integrated permitting
- **Water Environment (Controlled Activities) (Scotland) Regulations 2011** - water discharge / CAR
- **Waste (Scotland) Regulations 2012** - waste management
- **Pollution Prevention and Control (Scotland) Regulations 2012** - industrial emissions
- **UK REACH (Registration, Evaluation, Authorisation & Restriction of Chemicals)**
- **Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973** - time bar for claims
- **Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995** - formal execution
- **Land Reform (Scotland) Act 2016** - land rights and responsibilities

### Scotland-Specific Context, Environmental law is largely **devolved** to the Scottish Parliament, with SEPA as the principal regulator, Part IIA contaminated land regime is enforced by Local Authorities for most sites, and SEPA for "special sites"
- The **Purchase of Land** in Scotland uses Land Register titles and Sasines searches
- "Standard security" is the Scottish equivalent of a mortgage, Enforcement costs in Scotland, SEPA uses civil sanctions and criminal prosecution via COPFS
- **BS 10175:2011+A2:2017** - code of practice for investigation of potentially contaminated sites (Scottish/UK standard, replacing ASTM)
- **Scottish Law Commission** has not adopted US-style environmental liability rules; practitioner is advised to engage Scottish environmental solicitors
- **Contaminated land reports** in Scotland follow SEPA's framework, not EPA's

### Forms and Guidance, SEPA guidance on contaminated land: https://www.sepa.org.uk/environment/land/
- Part IIA statutory guidance for Scotland (SEPA)
- SEPA Enforcement Policy: https://www.sepa.org.uk/about-us/our-role/enforcement/
- Law Society of Scotland guidance on environmental due diligence in property transactions, Scottish Government planning & environmental guidance: https://www.gov.scot/policies/

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
