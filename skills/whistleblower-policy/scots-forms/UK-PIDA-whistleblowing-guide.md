# UK Whistleblowing Law, PIDA 1998 Guidance Note

## Key Legislation

- **Public Interest Disclosure Act 1998 (PIDA)** - primary whistleblowing protection law
- **Employment Rights Act 1996 (ERA)** - Part IVA (Protected Disclosures), ss.43A to 43L
- **Enterprise and Regulatory Reform Act 2013** - amended PIDA (added "public interest" test)
- **Small Business, Enterprise and Employment Act 2015** - extended whistleblowing protections to workers
- **Protection of Disclosures Act 2015 (Scotland)** - extends to certain NHS and police disclosures

## PIDA Framework

### Who is Protected?
- Employees, workers, agency workers, trainees, GP partners, non-executive directors, Police officers (separate procedure)
- NHS workers (in Scotland: additional Protection of Disclosures Act 2015)

### What is a "Protected Disclosure"?
A disclosure of information which, in the reasonable belief of the worker making it, is made in the **public interest** and tends to show one or more of:

1. Criminal offence
2. Failure to comply with legal obligation
3. Miscarriage of justice
4. Danger to health and safety
5. Environmental damage
6. Concealment of any of the above

### Key Changes from US Framework

| US Concept | UK Equivalent |
|------------|---------------|
| SOX §806 (public companies) | PIDA (all workers, all sectors) |
| Dodd-Frank §922 (SEC bounty) | No bounty equivalent, Employment Tribunal compensation only |
| SEC whistleblower programme | No regulator bounty; ICO not a whistleblower authority |
| State whistleblower statutes | Single PIDA framework (UK-wide, including Scotland) |
| Qui tam / False Claims Act | No UK equivalent; private criminal prosecutions rare |
| Retaliation → civil damages | Detriment + unfair dismissal claim to Employment Tribunal |
| Jury trial | Employment Tribunal (judge + panel, no jury) |
| Punitive damages | No punitive damages; compensation capped |

### Categories of Disclosure

**Internal disclosure** (to employer or prescribed person): Full protections apply.

**Regulatory disclosure** (to prescribed person, FCA, HMRC, SEPA, HSE, ICO): Full protections apply if made in good faith.

**Wider disclosure** (police, media, MPs): Must pass additional tests (reasonableness).

## Key UK Case Law

- *Parkins v Sodexho* [2002] IRLR 109 - legal obligation includes contractual duties
- *Chesterton Global v Nurmohamed* [2017] IRLR 837 - "public interest" test; reasonable belief of sufficient number of people
- *Blackbay v Gideonse* [2022] - victimisation for protected disclosure
- *Fecitt v NHS Manchester* [2012] IRLR 64 - chain of causation in victimisation

## Scotland-Specific

- Employment Tribunals in Scotland apply same PIDA/ERA framework, Scottish Ministers are prescribed persons for certain disclosures, Protection of Disclosures Act 2015 (Scotland) adds protection for certain NHS disclosures, COPFS may investigate criminal concerns raised by whistleblowers, Prescription period: 3 months less one day for ET claim (victimisation/detriment)

## Key Differences to Note in Policy Drafting

1. **No bounty/qui tam**: UK whistleblowers do not receive financial rewards, only compensation for detriment/dismissal
2. **Public interest test**: Established in 2013 - disclosure must be in the reasonable belief of the public interest
3. **No internal reporting prerequisite**: Worker may report externally to prescribed persons without going through internal channels first
4. **Prescribed persons**: SEPA, HSE, FCA, ICO, Ofcom, HMRC, specific list in The Public Interest Disclosure (Prescribed Persons) Order 2014
5. **Confidentiality agreements**: Cannot restrict whistleblowing (s.43J, ERA 1996)
6. **Compensation cap**: Unfair dismissal compensatory award capped (currently £105,707 or 52 weeks' pay) [VERIFY current figures]
7. **Legal aid**: Limited availability for Employment Tribunal claims
