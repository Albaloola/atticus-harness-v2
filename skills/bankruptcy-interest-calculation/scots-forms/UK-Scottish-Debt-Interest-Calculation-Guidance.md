# Pre-Judgment and Post-Judgment Interest, UK & Scottish Reference

## Key Legislation

- **Judgments Act 1838 s.17** - 8% per annum simple interest on judgment debts (England & Wales)
- **Interest on Damages (Scotland) Act 1958** - statutory interest on court awards in Scotland
- **Interest on Damages (Scotland) Act 1958 s.1** - Court may award interest on damages from date when right of action arose; current rate 8% unless court directs otherwise
- **Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973** - 5-year prescriptive period for obligations; 20-year long-stop
- **Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998** - statutory interest on late commercial payments: 8% + Bank of England base rate
- **Debt Arrangement and Attachment (Scotland) Act 2002** - regulates diligence on earnings and arrestment

## Key Differences from US Bankruptcy Interest

| US Concept | UK/Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| 11 U.S.C. §362 automatic stay | Administration moratorium / moratorium on diligence (Scotland) |
| 11 U.S.C. §506(b) post-petition interest for oversecured claims | No direct equivalent; creditors prove debt as at date of sequestration/administration |
| Pre-judgment interest | Interest on damages (Scotland) Act 1958 - from date of breach/damage |
| Post-judgment interest | 8% simple under Judgments Act 1838 (E&W) or Judicial Factors Act (Scotland) |
| Proof of claim in bankruptcy | Claim for sequestration/administration: must submit correct form and supporting documents |
| UCC-1 financing statement | Register of Charges (Companies House) - security interests in Scotland registered |
| Federal rate (28 U.S.C. §1961) | Judgment rate in Scotland: 8% (Judicial Factors (Scotland) Act 1889) |

## Interest Calculation in Scottish Courts

- **Simple interest** is the norm (compound only if contractually specified)
- **Contractual interest**: if contract provides express rate, that governs (subject to UCTA 1977 reasonableness test)
- **Statutory interest (Late Payment Act)**: 8% + Bank of England base rate (changed when base rate changes)
- **Judicial interest**: court exercises discretion, typically 8% simple from date of breach or decree
- **Day-count convention**: English courts use 365-day year; Scottish practice similar
- **Period**: from date of default/breach to date of decree (or earlier payment)

## Insolvency Claims in Scotland

- **Sequestration**: claims proved as at date of sequestration; no post-sequestration interest on unsecured claims
- **Administration**: claims proved as at date of administration order
- **Liquidation**: claims proved as at date of winding up order
- **Secured creditors**: may claim principal + interest to date of realisation, subject to security documentation
- **Priority**: bankruptcy expenses, preferential debts, floating charge holders, unsecured creditors

## Calculation Method

```
Interest = Principal × Rate × Days / 365
```

- Use 365-day year for Scottish court interest, Contractual terms may specify 360 or 365; follow the contract, Simple interest only unless contract expressly provides for compounding

## Key Pitfalls

- Prescription: stop interest accrual if underlying obligation becomes time-barred (5 years for most debts under Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973 s.6)
- Sheriff Court: Ordinary Cause / Simple Procedure have different costs rules
- "Unfair relationship" provisions (Consumer Credit Act 1974) may restrict contractual interest for consumer debts, Late payment legislation only applies to commercial debts (B2B)
