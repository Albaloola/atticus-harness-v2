# Scots/UK Forms Reference, Third-Party Intervention

Key forms and procedural documents for third-party intervention in Scottish and UK courts.

## UK Supreme Court

| Form / Document | Reference | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Application for permission to intervene | Form 1 (UKSC Practice Direction 7) | Must be filed promptly; UKSC Rules 23-25 |
| Intervener's written case (submissions) | As directed by the Court | PD 7 para 7.2.4 - typically limited to 10-15 pages |
| Corporate/entity disclosure of interest | PD 8 para 8.1.2 - declaration of any direct interest in the outcome | No direct equivalent of FRAP 26.1; PD 8 suffices |
| Funding source declaration | PD 7 para 7.2.2 - must state who is funding the intervention | Equivalent to FRAP 37.6 disclosure |
| Cover colours (PD 16) | Intervener: buff (greyish-yellow) cover for written case | See PD 16 para 16.2.1 |
| Digital Case Portal (e-filing) | UK Supreme Court e-filing system | Mandatory for all filings |

## Court of Session (Scotland)

| Form / Document | Reference | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Petition for leave to intervene | Inner House Bill Chamber or direct to court | Act of Sederunt (Rules of the Court of Session) 1994, Chapter 58 |
| Note of argument (intervener) | Rule 58.11 - concise written submissions | Scottish equivalent of "brief" |
| Minute of intervention | Rule 58.9 - formal minute to state interest | For third-party interventions in Outer House |
| Declaration of interest | Practice Note No. 1 of 2024 (current) | Must disclose any financial or other interest |
| Civil Online Portal | Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service e-filing | For initiating applications and lodging documents |

## Sheriff Court / Sheriff Appeal Court (Scotland)

| Form / Document | Reference | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Application to intervene (Simple Procedure) | Simple Procedure Rules, Rule 3.4 | Third-party notice / intervention |
| Note of argument (Sheriff Appeal Court) | Sheriff Appeal Court Rules | Written submissions with supporting argument |
| Motion for leave (Ordinary Cause) | OCR 15.1 - enrolled motion | Used for third-party intervention in ordinary cause proceedings |

## Key Differences from US Practice

1. **No FRAP 37.6 equivalent** - UKSC PD 7 replaces this with a funding source declaration
2. **No FRAP 26.1 equivalent** - UKSC PD 8 requires disclosure of direct interest instead
3. **Permission required** - most Scottish/UK interventions require leave; only US federal courts of appeal allow consent-based intervention without leave
4. **No 'amicus curiae' terminology** - use 'intervener' or 'third-party intervener' (though 'amicus' is understood in academic/historical contexts)
5. **Time limits differ** - UKSC interventions typically allowed only before or concurrently with main parties' submissions
