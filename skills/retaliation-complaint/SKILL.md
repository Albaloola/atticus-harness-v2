# Complaint for Retaliation / Whistleblowing Detriment

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

Generates an employment tribunal-ready complaint with jurisdictional, factual, and remedy components for the selected UK protective statute.

## Quick Start

1. Identify the governing statute (ERA 1996, PIDA 1998, EqA 2010, TUPE, or other protective statute).
2. Collect: parties, employment dates, protected-activity dates, detriment dates, decision-makers.
3. Confirm ACAS Early Conciliation has been completed (certificate number required).
4. Draft claim using the architecture and checklists below.

## Prerequisites

Before drafting, gather:

- **Governing law**: ERA 1996, PIDA 1998, EqA 2010, or other relevant UK protective statute
- **Jurisdiction**: employment tribunal has territorial jurisdiction (s. 94 ERA 1996 / territoriality principles)
- **Exhaustion**: ACAS Early Conciliation certificate number and date
- **Timeline**: employment start/end, protected-activity dates, detriment dates
- **Parties**: all claimants/respondents, entity structure, employer capacity
- **Evidence placeholders**: emails, meeting notes, witness statements, HR files, payroll records
- **Tribunal rules**: Employment Tribunals Rules of Procedure 2013 (SI 2013/1237), Presidential Guidance
- **Remedy strategy**: reinstatement, re-engagement, basic award, compensatory award, injury to feelings

## Complaint (ET1) Architecture

Draft sections in this order:

1. **ET1 heading block** - claimant and respondent details
2. **Claimant identity paragraph** - name, address, employment history
3. **Jurisdiction / time limits** - see checklist below
4. **Respondent details** - entity structure, employer identity
5. **Factual background** - numbered, chronological paragraphs
6. **Grounds of claim** - statutory basis + element-by-element pleading
7. **Remedy sought** and any additional information
8. **Signature block + statement of truth**

### ET1 Heading Template

```
IN THE EMPLOYMENT TRIBUNAL
CASE NO. [To Be Assigned]

BETWEEN:

[CLAIMANT FULL NAME]
  Claimant

AND

[RESPONDENT LEGAL NAME]
  Respondent

________________________________________________________________________

CLAIM FOR UNFAIR DISMISSAL / WHISTLEBLOWING DETRIMENT / VICTIMISATION
UNDER THE EMPLOYMENT RIGHTS ACT 1996 AND/OR THE EQUALITY ACT 2010

ET1 FORM, RULE 8, EMPLOYMENT TRIBUNALS RULES OF PROCEDURE 2013
```

*Note: ET1 does not use a judicial caption in the US sense. The claim is filed on the prescribed ET1 form, supplemented by a particulars-of-claim document setting out full grounds.*

| Topic | Allege | Reference |

| Time limit, unfair dismissal | Presented within 3 months less one day of effective date of termination | s. 111 ERA 1996 |

| Time limit, EqA victimisation | Presented within 3 months less one day of the act complained of | s. 123 EqA 2010 |

### Factual Allegations

- One key fact per numbered paragraph.
- Start with employment relationship, job title, continuous service, and relevant context.
- For each **protected activity**: date, medium, recipients, subject, and how it qualifies as protected.
- For each **detriment / adverse action**: date, actor, concrete impact on the claimant.
- Include **causal hooks** for each detriment:
  - Temporal proximity to the protected act
  - Decision-maker knowledge of the protected act
  - Shifting rationale / deviation from normal policy
  - Disparate treatment vs. comparators who did not engage in protected activity
  - Direct statements showing retaliatory motive

### Retaliation / Victimisation Elements Checklist

| Element | What to Plead |
|---|---|
| Protected activity (ERA 1996 s. 43A: qualifying disclosure) | What was disclosed, when, to whom; reasonable belief in truth; public interest (s. 43B) |
| Protected act (EqA 2010 s. 27) | What was done (allegation, complaint, proceedings, giving evidence) |
| Employer/respondent knowledge | How/when the respondent knew of the protected activity |
| Detriment or dismissal | Concrete adverse effect: dismissal, demotion, exclusion, salary reduction, harassment |
| Causation | The protected activity materially influenced the decision (ERA) / because of the protected act (EqA) |
| Remedy | Compensatory award, basic award, injury to feelings, reinstatement/re-engagement |

### Remedy Sought

```
1) Basic award for unfair dismissal (ERA 1996, ss. 118 to 122)
2) Compensatory award for unfair dismissal (ERA 1996, ss. 118 to 123 - capped at the lesser of 52 weeks' pay or the statutory cap [VERIFY current amount])
3) Compensation for whistleblowing detriment (ERA 1996, s. 49 - just and equitable; uncapped)
4) Injury to feelings (Vento bands if EqA victimisation claimed [VERIFY current rates])
5) Reinstatement or re-engagement (s. 113 ERA 1996)
6) Declaration of unlawful treatment
7) Recommendation (EqA 2010, s. 124(3)(c))
8) Interest on compensation (Employment Tribunals (Interest on Awards in Discrimination Cases) Regulations 1996)
```

**Key compensation distinctions:**
- **Whistleblowing detriment (s. 47B ERA 1996):** Compensation is uncapped, the ET awards what is just and equitable
- **Unfair dismissal (ordinary):** Compensatory award capped at the statutory maximum [VERIFY current amount - £105,707 for 2025/26] or 52 weeks' pay, whichever lower
- **Automatic unfair dismissal (whistleblowing):** Uncapped, no statutory limit on compensatory award (s. 124(1A) ERA 1996)
- **EqA victimisation:** Uncapped, injury to feelings, financial loss, and aggravated damages available; no upper limit

**Fee-shifting:** UK employment tribunals do not operate a 'loser pays' costs rule; costs are exceptional and only awarded where a party or representative has acted vexatiously, abusively, disruptively, or unreasonably (Rule 76, ET Rules 2013).

### Signing and Statement of Truth

- Claimant or representative signs and dates the ET1.
- Statement of truth: "I believe that the facts stated in this claim are true."
- False statements may result in contempt proceedings.
- No notarisation required.

## Pitfalls

- **Time limits are unforgiving**: 3 months less one day from the act complained of; only the ET can extend and only on 'reasonably practicable' grounds (or just and equitable for EqA) - rarely successful
- **Whistleblowing must meet the s. 43B test**: a qualifying disclosure must be in the public interest and made with a reasonable belief in its truth; purely personal grievances are not protected (Chesterton Global v Nurmohamed [2017] EWCA Civ 979)
- **Protected disclosure routes matter**: disclosures to employers (s. 43C), legal advisers (s. 43D), prescribed persons (s. 43F), wider disclosures (s. 43G/43H) - each has a different qualifying test
- **State of mind**: never plead motive without record support or inference basis
- **Timeline gaps**: do not omit known dates; tribunals scrutinise chronology for causation
- **Over-bundled facts**: keep one fact per paragraph for clarity and citation ease
- **Remedy overreach**: include only heads of loss supported by statute and evidence; uncapped awards still require proof of loss
- **Tribunal rules compliance**: confirm the ET1 form is correctly completed; additional particulars can be submitted separately
- **ACAS EC certificate**: the mandatory conciliation period is not optional; failure to obtain one is fatal to the claim

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from US employment-retaliation materials for use under the UK's protective employment legislation in Scotland.

**Legislation replacement:**
- Title VII (42 U.S.C. § 2000e-3(a)) → **Equality Act 2010, s. 27** (victimisation) and **ERA 1996, s. 103A** (automatic unfair dismissal for whistleblowing)
- FLSA (29 U.S.C. § 215(a)(3)) → **ERA 1996, s. 101A** (dismissal relating to the National Minimum Wage Regulations) / **ERA 1996, s. 104** (dismissal for asserting a statutory right)
- SOX (18 U.S.C. § 1514A) → **ERA 1996, ss. 43A to 43L (PIDA 1998)** - no Sarbanes-Oxley equivalent; whistleblowing to a prescribed person (s. 43F) is the closest analogue, Dodd-Frank SEC whistleblower → No direct UK equivalent; FCA whistleblowing regime is internal / prescribed person route, EEOC → **ACAS** (Early Conciliation) and **EHRC** (enforcement of EqA 2010)

**Terminology conversions:**
| US | Scotland/UK |
|----|------------|
| Plaintiff | Claimant (ET) / Pursuer (civil) |
| Defendant | Respondent (ET) / Defender (civil) |
| Complaint / Filing | ET1 claim form / Initial Writ |
| Discovery | Disclosure (ET Rules) / Commission and Diligence |
| Injunction | Interdict (only available in Sheriff Court / Court of Session for non-ET claims) |
| Trial | Hearing (ET) / Proof (civil) |
| Attorney | Solicitor / Trade Union Representative |
| Punitive damages | **Not available** in UK employment law |
| Class action | Group litigation order, opt-in, very rarely used for employment claims |
| 'At-will' employment | No UK equivalent; statutory unfair dismissal protection from day one of employment |

**Key UK/Scottish structural differences:**

1. **Employment Tribunal system:** All employment claims (except judicial review of ET decisions) go through the ET, not the civil courts; ET Scotland sits in Glasgow, Edinburgh, Aberdeen, and Dundee

2. **No 'fee-shifting':** Each party bears its own costs in the ET; costs orders are rare (Rule 76, ET Rules 2013) - fundamentally different from US fee-shifting statutes

3. **ACAS Early Conciliation is mandatory:** No ET claim can be presented without a valid EC certificate; time limits are paused during the mandatory conciliation period

4. **PIDA whistleblowing test:** A qualifying disclosure (s. 43B) requires *reasonable belief* in the truth of the information AND that it is made *in the public interest* - purely personal contractual grievances are not protected

5. **Time limits are extremely short:** 3 months less one day for most ET claims, shorter than any US equivalent; the 'reasonably practicable' extension test is rigorous

6. **Uncapped awards for whistleblowing:** Unlike ordinary unfair dismissal (statutory cap applies), whistleblowing dismissal/detriment compensation is unlimited

7. **No juries in employment tribunal:** Hearing before an employment judge (sitting alone or with lay members)

8. **Scotland-specific note:** Scottish employment tribunals follow the same Employment Tribunals Rules of Procedure 2013 (SI 2013/1237) as England, but Presidential Guidance on Vento bands (injury to feelings) may differ - [VERIFY current Presidential Guidance for Scotland]

9. **Legal aid:** Not available for employment tribunal claims in Scotland; trade union representation, pro bono, or private funding

10. **HMRC / FCA whistleblowing:** HMRC has its own whistleblowing framework; the FCA is a prescribed person under s. 43F for financial-services whistleblowing
