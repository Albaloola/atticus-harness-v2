# Scottish Civil Procedure, Witness Citation & Documentary Evidence

## No Direct "Deposition" or "Subpoena" Equivalent in Scotland

Scottish civil procedure **does not use depositions** (oral examination pre-trial) or **subpoenas** as understood in US federal practice. Evidence is gathered through different mechanisms:

## Scottish Evidence-Gathering Mechanisms

### Witness Citation (Court Attendance)
- **Citation of witnesses**: Sheriff Court Rules / Court of Session Rules provide for formal citation of witnesses
- **Form**: written citation served by a solicitor, citing witness to attend court on specified date
- **Failure to attend**: possible warrant for apprehension (second diligence)
- No pre-trial oral deposition of witnesses (except commission procedure below)

### Commission and Diligence
- **Purpose**: Obtain documents or recover property before trial (similar to US discovery of documents)
- **Procedure**: motion to the court → specification of documents → commissioner appointed to take evidence
- **Specification of documents**: detailed list of documents to be produced (must be specific, no fishing expeditions)
- **Commission to take evidence**: oral examination of a witness before a commissioner (rare; used for vulnerable witnesses or those unable to attend court)
- **Authority**: Administration of Justice (Scotland) Act 1972 s.1 - court may order recovery of documents and inspection of property

### Commission for Examination, Oral examination of a witness before a commissioner appointed by the court, Used for: witnesses abroad, seriously ill witnesses, or where pre-trial preservation of evidence needed, Evidence on oath; recorded and transcribed, Most similar US equivalent: deposition

### Recovery of Documents (Specification)
- **Sheriff Court**: Ordinary Cause Rules Chapter 28 - Recovery of evidence
- **Court of Session**: Rules of the Court of Session Chapter 35 - Recovery of documents, Must specify documents with reasonable particularity; general "all relevant documents" requests rejected

### Request for Further Information (Simple Procedure)
- Simple Procedure Rules (claims up to £5,000) allow the court to request further information from parties, No equivalent of US interrogatories or discovery requests

## Key Differences from US

| US Concept | Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| FRCP 30(b)(1) deposition | No direct equivalent; evidence by affidavit or commission |
| FRCP 30(b)(6) entity deposition | No entity examination; commission may be directed to an officer |
| FRCP 45 subpoena | Citation to attend / specification of documents |
| Subpoena duces tecum | Specification of documents (commission and diligence) |
| AO 88A/88B | No equivalent forms |
| Pre-trial discovery | Limited: commission and diligence for documents; no interrogatories |
| Witness fees | Witness expenses recoverable; must be tendered with citation |
| Motion to compel (discovery) | Court may order recovery; failure to comply = contempt |
| Protective order | Confidentiality orders available; standard in commercial actions |

## Scottish Court Rules (Civil)

- **Sheriff Court Ordinary Cause Rules** (2018 consolidating Act of Sederunt)
- **Simple Procedure Rules** (claims ≤ £5,000)
- **Rules of the Court of Session** (1994, as amended)
- **Commercial Actions** (in Court of Session and Sheriff Court) - have their own procedural rules with more active case management

## Practical Notes

- Scottish procedure is primarily **documents-based** rather than oral pre-trial discovery
- **Affidavits / witness statements**: used in certain applications (interim interdict, summary decree); increasingly used in commercial actions
- **No requirement to serve a "discovery plan"** or meet-and-confer before seeking recovery
- **Third party documents**: Application by minute in Court of Session, or by motion in Sheriff Court
- **Confidentiality**: parties can agree limited use terms; court may impose conditions
- **Pre-action disclosure**: available under Administration of Justice (Scotland) Act 1972 s.1 in limited circumstances
