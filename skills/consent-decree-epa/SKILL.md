### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. 3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

Drafts an enforceable environmental enforcement instrument resolving SEPA civil
enforcement actions under the Environmental Regulation (Enforcement Measures)
(Scotland) Order 2015 (SSI 2015/426), structured for SEPA's enforcement
regime. Unlike US EPA consent decrees, Scottish environmental enforcement
primarily uses **enforcement undertakings** (voluntary offers by operators to
remedy breaches), variable monetary penalties, and fixed monetary penalties.

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

Key differences from US EPA consent decrees:

| US Concept | Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| EPA (Environmental Protection Agency) | SEPA (Scottish Environment Protection Agency) |
| Consent decree (court-entered) | Enforcement Undertaking (SEPA accepts voluntary offer; no court order) |
| DOJ / U.S. Attorney approval | SEPA authorised officer alone; no Crown Office involvement needed |
| Federal court jurisdiction | Sheriff Court (appeals/JR) or Court of Session |
| CWA § 309(d) | Water Environment (Controlled Activities) (Scotland) Regulations 2011, Reg 44 |
| CAA | Pollution Prevention and Control (Scotland) Regulations 2012 |
| RCRA | Environmental Protection Act 1990, Part II (waste) |
| CERCLA | Environmental Protection Act 1990, Part IIA (contaminated land) |
| 40 CFR § 19.4 penalty caps | Fixed Monetary Penalty: £600; Variable Monetary Penalty: up to £50,000 (SEPA civil sanctions) |
| USD amounts | GBP amounts |
| 30-day public comment (FR notice) | SEPA publishes enforcement undertakings on its register; no statutory public comment |
| Covenant not to sue | SEPA agrees not to prosecute for specified offences; Crown remains independent |
| 28 U.S.C. § 2461 note | Regulatory Reform (Scotland) Act 2014, Part 3 |
| Treble damages / punitive damages | No punitive damages in Scots law |

[SCOTS: No direct equivalent, There is no direct Scottish equivalent to a US
federal court consent decree. The closest mechanism is a **SEPA Enforcement
Undertaking** under the Environmental Regulation (Enforcement Measures)
(Scotland) Order 2015, which is an administrative (not judicial) instrument.
For court-approved settlements, a joint minute in Sheriff Court proceedings
may be used, but this is rare in environmental enforcement.]

### 3. Compliance & Remedial Obligations

Corrective action provisions must specify:
- Equipment installation, process modifications, operational parameters, Monitoring: sampling frequencies, analytical methods, detection limits,
  reporting formats, SEPA review/approval triggers for work plans, design documents,
  completion reports, BMPs, training schedules, preventive maintenance, record-keeping

Cleanup standard basis, select applicable:
- [ ] Site-specific risk assessment (Scottish EPA’s
  "Remediation Targets Methodology" / CLEA model)
- [ ] Regulatory standards (BAT/NEQS)
- [ ] Institutional controls (where active remediation infeasible)

### 4. Compliance Schedule

Each milestone needs:
- Calendar date or days after triggering event (SEPA acceptance, approval,
  prior task completion)
- Sequential dependency mapping (design → approval → construction → certification)
- Interim reporting intervals for multi-year projects, Clear distinction between document-submission deadlines and physical
  performance deadlines

### 5. Financial Provisions

**Penalty elements (for VMP or settlement):**
- Total in GBP; itemise components (penalty / remediation costs / SEPA
  costs / oversight costs)
- Reflect statutory factors: seriousness, economic benefit, ability to pay,
  history, culpability, Fixed Monetary Penalty: **£600** (standard amount under SSI 2015/426 Sch 1)
- Variable Monetary Penalty: up to **£50,000** per breach (SEPA may impose
  higher with court approval)
- Penalties paid to SEPA are not tax-deductible; remediation costs may be
  deductible. Tax advice should be taken.

**Payment:** Payee is SEPA. Specify lump sum or instalment schedule, BACS/CHAPS,
proof of payment to SEPA within 14 to 30 days. Instalment default triggers
acceleration + interest at judicial rate (currently 8% p.a.).

**Financial security** (for significant remedial work): parent company guarantee,
bond, or letter of credit. Amount per SEPA-approved estimate plus contingency.
Reduction/release tied to milestone completion.

### 6. Stipulated Payments / Default Penalties

[SCOTS: SEPA enforcement undertakings do not use the US-style tiered stipulated
penalty structure. Instead, non-compliance with an undertaking may result in SEPA
revoking the undertaking and pursuing prosecution or imposing a variable monetary
penalty.]

- SEPA issues written notice of alleged non-compliance
- 14 to 28 days to remedy or submit representations, SEPA may revoke acceptance of undertaking and pursue full enforcement
- [SCOTS: Consider including contractual liquidate damages provisions in
  the undertaking agreement as a Scots-law enforceable mechanism]

### 7. Dispute Resolution

| Tier | Participants | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| Informal negotiation | Party representatives | 20 to 30 days |
| Senior escalation | SEPA senior officer + operator senior management | 15 to 20 additional days |
| Sheriff Court / judicial review | Application to Sheriff Court or Court of Session | After Tiers 1 to 2 exhausted |

Operator complies with SEPA position during pendency unless material prejudice.

### 8. Force Majeure

**Qualifying:** Acts of God, wars/riots, unanticipated government action
(statutory changes, permit refusal despite good faith).

**Excluded:** Economic conditions, financial inability, operator's labour disputes,
contractor failures (unless caused by qualifying FM event).

**Invocation** (written notice to SEPA within ___ days): describe event,
anticipated delay, affected milestones, mitigation measures. Operator must
exercise best endeavours to minimise delay.

### 9. SEPA's Reservation of Rights

SEPA always retains right to pursue:
- Criminal prosecution (COPFS prosecutorial independence, cannot be waived
  by SEPA alone)
- Post-acceptance breaches, Newly discovered imminent/substantial endangerment, Enforcement under other regulations not covered by the undertaking, Cost recovery (SEPA's investigative and monitoring costs)

[SCOTS: There is no covenant not to sue in SEPA enforcement undertakings of the
type found in US consent decrees. SEPA may agree to refrain from prosecution for
specified offences covered by the undertaking, but the Crown Office and Procurator
Fiscal Service (COPFS) retains independent prosecutorial discretion for criminal
matters, which cannot be contractually limited.]

### 10. Public Register & Transparency

Required procedure:
1. SEPA accepts undertaking or imposes penalty
2. SEPA publishes entry on its Enforcement Register (usually within 14 days)
3. Register entry includes: operator name, offence, penalty amount, date,
  undertaking summary
4. Personal data (names, addresses of individuals) redacted by default
5. No equivalent to US Federal Register public comment procedure

### 11. Modification & Closure

- **Modification:** SEPA written consent required
- **Closure:** Operator completes all obligations → SEPA issues closure
  confirmation → undertaking complete
- **Effective date:** Date of SEPA's written acceptance

### 12. Execution

| Signatory | Authorisation |
|---|---|
| Operator (corporate) | Director or Company Secretary + board resolution |
| SEPA | Authorised Officer |
| Witness | Required for execution as a deed (if applicable under Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995) |

Include counterparts clause and specify service addresses for all notices.

## Emerging Issues: PFAS and Scottish Environmental Regulation

PFAS regulation in Scotland is evolving separately from the US framework:

- **UK REACH** (retained EU REACH after Brexit) governs PFAS substance restrictions.
  The UK Health and Safety Executive (HSE) leads PFAS restriction proposals
  on behalf of the UK.
- **SEPA's PFAS regulatory approach** focuses on drinking water standards
  (Scottish Water monitoring) and industrial discharge authorisations under CAR.
- **Drinking Water Inspectorate (DWI)** sets PFAS standards for drinking water
  in Scotland; no equivalent to EPA's CERCLA hazardous substance designation.
- **No direct equivalent to US CERCLA PFAS designation.** The Scottish Parliament
  has not designated PFAS as a specific contaminant under the Contaminated Land
  regime (Part IIA of EPA 1990).
- **Site characterisation:** Where PFAS is a known issue, specify analytical
  methods aligned with UK Environment Agency guidance (LC-MS/MS for
  non-potable water).

Practical implications for Scottish enforcement instruments:

- **Check CAR authorisation conditions** - existing water use licences may
  not include PFAS limits; SEPA is adding these on renewal.
- **Covenant scope in undertakings** - specify which substances and regulations
  the undertaking covers; PFAS may be addressable under Part IIA (contaminated
  land) or CAR depending on pathway.
- **Risk assessment** - for sites with AFFF use, textile coating, or landfill
  PFAS sources, independent PFAS assessment via the Scottish contaminated
  land regime may be needed.

## Critical Checks

- **No admissions:** All breach references use "does not admit but does not
  deny" language.
- **SEPA concurrence:** Enforcement undertaking requires SEPA authorised
  officer sign-off; pre-discuss with SEPA case officer before formal offer.
- **Penalty caps:** Fixed Monetary Penalty = £600; Variable Monetary Penalty
  up to £50,000. SEPA may seek higher penalties through court prosecution
  (unlimited fines in Sheriff Court solemn procedure).
- **Criminal prosecution risk:** Unlike US consent decrees, an enforcement
  undertaking does not fully immunise from criminal prosecution, COPFS retains
  independence. Seek specific assurance only from SEPA's civil sanction powers.
- **Multi-regulation cases:** Address each regulation's liability framework
  and enforcement provisions independently.
- **Exhibit consistency:** Cross-reference all remediation plans and compliance
  programmes by annex number.
- **Third-party rights:** Scottish enforcement undertakings do not confer
  third-party rights equivalent to US CERCLA contribution protection
  (s 113(f)(2)). Consider separate contractual indemnities where needed.

## Troubleshooting

- **Operator cannot pay the proposed penalty.** Document ability to pay with
  audited financials. SEPA's ENFORCE model informs penalty level. If the
  operator can demonstrate financial hardship, SEPA may accept a payment plan
  or agree to a lower VMP. If the operator cannot fund remediation at all,
  SEPA may need to carry out the work and recover costs by charging order on
  the land.
- **SEPA rejects the enforcement undertaking offer.** SEPA publishes a
  rejection notice on its register. The operator may then face prosecution or
  a VMP. Pre-discuss the proposed undertaking terms with the SEPA case officer
  before formal submission.
- **Multi-operator sites.** Each operator of the site must be individually
  responsible; joint and several liability operates under Scots law but SEPA
  may proceed against each operator separately.
- **Contaminated land interaction.** If the site is also designated as
  "contaminated land" under Part IIA of EPA 1990, separate remediation
  requirements under the "suitable for use" regime apply, which SEPA (or the
  local authority) enforces independently.
- **Force majeure contested.** [SCOTS: Scots law recognises force majeure as
  a contractual concept. In the regulatory context, SEPA exercises discretion.
  If irreconcilable, the operator may seek judicial review of SEPA's decision
  at the Court of Session.]
