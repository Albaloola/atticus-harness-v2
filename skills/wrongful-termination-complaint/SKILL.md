# Wrongful Termination Complaint (Scotland/UK Adaptation)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Generates a claim establishing jurisdictional, factual, and legal elements for unfair dismissal, wrongful dismissal, discrimination, and related employment claims under Scots law and UK employment legislation.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

1. **Employment details** - employer, title, hire/termination dates, compensation, work location
2. **Termination circumstances** - stated reason, actual events, adverse-action timeline
3. **Protected activity or status** - discrimination basis (protected characteristics under Equality Act 2010), whistleblowing, leave exercise, complaint history
4. **Administrative exhaustion** - ACAS Early Conciliation certificate number, ET1 date-stamp, time limits
5. **Supporting documents** - employment contract, staff handbook, performance reviews, communications, settlement agreements
6. **Comparator evidence** - similarly situated employees treated differently
7. **Damages** - loss of earnings/benefits, injury to feelings, pension loss, out-of-pocket costs

## Quick Start

1. Confirm time limits are met (ET1 must be presented within 3 months less one day of dismissal, subject to early conciliation extension; this is jurisdictional)
2. Select forum, Employment Tribunal for most employment claims; Sheriff Court / Court of Session for contractual wrongful dismissal or breach of contract claims with value > £5,000
3. Identify applicable causes of action from the menu below
4. Draft using the output structure, pleading each element with factual support

## Output Structure

### 1. Heading and Jurisdiction

- Tribunal/court selection (Employment Tribunal, Sheriff Court, Court of Session)
- ACAS Early Conciliation certificate number and dates, Time-limit compliance statement

### 2. Parties

| Role | Allege |
|---|---|
| Claimant | Name, address, employment dates, position, relevant protected characteristics |
| Respondent | Legal entity, registered address, company number, nature of business |
| Individual respondents | Only where statute permits (e.g. whistleblowing detriment, Equality Act harassment by co-worker) |

Include successor entity allegations if TUPE applies.

### 3. Factual Allegations

Structure chronologically:

- **Employment relationship** - hiring, duties, performance history, contractual terms (express and implied)
- **Protected activity/status** triggering the claim
- **Temporal proximity** between protected activity and adverse action
- **Adverse actions** - progressive pattern if applicable
- **Termination** - circumstances and stated reason
- **Pretext indicators** - shifting explanations, policy deviations, comparator treatment

### 4. Remedies

| Category | Elements |
|---|---|
| Financial loss | Past and future loss of earnings, pension loss, loss of benefits, statutory rights |
| Injury to feelings | For discrimination claims only (Vento bands: lower £1,200 to £12,600; middle £12,600 to £36,700; upper £36,700 to £61,700 from April 2025 - [VERIFY current bands]) |
| Statutory awards | Basic award (unfair dismissal), uplift for failure to follow ACAS Code (up to 25%) |
| Aggravated damages | Only in discrimination claims where respondent acted in a high-handed manner |
| No punitive damages | **Not available** in Scots/UK employment law |
| Costs | Rare in Employment Tribunal; "loser pays" in Sheriff Court/Court of Session |

### 5. Causes of Action

Select based on facts and jurisdiction. For each, cite the statutory basis, plead every required element, cross-reference factual paragraphs, and state available relief.

| Claim | Basis | Key Elements |
|---|---|---|
| Unfair dismissal | Employment Rights Act 1996 s.94 | Continuous employment ≥ 2 years (unless automatically unfair), actual dismissal, no fair reason or unfair procedure |
| Automatically unfair dismissal | ERA 1996 ss.99 to 105 | Whistleblowing, family leave, health and safety, TUPE, zero-hours, no service requirement |
| Wrongful dismissal | Common law / Contract | Breach of contract by employer (notice period not given) |
| Direct discrimination | Equality Act 2010 s.13 | Protected characteristic, less favourable treatment, causal link |
| Indirect discrimination | Equality Act 2010 s.19 | Provision/criterion/practice, group disadvantage, individual disadvantage, not a proportionate means |
| Harassment | Equality Act 2010 s.26 | Unwanted conduct related to a relevant protected characteristic, purpose/effect of violating dignity |
| Victimisation | Equality Act 2010 s.27 | Protected act, detrimental treatment, causal link |
| Constructive dismissal | ERA 1996 s.95(1)(c) | Fundamental breach of contract (implied term of trust and confidence), resignation in response |
| Whistleblowing detriment | ERA 1996 s.47B / PIDA 1998 | Protected disclosure, reasonable belief in public interest, detrimental treatment |
| Breach of contract | Common law | Claim for notice pay or other contractual entitlements (over £5,000 goes to Sheriff Court, not ET) |

### 6. Remedy Sought

- Re-engagement or reinstatement (unfair dismissal, rarely ordered)
- Compensation (basic award + compensatory award for unfair dismissal)
- Compensation for discrimination (financial loss + injury to feelings)
- Declaration of rights, ACAS Early Conciliation certificate reference, Whether the Claimant wishes to have the matter heard as a preliminary hearing

## Pitfalls and Checks

- **Time limits** - ET1 must be presented within 3 months less one day of the act complained of (extended by ACAS Early Conciliation by up to 56 days). Jurisdictional, late claims are almost always barred.
- **ACAS Early Conciliation** - mandatory before presenting an ET1; obtain certificate (number) before filing. Failure is fatal.
- **Service requirement** - Claimant must have 2 years' continuous service for unfair dismissal (unless automatically unfair). No service requirement for discrimination.
- **Compensation cap** - Unfair dismissal compensatory award capped at the lower of 52 weeks' pay or the statutory cap (currently £105,707 from April 2025 - [VERIFY]).
- **No punitive damages** - Punitive/exemplary damages are not available in UK employment law.
- **Protected characteristics** - age, disability, gender reassignment, marriage/civil partnership, pregnancy/maternity, race, religion/belief, sex, sexual orientation (Equality Act 2010).
- **Fees** - Employment Tribunal fees were abolished in 2017; no fee to bring a claim. Sheriff Court/Court of Session have court fees.
- **Employment Tribunal vs. civil courts** - Decide whether to bring the claim in the ET (statutory claims) or Sheriff Court/Court of Session (wrongful dismissal, contract claims).

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Resolution |
|---|---|
| No ACAS Early Conciliation certificate | Contact ACAS to begin Early Conciliation before presenting ET1 |
| Individual respondent liability unclear | Check: Equality Act permits individual liability; ERA generally does not |
| 2-year service requirement not met | Consider automatically unfair dismissal grounds or discrimination claim (no service requirement) |
| Time limit expired | Exceptional circumstances / reasonably practicable extension is very difficult; seek urgent advice |
| Settlement agreement offered | Check statutory conditions (s.203 ERA 1996); independent legal advice required |
| Employment status disputed (worker vs. employee) | May need a preliminary hearing on status; gather written contract, working pattern evidence |

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

### Key Differences from US Version

| US Concept | Scotland/UK Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Plaintiff | Claimant |
| Defendant | Respondent |
| Complaint | Claim / ET1 |
| At-will employment | No equivalent; statutory unfair dismissal framework |
| Title VII | Equality Act 2010 (protected characteristics) |
| ADA | Equality Act 2010 (disability provisions) |
| ADEA | Equality Act 2010 (age discrimination) |
| FMLA | Family leave rights under ERA 1996 / Children and Families Act 2014 |
| EEOC charge | ACAS Early Conciliation (mandatory before ET claim) |
| Right-to-sue letter | ACAS Early Conciliation Certificate |
| FRCP / state civil rules | Employment Tribunal Rules of Procedure 2013 / Act of Sederunt |
| Punitive damages | **Not available** |
| Attorney's fees / contingency fees | **No contingency fees** in ET; costs rare in ET; "loser pays" in civil courts |
| Jury trial demand | **No jury** in employment claims |
| FRCP 9(b) / 12(b)(6) pleading | ET1 claim form (form ET1) - notice pleading style |
| US District Court | Employment Tribunal (most claims); Sheriff Court (wrongful dismissal over £5k) |
| State law | Scots law applies in Scotland; distinct from England & Wales |
| Damages caps (Title VII) | Statutory cap on compensatory award (currently £105,707 - [VERIFY]) |
| Discovery | No general discovery in ET; standard disclosure in Sheriff Court |
| Summary judgment | Summary decree (Sheriff Court) or strike-out (ET) |
| Administrative exhaustion (jurisdictional) | ACAS Early Conciliation (mandatory, jurisdictional) |

### Prescriptive Periods

- **Unfair dismissal**: 3 months less one day from effective date of termination (ET)
- **Discrimination**: 3 months less one day from the act complained of (ET)
- **Breach of contract**: 5 years (Sheriff Court) from breach
- **Personal injury (delict)**: 3 years from injury / date of knowledge

### Costs Rules

- Employment Tribunal: costs rarely awarded (unreasonable conduct); capped at £20,000 generally, Sheriff Court / Court of Session: standard "loser pays" (judicial discretion, typically expenses follow success)
- No contingency fee arrangements permitted in Scotland
- **Trade union funding** or before-the-event insurance may be available, Success fee agreements (speculative fee / "no-win no-fee") are permitted under limited regulation

### Key Statutes

| Statute | Application |
|---|---|
| Employment Rights Act 1996 | Unfair dismissal, wrongful dismissal, family leave, redundancy |
| Equality Act 2010 | All discrimination claims |
| Public Interest Disclosure Act 1998 | Whistleblowing protection |
| Transfer of Undertakings (Protection of Employment) Regs 2006 | TUPE |
| Working Time Regulations 1998 | Holiday pay, working time |
| Part-time Workers (Prevention of Less Favourable Treatment) Regs 2000 | Part-time worker rights |
| Agency Workers Regulations 2010 | Agency worker rights |

### Forum Selection

| Claim Type | Forum | Time Limit |
|---|---|---|
| Unfair dismissal | Employment Tribunal | 3 months −1 day from dismissal |
| Discrimination | Employment Tribunal | 3 months −1 day from act |
| Whistleblowing detriment | Employment Tribunal | 3 months −1 day |
| Wrongful dismissal (< £5,000) | Employment Tribunal (Breach of Contract claim) | 3 months −1 day |
| Wrongful dismissal (> £5,000) | Sheriff Court / Court of Session | 5 years from breach |
| Breach of contract (non-employment) | Sheriff Court / Court of Session | 5 years |

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
