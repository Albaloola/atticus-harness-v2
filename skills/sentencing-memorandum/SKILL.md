# Plea in Mitigation / Sentencing Submission (Defence) [SCOTS]

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Advocates for the most favourable sentence by presenting the accused as a complete person, analysing sentencing guidelines, and proposing a just sentence grounded in the principles of the Criminal Procedure (Scotland) Act 1995 and Scottish Sentencing Council guidelines.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

- **Criminal record / previous convictions** - schedule of previous convictions, antecedents
- **CJSWR (Criminal Justice Social Work Report)** - background report prepared for the court
- **Sentencing guideline** - applicable Scottish Sentencing Council guideline, if any
- **Accused's history** - background, family, education, employment, health, military
- **Letters of support** - family, employers, community, clergy
- **Mitigating evidence** - mental health records, substance abuse treatment, trauma
- **Victim impact** - compensation calculations, victim impact statements
- **Plea discount** - timing and extent of guilty plea (statutory discount under s. 196 Criminal Procedure (Scotland) Act 1995)
- **Proposed sentence** - what defence requests and why

## Quick Start

1. Identify the applicable Scottish Sentencing Council guideline and relevant statutory provisions
2. Review the CJSWR and antecedents for discrepancies or favourable content
3. Map accused's life history to sentencing factors (culpability, harm, mitigation, personal circumstances)
4. Organise mitigating factors by persuasive strength
5. Draft sentence proposal applying the sentencing process (SSC guideline steps)
6. Attach letters of support and evidentiary productions

## Document Structure

### Introduction

- Acknowledge the offence honestly, Introduce the accused as a person, Preview the sentence requested

### Sentencing Framework (Scottish Sentencing Council)

**Process Steps** (per SSC guideline):
1. Identify the sentencing guideline and disposals available
2. Assess culpability and harm to determine the initial sentence
3. Identify aggravating and mitigating factors
4. Determine the headline sentence
5. Take into account a plea of guilty (s. 196 discount, up to one-third reduction depending on timing)
6. Consider totality principle (multiple offences)
7. Consider if the sentence should be reduced due to any other factors

**Relevant Statutory Framework**:
- Criminal Procedure (Scotland) Act 1995 - sentencing powers, plea discount, compensation orders, Powers of Criminal Courts (Sentencing) Act, for relevant UK-wide provisions, Scottish Sentencing Council guidelines, applicable guideline for the offence type, Sentencing factors, culpability, harm, mitigation, personal circumstances

### Mitigating Factors

Present in order of persuasive strength. For each: supporting evidence (records, declarations, letters), link to reduced culpability or recidivism risk, connection to requested sentence.

**Common mitigating factors** (per SSC guidance):
- No previous convictions or minor record, Offender's age and maturity, Offender's physical or mental health, Remorse (genuine and demonstrated)
- Co-operation with authorities, Steps taken to address offending behaviour (rehabilitation, treatment)
- Impact of sentence on others (family, dependants, employment)
- Background circumstances (trauma, abuse, socioeconomic factors)

### Proposed Sentence

- Specific request (months, hours of unpaid work, fine amount, conditions)
- Alternatives to custody: Community Payback Order (CPO), monetary penalty, restriction of liberty order (RLO), drug treatment and testing order (DTTO)
- Custodial sentence: specify length and any early release implications, Compensation: amount, ability to pay, payment schedule, set-off, Non-harassment order (if applicable)
- Forfeiture, disqualification, or ancillary orders

### Letters of Support

Summarise letters attached, highlight key themes, note specific commitments (employment, housing, support network).

### Conclusion

Humanise the accused, restate requested sentence, tie to all sentencing considerations.

## Pitfalls and Checks

- **Be specific over general** - "works 60-hour weeks as a welder to support three children" beats "is a hard worker"
- **Acknowledge harm honestly** - courts respect candour; never minimise the offence
- **Preempt the Crown** - address their likely submissions for a higher sentence
- **Support every factual claim** with productions, records, or declarations
- **Cite comparable cases** - Sheriff Court and High Court sentencing cases for consistency
- **Coordinate on plea discount** - ensure timing of plea maximises the s. 196 discount
- **Consider CJSWR** - the social work report is a key document the court relies on; address any unfavourable content
- **Scottish Sentencing Council guidelines are advisory** - the court has discretion but must have regard to guidelines
- **Live evidence** - request a sentencing hearing for character witnesses when impactful
- **No statutory minimum sentences** - [SCOTS: Scotland does not have US-style mandatory minimums except for certain firearms and serious violent/sexual offences under the Custodial Sentences and Weapons (Scotland) Act 2007]

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

Key differences between US and Scottish sentencing practice:

| US Concept | Scottish/UK Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Sentencing Memorandum (Defence) | Plea in Mitigation / Defence Sentencing Submission |
| Presentence Report (PSR) | Criminal Justice Social Work Report (CJSWR) |
| US Sentencing Guidelines (USSG) | Scottish Sentencing Council (SSC) guidelines (advisory; courts must have regard to them) |
| 18 U.S.C. § 3553(a) factors | Statutory sentencing principles: Criminal Procedure (Scotland) Act 1995; SSC sentencing factors (culpability, harm, mitigation, personal circumstances) |
| Federal vs State courts | High Court of Justiciary / Sheriff Court / Justice of the Peace Court |
| Mandatory minimum sentences (varies by US jurisdiction) | [SCOTS: No direct equivalent] - limited statutory minima for serious offences (firearms, certain sexual/violent) under CSWS Act 2007 |
| § 5K1.1 substantial assistance motion | [SCOTS: No direct equivalent] - cooperation may be a mitigating factor but no formal prosecutorial motion mechanism |
| Plea bargaining (US-style charge negotiation) | Limited charge bargaining in Scotland; plea discount is for timing of plea (up to ⅓ reduction) |
| Supervised release (federal) | Licence conditions on early release; post-release supervision for certain sentences |
| Probation | Community Payback Order (CPO) with conditions |
| Home confinement | Restriction of Liberty Order (RLO) - electronic monitoring |
| Restitution | Compensation order (Criminal Procedure (Scotland) Act 1995, ss. 248 to 254) |
| USD | GBP (£) |
| Sentencing disparities (USSG departures) | Departures from SSC guidelines explained by reference to circumstances |
| Attorneys | Solicitors and advocates (instructed by solicitor) |
| Public defender | Duty solicitor / PD scheme (Scotland has a mixed model) |

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.

- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
