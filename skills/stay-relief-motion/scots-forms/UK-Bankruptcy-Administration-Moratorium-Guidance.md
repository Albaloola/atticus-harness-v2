# Moratorium / Automatic Stay Relief, UK & Scottish Equivalent Guidance

## UK Insolvency Frameworks

### England & Wales
- **Administration**: Moratorium under Schedule B1 Insolvency Act 1986 - equivalent to Ch.11 automatic stay
- **Moratorium (company)**: Corporate Insolvency and Governance Act 2020 - introduced standalone moratorium for companies
- **Individual voluntary arrangement (IVA)**: Interim order, court moratorium prevents creditor action

### Scotland
- **Sequestration** (personal bankruptcy) - under Bankruptcy (Scotland) Act 2016
- **Protected trust deed** - moratorium on diligence during protected status
- **Moratorium on diligence**: Bankruptcy (Scotland) Act 2016 ss.195 to 198 - allows 6-week moratorium (extendable) for DPP, trust deed or sequestration applications
- **Company administration**: Sch B1 Insolvency Act 1986 applies in Scotland (similar moratorium)
- **Liquidation**: no automatic stay equivalent, creditors may need court order

## Key Differences from US

| US Concept | UK/Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Ch. 11 automatic stay (11 U.S.C. §362) | Administration moratorium (Sch B1 Insolvency Act 1986) |
| Ch. 7 liquidation | Liquidation (compulsory / creditors' voluntary) |
| Ch. 13 individual reorganization | Bankruptcy (Scotland) Act 2016 / IVA (E&W) |
| §362(d) relief from stay | Application to court to lift/restrict administration moratorium or pursue enforcement |
| Adequate protection (§361) | No direct equivalent; court may impose terms on continuation of moratorium |
| SARE (single asset real estate) §101(51B) | No direct definition; LPA receivers may apply in some cases |
| UCC filings | Register of Charges (Companies House) for Scotland |
| FRBP 4001 | No equivalent rule in Scotland; ordinary motion practice |
| US Trustee | Accountant in Bankruptcy (Scotland) regulates insolvency practitioners |

## Scottish Sheriff Court Procedure

- **Motion to lift moratorium**: Application by Note in Court of Session or Sheriff Court depending on underlying proceeding
- **Debt enforcement in Scotland**: diligence (arrestment, attachment, adjudication) halted by moratorium
- **Creditor's challenge**: petition to court for warrant to continue diligence
- **Time limits**: moratorium typically 6 weeks; court can extend on cause shown
- **Key statute**: Bankruptcy (Scotland) Act 2016; Insolvency Act 1986 (for companies)

## Practical Notes

- US-style §362(d)(1) "cause" or "(d)(2) no equity" grounds do not exist as separate tests in UK/Scottish law, Scottish court considers: balance of interests between debtor's rehabilitation and creditor's right to enforce security, Secured creditors' enforcement rights in Scotland: calling-up notice / notice of default under standard security, Standard security (Scottish mortgage) enforcement may proceed if moratorium lifted, Interaction with Land Court: agricultural holdings / crofting matters
