## PART C: CHRONOLOGY OF DATA-RIGHTS BREACHES

This chronology is constructed from evidence sources OME-SRC-0027 (Accountability Matrix), OME-SRC-0041 (Timeline), OME-SRC-0044 (SAR Obstruction & DPO Failures), OME-SRC-0043 (Expanded Defendant Analysis), and OME-SRC-0042 (Executive Summary).

---

### PHASE 1: INITIAL SAR AND DISCLOSURE (APRIL–MAY 2025)

**16 April 2025:** Complainant files formal complaint 1176569 with University Complaints Resolution Office. This includes concerns about data handling practices. (SRC: OME-SRC-0041)

**May 2025:** Complainant submits first Subject Access Request (SAR) to University of Glasgow. (SRC: OME-SRC-0044)

**May–June 2025:** University fails to respond to SAR within the statutory one-calendar-month deadline required by Article 12(3) UK GDPR. No extension sought under Article 12(3) second paragraph. (SRC: OME-SRC-0044)

**BREACH 1:** Failure to comply with Article 12(3) UK GDPR — response not provided within one calendar month.

---

### PHASE 2: INCOMPLETE AND EVASIVE RESPONSES (JUNE–AUGUST 2025)

**Mid-2025:** Complainant submits multiple additional SARs including SAR 50, SAR 67, and SAR 86 seeking:
- Complete personal data held by the University
- Metadata, version history, and tracked changes for all documents
- Internal emails and communications referencing the complainant
- Data held by the Data Protection Office, Complaints Resolution Office, School of Medicine, and Fitness to Practise (FtP) office
(SRC: OME-SRC-0044)

**University responses received:**
- Responses provided outside the one-month statutory deadline
- No proper Article 12(3) extension notification
- Responses incomplete: metadata, version history, and tracked changes systematically omitted
- No adequate explanation of redactions or withheld material as required by Article 15(3) read with recital 63 UK GDPR
- No explanation of the legal basis for any withholding under Schedule 2, Part 3, Data Protection Act 2018 exemptions
(SRC: OME-SRC-0044)

**BREACH 2:** Failure to provide complete copies of personal data undergoing processing, contrary to Article 15(3) UK GDPR — metadata, version history, and tracked changes withheld.

**BREACH 3:** Failure to provide supplementary information specified in Article 15(1)(a)–(h) UK GDPR, including purposes of processing, categories of personal data, recipients, envisaged storage period, and information about the source of the data.

**BREACH 4:** Failure to provide meaningful explanations of redactions/withholds, contrary to the transparency obligation under Article 5(1)(a) and the right of access under Article 15.

---

### PHASE 3: DPO AND DATA PROTECTION OFFICE FAILURES (AUGUST–SEPTEMBER 2025)

**August–September 2025:** Complainant raises with the University Data Protection Officer (DPO) that SAR responses are incomplete and requests clarification on:
- Missing metadata, version history, tracked changes
- Basis for any withholding or redaction
- Confirmation that all personal data has been provided
(SRC: OME-SRC-0044)

**The DPO / Data Protection Office:**
- Fails to investigate or remedy the incomplete SAR responses
- Provides no substantive response to the complainant's specific concerns
- Does not confirm whether all personal data has been disclosed
- Does not explain the legal basis for failing to provide metadata/version history
(SRC: OME-SRC-0044)

**BREACH 5:** Failure by the Data Protection Officer to discharge obligations under Article 39(1)(a) UK GDPR — to inform and advise the controller of its obligations and to monitor compliance.

**BREACH 6:** Failure by the Data Protection Office to facilitate the exercise of data subject rights, contrary to Article 38(4) UK GDPR read with Article 12(2).

---

### PHASE 4: PRIVACY NOTICE AND TRANSPARENCY FAILURES

**Throughout 2024–2025:** The University's hardship fund application form contains a broken/defective privacy notice link, meaning data subjects (including the complainant) could not access information about how their personal data would be processed when applying for financial support. (SRC: OME-SRC-0044)

**BREACH 7:** Failure to provide a valid privacy notice at the point of data collection, contrary to Articles 13(1) and 13(2) UK GDPR (information to be provided where personal data are collected from the data subject).

---

### PHASE 5: UNLAWFUL PROCESSING OF SPECIAL CATEGORY DATA

**Throughout 2024–2025:** The University processes the complainant's health data (mental health, disability status, counselling records) and data concerning Fitness to Practise proceedings without a clearly established lawful basis for processing under Article 6 and without a clearly established condition for processing special category data under Article 9 UK GDPR. (SRC: OME-SRC-0044)

Specific instances:
- Dr Angela Cogan allegedly accesses welfare/medical meeting information without authorisation (SRC: OME-SRC-0043)
- Health data disclosed to FtP panel without explicit consent
- Disability status shared across departments without lawful basis

**BREACH 8:** Unlawful processing of special category data (health data) without satisfying a condition under Article 9(2) UK GDPR, read with Schedule 1, Data Protection Act 2018.

**BREACH 9:** Processing of personal data without a lawful basis under Article 6(1) UK GDPR.

---

### PHASE 6: CONFIDENTIALITY BREACHES — PRIVATE EMAIL FORWARDED TO FTP

**On or about [date — to be confirmed]:** Complainant sends a private, informal email to Dr Lesley Jackson inviting her to a casual coffee meeting. This is a personal communication, not a formal or professional representation. (SRC: OME-SRC-0043)

**Subsequently:** Dr Jackson immediately forwards the complainant's private email to Dr Margaret McMillan (FtP Investigator) without the complainant's knowledge or consent. The email is then incorporated into the FtP investigation file and characterised as "evidence of unprofessionalism and impaired fitness to practice medicine." (SRC: OME-SRC-0043)

**BREACH 10:** Unauthorised onward disclosure of personal data (the complainant's private email) by Dr Jackson to Dr McMillan, constituting processing without a lawful basis under Article 6 UK GDPR and in breach of the data protection principles under Article 5(1)(a)–(c).

**BREACH 11:** Processing of personal data for an incompatible purpose (weaponising a social invitation as disciplinary evidence) contrary to the purpose limitation principle in Article 5(1)(b) UK GDPR.

---

### PHASE 7: SYSTEMIC RETALIATION THROUGH DATA PRACTICES

**Throughout 2025:** The University's Complaints Resolution Office (led by Pauline Ewan, Complaints Resolution Officer):
- Uses the Expected Behaviour Policy to threaten the complainant for "demanding responses within inappropriate timescales" when the complainant is merely asserting statutory data subject rights under UK GDPR
- Delays complaint resolution beyond statutory timescales
- Coordinates with accused departments, potentially sharing complaint data without lawful basis
(SRC: OME-SRC-0043)

**BREACH 12:** Victimisation of the complainant for exercising data subject rights, contrary to the prohibition on penalising data subjects for exercising their rights, inherent in Article 12(2) UK GDPR (facilitation of exercise of data subject rights).

**BREACH 13:** Potential unauthorised disclosure of complaint data between Complaints Resolution Office and accused departments without a lawful basis under Article 6 UK GDPR.

---

### PHASE 8: DATA DELETION OR FALSIFICATION CONCERNS

**To be investigated:** The complainant has identified that the Academic Registry may have deleted data or sent a false "withdrawal" email concerning the complainant. If confirmed, this would constitute: (SRC: OME-SRC-0043, OME-SRC-0044)

**BREACH 14 (alleged):** Unlawful erasure or alteration of personal data without the data subject's request, contrary to the accuracy principle under Article 5(1)(d) and potentially constituting a criminal offence under section 170 of the Data Protection Act 2018 (unlawful obtaining or disclosure of personal data, or reckless retention).

## PART F: REMEDIES SOUGHT FROM THE ICO

The complainant respectfully requests that the Information Commissioner:

1. **Investigate** the University of Glasgow's compliance with UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018 in respect of the breaches identified above.

2. **Require** the University of Glasgow to provide complete, unredacted SAR responses including all metadata, version history, tracked changes, internal emails, and personal data held in all departments (School of Medicine, Data Protection Office, Complaints Resolution Office, FtP Office, Academic Registry, Disability Services, Human Resources, Safeguarding Team, and Student Representative Council).

3. **Require** the University of Glasgow to identify and disclose the lawful basis under Article 6 and (where applicable) the condition under Article 9 for each processing operation involving the complainant's personal data, particularly special category health data.

4. **Issue an enforcement notice** under section 149 of the Data Protection Act 2018 requiring the University to:
   - Rectify systemic failures in SAR handling
   - Implement compliant SAR procedures
   - Train staff on data subject rights obligations
   - Ensure the DPO is adequately resourced and independent
   - Fix the defective privacy notice on the hardship application form

5. **Consider imposing a penalty** under Article 83 UK GDPR / section 155 Data Protection Act 2018 for the serious, systemic, and sustained nature of the breaches.

6. **Assess and confirm** whether any criminal offences under section 170 of the Data Protection Act 2018 have been committed in respect of alleged data deletion, falsification, or unauthorised onward disclosure.

7. **Require** the University to confirm in writing that:
   - All personal data has now been fully disclosed
   - No personal data has been unlawfully deleted or altered
   - All onward disclosures of the complainant's personal data to third parties (including the FtP panel, SRC, and external bodies) are identified and the lawful basis for each is specified
   - Specific measures are in place to prevent future breaches

## NOTES FOR THE COMPLAINANT

1. **Before filing:** This complaint should be reviewed and finalised by a Scottish solicitor, particularly if court proceedings are also contemplated. The ICO may stay its investigation if parallel court proceedings address the same matters.

2. **ICO time limits:** The ICO generally expects complaints to be made within 3 months of the last meaningful contact with the data controller about the issue. You should file promptly.

3. **Supporting evidence:** Organise the documents listed in Part G into a numbered bundle with an index for submission with the complaint.

4. **Quantum in parallel proceedings:** The draft Schedule of Loss in OME-SRC-0042 includes £10,000 for GDPR damages. The ICO does not award compensation — that must be pursued through the courts under Article 82 UK GDPR / section 168 Data Protection Act 2018.

5. **Regulatory coordination:** If you also intend to complain to the Scottish Public Services Ombudsman (SPSO), Office for Students (OfS), or General Medical Council (GMC), coordinate the timing and scope of each complaint to avoid duplication or inconsistent positions.
