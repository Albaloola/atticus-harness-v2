# UK Export Controls & Technology Control Plans, Scottish/UK Guidance

## Key UK Legislation & Frameworks

- **Export Control Order 2008** (SI 2008/3231) - main instrument controlling strategic exports including military goods, dual-use items, and software/technology
- **Retained EU Dual-Use Regulation** (Regulation 2021/821) - as amended and retained in UK law post-Brexit plus UK Global Human Rights sanctions
- **Strategic Export Control Lists** - UK Military List (UKML), Dual-Use List, etc.
- **Export Control Act 2002** - enabling legislation
- **Customs (Enforcement) Regulations 2024** - enforcement and penalties
- **Trade Sanctions (UK)**: Russia sanctions regulations, other trade sanctions under Sanctions and Anti-Money Laundering Act 2018
- **Technology Security Plan (TSP)** - UK equivalent of ITAR Technology Control Plan; guidance from UK Export Control Joint Unit (ECJU)

## Key Differences from US ITAR

| US Concept | UK Equivalent |
|---|---|
| ITAR (22 CFR 120-130) | Export Control Order 2008 / UK Military List |
| USML (U.S. Munitions List) | UK Military List (UKML) |
| DDTC registration | Registration with Export Control Joint Unit (ECJU) for certain activities |
| Empowered Official (§120.25) | No direct equivalent; named responsible person within organisation |
| Deemed export | Same concept, release of technology to foreign national = export; covered by Trade Sanctions / Export Control regime |
| DSP-5 license | Standard Individual Export Licence (SIEL) / Open General Export Licence (OGEL) |
| TAA (Technical Assistance Agreement) | Not a distinct UK approval type; Technology transfer covered by export licences |
| US person verification (§120.62) | UK does not have nationality-based access control as a formal regime, but dual-use rules use "intended end-user" test |
| AECA penalties | Customs and Excise Management Act 1979 + Export Control Act 2002 penalties |
| DDTC voluntary disclosure | Voluntary disclosure to ECJU encouraged for mitigation |

## UK Export Control Joint Unit (ECJU)

- Part of the Department for Business and Trade (DBT)
- Issues licences, guidance, and compliance oversight, Website: www.gov.uk/government/organisations/export-control-joint-unit

## Technology Security Plans (TSP)

- UK government encourages TSPs as good compliance practice, Recommended structure: scope, access controls, handling/storage, training, audit, incident response, Similar to ITAR TCP but tailored to UK regulatory framework, Key: identify controlled technology by reference to UKML entries and EU Dual-Use list

## Scottish Considerations

- Export controls are **reserved** to UK Parliament (not devolved to Scottish Parliament)
- Same UK-wide Export Control Order 2008 applies in Scotland, Enforcement: HMRC (UK-wide); prosecution in Scotland by COPFS, Relevant sectors in Scotland: defence, oil & gas, marine technology, electronics, Universities: material to Scottish universities conducting research involving controlled technology, No separate Scottish export control regulator

## Common UK Licence Types

- **SIEL** (Standard Individual Export Licence) - for specific items, quantities, end-users
- **OGEL** (Open General Export Licence) - pre-published; applicable if terms met
- **OIEL** (Open Individual Export Licence) - for ongoing programme with multiple shipments
- **GEA** (General Export Authorisation) - equivalent of OGEL for specific contexts

## Penalties

- Criminal offences under Export Control Act 2002: up to 10 years imprisonment and/or unlimited fine, Civil penalties: HMRC can compound proceedings, Corporate liability: under Bribery Act 2010, Criminal Finances Act 2017, and corporate manslaughter
