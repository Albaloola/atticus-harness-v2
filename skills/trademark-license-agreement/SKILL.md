# Trademark License Agreement

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a defensible Trademark License Agreement that protects mark ownership and goodwill while establishing enforceable commercial terms for licensee use.

## Prerequisites

1. **Party details** - full legal names, entity types, jurisdictions of organisation, principal places of business
2. **Licensed marks** - mark names, registration numbers (UK IPO or EUIPO), jurisdiction (UK, EU or international), visual representations for Exhibit A
3. **License scope** - exclusive or non-exclusive; field of use; licensed products/services (specific categories, not broad descriptions)
4. **Territory** - countries (UK, EU, or worldwide); treatment of online/passive internet sales
5. **Financial terms** - upfront fees, royalty rate (% of Net Sales or per-unit), minimum guarantees, payment frequency
6. **Term** - initial duration, auto-renewal mechanics, notice period for non-renewal
7. **Quality control requirements** - approval process, sample submission timelines, applicable standards

## Output Structure

### 1. Header & Recitals, Title: **TRADEMARK LICENSE AGREEMENT**
- Effective date, full party identification, defined terms ("Licensor," "Licensee")
- Recitals: licensor's mark ownership; licensee's desire for authorised use

### 2. Grant of License

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Exclusivity | State exclusive or non-exclusive; exclusive = licensor cannot grant others in territory |
| Transferability | Non-transferable and non-sublicensable unless expressly permitted |
| Field of use | Tied to licensed products/services; no open-ended descriptions |
| Permitted uses | Enumerate: advertising, packaging, digital media |
| Rights reserved | All rights not expressly granted reserved to licensor |

### 3. Licensed Marks (Exhibit A)
- Reference Exhibit A for visual representations, registration numbers, jurisdictions, Specify required symbols (®, ™, ℠) per mark, Address combination use with licensee's or third-party marks; state prominence requirements, For UK-registered marks: state UK IPO registration number and classification (Nice class)

### 4. Licensed Products & Services, Specific product categories or service descriptions; avoid broad class-level language, Approval process for expanding scope during term, Pre-launch sample/prototype submission requirement

### 5. Territory, Define by country, or EU/UK economic area, International: address online sales and passive internet reach beyond territory, UK-specific: note that Northern Ireland remains subject to certain EU trademark rules post-Brexit

### 6. Quality Control, All goods/services bearing marks must meet licensor-specified standards
- **Approval workflow**: licensee submits samples → licensor has **15 business days** to approve/reject → silence = deemed approved, Licensor right to inspect facilities on reasonable notice, Immediate discontinuation right upon quality failure, Short cure period or immediate termination for QC violations (distinguish from standard 30-day cure)
- **NB**: Naked licensing (no quality control) risks mark revocation under s.46(1)(d) Trade Marks Act 1994

### 7. Ownership & Goodwill, Licensee acknowledges licensor's sole ownership of all right, title, and interest, including goodwill, All use inures to licensor's benefit, Licensee prohibited from challenging mark validity during and after term (acknowledge enforceability risk under EU/UK competition law)
- Required trademark notices and source indicators, Where applicable: record the licence at UK IPO to protect licensee rights against third parties (s.28 Trade Marks Act 1994)

### 8. Royalties & Payment

| Term | Specification |
|---|---|
| Net Sales | Gross revenue minus returns, allowances, taxes, shipping |
| Royalty rate | [% of Net Sales] or [per-unit amount] |
| Payment schedule | Quarterly, within **30 days** after quarter end |
| Minimum guarantee | State whether failure = material breach |
| Late payment | Interest rate on overdue amounts (default: late payment legislation, Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998) |
| Currency | Payment currency; allocate FX risk for international deals |
| VAT | UK VAT treatment of royalties (standard-rated supply; consider reverse charge for cross-border) |

### 9. Reporting & Audit Rights
- **Quarterly reports**: units sold, gross sales, deductions, net sales, royalties due, Audit right on **30 days' notice**, business hours, **once per year**
- Unlimited audits if prior audit revealed underpayment > **5%**
- Licensee bears audit cost if underpayment exceeds threshold, Record retention: **3 to 5 years**

### 10. Term & Renewal, Initial term (e.g., 3 or 5 years)
- Auto-renewal unless notice given (e.g., 90 days pre-expiration)
- Option: adjusted royalty rates or expanded territory on renewal

### 11. Termination

| Trigger | Cure Period |
|---|---|
| Material breach (general) | 30 days written notice |
| Quality control violation | Shorter period or immediate at licensor's election |
| Mark challenge by licensee | Immediate |
| Bankruptcy/insolvency | Immediate |
| Failure to meet minimums | Per agreement |
| Convenience | 90 to 180 days notice |

**Post-termination obligations:**
- Immediate cessation of all mark use (products, packaging, web, social media)
- **Sell-off period**: 60 to 90 days for existing inventory; royalties continue, Written certification of destruction of remaining materials, Transfer of domain names and social media accounts incorporating marks, Survival: accrued royalties, confidentiality, post-term use restrictions

### 12. Standard Provisions
- **Indemnification**: licensee indemnifies licensor for claims from licensee's use or licensed products
- **Representations**: both parties, authority to contract; licensor, mark ownership and registration validity; licensee, quality compliance
- **Liability cap**: mutual, appropriate to transaction value
- **Dispute resolution**: governing law (Scots law, English law, or other), jurisdiction (Scottish courts, English courts), litigation or ADR
- **Assignment**: licensee may not assign without consent; licensor assignment permitted
- **Notices**: method, addresses, deemed receipt timing
- **Boilerplate**: entire agreement, written amendment, severability, waiver, force majeure, counterparts, e-signatures (valid under Electronic Communications Act 2000)

### 13. Exhibits
- **Exhibit A**: Licensed marks, visual representations, UK IPO/EUIPO registration numbers, jurisdictions, Nice classes
- **Exhibit B** (if applicable): Quality control standards and guidelines

### 14. Signature Block, Authorised representative: name, title, date, Entity-appropriate authority (director/company secretary for company, member/manager for LLP)
- Scottish execution formalities: if executed in Scotland, a company can sign as prescribed by s.36 Companies Act 2006; traditional sealing no longer required

## Pitfalls

- **Quality control is validity-preserving**: naked licences (no QC) can result in mark revocation (s.46(1)(d) Trade Marks Act 1994) - ensure QC provisions are substantive, not pro forma
- **Exclusivity precision**: distinguish exclusive licence (no other licensees) from sole licence (licensor retains own use rights)
- **Field of use and territory**: define narrowly to avoid scope disputes
- **Goodwill inurement**: mandatory, all use must accrue to licensor
- **Minimum royalties**: tie to breach mechanics if commercially significant
- **International deals**: confirm mark registrations in each territory; unregistered marks may lack protection, For **registered marks**, verify current UK IPO or EUIPO registry status before drafting [VERIFY registrations with client]
- **Anti-challenge clause**: review enforceability in UK/Scots law; competition law considerations (Article 101 TFEU / Competition Act 1998 can limit no-challenge clauses)
- **Licence recordal**: record licence at UK IPO (Form TM50) within 6 months to protect licensee's rights and ensure priority against third-party transactions
- **Brexit**: post-Brexit, EU trademarks (EUTMs) no longer cover the UK; parallel UK rights created automatically for pre-2021 registrations; address separately

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from US trademark licensing practice to UK/Scots law.

### Key Terminology Changes

| US Term | UK/Scotland Equivalent |
|---|---|
| USPTO | UK IPO (Intellectual Property Office) |
| Lanham Act (15 USC §§1051 et seq.) | Trade Marks Act 1994 |
| Federal registration | UK IPO registration |
| State registration | Not applicable in UK (no equivalent to US state TM registrations) |
| Intent-to-use application | UK IPO application based on bona fide intention to use (s.3(1) TMA 1994) |
| Statement of use | Declaration of use (s.46 TMA 1994 - revocation for non-use after 5 years) |
| Section 15 (acquired distinctiveness) | Section 3(2) TMA 1994 (acquired distinctiveness through use) |
| USPTO TEAS (online filing) | UK IPO online application (TM forms) |
| MADRID Protocol (US as designated office) | UK also covers Madrid Protocol designations via UK IPO |
| Foreign Equivalent (state/federal courts) | UK IPO (tribunal) / High Court (Chancery Division) / Court of Session |
| Summary Judgment | Summary decree (Scotland) or summary judgment (England/Wales) |
| Attorney's fees (prevailing party) | Expenses (follow the event, loser pays) |

### Statutory Framework

| US Act | UK Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Lanham Act (15 USC) | Trade Marks Act 1994 (as amended) |
| - | Trade Mark Rules 2008 (SI 2008/1797) |
| State trademark laws | Not applicable, TM law is harmonised across UK (England, Scotland, Wales, NI) |
| - | EU Trade Mark Regulation (EU) 2017/1001 (for EUTMs) - post-Brexit: parallel UK rights |
| - | Common law (unregistered marks protected by passing off) |

### Registration and Enforcement in Scotland

- **UK IPO** covers all UK, including Scotland, no separate Scottish trademark register
- **Court of Session (Outer House)** has jurisdiction for Scottish TM infringement cases, Passing off claims (unregistered marks) are heard in Sheriff Court or Court of Session depending on value
- **Expenses**: Scottish practice, loser pays, with judicial discretion

### Key Differences from US Practice

| Aspect | US | UK/Scotland |
|---|---|---|
| Registration term | 10 years, renewable | 10 years, renewable |
| Use requirement | Must file use declaration (5-6 years) | Revocable for non-use after 5 continuous years |
| Constructive use | Filing date = nationwide priority | Filing date = UK priority from application |
| Incontestability (Section 15) | After 5 years' continuous use | No direct equivalent; vulnerability after 5 years | 
| Damages for infringement | Actual damages, profits, enhanced | Account of profits or damages (not both); no punitive |
| Injunction | Permanent injunction | Interdict (Scotland) / injunction (RUK) |
| Customs seizure | Record with CBP | UK IPO application to HMRC customs |
| Dilution | Federal dilution claims | No separate dilution claim; well-known marks protected |

### Scots Law Considerations

- **No punitive damages** in Scottish IP cases
- **Interdict** (not injunction) - available to prevent ongoing infringement
- **Expenses** follow the event, loser pays
- **Prescription** - 5-year prescriptive period for delictual (tort) claims relating to IP
- **Commission and diligence** - orders for recovery of documents and inspection
- **Execution**: signature formalities for Scottish entities, s.36 Companies Act 2006 (director + secretary or 2 directors); traditional seal no longer required, Crown preference in insolvency abolished in Scotland

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
