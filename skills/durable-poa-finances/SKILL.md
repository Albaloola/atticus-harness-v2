Drafts an execution-ready Continuing Power of Attorney under the Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000 covering financial and property matters, with mandatory OPG registration and certificate provider compliance.

[SCOTS: Note] This skill adapts the US Durable POA model for Scots law. The Scottish system is fundamentally different: (1) governed exclusively by the Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000 (no UPOAA equivalent); (2) requires mandatory registration with the Office of the Public Guardian (OPG) Scotland before use; (3) requires a certificate provider (solicitor, doctor, or registered mental health officer) to certify capacity and understanding; (4) uses a prescribed statutory form; (5) separates "continuing" (financial/property) from "welfare" (personal care) powers, these must be granted in separate documents unless combined in the prescribed form with both parts completed.

## Prerequisites

Collect before drafting:

1. **Granter's residence** - must be in Scotland (or intending to use in Scotland) - the Act applies to adults habitually resident in Scotland
2. **Granter identification** - full legal name, address, date of birth
3. **Attorney designation** - primary attorney (name, address, relationship); substitute attorney(s) if desired
4. **Scope** - general (unrestricted continuing powers) or limited (specific types of financial decisions, thresholds, property)
5. **Certificate provider** - identify a solicitor, registered medical practitioner, or approved mental health officer who can certify the granter understands the nature and extent of the POA
6. **Witnesses** - one witness required for granter's signature (and one for attorney's signature if separate signing)
7. **Activation** - Continuing POA can be used immediately if not expressly restricted to incapacity; note: once registered, it remains effective even if granter loses capacity (does not need to "spring" as in US)

## Workflow

### 1. Legal Framework Check

- The Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000 (the "Act") is the sole governing statute for Scottish POAs, The POA must be in (or substantially in) the prescribed form (Schedule 1 to the Act's regulations)
- There is no "state-by-state" variation, Scotland has a single national system, The OPG Scotland (part of Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service) administers registration, Two types under the Act: Continuing (financial/property) and Welfare (personal care)
- They can be in the same document but are separate powers with separate rules

### 2. Draft Document Sections

Assemble using the prescribed statutory form (or a form substantially to the same effect):

**A. Title & Granter Details**
- Title: "Continuing Power of Attorney" (or "Combined Continuing and Welfare Power of Attorney")
- Granter: full name, address, date of birth, Cognitive status: a statement that the granter is aged 16 or over and will understand the nature and consequences of granting the POA (to be certified by the certificate provider)

**B. Attorney Appointment**
- Primary attorney(s): full name, address. Can appoint joint attorneys (act jointly), joint and several (act independently), or with specified decision rules, Substitute attorney(s): who steps in if primary attorney(s) cannot act, Multiple attorneys: state whether they must act jointly, jointly and severally, or by majority

**C. Scope of Continuing Powers (Financial and Property)**
- **General**: "manage all the granter's financial, property and other affairs" (covers all matters the granter could manage if capable)
- **Limited**: specify exact matters, property transactions, bank accounts, investments, benefits collection, paying bills, etc.
- Unlike US POAs, Scottish continuing POAs do not typically use a detailed enumerated powers checklist, the general grant with optional limitations is standard

**D. Limitations and Conditions**
Optional restrictions (specify in the document):
- Prohibition on selling the granter's home without court consent, Limits on gifting (the Act imposes default gifting limits - £2,000 per year to spouse/partner; £200 to others unless excessive)
- Transaction value thresholds requiring OPG consent, No authority to make or revoke the granter's will (mandatory, attorney cannot create/alter a will)
- Restriction on exercising the granter's parental rights, Specific property or accounts excluded from the POA

**E. When Powers Can Be Exercised**
A continuing POA can be used:
- Immediately upon registration (unless the document specifically restricts to when the granter is incapable)
- There is no "springing" concept as in US law, the POA is registered from the start and remains effective regardless of capacity, If the granter wishes the attorney to act only when incapable, this must be stated (and the certificate provider must confirm the granter understands this restriction)

**F. Welfare Powers (if combined)**
If drafting a combined Continuing and Welfare POA:
- Welfare powers govern personal care decisions: medical treatment, care home placement, day-to-day care, communication, Separate rules for welfare: cannot be used unless the granter is incapable; subject to the general principles of the Act, Must be clear in the document which powers are continuing (financial) and which are welfare (personal)

**G. Certificate Provider Section**
The prescribed form includes a certificate to be completed by:
- A solicitor holding a current practising certificate (most common)
- A registered medical practitioner who has examined the granter, An approved mental health officer (AMHO)

The certificate confirms:
- The granter understood the nature and extent of the powers granted, The granter was not subjected to undue influence or fraud, The granter had capacity at the time of signing (and understands the restriction if a welfare-only or incapacity-only continuing POA)

**H. Execution Block**
- Granter signs in the presence of ONE witness (who must not be the attorney)
- Witness signs: name, address, signature, Attorney(s) must sign accepting appointment (witnessed, but separate witness recommended)
- Signature dates must be clear

**I. Registration**
- The completed POA must be registered with the OPG Scotland before it can be used (mandatory, even if the granter is still fully capable)
- Registration fee applies (currently £83 as of 2025/26 - verify)
- Submit via EPOAR (Electronic Power of Attorney Registration) or paper, OPG issues a Certificate of Registration, Registration takes approximately 4 to 6 weeks (longer if paper submission)
- The OPG maintains a public register, register entries are discoverable by third parties

### 3. Final Assembly

- Use the prescribed statutory form (Schedule 1) or a form substantially to the same effect, Numbered paragraphs for clarity, All fields completed, do not leave blank spaces (strike through unused options)
- Professional legal document formatting, Include a covering letter with registration instructions

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **Registration is mandatory** - a continuing POA cannot be used until registered with the OPG. This is a fundamental difference from US DPOAs
- **Certificate provider**: must be available to sign the certificate. If the granter is already losing capacity, the certificate is essential, cannot be completed later
- **Witnessing rules**: the witness cannot be the attorney. The granter must sign before the certificate provider certifies (certificate can be dated after or on the same date)
- **Do not confuse with welfare POA** - welfare powers (personal care, medical decisions) are separate. The same form can grant both but they operate under different rules
- **Registration delays**: allow 4 to 8 weeks for OPG registration. For urgent transactions, consider alternative temporary arrangements
- **Joint attorneys**: if appointed jointly, all must act together. This can be impractical. Joint and several appointments are more flexible
- **OPG supervision**: the OPG may supervise the attorney after registration and can investigate if concerns arise
- **HMRC / DWP**: separate notification may be needed for HMRC, DWP, and pension providers (the POA itself does not grant automatic authority to deal with these bodies)
- **Revocation**: properly executed revocation must be sent to the OPG (registered) and all known third parties. Revocation of a registered POA requires OPG recording
- **Cross-border**: if the granter moves to England or abroad, the Scottish POA may not be recognised, separate advice needed
- **No IRS Form 2848 equivalent**: Scottish POAs do not automatically confer HMRC agency authority, note this limitation

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Statutory framework:**
- Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000 (asp 4) - Parts 2 (Continuing POA) and 3 (Welfare POA)
- The Adults with Incapacity (Procedure) (Scotland) Regulations 2018 (SSI 2018/133)
- Office of the Public Guardian (OPG) Scotland, Practice Notes, Mental Health (Care and Treatment) (Scotland) Act 2003 (s. 326 - approved mental health officers)
- Solicitors (Scotland) Act 1980 - certificate providers, Adults with Incapacity (Public Guardian's Fees) Regulations, current fee schedule

**Key differences from US Durable POA:**

| US DPOA | Scottish Continuing POA |
|---|---|
| State-specific (UPOAA generally but variations) | Single national system for Scotland |
| Notarisation required in most states | Witness only (no notary required; but notary can also witness) |
| Springing POA common | Must specify if restricted to incapacity; default is immediate use upon registration |
| IRS Form 2848 needed separately for tax | HMRC has separate own procedures not covered by POA |
| Recording in county land records (real estate) | OPG registration only; no separate county recording (but Land Register needs to be notified of attorney acting) |
| Agent = "attorney-in-fact" | Agent = "attorney" (different usage in Scotland) |
| Health + financial often combined | Continuing (financial) and Welfare (personal) are legally separate, can be in one form but operate under different rules |
| $15k annual gift exclusion (tax-driven) | £2,000/year to spouse/partner; £200 otherwise (unless excessive, see Act s. 3(4)) |
| Creates fiduciary relationship | Equivalent fiduciary duties under common law and the Act's general principles |
| No registration requirement | OPG registration is mandatory; POA is void without registration |
| Typically drafted by any attorney | Should be drafted or reviewed by a Scottish solicitor; prescribed form is complex |

**General principles of the Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000:**
- Benefit: any intervention must benefit the adult, Least restrictive: must be the least restrictive option possible, Take account of the adult's past and present wishes, Consult relevant others (carers, relatives)
- Encourage the adult to exercise existing skills where possible

**Forms and resources (download to scots-forms/):**
- OPG Continuing Power of Attorney prescribed form (Schedule 1 to the 2018 Regulations)
- OPG Welfare Power of Attorney prescribed form, OPG Certificate Provider guidance notes, EPOAR online registration documentation, OPG guidance notes for attorneys

**Practitioner notes:**
- Only a Scottish solicitor can advise on the validity of a Scottish POA, The POA must be registered before it can be used, plan for registration delay, Financial institutions (banks, building societies) in Scotland are accustomed to Scottish Continuing POAs and generally accept registered documents, If the granter already lacks capacity, guardianship through the Sheriff Court is the only option (not POA)
- Cross-border issues: a Scottish POA is not automatically recognised in England/Wales/NI (and vice versa)
- HMRC agent authorisation is separate from the POA, note this to clients, The OPG can provide "Information and Advice" to attorneys on request
