# Related Party Transaction Policy, UK

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a Related Party Transaction Policy with Audit Committee governance, Companies Act 2006-compliant disclosure, and fiduciary duty safeguards under UK company law.

## Prerequisites

1. **Company profile** - legal name, registered number, place of incorporation (England & Wales / Scotland / Northern Ireland), public/private status, premium/standard/SEG listing (if listed)
2. **Governance documents** - articles of association, audit committee terms of reference, directors' conflicts-of-interest policy
3. **Existing related party relationships** - current or anticipated related party arrangements
4. **Dollar threshold (GBP)** - internal approval threshold (common: £100K, £250K, or £500K; SEC/Item 404 floor replaced by Companies Act and DTR thresholds)

## Quick Start

1. Collect prerequisites above
2. Draft policy sections in order below
3. Calibrate thresholds, Companies Act s. 412 sets disclosure of directors' benefits; DTR 7.3 (for listed companies) sets related party transaction rules; set internal pre-approval trigger at an appropriate level
4. Cross-reference audit committee terms of reference to avoid conflicting governance authority
5. Format as 4 to 7 page board-adoptable document with signature lines

## Policy Sections

### 1. Header & Purpose

- Title: `[Company Legal Name] Related Party Transaction Policy and Procedures`
- Purpose: transparency, conflict prevention, Companies Act compliance, shareholder protection, UK Corporate Governance Code compliance, Include effective date, version number, adoption authority (full Board)

### 2. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Related Party** | Directors (including shadow directors), persons discharging managerial responsibilities (PDMRs), their connected persons (s.252 to 253 Companies Act 2006), and persons with significant control (PSC Register, s.790C Companies Act 2006) |
| **Connected Person** | Spouse/civil partner, children/step-children under 18, body corporate in which the director has a "material interest", trustees of certain trusts (s.253 to 255 Companies Act 2006) |
| **Related Party Transaction** | A transaction, arrangement, or agreement (including loans, quasi-loans, credit transactions) in which a Related Party has a direct or indirect material interest |
| **Material Interest** | Excludes: interests arising solely from a directorship; de minimis transactions below applicable thresholds; and transactions with directors on normal commercial arm's-length terms |

Covered transaction examples: substantial property transactions (s.190), loans to directors (s.197 to 214), service contracts of more than 2 years (s.188), payments for loss of office (s.215 to 222), and any arrangement exceeding the applicable threshold.

### 3. Identification Procedures

- **Individual duty**: Directors and PDMRs must disclose potential RPTs (including proposed service contracts, property transactions, loans) to the Audit Committee Chair and Company Secretary before any binding commitment, s.177 Companies Act 2006 (duty to declare interest)
- **Institutional controls**: Finance, Legal, and Internal Audit monitor vendor relationships, significant contracts, expense reports, investment activities
- **Annual questionnaires**: All directors and PDMRs disclose related party relationships and connected persons

### 4. Audit Committee Review & Approval

- **Exclusive authority**: Audit Committee approves; interested parties recuse from deliberation and voting; independent director approval required for listed companies
- **Submission materials**: transaction terms, nature of interest, business purpose, arm's-length terms analysis, ordinary course assessment
- **Standard**: Committee determines in good faith the transaction is **fair and reasonable** and in the company's and shareholders' best interests; for listed companies, DTR 7.3 requires independent director approval
- **Ongoing RPTs**: Annual re-review required; Committee may set standing guidelines for recurring arrangements
- **Shareholder approval**: Where Companies Act requires member approval (substantial property transactions, loans, ss.190, 197), ensure committee approval precedes shareholder vote

### 5. Pre-Approved Categories

| Category | Condition |
|---|---|
| Executive director compensation | Board or Remuneration Committee approved per s.420 to 422 (DIR report) |
| Non-executive director fees | Board approved on recommendation of Remuneration Committee |
| Transactions with wholly-owned subsidiaries | Internal, no shareholder notification required |
| Transactions below de minimis threshold | Below Audit Committee threshold or DTR de minimis level |
| Transactions on normal market terms | Arm's-length, standard customer/supplier arrangements |
| Transactions already approved by shareholder vote | Where Companies Act s.190 et seq. conditions met |

### 6. Disclosure Obligations (Listed Companies)

- Listed companies must comply with **DTR 7.3** (Related Party Transactions) - requires independent director review and market announcement via RIS, Directors' remuneration disclosures per **s.412 and Schedule 5, Companies Act 2006** - annual report and accounts
- **UK Corporate Governance Code 2024, Provision 10**: transactions with related parties exceeding 5% of net assets or 1% of revenue must be brought to the Board and subject to independent review, Annual report: directors' remuneration report (s.420 to 422) and strategic report disclosures on related party transactions, Company Secretary and CFO own accuracy, completeness, and timeliness of disclosures, Audit Committee reviews RPT disclosures before annual report inclusion, Consider full Board reporting regardless of public disclosure requirement

### 7. Administration & Enforcement

| Topic | Provision |
|---|---|
| Administrator | Audit Committee |
| Annual review | Committee reassesses; recommends amendments to Board |
| Material amendments | Require Board approval; disclose per law and DTR requirements |
| Non-compliance | Disciplinary action up to termination; rescission, modification, or ratification of unapproved transactions; disgorgement of profits; breach of director duties (s.171 to 177 Companies Act 2006) |

### 8. Formatting

- Numbered sections with descriptive headings, Page headers: company name + policy title; footers: page numbers, Signature lines for Audit Committee Chair and Company Secretary, Cross-references to Audit Committee terms of reference, Directors' Conflicts Policy, Share Dealing Code, Corporate Governance statement

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **Public vs. private**: DTR 7.3 and UK Corporate Governance Code apply only to listed companies. Private companies must still comply with Companies Act 2006 requirements (ss. 177, 182 to 187 for directors' interests; ss. 190 to 214 for substantial property transactions and loans).
- **FCA Listing Rules**: The FCA has proposed moving RPT requirements from DTR 7.3 to the new UK Listing Rules, verify current rule number `[VERIFY]`.
- **Company law (Scotland)**: Companies Act 2006 applies throughout the UK including Scotland. Scottish registered companies have the same obligations. Registration at Companies House Edinburgh (for Scottish companies) / Cardiff (for E&W).
- **Directors' duties**: Part 10 Companies Act 2006 (ss. 171 to 177) codifies directors' duties including the duty to avoid conflicts of interest. The entire fairness standard is not imported from US Delaware law, UK company law applies a different framework (duty of loyalty and duty of care, s.172 promoting success for shareholders).
- **Definition scope**: Consider extending Related Party to entities where covered persons hold ≥10% equity or serve as director. Ensure alignment with DTR 7.3 definitions for consistency.
- **Ratification**: Address whether Audit Committee can ratify transactions entered without prior approval, and under what conditions, note s.180(4) of Companies Act 2006 and common law position.
- **Charter consistency**: Policy terminology and authority must not conflict with existing Audit Committee terms of reference or articles of association.

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

- **Governing Statute**: Companies Act 2006 (c. 46) - applies throughout the UK including Scotland. No separate Scottish company statute, registered companies in Scotland are governed by the same Act.
- **Key Sections**: s. 177 (duty to declare interest), s. 182 (declaration of interest in existing transaction), s. 190 (substantial property transactions), s. 197 (loans to directors), s. 412 / Schedule 5 (disclosure of directors' benefits), Part 16 (audit and accounts).
- **Regulatory Framework**: FCA Disclosure Guidance and Transparency Rules (DTR 7.3) for listed companies; UK Corporate Governance Code 2024 (comply or explain basis); replaced US SEC Item 404/Reg S-K.
- **No $120K threshold**: The US SEC Item 404(a) $120K threshold has no direct UK equivalent. DTR 7.3 uses a proportionate test; UK Corporate Governance Code Provision 10 uses percentage-based tests (5% net assets / 1% revenue).
- **Directors' Duties**: UK has a codified directors' duties framework (Part 10 CA 2006) rather than the US corporate law fiduciary duties (which vary by state of incorporation). No Delaware-law entire fairness test.
- **Currency**: GBP (£) replaces USD ($). All thresholds should be in pounds sterling.
- **Terminology**: US term → UK equivalent: SEC → FCA; SEC Item 404/Reg S-K → DTR 7.3 / Companies Act 2006; Audit Committee → Same term (UK usage); NYSE/Nasdaq → FCA Premium/Standard/SEG listing segments; 8-K filing → RIS announcement / annual report; General Counsel → Company Secretary; Delaware law → Companies Act 2006 / UK Corporate Governance Code.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
