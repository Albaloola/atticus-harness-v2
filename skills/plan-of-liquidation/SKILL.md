# Plan of Winding Up and Dissolution, Scotland/UK Adaptation

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a formal plan for winding down a UK company (registered in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK) - satisfying creditors, distributing assets, and achieving legal dissolution under the Insolvency Act 1986 and Companies Act 2006.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

1. **Company formation documents** - certificate of incorporation, memorandum and articles of association, register of members
2. **Board resolution** - approving winding up with vote count, quorum, date
3. **Member approval** - minutes or written resolution showing requisite vote threshold met (75% for special resolution)
4. **Creditor inventory** - known creditors with addresses, amounts, secured/unsecured status
5. **Asset inventory** - heritable property, equipment, IP, receivables, investments, cash
6. **Pending obligations** - litigation, contracts, employee entitlements (redundancy, notice, pension), environmental liabilities
7. **Insolvency status** - solvent (MVL) or insolvent (CVL); statutory declaration of solvency required for MVL

## Quick Start

1. Confirm board and member approvals are documented
2. Identify governing statute (Insolvency Act 1986; Companies Act 2006)
3. Draft plan sections in order below
4. Mark all jurisdiction-specific items with [VERIFY]
5. Use bracketed placeholders for dates and amounts the client must confirm
6. Appoint an authorised insolvency practitioner (IP) as liquidator (required for formal liquidation)

## Plan Sections

### 1. Preamble & Recitals

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Company identity | Full legal name, registered number, state of registration (Scotland/England & Wales/NI), registered office |
| Statutory authority | Cite Insolvency Act 1986, Companies Act 2006 [VERIFY] |
| Director determination | Winding up serves best interests of company's creditors/members |
| Business rationale | Brief factual basis, avoid detail creating liability exposure |

### 2. Approval & Authorization (Resolutions)

- **MVL:** Special resolution of members (75% majority); statutory declaration of solvency under IA 1986 s. 89
- **CVL:** Special resolution of members (75% majority); creditors' meeting under IA 1986 s. 98
- Board meeting: date, vote count, quorum confirmed, Written resolution: document compliance with Companies Act 2006
- Appointment of liquidator: named IP with IAA (Insolvency Appointments) number

### 3. Creditor & Stakeholder Notice

| Notice Type | Requirements |
|-------------|-------------|
| Known creditors | Written notice; creditor meeting (CVL under s. 98); statement of affairs; claim deadline |
| Unknown creditors | Gazette notice (Edinburgh Gazette for Scottish companies) |
| Employees | Redundancy notices; protective award if applicable; pension trustee consultation |
| Government | HMRC; DWP; Companies House; relevant regulatory bodies |
| Other | Customers, suppliers, landlords, contract counterparties |

Timeline must comply with Insolvency Act 1986 and Insolvency (Scotland) Rules 2016.

### 4. Liquidation Process

**Priority waterfall (Insolvency Act 1986, s. 175; Schedule 6):**

1. Expenses of the winding up
2. Preferential debts (employees: up to £800 per employee; pensions; certain HMRC deductions from employees)
3. Prescribed part for unsecured creditors (where floating charge exists) - IA 1986 s. 176A
4. Floating charge holder
5. Unsecured creditors (pari passu)
6. Deferred debts (e.g., loans by members in lieu of equity)
7. Members (if any surplus)

**Wind-down checklist:**

- [ ] Complete or terminate existing contracts
- [ ] Collect accounts receivable
- [ ] Realise assets (heritable and moveable)
- [ ] Resolve pending litigation
- [ ] Address IP assets (assignment, abandonment, licensing)
- [ ] Establish reserves for contingent/disputed/unliquidated claims
- [ ] Liquidator's progress reports to creditors and Companies House
- [ ] Final account and return of final meeting

### 5. Asset Distribution

- Per-share calculation methodology for each class of shares, Liquidation preferences and participation rights per articles (if any)
- Distribution timeline, In-kind distributions: valuation method and allocation, Tax treatment: company gains/losses; shareholder treatment under TCGA 1992
- Treatment of undistributable assets (charitable donation etc.)

### 6. Dissolution Filings

| Filing | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Notice of appointment of liquidator | Companies House (LIQ02 for MVL; LIQ01 for CVL) |
| Statement of affairs | CVL: s. 99 IA 1986; within 11 days |
| Annual progress reports | Insolvency (Scotland) Rules 2016 / Insolvency Rules 2016 |
| Final account | Notice to Companies House and creditors |
| Application for dissolution | Companies House (DS02 form for striking off) |
| Companies House strike off | Final dissolution via Registrar's action |

Obtain and retain certified copies of all filed documents.

### 7. Tax & Compliance

- **HMRC** - notify of liquidation; submit outstanding returns (CT600, RTI/PAYE, VAT)
- **Final corporation tax return** - mark as final
- **Employment taxes** - P45s, P11D, final RTI submission
- **VAT** - deregister and submit final return
- **CGT** - notify of asset disposals, Cancel PAYE scheme, VAT registration, VAT registration number
- **Record retention** - maintain statutory records (company books, tax records) for applicable period (6 years min.)

### 8. Effective Date & Execution

- Liquidation effective from date of special resolution, Liquidation signed off on final dissolution by Registrar, Signature blocks: Director(s) and Secretary (or equivalent officers)
- Certification of authority and accuracy

## Common Pitfalls

- **Jurisdiction variance** - Scottish companies follow Insolvency (Scotland) Rules 2016 (different from England & Wales)
- **Wrongful trading** - IA 1986 s. 214 - directors must cease trading when insolvent; personal liability risk
- **Fiduciary exposure** - demonstrate compliance with director duties under Companies Act 2006 ss. 172 to 177
- **Connected persons** - transactions at undervalue and preferences have longer look-back periods (2 years for connected)
- **Scottish IP requirement** - only IPs registered with recognised professional body (ICAS, ACCA, IPA etc.)
- **Prescribed part** - mandatory ring-fencing of assets for unsecured creditors where floating charge exists
- **Edinburgh Gazette** - Scottish companies must give notice in the Edinburgh Gazette (not London Gazette)
- **Speculative timelines** - never include concrete dates without client confirmation; use bracketed placeholders
- **Heritable property (Scotland)** - registered in the Land Register of Scotland; transfer/disposal requires Land Register entries
- **Pensions** - auto-enrolment duties must be addressed; pension trustee must be notified

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Adapted for:** Scotland and UK law
**Changes made:**
- US Corporation → UK company (Ltd, plc)
- State of incorporation → Registration in Scotland, England & Wales, or Northern Ireland, DGCL § 275, RMBCA § 14.02 → Insolvency Act 1986; Companies Act 2006
- Board/shareholder → Board/member approval (special resolution, 75%)
- Secretary of State dissolution filing → Companies House; Registrar of Companies, IRS → HMRC, Form 966 (IRS) → No UK equivalent, HMRC notification via different mechanism, Form 1120 → Corporation Tax Return (CT600)
- IRC § 331 → TCGA 1992 (shareholder capital gains treatment)
- WARN Act → UK collective redundancy consultation (TULRCA 1992, s. 188; 30+ employees, 30/45 day minimum)
- ERISA → Pensions Act 2004; Pension Protection Fund (PPF); auto-enrolment duties, State dissolution statute → Insolvency Act 1986 + Companies Act 2006 (for solvent strike-off)
- Tax clearance certificate → HMRC clearance if required for dissolution (some cases)
- Secretary of State → Companies House (Registrar of Companies for Scotland)

**Key Scottish/UK considerations:**
- Scottish companies use Insolvency (Scotland) Rules 2016, not the England & Wales Insolvency Rules, MVL requires statutory declaration of solvency (directors' personal liability if false)
- Liquidator must be an authorised insolvency practitioner (IP) - cannot self-liquidate, Preferential creditors: employee claims up to £800 per employee; certain HMRC debts from 2020 reforms, Prescribed part: ring-fenced portion of floating charge realisations for unsecured creditors (IA 1986 s. 176A)
- Edinburgh Gazette notice required for Scottish companies, Heritable property transfer must comply with Land Registration (Scotland) Act 2012
- Wrongful trading (IA 1986 s. 214) applies; directors face personal liability, Disqualification of directors (Company Directors Disqualification Act 1986)
- Post-liquidation: Registrar of Companies dissolves the company (usually 3 months after final accounting)
- Scottish companies can also apply for strike-off under Companies Act 2006 if solvent and meeting criteria

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.

- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
