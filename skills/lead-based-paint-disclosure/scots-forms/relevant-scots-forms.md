# Scotland/UK Forms & References for Lead-Based Paint Disclosure

## Scottish Housing Forms

| Form / Document | Purpose |
|-----------------|---------|
| Home Report (Scotland) | Mandatory pre-sale pack including Single Survey, EPC, Property Questionnaire |
| Property Questionnaire (Home Report) | Seller's disclosure of matters affecting the property |
| Single Survey (RIAS) | Condition survey by qualified surveyor |
| EPC (Energy Performance Certificate) | Energy assessment; valid 10 years |
| Section 11 Notice (Housing (Scotland) Act 2006) | Landlord's Repairing Standard notice |
| Repairing Standard | Minimum condition standard for private rented sector |
| Standard Security | Scottish mortgage deed |
| Missives | Binding sale contract (exchange of letters via solicitors) |

## Key Scottish Legislation

- **Housing (Scotland) Act 2006 (ss. 12-19 & ss. 71-73)** - Repairing Standard and Tolerable Standard; property must be wind and watertight, free from rising damp
- **Housing (Scotland) Act 2001** - Scottish Secure Tenancy terms, Right to Repair
- **Environmental Protection Act 1990 (Part IIA)** - Contaminated land (lead contamination triggers investigation)
- **Building (Scotland) Act 2003 & Building Standards Regs** - Construction/renovation standards re: lead
- **Consumer Protection from Unfair Trading Regulations 2008** - misrepresentation / omission of material defects in sale
- **Consumer Rights Act 2015** - goods/services quality standards

## UK-Wide Context

- **No equivalent to US federal lead-paint disclosure law** in UK or Scotland, Lead paint is not specifically addressed as a mandatory disclosure item in Scottish residential property transactions, The **Property Questionnaire** (part of Home Report) asks about alterations/extensions, damp/condensation, timber/damp specialist reports, but NOT specifically lead paint
- **The Tolerable Standard** (s.71 Housing (Scotland) Act 2006) includes: "The house must be free from lead" - but this relates to lead **pipes** and drinking water, not decorative lead paint, For **private rented sector**: Repairing Standard (s.12 Housing (Scotland) Act 2006) requires property be wind and watertight, free from rising/chronic damp; again, no specific lead paint requirement
- **Housing HAZ** (Housing Assessment Zone) - Scottish equivalent of HHSRS; assesses hazards including toxic substances. Lead paint may be classified as a Category 1 / Category 2 hazard
- **Practice:** sellers generally rely on **caveat emptor** (buyer beware) for latent defects; but active concealment of known lead hazard could found **delictual** claim (negligent misrepresentation)
- **Caveat:** solicitors may include specific enquiries about lead paint if transaction involves pre-1960s property with known history of lead paint use

## Comparable Scottish Hazards Disclosure Regimes

| Issue | Disclosure Required? | Source |
|-------|---------------------|--------|
| Japanese knotweed | Yes, in Property Questionnaire | Home Report Property Questionnaire |
| Damp/timber defects | Yes, if known | Property Questionnaire |
| Lead pipes | Indirectly, Tolerable Standard | Housing (Scotland) Act 2006 s.71 |
| Lead paint (decorative) | Not specifically | Caveat emptor generally applies |
| Asbestos | Yes, if known and if survey exists | Property Q + common law |
| Electrical safety | PRS only, Repairing Standard | Housing (Scotland) Act 2006 s.13 |
