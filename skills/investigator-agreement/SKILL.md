# Investigator Agreement, Scotland/UK

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a legally enforceable agreement governing the sponsor to principal investigator relationship for MHRA-regulated clinical research in the UK (covering Scotland, England, Wales, and Northern Ireland).

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

1. **Protocol** - number, version, phase, design, investigational medicinal product (IMP), indication, enrolment target, endpoints, special populations
2. **Parties** - sponsor type (pharma/biotech/device/academic/NHS), CRO if any, PI credentials (GMC / NMC number), institution legal name and type, hospital Trust / Health Board, relevant constituent country
3. **Contracting structure** - PI individually (NHS/non-NHS), institution as primary with PI signatory, or joint; note Scotland-specific requirements
4. **Site personnel** - sub-investigators, study coordinators, research nurses, pharmacy staff
5. **Prior agreements/templates** - mCTA (model Clinical Trial Agreement) per devolved administration; mandatory provisions (NHS indemnification); institutional policies
6. **Budget** - per-subject, per-procedure, or hybrid; fair market value basis; NIHR AcoRD compliance
7. **Single-site vs. multi-centre** - affects publication rights, data ownership, oversight
8. **Sponsor type** - commercial vs. non-commercial (academic/NHS, affects indemnity and contract terms)

## Agreement Sections

### 1. Recitals & Definitions

- Full legal names and roles for all parties, Define: Protocol, Investigational Medicinal Product (IMP), Study Site, Confidential Information, Study Data, Serious Adverse Event (SAE), Suspected Unexpected Serious Adverse Reaction (SUSAR), Effective Date, Study Completion

### 2. Investigator Obligations

Key regulatory obligations to address:

| Obligation | Regulatory Basis | Core Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Protocol compliance | SI 2004/1031, reg. 28 / The Medicines for Human Use (Clinical Trials) Regulations 2004 | Absolute; deviations only for immediate hazard; report within 3 days |
| Delegation | ICH E6(R2) §4.2 | Signed delegation log (site signature sheet); PI retains overall responsibility |
| IMP accountability | SI 2004/1031, reg. 46 / Annex 13 | Receipt, storage, dispensing, return/destruction records |
| Informed consent | SI 2004/1031, Schedule 1, Part 2, para. 1; ICH E6(R2) §4.8 | Written consent before any study procedure; current REC-approved form; GCP binder |
| Adults with incapacity | Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000 (if site in Scotland); Mental Capacity Act 2005 (E&W) | Additional protections for vulnerable adults, written authority or certificate required in Scotland |
| Children | Age of Legal Capacity (Scotland) Act 1991 / Children Act 1989 | Child assent and parental consent required in parallel |

### 3. REC (Research Ethics Committee) Governance

- **Recognised body**: REC recognised by UKECA (UK Ethics Committee Authority) for CTIMP review
- **Scotland A REC**: where trial is conducted wholly or partly in Scotland and CI is professionally based in Scotland, the Scotland A REC is the allocated committee
- **Initial submission**: Complete package before any subject contact; includes HRA approval (if in England/Wales)
- **Annual progress reports**: Per REC requirements (usually annually from date of favourable opinion)
- **Amendments**: REC + MHRA approval before implementation; exception for immediate hazard (document, notify within 3 days)
- **Reporting**: SUSARs, SAEs (unexpected + possibly related), urgent safety measures, protocol deviations
- **Lapse/expiry**: Cease all activities for lapsed REC approval
- **Document flow**: REC submissions/correspondence to sponsor within 5 business days

### 4. Data Integrity & Documentation

- Apply **ALCOA+ principles** (Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate + Available, Consistent, Enduring, Traceable)
- All CRF entries verifiable against source documents, Corrections: single line-through (paper) or audit trail (EDC); date, initial, reason, Essential documents per ICH E6(R2) §8 - investigator's brochure, signed protocol, REC correspondence, site signature log, signed ICFs, delegation log, IMP records, protocol versions, laboratory accreditation certificates
- **Retention**: longest of, MHRA required period (usually 5 years post-trial, 15 years for paediatric trials), institutional policy, or NHS records retention schedule; notify sponsor before destruction
- **UK GDPR compliance**: Clinical trial data processing under UK GDPR, lawful basis (public task / legitimate interest / consent depending on context); Article 89 safeguards for research

### 5. Adverse Event Reporting

| Category | Timeline | Recipient |
|---|---|---|
| All AEs | Document regardless of severity/causality | Study file |
| SAEs | Within 24 hours of awareness | Sponsor |
| SUSARs (Suspected Unexpected Serious Adverse Reactions) | Fatal/life-threatening: 7 days; other: 15 days | MHRA + REC |
| Urgent safety measures | Within 3 days | MHRA + REC |
| Annual safety report | Once per calendar year | REC + MHRA |

### 6. Monitoring & Inspection

- **Sponsor monitoring**: Business hours; reasonable notice (≥5 business days except urgent); direct source document access; PI responds promptly to findings
- **Regulatory inspections**: Notify sponsor within 24 hours of MHRA/REC authority contact; cooperate fully; copy MHRA inspection report within 5 business days
- **HRA inspection (England/Wales)**: HRA may inspect for research governance compliance
- **Corrective actions**: Collaborative development with sponsor; documented implementation

### 7. Confidentiality & IP

- **Scope**: Protocol, IB, CRFs, study data, product formulation, regulatory strategy, all non-public sponsor information
- **Duration**: Study term + [3 to 5] years post-termination or until publicly available
- **Permitted disclosures**: REC, MHRA, HRA, study personnel (bound by equivalent obligations), legal counsel, UK GDPR data subjects on request
- **Return/destruction**: Upon request or termination; retain one regulatory copy; certify deletion
- **Data ownership**: Sponsor owns all study data, results, inventions; survives termination
- **Biological samples**: Sponsor-owned; subject to Human Tissue Act 2004 (England/Wales) or Human Tissue (Scotland) Act 2006

### 8. Publication Rights

- Eligible after study completion + database lock + primary analyses
- **Review period**: Submit to sponsor [45 to 60] days before submission/presentation
- **Sponsor rights**: Remove confidential information; delay [60 to 90] days for patent filing; request accuracy modifications
- **Anti-suppression**: Sponsor may not suppress legitimate scientific findings; independent expert dispute resolution
- **Authorship**: Per ICMJE guidelines; CONSORT recommendations

### 9. Financial Terms

- **Compensation**: Per-subject/per-procedure/hybrid; attach itemised budget as exhibit
- **Fair market value**: Based on time, effort, complexity, qualifications, local rates; Bribery Act 2010 compliant
- **Invoicing**: [Monthly/quarterly]; payment within [30 to 45] days of compliant invoice
- **Adjustments**: Screen failures at [specified rate]; early withdrawals pro rata; no payment for unusable data from PI non-compliance
- **Reimbursable costs**: Protocol-required labs/imaging, pharmacy fees, IP storage, subject stipends/travel
- **Financial disclosure**: PI/sub-investigators disclose interests per GCP requirements; ABPI Code of Practice disclosure if applicable

### 10. Indemnification & Insurance

**NHS sites** (most common in Scotland, NHS Health Boards):
- NHS indemnity covers clinical negligent harm for NHS-employed staff; does not cover product defects, Commercial sponsor indemnifies for: protocol design, IMP manufacture/defects, product-related injuries when used per protocol, NHS Research Scotland (NRS) Permissions: standard indemnity provisions for non-commercial studies

**Non-NHS / private sites**:

| Party | Coverage |
|---|---|
| Sponsor (clinical trial liability) | £[5 to 10]M per claim / annual aggregate |
| PI/Institution (professional liability/PII) | £[1 to 3]M per claim (MPS/MDU or institutional indemnity) |

- Certificates of insurance/PII required; 14-day claims notice; indemnifying party controls defence
- **Scotland-specific**: Clinical negligence claims against NHS Health Boards follow separate procedure (NHS Scotland cannot indemnify beyond clinical negligence, product liability covered by sponsor)

### 11. Term & Termination

| Trigger | Notice | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Study completion | N/A | Ends after closeout complete |
| Sponsor convenience | [30 to 60] days written | Payment through termination + wind-down costs |
| Sponsor for cause | Immediate | May seek damages; may report to MHRA/GMC |
| PI, sponsor breach | 30-day cure period | Effective after cure period |
| PI, sponsor insurance lapse | Written notice | Effective upon notice |

**Wind-down**: PI ceases enrolment, notifies subjects, completes CRFs, returns IMP/confidential info, submits final REC report. Sponsor pays for completed services and reasonable wind-down costs.

### 12. Governing Law & Disputes

- Governing law: Scottish law (if site is in Scotland) or English law (if site is in England/Wales) - depending on contracting structure, Escalation: project managers → legal/commercial directors → mediation (shared costs) → Court of Session (Scotland) or High Court (E&W)
- Jurisdiction: Scottish courts for Scottish sites; English courts for E&W sites

### 13. Representations & Warranties

- **Sponsor**: Authority to contract; valid MHRA clinical trial authorisation (CTA); IB contains all known safety information; will maintain insurance; IMP manufactured per GMP
- **PI**: Authority to contract; qualified and licensed (GMC/NMC); disclosed all relevant interests; adequate facilities/personnel; not debarred from clinical research (MHRA check)
- **Disclaimer**: No implied warranties; IMP is investigational

### 14. Compliance Provisions

- **Anti-corruption**: Bribery Act 2010 - s. 7 (failure to prevent bribery) strict liability; ABPI Code of Practice if applicable
- **Sanctions**: PI not on UK sanctions list (HMT); no export of study data to restricted parties
- **Healthcare compliance**: Bribery Act 2010 compliance; GMC guidance on gifts; NHS research governance framework

### 15. Execution & Exhibits

- **Signature blocks**: For NHS sites, authorised Health Board signatory (Research & Development Director or delegated signatory); PI signatory for personal obligations
- **Scotland-specific**: NHS Health Board R&D approval required before recruitment
- **Startup timeline**: Allow 6 to 12 weeks for NHS R&D review, contract negotiations, and HRA approval (if England)
- **Exhibits**: A: Protocol, B: Budget/ID Card, C: Personnel List/Site Signature Log, D: PI CV, E: Financial Disclosures, F: Institutional Policies, G: Pharmacy Template (if applicable)
- **Amendments**: Material changes require written amendment signed by both parties; REC+/MHRA notification

### Regulatory Framework

- **Primary UK legislation**: The Medicines for Human Use (Clinical Trials) Regulations 2004 (SI 2004/1031) - as amended (including the 2026 reforms effective 28 April 2026)
- **Scotland-specific**: The Adults with Incapacity (Scotland) Act 2000 governs consent for adults unable to consent for clinical trials; the Human Tissue (Scotland) Act 2006 governs tissue storage/use
- **UK post-Brexit**: The UK operates an independent regulatory regime for CTIMPs; MHRA is the competent authority; the Windsor Framework applies to NI
- **HRA (Health Research Authority)**: Manages REC governance for England/Wales; NHS research governance for England. In Scotland, NHS Research Scotland (NRS) coordinates approvals.
- **NHS indemnity**: For NHS Health Board sites in Scotland, clinical negligence liability rests with the Health Board; the sponsor bears product liability
- **mCTA**: Model Clinical Trial Agreement templates exist for commercial and non-commercial trials; Scotland uses a variation agreed by NHS Research Scotland and Scottish Health Boards

### Practitioner Notes

- Scotland has separate legislation for adults with incapacity (not the MCA 2005 which applies in E&W); ensure the correct Act is referenced for Scottish sites, The Scottish Government operates NHS Research Scotland (NRS) for permissions, contracts, and co-ordination, Clinical trials in Scottish NHS sites require R&D approval from the relevant Health Board R&D office, UK GDPR applies uniformly across the UK; clinical trial participants should receive a privacy notice compliant with UK GDPR Art. 13/14
- Legal fees: loser-pays principle (judicial expenses) in the Court of Session; arbitration often preferred, The ABPI Code of Practice (voluntary code) covers pharmaceutical industry interactions with healthcare professionals, applicable to UK-based pharma sponsors

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
