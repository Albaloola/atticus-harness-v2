# Donation Agreement, Scotland/UK

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

Formalises a charitable transfer from donor to a Scottish charity with HMRC Gift Aid compliance, OSCR regulatory provisions, and protections for both parties. Target 5 to 10 pages; complex endowment or heritable property gifts may run longer.

[SCOTS: Note] This skill adapts the US 501(c)(3) donation agreement model for the Scottish/UK charitable sector. Key structural differences: (1) Scottish charities are registered with OSCR (Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator), not the IRS; (2) the principal tax incentive is Gift Aid (25% basic rate uplift) rather than US itemised deduction; (3) Scottish charitable status is governed by the Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005; (4) cy-pres is recognised in Scots law but under different statutory provisions.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

- **Donor** - legal name, address, entity type (individual/company/trust), NI number or UTR
- **Recipient** - legal name, address, OSCR charity number, HMRC charity reference
- **Donation** - cash amount or non-cash property description with market value
- **Appraisal** - qualified independent valuation if non-cash > £500 (for reporting purposes); formal valuation recommended for heritable property
- **Gift Aid eligibility** - donor must be UK taxpayer; confirm donor has paid sufficient Income/Capital Gains Tax
- **Restrictions** - unrestricted, purpose-restricted, or endowed; conditions (matching funds, naming rights)
- **Governing law** - Scots law (for Scottish charities) or the law of another UK jurisdiction

## Document Structure

### 1. Parties & Recitals

- Full legal names, addresses, entity types for Donor and Recipient, Recipient's OSCR registration number and HMRC charity reference, Recipient's legal form: SCIO (Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisation) / charitable company / trust / unincorporated association, Recitals: charitable intent, mission alignment, donation purpose

### 2. Description of Donation

| Asset Type | Required Detail |
|---|---|
| Cash | Amount (numerals + words), currency (GBP), schedule (lump sum/instalments/Gift Aid declaration reference) |
| Heritable property | Title number (Land Register of Scotland), postal address, burdens, standard securities |
| Moveable property | Description, condition, quantity, market value per valuation |
| Securities | Type, quantity, issuer, transfer method (CREST/certificate) |
| IP | Rights transferred, UK IPO registration numbers |
| In-kind | Nature, scope, valuation methodology |

Include delivery method, transfer date, conditions precedent, and Gift Aid declaration reference (if cash).

### 3. Terms and Conditions

- **Unrestricted** - recipient may use for any charitable purpose
- **Restricted** - permitted uses, prohibited uses, time period
- **Irrevocability** - irrevocable upon acceptance (recommended for Gift Aid eligibility)
- **Cy-pres** - if purpose becomes impossible/impractical, funds redirect to substantially similar charitable purpose under s. 12 to 13 Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005 (or s. 62 Charities Act 2011 for E&W charities, confirm which applies)
- **Endowment vs. expendable** - whether capital must be maintained
- **Conditions** - contingencies (matching gifts, naming rights, milestones) and non-fulfilment consequences
- **Reporting** - required accounts or use certifications

### 4. Tax Acknowledgment & HMRC Compliance

| Threshold | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Any amount | Written acknowledgment of receipt by the charity |
| Gift Aid (any amount) | Gift Aid declaration must be obtained before claim. Donor must have paid sufficient UK Income/Capital Gains Tax to cover the 25% Gift Aid uplift. Charity claims via HMRC online service |
| Non-cash > £500 | Donor may need valuation for their own tax records |
| Non-cash > £100,000 | HMRC may require formal valuation; specialist advice recommended |
| Quid pro quo (benefits received) | Charities must report benefit received where donor receives something in return (events, goods). Gift Aid only on net donation after benefit value |

**Gift Aid declaration wording (mandatory for Gift Aid):**
> "I confirm I am a UK taxpayer and understand that if I pay less Income Tax and/or Capital Gains Tax than the amount of Gift Aid claimed on all my donations in that tax year, it is my responsibility to pay any difference. Please treat this donation as Gift Aid."

Include disclaimer: neither party provides tax advice; donor should consult own tax adviser.

### 5. Representations & Warranties

**Donor warrants:**
- Full legal title and authority to transfer, Property free of undisclosed standard securities, burdens, or defects (heritable property)
- Donation does not breach any other agreement
- (Non-cash) Discloses all known defects, environmental issues, or legal problems
- (Gift Aid) Is a UK taxpayer with sufficient tax liability

**Recipient warrants:**
- Duly formed, validly existing, registered with OSCR (charity number: [ref])
- HMRC charity reference obtained, Authority under governing document to accept, Will use donation per stated restrictions, Will maintain Gift Aid records per HMRC requirements

### 6. Standard Provisions

- **Governing law** - Scots law (Law of Scotland)
- **Jurisdiction** - Scottish courts (Sheriff Court or Court of Session)
- **Dispute resolution** - optional mediation/arbitration before litigation
- **Expenses** - each party bears own costs (standard in Scottish charity transactions)
- **Severability**, **entire agreement**, **amendment** (written, signed by both)

### 7. Execution

- Signature blocks with printed name, title, date, Corporate donors: signatory authority per articles of association (board resolution for substantial gifts)
- Recipient: authorised signatory per governing document, Notarisation: not generally required for Scottish charities (but witnesses recommended; requirement for heritable property under Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995)
- Exhibits: property descriptions, valuations, restriction schedules, OSCR registration certificate, Gift Aid declaration form

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **Verify OSCR registration** via the Scottish Charity Register (www.oscr.org.uk) before closing
- **Pledge vs. gift** - pledges are not generally binding in Scots law absent consideration or formal deed (Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995); document as bilateral agreement if enforceability required
- **Scottish charities (SCIO)** - SCIOs have distinct constitutional requirements (Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005, Part 1); confirm governing document permits the specific type of donation
- **Gift Aid** - the charity must hold a valid declaration before claiming; retain records for HMRC audit; Gift Aid Small Donations Scheme (GASDS) may apply for small cash donations (up to £30/£20 per donor without declaration)
- **Naming rights** - address benefit valuation (ticketed events, named buildings) and impact on Gift Aid (quid pro quo rules)
- **AML / terrorist financing** - for large or foreign-sourced donations, conduct due diligence per Money Laundering Regulations 2017; report suspicious activity to NCA
- **Scottish charity fundraising** - Fundraising Preference Service, Fundraising Regulator (UK-wide) and the Scottish Fundraising Adjudication Panel may apply
- **Cross-border donations** - if donor is in E&W/NI and recipient is Scottish (or vice versa), verify HMRC Gift Aid rules for cross-border UK charity donations
- **Endowments** - Scottish charities must follow the Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005 s. 86 for permanent endowments (distinct from E&W rules)

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Statutory framework:**
- Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005 - principal Scottish charity legislation, Charities Act 2011 (England & Wales) - may apply to cross-border charities operating in Scotland, Charities Act 2022 (England & Wales) - recent amendments, Charities (Registration and Regulation) (Scotland) Regulations 2018
- Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995 - formalities for heritable property and obligations, Land Registration etc. (Scotland) Act 2012 - registration of heritable property interests, Income Tax Act 2007 ss. 413 to 430 (Gift Aid rules)
- Finance Act 2010 - Gift Aid Small Donations Scheme (GASDS)
- Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds (Information on the Payer) Regulations 2017

**Key differences from US approach:**
- No 501(c)(3) exemption; charities register with OSCR (Scotland) or Charity Commission (E&W)
- Gift Aid (25% basic rate uplift) is the primary tax incentive, different from US itemised deduction, Donors do not file a separate form to claim the tax benefit (carried out by the charity via HMRC)
- No equivalent of IRS Form 8283 for non-cash donations (but valuation evidence required for donor's own tax position if claiming relief)
- Scottish SCIO structure is unique (separate legal personality, no members)
- No "private foundation" distinction (but different HMRC rules for grant-making charities)
- OSCR is an independent regulator (different from Charity Commission / IRS structure)
- Scots law cy-pres follows the Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005 ss. 12 to 13 (not Uniform Trust Code)

**Regulatory bodies:**
- OSCR (Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator) - Scottish charity register and regulation, HMRC Charities, tax relief, Gift Aid, charity tax registrations, Fundraising Regulator, UK-wide voluntary fundraising regulation, Scottish Fundraising Adjudication Panel, specific to Scotland when Fundraising Regulator rules don't apply, Charity Commission (England & Wales) - if donor or recipient is registered in E&W

**Forms and resources (download to scots-forms/):**
- HMRC Gift Aid Single Donation Declaration (GAD_Single)
- HMRC Gift Aid Multiple Donation Declaration (GAD_Multiple)
- OSCR SCIO Constitution template, OSCR Charity Registration Application form, HMRC Gift Aid claim form (online / ChR1)

**Practitioner notes:**
- Scottish charities are uniquely regulated by OSCR; verify OSCR registration separately from HMRC registration, Heritable property gifts to Scottish charities require a Scottish solicitor to handle the disposition and registration in the Land Register, Gift Aid due diligence must confirm the donor is a UK taxpayer and has paid sufficient tax in the relevant year, For complex gift structures (charitable trusts, endowment funds), instruct a Scottish charity law specialist

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
