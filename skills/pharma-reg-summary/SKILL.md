# Pharmaceutical Regulation Compliance Summary (UK/Scotland)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

[SCOTS: Note] This skill adapts a US FDA-focused pharmaceutical regulatory summary template for the UK under the Human Medicines Regulations 2012 and MHRA regulation. The regulatory landscape differs significantly: no direct Hatch-Waxman equivalent, no Orange Book system, different patent/regulatory linkage, and a self-regulatory ABPI Code for promotion alongside MHRA enforcement. Scotland's access decisions are made by the Scottish Medicines Consortium (SMC), not NICE.

Produces an actionable regulatory compliance summary structured around MHRA frameworks, patent/IP strategy, and promotional compliance.

## Prerequisites

Gather before starting:

1. **Product portfolio** - marketed products (with UK marketing authorisation numbers), pipeline compounds, therapeutic areas
2. **Regulatory filings** - pending UK MAs (full / abridged / well-established use / informed consent), active clinical trial authorisations (CTA), post-authorisation commitments
3. **Commercial activities** - speaker programmes, digital promotion, consulting arrangements, patient support programmes
4. **Compliance history** - MHRA inspection findings, warning letters, regulatory undertakings, ABPI Code rulings

## Quick Start

Structure every summary into three sections:

1. **Marketing Authorisation & Post-Market Obligations** - pathway requirements, pharmacovigilance, safety reporting
2. **Patent & IP Regulatory Intersections** - SPCs, Bolar exemption, data exclusivity, market exclusivity
3. **Marketing & Promotional Compliance** - off-label rules, ABPI Code, financial compliance, Bribery Act 2010

## Core Workflow

### 1. Marketing Authorisation & Post-Market Obligations

| Pathway | Requirements | Pitfalls |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Full MA (Art 8(3) Dir 2001/83) | Full quality, safety, efficacy dossier | Incomplete clinical data, GxP inspection findings |
| Abridged / Well-established use (Art 10a) | Published scientific literature evidence | Insufficient bibliographic support; bioequivalence data gaps |
| Hybrid (Art 10(3)) | New indication/strength change with bridging studies | Incomplete BE data; differing therapeutic equivalence |
| Biosimilar (Art 10(4)) | Comparability exercise with reference product (EU-biosimilar approach retained via UK guidance) | Analytical similarity gaps; immunogenicity data |
| Conditional MA / Early Access (EAMS) | Promising Innovative Medicines (PIM) + EAMS designation; post-authorisation efficacy studies | Confirmatory trial milestone deadlines missed |

[SCOTS: Note] The UK's independent medicines regime post-Brexit uses the same scientific assessment framework (MHRA). Scotland additionally requires **Scottish Medicines Consortium (SMC)** health technology assessment for NHS Scotland access.

Per-product checklist:

- [ ] CTA compliance (The Medicines for Human Use (Clinical Trials) Regulations 2004): annual safety reports, substantial amendment notifications, end-of-trial declarations
- [ ] UK Clinical Trials Regulations (SI 2004/1031) and GCP compliance
- [ ] Pharmacovigilance: MHRA Yellow Card Scheme; UK PVP (Pharmacovigilance System Master File); serious adverse reaction reporting (15-day expedited, 90-day periodic)
- [ ] PSURs (Periodic Safety Update Reports) for all products; submission to MHRA
- [ ] PASS (Post-Authorisation Safety Studies) commitments tracking
- [ ] Risk Management Plan (UK-RMP) maintenance and updates

### 2. Patent & IP Regulatory Intersections

| Framework | Authority | Key Points |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| Supplementary Protection Certificates (SPCs) | Patents Act 1977, as amended; Regulation (EC) 469/2009 (retained) | Up to 5-year extension for medicinal products; 6-month paediatric extension (SPC Regulation Art 8) |
| Bolar exemption | Patents Act 1977, s 60(5) | Acts for regulatory approval purposes are not infringing |
| Data exclusivity | Human Medicines Regs 2012, Sch 27 | 8-year data exclusivity + 2-year market exclusivity + 1-year new indication |
| Orphan designation | Regulation (EC) 141/2000 (retained) | 10-year market exclusivity (UK modified regime post-Brexit) |
| Paediatric investigation plan (PIP) | Regulation (EC) 1901/2006 (retained) | 6-month SPC extension on compliance; MA application must include PIP or waiver/deferral |
| UK Patent Box | Corporation Tax Act 2010, Part 8A | Reduced corporation tax on qualifying IP income (relevant for pharmaceutical patents) |

[SCOTS: Note] Unlike the US Orange Book, the UK does not maintain a public patent-to-product linkage registry for regulatory purposes. Patent challenges are handled via the Patents Court (England) / Court of Session (Scotland) Outer House, or the Intellectual Property Office (IPO).

Flag in every summary:

- [ ] Patent strategy alignment with UK/SPC filing deadlines
- [ ] SPC application deadlines, file within 6 months of first MA (or within 6 months of patent grant, whichever is later)
- [ ] Upcoming data/market exclusivity expirations and generic/biosimilar entry risk
- [ ] SPC paediatric extension requirements, paediatric study compliance

### 3. Marketing & Promotional Compliance

**Off-label promotion** - Under Human Medicines Regulations 2012, Part 14 (Regulations 274 to 284), it is an offence to promote a medicinal product for which a marketing authorisation is not held, or for indications not within the terms of the MA. Narrow exceptions: unsolicited requests, scientific exchange with HCPs, publication of independent peer-reviewed articles.

**Promotional materials** - Must comply with Human Medicines Regs 2012 Part 14 and the **ABPI Code of Practice for the Pharmaceutical Industry** (self-regulatory, enforced by PMCPA). Key requirements: accurate, balanced, not misleading, must not encourage irrational use, must include prescribing information (if detailed), and must be in the public interest.

**Digital/social media** - Follow ABPI Code guidance and MHRA guidance on digital promotion. Monitor owned channels. Disclose KOL/influencer arrangements. Social media posts must meet the same standards as any other promotional material (including fair balance).

**DTC advertising** - Advertising prescription-only medicines to the public is **prohibited** under Human Medicines Regs 2012, Reg 280. Only non-prescription (P/GSL) medicines may be advertised direct to public. This is a fundamental difference from the US regime.

**Financial compliance:**

| Statute | Key Rules |
|---------|-----------|
| Bribery Act 2010 (s 1 to 2, 7) | No inducement to prescribe, supply, or recommend. Corporate offence: failure to prevent bribery |
| ABPI Disclosure UK | Public register of transfers of value to HCPs and healthcare organisations; all value >£10 reported |
| UK GDPR / DPA 2018 | HCPs' personal data processing for commercial engagement; consent / legitimate interest basis |
| Human Medicines Regs 2012 | Reg 282 - prohibition of gifts and hospitality for promotional purposes |

## Compliance Checklist

- [ ] SOPs for MA submissions, pharmacovigilance, labelling updates, batch release (QP)
- [ ] Commercial team training on ABPI Code and Bribery Act compliance (annual + new hire)
- [ ] MLR (Medical-Legal-Regulatory / Medical-Pharmaceutical-Regulatory) review of all promotional materials
- [ ] Signatory status, UK Qualified Person (QP) for batch release; pharmacovigilance QPPV
- [ ] Audit programme: labelling, claims review, speaker programme FMV, HCP engagement
- [ ] PASS/PMR tracking with escalation triggers
- [ ] Whistleblower/compliance hotline and investigation protocols (Bribery Act s 7 adequate procedures defence)
- [ ] International harmonisation: note UK vs EU differences post-Brexit (MHRA vs EMA assessments)

## Pitfalls

- **Missing product-type distinctions** - requirements differ for small molecule vs. biological vs. generics; always separate
- **Controlled substances** - Misuse of Drugs Act 1971 overlay (Home Office scheduling); flag when applicable
- **Scottish Medicines Consortium (SMC)** - separate HTA from NICE; Scotland access decisions are SMC only
- **Brexit divergence** - UK regulatory regime is diverging from EU; check current MHRA guidance for any changes [VERIFY]
- **ABPI Code updates** - updated annually; ensure latest version referenced
- **Off-label promotion risk** - MHRA has criminal enforcement powers; Bribery Act 2010 also applies
- **Data protection** - HCP engagement requires UK GDPR compliance; separate from US Sunshine Act
- **Stale citations** - mark uncertain or recently updated authorities with [VERIFY]
- **Scope creep** - present regulatory facts; do not provide legal conclusions on enforcement risk, let counsel assess

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
