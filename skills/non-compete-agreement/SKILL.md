# Non-Compete Agreement (UK/Scotland)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

## Quick Start

Gather before drafting:

1. **Governing law** - Scots law or English law (Scottish courts apply the same restraint of trade principles)
2. **Parties** - employee name/title/hire date/access level; employer legal name/registered address
3. **Legitimate business interests** - trade secrets, customer relationships, specialised training, staff stability (must be specific)
4. **Desired scope** - territory, duration, restricted activities
5. **Existing agreements** - prior contracts, NDAs, or restrictive covenants
6. **Timing** - new hire vs. existing employee (affects consideration for any variation)

## Legal Framework

Research and document before drafting:

| Factor | UK Position |
|---|---|
| Doctrine | Restraint of trade, prima facie void unless: (1) protects a legitimate business interest, (2) is reasonable between the parties, (3) is not contrary to the public interest |
| Standard of reasonableness | *Herbert Morris v Saxelby* [1916] - three-dimensional: geographic + temporal + activity |
| Reformation approach | Blue pencil severance only, court may strike offending words but cannot rewrite (*Tillman v Egon Zehnder* [2019] UKSC 32) |
| Consideration | Continued employment and access to confidential information sufficient at hire; new consideration required for variation during employment |
| Maximum duration | Non-compete: 3 to 12 months typical; 12+ months rare and needs strong justification |
| Key precedents | *Nordenfelt v Maxim* [1894]; *Herbert Morris v Saxelby* [1916]; *Tillman v Egon Zehnder* [2019] |
| Reform context | UK Government proposed 3-month statutory cap on non-competes (not yet enacted) - flag as reform risk |

## Core Workflow

### 1. Title and Parties

- Full title: "Post-Termination Restrictive Covenants Agreement" or "Restrictive Covenants Agreement"
- Employer: legal name, registered address, company number, Employee: full name, title, department, hire/promotion date, Include detail sufficient for service and jurisdiction

### 2. Recitals

Articulate **specific** legitimate business interests (not boilerplate):
- Proprietary systems and trade secrets the employee will access, Customer relationships and goodwill at stake, identify key clients, Employer's training investment, Geographic markets and competitive landscape, Sales cycle / information decay periods relevant to measuring harm

### 3. Definitions

| Term | Scope |
|---|---|
| Confidential Information | Specific categories with exclusions for public/independently developed information |
| Restricted Territory | Based on actual market presence, named local authority areas, regions, or customer-contact radius |
| Restricted Period | 3 to 12 months calibrated to seniority, access level, and information sensitivity |
| Competitive Activities | Specific prohibited conduct tied to identified competitors and similar roles |
| Relevant Customers | Those with whom employee had material contact in the 12 months before termination |

### 4. Restrictive Covenants

Each covenant must satisfy the three-dimensional reasonableness test (geographic + temporal + activity):

- **Non-Compete** - no broader than employer's actual market; temporal scope justified by information decay. Recommended maximum: 6 months.
- **Customer Non-Solicitation** - limited to customers with material contact during 12-month lookback; covers solicitation, servicing, and acceptance of business
- **Employee Non-Solicitation** - prohibit recruiting/hiring employer's key employees and contractors (senior roles only)
- **Confidentiality** - perpetual for trade secrets; time-limited for other proprietary information; return/destruction obligation on termination
- **Garden Leave** - contractual right to place employee on garden leave during notice period (alternative to non-compete; does not engage restraint of trade)

### 5. Consideration

| Timing | Required Consideration |
|---|---|
| At hire | Employment + access to confidential information + training |
| During employment | Promotion, raise, bonus, new confidential access, or agreed variation in writing |

State specific value to demonstrate bargained-for exchange. **Scottish courts** require proof of consideration for any post-contractual variation.

### 6. Employee Acknowledgment

Employee affirms: restrictions are reasonable and necessary; adequate consideration received; will not prevent earning a livelihood; opportunity to consult independent legal advice.

### 7. Enforcement and Remedies

- **Interdict (injunction)** - interim interdict available in Sheriff Court for urgent enforcement (within days); permanent interdict at proof
- **Damages** - compensatory damages for proven loss; account of profits in appropriate cases
- **Blue pencil severance** - authorise court to sever offending provisions; sub-clauses must be separately severable
- **Tolling** - restricted period may be extended by duration of any breach (ensure express provision; case law is mixed)
- **Preserve right to** damages, interdict, and account of profits

### 8. Dispute Resolution

Choose one framework:

| Option | Key Terms |
|---|---|
| Litigation | Sheriff Court (Scotland) for claims under £100,000; Court of Session for higher value; exclusive Scottish jurisdiction clause |
| Mediation (recommended) | Voluntary or contractual; preserve right to seek interim interdict in court |
| Arbitration | Less common for employment restrictive covenants; interim interdict must be preserved as court carve-out |

**Note:** Employment Tribunal does NOT have jurisdiction over post-termination restrictive covenant enforcement (these are breach of contract claims). Enforcement is in the civil courts.

### 9. Governing Law and Boilerplate

Choice of law: **Scots law** (for Scottish employees) or **English law** (for E&W employees). Conflict-of-laws waiver where reasonable nexus exists. Written amendment requirement; non-waiver; notice provisions; assignment to successors; entire agreement / integration; severability coordinated with blue pencil clause.

### 10. Execution

Employer and employee signature blocks with dates; separate voluntary-execution acknowledgment; witness recommended for Scottish execution (Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995).

## Pitfalls and Checks

- **UK law is unified** - no state-by-state analysis needed. Apply restraint of trade principles consistently.
- **Blue pencil only** - UK/Scottish courts will strike offending words; they will NOT rewrite. Draft separate severable sub-clauses.
- **Garden leave priority** - garden leave is enforceable as a contractual term (not subject to restraint of trade); consider it before a non-compete.
- **Narrow > broad** - UK courts enforce only the minimum restriction necessary. Overbroad provisions risk the entire clause being void.
- **Recitals are evidence** - detailed recitals establishing legitimate business interests are critical at enforcement.
- **Reform risk** - flag that the UK Government may introduce a 3-month statutory cap on non-compete clauses. If enacted, non-competes > 3 months will be void.
- **Not for US** - remove all references to FRCP, FTC, and US state law.
- **No bracketed placeholders** - output must be execution-ready.
- **Mark** citations with confirmations, UK case law is stable but reform context changes.
- **Include exhibits** as needed: competitor lists, territory definitions, customer contact records.

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from a US state-tailored non-compete agreement to a UK/Scotland employment restrictive covenants agreement.

### Key Structural Changes

| US Feature | UK/Scotland Equivalent |
|---|---|
| State-by-state analysis | **No state variation** - UK common law applies uniformly; Scottish courts follow same restraint of trade principles as English courts |
| FTC (federal regulation) | No FTC equivalent for non-competes; UK Government proposed 3-month cap (not yet enacted) |
| FRCP (Federal Rules of Civil Procedure) | **Not applicable** - UK employment uses Employment Tribunal (unfair dismissal) + Sheriff Court/CoS (restrictive covenant enforcement) |
| Punitive damages | **Not available** - UK remedies are compensatory (damages, interdict, account of profits) |
| State reformation (blue/red/re-write) | **Blue pencil only** - strike words; cannot rewrite |

### UK Restraint of Trade Principles

- **Prima facie void** - the starting point; employer must justify every restriction
- **Legitimate business interests** - trade secrets, customer connections, staff stability (not mere competition)
- **Geographic scope** - must reflect employer's actual market, not wherever the employee might go
- **Duration** - 3 to 6 months for non-compete is typical; 12 months needs strong justification
- **Void for unreasonableness** - an unreasonable restriction is void; it cannot be reduced by the court (blue pencil may salvage part of it)

### Key Cases

- *Nordenfelt v Maxim Nordenfelt Guns and Ammunition Co Ltd* [1894] AC 535 - restraint of trade doctrine
- *Herbert Morris Ltd v Saxelby* [1916] 1 AC 688 - three-dimensional reasonableness test
- *Office Angels Ltd v Rainer-Thomas* [1991] IRLR 214 - non-solicitation is more readily enforceable than non-compete
- *Tillman v Egon Zehnder Ltd* [2019] UKSC 32 - blue pencil test; unambiguous severability

### Garden Leave

- Employee remains employed during notice period but stays away from work, Full pay and benefits continue, Does NOT engage restraint of trade (not a post-termination restriction)
- Consider as a primary mechanism before drafting non-compete

### Proposed Reform

- UK Government consulted on a **3-month maximum** for non-compete clauses
- **Not enacted** (as of 2026) - but draft assuming possible future restriction, Non-solicitation, non-dealing, and confidentiality clauses would NOT be affected, Garden leave would NOT be affected

### Scottish Courts & Procedure

- **Sheriff Court** - claims up to £100,000; interim interdict available within days on summary application
- **Court of Session** - claims over £100,000; Outer House for first instance; petition for interdict
- **Employment Tribunals (Scotland)** - hears unfair dismissal, discrimination, breach of contract (Recoupment) but NOT post-termination restrictive covenant disputes
- **Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995** - formal validity of documents; witness recommended for execution

### Relevant UK Statutes

- **Employment Rights Act 1996** - written statement of particulars; unfair dismissal; notice periods (s.86)
- **Equality Act 2010** - non-discrimination provisions (relevant to enforceability context)
- **Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977** - applies to exclusion/restriction clauses in employment context (restrictive covenants themselves not typically UCTA-caught)
- **Data Protection Act 2018 / UK GDPR** - data processing considerations in employment context

### Agency Mappings

- **EEOC** → **ACAS** / **Employment Tribunal Scotland**
- **FTC** → **CMA** (general consumer protection, not non-compete specific)
- **DOL** → **DWP** / **BEIS**

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
