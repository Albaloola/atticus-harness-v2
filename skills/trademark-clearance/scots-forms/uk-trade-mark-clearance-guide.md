# UK Trade Mark Clearance, Quick Reference

## Key Legislation
- **Trade Marks Act 1994 (TMA 1994)** - UK trade mark law
- **s.5(2)** - Likelihood of confusion test (global appreciation)
- **s.3** - Absolute grounds for refusal (descriptiveness, lack of distinctiveness)
- **s.46** - Revocation for non-use (5-year grace period)
- **s.47** - Invalidity grounds
- **s.94** - Criminal offence for unauthorised use of ®

## UK IPO Right Start Service, Pre-application examination: UK IPO checks your mark before you commit to filing, Reduced filing fee (£170 for first class vs ~£200 standard)
- Response to examination report required, mark proceeds to publication if report satisfied

## Scottish-Specific Considerations
- **Scottish passing off**: Must show goodwill in Scotland, misrepresentation, damage (Advocat case)
- **Scottish Enterprise**: Free IP audit service for eligible Scottish SMEs
- **Companies House Edinburgh**: Scottish company name protection
- **.scot domain**: Register to protect brand online
- **Use in Scotland**: Documentary proof of first use in Scotland for passing off claims

## Key Case Law (UK)
- Sabel BV v Puma AG [1998] ETMR 1 - global appreciation, Canon KK v Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer [1999] ETMR 1 - interdependence, Lloyd Schuhfabrik Meyer [1999] ETMR 690 - average consumer, Erven Warnink v Townend [1979] AC 731 (Advocat) - passing off (classic trinity)
- Reckitt & Colman v Borden [1990] 1 WLR 491 - passing off (Jif Lemon)
