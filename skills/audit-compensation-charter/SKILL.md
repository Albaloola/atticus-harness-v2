# Audit and Remuneration Committee Charter

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Produces a board-resolution-adopted governance charter for Audit and Remuneration Committees with enforceable duties, delegated powers, and compliance documentation.

## Prerequisites

1. **Corporate documents** - certificate of incorporation (or equivalent), articles of association, existing board resolutions
2. **Company profile** - public vs. private, UK registration (Companies House number), exchange listing (Main Market / AIM / AQSE / none)
3. **Industry context** - sector-specific regulatory overlay (FCA/PRA financial services, healthcare, etc.)
4. **Director roster** - independence status (UK Corporate Governance Code Provisions 10 to 12), financial/relevant experience
5. **Audit context** - internal audit function, external auditor history (FRC Audit Firm Monitoring), material risk areas
6. **Remuneration context** - remuneration policy, equity incentive plans, director service agreements, clawback/malus framework
7. **Disclosure context** (listed) - annual report / strategic report workflow, directors' remuneration report, previous committee reports
8. **Authority confirmation** - whether articles of association and Companies Act 2006 permit committee creation by board resolution

## Quick Start

1. Gather prerequisites above; confirm listing status before applying UK Corporate Governance Code.
2. Complete the regime map (Step 1 below).
3. Draft charter sections in the prescribed order (Step 2).
4. Append compliance checklist; resolve every item before delivery.

## Workflow

### Step 1 - Regime Map

Determine the regulatory baseline before drafting:

| Input | Determination |
|---|---|
| Company type | Listed (Premium / Standard / AIM / AQSE) vs. private; if listed: FCA UKLR + UK Corporate Governance Code |
| Directors | Independence baseline (UK Code Provisions 10 to 12) + required financial/relevant experience |
| Company law | Companies Act 2006 - board committee authority (s.173 to 176); articles of association |
| Audit governance | Internal audit scope, auditor relationship, FRC Audit Committees Standard, FRC Audit Firm Monitoring |
| Remuneration governance | Executive pay exposure, clawback/malus, directors' remuneration report, shareholder vote |

### Step 2 - Draft Charter Sections

Use this section order:

| Section | Required Content |
|---|---|
| Preamble | Board adoption language, purpose, hierarchy, relationship to articles of association |
| Definitions | Committee, Independent Non-Executive Director, Connected Person, Senior Management, etc. |
| Formation | Committees created, terms, appointment, vacancies, removal, chair duties |
| Composition | Independence standards (UK Code Provision 24/32), financial/relevant experience, minimum membership |
| Powers and resources | Audit firm and advisor engagement authority, budget, information rights, access to company secretary |
| Meetings | Calendar, quorum, notice, written resolutions, executive sessions (NED-only), minutes |
| Audit duties | Financial reporting oversight, internal controls (Code Provision 29), internal audit, auditor relations, whistleblowing process |
| Remuneration duties | CEO/executive pay policy, equity administration, malus/clawback, risk controls, succession, workforce consultation |
| Reporting | Board reporting cadence, escalation triggers, annual report disclosure coordination |
| Compliance | FCA DTR 7.1, UK Corporate Governance Code, FRC Standards, FCA UKLR, AIM Rules, Companies Act 2006 |
| Evaluation and amendment | Annual self-evaluation, charter review, training, amendment procedure |

### Step 3 - Output Template

Structure the resolution as:

- **Article I** - Establishment and authority (both committees, delegated powers and limits per CA 2006 s.173 to 176)
- **Article II** - Purpose (audit purpose, remuneration purpose)
- **Article III** - Composition and independence (membership criteria, UK Code independence test, minimum 3 members / 2 for smaller companies)
- **Article IV** - Powers and duties (audit powers, remuneration powers)
- **Article V** - Procedures (meetings, quorum/voting, information rights, officer attendance, NED-only sessions)
- **Article VI** - Reporting (board report format/timing, annual report disclosures, directors' remuneration report, audit committee report)
- **Article VII** - Documentation and review (minutes standards, annual evaluation, charter refresh)

### Step 4 - Compliance Checklist

Append to every draft and resolve each item:

- [ ] Companies Act 2006 references verified (s.173 to 176, s.412, s.419 to 422, s.430)
- [ ] UK Corporate Governance Code provisions mapped to exact requirements (check 2024 edition)
- [ ] FRC Audit Committees and External Audit: Minimum Standard incorporated for listed companies
- [ ] Independence and relevant financial experience criteria validated against all named directors
- [ ] Auditor engagement and non-audit service controls addressed (FRC Ethical Standard)
- [ ] No management voting or conflicted attendance during sensitive deliberations
- [ ] Remuneration risk controls include malus, clawback, holding periods, risk metrics (UK Code Provision 40)
- [ ] Directors' remuneration report disclosure obligations included (The Large and Medium-sized Companies and Groups (Accounts and Reports) Regulations 2008)
- [ ] Executive session (NED-only) practices defined for both committees
- [ ] Workforce engagement / remuneration policy consultation provisions (UK Code Provisions 5, 39)

## Guidelines

**Do:**
- Use directive language: "shall," "must," "is prohibited"
- Separate mandatory rules (Companies Act / FCA DTR / UKLR) from Code provisions (comply or explain) with marked subsections, Keep private-company charters lean, omit UK Code / FCA requirements that do not apply, Tie every committee power to enforceable outputs (reviews, reports, escalation)

**Don't:**
- Assume UK Corporate Governance Code applies without confirming listing status (premium listing only; AIM companies apply QCA Code or similar)
- Mix charter authority with operational management tasks, Require unanimous committee action where the articles expect majority (unless intentionally stricter)
- Import boilerplate from other jurisdictions without UK statutory confirmation

## Compliance Notes

- Core UK touchpoints: Companies Act 2006, UK Corporate Governance Code (FRC), FCA Disclosure Guidance and Transparency Rules (DTR 7.1), FCA UK Listing Rules, FRC Audit Committees and External Audit: Minimum Standard, FRC Ethical Standard, Independence standards: UK Code Provisions 10 to 12 (not SEC/NYSE definition). Includes 9-year tenure cap (Provision 19). No bright-line independence categories, board assesses, FRC Audit Committees Standard: Replaced audit-related Code provisions in 2024. Mandatory for premium-listed companies, Remuneration: Directors' Remuneration Report requires binding shareholder vote on policy (every 3 years) and advisory vote on implementation (annually) - Companies Act 2006 s.439 to 439A, Malus/clawback: UK Code Provision 40 requires listed companies to include malus and clawback arrangements in incentive schemes, Cross-border groups: add host-country governance addenda; do not rely solely on UK statute language

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Resolution |
|---|---|
| Unclear whether company is premium/standard listed | Confirm with FCA List of Issuers or LSE website; default to private-company baseline until verified |
| Articles silent on committee delegation | Check CA 2006 s.173 (board may delegate) and articles, if silent, board can still delegate by resolution; flag for amendment |
| Director independence uncertain | Apply UK Code Provisions 10 to 12 criteria; mark with [VERIFY]; note 9-year tenure cap |
| Existing charter conflicts with articles | Note conflict, draft to article hierarchy, recommend articles amendment if needed |
| No internal audit function exists | Charter should address whether one must be established or if external auditor fills role (FRC Code Provision 26) |
| Comply or explain, unclear how to differentiate | Mark mandatory rules in bold, non-mandatory Code provisions in italic or with "(comply or explain)" annotation |

## Scotland / UK Adaptation

This section documents how the US-origin audit-compensation charter skill has been adapted for Scotland/UK.

### Statutory / Regulatory Replacements
| US Authority | UK / Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| SEC (Securities and Exchange Commission) | FCA (Financial Conduct Authority) |
| SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley Act) Sections 201/301/806 | Companies Act 2006 Part 16 (audit); UK Corporate Governance Code; FRC Standards |
| SEC Rule 10A-3 | FCA DTR 7.1; UK Corporate Governance Code Provisions 24 to 26; FRC Audit Committees Standard |
| NYSE / Nasdaq listing standards | FCA UK Listing Rules (UKLR); AIM Rules for Companies; AQSE Rules |
| SEC EDGAR | FCA National Storage Mechanism (NSM); Companies House |
| DGCL § 141(c) (Delaware) | Companies Act 2006 s.173 to 176 (board delegation powers) |
| SEC Reg S-K / S-X | FCA DTR; Companies Act 2006 Schedules (accounts and reports) |
| Rule 16b-3 (short-swing profit) | No direct equivalent, UK MAR (Market Abuse Regulation) Art. 19 deals with PDMR notification |
| Section 280G (golden parachute) | No direct UK equivalent, HMRC applies Income Tax (Earnings and Pensions) Act 2003 |
| Dodd-Frank § 954 (clawback) | UK Corporate Governance Code Provision 40 (malus/clawback) |
| Proxy advisory firms (ISS, Glass Lewis) | Same firms (ISS, Glass Lewis) + IVIS (Institutional Voting Information Service) |
| Public Company Accounting Oversight Board (PCAOB) | FRC Audit Quality and Enforcement; Audit Firm Monitoring |
| SEC proxy statement / proxy card | Annual report / Notice of AGM / Form of Proxy |

### Key UK / Scots Law Considerations
1. **"Comply or Explain"** - UK Corporate Governance Code operates on "comply or explain" basis (mandatory vs permissive rules). Charter should distinguish mandatory CA 2006 / FCA DTR requirements from Code provisions
2. **Remuneration (not Compensation)** - UK terminology uses "remuneration committee" and "remuneration report". "Compensation" is US usage
3. **Independent Non-Executive Directors** - UK Code Provision 24: Audit committee must have at least 3 independent NEDs (2 for smaller companies). One member must have recent/relevant financial experience (Provision 24)
4. **Remuneration committee composition** - UK Code Provision 32: at least 3 independent NEDs (2 for smaller companies). Chair must have served on committee for 12 months before chairing
5. **Shareholder vote on pay** - CA 2006 s.439: binding vote on directors' remuneration policy (every 3 years); s.439A: advisory vote on implementation report (annually)
6. **FRC Ethical Standard** - restricts non-audit services provided by statutory auditor (parallel to SOX but structured differently)
7. **Scottish company law** - Companies Act 2006 applies across UK including Scotland. Scottish registered companies file at Companies House (Edinburgh office). Court of Session / Sheriff Court handles disputes
8. **Workforce engagement** - UK Code Provisions 5 and 39: Remuneration committee should engage with workforce on remuneration policy. Board should develop engagement mechanisms

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
