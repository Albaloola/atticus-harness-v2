# Pollution Prevention Plan (PPP) - Scotland/UK

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

Produces a construction site Pollution Prevention Plan (PPP) covering site characterisation, BMP/control measure selection, inspection schedules, recordkeeping, and operator compliance under the Water Environment (Controlled Activities) (Scotland) Regulations 2011 (CAR) and SEPA regulatory framework.

## Prerequisites

1. **Project details** - name, location (grid reference or legal description), total disturbance area (m²/hectares), timeline
2. **Operator identification** - entity with day-to-day operational control and SEPA responsible person
3. **Site characterisation** - soil types, topography, drainage patterns, existing vegetation
4. **Receiving waters** - all water bodies receiving discharge, distance/direction, classification (under WEWS classification), status
5. **Environmental features** - wetlands, designated sites (SSSI, SAC, SPA), protected species, historic environment features within or adjacent to site
6. **Existing authorisations** - SEPA CAR licence number (if applicable), GBR registration, any existing discharge consents
7. **Site plans / drainage plans** - if available

## Output Structure

### 1. Cover Page & Certifications

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Project name & SEPA reference | Required |
| Operator name, address, contact | Required |
| Site manager / responsible person name | Required |
| Signature block | Operator or authorised representative, name, title, date |
| Certification | Declaration of compliance with CAR and GBR conditions |

### 2. Site Description

- Project narrative (scope, phases, timeline)
- Total site area vs. disturbance area (m²/hectares)
- Soil types (reference James Hutton Institute soil survey)
- Pre- and post-construction drainage patterns, Receiving waters table:

| Receiving Water | WEWS Classification | SEPA Status | Distance from Site |
|---|---|---|---|
| {name} | {class} | {high/good/moderate/poor/bad} | {m/km, direction} |

- Designated sites: SSSI, SAC, SPA, Ramsar within 2 km of site, Protected species survey and mitigation requirements

### 3. Control Measures (BMPs)

Organise into three categories. For each measure, specify location, install timing, design specification, and maintenance requirement.

**Erosion & Sediment Controls** - silt fencing, sediment basins (minimum 20 m³ per hectare disturbed), settlement ponds, inlet protection, stabilised construction access (minimum 15 m × 3.75 m, 75 mm stone on geotextile), temporary seeding or surface cover for areas inactive >14 days.

**Good Housekeeping & Pollution Prevention**

| Practice | Standard |
|---|---|
| Material storage | Bunded secondary containment for liquids (110% of largest container); covered erodible materials; storage at least 10 m from any drain or watercourse |
| Concrete washout | Designated contained area; no discharge to drains or water environment |
| Spill prevention | Spill kits on site; report to SEPA (0800 80 70 60) - determine reportable thresholds |
| Waste management | Covered skips; waste transfer notes per Environmental Protection Act 1990; SEPA waste exemptions or permits |
| Sanitary welfare | Licensed contractor; Council-registered disposal |
| Vehicle maintenance | Drip trays; no refuelling within 10 m of watercourse; bunded mobile bowsers |

**Stabilisation Practices**

| Condition | Deadline | Method |
|---|---|---|
| Inactive areas | 14 days from last disturbance (7 days if draining to high-status waterbody) | Temporary seed, mulch, hydroseeding, or erosion blanket |
| Final grade reached | Within 14 days (or as specified in CAR licence) | Permanent seeding + mulch or turf |
| Steep slopes (>1:3) | Immediately after grading | Biodegradable erosion control blanket + seeding |

### 4. Inspection & Monitoring

**Schedule**

| Trigger | Frequency |
|---|---|
| Routine active works | Weekly (every 7 calendar days) during active construction |
| Storm event | Within 24 hours following cessation of runoff |
| Reduced frequency | Monthly if site is temporarily stabilised (with SEPA agreement) |

**Inspector**: Competent person with CAR/PPP/SEPA knowledge. Must be able to identify pollution risks and control measure effectiveness.

**Each inspection must document:**

- [ ] All control measures operational and maintained
- [ ] No evidence of sediment or pollution leaving site
- [ ] Exposed slopes and disturbed areas assessed
- [ ] Sediment levels in basins/traps
- [ ] Construction access functioning (no mud tracking onto roads)
- [ ] Material storage properly contained
- [ ] New discharge pathways identified
- [ ] Prior corrective actions completed

**Corrective actions**: Initiate within 24 hours, complete before next forecast rain event or within 7 days, whichever is sooner.

### 5. Recordkeeping

| Record | Retention |
|---|---|
| Current PPP | Full project duration + 2 years post-completion |
| Inspection reports, rainfall records, maintenance logs | 2 years after project completion |
| PPP amendments (all versions) | 2 years after completion |
| Waste transfer notes, SEPA correspondence | 2 years (or as per waste regulations) |

### 6. Amendments

PPP is a living document. Amend when:
- Control measures prove ineffective, Site conditions change (new phases, redesign)
- Inspections reveal new pollution risks, SEPA directs changes or operator changes

Log each amendment with date, description, and responsible person.

### 7. Personnel & Training

- Identify PPP administrator / site manager by name and contact, All personnel: induction covering PPP requirements, pollution prevention duties, spill response procedures, SEPA reporting obligations, Refresher: at each project phase change or annually, whichever is sooner

### 8. Appendices

- A: Site plans (existing conditions, drainage, phasing, control measure locations)
- B: Control measure detail drawings (silt fence, settlement pond, access, outfall)
- C: SEPA CAR registration / licence documentation, D: Inspection report forms (blank + completed examples)
- E: Spill response procedure and contact list, F: Subcontractor pollution prevention agreements, G: Designated site / protected species correspondence (if applicable)

## Guidelines

- **SEPA authorisation** - Construction sites in Scotland may operate under GBRs (General Binding Rules), require a CAR licence (Level 1, Authorisation) for certain scales, or Level 2 / Level 3 for higher-risk sites. Determine applicable tier from SEPA's WAT-RM-08 `[VERIFY]`.
- **Key GBRs for construction** - GBR 10 (discharges from construction sites), GBR 11 (surface water drainage), GBR 12 (SuDS maintenance) - verify current SEPA guidance for your site `[VERIFY]`.
- **SuDS** - Sustainable Drainage Systems are mandatory for most new developments in Scotland under the Water Environment and Water Services (Scotland) Act 2003; must be designed and maintained per Sewers for Scotland standards and SEPA WAT-RM-08.
- **Designated sites** - SSSI/SAC/SPA near the site may trigger additional consent from NatureScot, flag prominently in site description.
- **Historic environment** - Scheduled monuments or listed buildings on or near site may require consultation with Historic Environment Scotland.
- **Linear projects** (roads, pipelines, utilities) - use phased approach with separate PPP sections per active segment.
- **Regulatory citations**: The Water Environment (Controlled Activities) (Scotland) Regulations 2011 (SSI 2011/209) as amended; Water Environment and Water Services (Scotland) Act 2003; Environmental Protection Act 1990 (waste duty of care); SEPA GBRs `[VERIFY current GBR version]`.
- **Mark all regulatory references with `[VERIFY]`** unless user provides current SEPA guidance/authorisation.
- **Do not certify** - include signature blocks but operator (not drafter) must sign.

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

- **Regulatory Authority**: Scottish Environment Protection Agency (SEPA) replaces US EPA. No equivalent to US NPDES state programs, SEPA is the single regulatory body for Scotland.
- **Governing Regulations**: The Water Environment (Controlled Activities) (Scotland) Regulations 2011 (SSI 2011/209 as amended) - "CAR" regulations. Construction sites with disturbance >0.5 hectares are typically regulated under GBRs; larger/higher-risk sites require CAR licence.
- **No "SWPPP"**: The Scottish equivalent is a Pollution Prevention Plan (PPP), Site Water Management Plan, or Construction Environmental Management Plan (CEMP) including a water management section.
- **Key Distinctions**: No US CGP (Construction General Permit) system; no 40 CFR references; no US-specific Clean Water Act §402. Units are metric (hectares, m², mm rainfall). SEPA charges application fees for CAR licences.
- **SuDS**: Mandatory for new developments in Scotland (Water Environment and Water Services (Scotland) Act 2003) - not discretionary as under US Clean Water Act.
- **Terminology**: US term → Scottish equivalent: SWPPP → Pollution Prevention Plan (PPP) / Site Water Management Plan; EPA → SEPA; NPDES → CAR (Controlled Activities Regulations); CGP → CAR General Binding Rules or Licence; Best Management Practices → Control Measures / Mitigation Measures; stormwater → surface water run-off; acre → hectare; ft → m; inches → mm; 303(d) listed waters → WEWS classification high-staus waterbodies.
