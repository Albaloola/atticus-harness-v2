# FCA Suitability & Consumer Duty, Guidance Note for Scotland/UK

## Overview

The UK does not have a direct equivalent to SEC Regulation Best Interest or FINRA Rule 2111. Instead, the FCA regulates financial advice and recommendations through:

1. **COBS (Conduct of Business Sourcebook)** - COBS 9 (Suitability), COBS 9A (Assessing suitability: the obligations)
2. **Consumer Duty (PRIN 2A)** - Cross-cutting rules on consumer outcomes
3. **FCA Handbook SYSC** - Systems and Controls

## Key FCA Rules

| US Rule | UK Equivalent |
|---------|--------------|
| SEC Reg BI | FCA Consumer Duty (PRIN 2A) + COBS 9A |
| FINRA Rule 2111 (Suitability) | COBS 9 / COBS 9A |
| Form CRS | Key Information Document (KID) / Key Features Document |
| SEC 17a-4 recordkeeping | FCA SYSC 13 (Recordkeeping) |
| SEC best execution | FCA COBS 11 (Best Execution) |

## Consumer Duty (PRIN 2A) - Four Outcomes

The Consumer Duty requires firms to deliver good outcomes for retail customers:

1. **Products and Services** - designed to meet needs of identifiable target market
2. **Price and Value** - fair value; no relationship between higher cost and poorer consumer outcome
3. **Consumer Understanding** - communications meet information needs; enable informed decisions
4. **Consumer Support** - fit for purpose; barriers identified and removed

Plus three cross-cutting rules:
- Act in good faith, Avoid foreseeable harm, Enable and support retail customers to pursue their financial objectives

## COBS 9A Suitability Obligations

When providing personal recommendations to retail clients:

- Understand the client's knowledge, experience, risk tolerance, objectives, financial situation, Obtain sufficient information (COBS 9A.2.3)
- Assess suitability against each recommendation (COBS 9A.2.1)
- Provide suitability report (COBS 9A.3)
- Keep records of the assessment (6 years minimum)

## Scotland-Specific

- FCA regulation extends to Scotland; Scottish firms must register with FCA, Scottish courts (Sheriff Court / Court of Session) have jurisdiction for FCA enforcement claims, Interdict (injunction in Scotland) available for FCA enforcement per FSMA, UK Corporate Governance Code applies to Scottish-incorporated companies listed on Main Market
