## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

- **Term sheet / commitment letter** - principal, rate, maturity, fees, borrowing base
- **Party documents** - formation documents, good standing, board resolutions
- **Collateral info** - asset schedules, equipment lists (serial numbers), Companies House charges register searches, Land Register of Scotland searches, title reports
- **Financial statements** - audited/unaudited, used to set covenant levels
- **Due diligence** - disclosed litigation, existing charges, material contracts, environmental reports
- **Subordination/intercreditor agreements** - if junior or senior debt coexists

[SCOTS: Note: UK company charges are registered under the Companies Act 2006 ss 859A-859H. Scotland maintains its own register of charges at Companies House. The registration deadline is 21 days from creation of the charge (not a filing deadline measured from the date of the instrument). Failure to register renders the charge void against a liquidator, administrator, or other secured creditor.]

## Quick Start

1. Identify parties and collateral from term sheet
2. Draft granting clause with security descriptions under Scots law / Companies Act 2006
3. Set loan economics (rate, repayment, default rate, prepayment)
4. Build reps, covenants, and default provisions
5. Define perfection steps, register charge at Companies House within 21 days
6. Add governing law (Scots law or English law), remedies, and boilerplate

## Agreement Sections

### 1. Parties and Recitals

For each party: exact legal name per formation documents, entity type, jurisdiction/date of formation (identify Scottish company, English company, or other), principal business address, registered office, registered number, authorised signatories per board resolution.

Recitals state: business purpose of financing, existing relationship (if any), and consideration basis (security grant in exchange for loan).

### 2. Loan Terms

- **Principal** - numerals and words ("Five Million Pounds (£5,000,000.00)")
- **Interest** - fixed (state rate) or variable (SONIA + spread, floor/ceiling, day count convention: Act/365).
- **Repayment** - interest-only period, amortisation schedule, balloon, maturity date.
- **Default rate** - contract rate + 2 to 5%. No general usury cap for commercial loans in the UK. Consumer credit regulated by Consumer Credit Act 1974.
- **Late charge** - typically 5% of overdue payment; no statutory cap for commercial loans.
- **Prepayment** - permitted/prohibited; if permitted: minimum amount, notice period, breakage costs or premium formula (consider FCA rules on early repayment for regulated mortgages).
- **Consumer credit carve-out**: If borrower is an individual or partnership of fewer than 4 persons, consider Consumer Credit Act 1974 implications. Most commercial loans to limited companies are exempt.

### 3. Security and Collateral

**Granting clause**: The borrower grants the lender a fixed security / floating charge (or both) over the collateral described, now owned or hereafter acquired.

**Security types available under Scots/UK law**:

| Collateral Type | Security Method |
|---|---|
| Heritable property (land/buildings in Scotland) | Standard security, registered at Registers of Scotland (Land Register) |
| Book debts / receivables | Fixed charge or assignment, registered at Companies House |
| Inventory / stock | Floating charge, registered at Companies House |
| Plant and machinery | Fixed charge with serial numbers, registered at Companies House |
| Intellectual property | Fixed charge; record at UK IPO / Copyright Office is advisable but not mandatory for perfection under Companies Act |
| Shares in subsidiaries / investments | Fixed charge or share pledge with share certificate delivery |
| Bank accounts (Scotland/UK) | Fixed charge. [SCOTS: Note: "Control agreements" (UCC § 9-104) are not recognised in Scotland. A charge over a bank account is perfected by registration at Companies House, not by control. The lender should also take an assignation of the borrower's rights under the account mandate or obtain a letter of non-objection from the bank.] |
| Floating charge (all assets/surplus) | Floating charge registered at Companies House, crystallises on default or insolvency |

**Perfection mechanics**:

| Security Type | Perfection Method | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| Company charge (general) | Registration at Companies House (apply online via WebFiling or paper MR01) | 21 days from creation |
| Standard security over heritable property (Scotland) | Registration at Regents of Scotland (Land Register) | Before competing interest arises |
| Floating charge (Scotland) | Registration at Companies House (separate Scottish register, Scotland has its own register which requires separate filing) | 21 days from creation |
| Intellectual property | Registration at Companies House, plus UK IPO recordation for patents/trade marks recommended | 21 days for CH filing |
| Ship mortgage | Registration at UK Ship Register | Before competing interest |

[SCOTS: Note: The Scottish register of charges at Companies House is a separate register from the English/Welsh register. Companies registered in Scotland must file on the Scottish register. Although the MR01 form is used UK-wide, the Scottish register has its own index. The charge document should also comply with the Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995 (for Scottish documents).]

**Collateral maintenance**: keep in good repair; insure at full replacement value with lender noted as loss payee (Scotland: confirm with insurer regarding "assignation of insurance proceeds"); no disposals except ordinary-course stock sales; no further charges unless permitted.

### 4. Representations and Warranties

Required reps: organisation/good standing, authority (including Scottish company authority requirements under Companies Act 2006), enforceability, title to assets (free of charges except permitted), financial statement accuracy (UK GAAP/IFRS), litigation disclosure, legal compliance, environmental compliance.

Key rules:
- Reps survive signing; deemed remade on each advance or compliance certificate date, Apply "material adverse change" qualifier to operational reps only, Never qualify organisation, authority, title, or enforceability reps

### 5. Covenants

**Affirmative**: maintain existence and good standing; maintain insurance; pay taxes; comply with law; notify lender of defaults, litigation, material adverse change, collateral loss above threshold, or name/structure changes; cooperate on Companies House registration amendments.

**Financial reporting**:

| Report | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Annual audited financial statements (UK GAAP/IFRS) | 9 months after financial year-end (or earlier per agreement) |
| Quarterly management accounts | 45 days after quarter-end |
| Monthly (if high-risk) | 30 days after month-end |
| Compliance certificate | With each periodic financial statement |

[SCOTS: Note: UK GAAP and IFRS apply instead of US GAAP. Financial statements are filed at Companies House under the Companies Act 2006. Private companies may qualify for audit exemption if below statutory thresholds.]

**Financial covenants** (set from trailing 12-month actuals with 15 to 25% cushion): min. fixed charge coverage (≥ 1.20 to 1.50x), max. leverage (Debt/EBITDA per term sheet), min. current ratio, min. tangible net worth. Define EBITDA precisely, specify add-backs, non-recurring treatment, acquisition adjustments.

**Negative covenants** - restrict without lender consent: additional borrowing, further charges, dividends/distributions, asset disposals outside ordinary course, mergers/change of control, affiliate transactions (must be arm's-length), investments/acquisitions, prepayment of junior debt.

Define **change of control**: transfer of >25 to 50% voting equity, majority board change, or key management change.

### 6. Events of Default

| Default | Cure |
|---|---|
| Payment (principal) | None |
| Payment (interest/fees) | 3 to 5 business days |
| Rep breach | None (or 10 days if curable) |
| Financial covenant | None |
| Non-financial covenant | 15 to 30 days |
| Cross-default (> threshold) | Per triggering agreement |
| Voluntary insolvency (administration/liquidation) | None, automatic acceleration |
| Involuntary insolvency | 60 days to dismiss |
| Judgment > threshold unsatisfied | 30 days |
| Collateral impairment / charge invalidity (failure to register) | None |
| Change of control | None |

### 7. Remedies

Upon default beyond cure period:

1. **Acceleration** - all amounts immediately due; automatic on insolvency event
2. **Enforcement of security** (Scotland/UK):
   - Appoint administrative receiver or administrator (depending on floating charge created before 15 September 2003 or after)
   - Standard security enforcement (Scotland): calling-up notice, notice of default, or entering into possession under the Heritable Securities (Scotland) Act 1894 (as amended) / Conveyancing and Feudal Reform (Scotland) Act 1970
   - Take possession of charged assets with or without court order
3. **Disposition** - sale of charged assets; commercially reasonable steps required; proceeds applied: enforcement costs → accrued interest → principal → surplus to borrower
4. **Deficiency** remains borrower's liability
5. **Set-off** - apply deposits held by lender (subject to set-off provisions in agreement)
6. **Equitable relief** - interdict/injunction as available
7. Remedies are **cumulative**; borrower pays all enforcement costs including legal fees on solicitor-and-client basis

[SCOTS: Note on floating charges and administration: The Enterprise Act 2002 (effective 15 September 2003) abolished the power to appoint an administrative receiver for floating charges created after that date, replacing it with administration (Schedule B1 Insolvency Act 1986). For floating charges created pre-15 September 2003, the holder may still appoint an administrative receiver or proceed under administration. Enforcement of a Scottish floating charge requires appointment of an administrator under Schedule B1 or, for pre-2003 charges, an administrative receiver.]

### 8. Governing Law and Boilerplate

- **Governing law** - Scots law or English law (contractual choice will generally be upheld). Specify clearly and consider which jurisdiction's property law governs different assets.
- **Venue** - exclusive jurisdiction (Court of Session or Sheriff Court for Scots law; High Court for English law). Borrower waives objections to inconvenient forum.
- **Service of process** - specify address for service in jurisdiction. For Scottish companies: registered office at Companies House.
- **Notices** - delivery by hand, registered post (Royal Mail), or email. Scottish notices under standard securities must comply with statutory requirements.
- **Amendments** - written and signed only; no waiver by course of dealing.
- **Assignment** - borrower may not assign; lender may assign freely and grant participations.
- **Indemnification** - borrower indemnifies lender except for gross negligence/wilful misconduct; survives repayment.
- **Definitions** - alphabetical: EBITDA, Material Adverse Change, Business Day, Security, Event of Default, Permitted Charges.

**Exhibits**: compliance certificate form, borrowing request form (if revolving), asset schedule, disclosure schedules (existing charges, litigation, permitted indebtedness).

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This section provides a structured reference for converting the US UCC-based framework above to Scottish/UK legal practice.

### Terminology Conversion Table

| UCC-1 Financing Statement | MR01 (Companies House) / Registration of charge at Companies House |

| Control agreement (UCC § 9-104) | Not recognised in Scotland. Use fixed charge over bank account + bank non-objection letter |
| Purchase money security interest (PMSI) | Not a distinct concept in Scotland/UK. Fixed charge or hire purchase / finance lease used instead |
| USPTO / Copyright Office recordation (IP) | UK IPO (patents/trade marks); no formal copyright registry in the UK |
| Debtor / Secured party | Borrower / Lender (or Chargor / Chargee) |
| Security agreement | Loan and Security Agreement / Debenture (creates both fixed and floating charges) |
| Secured party / secured creditor | Secured creditor / Charge-holder |
| Usury laws | No general usury for commercial loans in UK. Consumer Credit Act 1974 applies to consumer loans |
| GAAP | UK GAAP / IFRS |
| Administrative stay / Bankruptcy Code automatic stay | Moratorium in administration (Schedule B1 Insolvency Act 1986) / Liquidation stays |
| County recorder / Secretary of State | Companies House (for company charges) / Registers of Scotland (for heritable property) |
| Accrued interest / default interest | Same concept applies; no usury cap for commercial |
| Waiver of jury trial | Not standard in Scotland/UK commercial loan agreements. Civil trials in Scotland are judge-only (no civil jury in Sheriff Court; Court of Session juries limited to certain actions) |

### Charge Registration Procedure (Scotland/UK)

1. **Create the charge**: The security document creates the charge (fixed or floating) on signing
2. **File MR01**: Particulars must be delivered to Companies House within 21 days of creation (ss 859A-859B)
3. **Online filing** (recommended): £14 fee via WebFiling
4. **Paper filing**: £24, post MR01 to: The Registrar of Companies, Companies House, Crown Way, Cardiff CF14 3UZ (or Edinburgh Quay 2 for Scottish companies)
5. **Certificate of registration**: Companies House issues a certificate which is conclusive evidence of compliance
6. **Failure to register**: Charge is void against liquidator, administrator, or other creditor; debt becomes immediately repayable

### Scottish Company Charges, Special Points

- Scotland has a separate register of charges maintained by Companies House, Scottish floating charges are governed by the Companies Act 2006 ss 462-466 (special Scottish provisions)
- The charge instrument for a Scottish company should: comply with Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995; identify the property specifically; state whether it is a fixed or floating charge, Floating charges created by Scottish companies attach to property when crystallise (on default or insolvency) - the Scottish courts recognise automatic crystallisation clauses

### Forms Available in scots-forms/

- **MR01 v3.0**: Companies House form for registering particulars of a charge (current version)
- **RCS Chapter 35**: Recovery of Evidence rules (related diligence)

### Key Statutory References

- Companies Act 2006, Part 25 (ss 859A-859H) - registration of company charges, Companies Act 2006, Part 18 (ss 462-466 - Scottish floating charges)
- Conveyancing and Feudal Reform (Scotland) Act 1970 - standard securities, Heritable Securities (Scotland) Act 1894 - enforcement, Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995 - formal validity, Insolvency Act 1986, Schedule B1 - administration, Enterprise Act 2002 - abolition of administrative receivership, Consumer Credit Act 1974 - consumer lending regulation, Environmental Protection Act 1990, Part IIA, contaminated land
