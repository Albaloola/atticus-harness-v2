Drafts a subordination agreement establishing creditor claim priority, payment waterfall mechanics, and intercreditor rights for debt transactions under Scots law, with regard to the Insolvency Act 1986, the Companies Act 2006 (Register of Charges), and relevant Scottish security instruments (standard security, floating charge, assignation in security, arrestment).

## Quick Start

Gather before drafting:

1. **Senior Debt instruments** - principal, rate, maturity, security/collateral (floating charge, standard security, assignation), default provisions
2. **Junior Debt instruments** - same details plus any existing subordination or intercreditor arrangements
3. **Party information** - legal names, registered address (Companies House), jurisdictions (Scotland/England), authorised signatories for Senior Creditor, Junior Creditor, and Debtor
4. **Security interests** - collateral descriptions, ranking of securities (e.g., ranking of floating charges), guarantees, cross-default provisions; note registration requirements under the Companies Act 2006 s.859A (register of charges)
5. **Commercial terms** - complete vs. partial subordination, permitted junior payments (if any), standstill duration

## Core Workflow

Draft the following sections in order.

### 1. Recitals

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Senior Debt | Material terms, principal, rate, maturity, instrument date; identify the security (if any) |
| Junior Debt | Material terms, same |
| Business purpose | Why subordination is needed |
| Consideration | What supports the agreement (may include mutual obligations or "for good and onerous causes") |

### 2. Definitions

- **"Senior Debt"** - principal, accrued interest, fees, enforcement costs, post-liquidation interest (unless ranking of charges or contract restricts)
- **"Junior Debt"** - full scope of junior obligations
- **"Payment in Full"** - treatment of contingent obligations, indemnities, post-liquidation interest; note that in Scottish insolvency, a ranking of floating charge may define this differently
- **"Permitted Junior Payments"** - carve-outs from subordination (if partial)
- **"Default Period"** - trigger for standstill and enforcement restrictions

### 3. Subordination Mechanics

Waterfall: Senior Debt (principal + interest + fees + enforcement costs) → only after Payment in Full → Junior Debt.

- [ ] All Junior Debt payments/distributions/recoveries subordinated to prior Payment in Full of Senior Debt
- [ ] Junior Creditor barred from receiving payments except Permitted Junior Payments (if any)
- [ ] Standstill: no acceleration, enforcement, or remedy by Junior Creditor without Senior Creditor consent during Default Period. In Scottish terms: no calling-up of standard security, no enforcement of floating charge crystallisation, no diligence (arrestment, adjudication, pointing) by Junior Creditor.
- [ ] Turnover: Junior Creditor must immediately remit any prohibited payments to Senior Creditor
- [ ] Trust relationship: turnover payments received in trust for Senior Creditor (Scots law recognises trust mechanisms; the *Bank of Scotland v Liquidators of Hutchison, Main & Co* line of cases supports creditor-trust arrangements)

### 4. Representations & Warranties

**All parties:** valid existence (Companies House register), enforceability of debt instruments, no existing defaults, accuracy of stated amounts, authority to execute, no conflicts (constitutional documents).

**Junior Creditor additionally:** no prior subordination to other creditors; no other subordination agreements exist.

### 5. Junior Creditor Covenants

- No amendments to Junior Debt adverse to Senior Creditor without consent, No additional security interests (standard securities, floating charges, assignations) without consent, Notice obligations on defaults or amendments, Cooperation in insolvency/administration proceedings, Insolvency voting: consistent with Senior Creditor interests or abstain

### 6. Events of Default and Remedies

**Triggers:** breach of subordination provisions, unauthorised Junior Debt amendments, enforcement actions violating standstill, Junior Creditor insolvency.

**Remedies:** specific implement (Scots law equivalent of specific performance), interdict (injunction), turnover enforcement, damages for breach of contract.

### 7. Insolvency and Bankruptcy (Scotland/UK)

- Subordination effective before, during, and after any insolvency process (administration, liquidation, receivership, sequestration)
- Draft to satisfy s.74 Insolvency Act 1986 (shareholder subordination) and common law recognition of contractual subordination. The leading UK authority on contractual subordination is *In re Maxwell Communications Corp* (No 2) [1994] 1 BCLC 1 (CA) and *Re SSSL Realisations (2002) Ltd* [2004] EWHC 2836 (Ch) [VERIFY]. Scots law follows the same principles (*Sharp v Thomson* 1995 SLT 837 on trust retention of title).
- Junior Creditor waives right to challenge subordination in insolvency (subject to s.239/240 IA 1986 - preferences/transactions at an undervalue may be challengeable)
- Junior Creditor to lodge subordination proofs of claim (in administration or liquidation)

| Issue | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Adequate protection payments | Typically none until Senior paid in full |
| Administration / DIP financing | Standing to object typically waived or limited |
| Plan participation (CVA/IVA) | Subject to subordination waterfall |
| Post-liquidation interest | Clarify whether included in "Payment in Full" - note the Scottish rule on post-liquidation interest (*re Spectrum Plus Ltd*) |

### 8. General Provisions

- **Governing law** - Scots law (or English law, but maintain consistency with underlying debt documentation)
- **Jurisdiction/venue** - match Senior Debt forum (Sheriff Court or Court of Session)
- **Amendments** - written consent of all parties; specify whether Debtor consent required
- **Survival** - continues until Senior Debt paid in full regardless of Debtor discharge/release in insolvency
- **Assignment** - binds successors; Junior Creditor may not assign without Senior Creditor consent
- **Execution** - witnesses required for execution by individuals; companies may execute under s.36 Companies Act 2006 (with a director and secretary, or two directors, or one director witnessed); registration at Companies House under s.859A if the agreement creates or records a charge.
- Severability, integration, waiver, notice provisions

### 9. Signature Blocks

- Senior Creditor, Junior Creditor, Debtor, Witnessed signature for individuals (Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995, s.2)
- Company execution per s.36 Companies Act 2006 (Scotland-registered companies: seal not required; signature by two directors or director and secretary)
- If agreement creates a registrable charge (e.g., an assignation in security of receivables), it must be registered at Companies House within 21 days under s.859A Companies Act 2006

## Pitfalls and Checks

- **Complete vs. partial** - confirm with user; if partial, define Permitted Junior Payments precisely (e.g., scheduled interest while no default exists); note that in Scottish practice, a "negative pledge" is a separate concept from subordination
- **Cross-references** - verify alignment with any existing intercreditor agreement, ranking of floating charges agreement, or loan document subordination provisions
- **Defined terms** - audit all cross-references and defined terms for consistency before finalising
- **Recording/registration** - if the agreement creates a charge over property in Scotland:
  - **Standard security** (over heritable property): register in the Land Register of Scotland (or Books of Council and Session for older interests)
  - **Floating charge** (Scottish company): register at Companies House Edinburgh within 21 days (s.859A Companies Act 2006); also register the ranking of floating charges agreement if agreed by both secured creditors
  - **Assignation in security**: may require registration or intimation to be effective against third parties
- **UCC equivalence** - no Article 9 filing system in Scotland/UK; use Register of Charges (Companies House) and Register of Inhibitions (for Scotland)
- **Multi-tier debt** - if more than two tiers (senior, mezzanine, junior), expand waterfall accordingly
- **No placeholders** - flag missing information as targeted client questions rather than inserting brackets
- **Ranking of floating charges** - Scots law allows a ranking agreement between floating charge holders without affecting the underlying charges; such ranking agreements are contractual and do not require court approval (*Re Woodroffes (Musical Instruments) Ltd* [1986] Ch 366, *Scottish & Newcastle plc v Ascot* [1997])
- **Floating charge crystallisation** - in Scotland, a floating charge crystallises on the appointment of an administrative receiver or the commencement of administration/liquidation. The ranking of charges agreement should address pre- and post-crystallisation priority.

### Key Scottish/UK Legal Framework

- **Insolvency Act 1986** - Part VI for Scotland: receivership provisions (Sch. 2); s.51 to 71 (floating charges, receivers); s.242/243 (gratuitous alienations and unfair preferences specific to Scotland); Sch. B1 (administration).
- **Companies Act 2006** - s.859A to 859Q: registration of charges (including Scottish floating charges). Note Scotland-specific charges: s.859A(6)(d/e/f) - floating charges, charges on heritable property, charges on property in Scotland.
- **Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995** - formal validity of deeds, subscription, witnessing.
- **Diligence (Scotland) Act 2007** - modernisation of diligence (debt enforcement) including arrestment, inhibition, adjudication, relevant to enforcement of creditor rights.
- **Bankruptcy and Diligence etc. (Scotland) Act 2007** - introduced new diligence mechanisms including land attachment and replaced poinding with attachment.
- **Floating Charges (Scotland) Act 1961** (now repealed; provisions in Companies Act 2006 and IA 1986) - historical framework for the Scottish floating charge.
- **Land Registration (Scotland) Act 2012** - governs registration of standard securities in the Land Register.
- **Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973** - rights under a subordination agreement prescribe after 5 years if unprobative; 20 years if probative.
