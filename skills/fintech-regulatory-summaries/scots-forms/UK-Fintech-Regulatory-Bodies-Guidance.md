# UK Fintech Regulatory Landscape, Reference for Scottish Compliance

## UK Financial Regulators

| US Agency | UK Equivalent |
|---|---|
| SEC | FCA (Financial Conduct Authority) - securities, markets, asset management, cryptoassets |
| CFTC | PRA (Prudential Regulation Authority) - systemic risk/bank prudential regulation (Bank of England) |
| CFPB | FCA (consumer credit, unfair practices), Financial Ombudsman Service (complaints) |
| FinCEN | No direct equivalent; AML/CTF supervised by FCA + HMRC (non-FCA firms) |
| OCC | PRA (bank authorisation and prudential supervision) |
| Federal Reserve | Bank of England (monetary policy, financial stability) |
| FDIC | FSCS (Financial Services Compensation Scheme) - deposit protection |
| State regulators | FCA is single UK regulator (except PRA for prudential) |
| FTC (consumer protection) | CMA (Competition & Markets Authority) - competition and consumer enforcement |

## Key UK Fintech Legislation

- **Financial Services and Markets Act 2000** (FSMA) - primary financial services legislation
- **Financial Services and Markets Act 2023** - post-Brexit reforms incl. cryptoasset regulation
- **Payment Services Regulations 2017** (SI 2017/752) - payment services, e-money
- **Electronic Money Regulations 2011** (SI 2011/99)
- **Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds Regulations 2017** (MLRs)
- **Consumer Credit Act 1974** (legacy but still relevant for certain lending)
- **Consumer Rights Act 2015** - consumer protections
- **UK GDPR / Data Protection Act 2018** - data protection
- **Financial Services and Markets Act 2023** - cryptoasset regulation framework

## Key UK Fintech Regulatory Timeline (2024-2026)

- **February 2026**: FSMA 2000 (Cryptoassets) Regulations 2026 made, cryptoassets brought within FCA regulatory remit
- **Q4 2025**: FCA cryptoasset consultation papers (CP25/40, CP25/41, CP25/42)
- **Digital Securities Sandbox** (DSS) - live from 2024 (FCA + Bank of England joint programme)
- **Open Banking**: CMA Order (Retail Banking Market Investigation) 2017, evolving into Open Finance under FCA
- **BNPL**: FCA regulation of Buy Now Pay Later via FCA consultation, legislation forthcoming
- **PSD3 / PSR**: UK Post-Brexit payment services framework being developed

## Scottish Considerations

- Financial services regulation is **reserved** (UK-wide); no separate Scottish regulator, Trading Standards Scotland (TSS) handles consumer protection enforcement in Scotland, Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS) covers Scotland, Sheriff Court handles consumer credit litigation
- **Key legal firms** with fintech practice in Scotland, Fintech Scotland (industry cluster body) promotes Scottish fintech ecosystem

## Regulatory Reporting (UK)

- **Regulatory reporting**: FCA Connect / Gabriel system
- **SUP 16** (FCA Handbook) - reporting requirements
- **Consumer Duty** (FCA Principle 12) - came into force 31 July 2023 (retail market)
- **SM&CR** (Senior Managers and Certification Regime) - accountability framework
- **Approved Persons** regime under FSMA (being replaced by SMCR)

## Key FCA Permissions (Authorisation Types)

- Payment Institution (API) / Small Payment Institution (SPI)
- Electronic Money Institution (EMI) / Small EMI, Consumer Credit (interim permission / full authorisation)
- Full FCA authorisation (MiFID investment firm, credit institution, insurer)
- EEA firm passporting: ended post-Brexit; Temporary Permissions Regime (TPR) now closed
