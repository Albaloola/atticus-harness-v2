# Domestic Abuse Protection in Scotland, Guidance Note

## Scottish Civil Remedies for Domestic Abuse

### Key Legislation

1. **Matrimonial Homes (Family Protection) (Scotland) Act 1981** - Matrimonial interdicts and exclusion orders
2. **Protection from Abuse (Scotland) Act 2001** - Power of arrest attached to interdicts
3. **Protection from Harassment Act 1997** (applies Scotland) - Non-harassment orders
4. **Domestic Abuse (Scotland) Act 2011** - Domestic abuse interdicts (broader than matrimonial, covers cohabitants, civil partners, intimate relationships)
5. **Domestic Abuse (Scotland) Act 2018** - Creates criminal offence of domestic abuse (psychological, coercive control)
6. **Children (Scotland) Act 1995** - Parental responsibilities and rights
7. **Civil Partnership Act 2004** - Applies same protection to civil partners
8. **Family Law (Scotland) Act 2006** - Cohabitant rights

### Available Orders (US → Scotland Mapping)

| US DVRO | Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Temporary Restraining Order (TRO) | Interim interdict (granted before full hearing) |
| Permanent Protective Order | Interdict (permanent / until further order) |
| Stay-away order (residence, workplace) | Interdict with power of arrest under PASA 2001 |
| Move-out order (residence exclusion) | Exclusion order (Matrimonial Homes Act 1981 s.4) |
| Child custody / visitation | Section 11 orders under Children (Scotland) Act 1995 |
| Firearms relinquishment | No specific DV-related firearms provision, general firearms licensing (Police Scotland) |
| No-contact order | Interdict prohibiting contact (including indirect) |
| Full Faith and Credit (interstate) | Enforcement in England/Wales/NI under Family Law Act 1986 |

### Court Process

- **Sheriff Court** - Main forum for domestic abuse civil remedies (both interdicts and exclusion orders)
- **Court of Session** - For complex or high-value cases, Applications can be made **without notice** (ex parte) for interim interdicts, Petitioner applies by **Initial Writ** (Court of Session) or **Summary Application** (Sheriff Court)

### Evidence Required

- Chronological incident log (similar to US)
- Police reports (Police Scotland incident numbers)
- Medical evidence (GP, A&E records)
- Photographs of injuries / damage, Communications (texts, emails, social media)
- Witness statements, Previous criminal proceedings (Domestic Abuse Scotland Act 2018 charges)

### Practical Differences from US System

1. **No standardised form set** - Scottish courts do not use prescribed form packages like California's DV-100 series
2. **Solicitor required** - Most applicants require a solicitor (Legal Aid available for qualifying cases)
3. **Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service (SCTS)** manages all court processes
4. **No "CLETS" database** - different enforcement notification system
5. **Power of arrest** - Must be expressly applied for under PASA 2001; not automatic with interdict
6. **Family mediation** - Encouraged but never in domestic abuse cases
7. **Legal Aid** - Available through Scottish Legal Aid Board (SLAB) for qualifying applicants
8. **Support organisations** - Scottish Women's Aid, ASSIST (advocacy), Rape Crisis Scotland

### Scottish Specifics

- **Matrimonial interdict**: Requires marriage/civil partnership; covers matrimonial home
- **Domestic abuse interdict** (2011 Act): Broader, covers any intimate relationship
- **Relevant interdict** (Civil Partnership Act 2004): For civil partners
- **Exclusion order**: Can exclude the abuser from the family home even if they own it
- **Children**: Child welfare hearing in Sheriff Court if custody/contact issues

### Key Terms

- **Pursuer**: The person seeking protection (Petitioner/Applicant)
- **Defender**: The person alleged to have committed abuse (Respondent)
- **Interdict**: Court order prohibiting specific conduct (equivalent to injunction)
- **Interim Interdict**: Emergency/temporary order (equivalent to TRO)
- **Exclusion Order**: Order requiring defender to leave the family home
- **Power of Arrest**: Attached to interdict for breach, police can arrest without warrant
- **Breach of interdict**: Civil contempt; may result in fine or imprisonment
