# Scotland Home Report, Reference Summary

## Legal Basis

- **Housing (Scotland) Act 2006** - Part 3 (ss. 98 to 111)
- **The Selling of Residential Property (Home Reports) (Scotland) Regulations 2011** (SSI 2011/160)
- **Housing (Scotland) Act 2014** (minor amendments)

## When Required

Any residential property marketed for sale in Scotland must have a Home Report available before the property is marketed. The seller (or their estate agent) is responsible for commissioning and providing it.

## Exemptions

- New-build properties sold directly by a builder (first sale)
- Properties sold by a trustee in sequestration (bankruptcy)
- Properties sold by a liquidator or receiver, Sales to a "connected person" (e.g., transfer between spouses)
- Holiday/short-term let properties (certain circumstances)
- Mixed-use properties where residential element is ancillary

## Three Components

### 1. Single Survey

Prepared by a qualified surveyor (RICS or SAVA registered). Covers:

- Condition ratings (1 = no immediate repair; 2 = repairs needed; 3 = urgent repairs)
- Accessibility and services, External and internal condition of buildings, Garden grounds, outbuildings, parking, Environmental matters (flood risk, radon, ground stability)
- Market valuation, Accessibility for disabled persons

The surveyor must be independent, not connected to the seller, buyer, or agent.

### 2. Energy Performance Certificate (EPC)

Valid for 10 years. Required under **Energy Performance of Buildings (Scotland) Regulations 2008**. Assesses energy efficiency on a scale of A (best) to G (worst). Includes recommendations for improvement. The EPC must be lodged on the Scottish EPC Register.

### 3. Property Questionnaire

Completed by the seller. Covers:

- Council Tax band and charges, Factoring/management arrangements, Parking arrangements, Alterations, extensions, and associated consents/guarantees, Specialist installers (windows, roof, heating, electrical)
- Flooding history, Noise, access, and neighbour issues, Trees and boundaries, Service occupiers/tenants, Insurance details (if known)

The seller completes this at the time of marketing and must update it if circumstances change before settlement.

## Delivery Requirements

- Available to prospective buyers **before the property is marketed** (s.98 Housing (Scotland) Act 2006)
- Must be provided **free of charge** to any person requesting it, Can be provided in electronic form (PDF) or hard copy, Estate agents must ensure the report is available on request

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

- Local authority may serve a compliance notice, Fine up to level 3 on the standard scale (currently £1,000) for failure to provide, Estate agent / seller jointly and severally liable

## Relationship to Caveat Emptor

Scotland retains the principle of **caveat emptor** (buyer beware) in heritable property transactions. The Home Report does not replace the buyer's duty to inspect and investigate. However, failure to disclose known material defects (in the Property Questionnaire or otherwise) may constitute **misrepresentation** entitling the buyer to rescind the contract or claim damages.

## Key Practical Points

- The Single Survey remains the property of the seller or lender and is provided for information only, The buyer should always commission their own independent survey, The Home Report is **not** a warranty of condition, The seller should keep the Home Report updated if material changes occur, A new Home Report is required if marketing resumes after a gap of more than 30 days
