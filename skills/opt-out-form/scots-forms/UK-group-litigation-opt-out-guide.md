# Group Litigation & Opt-Out Forms, UK/Scotland Guidance Note

## Class Actions & Group Proceedings in the UK

### Scotland, Group Proceedings (Civil Litigation (Expenses and Group Proceedings) (Scotland) Act 2018)

The **Civil Litigation (Expenses and Group Proceedings) (Scotland) Act 2018** introduced group proceedings in Scotland.

**Key Features:**
- **Opt-in model** - individuals must actively join the proceedings, Available in the **Court of Session** only, The court must grant a **group proceedings order** (GPO) certifying the action, Applies to claims with "same, similar, or related" issues of fact or law
- **No opt-out model** (unlike the Competition Appeal Tribunal in England)

**Who can bring group proceedings:**
- A person who has a claim, A representative body (with sufficient interest) on behalf of a group, Court must be satisfied that the GPA brings the action on behalf of a defined group

**Expenses (costs) rules:**
- Modified "loser pays" regime, The group member is only liable for expenses in proportion to their share of damages sought, Protects group members from adverse cost exposure (safer than individual litigation)
- Success fee agreements (Damages-Based Agreements) available subject to regulation

### England & Wales, Collective Actions

**Competition Appeal Tribunal (CAT)** - Opt-out and opt-in collective actions available under the Competition Act 1998 s.47B (introduced by the Consumer Rights Act 2015)

**Features:**
- **Opt-out** available for competition damages claims, Opt-out class members can exclude themselves
- **Opt-in** also available (members must actively participate)
- Certified by the CAT: class representative, class definition, claims raise same/related issues, Damages opt-out: Distributed to class members; unclaimed amounts paid to Access to Justice Foundation

**Group Litigation Orders (GLOs):**
- Available under CPR 19.11 for general civil claims (not opt-out)
- Case management tool for multiple claims with common issues, All claimants must opt in by being named on the Group Register

### Arbitration Opt-Outs (Consumer)

For consumer arbitration clauses:
- Consumer Rights Act 2015 - unfair terms in consumer contracts, Arbitration Act 1996 (applies UK-wide; Scotland has its own Arbitration Act 2010)
- Consumer arbitration: Unfair Arbitration Agreements (Specified Amount) Order 1999

### Privacy Opt-Outs (UK GDPR)

UK GDPR (Data Protection Act 2018):
- Right to object to processing (Art. 21)
- Right to withdraw consent (Art. 7(3))
- No statutory opt-out form, organisations develop their own

### Form Structure Differences

| US | UK/Scotland |
|---|---|
| FRCP 23 class action | Group proceedings (Scotland Act 2018) / GLO (EW) / CAT collective action |
| CAFA / Class Action Fairness Act | No equivalent, jurisdiction follows normal Scottish rules |
| Opt-out as default | Opt-in is default in Scotland; opt-out only in CAT |
| Postmark rule for deadlines | UK uses receipt rule typically |
| Notarisation sometimes required | No notarisation generally needed |
| Sign under penalty of perjury | Witnessed signature sufficient; no standardised certification |

### Form Format, Scottish Adaptations

- Court name: Sheriff Court (if ~£5K to £100K) or Court of Session (if high-value/complex)
- "Case caption" format matches Scottish civil procedure (Petition / Initial Writ numbering)
- Claim/account IDs as per governing instrument, Scottish court forms typically lodged via the **Civil Online portal** (Sheriff Court) or e-filing (Court of Session)
- Group proceedings opt-out forms would be directed to the Group Register Keeper

### Key Statutes, Scotland

- Civil Litigation (Expenses and Group Proceedings) (Scotland) Act 2018
- Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010
- Courts Reform (Scotland) Act 2014
- Data Protection Act 2018 (UK GDPR)
- Consumer Rights Act 2015 (applies GB-wide)
