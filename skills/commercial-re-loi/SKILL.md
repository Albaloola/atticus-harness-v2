# Heads of Terms, Scottish Commercial Real Estate [SCOTS]

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts non-binding Heads of Terms for commercial real estate acquisition in Scotland with enforceable confidentiality and exclusivity provisions. Scottish property law operates through the missives system; this document sets out agreed commercial terms before formal missives are concluded.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

- **Parties** - full legal names, entity types, registered addresses (buyer and seller)
- **Property** - postal address, Title Number (Land Register), description, included/excluded items
- **Deal terms** - price, deposit amount, financing structure, target entry date
- **Timelines** - exclusivity period, due diligence duration, acceptance deadline
- **Brokerage** - commission structure and responsible parties
- **AML** - note that solicitors have anti-money laundering obligations; identity verification required

## Due Diligence Scope Checklist

Include in the due diligence section:

- Physical/structural survey, Environmental (Phase I / Phase II if warranted); check for contaminated land under Part IIA EPA 1990
- Title investigation (Land Register; any Sasine search if not yet registered)
- Searches: Property Enquiry Certificate (local authority), Planning, Building Standards (warrants and completion certificates), Road adoption, Drainage, Lease review (if leasehold: terms, rent reviews, tenant obligations, break clauses)
- Service contracts and vendor agreements review, Property tax (non-domestic rates) and assessment history, Insurance history / claims, Listed building / conservation area / Tree Preservation Order checks, Coal mining / other mining searches (relevant across Scottish central belt)
- Asbestos survey (non-domestic)
- Utilities and easements

## Drafting Rules

- **Binding vs. non-binding** - Clearly separate non-binding commercial terms from binding provisions (confidentiality, exclusivity). In Scottish practice, Heads of Terms are generally not intended to create legal relations; the binding contract is formed by conclusion of missives.
- **Assignment** - Include buyer's right to assign to affiliates/SPEs (standard).
- **Placeholders** - Use `[___]` for all unknown amounts, dates, and party details.
- **Tone** - Professional and collaborative. Avoid adversarial framing.
- **Missives note** - This document is NOT missives. Formal conclusion of missives remains required for a binding contract in Scottish property law.
- **AML awareness** - Solicitors must comply with the Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds (Information on the Payer) Regulations 2017; identity verification required for both parties.

## Pitfalls

- **Missing binding/non-binding distinction** - Without clear demarcation, courts may treat entire document as intending legal relations. Always use explicit language.
- **Heads of Terms vs. Missives** - Heads of Terms are NOT the contract in Scotland. The contract is formed by exchange of formal missives (offer → acceptance). Distinguish clearly.
- **Local variation** - LBTT rates, planning requirements, and searches vary. Flag with `[Confirm per local practice / solicitor instruction]`.
- **Sasine Register vs. Land Register** - Most Scottish properties now registered in Land Register; older ones remain in Sasines. Requires different form of title investigation.
- **VAT** - Critical to clarify whether the seller has opted to tax the property; VAT at 20% may apply on top of price.
- **No legal advice disclaimer** - Always include a disclaimer that the Heads of Terms do not constitute legal advice and each party should consult their own solicitor.
- **Good faith** - Scottish contract law does not recognise a general duty of good faith in negotiations (unlike some US jurisdictions); frame exclusivity as a contractual obligation not a duty of good faith.

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill is adapted from US commercial real estate LOI practice to Scottish property law (the missives system):

| US Concept | Scottish Equivalent |
|------------|-------------------|
| Letter of Intent (LOI) | Heads of Terms (non-binding; missives form the contract) |
| Purchase and Sale Agreement (PSA) | Formal missives (offer → qualified acceptance → unqualified acceptance) |
| Earnest money / escrow | Deposit held by solicitor as stakeholder or in deposit scheme |
| Closing | Settlement / entry date (completion of missives followed by disposition) |
| Deed / Warranty deed | Disposition (registered in Land Register) |
| APN (Assessor's Parcel Number) | Title Number (Land Register) / Search Sheet (Sasines) |
| ALTA survey | Site survey / measured survey (no ALTA equivalent) |
| Title insurance | Less common; title investigation via searches and indemnity insurance available |
| Transfer taxes (state/county) | LBTT (Land and Buildings Transaction Tax) - buyer's liability |
| 1031 exchange | No direct equivalent; capital gains tax rollover relief available but different regime |
| Escrow company | Solicitors (stakeholder) or regulated deposit scheme |
| USD | GBP (£) |
| FRE / local rules | Scottish court rules (OCR / Sheriff Court rules) if dispute arises |
| Good faith duty (negotiations) | No general duty of good faith in Scots contract law |

## Concepts with No Direct Scottish Equivalent [FLAGGED]

- **1031 like-kind exchange** - no direct equivalent; UK capital gains tax deferral is different
- **ALTA / ACSM survey standards** - no equivalent; survey standards determined by RICS
- **Title insurance (standard practice)** - available but not standard in Scotland; title investigation is primary method
- **Escrow companies** - not used; solicitors hold deposit as stakeholders
- **PSA as binding contract** - in Scotland, formal missives create the contract, not a single document
- **APN / parcel number system** - no equivalent; Title Number in Land Register
- **Transfer taxes paid by seller** - LBTT is buyer's liability in Scotland (not seller as in many US states)

**[SCOTS] All US terminology, procedures, and cost allocations replaced with Scottish equivalents. The missives system replaces the PSA. LBTT replaces transfer taxes. GBP used throughout.**
