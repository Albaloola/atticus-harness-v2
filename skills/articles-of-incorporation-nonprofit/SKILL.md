Draft filing-ready formation documents for a **Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisation (SCIO)** or a **company limited by guarantee** registered as a charity with OSCR, satisfying the Scottish charity test and HMRC charitable tax relief conditions under UK tax law.

## Key Scottish Charity Framework

### Legal Forms for Scottish Charities
1. **Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisation (SCIO)** - most common; incorporated with OSCR (not Companies House); limited liability for charity trustees; regulated solely by OSCR. Suitable for most new charities operating in Scotland.
2. **Company Limited by Guarantee** - incorporated with Companies House, then registered with OSCR as a charity. Dual regulation; required for activities requiring company status beyond Scotland.
3. **Scottish Trust** - unincorporated; older form; personal liability for trustees.
4. **Unincorporated Association** - simplest; no limited liability.

### Regulator
- **OSCR** (Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator) - approves SCIO applications; maintains Scottish Charity Register
- **Companies House** - for companies limited by guarantee (parallel registration)
- **HMRC** (HM Revenue & Customs) - for charity tax relief (Gift Aid, business rates relief, VAT exemptions)

### Charity Test (Scotland)
Under the **Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005**, a body meets the charity test if:
1. Its purposes consist only of **charitable purposes** (s.7)
2. It provides **public benefit** (s.8)
3. No disqualifying political purposes

Charitable purposes are defined in s.7 and include: prevention of poverty, advancement of education, advancement of religion, advancement of health, saving of lives, advancement of citizenship/community development, advancement of arts/heritage/culture/science, advancement of public participation in sport, provision of recreational facilities, promotion of animal welfare, environmental protection, relief of those in need, and any other purpose analogous to these.

## Prerequisites

Collect before drafting:

1. **Charity name** - must not be the same as an existing SCIO/charity (check OSCR register); must end with "Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisation" or abbreviation "SCIO"
2. **Principal office** - Scottish address (physical; PO boxes may not be acceptable)
3. **Charity trustees** - minimum 2 (for SCIO); legal names, addresses, disqualification check
4. **Proposed members** - SCIO can have member or no-member model; membership confers voting rights and may include liability provisions (limited to £1 where applicable)
5. **Charitable purposes** - must meet OSCR's charity test; describe in sufficient detail for public benefit assessment
6. **Activities / service delivery** - programmes, beneficiaries, geographic scope (Scotland / UK / international)
7. **Governance model** - SCIO constitution: board-only ("no-member SCIO") vs. membership SCIO; trustee numbers, terms, committee structure
8. **Effective date** - OSCR specifies date of registration; SCIOs take effect when OSCR registers the organisation

## Quick Start

1. Confirm name availability on OSCR charity register
2. Choose SCIO (recommended for Scotland-only activity) or company limited by guarantee (for UK-wide or cross-border activity)
3. Draft the SCIO constitution following the OSCR model constitution or OSCR-guidance-compliant custom document
4. Ensure charitable purposes satisfy all elements of OSCR's charity test
5. Include all mandatory provisions (dissolution, trustee liability, benefit rule, political activity)
6. Submit to OSCR with SCIO application form

## SCIO Constitution Articles

### Article I, Name

```
The name of the organisation is [FULL NAME] Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisation
(or "[FULL NAME]" SCIO).
```

- Must include "SCIO" or "Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisation"
- Must not duplicate an existing OSCR-registered name, Must not imply government connection or inappropriate status

### Article II, Principal Office

```
The principal office of the SCIO is [FULL ADDRESS, TOWN/CITY, POSTCODE, SCOTLAND].
```

- Must be a physical address in Scotland, Changes must be notified to OSCR within a reasonable period

### Article III, Purposes / Objects

Define purposes meeting the charity test under s.7 of the 2005 Act with sufficient precision:

```
The organisation's purposes are:
3.1 The advancement of [EDUCATION / HEALTH / RELIGION / ARTS / etc.] for public benefit, in particular [DESCRIPTION OF ACTIVITIES / TARGET POPULATION].
3.2 The prevention of poverty among [TARGET BENEFICIARIES] in [GEOGRAPHIC AREA].
3.3 [ANY ADDITIONAL CHARITABLE PURPOSES].

All purposes are charitable purposes within the meaning of section 7 of the Charities
and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005 and any subsequent enactment.
```

- All purposes must fall within the 2005 Act's charitable purpose categories, Public benefit must be demonstrable (OSCR requires public benefit statement)
- Avoid commercial or private benefit language, OSCR publishes guidance on public benefit, Draft broadly enough to allow evolution without amendment but precisely enough for OSCR scrutiny

### Article IV, Charity Regulation Provisions

**Irrevocable, do not soften or remove.**

**Benefit Rule (no private inurement):**
```
No part of the organisation's property, income, or assets may be paid or transferred
(directly or indirectly) to any charity trustee or connected person except as:
(a) reasonable remuneration for goods or services provided to the organisation;
(b) interest on a loan at a commercial rate;
(c) rent for premises occupied by the organisation at a market rate;
(d) expenses properly incurred in carrying out duties for the organisation;
(e) reasonable and proper hospitality of a modest nature.
```

**Public Benefit:**
```
The organisation must carry out its activities for the public benefit within the
meaning of section 8 of the Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005.
```

**Political Activity Clause:**
```
The organisation may not carry out political activities unless they are non-party
political and carried out in furtherance of its charitable purposes. The organisation
may not support or oppose any political party or candidate for political office.
```

**Dissolution Clause:**
```
If the organisation is dissolved, its property remaining after satisfaction of all
debts and liabilities shall be applied for charitable purposes that are the same as
or similar to the organisation's purposes and approved by OSCR. If OSCR approval
cannot be obtained in advance, the property shall be transferred to a charity or
charities with similar purposes selected by OSCR.
```

### Article V, Membership and Governance

**No-member SCIO (most common for operating charities):**
```
The SCIO has no members. The charity trustees are the persons who comprise the
organisation. Any action requiring member approval under the 2005 Act shall instead
require the approval of the charity trustees.
```

**Member SCIO (if members exist):**
```
The SCIO has a membership class. Members shall pay an annual subscription of [AMOUNT].
Members in good standing shall have one vote at general meetings. Member liability
shall be limited to £1 (one pound).
```

**Charity Trustees / Board:**
```
The affairs of the organisation shall be managed by not fewer than [MIN] and not
more than [MAX] charity trustees. Charity trustees serve a term of [X] years and
may be re-elected for further terms.
```

- At minimum 2 charity trustees (SCIO requirement)
- Officers: Chair, Secretary, Treasurer (at minimum)
- Trustee eligibility, check OSCR disqualification rules:
  - Unspent conviction for dishonesty
  - Undischarged bankrupt
  - Previously removed as charity trustee by OSCR/charity commissioners
  - Subject to disqualification order

### Article VI, Incorporation Statement

```
The organisation is a Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisation constituted
under the Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005 and registered
as a charity in Scotland.
```

### Article VII, Duration

```
The duration of the SCIO shall be perpetual unless dissolved under the terms of
Article IV or the 2005 Act.
```

### Article VIII, Additional Provisions

**Charity Trustee Liability Limitation:**
```
A charity trustee shall not be personally liable for losses to the organisation
except where the loss arises from the trustee's own:
(a) breach of fiduciary duty;
(b) failure to exercise reasonable care and diligence;
(c) act or omission in bad faith;
(d) unlawful act or omission.
```

**Indemnity (where SCIO has insurance):**
```
The organisation may indemnify charity trustees to the full extent permitted by
Scottish common law, provided insurance is maintained under section 73 of the
2005 Act at the organisation's expense.
```

**Amendment Procedure:**
```
This constitution may be amended by resolution of [[X]% of trustees / two-thirds
of members present and voting], subject to the following restrictions:
(a) no amendment to Articles III or IV shall take effect without OSCR consent;
(b) no amendment shall alter the charitable status of the organisation;
(c) any amendment must be notified to OSCR within three months.
```

## Pitfalls and Checks

| Topic | Rule |
|---|---|
| Charity test (2005 Act) | All purposes must be charitable under s.7; public benefit test under s.8 - OSCR assesses both at registration |
| Political purposes | Cannot have political party purposes; non-party political campaigning limited to charitable-objects-related issues |
| Private benefit | Any trustee/connected person benefit must be expressly permitted by constitution (benefit rule) |
| SORP (Statement of Recommended Practice) | SCIOs prepare accounts under Charities SORP (FRS 102) |
| OSCR name check | Search the Scottish Charity Register online; also check Companies House for company-registered charities |
| Post-incorporation steps | Register for Gift Aid (HMRC Charities), obtain a charity tax reference, register as a data controller with ICO, check business rates relief with local council |
| Cross-border | If operating in England/Wales/outside Scotland, consider company limited by guarantee instead of SCIO (SCIOs can operate beyond Scotland but may encounter recognition issues in other UK jurisdictions) |
| [SCOTS: No IRS 501(c)(3)] | No equivalent to IRS determination letter; OSCR registration is the definitive charity recognition in Scotland. HMRC grants tax relief separately for UK tax purposes |

## Post-Formation Steps

1. □ **OSCR registration** - confirmation received and charity number obtained
2. □ **HMRC charity tax relief** - apply via HMRC Charities Online
3. □ **Bank account** - open charity bank account in SCIO name
4. □ **Data protection** - register with ICO (unless exempt)
5. □ **Employer / PAYE** - register with HMRC if employing staff
6. □ **Insurance** - charity trustee liability insurance (recommended), public liability
7. □ **Governing document** - file signed copy with OSCR within required period
8. □ **Annual return** - OSCR annual return and accounts due within 9 months of year-end
9. □ **Fundraising** - register with Fundraising Regulator if public fundraising in Scotland

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Legal framework:**
- Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005 (primary charity legislation)
- Charities (Registration of Overseas Charities) Regulations 2012
- Charities SORP (FRS 102) - accounting framework, Companies Act 2006 - applies to charitable companies limited by guarantee

**Key differences from US non-profit model:**
- **[SCOTS: No IRS 501(c)(3)]** - OSCR registration replaces IRS determination letter; HMRC tax relief is separate
- **[SCOTS: No IRC]*** - UK tax law (Corporation Tax Act 2010, Income Tax Act 2007) governs charity tax exemptions, SCIO is the recommended Scottish-specific form; no direct US equivalent (closest is a non-profit corporation with state registration)
- No state-by-state variations, Scotland has a single charity regulator and charity test, Charity trustee liability is governed by common law and the 2005 Act, Public benefit is a statutory test, not a common law presumption (rebuttal not available)
- No Form 1023 equivalent, OSCR application is simpler but requires detailed purposes, Gift Aid requires HMRC registration, no direct US equivalent, Political activity is permitted if non-party-political and in furtherance of charitable purposes, Legal fees: "loser pays" principle (judicial expenses) for any litigation
