# Scottish Charity Annual Reporting, OSCR Guidance

## Key Regulatory Framework

### Primary Legislation

1. **Charities and Trustee Investment (Scotland) Act 2005** - The principal statute governing Scottish charities
2. **Charities Accounts (Scotland) Regulations 2006 (SSI 2006/218)** - Accounting and reporting requirements
3. **Charity Re-registration Regulations 2023** - Recent reforms to charity register

### Regulatory Body

**OSCR, Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator**
- Independent regulator of Scottish charities, Maintains the Scottish Charity Register, Enforces compliance, investigates misconduct, Website: www.oscr.org.uk

### Key Differences from US State Charity Bureaus

| US Concept | Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| State Attorney General / Charity Bureau | OSCR (Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator) |
| IRS Form 990 | **OSCR Annual Return** (online filing) + **Trustees' Annual Report** + **Accounts** |
| EIN (Employer ID) | **Scottish Charity Number** (SC0xxxxx) |
| State charity registration | Scottish Charity Register (maintained by OSCR) |
| 501(c)(3) tax exemption | Charitable status recognised by HMRC (Charity Tax) |
| Penalty of perjury certification | Trustees' declaration / certification |
| Professional fundraiser registration | OSCR scrutiny of fundraising; no separate "professional fundraiser" register |
| State audit thresholds | OSCR thresholds: receipts & payments (under £250K) or full accrual (£500K+) |

### Annual Filing Requirements (Scotland)

Scottish charities must submit to OSCR within **9 months** of financial year-end:

1. **Online Annual Return** - Via OSCR online system
2. **Annual Accounts** - Receipts and payments OR fully accrued format
3. **Trustees' Annual Report** - Narrative summary of activities, governance, achievements
4. **External Scrutiny Report** - Independent examination (under £500K income) or audit (£500K+)

### Annual Return Content

- Charity name, SC number, contact details, Significant events, changes to purposes or structure, Financial summary: income, expenditure, net assets, Confirmation of governing document compliance, Trustee details (changes, appointments)
- Fundraising activities and regulation compliance

### Accounts Format

**Receipts and Payments (income under £250K):**
- Receipts: donations, grants, fundraising, trading, investments, Payments: charitable activities, governance, fundraising, Cash at bank/in hand at year-end

**Accruals (income £250K+):**
- Full SOFA (Statement of Financial Activities)
- Balance sheet, Notes to accounts, Comparatives (prior year)

### Trustees' Annual Report Content

- Reference and administrative details (charity name, SC number, principal office, trustees)
- Structure, governance and management, Objectives and activities, Achievements and performance, Financial review (income, reserves policy, investment policy)
- Plans for future periods, Public benefit statement (must demonstrate public benefit from activities)

### Audit / Independent Examination Thresholds

| Gross Income | Scrutiny Required |
|---|---|
| Under £25K | None (but still file accounts) |
| £25K to £500K | Independent examination (by a reporting accountant / qualified person) |
| £500K+ | Full audit (by registered auditor) |
| Assets over £3.26M + income over £500K | Full audit regardless of income |

### Fundraising Regulation

- **Fundraising Preference Service** - Public can opt out of direct charity fundraising
- **Fundraising Regulator** - Self-regulatory body (UK-wide, but applies to Scottish charities fundraising across UK)
- **Scottish Fundraising Working Group** - Guidance for charities fundraising in Scotland
- **OSCR scrutiny** - May investigate if fundraising breaches public trust

### Cross-Border / UK-Wide Charities

- Cross-border charity: registered with both OSCR and Charity Commission (EW)
- Must comply with reporting requirements of both jurisdictions, OSCR guidance on cross-border charity regulation available

### Key Differences Summary

| Area | US | Scotland |
|---|---|---|
| Filing deadline | Varies by state | 9 months from financial year-end |
| Tax ID | EIN (IRS) | Charity number (OSCR) + Corporation Tax (HMRC) |
| Form type | State-specific | OSCR online annual return + accounts + TAR |
| Financial thresholds | State-dependent | Receipts & payments vs. accruals by size |
| Fundraiser contracts | Required disclosures | OSCR investigation-based; no separate registration |
| Certification | Penalty of perjury | Trustees' signed declaration |
| Regulator | State AG / charity bureau | OSCR (single national regulator) |
