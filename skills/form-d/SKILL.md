# Form D, UK Exempt Offering Notice (Adapted)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a notice of exempt offering for UK securities regulation purposes. Based on SEC Form D but reoriented for UK law under the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (FSMA) and UK Prospectus Regulation.

## Prerequisites

1. **Issuer organisational documents** - articles of association, certificate of incorporation, memorandum
2. **Companies House registration number** - if previously assigned
3. **Offering materials** - information memorandum, subscription agreements, term sheets
4. **Related persons list** - all directors, company secretary, and promoters with business addresses
5. **Compensation arrangements** - broker/intermediary agreements, finder's fees, FCA FRN numbers
6. **Prior filings** - if this is an amendment to a previous filing

|-------|-------|
| Companies House Registration Number | From prior filings; blank if first filing |
| Filing Type | New Notice or Amendment (specify number) |

| Field | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| Legal Name | Exactly as in certificate of incorporation |

### Item 3: Related Persons

For each director, company secretary, and promoter:

| Field | Required |
|-------|----------|
| Full Legal Name | Yes |
| Business Street Address | Yes |
| Relationship(s) | Director / Company Secretary / Promoter (all that apply) |

**Promoter**: person who takes initiative in founding/organising the business or receives compensation/securities in connection with the offering per the regulatory definition.

### Item 4: Securities Offered

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Type(s) | Equity, debt, option/warrant, pooled investment fund interests, other |
| Total Offering Amount | Aggregate maximum |
| Total Amount Sold | As of filing date |
| Price Per Unit | Or "variable pricing" |
| Minimum Investment | Per investor, if applicable |

If multiple classes/series, describe each separately with distinct rights and preferences.

### Item 5: Exemption(s) Claimed, UK Prospectus Regulation

| Exemption | Key Conditions |
|-----------|---------------|
| Articles 1 to 7 UK Prospectus Regulation | Exemptions from the requirement to publish a prospectus: offers to qualified investors only, offers to fewer than 150 persons per EEA state, minimum consideration €100,000 per investor, total consideration < €1,000,000 over 12 months [VERIFY current thresholds] |
| Section 86 FSMA 2000 | Restriction on public offers of unlisted securities |
| FCA Conduct of Business Sourcebook (COBS) | Applicable rules for private placements |
| AIM Rules | If offering on AIM, separate disclosure and admission document requirements apply |

Confirm alignment between actual offering conduct and claimed exemption. No direct UK equivalent of Rule 504/506 exists [VERIFY with current FCA Handbook].

## Guidelines

- **Filing deadline** - file no later than 14 days after first sale of securities (or as specified by applicable exemption)
- **Amendments** - required for material changes or ongoing offerings
- **Cross-reference** - verify all entries against organisational documents, offering materials, and actual conduct
- **Public record** - filings are publicly available via Companies House Electronic Filing Service / FCA National Storage Mechanism; avoid inadvertent disclosure of confidential terms
- **FCA/PRA notification** - FCA or PRA notification requirements apply separately; flag whether an approved prospectus, supplementary prospectus, or notification is required
- **No legal opinions** - flag uncertainties about exemption qualification for solicitor/advocate review, Mark unverified statutory thresholds or citations with `[VERIFY]`

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

### Key Adaptations, SEC Form D replaced with UK exempt offering notice structure, Regulation D (Rules 504/506) replaced with UK Prospectus Regulation exemptions (Articles 1 to 7), FSMA 2000, and FCA COBS rules, SEC → FCA; EDGAR → Companies House Electronic Filing Service / FCA National Storage Mechanism, CIK number replaced with Companies House registration number, CRD number replaced with FCA FRN (Firm Reference Number)
- SIC code uses UK 2007 SIC system, State blue sky laws removed; replaced with FCA/PRA notification requirements
- $10M threshold replaced with £8M equivalent (note: no direct UK regulatory threshold at £8M, the UK exemption thresholds are per Articles 1 to 7 UK Prospectus Regulation)
- Jurisdictional references changed from US states to UK jurisdictions (England & Wales, Scotland, Northern Ireland)
- Corporate entity types changed to UK equivalents (PLC, Ltd, LLP, LP)

### [SCOTS] Notes, Scottish companies are registered at Companies House with a SC prefix to their registration number, Scottish company law is governed by the Companies Act 2006 (which applies UK-wide) with specific Scottish provisions (e.g., floating charges, receivership differs)
- The Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (FSMA) applies across the UK including Scotland, The FCA Handbook applies in Scotland; no separate Scottish securities regulator exists, AIM is a London Stock Exchange market; shares admitted to AIM are UK-listed and regulated by FCA/UKLA

### [VERIFY] Items Before Use
- [VERIFY] Current FCA Handbook thresholds for prospectus exemptions, UK Prospectus Regulation thresholds have been amended post-Brexit
- [VERIFY] Whether the UK Prospectus Regulation has been amended by HM Treasury statutory instruments since retention
- [VERIFY] Applicability of any HM Treasury or FCA temporary exemptions or easements
- [VERIFY] Whether the offering constitutes a public offer under section 756 FSMA 2000
- [VERIFY] Companies House filing timeline and fees for the specific filing type
- [VERIFY] Whether financial promotions approval under section 21 FSMA 2000 is required

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
