# Notice of Violation Response (UK/Scotland)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a formal NOV response that defends the client's position while building a cooperative regulatory record for dismissal, penalty reduction, or negotiated resolution. [SCOTS: Note] In Scotland/UK, regulatory enforcement actions are typically initiated through NOVs (environmental/health & safety), penalty notices, information letters (FCA), or formal cautions. The response influences whether an enforcement agency escalates to criminal proceedings or accepts a civil/criminal settlement.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

- **Original NOV** - case/citation number, dates (issued + received), delivery method, cited provisions
- **Client facts** - chronological timeline, extenuating circumstances, compliance history
- **Supporting docs** - permits, correspondence, photos, receipts, expert opinions, witness statements
- **Regulatory framework** - applicable UK statutes, regulations, statutory instruments, response deadline
- **Submission rules** - deadline, delivery method, formatting, authentication requirements (formal letterhead, verification)
- **Scotland-specific context**: is the regulator SEPA, HSE (GB-wide), FCA (GB-wide), local authority EHO, Food Standards Scotland, or a Scottish Government body?

## Response Structure

### 1. Header Block

Include sender (full legal name, business, address, contact), date, recipient (title, department, agency, address), and Re: line (NOV case number + date issued).

### 2. Introduction & Acknowledgment

- State receipt date and delivery method, Summarise alleged violations using exact language and citation numbers from the NOV, Reference all cited regulatory code sections (statutory instruments, regulations)
- State purpose: address allegations, provide factual context, seek resolution

### 3. Factual Explanation & Timeline

Chronological, objective account with specific dates, times, locations. Address applicable items:

- [ ] Unforeseen events or emergencies beyond respondent's control
- [ ] Reliance on professional advice, permits, or prior approvals
- [ ] Misunderstanding of complex or recently changed requirements
- [ ] Good faith compliance efforts underway at time of citation
- [ ] Factual errors in the original notice

Reference supporting documentation by exhibit label for each assertion.

### 4. Legal Defence & Mitigation

**Procedural defences** - improper jurisdiction/service, statutory time bar (Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973), failure to follow required procedure, human rights issues (HRA 1998 Art.6 - fair hearing)

**Substantive defences** - conduct doesn't violate cited provision, regulatory ambiguity + reasonable interpretation, applicable exemption/variation, due diligence defence (common in H&S / environmental), reasonable practicability (HSWA 1974 s.2/s.3)

**Mitigation factors** - unintentional violation, clean compliance history, minimal/no actual harm, corrective action already taken, voluntary disclosure, cooperation with investigation

Cite statutory sections in proper citation format. Flag uncertain citations with [VERIFY].

**Scotland-specific defences:**
- For SEPA enforcement: Environmental Protection Act 1990, Water Environment and Water Services (Scotland) Act 2003, Pollution Prevention and Control (Scotland) Regulations 2012
- For HSE (Scotland): Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974 (GB-wide); HM Inspector of Health and Safety (Scotland)
- For Food Standards Scotland: Food Safety Act 1990, Food Hygiene (Scotland) Regulations 2006
- Criminal standard: COPFS (Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service) prosecutes regulatory offences in Scotland, Sheriff Court jurisdiction for most regulatory offences (summary or solemn procedure)

### 5. Proposed Resolution

State requested relief in preference order:

1. **Dismissal** - based on legal/factual defences
2. **Penalty reduction** - based on mitigation factors; ask regulator to consider variable penalty framework (SEPA, HSE, FCA all have penalty policies)
3. **Enforcement undertaking** - formal agreement to carry out remedial action (available for SEPA and EA; avoids formal caution/prosecution)
4. **Formal caution / warning** - accept lesser sanction in lieu of prosecution
5. **Hearing/Appeal request** - invoke procedural rights with preferred format/timing
6. **Enhanced compliance** - beyond minimum requirements as goodwill

For corrective actions: completed actions with dates and documentation; ongoing actions with realistic milestones.

**UK/Scottish enforcement tools to be aware of:**
| Agency | Enforcement Powers |
|---|---|
| SEPA | NOV, Fixed Penalty Notice, Variable Monetary Penalty, Enforcement Undertaking, Criminal prosecution (COPFS) |
| HSE | Improvement Notice, Prohibition Notice, Fee for Intervention (FFI), Criminal prosecution |
| FCA | Warning Notice, Decision Notice, Fine, Public Censure, Prohibition Order, Criminal prosecution |
| Food Standards Scotland | Improvement Notice, Prohibition Order, Remedial Action Notice, Fines, Criminal prosecution |
| Local Authority EHO | Improvement Notice, Prohibition Notice, Fixed Penalty Notice, Criminal prosecution |
| Ofcom | Enforcement Notice, Fine, Suspension/Revocation of Licence |

### 6. Conclusion

State relief sought, reiterate compliance commitment, provide preferred contact method, offer to supply additional information.

### 7. Signature Block

Full legal name, title/relationship, date. If representative: "Authorised on behalf of [Respondent Name]." State SRA registration number / Law Society of Scotland roll number if solicitor. Include authority letter if responding through representative.

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **Address every violation** - omissions are treated as admissions in many regulatory regimes
- **Tone** - assertive on merits but cooperative; never dismissive or adversarial
- **No speculation** - every factual statement must be verifiable
- **Assume response may be evidential** - UK/Scottish regulatory proceedings treat NOV responses as part of the administrative record; avoid unintended admissions
- **Deadline** - confirm and prominently note; late responses may waive rights or trigger automatic penalties
- **Proof of service** - use delivery method with verifiable record (recorded delivery, email delivery confirmation, hand-delivery receipt)
- **Jurisdiction rules** - verify regulator-specific form requirements, page limits, or mandatory language before drafting
- **Retain copies** - complete copies of response and all attachments for client file
- **Scotland-specific**: Sheriff Court procedures for enforcement challenges; summary application procedure for certain appeals (e.g., s.140 EPA 1990)

## Troubleshooting

- **Deadline already passed.** A late response may waive procedural rights but does not waive substantive defences. File anyway, address the lateness directly with a request for late acceptance, and document the cause of delay (mail-delivery failure, agency error in service, etc.) with supporting evidence. Scottish courts/regulators may accept late responses where this does not prejudice the enforcement process.
- **NOV cites regulation that has been repealed/amended.** Address both the version cited and the version currently in force. Post-Brexit retained EU law adjustments means some regulations have been modified, confirm correct version. If the cited version was never in force at the time of the alleged conduct, raise that as a procedural defence.
- **Multiple respondents named.** Coordinate before responding, divergent factual accounts among co-respondents become impeachment material. Joint response with separate solicitor verification is often cleanest where interests align; separate responses with cross-referencing where they don't.
- **Agency informally indicated the NOV is "just a record" or "information only."** Do not rely on informal assurances. Treat every NOV as a potential predicate to formal enforcement action; the response is the only piece of the regulatory record the respondent controls.
- **Underlying facts are still under investigation.** Where facts are genuinely in flux, respond with what is known, identify the scope of pending investigation, and request leave to supplement. Silence or hedging without explanation is treated as an admission in many UK regulatory regimes.
- **Criminal investigation underway.** If the NOV relates to conduct that could result in criminal prosecution (most SEPA/HSE/FSA enforcement), advise client to engage criminal defence solicitor before responding. Consider privilege and self-incrimination implications, caution required.

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Adapted for:** Scotland and UK law
**Changes made:**
- EPA (US Environmental Protection Agency) → SEPA (Scottish Environment Protection Agency) / EA, OSHA → HSE (Health & Safety Executive) - GB-wide, with specific Scotland inspectorate, US local code enforcement → Local Authority Environmental Health (EHO) / Trading Standards Scotland, FDA → Food Standards Scotland / MHRA, FCC → Ofcom, FERC → Ofgem, State environmental departments → SEPA (single Scottish environmental regulator)
- US DOJ → COPFS (Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service) - criminal prosecutions in Scotland, FRE → Scottish criminal/civil evidence rules (Criminal Procedure (Scotland) Act 1995 / Civil Evidence (Scotland) Act 1988)
- US federal consent decree → UK enforcement undertaking (SEPA/EA) / regulatory settlement, US penal order / criminal penalties → Scottish fines (summary or solemn procedure)
- Statute of limitations → Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973 (criminal: 6 months summary, no time limit solemn for certain offences)
- US due process → Human Rights Act 1998 Art.6 (fair hearing)
- Added specific UK/Scottish enforcement tools (Improvement Notice, Prohibition Notice, Fixed Penalty Notice, Enforcement Undertaking)
- Added COPFS prosecution framework (Procurator Fiscal)
- Added Sheriff Court / Court of Session jurisdiction for regulatory enforcement, Added post-Brexit retained EU law considerations, Added criminal investigation/cautions guidance (Scottish criminal procedure distinct)

**Key Scottish/UK considerations:**
- Regulatory enforcement in Scotland: COPFS prosecutes criminal offences under Sheriff Court (summary/solemn) or High Court, No US-style EPA administrative penalty system, SEPA uses variable monetary penalties (VMP), enforcement undertakings, fixed penalties, and COPFS referrals, HSWA 1974 applies GB-wide; HSE inspectors in Scotland exercise same powers as England/Wales, Food Standards Scotland (FSS) is the devolved food safety regulator, distinct from FSA (England/Wales)
- SEPA enforcement undertakings are a key resolution tool (avoid criminal record and formal caution). No equivalent in US system, Defences: due diligence, reasonable practicability (HSWA), best available techniques (BAT for environmental)
- Fixed Penalty Notices (FPN) and Penalty Notices for Disorder (PND) available for certain offences, HSE Fee for Intervention (FFI): cost recovery for HSE inspection time, not in US, Human Rights Act 1998 Art.6: right to fair trial applies to all regulatory enforcement, Legal professional privilege applies, caution before sharing privileged material with regulator, Self-incrimination: caution required in cases where criminal prosecution is possible (Scotland has no equivalent of US Fifth Amendment but common law privilege against self-incrimination)

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
