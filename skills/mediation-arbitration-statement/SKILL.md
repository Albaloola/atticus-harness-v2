# Mediation / Arbitration Statement

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

[SCOTS] Drafts a confidential brief for a mediator or arbitrator synthesising facts, law, and damages into a credible, persuasive narrative, adapted for Scottish civil procedure and the Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

- **Pleadings** - writ / initial writ, defences, pleadings notes, adjustment
- **Evidence** - contracts, correspondence, affidavits, precognitions, expert reports
- **Expert reports** - liability and quantum opinions
- **Authorities** - controlling case law (Scots law, UK Supreme Court), statutes (Acts of the Scottish Parliament / UK Parliament), statutory instruments
- **Prior positions** - settlement demands, earlier submissions, without-prejudice correspondence
- **Forum rules** - page limits, formatting, submission guidelines (arbitration rules or court practice note)

## Quick Start

1. Identify forum type (mediation vs. arbitration) - tone and strategy differ
2. Collect all productions/exhibits; number consecutively in order of first reference
3. Draft using the output structure below
4. Run the checks in Pitfalls before submission
5. Confirm without-prejudice status for mediation statements; arbitration statements are generally on the record

## Output Structure

### 1. Introduction (0.5 to 1 page)

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Parties | Names, roles, relationship |
| Dispute | One-paragraph summary |
| Procedural posture | Stage, pending motions, adjustment status |
| Relief sought | Specific damages figure or remedy |
| Key themes | 2 to 3 framing themes |

### 2. Factual Background (3 to 6 pages)

- Chronological narrative organised around claim/defence elements, Cite every material fact: `(Production [No.], [description])` or footnotes, Quote contracts, emails, affidavits where impactful, Acknowledge undisputed facts even when unfavourable, credibility is paramount

### 3. Legal Analysis (3 to 5 pages)

Per claim or defence:

```
### [Claim/Defence Name]

**Governing standard**: [Rule + citation]

**Element-by-element application**:
1. [Element] - [Facts + production cite]
2. [Element] - [Facts + production cite]

**Opposing argument & rebuttal**: [Strongest counterargument → why it fails]
```

- OSCOLA (Oxford Standard for Citation of Legal Authorities) or Scots law citation style (Green's Weekly Digest / Scots Law Times). Use forum-specified format if applicable.
- Distinguish unfavourable authorities, do not ignore them, Calibrate sophistication to audience (industry arbitrator vs. judge)

### 4. Damages / Quantum (1 to 3 pages)

| Category | Amount | Basis | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Compensatory | £X | [methodology] | Production [No.], [expert] |
| Consequential | £X | [methodology] | Production [No.] |
| Loss of profits | £X | [methodology] | Production [No.] |
| Interest | £X | [rate, Judicial Rate / contractual] | [statutory cite] |
| **Total** | **£X** | | |

- Show calculation methodology transparently with expert references, Address known damages challenges proactively, Interest on damages in Scotland: Judicial Rate fixed under the Judicial Rates (Scotland) Orders, cite current applicable rate
- **Mediation only**: present ranges for speculative categories to facilitate settlement

### 5. Conclusion (0.5 page)

- Reinforce 2 to 3 key themes, State relief with specificity
- **Mediation only**: express good-faith willingness to negotiate; reserve specific demands for confidential mediator communications

## Formatting

- **Header every page**: `CONFIDENTIAL, PREPARED FOR [MEDIATION/ARBITRATION] - WITHOUT PREJUDICE` (mediation only; arbitration statements are generally on the record)
- Table of contents if >15 pages; table of authorities if >20 pages (using OSCOLA or Scots law citation conventions)
- Number productions/exhibits consecutively in order of first reference, Conform to forum-specific formatting rules (arbitration procedural order or court practice note)

## Pitfalls

- **Overstatement** - destroys credibility with neutrals; balance advocacy with candour
- **Unsupported assertions** - cite everything; unsupported claims are worse than omissions
- **Privilege leaks** - review for inadvertent disclosure of legal advice privilege (Engl./Scots) or litigation privilege; mediation-specific communications protected by without-prejudice privilege
- **Without-prejudice protection**: For mediation statements, ensure header clearly marks the document as "Without Prejudice" and "Confidential" - accidentally sharing unprotected content may waive privilege
- **Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010 compliance**: Arbitration statements may become part of the arbitral record and potentially challengeable on appeal (ss. 67 to 70 of the Act) - draft with this scrutiny in mind
- **Stale citations** - verify all authorities are current and not overruled; note that UK Supreme Court decisions bind Scottish courts
- **Protective-order material** - redact anything covered by confidentiality agreements or protective orders made under the Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010 rules or court order
- **Distinguish Scots and English authorities**: English case law is persuasive but not binding in Scottish courts, flag which authorities are binding vs. persuasive

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

### Key Differences from US Practice

1. **Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010**: The primary legislation governing arbitration in Scotland. The UNCITRAL Model Law (as adopted in Schedule 1 of the Act) provides the default framework. Scottish Arbitration Rules (Schedule 1) can be modified by party agreement. Parties may choose institutional rules (Scottish Arbitration Centre, LCIA, ICC).

2. **Court-Connected Mediation**: Scottish courts actively encourage mediation. The Commercial Court in the Court of Session operates a mediation protocol; Sheriff Courts have similar provisions. Mediation is not compulsory but refusal to mediate without good reason may attract adverse expenses awards.

3. **Legal Privilege**: Scotland recognises legal advice privilege (communications with solicitors and advocates) and litigation privilege (communications in contemplation of litigation). Without-prejudice privilege applies to genuine settlement negotiations. Unlike the US FRE 408, the common law without-prejudice rule in Scotland is broader and covers all communications made in a genuine attempt to settle.

4. **Citation Conventions**: OSCOLA is the standard academic citation system; however, Scottish legal practitioners commonly use Scots Law Times (SLT), Session Cases (SC), and Green's Weekly Digest (GWD) citations. Case law references: e.g., *Smith v Jones* 2020 SC 123, 2020 SLT 456.

5. **No US-Style Bluebook**: There is no single US-style citation manual for Scottish practitioners. The Glasgow University / Edinburgh Law School citation guides are common, but in practice each court or arbitration body may set its own rules.

6. **Interest on Damages**: Scottish courts apply the Judicial Rate fixed by the Scottish Ministers under the Judicial Rates (Scotland) Orders. Currently the judicial rate of 8% per annum is typical (verify current rate). Contractual interest rates may differ.

7. **Costs / Expenses**: The general rule is that expenses (costs) follow the event, the unsuccessful party pays the successful party's costs. Mediation statements and without-prejudice communications are generally not taken into account on expenses unless the mediation achieves a settlement.

### Flagged Concepts with No Direct Scottish Equivalent

- **FRE 408**: No US Federal Rule equivalent. Without-prejudice privilege in Scotland is governed by common law and covers a similar scope but with different nuances.
- **Bluebook Citation**: No Scottish equivalent. OSCOLA or custom court/arbitration styles are used instead.
- **US Federal Judicial Panel on Multidistrict Litigation**: No Scottish equivalent. Cases are managed individually in the Court of Session or Sheriff Court.
- **US-style Summary Judgment (FRCP 56)**: Scottish procedure has the equivalent "summary decree" (Court of Session) or "summary decree" (Sheriff Court) - different rules and thresholds apply.
- **US Punitive Damages**: Not available in Scottish civil litigation. Damages are compensatory only. Exemplary or punitive damages have extremely limited application and are generally not pleaded.
- **US Magistrate Judges for ADR**: Scottish procedure uses commercial judges or active-sitting sheriffs for case management. Mediators are typically external, accredited through bodies such as the Scottish Mediation Network.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
