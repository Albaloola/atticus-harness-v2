## Why This Skill Exists

[SCOTS: Note] The US third-party Special Needs Trust (SNT) is designed to preserve SSI and Medicaid eligibility and must comply with 42 U.S.C. § 1396p. Neither SSI nor Medicaid exist in the UK; there is no direct statutory equivalent. This skill adapts the structure for Scottish/UK practice as a Disabled Person's Trust (DPT) under the Inheritance Tax Act 1984 s.89, with the goal of preserving means-tested benefit eligibility (PIP, Universal Credit, NHS continuing healthcare, local authority social care).

Third-party DPTs fail for two reasons: they contain language that causes the trust corpus to be treated as the beneficiary's own capital for means-tested benefits purposes, or they make distributions that count as income and reduce benefit entitlement. A trust that makes regular cash payments directly to a beneficiary without considering benefit rules can cost the beneficiary their entire Universal Credit award.

This skill produces a jurisdiction-aware, irrevocable third-party DPT that preserves means-tested benefit eligibility, uses solely supplemental distribution standards, requires no government payback at termination, and includes trustee guidance on benefit-compliant distributions. The defining characteristic, no payback provision, is the same as the US third-party SNT because the assets never belonged to the beneficiary. `[VERIFY local authority rules for trust treatment under applicable means-testing guidance]`

## Step 2: Draft the Trust Document

### Fixed Section Order

| Section | Contents |
|---|---|
| **Preamble** | Trust name, settlor, trustee, beneficiary, date, recitals of intent |
| **Art. I, Purpose & Intent** | Supplemental-only purpose; benefits preservation declaration (see template below) |
| **Art. II, Irrevocability** | Irrevocable from execution; no amendment except under Trusts (Scotland) Act powers |
| **Art. III, Distribution Standards** | Sole and absolute discretion; permissible categories; prohibited distributions; benefit guidance |
| **Art. IV, Trustee Powers & Duties** | Investment, hiring advisors, tax authority, recordkeeping; conformation to Trusts (Scotland) Acts |
| **Art. V, Trustee Compensation & Succession** | Fee schedule or reasonable compensation; resignation/removal; appointment of new trustees |
| **Art. VI, Administration** | Annual accountings, Scottish trust registration (if required), trust situs, tax filings |
| **Art. VII, Termination & Remainder** | Terminates at beneficiary death; NO government payback; remainder to designated beneficiaries |
| **Art. VIII, Tax Provisions** | DPT election under IHTA 1984 s.89; income tax treatment; tax allocation |
| **Art. IX, General Provisions** | Severability, governing law (Scotland), spendthrift, anti-alienation |
| **Execution** | Settlor signature (witnessed), trustee acceptance, one witness (Scotland) |
| **Schedule A** | Initial trust assets (if funded at execution) |

### Purpose Statement Template

```text
This Trust is established solely for the benefit of the Beneficiary who is a
disabled person for the purposes of section 89 of the Inheritance Tax Act 1984.
The Trust shall provide for the supplemental needs of the Beneficiary beyond
those provided by any statutory, means-tested, or local authority programme.
It is the Settlor's intent that Trust assets shall NOT replace, impair, or
reduce any benefits or services to which the Beneficiary is or may become
entitled under UK or Scottish legislation.
```

### Permissible Distribution Categories

- Medical/dental care not covered by NHS, Therapy, rehabilitation, habilitation services, Education, vocational training, tutoring, Entertainment, recreation, travel, companionship, Assistive technology, communication devices, electronics, Personal care attendants (beyond NHS/local authority coverage)
- Vehicle purchase/modification, transportation, Clothing, personal items, quality-of-life enhancements, Legal, financial, and benefits planning services, Trust-owned residence or property expenses

### Prohibited Distributions, Benefits-Aware Rules

| Prohibited | Compliant Alternative |
|---|---|
| Cash directly to beneficiary (may count as income) | Pay vendors/providers directly |
| Regular payments without benefits impact assessment | Use trustee discretion with benefit specialist advice |
| Distributions causing deprivation of capital | Structure as trust-owned assets; document cost-benefit analysis |
| Loans to beneficiary that may be treated as capital | Gift or direct payment to service providers |

> **Trustee instruction**: Before any distribution that could affect the Beneficiary's means-tested benefits, consult a welfare benefits specialist. Document the analysis in trust records.

### Remainder Distribution Template

```text
Upon the death of the Beneficiary, the Trustee shall distribute remaining
Trust assets to the following remainder beneficiaries, free of any obligation
to reimburse any governmental or local authority for benefits provided to the
Beneficiary during the Beneficiary's lifetime:
  [Remainder beneficiary designations with shares and contingencies]
```

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from a US third-party Special Needs Trust for Scottish/UK practice as a Disabled Person's Trust (DPT).

### Terminology Changes

| US Term | Scottish/UK Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Special Needs Trust (SNT) | Disabled Person's Trust (DPT) / Vulnerable Person's Trust |
| 42 U.S.C. § 1396p(d)(4)(A) | No equivalent, no statutory SNT category |
| SSI (Supplemental Security Income) | Universal Credit / Pension Credit / Income Support |
| Medicaid | NHS Continuing Healthcare / Local authority social care |
| Medicare | NHS Scotland (universal) |
| ABLE account | No direct UK equivalent |
| Crummey withdrawal powers | Not used in UK practice |
| In-Kind Support and Maintenance (ISM) | DWP benefit rules, trust distributions counted as income |
| Spendthrift under state law | Spendthrift under Trusts (Scotland) Acts |
| Grantor | Settlor |
| Trustee removal (court) | Trustee removal per Trusts (Scotland) Act 1921 |
| UPSIA / prudent investor | Trustee Investments Act / prudent investor (Scots common law) |

### Key Differences

- **No statutory SNT category** - no equivalent to 42 U.S.C. § 1396p in UK law
- **No ABLE accounts** - consider Junior ISA or UK savings products instead
- **No Crummey powers** - UK uses annual exemption, normal expenditure exemption
- **Means-tested benefits**: PIP (disability), Universal Credit (income replacement), not SSI/SSDI
- **NHS is universal** - most healthcare is free at point of use; long-term care is means-tested
- **No payback** - same as US third-party SNT: assets never belonged to beneficiary
- **DPT IHT treatment** - qualifying trusts under IHTA 1984 s.89 have favourable IHT treatment
- **Trusts (Scotland) Act 1921** - powers and administration differ from US state trust codes
- **Perpetuity**: Scottish trusts may last indefinitely (no RAP in Scotland)
