# Independent Contractor Agreement (UK/Scotland Adaptation)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a results-oriented ICA that survives HMRC IR35 scrutiny while protecting both parties on IP, confidentiality, and liability.

**⚠️ IR35 is the dominant risk in UK contractor engagements. This template assumes medium-to-high-risk engagements require either a) inside-IR35 deemed-employment with PAYE tax via umbrella route, or b) properly structured outside-IR35 with full controls and documentation.**

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

1. **Parties** - legal names, entity type/registered number (Companies House), registered address, authorised signatories
2. **Scope** - deliverables, milestones, acceptance criteria, start/end dates
3. **Compensation** - fixed fee, hourly, retainer, or milestone-based; reimbursable expenses
4. **Governing law** - Scots law (Sheriff Court / Court of Session) unless otherwise agreed
5. **IP sensitivity** - pre-existing contractor IP vs. novel work product
6. **Insurance** - coverage types and minimums for the engagement
7. **IR35 determination** - status determination statement; size exemption check under off-payroll working rules

## Agreement Sections

### 1. Parties and Recitals

- Legal names, entity type, registered number, registered address, Confirm signatory authority (board resolution if company)
- One-sentence engagement purpose
- **State IR35 status determination** and basis for decision

### 2. Scope of Services

- Specific deliverables with measurable acceptance criteria, Milestones and deadlines
- **State explicitly:** contractor controls manner, means, method, and schedule, key to outside-IR35
- List exclusions to prevent scope creep, Substitute performance clause (right to send a substitute) - strong indicator of self-employment

### 3. Compensation and Payment

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Rate/fee | Exact amount or formula (GBP) |
| Invoicing | Contractor submits VAT invoices (if VAT-registered); net 30 |
| Expenses | Itemised reimbursables; receipts required |
| Taxes | Contractor solely responsible (Income Tax, Class 2/4 NIC via Self Assessment) |
| No withholding | No PAYE, no employer NIC, no pension auto-enrolment |
| IR35 | If inside-IR35: fee payer deducts PAYE + NIC. If outside: no deduction |
| CIS (construction only) | CIS withholding may apply, confirm eligibility |
| Late payment | Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 - 8% above Bank of England base |

### 4. Contractor Classification

Include affirmative statements that contractor:

- Retains full control over manner and means of performance, Provides own tools, equipment, and workspace (note exceptions)
- May work for other clients concurrently, Bears risk of profit or loss, Receives no employee benefits (holiday pay, sick pay, pension, workplace pension auto-enrolment)
- Is not supervised or subject to set hours, Has right of substitution, Is not integrated into hirer's business structure

**HMRC Status Tests (key factors):**

| Factor | Outside IR35 | Inside IR35 |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Substitution | Genuine right of substitution | Personal service required |
| Control | Contractor controls how/when/where | Hirer directs work |
| Financial risk | Contractor bears risk of loss, has investment | No risk; paid by time |
| Part and parcel | Not integrated into business | Works as part of hirer's team |
| Mutuality of obligation | No ongoing obligation to offer/provide work | Ongoing engagement |

**Recommended:** Use HMRC's Check Employment Status for Tax (CEST) tool. Retain a Status Determination Statement (SDS) for medium/large clients.

**Additional considerations:**
- [SCOTS: Note] - No UK equivalent of state-level ABC tests; HMRC employment-status test is single unified framework
- [SCOTS: Note] - Employment Tribunal / ACAS separate jurisdiction from tax status

### 5. Term and Termination

| Trigger | Notice |
|---|---|
| Without cause | 15 to 30 days written |
| Material breach (uncured) | Written + 14-day cure; immediate if uncurtable |
| Insolvency/bankruptcy | Immediate |

On termination: deliver work in progress, return property, submit final invoice. Hiring party pays for accepted work through termination date. [SCOTS: Note] Scots law requires express termination clauses, no common-law implied right.

### 6. Confidentiality

- **Scope:** trade secrets, business strategies, customer lists, financial data, proprietary processes, anything marked or reasonably understood as confidential
- **Duration:** 2 to 5 years general; indefinite for trade secrets
- **Carve-outs:** publicly available, independently developed, received from third party, legally compelled (with prompt notice)
- **Return/destroy** all materials on termination
- **Data Protection:** contractor to comply with UK GDPR / Data Protection Act 2018 as data processor

### 7. Intellectual Property

Use belt-and-suspenders approach:

1. UK has no statutory "work made for hire" concept, use explicit assignment of all IP rights arising from the services
2. Assignment of all right, title, and interest (worldwide, perpetual, for the full duration of all IP rights)
3. Contractor executes additional documents to perfect ownership
4. Pre-existing contractor IP listed on Exhibit A; non-exclusive licence granted for use as embedded in deliverables
5. Moral rights waiver (Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988, s. 87) - limited waiver recommended; full waiver not permitted in UK

### 8. Representations and Warranties

**Contractor:** authority to contract, no conflicting obligations, possesses required skills/licenses, work meets professional standards, deliverables non-infringing, compliance with applicable laws, registered for VAT if threshold met.

**Hiring party:** authority to contract, reasonable cooperation, timely information.

### 9. Indemnification and Liability

| Party | Indemnifies against |
|---|---|
| Contractor | Breach, negligence/willful misconduct, IP infringement, bodily injury/property damage |
| Hiring party | Breach, negligence/willful misconduct |

- Liability cap: total fees paid (carve-out for confidentiality/IP breaches)
- Exclude consequential, incidental damages (same carve-outs)
- [SCOTS: Note] - Scots law does not recognise punitive damages; exclude reference
- [SCOTS: Note] - Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977 (UCTA) applies to limitation/exclusion clauses, ensure reasonableness

**Contractor insurance minimums:**

| Type | Minimum |
|---|---|
| Public liability | £1m per occurrence / £2m aggregate |
| Professional indemnity (E&O) | £500k to £1m per claim |
| Employers' liability (if contractor has employees) | £5m (statutory minimum) |

Require certificates of insurance; hiring party named as additional insured on public liability.

### 10. Dispute Resolution

- Escalation: good-faith negotiation → mediation (30 days) → arbitration or litigation
- [SCOTS: Note] - Scottish Arbitration Centre / Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010 for arbitration; or Court of Session (Outer House) or Sheriff Court for litigation, Governing law: Scots law
- **Pre‑action protocol** - commercial actions require compliance with pre-action protocol (letter of claim, 21-day response)
- Standard judicial expenses (costs) rule: loser pays the winner's reasonable costs, taxed by the Auditor of the Court of Session

### 11. General Provisions

- Entire agreement / merger clause, Written amendments signed by both parties, No contractor assignment without consent; hiring party may assign to successors/affiliates, Severability; no waiver by failure to enforce, Notices via email + post (recorded delivery)
- Force majeure (exclude payment obligations)
- Counterparts; e-signatures valid under Electronic Communications Act 2000 / eIDAS Regulation

### Signature Block

Both parties sign with name, title (if entity), and date.

## Pitfalls and Checks

- **Never use supervision language** - avoid "direct," "supervise," "report to," "schedule set by" - these flag IR35 inside-status
- **IR35 off-payroll rules:** Medium and large clients must issue a Status Determination Statement (SDS) and pass it down the chain; small clients exempt
- **Right of substitution** - HMRC views this as a strong outside-IR35 indicator; ensure it is genuine and not fettered
- **Substitute performance:** If contractor substitutes, confirm substitute meets same standards; indemnify for substitute acts
- **Non-competes** - UK law: restraint of trade doctrine, enforceable only if reasonable in scope, duration, and geography; [SCOTS: Note] Scots law requires a legitimate business interest
- **Multi-jurisdiction work:** UK-wide rights; [SCOTS: Note] Scottish courts govern work performed in Scotland unless otherwise agreed
- **Never guarantee IR35 status:** include disclaimer; recommend client conduct CEST check and retain SDS
- **VAT:** Confirm contractor VAT status; reverse charge may apply for some services
- **Umbrella companies:** Flag if contractor proposes umbrella route; separate due diligence required
- **Agency Workers Regulations 2010:** May apply if contractor supplied through an agency after 12 weeks

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Adapted for:** Scotland and UK law
**Changes made:**
- Replaced IRS classification tests with HMRC IR35 framework (CEST tool, Status Determination Statement, off-payroll rules)
- Replaced FLSA/ABC tests with HMRC employment-status factors (substitution, control, mutuality of obligation, financial risk, part-and-parcel)
- Replaced 1099-NEC tax reporting with UK Self Assessment (SA800), PAYE, Class 2/4 National Insurance, Removed "work made for hire" (no UK equivalent) - replaced with express assignment of IP rights, Replaced USD amounts (£1m, £2m) with GBP equivalents, Replaced ESIGN Act / UETA with Electronic Communications Act 2000 / eIDAS, Replaced AAA Commercial Rules with Scottish Arbitration Centre / Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010
- Added UK GDPR / Data Protection Act 2018 obligations, Added UCTA 1977 reasonableness test for limitation clauses, Added Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998
- Replaced "attorneys' fees" with "judicial expenses" (Scots costs rule)
- Updated insurance types: public liability, professional indemnity, employers' liability, Removed punitive damages reference (not available in Scots law)
- Applied Scottish court system: Sheriff Court (Simple Procedure / Ordinary Cause) and Court of Session

**Key Scottish/UK considerations:**
- IR35 is the dominant compliance risk, Status Determination Statement essential for medium/large clients, No state-level ABC test divergence, single national HMRC framework, Right of substitution is the strongest outside-IR35 indicator, Substitution clause must be genuine, not fettered by unrealistic conditions, Scots law has no common-law implied termination right, all termination must be express, UCTA 1977 limits enforceability of exclusion clauses, must be reasonable, VAT implications (registration, reverse charge) for cross-border contractor work within UK

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
