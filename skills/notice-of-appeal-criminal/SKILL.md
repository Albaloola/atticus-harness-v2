# Note of Appeal, Criminal (Scotland)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. Drafts a procedurally compliant Note of Appeal or intimation of intention to appeal initiating an appeal in Scottish criminal proceedings, preserving rights within strict filing deadlines.

## Prerequisites

Collect before drafting, do not guess dates or case numbers:

1. **Extract of conviction / sentencing minute** - clerk's date (not oral announcement)
2. **Complaint or indictment** - charges, statutory citations, libelled contraventions
3. **Party names** - as in court records including any known aliases
4. **Court and Sheriffdom** - exact court name (e.g. "Sheriff Court of Grampian, Highland and Islands at Inverness")
5. **Appellant contact** - prison facility and number, or home address
6. **Solicitor info** - or confirmation appellant is acting as a party litigant

## Preliminary, Scottish Criminal Procedure Context

Scottish criminal appeals differ substantially from US/English procedure:

| Proceeding Type | Appeal Route | Time Limit | Key Document |
|----------------|--------------|-----------|--------------|
| **Solemn** (Sheriff Court or High Court trial) | Sheriff Court → High Court of Justiciary | Intimation of intention to appeal within 14 days of sentence | Note of Appeal (full grounds within 8 weeks of intimation, extendable court) |
| **Summary** (Sheriff Court) | Sheriff Court → Sheriff Appeal Court | Note of Appeal within 14 days of sentence or conviction | Note of Appeal |
| **Summary** (Justice of the Peace Court) | JP Court → Sheriff Appeal Court | Note of Appeal within 14 days | Note of Appeal |
| **Emergency / competency challenges** | Bill of Suspension | No fixed time but must be presented promptly | Bill of Suspension (with note of argument) |
| **Solemn, sentence only** | Sheriff Court → High Court of Justiciary | Intimation + Note within prescribed periods | Note of Appeal (sentence only) |
| **Miscarriage of justice / fresh evidence** | High Court of Justiciary | Note of Appeal, may be time-limited | Note of Appeal (may require leave) |

Missing the intimation deadline = permanent loss of appellate rights. Always state the deadline explicitly.

## Document Sections, Note of Appeal (Summary Procedure)

### 1. Heading

- Sheriff Appeal Court or High Court of Justiciary, Appellant's name, Respondent: "Procurator Fiscal, [Sheriffdom]"
- Court of first instance and case reference number, Title: `NOTE OF APPEAL`

### 2. Appellant Identification

**Appellant:** Full name as in court records. Incarceration details (prison, number). Solicitor's firm, address, and reference.

**Respondent:** Procurator Fiscal, [local office].

### 3. Grounds of Appeal

Numbered paragraph list. Common categories:

| Category | Examples |
|---------|----------|
| Miscarriage of justice | Verdict unreasonable / unsupported by evidence (s.106 Criminal Procedure (Scotland) Act 1995) |
| Unreasonable verdict | No reasonable sheriff could be satisfied beyond reasonable doubt |
| Misapplication of law | Error in law by the sheriff; wrong legal test applied |
| Evidential error | Inadmissible evidence admitted; admissible evidence wrongly excluded |
| Irregularity in proceedings | Breach of natural justice; procedural irregularity |
| Sentence excessive | Principle of proportionality; outwith range for similar offences |
| Wrong in principle | Custodial sentence when community disposal appropriate |
| Procedural unfairness | Failure to observe requirements of summary procedure |

No frivolous grounds, solicitor certifies good faith (breach of professional conduct rules).

### 4. Note of Argument

Full statement of argument supporting each ground. Include:
- Reference to relevant evidence (trial transcript pagination where available)
- Statutory authorities (Criminal Procedure (Scotland) Act 1995)
- Case law citations (Scottish Criminal Case Reports / Justiciary Appeal Cases)
- Convention rights arguments (Human Rights Act 1998 / ECHR)

### 5. Disposal Sought

Specify the outcome sought:
- Quash conviction, Substitute lesser verdict (e.g. attempt, culpable homicide)
- Reduce sentence, Order new trial (High Court only, solemn appeals)
- Desert the diet (summary)

### 6. Transcript Request

- Full trial proceedings (solemn appeals)
- Sheriff's note / stated case (summary appeals)
- Copy of complaint, minutes, and interlocutors

### 7. Indigency / Legal Aid (if applicable)

- Referral to Scottish Legal Aid Board for criminal legal aid, Existing legal aid certificate number, Pending application

### 8. Certificate of Service / Intimation

- Service on Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service (COPFS)
- Service on co-accused solicitors (if any)
- Submitted to the Clerk of Justiciary / Sheriff Appeal Court Clerk

### 9. Signature

**Solicitor:** Signed, full name, firm, address, DX number, phone, email.

**Party litigant:** Signature, name, address, phone, date.

## Alternative Procedure, Bill of Suspension

For summary criminal proceedings where immediate challenge is needed (competency, oppression, excess of jurisdiction):

- **Form**: Bill of Suspension with Note of Argument
- **Statutory basis**: Criminal Procedure (Scotland) Act 1995, s.190
- **Grounds**: Narrow, inappropriate for routine conviction appeals
- **Timing**: Presented promptly after the grievance arises

## Deadlines

| Proceeding | Deadline | Authority |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Solemn, Intimation of intention to appeal | 14 days from sentence | Criminal Procedure (Scotland) Act 1995, s.109 |
| Solemn, Full Note of Appeal | 8 weeks from intimation (extendable by court) | Act of Adjournal (Criminal Appeals) 2022 |
| Summary, Note of Appeal | 14 days from conviction or sentence | Criminal Procedure (Scotland) Act 1995, s.174 |
| Summary, Stated Case | Within 14 days of lodging Note of Appeal | Criminal Procedure (Scotland) Act 1995, s.175 |
| Solemn, Sentence only appeal | 14 days intimation + Note within 4 weeks | Act of Adjournal (Criminal Appeals) 2022 |

Missing by one day = permanent loss of appellate rights.

## Pitfalls

- **Date source**: Use court clerk's minute/interlocutor date, not oral pronouncement.
- **Name mismatch**: Party names must match court record exactly, discrepancies create procedural defects.
- **Formatting**: Comply with Acts of Adjournal and Practice Notes of the High Court of Justiciary / Sheriff Appeal Court.
- **Leave to appeal**: Solemn conviction appeals are as of right; summary appeals and certain sentence-only appeals may require leave at sift stage.
- **Guilty plea appeals**: Limited grounds, miscarriage of justice on plea, or sentence only.
- **Time limits**: Intimation + service must comply with strict deadlines; no automatic extension.
- **Legal aid**: Ensure criminal legal aid in place before lodging; SLAB may limit scope.
- **Preservation**: All grounds must be stated in the Note; new grounds not generally permitted later.
- **Bail pending appeal**: Apply separately; not automatic, High Court may grant interim liberation.

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from US originals for use under Scots criminal procedure.

**Key adaptations:**
- **Legal framework**: Entirely replaced US FRAP 4(b) / state appellate rules with Scottish criminal appeal framework under Criminal Procedure (Scotland) Act 1995 and Acts of Adjournal.
- **Courts**: High Court of Justiciary (solemn appeals), Sheriff Appeal Court (summary appeals). No US federal circuit / state appellate court distinction.
- **Documents**: Note of Appeal, Bill of Suspension, Minute of Appeal replace US Notice of Appeal. Intimation of intention to appeal is a separate first step in solemn procedure.
- **Deadlines**: 14 days (both solemn intimation and summary) - similar to FRAP 4(b) 14 days but calculation rules differ.
- **Prosecutor**: Procurator Fiscal (PF) / Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service (COPFS) replaces US District Attorney / US Attorney.
- **Criminal legal aid**: Scottish Legal Aid Board (SLAB) replaces US indigency / *Douglas v. California* framework.
- **Human rights**: Human Rights Act 1998 / ECHR Convention rights are directly pleaded (not US Constitution Bill of Rights).
- **Grounds**: Miscarriage of justice (s.106 Criminal Procedure (Scotland) Act 1995) replaces US sufficiency/ineffective assistance categories. "Unreasonable verdict" per Scottish authority.
- **No US-style ineffective assistance doctrine**: *Strickland* test does not apply; solicitor competence assessed under Scottish professional standards.
- **Sift process**: Single judge or appeal sheriff may refuse permission at first sift where grounds are not arguable, no US equivalent.
- **Stated case**: Summary appeals proceed by stated case (sheriff's findings in law and fact) - no US equivalent.
- **Fresh evidence**: Governed by s.108 / s.182 Criminal Procedure (Scotland) Act 1995 - no US evidentiary hearing default.
- **Terminology**: Accused/Appellant, complainer (complainant), desert diet (dismissal), libel (indictment), interlocutor (court order).
- **No jury in summary appeals**: Summary trials are sheriff or JP alone; Sheriff Appeal Court panel of three appeal sheriffs.

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
