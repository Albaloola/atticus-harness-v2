Drafts a recordable Deed of Discharge of Standard Security combined with a Disposition (where required) transferring heritable property from borrower to lender in full satisfaction of a defaulted secured debt, as an alternative to formal calling-up, Notice of Default, or court repossession proceedings. This document is governed by the Conveyancing and Feudal Reform (Scotland) Act 1970 and the Home Owner and Debtor Protection (Scotland) Act 2010.

## Prerequisites

Collect before drafting:

1. **Loan documents** - Personal Bond / Loan Agreement, Standard Security (Form A or Form B) with registration date, Land Register title number, Keeper's registration details (not "recorder's office")
2. **Payoff figures** - outstanding principal, accrued interest, default interest, expenses, enforcement costs as at a specific date
3. **Particular description** - verbatim from the Title Sheet in the Land Register of Scotland (not "legal description")
4. **Party identification** - full legal names matching registered instruments; entities need Scottish company number (not jurisdiction), signatory authority according to memorandum and articles (company) or partnership deed (partnership); trust deeds need trustee certification
5. **Deal terms** - deficiency waiver or survival (less formalised than US, usually in a Minute of Agreement or Deed of Discharge), cash consideration, relocation assistance, credit reporting treatment
6. **Junior securities** - subordinate standard securities, inhibitions (not "judgments"), or encumbrances requiring discharge or ranking agreement (subordination)
7. **Property situs** - Scotland; governed by Scots law. Heritable security enforcement rules differ from US state laws. Residential and commercial property have different statutory protections.

## Drafting Workflow

### 1. Caption & Parties

Identify borrower (granter of standard security) and lender (grantee/holder of standard security) with full legal name, address, and Scottish company number (for entities) as on the registered Standard Security and Personal Bond.

### 2. Recitals

State: original loan date and amount, Personal Bond and Standard Security execution and registration details (title number, date of registration), nature of default (monetary or non-monetary), outstanding debt total, lender's enforcement rights under s.19/s.21 of the 1970 Act, and parties' preference for negotiated resolution. Emphasise voluntary, arms-length nature. For residential property, note the protections of the Home Owner and Debtor Protection (Scotland) Act 2010 and that the borrower has received independent legal advice or has waived the statutory protections voluntarily.

### 3. Property Conveyance

- **Disposition clause** - borrower (as granter) dispones, conveys, and transfers (not "grants, conveys, and transfers all right, title, and interest") all right, title, and interest in the heritable property
- **Particular description** - reproduce verbatim from the Title Sheet in the Land Register of Scotland (not from the Standard Security)
- **Deed type** - **Disposition** (standard Scottish form for transferring heritable property). Quitclaim has no Scottish equivalent, a Disposition always warrants title. If no transfer of title is needed (lender already holds the Standard Security), a Deed of Discharge of Standard Security alone may suffice, with the lender entering into possession or selling under its powers.
- **Fixtures and moveables** - list items transferring with the heritable property; Scottish property law treats fixtures differently, items physically attached to the land are part of the heritage
- **Delivery condition** - vacant possession, broom-clean by entry date; surrender keys, codes, fobs, security passwords

### 4. Consideration & Debt Resolution

- Lender accepts the Discharge (and/or Disposition) in **full satisfaction and discharge** of the Personal Bond and Standard Security
- **Deficiency** - state explicitly whether waived or surviving. Under Scots law, deficiency waivers are less formalised than US anti-deficiency statutes. They are usually dealt with by Minute of Agreement or incorporated in the Deed of Discharge.
  - **Residential property**: The **Home Owner and Debtor Protection (Scotland) Act 2010** restricts lenders' ability to enforce calling-up alone. For dwelling houses, the lender must apply to the sheriff court for repossession. A borrower cannot simply "walk away" - court involvement is mandatory for residential enforcement.
  - **Commercial property**: Calling-up Notice (s.19, 1970 Act) - 2-month period to redeem. After expiry, lender may sell or enter into possession. Notice of Default (s.21) for non-monetary breaches, with 1-month compliance period.
- **Additional consideration** - cash, relocation assistance, credit reporting accommodation with amounts, timing, conditions

### 5. Mutual Releases

- **Lender releases** borrower from all liability under the Personal Bond and Standard Security, effective upon delivery of the Deed of Discharge and (if applicable) registration of the Disposition in the Land Register of Scotland. The lender records a Discharge of Standard Security (Form E, Schedule 5 to the 1970 Act) and withdraws the entry in the Register of Inhibitions.
- **Borrower releases** lender from claims arising from origination, servicing, and enforcement; carve out unrelated claims
- **Indemnification** (borrower → lender) - undisclosed securities, inhibitions, overriding interests, environmental contamination, title sheet discrepancies, property defects; survival period 1 to 3 years (negotiated). Scottish "title sheet" warranties are limited by the Keeper's indemnity under the Land Registration (Scotland) Act 2012.

### 6. Representations & Warranties

**Borrower**: good and marketable title free from undisclosed standard securities, inhibitions, or encumbrances; no pending proceedings affecting title; compliance with planning and building regulations; material defects disclosed; no known environmental contamination; full authority to execute. Use "to the borrower's actual knowledge, without duty of enquiry" qualifiers where appropriate.

**Lender**: authority to accept the Discharge/Disposition, release debt, discharge the Standard Security; necessary corporate authorisations obtained (board resolution under the articles of association or LLP agreement).

### 7. Closing Mechanics

**Borrower delivers**: executed Deed of Discharge and/or Disposition (properly witnessed, see Execution & Registration below), entity authority documents (board minutes, certified copy of memorandum/articles), keys, fobs, access codes, planning/building warrants if held.

**Lender delivers**: executed Deed of Discharge of Standard Security (Form E), cash consideration/relocation payment, officer's certificate confirming authorisation, credit reporting accommodation letter.

**Cost allocation** (customise per deal): Registers of Scotland registration fees and Keeper's search fees typically lender; land and buildings transaction tax (LBTT) - confirm liability; legal costs, own account in Scotland (general rule).

**Registration**: The lender registers the Disposition and/or Discharge in the Land Register of Scotland within [X] business days; provides borrower with certified copy of the updated Title Sheet within [X] days.

### 8. Tax Provisions

- **HMRC treatment**: The lender may be able to claim bad debt relief. Unlike the US, there is **no HMRC equivalent of IRS Form 1099-C**. The borrower should consider the UK tax treatment of debt write-off: if the borrower is a company, the loan relationship rules (CTA 2009) apply. If an individual, the write-off of the personal obligation may have no immediate tax charge unless a benefit in kind arises.
- **Land and Buildings Transaction Tax (LBTT)**: May be chargeable on the transfer of heritable property. The value is the debt released (market value of the property). Check whether reliefs apply (e.g., transfer between connected parties, lender entering into possession, seek professional advice).
- **Capital Gains Tax (CGT)**: For individuals, the disposal of heritable property to a lender may crystallise a chargeable gain. Principal private residence relief may apply (s.222 TCGA 1992).
- Each party bears its own tax liability; the agreement is not tax advice, direct parties to independent tax counsel experienced in Scottish property transactions.

### 9. General Provisions

Governing law (Scotland, Scots law as the law of the contract, with Scottish courts having exclusive jurisdiction), entire agreement, written amendments only, severability, written notices (personal delivery/courier/recorded delivery with 2-day deemed receipt, Scots law differs from US certified mail), no waiver by failure to exercise rights.

### 10. Execution & Registration

- **Signature blocks** with full name, title if entity, date. Under Scots law, an individual may sign or an authorised signatory may sign on behalf of an entity.
- **Witnessing requirements**: Scottish registration requirements differ from US notary rules.
  - For registration in the Land Register of Scotland, deeds must be properly witnessed:
    - **2 witnesses** recommended (or 1 witness with attestation clause)
    - Witnesses must be independent (not the other party, not the spouse of the granter)
    - Witness must provide full name and address
  - **Notarisation is NOT required** for Standard Securities or Dispositions under Scots law (unlike US deeds, Scotland abolished notarisation for most land deeds).
  - **Attestation clause**: Standard Scottish wording - "IN WITNESS WHEREOF these presents [substituted/recorded as applicable] are executed as follows: Signed by the Granter at [place] on [date] in the presence of [witness name and address]..."
- **Registration elements** (confirm per Registers of Scotland practice):
  - Application for Registration (Land Register of Scotland form)
  - Title Sheet number (not legal description)
  - Grantor/Grantee names as on Title Sheet
  - Disposition or Discharge form
  - Keeper's registration fee
  - LBTT return receipt (if required)
  - Certified copy of underlying authority resolution (for entities)

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **Junior standard securities are not extinguished** - arrange discharge or ranking agreement before closing, or the lender accepts subject to them. Inhibitions (registered interdicts) must also be cleared.
- **Involuntariness risk** - document voluntary, arms-length nature throughout; inadequate consideration or facility (undue influence) vitiates the deed. Scottish courts may set aside a disposition granted under facility and circumvention.
- **Title search** - lender should obtain a current **Title Sheet search from Registers of Scotland** (not US title insurance) before closing to catch undisclosed securities, inhibitions, or overriding interests.
- **Entity borrowers** - Scottish company/LLP must provide board minutes, memorandum/articles demonstrating authority. Trusts need trust deed and trustee certificate confirming authority. Unincorporated association, check constitution.
- **Environmental** - for commercial property, consider Phase I Environmental Audit before acceptance (but under Scots law, the acquiring owner can inherit liability under the contaminated land regime, Part IIA EPA 1990 as applied in Scotland).
- **Register promptly** - registration in the Land Register perfects the lender's title. Until registration, the lender has only a personal right, not a real right. The borrower's creditors may attach the property through an inhibition or sequestration (bankruptcy).
- **Residential vs. commercial** - ensure correct procedure: the 2010 Act protections apply only to "dwelling houses" (residential). Commercial property enforcement follows the 1970 Act with calling-up.
- **Spousal/partner occupancy rights** - Mortgage Rights (Scotland) Act 2001 gives the borrower's spouse/civil partner/cohabitant the right to occupy, even after enforcement proceedings have begun. The lender may need to apply to the sheriff to terminate these rights.
- **No tax/financial advice** - refer parties to independent Scottish property tax counsel.
- **Keeper's indemnity** - if a title defect is discovered post-acquisition, the lender may claim against the Keeper's indemnity fund under the Land Registration (Scotland) Act 2012.

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

**Adapted for:** Scotland and UK law

**Changes made:**
- **Mortgage/deed of trust** → Standard Security (Conveyancing and Feudal Reform (Scotland) Act 1970, Form A or Form B)
- **Deed in lieu of foreclosure** → Voluntary surrender under Home Owner and Debtor Protection (Scotland) Act 2010 (residential) or formal Discharge of Standard Security combined with Disposition of heritable property (commercial)
- **Note** → Personal Bond / Loan Agreement
- **Foreclosure** → Calling-up Notice (s.19, 2-month period) / Notice of Default (s.21-23, 1-month compliance) for commercial; court application to sheriff for repossession (residential under 2010 Act)
- **Real Property** → Heritable Property
- **Personal Property** → Moveable Property
- **Title Insurance** → Title Sheet search from Land Register of Scotland / Keeper's indemnity fund
- **County Recorder** → Registers of Scotland (Keeper of the Registers)
- **Warranty/Quitclaim Deed** → Disposition (standard Scottish form, no Scottish quitclaim equivalent)
- **Legal description** → Particular description from Title Sheet (Land Register of Scotland)
- **State of situs** → Scotland (Scots law governs, no US state law variation)
- **Notary / witness rules** → Notarisation NOT required for Standard Securities or Dispositions under Scots law. Proper witnessing required: 2 independent witnesses, attestation clause
- **Anti-deficiency statutes** → Replaced by Home Owner and Debtor Protection (Scotland) Act 2010 (residential, court application mandatory; lender cannot enforce by calling-up alone for dwelling houses)
- **Deficiency waivers** → Less formalised than US; usually done by Minute of Agreement or incorporated in Deed of Discharge
- **IRS Form 1099-C (COD income)** → No UK equivalent. HMRC bad debt relief for lenders; loan relationship rules for corporate borrowers
- **IRC § 108 (insolvency exclusion)** → UK loan relationship rules (CTA 2009, Part 5) / corporate debt rules; no equivalent individual insolvency exclusion from COD income
- **Florida/Georgia/South Carolina/Connecticut witness rules** → Scottish witnessing under Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995; 2 witnesses recommended
- **Entity jurisdiction** → Scottish company number (registered in Scotland) + registered office; memorandum and articles of association
- **Governing law** → Scotland (Scots law) with Scottish courts having exclusive jurisdiction
- **Power of sale** → Standard Security remedy under the 1970 Act: lender may sell property after serving Calling-Up Notice (2 months) and expiry. For residential property, court application required under 2010 Act
- **Recording fees** → Registers of Scotland registration fees (not county recording fees)
- **Transfer tax** → Land and Buildings Transaction Tax (LBTT) (not US state transfer taxes)
- **Certified mail** → Recorded delivery post / sheriff officer service
- **Zoning** → Planning permission and building warrants (Scottish planning system under Town and Country Planning (Scotland) Act 1997)

**Key Scottish/UK considerations:**
- The Home Owner and Debtor Protection (Scotland) Act 2010 fundamentally changes enforcement for residential property, lenders cannot rely on calling-up alone; they need a sheriff court repossession order. This affects feasibility of "voluntary surrender" as an alternative.
- Mortgage Rights (Scotland) Act 2001 gives spousal/partner occupancy rights that survive enforcement, lender must check if non-borrower occupants have rights and whether court steps are needed to terminate them.
- The Keeper of the Registers of Scotland provides an indemnity for title defects, equivalent to US title insurance but operates differently and is funded by registration fees.
- No notarisation requirement for Standard Securities or Dispositions, but proper witnessing is mandatory for Land Register registration.
- Scottish property law distinguishes between "personal right" and "real right" - until a Disposition is registered in the Land Register, the lender has only a personal right; the borrower's creditors or trustee in sequestration could intervene.
- LBTT may apply on the transfer, check reliefs (e.g., transfer to lender in satisfaction of debt, acquisition by financial institution in course of lending). Professional advice essential.
- The Register of Inhibitions is the Scottish equivalent of judgment liens, must be searched pre-completion.
- Commercial property enforcement under the 1970 Act is faster than residential (no court application) but still requires strict compliance with the calling-up/notice of default statutory procedure.
- The "voluntary surrender" concept is less developed in Scots law than US deed-in-lieu, careful deed-drafting is essential to avoid it being recharacterised as a standard enforcement.
