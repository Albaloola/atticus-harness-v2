# Employment Offer Letter [SCOTS, Scotland/UK Adaptation]

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a formal employment offer letter for Scotland/UK communicating all material terms while ensuring compliance with the Employment Rights Act 1996 (ERA 1996), the National Minimum Wage Act 1998, the Working Time Regulations 1998, the Equality Act 2010, the Pensions Act 2008, and the Immigration, Asylum and Nationality Act 2006. This template replaces US at-will concepts with UK contractual notice provisions and statutory employment protections.

**Important**: Employment in Scotland/UK is not "at-will". It is governed by the contract of employment and the ERA 1996. Either party may terminate on contractual notice (or pay in lieu), subject to statutory minimum notice periods. Unfair dismissal protections apply after two years' continuous service (or immediately for automatically unfair reasons).

## Required Inputs

1. **Employer** - legal entity name, registered address, company registration number (if limited company), authorised signatory name and title
2. **Employee** - full legal name, home address
3. **Position** - job title, department, line manager, start date, place of work, hybrid/remote/travel requirements
4. **Compensation** - annual salary or hourly rate, pay frequency (usually monthly), National Living Wage / National Minimum Wage compliance check
5. **Variable pay** - bonus, commission, or incentive details (if any) - note these are typically discretionary unless otherwise agreed
6. **Benefits and pensions** - workplace pension auto-enrolment (Pensions Act 2008), employer contribution rate, opt-out information, private medical/health cover, life assurance, cycle-to-work scheme, etc.
7. **Leave** - statutory holiday entitlement (28 days pro-rata including bank holidays if applicable), sick pay (SSP), family leave
8. **Conditions precedent** - right to work check (Immigration, Asylum and Nationality Act 2006), Disclosure Scotland check (Basic, Standard, or Enhanced PVG where appropriate), reference checks, professional qualifications verification, execution of post-termination restrictive covenants (if applicable)
9. **Probationary period** - typical duration (3 to 6 months), notice period during probation (usually 1 week), confirmation of passing probation
10. **Notice period** - contractual notice by employer and employee after probation (e.g. 1 week per full year of service, capped at 12 weeks for statutory); shorter notice during probation
11. **Acceptance deadline** - typically 5 to 10 business days from letter date

## Letter Structure

### 1. Header

Company letterhead, date, employee name and address in formal business letter format. Include company registered number and registered office if desired.

### 2. Opening

State the nature of the offer clearly. Do not imply guaranteed or permanent employment:

> "We are pleased to offer you the position of **[Job Title]** on the terms set out in this letter and the attached written statement of particulars."

### 3. Position Details

Include: job title, department, reports-to, start date, place of work (or homeworking/hybrid arrangement), travel/relocation requirements. Add duties reservation clause:

> "Your duties may be reasonably varied by the Company as business needs evolve. Any material change to your role or responsibilities will be discussed with you in advance."

### 4. Compensation

- **Base salary**: annual figure + per-period breakdown (e.g., "£68,000/yr, paid monthly in arrears by bank transfer")
- **National Living Wage / National Minimum Wage**: verify rate exceeds applicable statutory minimum (review current rates at time of offer)
- **Bonus/commission**: label as **discretionary**, subject to company policy, contingent on continued employment and performance; note no contractual right to bonus unless specified
- **Benefits**: list coverages with eligibility date; include: "Benefits are governed by the relevant rules and policy documents, which control in case of any discrepancy and are subject to change at the Company's discretion."

### 5. Notice Period and Termination Provisions

This clause replaces the US at-will clause entirely:

> "Your employment will be subject to a contractual notice period of **[X] weeks/months** by either party, once you have successfully completed your probationary period. During the probationary period of **[X] months**, the notice period is **[Y] week(s)** (or 1 week if none specified). After probation, the notice period shall be as set out in the written statement of particulars provided under section 1 of the Employment Rights Act 1996. The Company reserves the right to make a payment in lieu of notice (PILON) and/or place you on garden leave during your notice period."

> "This does not affect any statutory rights either party may have under the Employment Rights Act 1996, including the right to bring a claim for unfair dismissal or wrongful dismissal."

> **Statutory minimum notice** (ERA 1996 s.86): employee with 1 month+ continuous employment, at least 1 week per full year of service up to 12 weeks. The contractual notice period should not be less than the statutory minimum.

### 6. Probationary Period

> "Your employment is subject to a probationary period of **[X] months**. During this period, we will assess your suitability for the role. The Company may extend the probationary period at its discretion. On successful completion of probation, your employment will continue subject to the notice period set out above. The Company may terminate your employment during probation on **[Y] week(s)** notice. Passing probation does not guarantee continued employment for any fixed term or period."

### 7. Conditions Precedent

List all applicable conditions:

- Right to work check under the Immigration, Asylum and Nationality Act 2006 (including provision of original documents)
- Disclosure Scotland check (specify Basic, Standard, or Enhanced PVG as applicable to the role) [NOTE: drug screening only applies to specific regulated roles and is generally not standard for most positions]
- Reference checks and verification of professional qualifications, Execution of any post-termination restrictive covenant agreement (enforceable only to the extent it protects a legitimate business interest)
- Registration with a professional body (if required)

**Important**: Under UK data protection law (UK GDPR / Data Protection Act 2018), disclose the lawful basis for processing criminal records data.

Reference separate agreements by name but **do not incorporate their terms** into the letter.

Include integration clause:

> "This letter, together with the written statement of particulars and any agreements referenced herein (including any post-termination restrictive covenant agreement), constitutes the entire agreement regarding your employment terms and supersedes all prior discussions, correspondence, or representations."

### 8. Written Statement of Particulars

Under section 1 of the ERA 1996, the employer must provide a principal written statement within 2 months of the start of employment listing:

- Names of employer and employee, Date employment began and date of continuous employment, Scale or rate of pay and frequency, Hours of work (including any variable hours arrangement)
- Holiday entitlement and public holiday arrangements, Sick pay and sick leave provisions, Pension and pension scheme details, Notice periods, Job title or brief description, Place of work, Any collective agreements affecting terms, Grievance and disciplinary procedures, All terms relating to absence due to incapacity

Indicate in the offer letter that a full written statement of particulars will be provided on or before the start date (or within 2 months as required by law).

### 9. Post-Termination Restrictive Covenants (if applicable)

If post-termination restrictions (non-compete, non-solicitation, non-dealing) are required, they must be set out in a separate agreement, not in the offer letter. They are enforceable in Scotland/UK only to the extent they protect a legitimate business interest and are no wider than reasonably necessary.

### 10. Acceptance and Signature

- State deadline: "This offer expires at [time] on [date] if not accepted."
- Instruct the employee to sign, date, and return via specified method, Note offer may be withdrawn if conditions precedent are not met, Include company signatory block (name, title, company) and employee acceptance block with signature, date, and printed name lines

## Pitfalls and Checks

- **Never** use the term "at-will" - it has no legal meaning in Scotland/UK and could create confusion
- **Never** suggest employment is "permanent" or for an indefinite term without acknowledging notice provisions
- **Ensure** written statement of particulars is issued within 2 months of start (ERA 1996 s.1)
- **Non-compete / post-termination restrictions**: ensure they are no wider than necessary to protect a legitimate business interest, unreasonably wide restrictions are unenforceable
- **Pension auto-enrolment**: confirm opt-in/opt-out obligations under the Pensions Act 2008; reference the pension scheme in the offer
- **National Minimum Wage / National Living Wage**: confirm rate exceeds the current applicable statutory minimum
- **Right to work checks**: carry out before employment begins; retain copies as required by Home Office guidance
- **Probation**: set clear expectations; note that passing probation does not mean employment becomes "permanent" - the notice provisions continue to apply
- **Holiday entitlement**: 5.6 weeks (28 days for full-time worker) inclusive of bank holidays unless otherwise stated
- **Family leave**: inform employee of statutory entitlements, maternity, paternity, adoption, shared parental leave, parental bereavement leave
- **Disclosure Scotland**: specify correct level (Basic, Standard, Enhanced, or PVG); comply with the Rehabilitation of Offenders Act 1974 (as amended in Scotland)
- Keep benefits high-level; defer detail to plan documents, Do not incorporate terms of separate agreements (restrictive covenants, NDA) into the letter body, Target 2 to 3 pages; professional tone, plain language
- **Equality Act 2010**: ensure terms are non-discriminatory across all nine protected characteristics

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
