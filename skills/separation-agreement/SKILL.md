# Settlement Agreement (Scotland/UK)

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

### Expected work product

Where proportionate, produce a chronology, issue map, source log, evidence matrix, merits/risk table, remedy/damages table, procedural route note, draft document, bundle index, service/filing checklist, and operator handoff note. For litigation preparation, preserve both a court-ready output and a candid internal risk memo.

Drafts a settlement agreement with ex gratia consideration, comprehensive releases, and post-employment covenants tailored to Scots law and UK employment legislation.

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

1. **Employee details** - name, title, hire date, separation date, reason
2. **Compensation data** - salary, accrued holiday pay, unpaid bonuses/commissions, share scheme details
3. **Existing agreements** - offer letter, employment contract, invention assignment, prior restrictive covenants
4. **Settlement terms** - ex gratia payment amount/schedule, benefits continuation, outplacement
5. **Governing jurisdiction** - Scots law (parties may choose; default for Scotland-based employment)
6. **Employee age** - Equality Act 2010 age discrimination provisions apply at any age; no specific 40+ trigger

## Agreement Sections

Draft these sections in order:

### 1. Opening and Recitals

Full legal names, corporate form (e.g. limited company), registered address, effective termination date. Recitals: acknowledge period of employment, mutual desire to settle all matters, agreement is in full and final settlement.

### 2. Termination Terms and Final Compensation

Address each: termination date, unpaid salary through that date, accrued holiday pay (min 5.6 weeks statutory; contractual if higher), earned bonuses/commissions, outstanding expense reimbursement, benefits cessation date, contractual PILON if applicable, and property return deadline.

### 3. Consideration and Settlement Payment

- [ ] Ex gratia payment amount and schedule (lump sum or instalments)
- [ ] State explicitly this exceeds statutory or contractual entitlements
- [ ] Tax position: first £30,000 of termination payment is generally tax-free under ITEPA 2003 s.403-404; amounts above taxable. Employer may apply HMRC rules
- [ ] Private medical insurance continuation if offered (no COBRA equivalent in UK)
- [ ] Outplacement services if offered
- [ ] Share scheme treatment, vesting acceleration, exercise windows per scheme rules and Articles
- [ ] Tax withholding language (direct employee to consult tax advisor)

### 4. Release of Claims

**Full and final settlement** covering employer, subsidiaries, officers, directors, employees, agents, successors, assigns.

Released claims (non-exhaustive): unfair dismissal (Employment Rights Act 1996 s.94), wrongful dismissal, Equality Act 2010 claims (age, disability, race, sex, religion, sexual orientation, gender reassignment, marriage/civil partnership, pregnancy/maternity), Working Time Regulations 1998, National Minimum Wage Act 1998, Trade Union and Labour Relations (Consolidation) Act 1992, Part-time Workers Regulations 2000, Fixed-term Employees Regulations 2002, Transfer of Undertakings (TUPE) Regulations 2006, breach of contract, delict claims, defamation. No equivalent of Cal. Civ. Code § 1542 in Scots law.

**Statutory conditions for validity:**
- Agreement must be in writing, Must relate to the particular proceedings (or particular claims)
- Employee must have received independent legal advice from a relevant independent adviser (solicitor, authorised trade union official, or advice centre worker)
- The adviser must have a current contract of insurance or professional indemnity covering the risk of a claim for negligence, The agreement must identify the adviser

**Mandatory carve-outs** (non-waivable):
- Personal injury claims not known at the date of the agreement, Accrued pension rights, Claims arising after execution, Indemnification rights under Articles of Association/D&O insurance, Public interest disclosure (whistleblowing) claims under Employment Rights Act 1996 s.43A-L, Auto-enrolment pension duties

### 5. Equality Act 2010 - Age Discrimination

No separate ADEA/OWBPA framework in UK. Age discrimination claims are brought under the Equality Act 2010. No special consideration periods or revocation rights unique to age. General principles:
- Employee must have opportunity to take independent legal advice, No minimum consideration period (customary: 7 to 14 calendar days)
- No statutory revocation period (employer may include one contractually)

**Note:** If the settlement relates to a collective redundancy situation, the consultation requirements under TULR(C)A 1992 s.188 apply independently.

### 6. Restrictive Covenants

**Non-disparagement** (mutual): Employee and employer officers/directors. Carve-outs for truthful testimony, whistleblowing, and protected disclosures. Express reference to Employment Rights Act 1996 s.43A-L and Public Interest Disclosure Act 1998.

**Confidentiality**: Trade secrets, customer lists, financials, business strategies, non-public information. Agreement terms also confidential. Permitted disclosures: spouse/civil partner, solicitor, accountant, tax advisor, HMRC, as required by law. Trade Secrets (Enforcement, etc.) Regulations 2018 - no equivalent of DTSA immunity notice required. Survives indefinitely.

**Non-compete** (if applicable): Enforceability governed by Scots common law restraint of trade doctrine. Must protect a legitimate business interest; narrowly tailored in scope, geography, and duration (typically 3 to 12 months in Scotland). Greater risk of unenforceability than US at-will states. No consideration beyond continued employment generally insufficient for post-termination restrictions formed during employment.

**Non-solicitation**: Customers and employees, typically 6 to 12 months in Scotland. Confirm reasonableness under Scots law; must protect a legitimate business interest.

**Reaffirmation**: Incorporate surviving obligations from prior confidentiality or restrictive covenant agreements by reference.

### 7. Cooperation and Non-Admission

Employee cooperates in pending/future litigation, investigations, regulatory matters. Employer reimburses reasonable expenses and compensates at reasonable hourly rate. Non-admission clause: agreement is not admission of liability.

### 8. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

Governing law: Scots law. Exclusive jurisdiction: Scottish courts (Court of Session or Sheriff Court depending on value). If arbitration: specify rules (e.g., Scottish Arbitration Centre Rules, or LCIA), seat in Scotland, single arbitrator, cost allocation.

### 9. General Provisions

Severability, entire agreement, written amendment requirement, no-waiver, counterparts (electronic signatures valid under Scots law), notice with delivery methods.

### 10. Execution and Acknowledgments

Signature blocks with dates for employee and authorised employer representative. Execution as a deed may be required if no consideration or to extend the prescriptive period.

Employee acknowledgment:
- [ ] Read and understood the agreement
- [ ] Received independent legal advice from [Name of Adviser/Firm]
- [ ] Understands adviser is insured against negligence claims
- [ ] Enters voluntarily
- [ ] Understands rights being released and statutory conditions for validity

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from the US original for Scots law and UK employment legislation.

| US Concept | Scotland/UK Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Separation Agreement | Settlement Agreement (formerly Compromise Agreement) |
| ADEA/OWBPA (40+ age) | Equality Act 2010 (age, any age; no OWBPA equivalent) |
| Title VII, ADA, GINA | Equality Act 2010 |
| FMLA | Maternity/Paternity/Adoption/Shared Parental Leave Regulations; Parental Bereavement Leave |
| FLSA (wage/hour) | National Minimum Wage Act 1998; Working Time Regulations 1998 |
| WARN Act (layoff notice) | TULR(C)A 1992 s.188 (collective redundancy consultation) |
| ERISA | Pensions Act 2004; The Pensions Regulator |
| COBRA subsidy | No UK equivalent (NHS, no continuation requirement) |
| EEOC charge filing | Employment Tribunal claim; ACAS early conciliation |
| SEC whistleblower (Dodd-Frank) | Employment Rights Act 1996 s.43A-L (protected disclosures); Public Interest Disclosure Act 1998 |
| DTSA immunity (18 U.S.C. § 1833(b)) | Not required in UK; Trade Secrets (Enforcement, etc.) Regulations 2018 apply |
| Cal. Civ. Code § 1542 | No Scots equivalent |
| State law | Scots law |
| AAA Employment Arbitration Rules | Scottish Arbitration Centre / LCIA rules |
| PTO payout | Holiday pay (statutory 5.6 weeks + contractual); check written statement of particulars |
| At-will employment (generally) | Notice periods required; statutory minimum under ERA 1996 s.86 |
| Tax treatment (severance) | ITEPA 2003 s.403-404; first £30,000 tax-free termination payment |
| Workers' compensation | No direct equivalent; employer liability insurance covers workplace injury |
| Jury trial waiver | No civil jury in Scottish civil proceedings (rare exceptions) |
| US dollar amounts | Convert to GBP (roughly £0.80 per USD) |

**Key differences from US employment law:**
- No at-will employment; minimum statutory notice periods under ERA 1996 s.86
- No ADEA/OWBPA framework; age discrimination under Equality Act 2010 without age-40 threshold, Settlement agreements require independent legal adviser, written form, and specified conditions for validity, First £30,000 of termination payment tax-free (no equivalent US tax treatment)
- No COBRA; NHS means no mandatory health insurance continuation, Prescriptive period: delict claims 5 years (Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973); personal injury 3 years, Restrictive covenants assessed under restraint of trade doctrine (less employer-friendly than many US states)
- Tribunal fees: abolished in Scotland (2017); employment tribunal claims no longer fee-barred

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.

## Final Quality Gate (Mandatory)

Before marking the task complete, confirm:

- Jurisdiction/forum/procedure have been identified and are not imported from the wrong legal system.
- Current law, rules, forms, fees, deadlines, and public-body guidance have been verified from official sources where necessary.
- Every material factual assertion is tied to evidence, a source, an admission, an instruction, or a clearly labelled assumption.
- Prescription, limitation, time bar, appeal periods, service rules, competency, standing/title to sue, expenses/costs exposure, and enforcement have been considered where relevant.
- The output separates client-facing conclusions from internal risk analysis.
- Drafts include placeholders only where evidence or instructions are genuinely missing; no fabricated citations, authorities, quotes, dates, forms, or procedural steps are allowed.
- A hostile reviewer could reconstruct the reasoning from the evidence matrix and source log.
