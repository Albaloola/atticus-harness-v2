Drafts a structured agreement allowing a debtor who has been sequestrated (Scottish bankruptcy) to retain secured collateral by continuing payment obligations. Note: Scots law does not have a direct "reaffirmation" concept equivalent to 11 U.S.C. § 524(c). This skill provides a general methodology adapted for Scottish practice, with appropriate caveats.

[SCOTS: Note: Reaffirmation agreements are specific to US Chapter 7 bankruptcy under 11 U.S.C. § 524(c). In Scotland, sequestration is governed by the Bankruptcy (Scotland) Act 2016. There is no statutory reaffirmation process. A debtor wishing to retain secured property after sequestration must either (a) redeem the security by paying the secured creditor the value of the asset, or (b) negotiate a voluntary arrangement with the creditor. The Trustee in the sequestration may also sell the asset and distribute proceeds. This skill provides an analogous drafting framework but Scots law does not recognise a court-filed reaffirmation agreement.]

## Prerequisites

Collect before drafting:

1. **Parties** - debtor/creditor legal names, addresses, sequestration case number, date of sequestration award
2. **Original loan** - date, principal, interest rate, payment amount/frequency, maturity
3. **Current status** - outstanding balance (principal + accrued interest + fees), payment history, default status
4. **Collateral** - description, current fair market value, security (standard security / floating charge)
5. **Modified terms** (if any) - reduced rate, extended term, principal reduction
6. **Income/expenditure statement** - debtor's current income and expenditure for affordability analysis
7. **Representation status** - whether debtor has a solicitor (determines court approval / Accountant in Bankruptcy involvement)

### Part B: The Agreement

1. **Party identification** - full legal names, addresses
2. **Original loan terms** - date, principal, rate, payment, frequency, maturity
3. **Reaffirmed terms** - state whether original or modified terms apply
4. **If modified**, include side-by-side comparison:

| Term | Original | Reaffirmed |
|---|---|---|
| Interest Rate | {orig}% | {new}% |
| Monthly Payment | £{orig} | £{new} |
| Remaining Term | {orig} | {new} |
| Total Remaining | £{orig} | £{new} |

### Part C: Debtor Certification

Debtor certifies (under oath, confirm procedural form):

- Agreement is voluntary and not required by the Trustee or the court, Debtor can make required payments from income, Agreement does not impose undue hardship on debtor or dependents, Debtor understands the right to cancel within the period prescribed by the Bankruptcy (Scotland) Act 2016
- Debtor understands default allows the creditor to enforce the standard security and pursue recovery of any shortfall

### Part D: Solicitor/Solicitor Certification

Required when debtor is legally represented. Solicitor certifies:

- Agreement fully explained to debtor, Debtor entered agreement voluntarily with informed consent, Debtor advised of legal effect, consequences, and default consequences, Solicitor believes debtor can afford payments, Agreement does not impose undue hardship

### Part E: Court / AiB Approval

Required when:
- Debtor is **not represented by a solicitor**, OR, Income/expenditure shows income minus expenses **insufficient** to cover reaffirmed payments

Include notice of scheduled hearing (Sheriff Court); court must find (1) no undue hardship and (2) agreement is in debtor's best interest.

### Signature Block

Include signature lines for:
- **Debtor** - signature, printed name, date
- **Creditor** - signature, printed name, title, date
- **Debtor's solicitor** (if applicable) - signature, printed name, date
- **Notarization block** - include if required

## Pitfalls and Checks

- **Rescission rights** - ensure the debtor is aware of any statutory cancellation rights under the Consumer Credit Act 1974 or other applicable legislation
- **No coercion language** - agreement must affirmatively state the debtor is not required to reaffirm
- **Default consequences** - prominently warn: creditor may repossess collateral AND pursue shortfall (if permitted by law)
- **Collateral-to-debt ratio** - if debt substantially exceeds collateral value, flag for additional disclosure
- **Accountant in Bankruptcy (AiB) review** - the trustee or AiB may review any agreement that affects estate realisation; draft anticipating scrutiny
- **Plain language** - maintain legal precision while ensuring the debtor can actually understand the obligation

## Scotland/UK Adaptation

This skill has been adapted from a US reaffirmation agreement under 11 U.S.C. § 524(c) to the Scottish legal context.

### Key Differences

| US Concept | Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Bankruptcy (Chapter 7/13) | Sequestration (Bankruptcy (Scotland) Act 2016) |
| 11 U.S.C. § 524(c) reaffirmation | No direct equivalent; voluntary payment agreement |
| U.S. Trustee | Accountant in Bankruptcy (AiB) |
| Bankruptcy Rule 4008 | No equivalent rule |
| Official Form 240A | No prescribed Scottish form |
| Collateral (UCC Article 9) | Standard security / heritable security / floating charge |
| Adversary proceeding | Ordinary cause in Sheriff Court |
| Section 341 meeting of creditors | Meeting of creditors (or AiB determination) |
| Discharge of debtor | Discharge under Bankruptcy (Scotland) Act 2016 |

### Applicable Legislation, Bankruptcy (Scotland) Act 2016
- Consumer Credit Act 1974
- Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995
- Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973

### Forms
Scottish sequestration does not use Official Form 240A. For voluntary payment agreements with secured creditors, there is no prescribed form. A solicitor should draft a bespoke agreement. Relevant forms for the sequestration itself are available from the Accountant in Bankruptcy website (www.aib.gov.uk).

[SCOTS: Note: This skill provides an analogous drafting framework only. In Scotland, a sequestrated debtor wishing to retain secured property should seek advice from a solicitor or insolvency practitioner. The Trustee in Sequestration has powers over the debtor's estate that may override any private agreement. Always verify with a Scottish insolvency specialist before finalising any document.]
