### UK Regulatory Framework

#### UK GDPR (Retained EU Law)

The UK GDPR is substantially identical to the EU GDPR at the time of Brexit (31 December 2020), but the UK may diverge over time. Key provisions:

| Article | Subject |
|---|---|
| Art. 5(1) | Principles (lawfulness, fairness, transparency, purpose limitation, data minimisation, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity, accountability) |
| Art. 6(1) | Lawfulness of processing (consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, legitimate interests) |
| Art. 7 | Conditions for consent (freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous; withdrawal right) |
| Art. 13 | Information to be provided where personal data collected from data subject |
| Art. 15 to 22 | Data subject rights (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection) |
| Art. 25 | Data protection by design and default |
| Art. 35 | Data protection impact assessment |

#### Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA 2018)

- Sits alongside UK GDPR, Provides derogations, exemptions, and supplementary provisions, Schedules 1 to 4: Exemptions, special categories of data, etc.
- Enforcement powers: ICO can issue fines up to £17.5m or 4% of annual worldwide turnover (whichever is higher) for UK GDPR breaches; up to £8.7m or 2% for other breaches

#### Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR) 2003 (SI 2003/2426)

The core cookie-specific regulation in the UK. Key provisions:

| Regulation | Subject |
|---|---|
| Reg. 6 | **Cookie consent** - "A person shall not store or gain access to information stored in the terminal equipment of a subscriber or user unless the subscriber or user of that terminal equipment has given his or her consent" |
| Reg. 6(4) | **Strictly necessary exception** - consent not required where the storage/access is "strictly necessary for the provision of a service requested by the subscriber or user" |
| Reg. 7 | **Safeguards** - information must be provided about the purposes of the processing |
| Reg. 22 | **Unsolicited marketing communications** (separate but related) |

**PECR amendments:** The Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (EC Directive) Regulations 2003 have been amended to align with ePrivacy Directive requirements, and further revised by The Privacy and Electronic Communications (EC Directive) (Amendment) Regulations 2011.

### Post-Brexit Changes

| Aspect | EU Position | UK Position (Post-Brexit) |
|---|---|---|
| GDPR reference | EU GDPR | UK GDPR (retained; separate instrument) |
| Lead regulator | Lead DPA in member state | ICO sole regulator |
| EDPB guidance | Binding on ICO | ICO can diverge (may issue own guidance) |
| Adequacy decision | EU adequacy for UK until expiry | UK adequacy for EU (interim) |
| ePrivacy Regulation | Proposed EU ePR (not final) | PECR 2003 remains in force |
| International transfers | Adequacy decisions + SCCs | UK adequacy decisions + UK SCCs/IDTA |
| DPA reference | No change | DPA 2018 retained |

**Current status (2026):** UK GDPR remains substantially aligned with EU GDPR. ICO issues guidance independently. Monitor ICO guidance for divergence on cookies, AI, legitimate interests, and consent.

### ICO Guidance on Cookies

The ICO has issued comprehensive guidance on cookies and similar technologies:

- **Cookies and similar technologies guidance** (updated regularly)
- **Key positions:**
  - Disguised or implied consent (e.g., continuing to browse) is **not valid**
  - Cookie walls are unlikely to produce valid consent
  - Pre-ticked boxes do not constitute valid consent
  - Granular opt-in required per category for non-essential cookies
  - Consent must be as easy to withdraw as to give
  - Analytics cookies require consent (unless strictly necessary)
  - First-party analytics cookies that are non-intrusive may be argued as legitimate interests (ICO has indicated some flexibility, but consent remains the safest approach)

### Scotland-Specific Notes

- **No separate Scottish cookie regime** - UK GDPR + DPA 2018 + PECR 2003 apply UK-wide
- **ICO is the sole UK regulator** - based in Wilmslow, Cheshire (not Scotland-specific)
- **Scottish public bodies** must comply with same UK GDPR/PECR requirements
- **Scottish courts:** Enforcement/regulatory action can proceed through Scottish courts (Court of Session or Sheriff Court) for ICO enforcement
- **No Scottish-specific cookie legislation**
- **Scottish Government** has no devolved competence on data protection (reserved matter under Scotland Act 1998)
- **Data Protection and Digital Information Bill** (UK Parliament) may introduce further divergence, monitor progress

### Consent Requirements (UK)

| Requirement | Source | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Affirmative action | UK GDPR Art. 4(11), Art. 7 | Unambiguous indication of wishes; active opt-in (no pre-ticked boxes) |
| Separate per purpose | UK GDPR Art. 7 | Cannot bundle multiple purposes into single consent |
| Withdrawal right | UK GDPR Art. 7(3) | Must be as easy to withdraw as to give |
| Evidence | UK GDPR Art. 7(1) | Controller must demonstrate consent |
| Specific and informed | UK GDPR Art. 4(11) | Each purpose clearly described |
| Freely given | UK GDPR Art. 4(11), Art. 7(4) | No coercion; cookie walls create imbalance |
| PECR consent | PECR Reg. 6 | Specific to storage/access to terminal equipment |
| Exemption | PECR Reg. 6(4) | Only "strictly necessary" cookies exempt |

### Legitimate Interests Assessment (for analytics cookies)

If relying on legitimate interests for analytics cookies (following ICO's potential flexibility for non-intrusive first-party analytics):

1. **Identify the legitimate interest** - e.g., website improvement, user experience analytics
2. **Necessity test** - is the processing necessary for that interest?
3. **Balancing test** - weigh against user rights and freedoms
4. **Document the LIA**
5. **Provide opt-out** - users must still have a right to object

*Note: The ICO's position on analytics consent has evolved. As of most recent guidance, the safest approach remains consent for all non-essential cookies, including analytics. The legitimate interests route for analytics carries risk and requires careful documentation.*

### Recommended Consent Flow (UK)

1. **First visit:** Banner appears with clear description and three buttons (Accept All, Reject Non-Essential, Customise)
2. **Customise:** Granular category toggles (strictly necessary, analytics, functionality, advertising/social)
3. **Default state:** Essential cookies only; all non-essential deselected
4. **Accept All:** Consent recorded for all categories
5. **Reject Non-Essential:** Only essential cookies set
6. **Withdrawal:** Same interface available through preference centre (link in footer)
7. **Re-consent:** After renewal period or material change
8. **Proof:** Record timestamp, choice, policy version, user-agent/IP hash
