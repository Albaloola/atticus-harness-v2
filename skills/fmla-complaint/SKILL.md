# UK Family & Parental Leave ET1 Claim, Employment Tribunal [SCOTS]

## Atticus UK/Scots Legal Excellence Overlay

Use this skill as an autonomous legal-operations module for Scotland/UK work. Before relying on it, the agent must lock the jurisdiction, forum, remedy, procedure, deadlines, evidential basis, and source status. Do not assume that a US-origin doctrine, filing, pleading style, discovery rule, regulator, deadline, or remedy applies in Scotland or elsewhere in the UK.

### Mandatory operating rules

1. **Jurisdiction lock.** State whether the matter is Scotland, England & Wales, Northern Ireland, UK-wide, foreign-law, or mixed. If Scotland is plausible, distinguish sheriff court, Court of Session, tribunals, regulators, ombudsmen, and internal institutional processes.
2. **Official-source hierarchy.** Prefer legislation.gov.uk, Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service rules/forms, Court of Session and sheriff court rules, tribunal/regulator guidance, UK Supreme Court materials, GOV.UK, Scottish Government, ICO, FCA, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, Land Register of Scotland, registers of Scotland, and other primary public sources. Treat secondary commentary as orientation only.
3. **Live verification.** Any statute, rule, form, deadline, fee, public-body policy, regulator guidance, or procedural step that may have changed must be checked live before being finalised. Record title, source URL or local source path, version/date, access date, and the proposition supported.
4. **Evidence discipline.** Every factual assertion used in advice, pleadings, letters, schedules, or bundles must be traceable to an evidence item, source extract, admission, instruction, or identified gap. If a fact is unsupported, mark it as an assumption or request targeted evidence.
5. **Element-by-element reasoning.** Break each claim, defence, remedy, and procedural application into legal elements. Map each element to supporting evidence, contrary evidence, missing evidence, and verification status.
6. **Autonomous depth.** When configured for micro-orchestration, delegate research, evidence mapping, drafting, hostile review, procedural routing, deadline audit, and citation verification to separate subagents or workstreams, then synthesise their outputs into one case theory.
7. **External-action boundary.** Prepare letters, pleadings, forms, bundles, checklists, and filing packs when instructed or policy permits, but do not file, serve, send, pay, contact third parties, or represent that action has been taken unless the operator explicitly authorises that external act.
8. **Uncertainty handling.** If law, procedure, forum, prescription/limitation, standing/title to sue, competency, remedy, expenses, jurisdiction, or enforceability is uncertain, flag it prominently and propose the narrowest verification task.

Drafts an Employment Tribunal claim alleging employer interference with, detriment for, or dismissal arising from exercise of family-related statutory leave rights under UK law.

> **Scotland/UK Adaptation:** This skill has been converted from US FMLA (29 U.S.C. § 2601 et seq.) to UK family leave rights under the Employment Rights Act 1996, Maternity and Parental Leave Regulations 1999 (SI 1999/3312), Paternity and Adoption Leave Regulations 2002, Shared Parental Leave Regulations 2014, Parental Bereavement Leave Regulations 2020, and the Employment Tribunals (Constitution and Rules of Procedure) Regulations 2013. Claims proceed in the Employment Tribunal (not federal court). Scotland shares the same UK-wide employment legislation via the Employment Tribunals (Scotland) Regulations.

## No Direct FMLA Equivalent

UK employment law has **no single statute** equivalent to the FMLA. Instead, family-related leave entitlements are spread across multiple statutes and regulations:

| UK Right | Duration | Pay | ERA 1996 Section |
|----------|----------|-----|-------------------|
| Ordinary Maternity Leave | 52 weeks | SMP/MA (up to 39 wks) | §71-75 |
| Paternity Leave | 2 weeks | SPP (or flat rate) | §80A-80E |
| Shared Parental Leave | Up to 50 weeks | ShPP | §75E-75K |
| Parental Leave | 18 wks/child (under 18) | Unpaid | §76-80 |
| Parental Bereavement Leave | 2 weeks | SPaBL | §80EA-80ED |
| Time Off for Dependants | Reasonable (emergency) | Unpaid | §57A-57B |
| Carer's Leave | 1 week/year | Unpaid | §80F-80J |
| Flexible Working (statutory right) | Ongoing | - | §80F |

## Prerequisites

Gather before drafting:

1. **Employment records** - contract, offer letter, written statement of particulars, pay slips, P60
2. **Leave documentation** - requests, employer responses, notifications, correspondence
3. **Adverse action evidence** - dismissal letter, detriment notice, disciplinary records, pay reduction
4. **Medical/eligibility documentation** - MATB1 certificate, medical evidence, birth/adoption certificate
5. **Employer family leave policy** - employee handbook, written policies
6. **ACAS Early Conciliation certificate** - mandatory before Employment Tribunal claim
7. **Witness information** - names, titles, contact info
8. **Damages documentation** - pay slips, pension statements, out-of-pocket expenses

## Quick Start

1. Identify the correct statutory leave right and entitlement
2. Confirm eligibility (length of service, qualifying conditions, notification compliance)
3. Build timeline of request, employer response, and adverse action
4. Draft claim in ET1 format (detriment / automatic unfair dismissal / breach of contract)
5. Calculate remedy (compensatory award, basic award, injury to feelings)
6. Complete ET1 form and submit to Employment Tribunal (Scotland or England & Wales)

## ET1 Claim Structure (Employment Tribunal)

### I. Claimant & Respondent Details (ET1 Form)

- **Claimant**: Full legal name, address, NI number, job title, employment start/end dates
- **Respondent**: Employer's full legal name, registered address, Companies House number (if incorporated), payroll/HR contact
- **Tribunal**: Employment Tribunal (Scotland) - Glasgow, Edinburgh, Aberdeen, Dundee or Employment Tribunal (England & Wales)

### II. Jurisdiction & Time Limits

| Claim Type | Time Limit | Legislation |
|------------|------------|-------------|
| Detriment for family leave | 3 months less one day from act/omission | ERA 1996 §48 |
| Automatic unfair dismissal | 3 months less one day from effective date of termination | ERA 1996 §111 |
| Breach of contract | 3 months less one day | ERA 1996 §191-200 |

**ACAS Early Conciliation** must precede any claim. Time limit is paused for the EC period (typically up to 6 weeks).

### III. Eligibility & Qualifying Conditions

Establish eligibility with documentary support:

| Leave Type | Qualifying Conditions | Evidence |
|------------|----------------------|----------|
| Maternity | Employee status; notify employer by 15th week before EWC; MATB1 | MATB1, employment contract |
| Paternity | 26 weeks' continuous service by 15th week before EWC; intend to care for child | Birth certificate, SC3/SC4 form |
| Parental Leave | 1 year's continuous service; child under 18; 21 days' notice | Employment records |
| Time off for dependants | Day 1 right (no qualifying period); emergency involving dependant | Correspondence, records |
| Carer's Leave | Day 1 right; care for dependant with long-term care need | Notice to employer |
| Flexible working | 26 weeks' continuous service (or day 1 right under ERA 2025 reforms) | Application, response |

### IV. Leave Timeline

Build chronological compliance table. Each missed step or obstruction is a potential detriment:

| Event | Statutory Requirement |
|-------|----------------------|
| Employee notice of intention | Maternity: 15th week before EWC; Paternity: 15th week before EWC |
| Employer confirmation | Within 28 days of notification (maternity/paternity) |
| Employer notification of rights | Implied duty, employer must not mislead as to leave entitlements |
| Return to work notification | If returning early from maternity leave: 8 weeks' notice |
| Flexible working request response | Within 3 months (or notification of delayed decision) |
| Refusal grounds | Must be one of 8 statutory business grounds (ERA 1996 §80G(1)) |

### V. Causes of Action

Number each ground separately.

**Detriment (ERA 1996 §47C / §48):**

| Violation Type | Key Provision |
|---------------|---------------|
| Subjecting employee to detriment for taking/ seeking leave | ERA 1996 §47C(1) |
| Failure to pay statutory maternity/paternity/shared parental pay | SSPA 1992 |
| Refusal to allow time off for dependants | ERA 1996 §57A |
| Dismissal or unfavourable treatment for flexible working request | ERA 1996 §47C(2) |
| Failure to notify leave rights | Breach of implied term/statutory duty |
| Discrimination due to pregnancy/maternity leave | Equality Act 2010 §18 |

**Automatic Unfair Dismissal (ERA 1996 §99):**

Dismissal is automatically unfair if the reason (or principal reason) is:
- Pregnancy or any reason connected to pregnancy, Birth of a child, Taking or seeking ordinary/additional maternity leave, Taking or seeking paternity leave, Taking or seeking adoption leave, Taking or seeking shared parental leave, Taking or seeking parental leave, Taking time off for dependants, Taking parental bereavement leave, Making a flexible working application

Establish causation through:
- Temporal proximity between protected leave and dismissal, Comparator evidence (non-leave employees treated more favourably)
- Employer statements linking dismissal to leave usage, Pretext (shifting or unsupported justifications)

For every ground: specify dates, actors (name + job title), precise act/omission, consequence, and cite the statutory provision. Use direct quotations where available.

### VI. Evidence Inventory

- Employment records (contract, written statement, personnel file)
- Time/attendance records (pay slips, timesheets)
- Leave correspondence (emails, letters, text messages)
- Medical documentation (MATB1, fit notes, GP/hospital letters)
- Employer family leave policies (handbook, intranet)
- Performance/discipline records (pre- and post-leave)
- Comparative evidence (treatment of comparable non-leave employees)
- ACAS Early Conciliation certificate (mandatory)
- Documents in employer's exclusive possession (flag for disclosure)

### VII. Remedy

**Detriment (ERA 1996 §49):**

| Category | Basis | Note |
|----------|-------|------|
| Compensation for financial loss | ERA 1996 §49(2) | Lost earnings, benefits |
| Injury to feelings | Vento bands (Scotland applies same bands as E&W) | Up to £46,900 (2024/25 Vento upper band) |
| Interest | Employment Tribunals (Interest) Order 1990 | 8% per annum on past losses |
| Declaration of rights | Tribunal's inherent power | |

**Automatic Unfair Dismissal (ERA 1996 §118-127):**

| Award | Basis | Calculation |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| Basic award | ERA 1996 §119-122 | Age × service × weekly pay (capped at £700/week for 2024/25) |
| Compensatory award | ERA 1996 §123-126 | Just and equitable; capped at £105,707 (2024/25) |
| Additional award | ERA 1996 §117 | Where re-engagement/reinstatement order not complied with |

**Vento Bands for Injury to Feelings (2024/25):**
- Lower band: £1,200, £12,900 (less serious cases)
- Middle band: £12,900, £38,600 (serious but not exceptional)
- Upper band: £38,600, £64,900 (most serious)
- For discriminatory dismissal: Equality Act 2010 claims attract Vento damages

Provide itemised schedules of loss.

### VIII. ET1 Completion

- ACAS Early Conciliation number (mandatory)
- Grounds of complaint, concise, numbered paragraphs, Remedy sought, specific and quantified where possible, Signed and dated

## Pitfalls & Checks

- **Time limits**: 3 months less one day from the act complained of (strictly enforced in Employment Tribunals)
- **ACAS Early Conciliation**: Mandatory, no certificate, no claim
- **Employment Tribunal (Scotland)**: Separate administration from England & Wales; use ET1(S) form or online portal
- **No 'right to sue' letter required**: Unlike US, no DOL complaint required before tribunal claim
- **Compensatory award cap**: £105,707 for ordinary unfair dismissal; uncapped for automatic unfair dismissal linked to discrimination
- **Protected disclosure**: If leave refusal connects to whistleblowing, consider additional claim under ERA 1996 §43A-43L
- **Pregnancy/maternity discrimination**: Equality Act 2010 §18 provides additional protection beyond ERA 1996 - consider dual claims
- **Identify correct respondent**: Not always the named employer; check whether subsidiary, parent company, or individual is proper respondent
- **Preserve for disclosure**: Keep all correspondence, medical evidence, and employer policies, Mark uncertain citations with [VERIFY]

## Foreign-Law / US-Origin Guardrail

This skill may contain inherited US terminology. For Scotland/UK use, translate rather than copy. Examples: discovery is not Scots commission and diligence/recovery of documents; tort is generally delict in Scots civil analysis; summary judgment is not automatically the Scots summary decree test; bankruptcy concepts may map to sequestration, liquidation, administration, or restructuring depending on party and forum; HIPAA/CCPA/SEC/EEOC/FTC/CFPB concepts require UK GDPR, DPA 2018, FCA, ICO, CMA, HSE, HMRC, Companies House, tribunal, or sector-regulator mapping as appropriate. If the matter is genuinely US or foreign-law, quarantine the foreign-law analysis and warn that local counsel/source verification is required.
