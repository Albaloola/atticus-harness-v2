# Petition / Motion for Approval of Group Proceedings Settlement, Scotland Template

> Adapt to the live Court of Session procedural route and any case-management order. Verify whether a petition, motion, note, minute, or other form is required.

## Court heading

Court of Session  
[Outer House / Inner House if applicable]  
[Case name and reference]

## Craves / orders sought

The petitioner/representative party respectfully seeks orders:

1. Approving the proposed settlement agreement dated [date].
2. Approving the notice plan and form of notice.
3. Approving the distribution scheme and settlement administrator.
4. Approving expenses/fees/admin arrangements, if competent.
5. Making such further orders as are necessary to give effect to settlement.

## Factual background

Set out procedural history: authorisation, group definition, pleadings, case-management orders, evidence exchanged, negotiations/mediation, expert input, and settlement terms.

## Legal basis and competency

Identify the statutory and rules basis for court approval. Cite the 2018 Act, relevant Court of Session rules, interlocutors, and any practice directions. Mark every proposition `[VERIFY]` until checked against current sources.

## Fairness and adequacy analysis

Address:

- Strengths and weaknesses of liability, causation, quantum, prescription/limitation, defences, appeal risk.
- Risks, cost, delay, enforcement, solvency, evidential uncertainty.
- Range of reasonable outcomes compared with settlement value.
- Treatment of different group-member categories.
- Objections received and response.
- Independence and adequacy of representation.

## Notice and participation

Explain notice method, accessibility, deadlines, opt-in/opt-out/objection handling, and evidence that notice reached group members as far as practicable.

## Distribution and administration

Attach distribution schedule, claims process, audit method, residual funds proposal, data-protection safeguards, and reporting timetable.

## Evidence list

Attach settlement agreement, proposed notice, distribution plan, group definition, mediator statement if appropriate, expert/quantum reports, objections, source log, and draft interlocutor.
