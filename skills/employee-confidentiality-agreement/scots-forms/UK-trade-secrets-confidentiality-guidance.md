# UK Trade Secrets & Confidentiality, Reference for Scottish Drafting

## Key Legislation

- **Trade Secrets (Enforcement, etc.) Regulations 2018** (SI 2018/597) - implements EU Trade Secrets Directive; defines trade secret, remedies, preservation of confidentiality during proceedings
- **Employment Rights Act 1996** - post-termination restrictions, implied duties of good faith and confidentiality
- **Equality Act 2010** - protections relevant to whistleblowing (similar to US DTSA/NLRA carveouts)
- **Public Interest Disclosure Act 1998** (PIDA) - protects whistleblowers who disclose in the public interest; replaces DTSA notice requirement
- **Data Protection Act 2018 / UK GDPR** - governs processing of personal data in employment context
- **Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977** - may apply to enforceability of certain terms
- **Consumer Rights Act 2015** - for B2C contexts

## Key Differences from US

| US Concept | UK/Scottish Equivalent |
|---|---|
| DTSA (18 U.S.C. § 1833(b)) | No direct equivalent; PIDA protects whistleblowers |
| DTSA whistleblower notice | Not required in UK; instead include PIDA/whistleblowing acknowledgement |
| NLRA carveout | No equivalent; UK has separate union/collective bargaining consultation rights |
| State-specific enforceability (CA, ND, MN) | UK-wide; reasonableness test applies to all restrictive covenants |
| Blue-pencil doctrine | Available in England/Wales; Scottish courts apply similar approach but less codified |
| Trade secret definition | Trade Secrets Regulations 2018 Reg 2 - broad definition similar to UTSA |
| Consideration for existing employees | UK: contract variation requires fresh consideration (or deed) |
| At-will employment | UK has unfair dismissal protections from day 1 (2 years for most claims) |
| FTC Non-Compete Rule | UK: non-competes must protect legitimate business interest; no general ban; post-termination restrictions subject to reasonableness |

## UK Post-Termination Restrictions

UK law applies a **strict reasonableness test**: a restrictive covenant is void as an unreasonable restraint of trade unless the employer can show it:
1. Protects a **legitimate business interest** (trade secrets, customer connections, employee stability, goodwill)
2. Goes no further than **reasonably necessary** (geography, duration, scope)
3. Is consistent with the **public interest**

Typical durations accepted by UK courts:
- Non-solicitation of customers: 6 to 12 months, Non-solicitation of employees: 6 to 12 months, Non-dealing clauses: 6 to 12 months (harder to justify)
- Non-competition: 3 to 6 months (rarely exceeds 12 months outside senior executives)

## Scottish Courts

- Sheriff Court or Court of Session for breach of confidence claims, Interdict (injunction) available for threatened breach, Interim interdict available on balance of convenience, No presumption of irreparable harm, specific evidence of harm required
