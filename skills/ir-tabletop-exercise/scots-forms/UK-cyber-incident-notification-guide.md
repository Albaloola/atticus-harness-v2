# UK Cyber Incident Notification & Response, Guidance Note

## Notification Deadlines (UK/Scotland)

| Regime | Deadline | Authority |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| UK GDPR (Art. 33) | 72 hours to ICO | ICO (Information Commissioner's Office) |
| NIS Regulations 2018 | As soon as possible (24hrs for sector specific) | ICO + relevant Competent Authority (sector-specific) |
| PSN (Public Sector Network) | As per PSN terms | Cabinet Office |
| PCI DSS (UK) | Per card brand rules, typically 24 to 72 hrs | Card brands + acquiring bank |
| FCA (regulated firms) | Immediately upon awareness | FCA (PRIN, SYSC) |
| Ofcom (telecoms) | 24 hours to Ofcom | Ofcom (Communications Act 2003) |
| ICO breach report | 72 hours to ICO; affected individuals without undue delay | ICO |

## UK Cyber Resilience Bodies

- **NCSC** (National Cyber Security Centre) - UK's technical authority for cyber security; provides Exercise in a Box, tabletop guidance
- **ICO** (Information Commissioner's Office) - data protection regulator
- **OFSI** (Office of Financial Sanctions Implementation) - sanctions compliance
- **National Crime Agency (NCA)** - cyber crime investigation
- **Police Scotland** - cyber crime reporting and investigation
- **Scottish Government Resilience Division** - emergency response coordination in Scotland

## NCSC Tabletop Exercise Resources

The NCSC provides free tabletop exercise materials via **Exercise in a Box** (https://www.ncsc.gov.uk/section/exercise-in-a-box/overview):
- Pre-built scenarios (ransomware, phishing, supply chain)
- Facilitation guidance, After-action report templates, Aimed at organisations with no dedicated cyber exercise capability

## Scotland-Specific Considerations

- SEPA for environmental/hazardous data impact in breach scenarios, COPFS for criminal investigation of cyber offences, Police Scotland's Cyber Crime Unit, Scottish Courts handle data breach civil claims / interdict applications, Prescription (limitation): 6 years for delict claims (personal data breaches)
- UK GDPR/DPA 2018 applies across the UK including Scotland, No equivalent to state-level breach notification; single ICO regime

## Sanctions (Ransomware) - UK

- OFSI must be notified for any sanctions implications in ransomware payments, No formal OFAC-style ransomware advisory, but NCSC strongly advises against paying ransoms, National Cyber Security Centre: "Paying ransoms does not guarantee data recovery"
- Financial sanctions: reporting to OFSI within 24 hours of suspicion
