# UK Guidance: Life Insurance Trusts in Scotland

## Key Difference: Tax Framework

The US ILIT is designed to exclude life insurance from the **US estate tax** (IRC § 2042) using **Crummey withdrawal powers** to qualify for **annual gift tax exclusions** (IRC § 2503(b)).

In the UK (including Scotland):
- **Inheritance Tax (IHT)** replaces US estate tax
- **No IRC § 2042 equivalent** - instead, life insurance proceeds are part of the deceased's estate for IHT purposes unless written into an appropriate trust
- **No Crummey powers** - UK trust law does not use this mechanism
- **No annual gift tax exclusion** - instead, the UK has: Annual Exemption (£3,000 p.a.), Normal Expenditure exemption, Small Gifts exemption

## UK Life Insurance Trust Framework

### Writing Life Insurance in Trust

The most common approach is to write the policy into a **trust from inception** (not transferring an existing policy). This can be:
1. **Absolute Trust** - policy held for named beneficiaries with immediate vested interest
2. **Flexible / Discretionary Trust** - trustees have discretion over distribution (most common for IHT planning)
3. **Loan Trust** - for IHT mitigation with retained access to capital
4. **Discounted Gift Trust** - for larger policies

### Inheritance Tax Treatment

- **Written in trust from outset**: proceeds outside the estate, no IHT on the payout
- **Existing policy transferred into trust**: potentially a chargeable transfer for IHT; can use a "gift" with reservation of benefit rules
- **Seven-year rule**: gifts into trust may be subject to IHT if the settlor dies within 7 years (taper relief applies)
- **Discretionary trust IHT**: periodic 10-year charges and exit charges may apply

### Key UK Legislation

- **Inheritance Tax Act 1984** - primary IHT legislation (s.3A for gifts; s.43 for trusts)
- **Finance Act 2006** - major reforms to trust IHT treatment
- **Trusts and Succession (Scotland) Act 2024** - modernises Scottish trust law (currently progressing)
- **Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995** - formal validity of trust deeds in Scotland

### Scottish Trust Law Differences

- **Trustees**: Scottish trust law allows a sole trustee for all trusts (unlike England where land-owning trusts require multiple trustees)
- **No perpetuities period** in Scotland (English RAP abolished in Scotland):
  - Scottish trust can last indefinitely
- **Trustee powers** governed by Trusts (Scotland) Act 1921 and Trusts (Scotland) Act 1961
- **Execution**: trust deeds require writing and, for certain purposes, notarisation

### Tax Treatment of Life Insurance Payouts

- **Income tax**: life insurance payouts are generally tax-free in the UK (unlike the US where interest may be taxable)
- **IHT**: as above, trust planning keeps the payout outside the estate

## Practical Note

The US ILIT skill is a poor fit for Scottish practice because the entire tax rationale (IRC § 2042, Crummey powers, gift tax annual exclusion) has no UK equivalent. However, the **structural knowledge** of life insurance trusts, irrevocability, trustee powers, distribution provisions, transfers to UK trust drafting.

For a Scottish practitioner, the core value of this skill would be in drafting a:
- **Discretionary trust** for life insurance owned by the trust from inception, With **standard Scottish trust provisions** (Trusts (Scotland) Acts)
- Using **UK IHT exemptions** rather than US gift tax exclusions, With **no requirement for Crummey notices**

Sources: Inheritance Tax Act 1984; Trusts (Scotland) Act 1921; Trusts and Succession (Scotland) Act 2024; HMRC Trusts and Estates Manual; STEP Scotland guidance.
